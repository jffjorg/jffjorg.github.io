The Honorable Gary Y. Tanaka, Judge of the Los Angeles Superior Court - Southwest District, has issued a tentative ruling on the Motion to Approve Settlement in the case of Keep America Safe and Beautiful, Inc. v. Memebox Corporation, et al. (Case Number: 23TRCV01165). The Court grants the Motion to Approve Settlement and enters judgment in accordance with the terms of the proposed consent judgment.

The case involves allegations that the Defendant, Memebox Corporation, manufactures, packages, distributes, markets, and/or sells consumer products containing DEHP, a chemical that must be warned about under Proposition 65. The Plaintiff, Keep America Safe and Beautiful, Inc., seeks to cause the Defendant to warn consumers in California about exposure to DEHP and requests an injunction and civil penalties.

The Court finds that the warning required by the settlement complies with Chapter 6.6 of the Safe Drinking Water and Toxic Enforcement Act of 1986, and that the award of attorneys' fees of $18,000 and the penalty amount of $2,000.00 are reasonable. The Court grants the Motion to Approve Settlement and enters judgment in accordance with the terms of the consent judgment.
