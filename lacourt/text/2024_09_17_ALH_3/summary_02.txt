The tentative ruling by Judge William A. Crowfoot grants Defendant's petition to compel arbitration and stay the action in the case of Yaping Dang, Austin Yuen, Sheng-Ching Tsao Chen, Xin Zheng v. WFG National Title Insurance Company, Dawn Weller, Jeff Leung. The case involves a dispute over a title insurance policy and whether the defendants failed to act on a claim regarding a covered loss.

The defendants argue that the arbitration clause in the policy is enforceable and that the plaintiffs' claims are arbitrable. The judge agrees, finding that the arbitration clause is valid and that the claims are covered within the scope of the agreement. The judge also rejects the plaintiffs' argument that the arbitration clause is unconscionable, finding that it is a standard form contract that is not overly oppressive.

The judge orders that the defendants' petition to compel arbitration be granted, and that the action be stayed pending arbitration. The parties who intend to submit on this tentative must send an email to the court indicating their intention to submit, as directed by the court's instructions.
