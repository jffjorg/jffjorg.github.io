 Case Number:  24LBCV01550    Hearing Date:   January 21, 2025    Dept:  S25 Tentative RulingThe Court has reviewed the proposed settlement and finds that the settlement itself is fair and reasonable. The minor claimant here, Joseph Elijah Lashley, has agreed to dismiss his claims and will receive $20,000.00 from Defendant 630 Cedar Avenue, LLC (“Defendant”). (Pet. ¶ 10.) A settlement payment is also being made to Plaintiff Chanel Groomes in the amount of $100,000.00. (Pet., ¶ 11.b; see also Attachments 11.b.3 and 11.b.6.) The amount of attorney’s fees requested, representing 30 percent of the gross settlement to minor claimant, is $6,000.00. (See Attachment 13a [Declaration of Jon Naudi, Esq.] ¶¶ 2-4.) The Claimant, through his parent and Guardian ad litem, Chanel Groomes, agreed to a 40 percent contingency fee arrangement. (See Attachment 17a [Retainer Agreement].) In relevant part, the retainer agreement provides: “For representation in the Case, Attorney shall be entitled to forty percent (40%) of all gross amount(s) recovered from any source, whether by judgment, settlement, relocation fees, unpaid/forgiven rent, from Clients’ landlord and/or any other party/defendant/insurance carrier (hereinafter “Defendant” or “Defendants”), with respect to matters/controversies/claims asserted in or related to the Case (the “Contingency Fee”).” (Attachment 17a § B.) The Court finds that Plaintiffs’ counsel has set forth sufficient facts breaking down and explaining the legal fees and costs incurred in litigating this matter. (See Cal. Rules of Court, rule 7.955(b).)The Court GRANTS the instant petition as to Joseph Elijah Lashley. The Court will sign the Proposed Order.
