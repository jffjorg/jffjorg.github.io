The tentative ruling addresses the demurrer and motion to strike filed by Lorraine D'Alessio and D'Alessio Law Corporation, defendants in a lawsuit brought by SmartSilk Corp. The plaintiff alleges that the defendants made fraudulent and negligent misrepresentations regarding their ability to source and deliver medical grade gloves. The demurrer argues that the complaint fails to state a cause of action, while the motion to strike argues that the complaint is vague and fails to plead the circumstances surrounding the alleged fraud with particularity.

The court rejects the demurrer, finding that the complaint sufficiently alleges a cause of action for fraudulent and negligent misrepresentation. The court also rejects the motion to strike, finding that the complaint is not vague and that the allegations of fraud and misrepresentation are sufficient to support the claims.

The court also addresses the attorney-client privilege and the marital privilege, finding that they do not bar the defendants from presenting a meaningful defense in the case. Finally, the court denies the motion to strike punitive damages, finding that the complaint sufficiently alleges a basis for punitive damages.
