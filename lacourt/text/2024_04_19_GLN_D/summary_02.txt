```
Case Number: 21STCV18257
Case Name: Cui v. Snow Joe, LLC, et al.
Hearing Date: April 19, 2024
Department: D
Tentative Ruling:

**Case Facts**

* Plaintiff Tracy Cui alleges that she suffered physical and mental injuries due to a Snow Joe Electric 3-in-1 Blower/Vacuum/Mulcher.
* Defendant Snow Joe LLC seeks to conduct an independent medical examination (IME) of Plaintiff to evaluate her claimed injuries.

**Procedural Issues**

* Defendant's motion to conduct an IME lacks specific details regarding the examination, testing, and procedures.
* Defendant failed to provide a proposed order and did not specify the qualifications of the examiner.
* Plaintiff objects to the broad scope of the examination and lack of notice.

**Merits**

* Defendant argues that good cause exists for the IME, citing Plaintiff's allegations of physical and mental injuries.
* Defendant's expert, Dr. Kalechstein, has expertise in neuro-psychology and would evaluate Plaintiff's claimed injuries of a psychological nature.

**Conclusion**

* The court grants Defendant's motion to conduct an IME of Plaintiff, limited to evaluating her claimed injuries of a psychological nature, including anxiety, depression, stress, and trauma.
* The court orders Defendant to provide a proposed order and to specify the qualifications of the examiner.
* Plaintiff is ordered to appear for the IME.

**Significant Statements**

* The court notes that Plaintiff's stipulation does not comply with Code of Civil Procedure section 2032.320.
* The court orders Plaintiff to refrain from pursuing damages for mental or emotional distress over and above that usually associated with the physical injuries claimed.
* The court notes that Plaintiff intends to testify to her emotional distress and will not claim clinical diagnoses of depression, anxiety, acute stress disorder, acute stress reaction, or posttraumatic stress disorder.
