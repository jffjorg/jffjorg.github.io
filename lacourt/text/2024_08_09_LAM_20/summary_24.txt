And here is the English-text summary:

This tentative ruling is in response to a Petition filed by Rancho del Valle Preservation, L.P. (Petitioner) to allow the filing of an unsigned certification of the removal of its general partner, Respondent Rancho Del Valle Preservation, LLC. The Petitioner contends that the Respondent defaulted under the limited partnership agreement and has been replaced. The Petitioner was unable to obtain a signature from the Respondent and has been unable to reach any officer or employee of the Respondent for purposes of obtaining a signature on the Amendment of Limited Partnership form. The California Secretary of State requires either a signature from the Respondent or an order from the Court to file the Certificate of Amendment. The Court has entered a default for the Respondent and has granted the Petition under California Corporations Code Section 15902.05. The Court finds that the Respondent has been removed as general partner, Kingdom Rancho Del Valle, LLC has replaced the Respondent as general partner, and the California Secretary of State is to accept the Certificate of Amendment even though it is not signed by the Respondent.
