The Court may issue
an order allowing an attorney to withdraw from representation, after notice to
the client.  (CCP § 284.)  The attorney may withdraw from representation
where the withdrawal would not result in undue prejudice to the client’s
interest—that is, counsel cannot withdraw at a critical point in the
litigation.  (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal. App. 4th 904, 915; see
California Rule of Professional Conduct 3-700.) 
 
Attorney Maurice D.
Pessah, counsel of record for Defendants Christopher Phelps, Salt’s Cure, LLC,
Shahryar Ravanshenas, and Highway Equities, LLC, seeks to withdraw from
representation of Defendant. Counsel states that there has been a breakdown of
the attorney-client relationship. Trial is set for October 2025; no prejudice
will result from Counsel’s withdrawal.
 
There is good cause
for Counsel’s withdrawal. As to Defendants Shahryar Ravanshenas and Highway
Equities, LLC, counsel has complied with CRC 3.1362. The motions as to
Defendants Shahryar Ravanshenas and Highway Equities, LLC is GRANTED.  The Court will set an OSC re: legal
representation of Defendant Highway Equities, LLC.
 
As to Defendants Christopher Phelps,
and Salt’s Cure, LLC, the motions were rescheduled to September 5, 2024. Counsel
filed a proof of service showing notice to Plaintiff’s counsel that the motions
were rescheduled, but there is no proof of service indicating these Defendants
were served with notice of the rescheduled hearings. The motions as to these Defendants
are denied without prejudice.
