And here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The court has issued a tentative ruling on a motion filed by Plaintiff Maria Rodriguez to compel further responses to Special Interrogatory Nos. 14, 25, 40-45 and 59. The plaintiff is seeking to force the defendant, General Motors, LLC, to provide more comprehensive responses to these interrogatories, which relate to the company's policies and procedures for evaluating customer requests for repurchase under the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act.

The court has granted the motion, finding that the defendant's responses to these interrogatories are incomplete or evasive. Specifically, the court has ordered the defendant to provide further responses regarding the identities of individuals responsible for the decision to not repurchase or replace the subject vehicle, as well as the documents consulted, reviewed and/or obtained as part of their investigation into the subject vehicle.

The defendant is required to provide these further responses within 10 days of the court's ruling. If all parties to the motion submit on this tentative, the court will adopt it as the final order.
