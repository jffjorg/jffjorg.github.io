Princess Apartments v. Arbab, Case
no. 24SMCV02483
Hearing date October 7, 2024
Plaintiff’s
Motions to Compel Discovery and Deem Admitted
Landlord
plaintiff Princess Apartments sues tenant defendant Abab for failure to pay
rent and for forfeiture of the rental agreement. Plaintiff moves to compel
production of documents, responses to interrogatories and to deem admitted
plaintiff’s first set of requests for admissions and seeks sanctions of
$2,207.74. Defendant failed to respond to any of the discovery and has not
opposed plaintiff’s motions.
When
a party fails to timely respond to written discovery requests, including
interrogatories and requests for production of documents, propounding party can
move for an order compelling a response without objections. Code of Civ. Proc.
§2023.290. If a party fails to serve timely responses to requests for
admission, the requesting party can move for an order deeming the truth of any
matter specified in the requests admitted. Code of Civ. Proc. §2033.280.
Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel
Responses to Interrogatories
Plaintiff
has provided the declaration of Lane Nussbaum stating defendant failed to timely
respond to plaintiff’s interrogatories, set one, due June 17, 2024. Decl.
Nussbaum paras. 2-3. There is no opposition. GRANTED.
Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel
Production of Documents
Plaintiff
has provided the declaration of Lane Nussbaum stating defendant failed to timely
respond to plaintiff’s demand for production of documents, set one, due June
17, 2024. Decl. Nussbaum paras. 2-3. There is no opposition. GRANTED.
Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem Admitted
Plaintiff
has provided the declaration of Lane Nussbaum stating defendant failed to
respond to plaintiff’s request for admission, set one, due June 17, 2024. Decl.
Nussbaum paras. 2-3. There is no opposition. 
Plaintiff’s
counsel spent 2.5 hours on the motions at $475/hour. Decl. Nussbaum para. 5. Counsel
anticipates spending 2 hours reviewing any opposition papers, preparing for the
court hearing, traveling to and from the hearing and attending the hearing. Id.
The filing fee for this motion is $70.24. Id. As there was no
opposition, 2 hours to review the opposition and appear is excessive. 
The
court awards a total of 3.5 hours at $475/hour, plus costs of $70.24, payable
within 30 days. GRANTED.
