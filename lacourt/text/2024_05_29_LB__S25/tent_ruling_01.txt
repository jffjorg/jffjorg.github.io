  Case Number:  23LBCV02250    Hearing Date:   May 29, 2024    Dept:  S25 Procedural BackgroundOn November 27, 2023, Plaintiffs Sarah Keith, an individual and successor-in-interest to Decedent, Marissa Offlee; Daniel Offlee, an individual and successor-in-interest to Decedent, Marissa Offlee, filed this action against Defendants LYFT, Inc., Jamie P. Ellet, and Does 1 through 100. Plaintiffs allege four causes of action: (1) negligence (wrongful death), (2) negligence per se, (3) negligence (survivor claim), and (4) negligent infliction of emotional distress.On December 9, 2022, Plaintiff Sarah Keith was operating her vehicle northbound on Harbor Boulevard in the city of Los Angeles. (Compl., ¶ 26.) Sarah Keith alleges that while entering the intersection of Harbor Boulevard and Swinford Street, she had a solid green light indicating that she had right of way. (Compl., ¶ 26.) At the same time, Defendant Jamie P. Ellet (“Defendant Ellet”) was transporting two passengers he had picked up using the LYFT Application. (Compl., ¶ 27.) Defendant Ellet ran a red light and caused his vehicle to collide violently with Plaintiff’s vehicle; Sarah Keith survived severe physical and mental injuries and Decedent Marissa Offlee (“Decedent”) passed away from her injuries on February 25, 2023. (Compl., ¶¶ 27, 34.)On January 23, 2024, Defendant Ellet filed a cross-complaint against Sarah Keith and Roes 1 through 25. In the form complaint, Defendant Ellet asserts three causes of action: (1) indemnification, (2) apportionment of fault, and (3) declaratory relief. Due to the cross-complaint filing, Sarah Keith is also a cross-defendant in this action.Sarah Keith is represented by Sweet James, LLP in her capacity as Plaintiff and represented by Acker & Whipple, APC in her capacity as Cross-Defendant. On May 6, 2024, Neyilah A. Carter, Esq. moved to be relieved as counsel for Cross-Defendant Keith.AnalysisCross-Defendant Keith’s counsel’s notice of motion and motion to be relieved as counsel is made on the proper Judicial Council of California form MC-051. The notice of motion and motion complies with Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(a).Cross-Defendant Keith’s counsel provided a declaration explaining that counsel has been undermined by circumstances between counsel’s firm and is unable to fulfill counsel’s duties as an officer of the Court and as a representative of cross defendant due to such differences. (Carter Decl., ¶ 2.) The declaration states the reason for the motion in general terms and declines to be more specific without prejudicing cross-defendant’s case. (Carter Decl., ¶ 2.) Further, the declaration is on the proper Judicial Council of California form MC-052. The declaration complies with Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(c).As for service, counsel for Cross-Defendant Keith has provided proper proof of service for the notice of motion and motion, the declaration, and the proposed order. Service complies with Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d).The proposed order has been prepared on the correct Judicial Council of California form MC-053 and has been lodged with the court with the moving paper. The proposed order complies with Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).The following upcoming hearings are scheduled in this matter as of May 23, 2024: (1) the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel hearing on May 30, 2024; (2) the Final Status Conference on November 22, 2024; and (3) the Jury Trial on December 2, 2024.RulingThe motion is GRANTED. The motion complies with all requirements of Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362. Further, there is no showing that granting the motion to be relieved as counsel would result in prejudice.Moving party to give notice.
