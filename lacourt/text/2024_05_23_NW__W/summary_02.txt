**Tentative Ruling**

**Case Number:** 22VECV02441
**Case Name:** R.V. v. LOS ANGELES UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

**Facts:**

* Plaintiff R.V. filed a complaint against Defendant Los Angeles Unified School District (LAUSD) alleging causes of action for negligent supervision, retention, and hiring of an employee who sexually abused her.
* The alleged abuse occurred when plaintiff was approximately 5 years old, around 1994-1995.
* Plaintiff alleges LAUSD failed to take reasonable steps to prevent the abuse.

**Disputes:**

* Defendant LAUSD moves for judgment on the pleadings, arguing that the first, second, third, fourth, fifth, and sixth causes of action are legally impermissible as a violation of the California Constitution.
* Defendant argues plaintiff fails to plead facts with required particularity as required against a public entity.
* Plaintiff argues that defendant LAUSD is liable for the alleged abuse and failure to report suspected child abuse.

**Intentions:**

* Plaintiff intends to seek damages for the alleged abuse and negligence of LAUSD.
* Defendant LAUSD intends to argue that the causes of action are legally impermissible and that plaintiff fails to plead facts with required particularity.

**Rules and Laws:**

* California Constitution, Article XVI, Section 6 (gift clause)
* California Code of Civil Procedure, sections 438 and 439
* Government Code, Section 815.2(a)
* Penal Code, Section 11166, et seq.
