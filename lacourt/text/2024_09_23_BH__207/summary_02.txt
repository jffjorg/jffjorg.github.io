**BACKGROUND**

This case arises from a dispute regarding competing claims on the property located at 9996 Sunset Boulevard.

**DISCUSSION**

The Court's July 23, 2024 Order Appointing Receiver provides that the Receiver shall have the power to take all lawful actions necessary to seek and/or effectuate the removal of any and all unauthorized occupants on the Property.

The Occupants have refused to comply with the Order, despite adequate notice and knowledge of their obligations thereunder.

**FACTS**

* The Occupants have occupied the Property in violation of the Court's Order.
* The Occupants have refused to provide access to the Property for the Receiver to perform their duties.
* The Occupants have refused to provide alternative dates for inspection or turnover of the Property.
* The Occupants have refused to identify the other occupants of the Property.
* The Occupants have refused to provide insurance information or proof of payment of rent.
* The Occupants have been engaging in unlicensed "gaming" and web streaming activities on the Property.

**INTENTIONS**

The Receiver intends to seek relief from the Court to enforce the Order and to ensure that the Occupants comply with their obligations thereunder.

**FINDINGS**

Based on the evidence presented, the Court finds that the Occupants have refused to comply with the Order and have engaged in unlicensed activities on the Property.

**RULING**

The Court grants the OSC re: Contempt and orders the Occupants to show cause why they should not be held in contempt.

The Court also orders the Occupants to:

* Provide access to the Property for the Receiver to perform their duties.
* Identify the other occupants of the Property.
* Provide insurance information or proof of payment of rent.
* Cease engaging in unlicensed "gaming" and web streaming activities on the Property.

The Court further orders the Occupants to post a bond to protect the Receiver for the amount of reasonable monthly rental value of the Property.

(Code Civ. Proc., § 1218, subd. (a))
