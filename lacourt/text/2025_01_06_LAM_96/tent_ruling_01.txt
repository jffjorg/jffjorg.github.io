  Case Number:  23LBUD02695    Hearing Date:   January 6, 2025    Dept:  96 3200 Lakewood LP v. Debra McGee23LBUD02695After a one-day unlawful detainer jury trial, the jury returned a verdict in favor of defendant. On September 10, 2024, the Court entered judgment for Defendant. The lease which both parties entered and was the subject of the lawsuit has an attorney fee cap of $500. Civil Code Section 1717. Reynolds Metals Co. v. Alperson (1979 ( 25 Cal.3d. 124, 130). Nonetheless, Defendant seeks attorney fees of $45,840 at an hourly billing rate of $800.00.Defendant argues that that the lease was forfeited and therefore not controlling. See Defendant’s Motion for Attorney Fees, page 3, line 21-28. In fact a lease would have to be forfeited by the court, not by Plaintiff, which never occurred because Defendant prevailed. Plaintiff attempted to forfeit the lease, have it cancelled and regain possession, but this never happened in light of the jury’s verdict. Therefore, the lease is still enact, with the rights and obligations to either sideremaining, including the limitations on the amount of attorney fees that can be collected.Therefore, Defendant is entitled to the fees as limited in the lease to $500.00.
