 Case Number:  22STCV30713    Hearing Date:   September 30, 2024    Dept:  28  Having considered the petitioning papers, the Court rules as follows. BACKGROUND On September 20, 2022, Plaintiff Jack Feresten (“Plaintiff”), by and through his guardian ad litem Michael Feresten, filed this action against Defendants Palisades Village Center Associates LLC (“Palisades”), Academy of Technology Art and Music LLC (“Academy”), Anthony Wamble (“Wamble”), Brett Sutherlin, individually and as trustee of the Sutherlin Family Trust (“Brett Sutherlin”), Karen Sutherlin, individually and as trustee of the Sutherlin Family Trust (“Karen Sutherlin”), and Does 1-50 for general negligence and premises liability. On January 20, 2023, Palisades, Academy, and Wamble filed an answer and a cross-complaint against Cross-Defendants Brett Sutherlin, Karen Sutherlin, and Roes 1-20 for indemnity and contribution.  On July 18, 2023, Karen Sutherlin and Brett Sutherlin filed an answer to the cross-complaint.  On October 4, 2023, Palisades, Academy, and Wamble amended the cross-complaint to include Cross- Defendant Erika Feresten (“Erika Feresten”) as Roe 1. On December 26, 2023, Erika Feresten filed an answer to the cross-complaint. On April 11, 2023, Brett Sutherlin and Karen Sutherlin filed an answer to Plaintiff’s complaint and filed a cross-complaint against Cross-Defendants Michael Feresten (“Michael Feresten”) and Moes 1-10 for contribution, indemnity, declaratory relief, and negligent supervision. On May 18, 2023, Michael Feresten filed an answer to the cross-complaint. On August 28, 2023, the Court dismissed the cross-complaint without prejudice at the request of Brett Sutherlin and Karen Sutherlin. On December 5, 2023, Palisades, Academy, and Wamble filed a first amended answer to Plaintiff’s complaint. On March 26, 2024, Plaintiff filed a notice of settlement. On August 15, 2024, Petitioner Michael Feresten (“Petitioner”) filed a petition to approve the compromise of Plaintiff’s action to be heard on September 24, 2024.  On September 24, 2024, the Court issued a tentative ruling and continued the hearing to September 30, 2024. On September 30, 2024, Petitioner filed an amended petition.PETITIONER’S REQUEST Petitioner Michael Feresten asks the Court to approve the compromise of the pending action of minor Plaintiff Jack Feresten. DISCUSSION The amended petition states the following: Gross settlement amount:                                         $235,000.00          Total medical expenses:                                           $11,960.00Medical expenses paid:                                            $9,251.62Reductions:                                                              $2,708.38To be paid/reimbursed from proceeds:                     $9,251.62Liens:                                                                       $3,709.90             Attorney’s fees:                                                        $47,000.00Costs:                                                                       $13,421.15Net settlement amount:                                             $165,327.23 Petitioner has submitted a completed amended petition which includes all required information.  The Court grants the petition.  CONCLUSION  The  Court GRANTS the amended petition to approve the compromise of minor Plaintiff Jack Feresten’s action filed by Petitioner Michael Feresten on September 30, 2024.  The net  balance of $165,327.23 is to be deposited in insured accounts in City National Bank, Account #123373715, Routing #122016066, at 400 N. Roxbury Dr., Beverly Hills, CA 90210, subject to withdrawal only on authorization of the Court.   The  Court sets an OSC Re: Distribution of Net Settlement on December 6, 2024, at  8:30 a.m., in Department 28 of the Spring Street Courthouse.  The  Court sets an OSC Re: Dismissal of Case (Settlement) on December 6, 2024, at  8:30 a.m., in Department 28 of the Spring Street Courthouse.  Petitioner is ordered to give notice of this ruling.  Petitioner is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within  five days.
