The judge's tentative ruling addresses two motions filed by the plaintiff, Myung Shin. The first motion is to compel the trustee, Elizabeth Yang, to respond to special interrogatories served on February 23, 2024. The trustee failed to serve timely responses, and the court grants the motion, ordering the trustee to serve verified code-compliant responses by October 31, 2024. The court also grants the plaintiff $810.00 in sanctions for the trustee's delay.

The second motion is to deem admitted matters specified in requests for admission served on February 23, 2024. The trustee failed to serve timely responses, but subsequently served verified responses on September 27, 2024. The court denies the motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (c).

The court grants in part the plaintiff's requests for sanctions and orders the trustee and her former counsel, Law Offices of Scott C. Stratman, jointly and severally, to pay the plaintiff $1,620.00 ($810.00 for each motion) by October 31, 2024.
