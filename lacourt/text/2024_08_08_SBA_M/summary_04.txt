* Defendant failed to provide accurate payroll records

**Arbitration Agreement**

* The agreement was signed by Plaintiff without explanation or adequate disclosure

**Unconscionability**

* Plaintiff argues that the arbitration clause is procedurally and substantively unconscionable
* The court finds that the arbitration clause is not procedurally unconscionable because Plaintiff was given an opportunity to read and consider the agreement before signing
* The court does not address the substantive unconscionability argument

**PAGA Claims**

* The parties dispute whether representative PAGA claims are subject to arbitration
* The California Supreme Court has held that a plaintiff whose individual claims are compelled to arbitration retains standing to pursue representative PAGA claims in court

**Ruling**

* The Motion to Compel Arbitration is granted
* The individual PAGA claims are ordered to proceed through arbitration
* The instant action, which would now only encompass representative PAGA claims, is ordered stayed
