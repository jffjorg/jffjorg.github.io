The court has issued a tentative ruling on a motion for summary judgment/summary adjudication filed by State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company. The case involves a breach of contract and bad faith claims arising from State Farm's denial of an insurance claim related to water damage to the plaintiff's home.

The court has granted State Farm's motion for summary adjudication on the punitive damages claim, finding that the circumstances do not demonstrate oppressive or malicious conduct under the heightened factual standard required for punitive damages.

The court has denied State Farm's motion for summary judgment on the breach of contract and bad faith claims, finding that there are triable issues of material fact regarding the cause of the water damage and the reasonableness of State Farm's investigation and denial of the claim.

The court has also denied State Farm's motion for summary adjudication on the breach of contract and bad faith claims, finding that the dispute between the experts on the cause of the subject claim demonstrates a genuinely valid dispute over the origin of the damage on otherwise undisputed contractual terms and circumstances.
