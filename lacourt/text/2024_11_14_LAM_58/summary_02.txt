**Facts**

* Plaintiff filed a complaint on May 3, 2024, alleging breach of contract, fraud, unfair competition, and wage and hour Labor Code claims against his former employer, defendants CitiStaff Solutions Inc., Toro Pallets Inc., and Rodrigo Toro.
* Defendant CitiStaff Solutions Inc. filed a motion to compel arbitration on September 30, 2024.

**Legal Standard**

* Under Code of Civil Procedure section 1281.2, a court may order arbitration of a controversy if it finds that the parties have agreed to arbitrate that dispute.

**Analysis**

* Defendant CitiStaff Solutions Inc. seeks to compel arbitration based on a 2017 Arbitration Agreement, which provides that the parties agree to exclusively engage in Mandatory Mediation and Binding Arbitration for any and all disputes, claims, or controversies.
* The Arbitration Agreement was executed by Plaintiff on September 1, 2017, as part of CitiStaff's employee onboarding process.
* Plaintiff argues that the Arbitration Agreement is barred by Labor Code section 432.6, but the court finds that this statute does not apply to the Arbitration Agreement.
* Even if Labor Code section 432.6 applied, the application of this statute would be preempted under the Federal Arbitration Act (FAA).
* Defendant CitiStaff Solutions Inc. has met its evidentiary burden of showing that the Arbitration Agreement involves interstate commerce such that the FAA applies.

**Intentions**

* Defendant CitiStaff Solutions Inc. moves to compel arbitration of Plaintiff's claims and dismiss the action, or in the alternative, stay the matter pending arbitration.

**Disputes**

* The dispute turns on whether the Arbitration Agreement is invalid under Labor Code section 432.6.
* Plaintiff also argues that the Arbitration Agreement does not apply to Toro Pallets Inc. and Rodrigo Toro.
