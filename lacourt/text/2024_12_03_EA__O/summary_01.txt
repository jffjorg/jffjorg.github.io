Plaintiff Cynthia Escutia-Cobian brought a petition for writ of mandate, declaratory relief, and injunction against the HOA, alleging that two board members are not resident unit owners. The case has been ongoing since March 9, 2023.

Tentative Ruling:
(1) Motion to Set Aside and Vacate Judgment due to Extrinsic Fraud and Lack of Notice to Petitioner Regarding Continued Trial Date is GRANTED.
(2) Plaintiff's Motion for Attorney Fees is MOOT.

Background:
The Plaintiff alleged that the HOA's bylaws were violated by the election of non-resident unit owners to the board. The case has seen numerous continuances and hearings.

Discussion:
The Plaintiff claimed that she did not attend the trial on September 24, 2024, due to lack of notice. The notice was sent to a wrong address, "Pacomino Drive", instead of her actual address, "Palomino Drive". The court and Defendant made an inadvertent clerical error.

Ruling:
The court finds that the Plaintiff's mistake was excusable and that she did not receive notice of the continued trial date. The court grants the motion to set aside and vacate judgment and finds that the Plaintiff's motion for attorney fees is moot.
