Defendant Healthcare Financial Solutions LLC’s joinder to  Defendants Mary Aviles’ and Healthcare Management Associates, Inc.’s motion to quash is denied as moot.Defendants Mary Aviles (“Aviles”) and Healthcare Management Associates, Inc. (“HMA”) (collectively “Moving Defendants”) moves to quash a subpoena duces tecum served by Plaintiff Allstate Insurance Company (“Allstate”) (“Plaintiff”) on non-party American Financial Investment Services, Inc. (“AFIS”) on June 17, 2024.  (Notice Motion, pg. 2; C.C.P. §1987.1.)  Moving Defendants also request Plaintiff and its counsel of record, Knox Ricksen LLP, jointly and severally, pay Moving Defendants sanctions in the amount of $3,134.65.  (Notice Motion, pg. 2; C.C.P. §§1987.2, 2023.010, 2023.030.)Defendant Healthcare Financial Solutions LLC (“HFS”) (“Joining Defendant”) moves to join Moving Defendants’ motion.  (Notice Joinder, pgs. 2-3.)Motion to QuashLegal StandardC.C.P. §1987.1(a) provides, in pertinent part, as follows: “If a subpoena requires the attendance of a witness . . . at the taking of a deposition, the court, upon motion reasonably made by [a party] . . ., may make an order quashing the subpoena entirely, modifying it, or directing compliance with it upon those terms or conditions as the court shall declare, including protective orders. In addition, the court may make any other order as may be appropriate to protect the person from unreasonable or oppressive demands, including unreasonable violations of the right of privacy of the person.” C.C.P. §1985.3(g) provides, in pertinent part, “[a]ny consumer whose personal records are sought by a subpoena duces tecum and who is a party to the civil action in which this subpoena duces tecum is served may, prior to the date for production, bring a motion under Section 1987.1 to quash or modify the subpoena duces tecum.”  (C.C.P. §1985.3(g).)C.C.P. §2020.410(a) states, “[a] deposition subpoena that commands only the production of business records for copying shall designate the business records to be produced either by specifically describing each individual item or by reasonably particularizing each category of item, and shall specify the form in which any electronically stored information is to be produced, if a particular form is desired.”  (C.C.P. §2020.410(a).)C.C.P. §2020.410(c) states, in part, “[a] deposition subpoena that commands only the production of business records for copying need not be accompanied by an affidavit or declaration showing good cause for the production of the business records designated in it. It shall be directed to the custodian of those records or another person qualified to certify the records.”  (C.C.P. §2020.410(c), emphasis added.) BackgroundOn June 17, 2024, Plaintiff served on AFIS its deposition subpoena for production of business records pertaining to Defendant HFS.  (Decl. of Muller ¶6, Exh. 1.)  On July 5, 2024, AFIS objected to Plaintiff’s subpoena.  (Decl. of Walters ¶5, Exh. A.)  Plaintiff met and conferred with AFIS on August 15, 2024, and the parties agreed to revise the requests.  (Decl. of Walters ¶6, Exh. B.)Moving Defendants filed the instant motion on July 3, 2024.  Joining Defendant filed its joinder on July 24, 2024.  Plaintiff filed its opposition on September 19, 2024.  Moving Defendants filed their reply on September 24, 2024.DiscussionPlaintiff’s revised subpoena to AFIS contains the following requests:All documents that refer to or reference HFS’s business practices, including but not limited to:1. Referral of patients to healthcare providers2. Marketing of HFS and/or New Hope’s services to personal injury attorneys3. Marketing of HFS and/or New Hope’s services to healthcare providers4. HFS’s business model (including but not limited to HFS’s lien purchases, lien compromises, relationships with attorneys, and relationships with healthcare providers or medical facilities).(Decl. of Walters ¶6, Exh. A.)On September 6, 2024, AFIS responded to the subpoena with a declaration of no records.  (Decl. of Walters ¶8, Exh. D.)Moving Defendants’ motion to quash is denied as moot.SanctionsMoving Defendants’ request for sanctions is denied.Plaintiff’s request for reasonable costs in opposing this motion is denied.ConclusionMoving Defendants’ and Joining Defendant’s motion to quash is denied as moot.  Moving Defendants’ request for sanctions is denied.Plaintiff’s request for reasonable costs in opposing this motion is denied.Moving Party to give notice.Dated:  October _____, 2024 ________________________________________Hon.
