RE:DEMURRER  BACKGROUNDA.   AllegationsPlaintiff RRRM Holding Company, LLC (“Plaintiff”) alleges that it is entitled to possession of 7139 Foothill Boulevard, Tujunga, CA 91042 as the owner of the real property.  Plaintiff alleges that on April 27, 2002, Plaintiff entered into a written lease agreement with Defendant Vigan Sardariani (“Defendant”).  Plaintiff alleges that the lease expired and became a month-to-month tenancy effective May 1, 2012.  Plaintiff alleges that since October 1, 2023, rent was due and owing in the amount of $121,000. The complaint, filed December 29, 2023, allege causes of action for: (1) non-payment of rent; and (2) failure to vacate after termination of tenancy.On February 2, 2024, the default of All Tenants, Subtenants, Named Claimants and Other Occupants was entered. B.    Motion on CalendarOn January 16, 2024, Defendant filed a demurrer to the complaint. On February 5, 2024, Plaintiff filed two copies of the opposition brief, one at 10:45 a.m. and the other at 3:25 p.m.  The Court will consider the later-filed opposition brief as the operative opposition papers. On February 9, 2024, Defendant filed a reply brief.DISCUSSION            Defendant demurs to the complaint on the ground that that it is uncertain at paragraphs 2 and 7.             Paragraph 2 alleges that on April 27, 2022, Plaintiff entered into a lease agreement with Defendant.  However, Defendant argues that the attached lease agreement states that the commercial lease for the subject property was entered between “ROLAND MARKARIAN” as landlord and Defendant as tenant.  (Compl., Ex. 3.)  Defendant argues that the complaint fails to allege how Plaintiff is involved with the lease to the subject property and, therefore, Plaintiff has not alleged standing.  In opposition, Plaintiff explains that it became the legal owner of the property in 2016 and stepped into the shoes the predecessor-in-interest.  However, such facts are extrinsic to the allegations of the complaint.  Plaintiff should amend the complaint and allege these additional facts to cure the issues in the pleading.             Paragraph 7 alleges that the sum of $121,000 in rent was due and owing as of October 1, 2023.  Defendant argues that the notice to pay rent or quit attached to the complaint is dated September 28, 2023, meaning that rent for October 2023 could not have been a part of the demand for rent.  The notice is dated September 28, 2023.  (See Compl., Ex. 1.)  In opposition, Plaintiff argues that the date of the notice does not matter because rent was due the 1st of the month and Plaintiff is seeking September 2023 rent.  However, the complaint alleges that it is seeking rent up until October 1, 2023, which would begin a new month of rent that is due.  If Plaintiff is only seeking rental payments up until September 30, 2023 (and not a new rental period starting on October 1, 2023), then Plaintiff should correct and clarify this in the complaint’s allegations.  Seeking rent up until October 2023 in the complaint’s allegations is improper.            For these reasons, the demurrer to the complaint is sustained on the basis of uncertainty.  As this is the first attempt at the pleading, the Court will allow Plaintiff an opportunity to amend.   CONCLUSION AND ORDER            Defendant Vigan Sardariani’s demurrer to the complaint is sustained with 20 days leave to amend.
