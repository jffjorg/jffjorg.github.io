 Case Number:  23BBCV01530    Hearing Date:   March 1, 2024    Dept:  NCB  Superior Court of CaliforniaCounty of Los AngelesNorth Central DistrictDepartment B  JOSHUA PEVNICK,                         Plaintiff,            v. GENERAL MOTORS LLC,                         Defendant.   Case No.:  23BBCV01530 Hearing Date:  March 1, 2024  [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:MOTION TO COMPEL THE DEPOSITION OF DEFENDANT’S PERSON MOST QUALIFIED  BACKGROUNDA.   AllegationsOn July 6, 2023, Plaintiff Joshua Pevnick (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint for restitution and damages for violation of the Song Beverly Consumer Warranty Act against Defendant General Motors, LLC (“GM” or “Defendant”).  Plaintiff alleges that on April 18, 2021, he purchased a 2021 Chevrolet Bolt.  GM is alleged to be the manufacturer and to have offered an express warranty to Plaintiff pursuant the Act.  Plaintiff alleges the vehicle suffered from nonconformities to the warranty including, but not limited to “the seat belt pretensioner, fire risk, electrical, battery, and other defects.”  (Compl., ¶10.)  Plaintiff alleges that GM was unable or refused to conform the vehicle to the applicable express and implied warranties under the Act.  Plaintiff alleges that GM has an obligation to repurchase the vehicle and make restitution. B.    Motion on CalendarOn February 20, 2024, Plaintiff filed a motion to compel the deposition of GM’s person most qualified (“PMQ”) with production of documents.   The Court is not in receipt of an opposition brief.DISCUSSION            According to CCP § 1005(b), all moving and supporting papers shall be filed and served at least 16 court days before the hearing; all opposing papers shall be filed with the court and served at least 9 court days before the hearing; and all reply papers at least 5 court days before the hearing.  Pursuant to CRC, Rule 3.1300(d), the court may in its discretion refuse to consider a late filed paper.  As such, Plaintiff should have filed his motion by latest February 6, 2024, such that the opposition brief would have been due by February 16, 2024, and the reply brief would have been due by February 23, 2024.  Plaintiff filed this motion on February 20, 2024, which is past the deadline to file the motion and, therefore, deprived Defendant the opportunity to file an opposition brief.As such, the Court will continue the hearing on the motion to March 29, 2024.  The opposition brief shall be due by March 18, 2024.  The reply brief shall be due by March 22, 2024. CONCLUSION AND ORDER            Plaintiff Joshua Pevnick’s motion to compel the deposition of Defendant’s person most qualified is continued to March 29, 2024.  The opposition brief shall be due by March 18, 2024.  The reply brief shall be due by March 22, 2024.
