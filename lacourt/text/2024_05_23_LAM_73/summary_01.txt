The Court's tentative ruling denies Defendants' motion to quash Plaintiffs' subpoena to Concord Real Estate Service, Inc. The subpoena seeks documents and communications related to the subject property, a 58-unit apartment building. Plaintiffs allege that Defendants failed to properly manage the property, and that Concord was unfit or incompetent in acting as a property management company. The Court finds that Plaintiffs' requests are not overly broad, and that they are narrow in scope and time. The Court also finds that Plaintiffs' requests are not unduly burdensome, and that Concord would likely be able to search for the requested documents. The Court denies Defendants' motion to quash the subpoena.
