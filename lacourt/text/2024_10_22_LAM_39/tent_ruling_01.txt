Attorney Jeremy Macklin (“Counsel”) moves to
be admitted pro hac vice as counsel
for Plaintiff Admiral Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”).
Pursuant to California Rules of Court, rule
9.40, an attorney who is licensed to practice and in good standing in other
states may, upon court approval, appear as counsel pro hac vice in a
pending case if an active member of the State Bar of California also appears as
counsel of record.  (Cal. Rules of Court,
rule 9.40, subd. (a).)  Counsel is
licensed to practice and is in good standing in Illinois, Texas, and Wisconsin,
and federal courts, resides in Illinois, has not applied to appear pro hac
vice in California in the past two years, and is associated with Plaintiff’s
counsel, who is licensed to practice in California.  Further, Counsel has demonstrated he served
this application on the State Bar of California at its San Francisco office and
paid the $50 fee as required.  (Cal.
Rules of Court, rule 9.40, subds. (c), (e).) 
Accordingly, the Court grants Counsel’s application.
Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of
this order and to file proof of service of same.
