 Defendants Roy Lee and RL Films2, Inc. seek to compel “(1) Plaintiff and Cross-Defendant John P. Middleton to produce all documents responsive to Defendants and Cross-Complainants’ Roy Lee and RL Film2, Inc.’s Requests for Production of Documents to Plaintiff and Cross-Defendant John P. Middleton, Set Six within fifteen days; (2) Plaintiff and Cross-Defendant John Powers Middleton Companies, LLC to produce all documents responsive to Defendants and Cross-Complainants’ Roy Lee and RL FILMS2, Inc.’s Requests for Production of Documents to Plaintiff and Cross-Defendant John Powers Middleton Companies, LLC, Set Three within fifteen days; and (3) to deem all objections to both sets of requests – including objections based on the attorney-client privilege – waived.” On November 6, 2023, the Court entered an order relating to the subject discovery that was made pursuant to stipulation.  The order provides that Plaintiffs must produce all documents as to communications and damages by November 27, 2023.  It also indicates that: “Plaintiffs shall be precluded from introducing as evidence at trial any documents responsive to the Requests that have not been produced to Defendants as of November 27, 2023, with the exception of documents later-obtained through third parties not in Plaintiffs’ control.”Because the parties already stipulated to evidentiary sanctions, it’s not apparent that there is anything to compel.  Plaintiffs shall be precluded from introducing any responsive documents that were in their control at trial.  The Motion is denied.  The Court declines to award monetary sanctions.Moving parties to give notice.If counsel do not submit on the tentative, they are strongly encouraged to appear by LACourtConnect rather than in person due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
