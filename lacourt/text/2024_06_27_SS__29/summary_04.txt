The Judge's tentative ruling grants the petition for approval of the minor's compromise for Elijah Lailson, age 3. The Court excuses the personal appearance of the claimant and the guardian ad litem, allowing counsel to appear by telephone or video conference call. The proposed settlement and fees are found to be fair and reasonable. The petition proposes the creation and funding of a Special Needs Trust (SNT) to receive the settlement funds and avoid loss of benefits eligibility for Medi-Cal, SSI, and other benefits.

The Court finds that the proposed trust instrument meets the requirements for court-created or funded trusts and approves the purchase of an annuity to provide a guaranteed income stream for Elijah Lailson's life. The Court also retains jurisdiction over the Department of Health Care Service's lien claim and grants authority for the trustee to invest in proprietary funds, mutual funds, and bonds with maturity dates greater than five years.

The Court makes the required findings for the SNT, including that Elijah Lailson has a disability that substantially impairs his ability to provide for his own care, that he is likely to have special needs that will not be met without the trust, and that the funds paid to the trust do not exceed the amount reasonably necessary to meet his special needs. The Court sets an OSC re Purchase of Annuity for a future date and requests that the Judicial Assistant complete the proposed orders.
