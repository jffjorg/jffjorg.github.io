 Case Number:  22STCV30815    Hearing Date:   November 14, 2024    Dept:  28 Having
considered the moving papers, the Court rules as follows. 
BACKGROUND 
On September 21, 2022, Plaintiff George Leong
(“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Xie Zhijing, Uber
Technologies, Inc. (“Uber”), and Does 1-50 for negligence and motor vehicle
tort. 
On February 15, 2023, Uber filed an answer. 
On September 26, 2023, the Court granted Plaintiff’s
counsel’s motion to be relieved as counsel. 
On June 7, 2024, the Court granted Uber’s unopposed
motion to deem admitted matters specified in requests for admission, set one,
served on Plaintiff, and granted Uber’s request for sanctions. 
On August 6, 2024, the
Court granted Uber’s unopposed motion for judgment on the pleadings with 30
days leave to amend. 
On September 17, 2024, Uber filed a motion for entry
of judgment.  The motion was set for
hearing on November 14, 2024.  Plaintiff
has not filed an opposition. 
Trial is currently scheduled for December 4, 2024. 
PARTY’S REQUEST 
Uber asks the Court to enter judgment against Plaintiff
on his complaint. 
LEGAL STANDARD 
Code
of Civil Procedure section 438, subdivision (i)(1)(B), provides: 
“(i) (1) Where a motion for judgment on the pleadings
is granted with leave to amend, the court shall not enter a judgment in favor
of a party until the following proceedings are had:  * * *
“(B) If no amended pleading is filed, then the party
shall move for entry of judgment in its favor. . . .” 
(Code Civ. Proc., § 438,
subdivision (i)(1)(B).) 
DISCUSSION 
On
August 6, 2024, the Court granted Uber’s unopposed motion for judgment on the
pleadings with 30 days leave to amend. 
Plaintiff has not filed an amended complaint. 
Uber
now moves for entry of judgment in its favor under Code of Civil Procedure
section 438, subdivision (i)(1)(B). 
Plaintiff has not filed an opposition. 
The Court grants the motion. 
CONCLUSION 
The
Court GRANTS Defendant Uber Technologies, Inc.’s motion for entry of judgment
under Code of Civil Procedure section 438, subdivision (i)(1)(B).  Defendant Uber Technologies, Inc. is to
submit a proposed judgment. 
Moving
party is ordered to give notice of this ruling. 
Moving
party is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court
within five days.
