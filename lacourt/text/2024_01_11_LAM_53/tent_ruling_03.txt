MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant iColor Printing and
Mailing, Inc.  
 
RESPONDING PARTY:       Plaintiff Blanca Diaz De Leon
Motion to Strike Punitive Damages Allegations from Complaint
The court
considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with
this motion. 
DISCUSSION
Defendant iColor Printing & Mailing, Inc. (“Defendant”) moves the
court for an order striking from the Complaint, filed by plaintiff Blanca Diaz
De Leon (“Plaintiff”) on September 12, 2023, the prayer for punitive damages
and the related allegations set forth in paragraphs 28, 39, 51, 57, 67, 75, 83,
and 95.
The court grants Defendant’s motion to strike the prayer for punitive
damages and related allegations because Plaintiff has not alleged facts
establishing that (1) Defendant is guilty of oppression, fraud, or malice, and
(2) the advance knowledge and conscious disregard, authorization, ratification
or act of oppression, fraud, or malice was on the part of an officer, director,
or managing agent for Defendant.  (Civ.
Code, § 3294, subds. (a), (b).) 
ORDER
            The court grants defendant iColor
Printing & Mailing, Inc.’s motion to strike punitive damages allegations
from complaint. 
            The court grants plaintiff Blanca
Diaz De Leon 20 days leave to file a First Amended Complaint that cures the
deficiencies with her requests for punitive damages. 
            The court orders defendant iColor
Printing & Mailing, Inc. to give notice of this ruling.
IT IS SO ORDERED.
