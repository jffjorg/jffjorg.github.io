The judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case between Paul Sukhoon Oh (plaintiff) and Volkswagen Group of America, Inc. and LAD-AU, LLC (defendants). The plaintiff had filed numerous discovery motions, including requests for admission, requests for production, form interrogatories, and special interrogatories, and had sent meet and confer letters to the defendants. The defendants failed to provide timely responses, and the plaintiff filed motions to compel and deem matters admitted.

The judge grants the plaintiff's motions to compel and deem matters admitted, requiring the defendants to provide responses to the discovery requests. The judge also orders that the genuineness of each request for admission be admitted with respect to each defendant. In addition, the judge awards sanctions against the defendants for failing to provide timely responses. Specifically, the judge orders $642.50 in sanctions against each defendant (Volkswagen and LAD) and $2,455 in sanctions against LAD for unsuccessfully opposing the motions to compel. The total sanctions awarded to the plaintiff are $3,097.50. The judge orders that the sanctions be paid within 30 days.
