  Case Number:  24VECV03310    Hearing Date:   November 13, 2024    Dept:  T 24VECV03310  ALLY BANK V ORTIZ Request for writ of possession: Granted.   No bond.  Writ of attachment will  issue.Discussion
            Plaintiff’s proofs of service showed
that Defendants were properly sub-served with the summons and complaint and
properly notified of the hearing on the application.  (Code Civ. Proc. sec. 512.010.)  Plaintiff
provided a declaration to show the probable validity of their claim and right
to repossess the vehicle from Defendants because Plaintiff provided evidence
showing: Defendants’ entry into the Retail Installment Sales Contract (RISC)
for the vehicle; Plaintiff assignor’s performance of delivery of the vehicle;
Defendants’ breach in failing to pay; and Plaintiff’s damages.  (Code Civ. Proc sec. 512.040.)  (James Singleton Decl. pars. 5-6.)  Plaintiff further provided that the vehicle
is in the custody and control of Defendants and concealing the vehicle.  (Singleton Decl. par. 8.)  (Code Civ. Proc. sec. 512.060.)  With Defendants’ breach, Defendants no longer
has rights to possess and further refuse to surrender the vehicle.  Plaintiff has shown their rights to
possession.    Plaintiff
submitted the amount owed on the account and the value of the vehicle.  (Singleton Decl. pars. 6-7.)  The evidence showed that the account balance
exceeds the value of the vehicle and no equity exists for the Defendant.  The Court finds that the requirement to post
an undertaking is waived.  (Code Civ.
Proc. sec. 515.010.)  







            Plaintiff’s application for writ of
possession is GRANTED.
