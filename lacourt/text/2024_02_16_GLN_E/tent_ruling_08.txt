RELIEF REQUESTED:
 
Defendant Calstar Motor, Inc.
demurs to Plaintiff’s entire complaint on the grounds that:
 
(1)  
The first, second, third, fourth, fifth, and sixth
causes of action fail to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action
against Defendant. 
 
BACKGROUND:
 
On October 6, 2023, Plaintiff Gagik
Grigoryan filed a Complaint against Defendant Calstar Motors, Inc. and DOES 1
through 30, inclusive. The Complaint alleged causes of action for (1) Fraud and
Deceit; (2) Breach of Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing; (3) Selling
Automobile Above Advertised Price; (4) Violation of Business & Professions
Code § 17200, et seq; (5) Violation of Business & Professions Code § 17500,
et seq; and (6) Violation of California Consumer Legal Remedies Act. 
 
On December 20, 2023, Defendant
Calstar Motor, Inc. filed the instant Demurrer with Motion to Strike. The
demurrer and motion to strike are unopposed. 
 
On February 5, 2024, Plaintiff
filed the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) alleging the same causes of
action. Provided that Plaintiff filed the operative FAC, Defendant Calstar
Motor, Inc.’s Demurrer with Motion to Strike are rendered moot. 
 
Accordingly, Defendant Calstar
Motor, Inc.’s Demurrer with Motion to Strike are DENIED as MOOT.
