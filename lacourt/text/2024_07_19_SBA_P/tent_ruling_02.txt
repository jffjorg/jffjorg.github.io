 Case Number:  23TRCV04064    Hearing Date:   July 19, 2024    Dept:  P Having considered plaintiff’s default judgment application and associated exhibits, the court rules as follows: the default judgment application is denied without prejudice.I. PROCEDURAL BACKGROUNDOn December 5, 2023, Plaintiff Umpqua Bank (“Plaintiff”) filed a Complaint against Defendants Simon Park Holdings (“SPH”) and Simon Park (“Park”) (collectively, the “Defendants”), alleging causes of action for: (1) Possession of Personal Property; (2) Deficiency Judgment; (3) Foreclosure of Security Interest with Deficiency Judgment; (4) Breach of Express Contract; (5) Money Lent; (6) Account Stated; and (7) Conversion.On January 9, 2024, Defendants SPH and Park were both served with the summons and complaint.On February 22, 2024, default was entered against Defendants.On April 16, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant default judgment application.II. FACTUAL BACKGROUNDPlaintiff alleges that Defendants defaulted on a U.S. Small Business Administration Note (the “Note”) and Unconditional Guarantee (the “Guarantee”). (Complaint, ¶¶ 5-6.) Plaintiff alleges that due to such default, Defendants are indebted to Plaintiff in the principal sum of $699,438.83, plus interest, late fees, site visit fees, and costs. (Complaint, ¶ 27.)Plaintiff now moves for default judgment against Defendants in the sum of $785,006.72.III. LEGAL STANDARDCalifornia Rules of Court, Rule 3.1800 sets forth the requirements for default judgments. In pertinent part, the rule dictates that a party must use form CIV-100 and file the following documents with the clerk: (1) except in unlawful detainer cases, a brief summary of the case identifying the parties and the nature of plaintiff's claim; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3)interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5)a declaration of nonmilitary status for each defendant against whom judgment is sought; (6) a proposed form of judgment; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment against specified parties under Code of Civil Procedure section 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8)exhibits as necessary; and (9)a request for attorney fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1800(a)(1)-(9).)IV. DISCUSSIONThe Court finds that Plaintiff has failed to meet the requirements of California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1800 to obtain entry of default judgment against Defendants. Plaintiff has failed to: (1) provide adequate interest computations. Additionally, although requesting attorneys’ fees pursuant to a contract (Palmer Decl., ¶ 18), Plaintiff has failed to cite to “the precise clause providing for fees.” (Los Angeles Sup. Ct., Court Rules, Rule 3.207(b).)V. CONCLUSIONBased on the foregoing, Plaintiff’s default judgment application is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE.Plaintiff to give notice.The default judgment application filed by Plaintiff Umpqua Bank on 04/16/2024 is denied without prejudice.
