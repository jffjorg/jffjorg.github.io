**Case Information**

* Case Number: 23STCV30066
* Hearing Date: February 28, 2024
* Department: 40 Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles
* Parties:
	+ Plaintiff: Careismatic Brands, LLC (formerly Strategic Partners, Inc.)
	+ Defendants: Michelman & Robinson, LLP, Sanford L. Michelman, and Does 1-100

**Motion to Compel Arbitration**

* Motion: Michelman & Robinson, LLP, and Sanford Michelman's Motion to Compel Arbitration
* Ruling: GRANTED
* Reasons: The arbitration agreement in the Engagement Agreements between Careismatic Brands and Michelman & Robinson, LLP is valid and applicable to the claims alleged against Michelman & Robinson, LLP and Sanford Michelman.

**Facts**

* Careismatic Brands sued Michelman & Robinson, LLP, Sanford Michelman, and Does 1-100 for breach of fiduciary duty, breach of written contract, and declaratory relief.
* The claims arise from allegations that Michelman & Robinson, LLP breached its fiduciary and contractual obligations owed to Careismatic Brands during the representation of Careismatic Brands in the FIGS case.

**Disputes**

* The dispute is whether the arbitration agreement in the Engagement Agreements between Careismatic Brands and Michelman & Robinson, LLP is valid and applicable to the claims alleged against Michelman & Robinson, LLP and Sanford Michelman.

**Acts**

* Careismatic Brands engaged Michelman & Robinson, LLP to represent it in the FIGS case.
* Michelman & Robinson, LLP charged Careismatic Brands fees for its services, which were allegedly excessive and in breach of contract.

**Intentions**

* Michelman & Robinson, LLP intended to represent Careismatic Brands in the FIGS case and to charge it fees for its services.
* Careismatic Brands intended to hold Michelman & Robinson, LLP and Sanford Michelman responsible for any breaches of fiduciary duty or contract.

**Laws**

* California Code of Civil Procedure, sections 1281.2 and 1281.4
* California Rules of Court, rule 3.1330

**Significant Statements**

* "A party to an arbitration agreement may seek a court order compelling the parties to arbitrate a dispute covered by the agreement." (Code Civ. Proc., ยง 1281.2)
* "The court shall order a matter to arbitration if it determines that there is an agreement to arbitrate and (1) the agreement has not been waived or (2) the agreement has not been revoked." (Code Civ. Proc., ยง 1281.2)
* "The strong public policy in favor of arbitration does not override ordinary principles of contract interpretation holding that the contractual terms themselves must be carefully examined before the parties to the contract can be ordered to arbitration." (Rice v. Downs (2016) 248 Cal.App.4th 175, 185)

**Order**

* The Court GRANTS the motion to compel arbitration of the claims against Michelman & Robinson, LLP and Sanford Michelman.
* The action is STAYED pending the outcome of the arbitration between the parties or until such earlier time as the Court specifies.
