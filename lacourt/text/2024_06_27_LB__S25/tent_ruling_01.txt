  Case Number:  23LBCV00315    Hearing Date:   June 27, 2024    Dept:  S25 Factual BackgroundOn February 21, 2023, Plaintiff Victor M. Morales (“Plaintiff”) filed this lemon law action against Defendant Ford Motor Co. (“Defendant”) for violations of the Song Beverly Act. Plaintiff alleges that he purchased a 2020 Ford F-150 (“Subject Vehicle”) that contained and developed defects such as engine, electrical, emission, and transmission system defects. (Compl., ¶¶ 8-10, 18; Altman Decl., ¶¶ 6-9.) Plaintiff alleges that presenting the Subject Vehicle to Defendant’s authorized repair facility on numerous instances to repair the defects, but none of the defects were permanently repaired. (Compl., 18, 22-24, 37; Altman Decl., ¶¶ 8-9.)Procedural HistoryOn August 18, 2023, Plaintiff served a notice of deposition of Defendant’s PMK and request for production of documents for the deposition to take place on September 7, 2023. (Altman Decl., ¶ 15, Exh. 2; Lee Decl., ¶ 3, Exh. B.) On September 1, 2023, Defendant served objections to Plaintiff’s Deposition notice and indicated that witnesses would be produced as to some but not all of Plaintiff’s “Matter of Examination.” (Altman Decl., ¶ 16, Exh. 3; Lee Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. C.) On September 28, 2023, Defendant offered its witness availability for December 7, 2023, and requested Plaintiff served an amended notice, but received no response. (Lee Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. E.) Subsequently, on October 24, 2023, Defendant contacted Plaintiff again regarding its witness’ availability on December 7, 2023, but received no response. (Lee Decl., ¶ 7, Exh. F.) On November 30, 2023, Defendant notified plaintiff that is witness was no longer available on December 7, 2023, but would be available on January 31, 2024. No response was received from Plaintiff. (Lee Decl., ¶ 9, Exh. H.) On December 21, 2023, Defendant notified Plaintiff if no amended notice for the deposition was received by January 2, 2024, Defendant would release their witness’ availability. No response was received to this request. (Lee Decl., ¶ 10, Exh. I.) On April 15, 2024, Plaintiff sent an amended notice for Defendant’s PMK for a deposition on April 29, 2024. (Altman Decl., ¶ 17, Exh. 4; Lee Decl., ¶ 14, Exh. K.) On April 24, 2024,Defendant responded to the amended notice with objections and an indicated no produce a deponent. (Altman Decl., ¶ 18, Exh. 5; Lee Decl., ¶ 15, Exh. L.)Judicial NoticeDefendant requests judicial notice of the following exhibits:· Exhibit 1: The Los Angeles Superior Court’s Order on Ford’s Ex Parte Application to Compel Plaintiff’s Deposition, dated February 19, 2024, in Victor Morales v. Ford Motor Company, Case No. 23GDCV00315· Exhibit 2: The Los Angeles Superior Court’s Order on Ford’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff’s Deposition, dated May 9, 2024, in Victor Morales v. Ford Motor Company, Case No. 23GDCV00315.Defendant’s request is GRANTED pursuant to Evidence Code, § 452, subds. (d) & (h).Meet and ConferPlaintiff sent a letter to Defendant on April 15, 2024, regarding Defendant’s objections to the notice of deposition and Defendant’s opposition to producing its PMK. (Altman Decl., ¶ 21, Exh. 7.) In response, Defendant stated discovery was closed. (Ibid.) On May 9, 2024, Plaintiff requested Defendant produce its PMK for deposition; Defendant again indicated that the discovery window was closed, thereby, requiring Plaintiff to file the instant motion. (Altman Decl., ¶ 22, Exh. 8.) Plaintiff believes Defendant has not participated in meet and confer attempts in regard to the PMK deposition and its request for production of documents. (Altman Decl., ¶ 24.) Defendant contests Plaintiff’s declaration, asserting that Defendant reached out to Plaintiff at least four times on September 28, 2023, October 24, 2023, November 30, 2023 and December 21, 2023 to schedule the PMK deposition, but received no response from Plaintiff each time. (Lee Decl., ¶¶ 6-10, Exhs. E, F, H, I.) The Court finds both sides have fulfilled the meet and confer requirements.RulingAs set forth in the February 16, 2024 Minute Order, all discovery cut off dates remained based on the March 4 2024 trial date. (February 16, 2024 Minute Order; Defendant’s Request for Judicial Notice (“RJN”), Exh. 1.) Accordingly, fact discovery window closed on February 5, 2024. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2024.020, subd. (a).) Plaintiff served an amended notice of deposition of Defendant’s PMK on April 15, 2024 and filed the instant motion on May 16, 2024. The Court finds Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel as untimely as there was no extension of any discovery deadlines. While the Court has discretion to grant leave to reopen discovery under Code Civ. Proc., § 2024.050, the Court notes that Plaintiff has not submitted a response to Defendant’s Opposition as to why Plaintiff failed to respond to Defendant’s multiple attempts, over several months, to comply with the deposition request. Accordingly, the motion is DENIED
