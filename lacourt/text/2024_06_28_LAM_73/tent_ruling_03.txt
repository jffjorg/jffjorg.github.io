I. BACKGROUND
 
On November
29, 2023, Plaintiff Cathay Bank (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against Defendants
Dreamfit USA Inc. (“Dreamfit”) and Gloria Chongae Won (“Won”) alleging causes
of action for:
 
            C/A 1: Breach of Written Promissory
Note and Business Loan Agreement
            C/A 2: Breach of Written Guaranty
            C/A 3: Money Lent
            C/A 4: Account Stated
            C/A 5: Indebtedness 
 
Plaintiff alleges that on April 14, 2022, Dreamfit
executed and delivered to Plaintiff a written Promissory Note and Business Loan
Agreement pursuant to which Dreamfit sought the use of Plaintiff’s credit
facilities, including a loan in the amount of $50,000.00. Plaintiff alleges
that Defendants have failed to pay the entire balance due under the Note when
it matured and was due in full on May 1, 2023. 
 
On May 31, 2024, default was entered against Defendant.  
 
On
May 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed an OSC re: Default Judgment. Plaintiff seeks judgment against Defendants
in the total amount of $63,143.95. 
 
II.ANALYSIS
 
A.     Legal
Standard
 
CCP § 585 permits entry of a judgment after a Defendant has
failed to timely answer after being properly served.  A party seeking
judgment on the default by the Court must file a Request for Court Judgment,
and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible
evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as
necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) a proposed form of
judgment; (6) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought;
(7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an
application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of
grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for
attorneys’ fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. 
(CRC Rule 3.1800.)

Here,
Plaintiff has complied with all requirements for a default judgment.  
 
Plaintiff
submitted the declaration of Jose Ramirez, the Assistant
Vice President, Special Assets Department of Cathay Bank. (Ramirez Decl., ¶ 1.) Ramirez provided a summary of the case, attached the Promissory
Note, Business Loan Agreement, Personal Guaranty and Statement of Account
showing the payments due and provided a computation of interest. Ramirez states
that Defendants’ outstanding balance of principal and interest totaled
$60,674.95, including the principal sum of $50,000, and total accrued interest
in the sum of $10,606.74, late charges in the amount of $43.21 and NSF fees in
the amount of $25.00. (Id., ¶ 12.)
 
Plaintiff
also submitted the declaration of its counsel, Irwin M. Wittlin, who provided a
computation of attorneys fees calculated according to Los Angeles
County Superior Court Rule 3.214(a). (Wittlin Decl., ¶
2.)
 
Since
Plaintiff has complied with the requirements, Plaintiff’s request for default
judgment is GRANTED.
 
III. CONCLUSION 
 
Plaintiff’s
request for default judgment is GRANTED.
