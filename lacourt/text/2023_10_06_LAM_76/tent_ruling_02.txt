   [1]  Evidence of a writing made as a record of an act,  condition, or event is not made inadmissible by the hearsay rule when offered to  prove the act, condition, or event if:   (a)    The writing was made in the regular  course of a business;  (b)    The writing was made at or near  the time of the act, condition, or event; (c)    The custodian or other qualified  witness testifies to its identity and the mode of its preparation; and (d) The sources of information and method  and time of preparation were such as to indicate its trustworthiness.      (Evid.  Code § 1271.)
