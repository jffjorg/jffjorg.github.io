  Case Number:  23STCV03132    Hearing Date:   May 2, 2024    Dept:  17 Superior  Court of California County  of Los Angeles   DEPARTMENT 17   TENTATIVE RULING      JAMES    BOYD, by and through SERENA JEFFRIES, as Personal Representative of the    Estate of JAMES BOYD, et al.                                       vs.   PACIFICA    SENIOR LIVING SANTA CLARITA., et al.        Case No.:     23STCV03132          Hearing    Date:  May 2, 2024                  Plaintiff’s  motion to compel Defendant’s PMK’s deposition is GRANTED, as is their request  that the deposition take place within 10 days of entry of this order. Defendant  is sanctioned, jointly and severally with counsel, $1,750, payable with 30 days.               On  2/14/2023, Plaintiff James Boyd,  by and through Serena Jeffries, as Personal Representative of the Estate of  James Boyd and Serena Jeffries, as Personal Representative of the Estate of  James Boyd filed a petition against Pacifica Senior Living Santa Clarita, W  Lyons LLC dba Pacifica Senior Living Santa Clarita, West Lyons LP dba Pacifica  Senior Living Santa Clarita, Pacifica Senior Living Management, LLC, alleging:  (1) elder abuse/neglect; (2) negligence/willful misconduct; and (3) wrongful  death.                 On 2/1/2024, Plaintiff James  Boyd, by and through Serena  Jeffries, as Personal Representative of the Estate of James Boyd (Plaintiff)  moved for an order to compel the deposition of Defendant W Lyons LLC dba Pacifica Senior  Living Santa Clarita’s (Defendant) Person(s) Most Knowledge (PMK).                The  motion is unopposed.    Legal Standard    If, after  service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director,  managing agent, employee or person designated by an organization that is a  party…without having served a valid objection under section 2025.410 [regarding  defects in deposition notice], fails to appear for examination, or to proceed  with it…the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the  deponent's attendance and testimony...” C.C.P. § 2025.450(a).   Discussion               On  12/18/2023, Plaintiff served Defendant with a Notice of Deposition of  Defendant’s Person Most Knowledgeable (PMK) and Requests for Production. The  deposition was noticed for January 12, 2024.   On 1/5/2024,  Defendant served objections to the PMK deposition notice, contending that the  deposition was unilaterally noticed.    On 1/8/2023,  Plaintiff’s counsel addressed the objection with Defense counsel, writing:    Preliminarily,  to address the general objection re deposition scheduling, we invite you and  your client to provide this office with at least five (5) alternative dates for  the deposition to occur no later than 1/31/24. Please provide the alternative  deposition dates by 1/15/24. If you have any questions or concerns, please let  me know. Thank you and I look forward to hearing back from you in this regard.               (Vitug  Decl., Exh 2.)                Then  on 1/9/2024, the parties met and conferred. Plaintiff again requested that  deposition dates be provided by 1/15/2024. (Vitug Decl., Exhibit 3.)                To  date, Defendant’s counsel still has not provided available dates, despite that  trial is set for August 2024.                Defendant  did not oppose this motion and not set forth any substantial justification for  its conduct.               Plaintiff’s  motion to compel Defendant’s PMK’s deposition is granted, as is their request  that the deposition take place within 10 days of entry of this order. Defendant  is sanctioned, jointly and severally with counsel, $1,750.00 ($350/hr x 5.)       It is so ordered.    Dated:  May     , 2024                                                                                                                                                                Hon.
