And here is a summary of the Judge's Tentative Ruling in English text:

The Judge has issued a tentative ruling on the case Smith v. Yankee Hotel, et al. The case involves allegations that the Defendants failed to properly repair and maintain the Plaintiff's residential unit, which was uninhabitable due to mold growth and other issues. The Defendant has filed a demurrer to the fifth cause of action, alleging that the Plaintiff has not alleged sufficient facts to support a claim for intentional infliction of emotional distress. The Judge has sustained the demurrer, finding that the Plaintiff has not alleged conduct that is "so extreme as to exceed all bounds of that usually tolerated in a civilized community."

Additionally, the Defendant has moved to strike certain portions of the complaint, including allegations that the Defendant's conduct was oppressive, fraudulent, or malicious. The Judge has granted the motion, finding that the Plaintiff has not met their burden of alleging sufficient facts to support a claim for punitive damages.

The Judge will inquire at the hearing whether leave to amend should be granted for the fifth cause of action.
