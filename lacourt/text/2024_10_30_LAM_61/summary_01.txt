And here is a summary of the Tentative Ruling in English text:

The Court has granted Defendant JM Eagle Manufacturing Company's motion to compel arbitration of Plaintiff Hossein Fathi's claims for employment discrimination and retaliation. The Court found that the arbitration agreement contained in the employment contract is procedurally unconscionable due to its presentation to the employee as a condition of employment. However, the Court also found that one provision of the agreement is substantively unconscionable, allowing the employer to seek equitable relief without establishing the essential elements for issuance of an injunction. The Court severed this provision from the agreement and ordered the parties to appear at a status conference regarding arbitration in 180 days. The action is stayed pending arbitration, and the Defendant is ordered to provide notice.
