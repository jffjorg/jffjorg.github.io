 Case Number:  22STCV24704    Hearing Date:   December 30, 2024    Dept:  28 Having  considered the moving papers, the Court rules as follows.  BACKGROUND  On  August 1, 2022, Plaintiffs Kamran Atarzadeh (“Atarzadeh”) and Susan Jalili  (“Jalili”) filed this action against Defendants Gabriel Michael Frischer  (“Frischer”) and Does 1-25 for motor vehicle tort and general negligence.  On  September 2, 2022, Frischer filed an answer.  On  November 10, 2022, Plaintiffs amended the complaint to include Defendant Edward  Joaquin Ortega as Doe 1.  On  December 21, 2022, Plaintiff amended the complaint to include Defendant Eduardo  Joaquin Ortega as Doe 2.  On August  8, 2024, the Court granted Ali R. Moghaddami, Esq.’s motion to be relieved as  counsel for Susan Jalili.  On  August 13, 2024, the Court dismissed Atarzadeh’s complaint without prejudice at  Atarzadeh’s request.  On  November 15, 2024, Frischer filed a motion to deem admitted the truth of matters  specified in requests for admission, set one, served on Jalili and for  sanctions.  The motion was set for  hearing on December 30, 2024.  Jalili has  not filed an opposition.  Trial  is currently scheduled for January 30, 2025.  PARTY’S REQUESTS  Frischer  asks the Court to deem admitted the truth of matters specified in requests for  admission, set one, served on Jalili and to impose sanctions on Jalili.  LEGAL STANDARD  Code  of Civil Procedure section 2033.280 provides:  “If a party to whom requests for admission are  directed fails to serve a timely response, the following rules apply:  “(a) The party to whom the requests for admission are  directed waives any objection to the requests, including one based on privilege  or on the protection for work product under Chapter 4 (commencing with Section  2018.010). The court, on motion, may relieve that party from this waiver on its  determination that both of the following conditions are satisfied:  “(1) The party has subsequently served a response that  is in substantial compliance with Sections 2033.210, 2033.220, and 2033.230.  “(2) The party’s failure to serve a timely response  was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect.  “(b) The requesting party may move for an order that  the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the  requests be deemed admitted, as well as for a monetary sanction under Chapter 7  (commencing with Section 2023.010).  “(c) The court shall make this order, unless it finds  that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has  served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests  for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220. It is  mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing  with Section 2023.010) on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to  serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion.”  (Code  Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.)   DISCUSSION  On August  15, 2024, Frischer served requests for admission,  set one, on Jalili.  The requests asked Jalili  to admit the following:  “1. Defendant Gabriel Michael Frischer was not  negligent in the INCIDENT. “2. Defendant Gabriel Michael Frischer was not at  fault for the INCIDENT. “3. You felt 1 (one) impact in the INCIDENT. “4. Edward Joaquin Ortega was negligent in the  INCIDENT. “5. Edward Joaquin Ortega was fully at fault for the  INCIDENT. “6. You were not injured in the INCIDENT. “7. You did not sustain any property damage as a  result of the INCIDENT. “8. You did not receive any TREATMENT as a result of  the INCIDENT. “9. You will not need any future TREATMENT as a result  of the INCIDENT. “10. You did not lose any income as a result of the  INCIDENT. “11. You will not lose any income in the future as a  result of the INCIDENT. “12. You did not suffer any loss of earning capacity  as a result of the INCIDENT.” Jalili did not serve timely responses to the requests for admission and had not served responses by the time Frischer filed this motion.  The Court grants Frischer’s motion and deems admitted the truth of the matters specified in Frischer’s requests for admission.Frischer asks the Court to impose $455.34 in monetary sanctions on Jalili based on two  hours of attorney time at a rate of $160.17 per hour, one hour of legal  assistant time at a rate of $75.00 per hour, and one $60.00 filing fee.  Counsel anticipated spending two hours to  meet and confer, review the motion and declaration, and appear at the  hearing.  Sanctions  are mandatory under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (c).  The Court awards Frischer $380.34 based on two  hours of attorney time and one filing fee.  CONCLUSION  The Court GRANTS Defendant Gabriel Michael Frischer’s motion to deem admitted the truth of the matters  specified in requests for admission, set one, served on Plaintiff  Susan Jalili.  The Court GRANTS Defendant  Gabriel Michael Frischer’s request for sanctions and orders Plaintiff Susan Jalili  to pay Defendant Gabriel Michael Frischer $380.34 by January 29, 2025.  Moving  party is ordered to give notice of this ruling.  Moving  party is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court  within five days.
