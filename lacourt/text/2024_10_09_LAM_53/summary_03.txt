And here is a brief summary of the judge's tentative ruling:

The court grants in part defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation's motion to compel the mental examination of plaintiff Tammy Davis. The court orders that Dr. Marc A. Cohen conduct the mental examination at 333 S. Beverly Drive, Suite 200, Beverly Hills, California, on a date to be determined, to begin at 10:00 a.m. and to last no more than eight hours, excluding breaks. The examiner may examine the plaintiff regarding her psychiatric and mental health treatment history, substance use, criminal history, and medical history and current medications, and may conduct certain psychological tests or assessments. The court orders that the examiner may not examine the plaintiff regarding her sexual history and sexual conduct with anyone other than defendant Tony Rositas, her family medical history, the specific terms of any prior settlement between the plaintiff and defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation, and attorney-client communications with her counsel. The court also orders that no party may record the mental examination by video recording, and that defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation shall make available to counsel for the plaintiff any audio recordings of the mental examination. The court denies all requests for monetary sanctions.
