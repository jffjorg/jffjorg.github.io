The court has issued a tentative ruling in the case of George Nicklas et al. v. Goodrich Valley LLC and Sky Properties, Inc. The court denied the motion to compel arbitration filed by Defendant Sky Properties, Inc. The court found that the arbitration agreement is procedurally unconscionable because Plaintiffs did not have equal bargaining power and the agreement was buried in a 35-page document. The court also found that the arbitration agreement is substantively unconscionable because it does not provide for a neutral arbitrator and does not guarantee access to more than minimal discovery and other types of relief. Finally, the court found that the arbitration agreement is contrary to public policy because it waives Plaintiffs' right to a jury trial in a breach of warranty of habitability case.
