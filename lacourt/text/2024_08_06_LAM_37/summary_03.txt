**Motion to Quash**

* Defendant Bott moves to quash service of summons due to lack of personal jurisdiction
* Plaintiff opposes the motion

**Legal Standard**

* CCP ยง 418.10(a) states: "A defendant, on or before the last day of his or her time to plead or within any further time that the court may for good cause allow, may serve or file a motion...to quash service of summons on the ground of lack of jurisdiction of the court over him or her."
* Plaintiff has the initial burden of demonstrating facts justifying the exercise of jurisdiction

**Evidentiary Objections**

* Defendant Bott submitted evidentiary objections to the Declaration of Richard R. Farcus
* Objections Nos. 1, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, and 16 were overruled
* Objections Nos. 2, 3, 5, 10, 11, 12, 13, 14, and 15 were sustained

**Discussion**

* Defendant Bott asserts he has not lived in California for over 12 years, has no bank accounts, pays taxes, owns real property, or works for a company in California
* Plaintiff offers only the declaration of his attorney, Richard D. Farkas, which includes evidence of Defendant Bott's involvement in another case in Utah
* Plaintiff's reliance on allegations made in the complaint of another action is insufficient to establish personal jurisdiction

**Conclusion**

* Defendant Bott's motion to quash is granted
* Plaintiff's request for sanctions is denied
* Defendant to give notice

**Significant Statements**

* "Courts consider the relationship among the defendant, the forum, and the litigation." (Snowey v. Harrah's Entertainment, Inc. (2005) 35 Cal.4th 1054, 1062)
* "Each party's 'contacts with the forum state must be assessed individually.' " (In re Boon Global Limited (9th Cir. 2019) 923 F.3d 643, 651)
* "The Complaint fails to allege what information led Plaintiff to believe that Bott is an authorized agent or representative or owner or operator of Defendant Traders."
