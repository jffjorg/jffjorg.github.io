


[1] “[A]ll
material facts must be set forth in the separate statement. ‘This is the Golden
Rule of Summary Adjudication: if it is not set forth in the separate statement,
it does not exist. Both the court and the opposing party are entitled to
have all the facts upon which the moving party bases its motion plainly set
forth in the separate statement.’” (United Community Church v. Garcin
(1991) 231 Cal.App.3d 327, 337 [emphasis in original, citation omitted]
[superseded by statute on other grounds] [“it is no answer to say the facts set
out in the supporting evidence or memoranda of points and authorities are
sufficient”].)
