Here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The judge has issued a tentative ruling on the motion for attorney fees filed by Wilmington Trust, N.A. as Trustee for Structured Asset Mortgage Investments II Trust 2007-AR7 Mortgage Pass-Through Certificates 2007-AR7 (Wilmington Trust). The motion was filed in response to the foreclosure of residential real property commonly known as 2651 Aberdeen Avenue, Los Angeles, California 90027 (the Property).

The judge grants the motion for attorney fees, finding that Wilmington Trust is the prevailing party in the action and is entitled to recover its attorneys' fees. The judge awards Wilmington Trust a total of $73,818.50 in attorney fees, which includes the original amount of $68,130.50 claimed by Wilmington Trust, plus an additional $5,688.50 for reduced hours related to the motion.

The judge finds that the Deed of Trust and Note include provisions authorizing attorney fees in case of default, and that Wilmington Trust is entitled to recover its attorneys' fees as the prevailing party in the action. The judge also finds that the Plaintiff's contention that the action arose in tort, not breach of contract, is without merit.

The judge notes that the Plaintiff failed to provide any meaningful opposition to the motion for attorney fees and that the requested amount of $79,625.00 is excessive. The judge reduces the hours related to the motion and awards Wilmington Trust a total of $73,818.50 in attorney fees.
