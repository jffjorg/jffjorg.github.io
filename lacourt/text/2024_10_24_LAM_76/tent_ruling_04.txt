 Case Number:  24STCV00676    Hearing Date:   October 24, 2024    Dept:  76             Plaintiff alleges that she was
accidentally hit by an employee while in Defendant’s store due to unsafe
flooring.
Defendant Trader Joe’s Company
moves to strike portions of the First Amended Complaint.
TENTATIVE RULING
Defendant Trader Joe’s Company’s motion to strike is GRANTED without
leave to amend as to “5. Awarding plaintiff’s attorneys fees” (FAC, 7:20.); and
“6. For punitive damages” (FAC, 7:21.) 
Defendant is to answer the remaining allegations of the First Amended
Complaint within 10 days.
ANALYSIS
Demurrer
Meet and Confer
            The
Declaration of Matthew J. Lippold indicates that Defendant’s counsel satisfied
the meet and confer requirement set forth in Civ. Proc. Code, § 435.5.
Discussion
Defendant Trader Joe’s Company
moves to strike the following portions of the 1AC:
 “5. Awarding plaintiff’s attorneys fees” (FAC,
7:20.) GRANTED without leave to amend.
“[A]s a general rule, attorney fees
are not recoverable as costs unless they are authorized by statute or
agreement.” (People ex rel. Dept. of
Corporations v. Speedee Oil Change Systems, Inc. (2007) 147 Cal.App.4th
424, 429.)
There is no contractual or
statutory basis for attorney’s fees pled.
 “6. For punitive damages” (FAC, 7:21.) GRANTED
without leave to amend.
There are no facts pled whereby
Defendant acted with malice, oppression or fraud, as required by Civil Code, §
3294[1], which
mental state is inapposite with this negligence cause of action.
Defendant is to answer the remaining allegations of the First Amended
Complaint within 10 days.
