Summary of the Judge's Tentative Ruling:

The Court has reviewed the Cross-Defendant Gail Copley's Demurrer to the First Amended Cross-Complaint (FACC) and has made the following findings:

* The Court sustains the demurrer as to the second, third, and fourth causes of action, and overrules the demurrer as to the first cause of action.
* The Court finds that Copley did not owe the alleged duty of care to Cross-Complainants.
* The Court agrees with Copley that Cross-Complainants have not pled any right to contribution.
* The Court finds that the allegations are sufficient to state a cause of action for negligent misrepresentation against Copley.

The Court's ruling is based on the legal sufficiency of the complaint against the demurrer, and the Court has treated the demurrer as admitting all material facts properly pleaded, but not contentions, deductions, or conclusions of law.
