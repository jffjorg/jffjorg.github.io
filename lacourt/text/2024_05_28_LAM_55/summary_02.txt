Here is the YAML summary of the tentative ruling:

**Case Information**

* Case Number: 23STCV04990
* Hearing Date: May 28, 2024
* Department: 55

**Parties**

* Plaintiff: HOPE VINITSKY
* Defendant: GENERAL MOTORS, LLC

**Background**

* Lemon Law case regarding a 2020 Chevrolet Bolt EV
* Plaintiff alleges defects in the vehicle, including battery module and seatbelt issues

**Legal Standard**

* Respondent has burden to justify objections to discovery requests
* Judge has discretion to compel further responses

**Analysis**

* Special Interrogatories:
	+ 39: Identify individual(s) responsible for ensuring compliance with Song-Beverly Act
	+ 43: Identify individuals responsible for decision to repurchase or replace vehicle
	+ Defendant failed to provide sufficient response, citing boilerplate objections
* Document Requests:
	+ Defendant failed to provide sufficient response, citing overbreadth
	+ Plaintiff's requests included knowledge of defective battery and inability to fix it

**Ruling**

* Grant the motion to compel further responses as to special interrogatories 39 and 43
* Deny the motion to compel further responses as to document requests, except for request number 77
* Order Defendant to serve verified, full, and complete responses by June 28, 2024

**Significant Statements**

* "A determination of whether an attempt at informal resolution is adequate involves the exercise of discretion."
* "A party is permitted to use multiple methods of obtaining discovery and the fact that information was disclosed under one method is not, standing alone, a proper basis for refusing to provide discovery under another method."
* "Any documents or category of documents produced in response to a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling shall be identified with the specific request number to which the documents respond."
