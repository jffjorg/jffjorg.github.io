The Court has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Alan Aguilar, et al. v. Thong H Pham, demurring to the answer filed by Defendant. The Court sustains the demurrer to the Tenth Affirmative Defense with leave to amend, as the defense does not state a sufficient defense based on the statute of limitations. On the other hand, the Court overrules the demurrer to the Twenty-Fourth Affirmative Defense for parol evidence and integration, finding that the defense is not uncertain or unintelligible.

The Court orders Defendant to file and serve an amended answer within 30 days of the order, and Plaintiff is ordered to give notice of the Court's ruling and file proof of service of same within five calendar days of this order.

The case involves a habitability dispute, with Plaintiffs alleging various causes of action, including violation of Civil Code section 1942.4, tortious breach of the warranty of habitability, private nuisance, and other claims. Defendant filed an answer with affirmative defenses, and Plaintiffs demurred to the Tenth and Twenty-Fourth affirmative defenses. The Court has reviewed the pleadings and finds that the Tenth Affirmative Defense fails to state a sufficient defense based on the statute of limitations, while the Twenty-Fourth Affirmative Defense is not uncertain or unintelligible.
