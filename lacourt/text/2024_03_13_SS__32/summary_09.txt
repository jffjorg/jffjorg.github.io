The Court has issued a tentative ruling on the motion for leave to conduct a neuropsychological examination of the Plaintiff, Terecita Sanchez. The examination is requested by the Defendants, Maria Villalon, Eric Villalon, and Armando Villalon, to determine the extent of the Plaintiff's injuries resulting from a motor vehicle accident. The Plaintiff has alleged mental injuries such as cognitive issues, post-traumatic stress disorder, and anxiety disorder.

The Court has granted the motion, but with certain conditions. The examination will be limited to the areas of questioning and the amount of time necessary for the expert to reach his opinions. The Plaintiff will be allowed to record the examination, but the recording will be limited to counsel for the Plaintiff and any experts. The Court has also denied the Plaintiff's request to have an attorney present at the examination.

The Court has also denied the Plaintiff's request to limit the examination to four hours and to prohibit the examiner from asking questions about the litigation. The Court has overruled the Plaintiff's objections to the proposed tests, stating that the issues raised can be explored in cross-examination of the expert.
