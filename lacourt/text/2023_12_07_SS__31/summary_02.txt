The judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Kenyce Smith and Abigail Grey vs. Cody Araghachi, et al. (Case No. 20STCV30675). The Uber Defendants, which are Uber Technologies, Inc., Rasier, LLC, and Rasier-CA, LLC, have propounded discovery requests and sent a meet and confer letter requesting responses from Plaintiff Abigail Grey. The Plaintiff has failed to respond to the discovery requests, and the Uber Defendants have moved to compel responses without objections.

The judge has granted the motion to compel discovery responses, ordering Plaintiff Abigail Grey to serve verified responses to the Uber Defendants' discovery requests without objections within 15 days.

The Uber Defendants have also moved to deem admissions admitted against Plaintiff Abigail Grey, as the Plaintiff failed to respond to the requests for admissions. The judge has granted this motion, deeming the admissions admitted against Plaintiff Abigail Grey.

Finally, the judge has imposed sanctions against Plaintiff Abigail Grey and her counsel, ordering them to pay $873.30 in sanctions to the Uber Defendants by and through counsel of record within 20 days. The sanctions include attorney's fees and costs.
