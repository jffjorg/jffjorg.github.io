The court has granted the defendant's motion to compel arbitration and stay the case. The plaintiff, Phebe Shin Kwon, filed a complaint against Tesla Motors, Inc. alleging that the vehicle they purchased, a 2023 Tesla Model S, contained defects that substantially impaired its use, safety, and value. The defendant argued that the arbitration agreements between the parties, contained in the Motor Vehicle Order Agreement (MVOA) and the Retail Installment Sale Contract (RISC), are enforceable and that the case should be stayed pending arbitration. The court found that the arbitration agreements exist and that the plaintiff did not opt-out. The court also found that the arbitration agreements are not unconscionable and that the defendant's payment of the arbitration fees does not create a conflict of interest for the arbitrator. The court granted the defendant's motion and stayed the case pending arbitration.
