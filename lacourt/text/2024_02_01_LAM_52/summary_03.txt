The judge has issued a tentative ruling on a motion to compel arbitration and stay proceedings brought by Garrett J. Gentry General Engineering Incorporated (Garrett) against Jose Cordoba. Garrett moves to compel arbitration of the action, but the court finds that the agreement is unconscionable and will not enforce it. The agreement shortens the statute of limitations for plaintiff's claims under the Labor Code and the unfair competition law, and requires plaintiff to bear the costs of arbitration. The court also finds that the agreement is one-sided and does not allow plaintiff to opt out of the arbitration agreement.

The court further denies the motion because of the presence of a co-defendant, GJ Gentry General Engineering, Inc. (GJ), who is not a party to the arbitration agreement. The claims against both defendants arise out of identical transactions and present a possibility of conflicting rulings on common issues of law or fact. Therefore, the court exercises its discretion to refuse to enforce the arbitration agreement and stay proceedings.
