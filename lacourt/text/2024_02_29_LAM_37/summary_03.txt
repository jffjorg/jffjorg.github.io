**Legal Issues**

* Legal standard for evaluating a demurrer: accept pleadings as true, ignore contentions and conclusory statements, and do not consider whether plaintiff can prove allegations
* Burden is on the complainant to show that a pleading can be amended successfully
* Leave to amend must be allowed where there is a reasonable possibility of successful amendment

**Demurrer Issues**

* Defendant demurs to FAC on basis of misjoinder of parties (prior tenants)
* Court finds that complete relief can be afforded to parties already joined in the action
* Defendant fails to show that there is a risk of inconsistent liability if prior tenants are not joined in the action
* Demurrer based on misjoinder of parties is overruled

**Third Cause of Action - Fraud**

* Fraud must be pled specifically; general and conclusory allegations do not suffice
* Elements of fraud: misrepresentation, knowledge of falsity, intent to defraud, justifiable reliance, and resulting damage
* Plaintiff fails to allege that Defendant had prior knowledge of code violations listed in the March 10, 2023, Notice
* Fraud cause of action is sustained with leave to amend

**Significant Statements**

* "In California, fraud must be pled specifically; general and conclusory allegations do not suffice."
* "The elements of fraud, which give rise to the tort action for deceit, are (a) misrepresentation (false representation, concealment, or nondisclosure); (b) knowledge of falsity (or 'scienter'); (c) intent to defraud, i.e., to induce reliance; (d) justifiable reliance; and (e) resulting damage."
