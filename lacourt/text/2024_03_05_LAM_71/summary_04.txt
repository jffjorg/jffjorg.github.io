The Court has issued a tentative ruling in the case of ANA MEDIA, INC. v. CRISTAL PICTURES, INC. et al. The Court denies the Plaintiff's request for default judgment, citing several grounds. First, the request does not properly seek an amount equal to or less than the amount sought in the complaint. Second, the Plaintiff's prayer does not provide a specific amount of damages. Third, the declarant did not submit exhibits with authenticated foundation in support of the amount of damages sought. Fourth, the Plaintiff has not shown liability as to each defendant and that recovery is not duplicative. Fifth, the Plaintiff did not provide an interest calculation.

The Court sets a hearing on an order to show cause why the complaint should not be dismissed and/or Plaintiff's counsel should be sanctioned $250 for failing to enter default judgment. The hearing is scheduled for April 5, 2024, at 8:30 AM in Department 71 at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse. The Court's ruling is based on California Rule of Court, rule 3.110(h) and C.C.P. §425.11, as well as the case of Falahati v. Kondo (2005) 127 Cal.App.4th 823, 830-831.
