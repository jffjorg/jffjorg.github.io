The judge's tentative ruling is to grant the defendant's motion to compel arbitration. The plaintiff, Pablo Flores, filed an employment discrimination action against the defendants, Emerald Textiles, LLC and Emerald SoCal, LLC. The defendant Emerald SoCal, LLC filed a motion to compel arbitration on June 11, 2024, and the plaintiff filed an opposition on July 25, 2024. The defendant filed a reply on July 26, 2024.

The judge found that the defendant has satisfied its burden of proving the existence of an arbitration agreement by attaching a copy of the agreement to the motion. The agreement was signed by the plaintiff on November 30, 2021, and covers all claims or disputes brought by either party relating to the plaintiff's application for employment, employment, or separation of employment.

The plaintiff argued that the agreement is unconscionable, but the judge found that the agreement is enforceable. The judge considered the procedural and substantive unconscionability of the agreement and found that neither element was present. The judge also found that the agreement provides for a reasonable opportunity for discovery and that the plaintiff has not shown that the agreement is one-sided or shocks the conscience.

The judge granted the defendant's motion to compel arbitration and stayed the case in its entirety. The hearing is set for August 2, 2024.
