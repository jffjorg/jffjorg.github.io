 Case Number:  20STLC04901    Hearing Date:   September 27, 2023    Dept:  25  State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company  v. Lealani Vargas 20stlc04901ANALYSIS:   I.                 Background   On June 11, 2020, Plaintiff State  Farm Automobile Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) filed an action against  Defendant Lealani Vargas (“Defendant”) seeking damages of $18,114.36.    On December 7, 2021 Plaintiff and  Defendant entered into a Settlement Agreement and Stipulation for Dismissal  with Reservation to Vacate and Enter Judgment upon Breach (“Stipulation”). On  January 4, 2022, the Court dismissed the case without prejudice and retained  jurisdiction pursuant to CCP section 664.6. (01/04/2022 Minute Order.)    Plaintiff filed in the instant  Motion to Set Aside Dismissal and to Enter Judgment (“Motion”) on August 25,  2023.   II.               Legal Standard    Under Code of Civil Procedure,  section 664.6:    (a)¿If parties to pending  litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside¿of¿the  presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case,  or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms  of the settlement. If requested by the parties, the court may retain  jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in  full of the terms of the settlement.    (b) For purposes of this section, a writing is signed by a  party if it is signed by any of the following:    (1) The party.  (2) An attorney who represents the party.  (3) If the party is an insurer, an agent who is authorized  in writing by the insurer to sign on the insurer's behalf.”                  III.             Discussion    As a preliminary matter, Plaintiff filed  a Declaration of Service on August 25, 2023. Plaintiff’s Declaration of Service  is consistent with the requirements for a proof of service pursuant to CRC,  rule 3.1300. Further, Defendant has not disputed service of the motion. The  Court will determine the motion on its merits.    The Stipulation was by signed by  Defendant, Plaintiff’s counsel and by Plaintiff’s authorized representative on  December 7, 2021. (12/07/2021 Stipulation.) The Stipulation provides that  Defendant would pay Plaintiff $4,800.00, $2,000.00 of which shall be paid by  Defendant’s Insurance Carrier on or before January 10, 2022 and fourteen  monthly payments of $200.00 each to be made beginning on February 1, 2022 with  all subsequent payments being due on the same day of each month. (Id.)  Failure to make a timely payment constitutes a material breach. (Id.) In  such event, Plaintiff will mail Defendant a letter advising that payment has  not been received. (Id.) If the payment is not made within 14 days,  Plaintiff is entitled to have any dismissal set aside and judgment entered for  the settlement amount minus credit for payments received, plus any costs and  Plaintiff shall provide written notice of the request that judgment be entered  to Defendant. (Id.)    The Court finds the Stipulation to be valid and enforceable under  Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6. Plaintiff provides evidence that  Defendant made monthly payments pursuant to the stipulating totaling $2,500.00  (Reese Decl. ¶ 3.) However, Defendant has since defaulted on payments. (Id.)  On February 21, 2023, Plaintiff mailed Defendant a letter regarding the missed  payments. (Id. at ¶ 5, Ex. B.) Defendant did not make the payment within  fourteen days. (Id. at ¶ 6.)    Plaintiff requests that the  dismissal be vacated and a judgment of $2,819.50 be entered against Defendant.  (Id. at ¶ 8.) However, Plaintiff does not explain which amount of the  judgment is based on the principal amount and costs. Plaintiff only mentions  the judgment amount sought in the proposed order but fails to mention the  judgment amount sought in the Memorandum of Points and Authorities. Plaintiff  is ordered to provide additional clarification regarding its calculation of the  principal and costs. Plaintiff’s request is CONTINUED.    IV.             Conclusion & Order   For the foregoing reasons,  Plaintiff’s Motion to Set Aside Dismissal and Enter Judgment is CONTINUED TO NOVEMBER 13, 2023 at 10:00 A.M. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse.As discussed herein, Plaintiff  is ordered to file supplemental papers providing additional clarification regarding its calculation of the  principal and costs at least 16 court days prior to the continued hearing date.  Failure to do so will result in the Motion being placed off calendar or denied.    Moving party is ordered to  give notice.
