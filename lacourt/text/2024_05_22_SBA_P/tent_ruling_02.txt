Tentative RulingThe Court DENIES as MOOT an order compelling Defendant’s response to Special Interrogatories Set One and Request for Production of Documents, Set Two. BackgroundOn January 26, 2024, Plaintiff served Special Interrogatories, Set One, Requests for Production of Documents, Set Two, and Requests for Admissions, Set One on Defendant, Rousta, pursuant to C.C.P. §§ 2030.210 et seq which were due on or before February 27, 2024. (Manzano Decl ¶ 3.)On April 9, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motions.On March 18, 2024, Defendant provided the verified discovery requests.On May 10, 2024, Defendant filed its oppositions only seeking sanctions.On May 15, 2024, Plaintiff filed her reply to Defendant’s oppositions to the Motion to Compel Responses Special Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Production, Set Two.VII. AnalysisLegal StandardCCP § 2033.280 (c) states “it is mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion.”Plaintiff seeks sanctions in the amount of $1,980 for the Special Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Production of Documents, Set Two for a total of $3,960.00 because of the misuse of discovery processes. Here, Defendant served the overdue discovery requests on March 18, 2024. (Gaugh Decl. ¶ 4.) Therefore, the Court finds that sanctions are warranted. However, the requested sanction amount is excessive because of thesimple and nearly identical motions. Therefore, the Court reduces sanctions to $1,685.00 which includes 1 hour preparing and drafting the Motions at the attorney’s rate of $495.00 per hour, 1.0 hour for the paralegal’s time at $200.00 per hour, and 1 hour in attorney time at the rate of $495.00 drafting a reply and reviewing the opposition, and one hour of attorney time for the court hearing.VIII. ConclusionAccordingly, the Court DENIES as MOOT the Motion to Compel Special Interrogatories, Set One and the Motion to Compel Requests for Production. Additionally, the Court DENIES as MOOT the Requests for Admission, Set One.The Court GRANTS sanctions in a reduced amount of $1,685.00 for the Motion to Compel Special Interrogatories, Set One and the Motion to Compel Requests for Production. Those sanctions are awarded against Defendant only as the Notice did not request sanctions against Defendant’s Counsel.
