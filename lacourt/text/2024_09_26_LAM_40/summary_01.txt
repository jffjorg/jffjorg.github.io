**Facts**

* Angel Manzo, a security guard, was injured at a commercial building owned by Brimel and leased to Chen.
* Norguard alleges that Brimel's negligence caused Manzo's injuries and seeks reimbursement for workers' compensation benefits paid to Manzo.
* Brimel filed a motion for summary judgment, arguing that it had no notice of the alleged unsafe condition that caused Manzo's injuries.

**Legal Issues**

* Whether Brimel breached a duty of care owed to Manzo.
* Whether Brimel had actual or constructive knowledge of the dangerous condition that caused Manzo's injuries.
* Whether Brimel's inspection of the property was reasonable under the circumstances.

**Arguments**

* Norguard argues that Brimel did not adequately inspect the property to make the premises reasonably safe from dangerous conditions.
* Brimel argues that it had no notice of the alleged unsafe condition and that its inspection of the property was reasonable under the circumstances.

**Ruling**

* The court denies Brimel's motion for summary judgment, finding that there is a triable issue of fact regarding whether Brimel had actual or constructive knowledge of the dangerous condition that caused Manzo's injuries.
* The court finds that Norguard has established a triable issue of material fact regarding whether Brimel's inspection of the property was reasonable under the circumstances.

**Significant Statements**

* "The owner of premises is under a duty to exercise ordinary care in the management of such premises in order to avoid exposing persons to an unreasonable risk of harm."
* "The landlord's obligation is only to do what is reasonable under the circumstances."
* "A landlord should anticipate the danger and conduct a reasonable inspection before passing possession to the tenant."
