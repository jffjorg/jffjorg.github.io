  Case Number:  19STCV21240    Hearing Date:   November 20, 2024    Dept:  B Plaintiff Tammy Lynn Gardner’s Requests for Default JudgmentDefendants: Laura Rabelo and Marcos GuerecaTENTATIVE RULINGPlaintiff Tammy Lynn Gardner’s request for default judgment against Defendants Laura Rabelo and Marcos Guereca is GRANTED.BACKGROUNDThis action arises from a May 6, 2019, motor vehicle accident in which Plaintiff Tammy Lynn Gardner (“Plaintiff”) alleges Defendants Laura Rabelo (“Rabelo”), Marcos Guereca (“Guereca”), and Does 1 to 10 (collectively “Defendants”) negligently operated their motor vehicle, colliding with Plaintiff’s motor scooter and causing serious injuries thereof.On June 18, 2019, Plaintiff filed a complaint against Defendants alleging motor vehicle negligence and negligent entrustment.On June 5, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.On November 14, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant request for default judgment against Defendants.LEGAL STANDARDCode of Civil Procedure section 585 permits entry of a default judgment after a party has failed to timely respond or appear. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 585.) A party seeking judgment on the default by the court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) declaration of nonmilitary status; (6) a proposed form of judgment; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorney’s fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1800.)ANALYSISPlaintiff seeks damages in the amount of $151,167.26 in general and special damages against Rabelo. Plaintiff also seeks damages in the amount of $15,000.00 against Guereca as he is the registered owner of the vehicle which struck Plaintiff.Defendants have failed to timely respond or appear. Plaintiff’s default packet meets all statutory requirements under CCP § 585. Therefore, Plaintiff’s request for default judgment against Defendants is GRANTED.Moving party is ordered to give notice.
