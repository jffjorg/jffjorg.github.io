The judge has ruled on the motion to quash the deposition subpoenas served on the Los Angeles Department of Building and Safety, the Housing Authority of the City of Los Angeles, and the Los Angeles Housing Department. The plaintiff, Yolanda Amador, had served the subpoenas in an effort to gather evidence related to the incident that occurred at 2335 Wall Street, Los Angeles, California, in which a piece of ceiling plaster fell in unit 105. The defendant, 555 Holdings LLC, had argued that the subpoenas were overbroad, unlimited in time, and unduly burdensome, and that they sought documents that were not relevant or reasonably calculated to lead to the production of admissible evidence. The judge, however, concluded that the subpoenas were not overbroad and that they sought documents that were reasonably calculated to lead to the production of admissible evidence. The judge denied the motion to quash the deposition subpoenas.
