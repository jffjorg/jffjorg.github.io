And here is the English-text summary:

This tentative ruling is about a case between Plaintiff Carlos Quezada and Defendant Zeno King Jr. regarding an automobile accident that occurred on June 25, 2021. The Plaintiff filed the present case and subsequently filed a Doe amendment naming Zeno King Jr. as a defendant. The Defendant demurred, arguing that the Plaintiff had knowledge of sufficient facts to cause a reasonable person to believe King Jr.'s liability is probable, and that the Plaintiff's filing of the Doe amendment was not made in good faith. The Court finds that the Plaintiff's filing of the Doe amendment was made in good faith and that the Defendant failed to demonstrate that the Plaintiff had knowledge of sufficient facts to cause a reasonable person to believe King Jr.'s liability is probable. Therefore, the Court OVERRULES the Defendant's demurrer.
