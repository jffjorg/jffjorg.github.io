  Case Number:  20STCV21816    Hearing Date:   December 5, 2024    Dept:  SEC Skinner v. Downey Unified School District, et al.
20STCV21816
 
This personal injury action was filed on June 9, 2020.
Trial is currently scheduled to begin on January 21, 2025. Plaintiff moves ex
parte to Reopen Discovery After Trial Date Has Been Set due to a prior
counsel’s failure to depose Defendants’ person most knowledgeable. 
 
Plaintiff’s ex parte application to Reopen Discovery After
Trial Date Has Been Set is DENIED without prejudice. “[A]ny party shall
be entitled as a matter of right to complete discovery proceedings on or before
the 30th day, and to have motions concerning discovery heard on or before the
15th day, before the date initially set for the trial of the action.” (Code Civ.
Proc. § 2024.020 subd. (a).) Any request to reopen discovery must be filed and
served as a separately noticed motion and must be accompanied by a meet and
confer declaration in compliance with Code of Civil Procedure section 2016.040.
The contents of a motion to reopen discovery must comply with Code of Civil
Procedure section 2024.050. 
 
In opposition, Defendants request monetary sanctions under
Code of Civil Procedure section 2024.050 subd. (c) for opposing this
application. The Court DENIES Defendants’ request for monetary sanctions
because this ex parte application is not a noticed motion. Moving Party to give
notice.
