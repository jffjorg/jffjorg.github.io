Delgado, counsel of record for Plaintiff Mocha Holdings LLC, seeks to be relieved  as counsel.  Counsel’s declaration states  that Plaintiff is in breach of the retainer agreement.  Counsel also declares that he has made  numerous unsuccessful attempts to contact Plaintiff, and the attorney-client  relationship has broken down.  No party opposed  the motion. Absent  a showing of resulting prejudice, an attorney’s request for withdrawal should be  granted.  (People v. Prince (1968)  268 Cal.App.2d 398, 406.)  Counsel’s motion  complies with California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362.  Trial is not until April 8, 2024, and there is  no prejudice to Plaintiff. The  proposed order does not include Plaintiff’s current or last known phone  number.  Counsel must provide this  information at the hearing. Subject  to the provision of Plaintiff’s phone number, the unopposed motion to be relieved  is GRANTED and effective upon filing a proof of service showing service of the signed  Form MC-053 order and this order on Mocha Holdings LLC and all parties who have  appeared. However,  “a corporation cannot represent itself in a court of record either in propria persona  or through an officer or agent who is not an attorney.”  (Caressa Camille, Inc. v. Alcoholic Beverage  Control Appeals Bd. (2002) 99 Cal.App.4th 1094, 1101.)  Therefore, an Order to Show Cause Re: Failure  to Retain New Counsel for Mocha Holdings LLC is scheduled for December 28, 2023  at 8:30 a.m. in Department 48 at Stanley Mosk Courthouse.  Plaintiff must retain new counsel by that date  or the Court will dismiss this action. Moving  party to give notice.
