And here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Dena Feingold vs. FCA US, LLC (Case No. 24STCV06062). The plaintiff, Dena Feingold, filed suit against the defendant, FCA US, LLC, on March 11, 2024, alleging breach of express warranty, breach of implied warranty, and Civil Code section 1793.2(b). On December 4, 2024, the plaintiff moved to compel further responses to her Requests for Production (RFPs). The judge has granted the plaintiff's motion in part and denied it in part.

The judge has ordered the defendant to produce certain documents, including:

* The "Warranty Policy and Procedure Manual" published by the defendant and provided to its authorized repair facilities in California
* Internal analysis or investigation regarding defects concerning the transmission defect in vehicles for the same year, make, and model of the subject vehicle
* Customer complaints relating to defects that are similar to the alleged defects claimed by the plaintiff in vehicles within California for the same year, make, and model of the subject vehicle, for the period of purchase to present
* Documents evidencing policies and procedures used to evaluate customer requests for repurchase pursuant to the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act, for the period of purchase to present
* Technical Service Bulletins and Recall Notices for vehicles purchased in California for the same year, make, and model of the subject vehicle

The judge has denied all other requests for further production. The plaintiff's motion is granted in part and denied in part, and the judge declines to award sanctions at this time.
