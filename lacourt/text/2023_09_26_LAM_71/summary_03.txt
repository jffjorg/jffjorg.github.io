Here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English:

The court grants the plaintiff's motion for attorneys' fees in the reduced amount of $70,021.50 and costs in the reduced amount of $4,005.43, for a total of $74,026.93. The court finds that the plaintiff is the prevailing party because he obtained his litigation goals to be compensated for his causes of action under the Consumers Legal Remedies Act and the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act. The court reduces the plaintiff's requested attorneys' fees by $17,825.00 due to excessive fees claimed and unnecessary discovery. The court also reduces the plaintiff's requested costs by $4,792.37 due to excessive costs claimed. The court awards the plaintiff's counsel reasonable hourly rates and recognizes the time spent dealing with the issues related to Ashley Marie Gomez's death as reasonably spent. The court denies the plaintiff's requested mediation fees, travel expenses, photocopying costs, and postage as not recoverable by statute.
