The Judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Jeremy Brooks v. JPMorgan Chase Bank, et al. regarding a demurrer to the First Amended Complaint. The demurrer was filed by Defendant JPMorgan Chase Bank, which argues that the Plaintiff's allegations do not state a cause of action for violations of the California Consumer Credit Reporting Accountability Act.

The Judge has sustained the demurrer with leave to amend with respect to the allegations pertaining to Civil Code section 1785.25(a) only and otherwise overruled it. This means that the Plaintiff may amend their complaint to cure the defects described in the demurrer, but only with respect to the allegations related to Civil Code section 1785.25(a).

The Judge has also granted the Plaintiff 30 days' leave to file a Second Amended Complaint. The Defendant is required to give notice of this tentative ruling. The Judge will conduct a hearing on December 4, 2023, and any party may submit on the tentative ruling by contacting the courtroom via email by 4:00 p.m. the day before the hearing.
