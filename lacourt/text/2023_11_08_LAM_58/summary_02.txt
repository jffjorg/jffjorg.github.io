**Case Information**

* Case Number: 19STCV13263
* Case Name: Maria Barajas Quintero v. KIA Motor America, Inc.
* Hearing Date: November 8, 2023

**Parties**

* Plaintiff: Maria Barajas Quintero and Oscar Quintero
* Defendant: KIA Motor America, Inc.

**Motion**

* Motion: Motion to Strike and/or Tax Costs
* Moving Party: Defendant KIA Motor America, Inc.
* Responding Party: Plaintiffs Maria Barajas Quintero and Oscar Quintero

**Tentative Ruling**

* The motion to tax costs is granted in part.

**Facts**

* The parties settled the Song-Beverly matter for $109,000.
* Plaintiffs purchased a new 2016 Kia Optima with numerous issues related to the suspension and engine.
* Plaintiffs filed this action against KIA alleging violations of the Song-Beverly Act and fraud.

**Legal Standard**

* The "prevailing party" is entitled to recover costs for suit in any action or proceeding.
* Recoverable costs "shall be reasonably necessary to the conduct of the litigation rather than merely convenient or beneficial to its preparation."

**Discussion**

* Defendant moves to strike $250.35 in filing fees for unsuccessful and unnecessary motions.
	+ The Court finds that these costs were reasonably necessary to the conduct of the litigation and denies the request to strike.
* Defendant moves to strike $12,595.04 in deposition costs.
	+ The Court finds that these costs were reasonably necessary to the conduct of the litigation and denies the request to strike.
* Defendant moves to strike $6,483.74 for expert witness fees.
	+ The Court finds that these costs were reasonably necessary to the conduct of the litigation and reduces the amount by $1,100.
* Defendant moves to strike $5,614.75 for court reporter fees.
	+ The Court finds that these costs were reasonably necessary to the conduct of the litigation and denies the request to strike.
* Defendant moves to strike $1,104.08 for attorney services and messengers, appearance attorneys, and travel.
	+ The Court finds that these costs were reasonably necessary to the conduct of the litigation and grants the motion to strike as to travel costs in the amount of $119.45.

**Conclusion**

* The motion to tax costs is granted in the total amount of $1,219.45.
* Plaintiffs are entitled to costs of suit in the amount of $29,696.69 and attorney fees for the motion in the amount of $2,125.
