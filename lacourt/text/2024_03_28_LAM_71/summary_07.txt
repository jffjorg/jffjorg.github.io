**Case Summary:**

The Judge's Tentative Ruling in the case of Adan Gomez-Rivera vs. ADB Global Trade LLC, et al. addresses the motions to quash service of summons filed by Caesarstone Ltd., Stylenquaza LLC, Cosentino Group, and ADB Global Trade LLC. The ruling grants Caesarstone's motion to withdraw its appearance, grants Caesarstone's motion to quash service of summons, denies Stylenquaza's motion to quash service of summons, continues Cosentino Group's motion to quash service of summons to June 5, 2024, and denies ADB Global Trade LLC's motion to quash service of summons.

**Key Points:**

1. Caesarstone's motion to withdraw its appearance is granted due to its inadvertent filing of a notice of appearance and failure to meet the requirements for withdrawal under California Code of Civil Procedure (C.C.P.) ยง473(b).
2. Caesarstone's motion to quash service of summons is granted due to the lack of personal jurisdiction over the company.
3. Stylenquaza's motion to quash service of summons is denied, despite its claims that it was not properly served and did not intentionally direct its activities towards California.
4. Cosentino Group's motion to quash service of summons is continued to June 5, 2024, to allow for jurisdictional discovery.
