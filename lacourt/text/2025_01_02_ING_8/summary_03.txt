And here is the summary in English text:

The Judge has issued a tentative ruling on a motion by the Plaintiff, Lamar Mosley, to add punitive damages to his complaint against the Defendants, Robert Levin, M.D., Marsha Budhan, PA-C, Olukemi Wallace, M.D., Uptimum Medical Group, and IPA, Inc.

The Plaintiff's expert testifies that the Defendants acted with a conscious, callous, and reckless disregard for the life, safety, and well-being of the Plaintiff, resulting in the Plaintiff's amputation due to chemotherapy administered by the Defendants despite a negative pathology report.

The Judge has granted the motion as to Dr. Wallace and Uptimum Medical Group, finding that the evidence submitted by the Plaintiff is sufficient to add a claim for punitive damages against them. However, the Judge has deferred ruling on the motion as to Dr. Levin, pending a potential settlement of the claims against him.

The Judge has also ordered oral argument or potentially additional allegations as to whether evidence of the standard of care of a physician's assistant is required to allege punitive damages against Budhan.
