**Key Points**

* Defendant Caesarstone USA, Inc.'s demurrer and motion to strike portions of the complaint are overruled and denied.
* The court finds that the plaintiffs have pleaded sufficient facts to state a cause of action against the defendant.
* The defendant's arguments that the claims are uncertain, lack causation, and fail to plead the locations of exposure, precise dates, or methods of exposure are unpersuasive.
* The court finds that the plaintiffs have pleaded sufficient facts to support a claim for fraudulent concealment, including allegations that the defendant concealed the hazardous nature of its products from the plaintiffs and their employers.
* The defendant's motion to strike plaintiffs' request for punitive damages is denied, as the allegations are sufficient to support a claim for punitive damages.

**Facts**

* The plaintiffs allege that they were exposed to toxic airborne dust and particulates emanating from the defendant's products while working with them from 2012 to 2022.
* The plaintiffs allege that they suffered illness as a result of their exposure to the defendant's products, including silicosis requiring a lung transplant.
* The plaintiffs allege that the defendant concealed the hazardous nature of its products from them and their employers during the period of time they worked with the products.

**Disputes**

* The defendant disputes the plaintiffs' allegations that the defendant failed to warn users of the hazards of its products and that the defendant concealed the hazardous nature of its products from the plaintiffs and their employers.
