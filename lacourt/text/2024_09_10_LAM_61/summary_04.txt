The judge has denied Defendant Midway Rent A Car, Inc.'s motion to compel arbitration of Plaintiff Elizabeth Sanchez's claims. The plaintiff alleges sexual harassment and discrimination by the defendant. The defendant argued that the arbitration agreement executed by the plaintiff on July 27, 2022, is valid and should be enforced. However, the judge ruled that the arbitration agreement is unenforceable under the Ending Forced Arbitration of Sexual Assault and Sexual Harassment Act (EFAA) because the plaintiff's claims relate to a "sexual harassment dispute" as defined by the EFAA.

The judge found that the plaintiff's claims are viable and that the defendant failed to establish that the arbitration agreement was valid. The judge also ruled that the EFAA applies to all claims alleged by the plaintiff, including her meal and rest break claims, which are intertwined with her sexual harassment claims.

The judge cited various cases and statutes in support of their decision, including California Code of Civil Procedure, Section 1281.2, and 9 U.S.C. § 402. The judge also noted that the defendant's argument that the court should sever the harassment claims from the rest of the lawsuit was impermissible under federal law.
