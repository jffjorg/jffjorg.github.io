This tentative ruling concerns a dispute between Northern California Collection Service, Inc. (Plaintiff) and Valley View Congregate Living Inc. (Defendant) regarding discovery responses and sanctions. The Plaintiff filed five motions to compel initial responses from the Defendant, including motions for Form Interrogatories, Special Interrogatories, and Requests for Production of Documents. The Plaintiff also filed a motion for an order deeming the matters admitted in its Requests for Admissions and a motion for an order deeming the documents genuine. The Plaintiff filed a notice of no opposition, stating that it did not receive an opposition brief to the motions. The Judge, John J. Kralik, granted the Plaintiff's unopposed motions to compel responses to the discovery requests and ordered the Defendant to provide verified responses within 20 days. The Judge also granted the Plaintiff's unopposed motion for an order deeming the Requests for Admissions admitted and the documents genuine. The Plaintiff requested sanctions against the Defendant and its counsel, and the Judge awarded $1,800 in sanctions. The Judge also awarded the Plaintiff $1,500 for the motions and $300 in filing fees.
