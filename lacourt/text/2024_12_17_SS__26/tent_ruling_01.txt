  Case Number:  24STLC03924    Hearing Date:   December 17, 2024    Dept:  26 Nekuryan Property Investment, LLC v. Noga, et al.,TENTATIVE RULING:Plaintiff Nekuryan Property Investment, LLC’s Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories (UD) and for Sanctions is DENIED.ANALYSIS:Plaintiff Nekuryan Property Investment, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action for recovery of COVID-19 rental debt against Defendant Assil Noga aka Noga Assil (“Defendant”) on May 30, 2024. Defendant filed an answer on August 19, 2024. On November 14, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories (UD) and for Sanctions. No opposition has been filed to date. However, at the December 16, 2024, hearing on Plaintiff's separately filed Motion to Compel Responses to Plaintiff's Requests for Production of Documents, Defendant represented to the court that she had complied with all discovery demands and had served responses to the Form Interrogatories on Sunday December 15, 2024. (Minute Order 12/16/24). Plaintiff's counsel indicated that despite compliance from Defendant, Plaintiff intended to appear for the hearing on the instant motion to seek sanctions. DiscussionDefendant’s Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories (UD) and for Sanctions is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 2030.260 and 2030.290. The Form Interrogatories—General, Set One, consisting of 37 interrogatories with subparts, was served on Plaintiff on September 30, 2024. (Motion, Spinrad Decl., ¶3 and Exh. A.) Responses were due by October 30, 2024. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.260, subd. (a).) As of the filing of this Motion, however, Plaintiff has received no responses nor objections to the discovery. (Motion, Spinrad Decl., ¶4.)orform interrogatories propounded by Defendant are in violation of the Code of Civil Procedure with respect to discovery in the limited jurisdiction court. Specifically, Code of Civil Procedure section 94, limits the total number of interrogatories, demands to produce documents, and requests for admission to 35. (Code Civ. Proc., § 94, subd. (a).) The statute also prohibits interrogatories with subparts. (Code Civ. Proc., § 94, subd. (a)(1).) The Court will not order a party to provide responses to discovery propounded in violation of the Code of Civil Procedure. For the same reason, sanctions are not warranted.ConclusionTherefore, Plaintiff Nekuryan Property Investment, LLC’s Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories (UD) and for Sanctions is DENIED.Court clerk to give notice.
