Royal Investment Holdings, LLC, et al.Case No. 23LBCV01292Motion: Defendants Royal Investment Holdings, LLC; Royal California, LLC; Royal California One, LLC; and David Davoudpour’s Motion for Leave to file a Cross-Complaint against Plaintiff Pacific Premier Bank and proposed Cross-Defendant Cajun Global, LLC d/b/a Church’s ChickenMoving Party: Defendants Royal Investment Holdings, LLC; Royal California, LLC; Royal California One, LLC; and David Davoudpour (“Defendants”)Responding Party: Plaintiff Pacific Premier Bank (“Plaintiff”)Tentative RulingCode of Civil Procedure section 428.10, subdivision (b), states that a party may file a cross-complaint setting forth “[a]ny cause of action he [or she] has against a person alleged to be liable thereon, whether or not such a person is already a party to the action, if the cause of action in his [or her] cross-complaint, arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences as the cause brought against him [or her].” If the proposed cross-complaint is permissive, leave of court may be granted “in the interest of justice” at any time during the course of the action. (Code Civ. Proc. § 428.50, subd. (c).)Because the causes of action asserted arise out of the same occurrence as the original action, the Court grants leave to file the cross-complaint. Plaintiff’s argument concerning the merits of Defendants’ cross-complaint are inapplicable to the relief sought now and does not preclude Plaintiff from challenging the merit at a later date.Defendants’ motion for leave to file cross-complaint is granted, with leave to file the cross-complaint within 10 days.
