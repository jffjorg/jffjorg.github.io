 Case Number:  24NNCV00493    Hearing Date:   November 5, 2024    Dept:  X MOTION FOR TURNOVER ORDER IN AID OF
EXECUTION 
Plaintiff moves for a turnover order
against Judgment Debtor-Defendant
 
Plaintiff and Defendants entered into a Small Business Loan
Application and Promissory Note for a line of credit Defendants subsequently
defaulted and failed to cure the default or pay the monies due and owing.  On
March 26, 2024, Plaintiff Umpqua Bank filed a complaint against Defendants SX
Super, Inc. and Khoren Levon Dallakyan related to the breach of the loan
agreement.  The complaint alleges a
principal sum of $59,782.48 is due, with an interest rate of 10 percent per
annum from October 7, 2022.
 
Plaintiff’s motion is GRANTED.  The
Court finds that Judgment Creditor Hollywood establishes sufficient need for a
turnover order in the present case.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 699.040(b).)  The order shall be personally served on the
judgment debtor and shall contain a notice to the judgment debtor that failure
to comply with the order may subject the judgment debtor to arrest and
punishment for contempt of court.  (Code
Civ. Proc., § 699.040(c).) 
The Court will sign the proposed order submitted, which is amended to be consistent with the Court’s order.
