 Case Number:  24SMCV03370    Hearing Date:   January 16, 2025    Dept:  S25 Tentative Ruling1st Cause of Action – Elder Abuse/NeglectDefendant Posada’s demurrer to the First Cause of Action (Elder Abuse) is OVERRULED. The Court finds that the facts alleged in Plaintiffs’ complaint are like those alleged in Fenimore v. Regents of the Univ. of Calif. (2016) 245 Cal.App.4th 1339. Plaintiffs allege Defendant Posada engaged in a knowing pattern of understaffing, a conscious disregard of fall risk care plans, lack of training and failure to update or reappraise treatment and/or fall prevention plans. Further, Plaintiffs sufficiently alleged Defendant Posada’s ratification or authorization. (See Compl., ¶¶ 19, 22, 26-27, 30, 31, 33-34, 36, 53, 54 and 59.)3rd Cause of Action – Wrongful DeathDefendant Posada’s demurrer to the Third Cause of Action (Wrongful Death) is OVERRULED. “The elements of a cause of action for wrongful death are a tort, such as negligence, and resulting death. [Citation.]” (Lopez v. City of Los Angeles (2011) 196 Cal.App.4th 675, 685.) To prevail on a cause of action for wrongful death, “plaintiffs must prove (1) a wrongful act or neglect on the part of one or more persons [that is negligence' that (2) causes (3) the death of [another] person. [Citations.]” (Musgrove v. Silver (2022) 82 Cal.App.5th 694, 705.) The Court finds that Plaintiffs have stated sufficient facts to state a wrongful death claim. (See Compl., ¶¶ 24, 26-27, 67, 72-75.)Defendant Posada to Answer within 10 days of this date of this ruling.
