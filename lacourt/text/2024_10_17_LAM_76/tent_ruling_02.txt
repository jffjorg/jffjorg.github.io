 Case Number:  21STCV38826    Hearing Date:   October 17, 2024    Dept:  76  
            Plaintiff alleges that
Defendants, its two former officers, engaged in direct competition with
Plaintiff while still employed by Plaintiff, and induced multiple employees to
resign from their employment with Plaintiff to go to work for a direct
competitor in which Defendant Haensich has a direct pecuniary interest. 
On August 2, 2024, the Court
entered the judgment on special verdict against Defendant Hardy Haenisch in the
sum of $945,600.00 and in favor of Defendant Christina Suen against whom
Plaintiff recovered nothing. 
Defendants Hardy Haensich and
Christina Suen move to tax Plaintiff’s memorandum of costs. 
TENTATIVE RULING          
For the first time in the Reply,
Defendants present detailed argument as to why certain items should be taxed.
Plaintiff will be given an opportunity to file a sur-reply to address these new
arguments.
The hearing on Defendants Hardy
Haensich and Christina Suen’s motion to tax Plaintiff’s memorandum of costs is
CONTINUED to December 6, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. Plaintiff’s sur-reply is due
November 22, 2024.
