


[1] “A dismissal with prejudice operates as a judgment in
favor of the party dismissed.”   (Lea
v. Shank (1970) 5 Cal.App.3d 964, 974; see also Roybal v. University
Ford (1989) 207 Cal.App.3d 1080, 1086 [“A dismissal with prejudice
terminates the action and the rights of the parties are affected by it. It is a
final judgment in favor of defendants”].)
