The court has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Princeton Lofts, Inc. (Plaintiff) versus Gamma Investors, Group, LLP (Defendant). The case concerns a barking dog nuisance on the property that was abated by the Defendant. The Plaintiff filed a motion for $12,000 in attorneys' fees and $744.96 in litigation costs as the "prevailing party" pursuant to Civil Code section 5975.

The court has determined that the Plaintiff is the "prevailing party" as they achieved their main litigation objective, namely the cessation of the barking dog nuisance. The court has also found that the Plaintiff provided the requisite minimal procedural due process to the Defendant by serving the Notice of Continued Hearing and motion papers concurrently.

The court grants the Plaintiff's motion for attorneys' fees and costs. The Plaintiff shall file a proposed order in conformance with the court's ruling for the court's consideration and approval. Additionally, the Plaintiff shall provide notice of the court's ruling and file a proof of service regarding the same.
