The court has issued a tentative ruling on the defendants' motion to compel arbitration in the case of Yolanda Flores v. Walters Residence Homes, Inc. et al. The motion was filed in response to the plaintiff's wrongful termination and discrimination claims. The defendants presented evidence of an arbitration agreement signed by the parties on September 21, 2016, which states that all disputes will be resolved through binding arbitration. The plaintiff alleges that the agreement is unconscionable due to a lack of notice and pressure to sign.

The court finds that the arbitration agreement was not oppressive and that the plaintiff has failed to show any procedural unconscionability. Therefore, the court grants the motion to compel arbitration and orders the parties to proceed with arbitration. The defendants are ordered to give notice to the plaintiff and the court.
