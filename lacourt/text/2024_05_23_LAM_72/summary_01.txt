**TENTATIVE RULING**

**Case:** FOSTER WASHINGTON v. DANIEL STURRIDGE

**Summary:**

* The Court DENIES Defendant's motion to quash service by mail or publication.
* The Court will discuss whether additional publication in a United Kingdom publication is appropriate.
* The Court requests that Plaintiff's counsel come to the hearing with a suggestion as to what publication would be appropriate to use for publication in the United Kingdom.
* The Court would also consider arguments by defense counsel as to what publication is most likely to be seen by Defendant.

**Key Points:**

* Defendant is a professional soccer player and currently resides in London, England.
* Plaintiff has exercised reasonable diligence to locate Defendant, including searching public records, databases, and contacting individuals who may know Defendant's whereabouts.
* The Hague Convention does not apply in this case because Defendant's address is not known despite reasonable diligence.
* The Court is inclined to reconsider its order for publication or issue a nunc pro tunc to select an additional publication that circulates in London, England, where Defendant has stated he lives.

**Intentions:**

* To ensure that the case proceeds with proper service of process on Defendant.

**Facts:**

* Plaintiff filed this action against Defendant on May 24, 2021, raising claims for breach of oral contract and breach of written contract.
* Defendant is a professional soccer player and resides in London, England.
* Plaintiff has exercised reasonable diligence to locate Defendant, including searching public records, databases, and contacting individuals who may know Defendant's whereabouts.

**Disputes:**

* Defendant argues that service by publication was not proper because Plaintiff did not conduct reasonable diligence to serve Defendant in another manner.
* The Court will consider arguments from both sides regarding the appropriateness of the publication ordered.

**Acts:**

* Plaintiff has attempted to locate Defendant through various means, including searching public records, databases, and contacting individuals who may know Defendant's whereabouts.
* Defendant has not provided his address in any of his declarations.

**Roles:**

* Plaintiff's counsel has attempted to locate Defendant through various means, including searching public records, databases, and contacting individuals who may know Defendant's whereabouts.
* Defendant's counsel has not provided any information regarding Defendant's address.

**Significant Statements:**

* "The showing of diligence in a given case must rest on its own facts and no single formula nor mode of search can be said to constitute due diligence in every case." (Kott v. Superior Court (1996) 45 Cal.App.4th 1126, 1138)
* "The Hague Convention does not apply where the address of the person to be served with the document is not known." (Hague Convention, art. 1, 20 U.S.T. 361)
