English-text summary:

This tentative ruling addresses a demurrer filed by United Airlines, Inc. regarding a complaint brought by Keith Feder, M.D. alleging negligent misrepresentation, promissory estoppel, and recovery of benefits under ERISA. The Court finds that the first two causes of action are preempted by ERISA because they arise out of and relate to the existence of an ERISA plan. The Court sustains the demurrer to these causes of action with 20 days leave to amend. However, the Court overrules the demurrer to the third cause of action, which alleges recovery of benefits under ERISA. The Court's ruling is based on its interpretation of ERISA preemption and the facts alleged in the complaint.
