The judge has issued a tentative ruling denying the plaintiff's motion for an order deeming the truth of matters admitted in their Requests for Admission. The plaintiff sought to have the matters deemed admitted and to award monetary sanctions in the amount of $2,904 against the defendant. The court found that the plaintiff failed to show that they properly served the defendant with the Requests for Admission at the correct email address for electronic service. The court also found that the defendant did not manifest affirmative consent to electronic service, as required by California Rules of Court, rule 2.251. As a result, the court denied the plaintiff's motion.
