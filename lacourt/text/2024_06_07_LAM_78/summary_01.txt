**Case Summary**

**Case Number:** 22STCV05381

**Case Name:** Luis Manuel Lopez, Plaintiff, vs. Outfront Media, Inc., et al., Defendants

**Tentative Ruling:**

* The motion for summary adjudication is DENIED as to the Second, Fifth, Sixth, and Tenth causes of action and as to the issue of punitive damages.
* The motion for summary adjudication is GRANTED as to the First, Third, Fourth, Eighth, and Ninth causes of action.

**Key Points:**

* The case involves claims of disability discrimination, harassment, failure to accommodate, failure to engage in the interactive process, and failure to prevent discrimination, harassment, or retaliation.
* The Plaintiff, Luis Manuel Lopez, alleges that he was subjected to disability discrimination, harassment, and retaliation by his former employer, Outfront Media, Inc.
* The court granted summary adjudication in favor of the defendants on several causes of action, including the claim of disability discrimination, failure to accommodate, and failure to engage in the interactive process.
* The court denied summary adjudication on the causes of action for harassment and failure to prevent discrimination, harassment, or retaliation.
* The parties will proceed to trial on the remaining causes of action.

**Procedural History:**

* The Plaintiff filed his complaint on February 14, 2022.
* The defendants filed their demurrer, which was partially sustained on August 16, 2022.
* The Plaintiff filed his opposition to the motion for summary adjudication on April 14, 2024.
* The defendants filed their reply to the opposition on April 24, 2024.

**Facts:**

* The Plaintiff was employed by the defendants as a billposter, but was terminated from his position.
* The Plaintiff alleged that he was subjected to disability discrimination, harassment, and retaliation by his supervisors, including Rick Guzman and Anthony Zubiate.
* The Plaintiff claimed that he was not provided with reasonable accommodations for his disabilities, including his partial deafness and injuries sustained in a workplace accident.
* The Plaintiff alleged that he was subjected to a hostile work environment, including being yelled at and belittled by his supervisors.
* The Plaintiff claimed that he was not provided with any available positions after he was terminated, and that his supervisors failed to engage in an interactive process to determine reasonable accommodations.

**Legal Issues:**

* The court considered the following legal issues in its tentative ruling:
	+ Whether the Plaintiff was a qualified individual with a disability under the Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA).
	+ Whether the defendants failed to accommodate the Plaintiff's disability.
	+ Whether the defendants failed to engage in the interactive process to determine reasonable accommodations.
	+ Whether the defendants failed to prevent discrimination, harassment, or retaliation.
