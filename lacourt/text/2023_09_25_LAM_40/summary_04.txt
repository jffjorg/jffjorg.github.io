The tentative ruling concerns the demurrer filed by the Los Angeles Sheriff’s Department to Plaintiff Alyssa M. Coley’s First Amended Complaint. The Plaintiff, who is representing herself, has filed a complaint alleging putative claims under various laws, including California Civil Code 52(b)(3), Title VI of the Civil Rights Act, and 18 USC 242 of Title 18. The Defendant has demurred to the complaint on grounds of failure to comply with the mandatory statutory time of the California Tort Claims Act, sufficiency and uncertainty in pleading, and legal immunity. The Plaintiff has failed to oppose the demurrer and has not filed any attachments or provided facts explaining the grounds for the putative claims. The Court has STRIKED the Plaintiff’s First Amended Complaint and granted the Plaintiff 30 days’ leave to amend her pleadings.
