The judge has tentatively ruled that the defendants' motion to compel arbitration is granted. The plaintiff, Ruth Montury, was admitted to a skilled nursing facility (SNF) and alleges that she experienced a dramatic decline in condition during her stay, including a fall that resulted in a fractured hip. The defendants, Gem Healthcare, LLC and Saint Cabrini Healthcare Services LLC, argue that the plaintiff signed an arbitration agreement as part of her admission to the facility, and that the agreement covers the claims alleged in the lawsuit.

The judge concludes that the arbitration agreement exists and is enforceable, and that it covers the claims alleged by the plaintiff. The judge also finds that the arbitration agreement is not unconscionable, despite the plaintiff's arguments that it is an adhesion contract and that Frank Montury, Jr. did not have the opportunity to refuse to sign it.

The judge further concludes that the Health and Safety Code Section 1430 claims are not precluded from arbitration under the Federal Arbitration Act (FAA). Finally, the judge grants the defendants' motion to compel arbitration and stays the action pending arbitration.

A post-arbitration status conference is set for 2024, and the parties are ordered to file a joint status report at least five days before the conference.
