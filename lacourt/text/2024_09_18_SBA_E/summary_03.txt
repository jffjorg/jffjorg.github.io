The court has ruled on a motion to compel further responses to requests for production of documents related to a lawsuit filed by Janie Vieyra against General Motors, LLC. The lawsuit alleges that General Motors, LLC failed to comply with the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act by not repairing a transmission defect in Vieyra's 2018 Cadillac XT5 vehicle. The court has granted Vieyra's motion to compel further responses to certain requests, including those related to internal investigations and analysis of transmission and electrical defects in Cadillac XT5 vehicles. The court has denied General Motors, LLC's objections to these requests, finding them to be overly broad and insufficient. The court has also denied Vieyra's request for sanctions against General Motors, LLC, finding that the defendant made a good faith effort to comply with discovery obligations.
