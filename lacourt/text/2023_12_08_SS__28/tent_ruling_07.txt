 Case Number:  22STCV15458    Hearing Date:   December 8, 2023    Dept:  28 Having considered the moving papers, the Court rules
as follows. 
BACKGROUND 
On May 10, 2022, Plaintiff Francisco
Geribay Enriquez (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Emma
Montes (“Defendant”) and Does 1-25 for motor vehicle tort. 
On March 7, 2023, Defendant filed an answer. 
On July 26, 2023, Defendant filed a motion to deem
admitted matters specified in requests for admission, set one, served on
Plaintiff.  Plaintiff did not file an opposition.  
On September 12, 2023, the Court granted the motion,
deemed admitted the matters specified in Defendant’s requests for admission, and
imposed monetary sanctions.  On September 15, 2023, Defendant served notice of the ruling. 
On September 26, 2023, Plaintiff filed a motion for reconsideration
to be heard on December 8, 2023. On November 22, 2023, Defendant filed an
opposition. On December 1, 2023, Plaintiff filed a reply. 
Trial is currently scheduled for May 7, 2024. 
PARTIES’ REQUESTS 
Plaintiff requests that the Court reconsider and
vacate its September 12, 2023, order deeming admitted the matters specified in
Defendant’s requests for admission. 
Defendant requests that the Court deny the motion. 
LEGAL STANDARDS 
Code of Civil Procedure section
1008 provides in part: 
“When an
application for an order has been made to a judge, or to a court, and refused
in whole or in part, or granted, or granted conditionally, or on terms, any
party affected by the order may, within 10 days after service upon the party of
written notice of entry of the order and based upon new or different facts,
circumstances, or law, make application to the same judge or court that made
the order, to reconsider the matter and modify, amend, or revoke the prior
order. The party making the application shall state by affidavit what
application was made before, when and to what judge, what order or decisions
were made, and what new or different facts, circumstances, or law are claimed
to be shown. 
(Code Civ.
Proc., § 1008, subd. (a).) 
DISCUSSION 
Plaintiff asks the Court to reconsider its September
12, 2023 order granting Defendant’s motion to deem admitted matters specified
in requests for admission, arguing Defendant did not properly serve the motion
on Plaintiff. 
Defendant served notice of the Court’s September 12, 2023
ruling on September 15, 2023.  Plaintiff
does not challenge the service of the ruling. 
Under Code of Civil Procedure section 1008, subdivision (a), Plaintiff
had until September 25, 2023 to file a motion for reconsideration.  Plaintiff filed the motion for
reconsideration on September 26, 2023.  The
motion is untimely. 
The Court denies the motion. 
CONCLUSION 
The Court DENIES Plaintiff Francisco Geribay Enriquez’s
motion for reconsideration. 
Moving party is ordered to give notice of this ruling. 
Moving party is ordered to file the proof of service
of this ruling with the Court within five days.
