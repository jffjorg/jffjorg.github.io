  Case Number:  19STCV21240    Hearing Date:   October 22, 2024    Dept:  B Plaintiff Tammy Lynn Gardner’s Requests for Default JudgmentDefendants: Laura Rabelo and Marcos GuerecaTENTATIVE RULINGPlaintiff Tammy Lynn Gardner’s request for default judgment against Defendants Laura Rabelo and Marcos Guereca is DENIED.BACKGROUNDThis action arises from a May 6, 2019, motor vehicle accident in which Plaintiff Tammy Lynn Gardner (“Plaintiff”) alleges Defendants Laura Rabelo (“Rabelo”), Marcos Guereca (“Guereca”), and Does 1 to 10 (collectively “Defendants”) negligently operated their motor vehicle, colliding with Plaintiff’s motor scooter and causing serious injuries thereof.On June 18, 2019, Plaintiff filed a complaint against Defendants alleging motor vehicle negligence and negligent entrustment.On June 5, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.On October 21, 2024, Defendants entered default.On October 21, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant request for default judgment against Defendants.LEGAL STANDARDCode of Civil Procedure section 585 permits entry of a default judgment after a party has failed to timely respond or appear. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 585.) A party seeking judgment on the default by the court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) declaration of nonmilitary status; (6) a proposed form of judgment; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorney’s fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1800.)ANALYSISDefendant RabeloPlaintiff seeks monetary damages against Rabelo in the amount of $203,440.87, representing $181,483.07 in general and special damages, $21,282.80 in prejudgment interest, and $675.00 in costs.Plaintiff’s request is denied for the following deficiencies:1. Plaintiff did not file a copy of the Statement of Damages served upon Defendants. Additionally, the amount of principal damages sought in JUD-100 ($181,483.07) is more than that in Plaintiff’s CIV-100 ($180,808.07).2. Plaintiff requests interest but does not provide interest calculation. (CRC 3.1800(a)(3).)3. Plaintiff is suing Defendant Guereca on the sole basis that he owns and entrusted the involved vehicle to Rabelo. Accordingly, Plaintiff must provide proof of Guereca’s ownership pursuant to Cal. Veh. Code § 17151(a).Plaintiff’s request for default judgment has been denied for deficiencies on three separate occasions. Accordingly, the Court dismisses this action for failure to prosecute pursuant to Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 583.410 and CRC 3.110(h).Plaintiff’s request for default judgment against Defendants Laura Rabelo and Marcos Guereca is DENIED and the action is dismissed without prejudice pursuant to CCP § 583.410 and CRC 3.110(h).Moving party is ordered to give notice.
