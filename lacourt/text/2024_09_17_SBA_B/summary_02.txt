**Parties**

* Plaintiff: Antonio Zaragoza
* Defendants: The Picerne Group, Inc., et al. (including Wallcraft Drywall, Inc. and De La Torre Construction)

**Facts**

* Antonio Zaragoza was employed by Trencore Plastering, Inc. on March 3, 2022, to provide stucco, plastering, and/or other work to the exterior of a building under construction.
* Defendant The Picerne Group, Inc. was the general contractor at the worksite, retaining control over the safety and interactions between contractors.
* Defendant Wallcraft Drywall, Inc. was a subcontractor responsible for drywalling at the worksite, retaining control over the scrapping work entrusted to De La Torre Construction.
* On March 3, 2022, De La Torre Construction performed scrapping work at the worksite, including the removal of drywall scraps from the interior of the building, without warning or a spotter.
* Antonio Zaragoza was struck in the head by a sheet of drywall and suffered severe personal injuries.

**Legal Authority**

* The Privette doctrine holds that a hirer generally delegates to an independent contractor all responsibility for workplace safety and is not liable for injuries sustained by the contractor or its workers while on the job.
* There are two exceptions to the Privette doctrine: the retained control exception and the concealed hazard exception.

**Ruling**

* The demurrer is OVERRULED as to the first and second causes of action in the FAC.
* The allegations in the FAC are sufficient at the pleading stage to meet the elements that plaintiff's claims fall within the retained control exception to the Privette doctrine.
* Defendants are ordered to file answers within twenty days.
* Plaintiff is ordered to give notice of ruling.
