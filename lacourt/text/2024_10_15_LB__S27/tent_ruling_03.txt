Background FactsPlaintiff, Kae Kwon filed this action against Defendant, Intercommunity Care Centers, Inc. for damages arising out of assault, battery, and related claims. Plaintiff filed the action on 12/23/22. On 5/22/23, the Court held a Case Management Conference. Plaintiff had not yet served Defendant with the summons and complaint. Neither party appeared. The Court set an OSC re: Sanctions, including dismissal, for 6/14/23. On 6/14/23, no party appeared, and the Court dismissed the action.2. 2/27/24 MotionOn 12/18/23, Plaintiff filed a motion to vacate the dismissal, contending it was entered as a result of Plaintiff’s attorney’s mistake or neglect. The hearing was originally set for 1/02/24 in Department S26.Prior to the hearing, Defendant filed a preemptory challenge against Judge Vicencia, and the case was reassigned to Department S27. The hearing was rescheduled for 2/27/24.On 2/27/24, Plaintiff failed to appear at the hearing. Because Plaintiff failed to appear, the Court denied the motion.3. Renewed MotionOn 3/22/24, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to vacate the dismissal. Plaintiff’s attorney declares the failure to attend the 2/27/24 hearing was due to the assigned attorney’s personal issues, which the firm did not learn about until after the dismissal was entered.Defendant opposes the motion, correctly noting that it is a motion for reconsideration of the 2/27/24 ruling, and is made without any new facts, circumstances, or law, as required by CCP §1008(b). Pursuant to Even Zohar Const. & Remodeling, Inc. v. Bellaire Townhouses, LLC (2015) 61 Cal.4th 824, 842, §1008(b) applies to renewed motions to vacate dismissal under §473(b). Because there are no new facts, circumstances, or law, the motion for relief is denied.Plaintiff is ordered to give notice.Parties who intend to submit on this tentative must send an email to the court at gdcdepts27@lacourt.org indicating intention to submit on the tentative as directed by the instructions provided on the court website at www.lacourt.org.
