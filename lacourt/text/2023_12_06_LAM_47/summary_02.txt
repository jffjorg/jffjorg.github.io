The Judge, Theresa M. Traber, has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Gladys Navarro v. South Bay Safety Inc., et al. The case is a wrongful termination lawsuit, with Plaintiff alleging that Defendant terminated her employment in retaliation for taking sick days. Plaintiff moves to compel responses to requests for production and form interrogatories, and also seeks sanctions for Defendant's failure to respond to discovery.

The Judge grants Plaintiff's motions to compel responses, ordering Defendant to provide verified, code-compliant responses without objections within 30 days. The Judge also grants Plaintiff's request for sanctions in the amount of $1,530 against Defendant and its counsel, jointly and severally. The sanctions are for Defendant's failure to respond to discovery, and the Judge finds that the sanctions are mandatory in this case. The Judge notes that Defendant has not responded to the discovery requests, and that Plaintiff's counsel prepared motions and declarations in support of the motions.
