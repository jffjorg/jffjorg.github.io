The judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Bagdasaryan v. General Motors Company, Case no. 24SMCV00862. The plaintiff, Bagdasaryan, moves to compel further discovery from the defendant, General Motors Company, and Cadillac of Beverly Hills. The defendant opposes the motion, arguing that it has already provided compliant responses to the discovery requests.

The court finds that the defendant's responses are compliant with discovery rules, including Cal. Code Civ. Proc. §2031.220 and §2031.230. The court also notes that the defendant's objections to certain requests are specific and comply with Cal. Code Civ. Proc. §2031.240.

The court denies the motion to compel further discovery, finding that the defendant has already provided all responsive documents in its possession, custody, or control. The court also denies the plaintiff's request for sanctions, finding that the defendant's objections were not frivolous and that the plaintiff failed to meet and confer in good faith.

The court notes that the defendant is entitled to raise attorney-client privilege, work product, and trade secret objections, even if the plaintiff has excluded such documents from its requests for production. The court also finds that the defendant's failure to comply with the court's order to meet and confer on October 8, 2024, did not nullify its production of responsive documents.
