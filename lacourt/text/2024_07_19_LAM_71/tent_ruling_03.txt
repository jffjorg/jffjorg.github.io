1.    
Motion to Compel Femina- FROG
Having reviewed Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Responses from Femina to
Plaintiff’s FROGs, the Court rules as follows.
          On October 26, 2023, Plaintiff served
FROG on Femina by electronic service to his counsel, Bradley Brook.  (Decl. of Dicecca ¶5, Exh. 1.)  Femina’s counsel requested multiple extensions
to respond to Plaintiff’s FROG, which were granted, with a final deadline to
respond on February 6, 2024.  (See
Decl. of Dicecca ¶6, Exhs. 2-12.)  On February 6, 2024, Femina failed to provide
Plaintiff with responses to the FROG; instead, Brook served on Plaintiff
discovery requests.  (See Decl. of
Dicecca ¶7, Exh. 3-1.)  As of the date of
this motion no response to FROG has been served on Plaintiff.  (Decl. of Dicecca ¶8.)  Plaintiff now moves to compel responses.  

          Plaintiff’s motion to compel responses is granted pursuant
to C.C.P. §2030.290(b).  Femina
is ordered to provide verified responses to Plaintiff’s FROG compliant with
C.C.P. §§2030.210(a) and 2030.220 without objections within 20 days.
Plaintiff requests monetary sanctions totaling $1,160.00 against Femina and his
counsel, Bradley Brook, jointly and severally. 
          The Court awards sanctions pursuant
to C.C.P. §2030.290(c) in the amount of $560.00,
calculated as follows:
[(1 hour to prepare instant motion x $500.00)] + $60.00 filing fee = $560.00

The sanctions are payable by Femina and his attorney, jointly and
severally, within 20 days of this order.
Moving Party is to give notice of this ruling.

5.    
Motion to Compel Further Femina- RFP
Having reviewed Plaintiff’s
Motion to Compel Further Responses to RFP 1, the Court rules as follows.
          On October 26, 2023, Plaintiff served RFP 1 on Femina by
electronic service to his counsel, Bradley Brook.  (Decl. of Dicecca ¶5, Exh. 1.)  The response was originally due November 29,
2023.  Femina’s counsel requested
multiple extensions to respond to Plaintiff’s RFP 2, which were granted, with a
final deadline to respond on February 6, 2024. 
(See Decl. of Dicecca ¶6, Exhs. 2-12.)  On February 6, 2024, Brook served Femina’s
Responses to RFP 1.  In RFP Nos. 3, 5-6,
9-18, 20, 23-24, 27, 31, 42 and 47, Femina asserted insufficient and incomplete
responses, and as to Request for Production of Documents Nos. 1-2, 7-8, 21-22,
33, 36, 39-40, 43, and 46, Defendant agreed to produce documents but failed to
do so. (Decl. of Dicecca ¶7, Exh. 2).  Brook’s
email, which served the responses, stated that “the documents will be forwarded
tomorrow,” February 7,
2024. The next day, Brook
sent another email to Plaintiff’s counsel advising that the documents “will be
provided on Friday,” February 9, 2024. Despite those representations, however,
no documents were ever received.  (Decl. of
Dicecca ¶7, Exh. 3).  Plaintiff now moves
to compel further responses. 
          Plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses to RFP 1 Nos.
3, 5-6, 9-18,
20, 23-24, 27, 31, 42, and
47 and produce documents for RFP 1 Nos. 1-2, 7-8, 21-22, 33, 36, 39-40, 43, and
46 is granted pursuant to C.C.P.
§2031.320. 
Femina is ordered to provide Code-compliant responses pursuant to C.C.P.
§2031.010 and to produce the documents, electronically stored information,
and/or other things requested within 20 days of this ruling.
Plaintiff requests monetary
sanctions totaling $2,810.00 against Femina and his
counsel, Bradley Brook, jointly and severally. 
          The Court awards sanctions pursuant to C.C.P. §2031.310(h) in
the amount of $2,260.00, calculated as follows:
[(4 hours to prepare instant
motion x $550.00)] + $60.00 filing fee = $2,260.00
The sanctions are payable by
Femina and his attorney, jointly and severally, within 20 days of this order.
Moving Party is to give
notice of this ruling.
