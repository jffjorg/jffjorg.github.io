**Case Information**

* Case Number: 21STCV36175
* Hearing Date: October 25, 2023
* Department: 27
* Judge: Kerry Bensinger

**Parties**

* Plaintiff: Sandra Martinez Basurto
* Defendant: Bodega Latina Corporation dba El Super

**Background**

* Incident: Slip and fall on July 7, 2023
* Plaintiff filed motions to compel discovery responses and request sanctions on July 7, 2023
* Defendant served untimely responses, and the Court denied the motions on August 8, 2023, but granted sanctions against Defendant and its counsel
* Defendant and its counsel paid the sanctions on August 29, 2023

**Legal Standards**

* Contempt is any act that tends to impede, embarrass, or obstruct the court in the discharge of its duties
* Direct contempt is committed in the court's presence, while indirect contempt is committed outside the court's presence

**Discussion**

* Plaintiff argues that the motion should be granted because the elements of contempt are met
* Defendant and its counsel had actual knowledge of the Court's order, had the ability to comply, and willfully disobeyed the order by failing to pay the sanctions on time
* However, Defendant and its counsel paid the sanctions on August 29, 2023, and the Court finds that the delay in payment does not amount to willful disobedience

**Conclusion**

* The motion is denied
* The requests for sanctions are denied

**Significant Statements**

* "The contempt power is a court's 'ultimate weapon.'" (McCann v. Municipal Court (1990) 221 Cal.App.3d 527, 536-37)
* "The power to adjudicate direct contempt is necessarily arbitrary and should be used with great prudence." (People v. Gonzalez (1996) 12 Cal.4th 804, 816)
* "A mere mistaken act by counsel cannot render him in contempt of court." (Gallagher v. Municipal Court (1948) 31 Cal.2d 784, 788)
