The judge has granted Richmond Plastering, Inc.'s motion for summary adjudication as to the duty to defend issue within the 5th cause of action for breach of contract. The court finds that there are no triable issues of fact remaining as to the duty to defend, and therefore, the motion is granted.

The judge's ruling is based on the court's interpretation of the Master Subcontract Agreement and the relevant statutes, including CCP §437c(f)(1) and CC §2778(4). The court has determined that the duty to defend is not a question of fact for the jury and that the indemnification agreement is broad enough to cover work that arises out of or is related to the claim.
