And here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The judge has granted the motion to approve and enter a consent judgment between the plaintiff, The Chemical Toxin Working Group Inc., and the defendant, California Olive Ranch, Inc. The consent judgment requires the defendant to provide clear and reasonable warnings on its products that contain chemicals harmful to the public, and to take remedial actions to reduce exposure to those chemicals. The judge finds that the consent judgment meets the requirements of Proposition 65 and serves the public interest. The judge also approves the attorney's fees and civil penalties specified in the consent judgment.

The consent judgment requires the defendant to:

* Provide clear and reasonable warnings on its products that contain chemicals harmful to the public
* Take remedial actions to reduce exposure to those chemicals, including reformulating its products and requiring certification from its suppliers that they do not use certain chemicals
* Pay civil penalties of $48,221.25 to the Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment (OEHHA)
* Pay an additional settlement payment of $43,500.00 to the plaintiff

The judge finds that the consent judgment is reasonable and serves the public interest. The judge grants the motion to approve and enter the consent judgment.
