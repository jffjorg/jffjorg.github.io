  Case Number:  22PSCV00508    Hearing Date:   September 17, 2024    Dept:  O Tentative Ruling
 
Plaintiff’s Application for Default Judgment is is GRANTED, in the reduced
amount of [see below]. The court requests Plaintiff to file a revised
proposed order. 
 
Background
 
This is a civil rights/disability case. 
 
On May 27, 2022, Plaintiff filed suit for violation of Unruh Civil Rights Act (UCRA). 
 
On September 6, 2022, default was entered against Defendant.

 
On October 12, 2023, Plaintiff filed a ‘Notice of Withdrawal
of Notice of Settlement.’
 
On October 13, 2023, Plaintiff an application for default
judgment, which the court on 11/17/23 denied without prejudice.
 
On March 14, 2024, Plaintiff filed her FAC.
 
On June 12, 2024, default was entered against Plaintiff. 
 
On September 4, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant
application.[1]

 
Discussion
 
Plaintiff
seeks Plaintiff $8,000.00 in statutory damages pursuant to Cal. Civ. Code §
52(a), attorney’s fees in the amount of $570.00, and costs in the amount of $1,504.00,
for a total amount of $10,074.00. 
 
The court finds Plaintiff’s declaration is insufficient to
support a request for deterrence damages insofar as there are no details to
support how and why Plaintiff will be further deterred from visiting the Arco
gas station.[2]

 
Conclusion
 
Based on the foregoing, the application GRANTED, minus
$4,000 in deterrence damages for a revised total of $6,074.00. Plaintiff is
to submit a new proposed order.
