Notice: Sufficient 
Ruling: THE COURT ADOPTS DISCOVERY REFEREE’S
RECOMMENDATION
 
On August 14, 2024, the
Court appointed Hon. Ann Kough (Ret.) as Discovery Referee under CCP 639 to
address issues arising from each party’s initial discovery requests, which
resulted in motions to compel filed by both parties. The Referee heard the parties’
arguments regarding the outstanding discovery and submits the following
recommendations to the Court:
1.  Defendant
Organizers be ordered to further respond without objection to Special
Interrogatories #1, #3, #4, #26-27, #29, #31 and #36-85. 
2.  However,
as to #36-85, the Referee recommends that this order be stayed for fourteen
(14) days in order for Organizers to file a motion for a protective order if it
wishes to do so.
3.  Defendant
Organizers be ordered to further respond without objection to Form
Interrogatories #12.1, #12.3, #12.7, #15.1 and #17.1. 
4.  Defendant
Organizers be ordered to further respond without objection to Requests for
Admission #2, #7, #8-10, #13, #15, #23 and #25. 
5.  Defendant
Organizers be ordered to further respond without objection to Requests for
Production #1-15 of Set 1 and #1-3, #5-12 of Set 2.
6.   Plaintiff be ordered to further respond
without objection to Form Interrogatories #1.1, #2.8, #2.12, #2.13, #4.1-4.2,
#6.1-6.7, #7.1-7.3, #8.1-8.8, #9.1-9.2, #10.1-10.3, #11.1- 11.2 and #12.1-12.7.

7.  Plaintiff
be ordered to further respond without objection to Special Interrogatories #3,
#5, #6 and #12.
8.   Plaintiff be ordered to further respond
without objection to Requests for Production #1, #3-5, and #7. 
9.  All
documents be ordered to be produced as TIFF images with metadata intact,
including a statement compliant with CCP section 2031.230 as to any lost or
destroyed documents, within thirty (30) days. 
10.     
All sanctions be denied.
The parties did not
file an objection to the recommendation within 20 days of the service of the
recommendation. The court having read and considered the Discovery Referee’s
Recommendation, adopts the recommendations as the rulings of the Court.
