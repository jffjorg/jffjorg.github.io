The court has tentatively ruled that the motion for leave to conduct an additional physical examination in neurology filed by defendants Jardiel Ochoa and Melissa Rosann Diazochoa is GRANTED. The examination will be conducted by Dr. Kenneth L. Nudleman, a neurologist, on November 19, 2024 at 9:00 a.m. at 801 North Tustin Avenue, Suite 304, Santa Ana, California 92705.

The court finds good cause to grant leave to conduct the neurological examination based on the plaintiff's discovery responses regarding her neurological issues. The defendants have properly included the time, place, manner, conditions, scope, and nature of the examination in their motion.

The court notes that the plaintiff's counsel refused to stipulate to an additional examination, but the defendants' counsel did meet and confer with the plaintiff's counsel regarding the issue. The court also notes that the plaintiff did not respond to the meet and confer emails.

The court's ruling is based on the California Code of Civil Procedure and case law, including Code of Civil Procedure, § 2032.220, Code of Civil Procedure, § 2032.310, Code of Civil Procedure, § 2032.320, Vinson v. Superior Court (1987) 43 Cal.3d 833, Sporich v. Superior Court (2000) 77 Cal.App.4th 422, and Roberts v. Superior Court (1973) 9 Cal.3d 330.

The court orders that the defendants shall provide notice of the court's order and file a proof of service of such.
