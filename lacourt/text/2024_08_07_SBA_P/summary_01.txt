The court has granted Defendant American Honda Motor Company, Inc.'s Motion to Compel Arbitration. The case involves a dispute between Plaintiff Jazmin Gray and the Defendant regarding a leased 2022 Honda Civic that allegedly suffered from various defects and nonconformities. The Defendant relies on an arbitration agreement contained in the Lease Agreement between Plaintiff and non-party Carson Honda, which states that disputes will be resolved through arbitration. The court concludes that the Defendant is a named third-party beneficiary to the arbitration agreement and that the agreement applies to Plaintiff's claims against the Defendant. The court declines to address whether arbitration may be compelled under the doctrine of collateral estoppel and grants the Defendant's motion to compel arbitration, staying the action.
