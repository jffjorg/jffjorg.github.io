 Case Number:  22STCV10043    Hearing Date:   October 13, 2023    Dept:  30 STACEY JOEL HUNT, JR. vs TESLA MOTORS, INC., et al.Motion to Compel Responses to Form InterrogatoriesTENTATIVEPlaintiff Stacey Joel Hunt, Jr’s motion to compel Defendant Lawrence Richard Rodriguez's responses to Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, General, Set One, Number 17.1 is GRANTED. Defendant Rodriguez is ordered to serve verified responses, without objection, within 20 days. Request for sanctions is denied.  Moving party to give notice.DISCUSSIONOn March 8, 2023, Plaintiff Stacey Joel Hunt, Jr. (Plaintiff) propounded on Defendant Lawrence Richard Rodriguez (Defendant Rodriguez) Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, General, Set One, Number 17.1 served via U.S. mail and e-mail. (Rodriguez Decl., § ¶ 14.) Plaintiff states that the Form Interrogatories, General, Set One requested Defendant Rodriguez answer Number 17.2 for the concurrently served Request for Admission, Set One. (Id. at ¶ 16.) Plaintiff asserts that on April 11, 2023, Defendant Rodriguez emailed purported responses but they contained no objections and were completely blank where Defendant Rodriguez’s responses should be. (Id. at ¶¶ 18-19, Exh. 2.) Plaintiff states that these responses were signed by Defendant Rodriguez’s counsel. (Id. at ¶ 20.)  Defendant Rodriguez has not served any response. A party who fails to timely respond to the interrogatories waives any objection to the interrogatories, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product under Chapter 4 (commencing with Section 2018.010).” (Code of Civ. Proc., § 2030.290(a).)  The Court declines to award sanctions as the motion is unopposed.
