  Case Number:  19STCV29977    Hearing Date:   May 14, 2024    Dept:  28 Having
considered the petitioning papers and supplemental declaration, the Court rules
as follows. 
BACKGROUND 
On
August 23, 2019, Plaintiff Yassine Belmlih (“Plaintiff”) filed this action
against Defendants Los Angeles Unified School District (“Defendant”) and Does
1-100 for negligence. 
On
August 4, 2020, Plaintiff filed a first amended complaint against Defendant and
Does 1-100 for negligence (Gov. Code, § 910). 
On
August 20, 2020, Defendant filed an answer. On August 29, 2022, Plaintiff’s
counsel informed the Court that the case had settled. 
On
March 22, 2024, Petitioner Mohammed Belmlih (“Petitioner”) filed a petition to
approve the compromise of the claim of a person with a disability, to be heard
on April 16, 2024. 
On
April 16, 2024, the Court issued a tentative ruling, heard argument, continued
the hearing to May 14, 2024, and gave Petitioner leave to file a supplemental
declaration. 
On
May 7, 2024, Plaintiff filed a declaration by his counsel. 
PETITIONER’S REQUEST 
Petitioner
asks the Court to approve the compromise of the pending action of Plaintiff
Yassine Belmlih. 
DISCUSSION      
Proposed
settlement terms:
          Gross settlement amount:                    $57,023.20
Attorney’s fees:                                   $13,405.92

Payment to mediator Alan Saler:         $3,975.00 
Payment to medical providers:            $5,294.97 
Net balance:                                         $34,347.31 
At
the April 16, 2024 hearing, the Court granted Petitioner leave to submit a
declaration explaining why the petition listed $99,167.66 in medical expenses and $5,294.97
in reductions but only $5,294.97 to be paid or reimbursed from the settlement
proceeds, leaving $88,577.72 in medical expenses unaccounted for. 
Plaintiff’s
counsel has submitted a declaration stating that “[t]he total amount of medical
services charged for services rendered was $99,167.66, however, after insurance
write-offs and reduction by Department of Health Care Services (Medi-Cal) the
¿nal amount $5,294.97.”  Counsel also
states: “I have made a diligent search and there are no other outstanding lien
from any other medical provider.” 
Counsel signed the declaration under penalty of perjury. 
Based
on counsel’s representations, the Court finds that Petitioner has submitted a
completed petition.  The Court grants the
petition. 
The
petition requests that the net balance of $34,347.31 be
paid or delivered to Plaintiff.  The
Court so orders. 
CONCLUSION 
The Court GRANTS the petition for approval of the
compromise of the pending action of Plaintiff Yassine Belmlih filed by Petitioner
Mohammed Belmlih on March 22, 2024.  The net balance of $34,347.31 is to be paid
or delivered to Plaintiff Yassine Belmlih. 
The
Court sets an OSC re: distribution of net settlement proceeds on July 15, 2024 at
8:30 a.m. in Department 28 of the Spring Street Courthouse. 
The
Court sets an OSC re: dismissal (settlement) on July 15, 2024 at 8:30 a.m. in
Department 28 of the Spring Street Courthouse. 
          Petitioner is ordered to give notice of the ruling. 
Petitioner
is ordered to file the proof of service of the ruling with the Court within
five days.
