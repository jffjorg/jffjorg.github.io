And here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The court has considered the motion to enforce the settlement agreement between Westlake Flooring Company, LLC (Plaintiff) and Kenton William Merrihew (Defendant). The parties entered into a written settlement agreement on June 30, 2020, which required Defendant to pay $15,000 to Plaintiff via consecutive monthly payments of no less than $250, starting from July 15, 2020 and continuing for a period of sixty months. Defendant made payments for a total of $11,250 and has not made payments thereafter.

The court finds that the requirements of California Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6 have been satisfied, and that the settlement agreement was entered into by the parties as part of a pending litigation. The parties stipulated for the court to retain jurisdiction to enforce the agreement.

Plaintiff has submitted evidence that Defendant has breached the terms of the settlement agreement, and is owed $13,769.26 in remaining principal. Defendant has not filed any opposition.

The court grants Plaintiff's motion to enforce the settlement agreement and orders Defendant to pay a total amount of $14,721.76 in remaining principal, attorney's fees, and court costs.
