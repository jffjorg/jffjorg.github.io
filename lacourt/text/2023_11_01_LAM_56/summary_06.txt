The court has considered the defendant's motion for judgment on the pleadings, which argues that the plaintiff's claims are exempt from the Government Claims Act under AB 218. The plaintiff alleges that he was sexually abused by a teacher employed by the defendant from 1978 to 1981, and that the defendant had reason to know of the teacher's misconduct. The court has denied the motion, finding that the retroactive elimination of the claim presentation requirement for childhood sexual abuse claims that arose before 2009 in AB 218 does not constitute a "gift of public money or thing of value" within the meaning of the Anti-Gift Provision of the Constitution.
