The judge has issued a tentative ruling on a motion to compel responses to special contention interrogatories served by San Fernando Valley Automotive, LLC. The motion was filed on July 16, 2024, and seeks an order compelling the plaintiffs, Ted Shuzo Sakaida and Pamela A. Sakaida, to provide answers without objections to the interrogatories, as well as imposing sanctions on the plaintiffs and their attorney in the amount of $1,675.00. The motion was granted, and the plaintiffs are ordered to provide verified responses without objections to the interrogatories within 30 days. Sanctions in the amount of $1,000.00 are also imposed against the plaintiffs and their attorney, Jack E. Kaufman, payable within 30 days. The judge found that the plaintiffs' failure to serve timely responses waived their objections to the interrogatories, and that the amount of time claimed to have been spent preparing the motion was excessive.
