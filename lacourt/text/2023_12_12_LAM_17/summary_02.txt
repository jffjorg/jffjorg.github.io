The judge has granted the defendant's motion to compel arbitration and stayed the court action pending the completion of arbitration. The plaintiff, Ester Machado, had filed a lawsuit against LA Downtown Medical Center alleging retaliation in violation of labor codes and unfair business practices. The defendant had moved to compel arbitration and stay the court action pending completion of arbitration.

The judge found that the preponderance of evidence supports a finding that Machado and the defendant had entered into a binding agreement to arbitrate. The agreement was part of the onboarding packet for Machado's employment at the defendant's facility, and Machado did not dispute that she executed the agreement.

The judge also found that Machado did not present credible evidence of procedural or substantive unconscionability. Machado had argued that the agreement was procedurally unconscionable because she was forced to sign the agreement under pressure and could not understand the agreement due to its formatting and language. However, the judge found that the evidence showed only a low degree of procedural unconscionability.

As for substantive unconscionability, the judge found that Machado had not presented any evidence to support her claim that the agreement was one-sided and favored the defendant. The judge noted that the agreement provided for both sides to be bound by the arbitration agreement.

Based on the foregoing, the judge granted the defendant's motion to compel arbitration and stayed the court action pending the completion of arbitration.
