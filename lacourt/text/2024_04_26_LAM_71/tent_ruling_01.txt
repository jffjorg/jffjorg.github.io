John P. Murray’s
Request for Default Judgment
          Having considered all documents submitted in support of a default
judgment, the Court rules as follows:
 
Background
On April 11, 2019,
John P. Murray (“Plaintiff”) filed this action in pro per against
Defendants David Caro, Fernando Montes, Raul Avila, and Avfund Capital Group
Inc. (collectively, “Defendants”) for dissolution of partnership and accounting
and breach of fiduciary duties. 
On January 2, 2024,
the clerk entered default against Defendant Fernando Montes.  On April 10, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Request
for Court Judgment to be heard on April 26, 2024.
 
Party’s Request
Plaintiff requests the Court to enter a
default judgment against Defendant Fernando Montes and award Plaintiff $105,276.49 consisting of $66,615.94 as the demand
of the complaint, $33,288.00 in prejudgment interest, and $5,372.55 in costs. 
 
Discussion
Plaintiff
submitted a completed default judgment packet, with all applicable attachments.
The Court finds sufficient evidence to justify the requested award and grants
Plaintiff’s request in full.
 
Conclusion
The application
for default judgment filed on April 10, 2024, is granted.  Judgment of $105,276.49 is entered against Defendant Fernando Montes.
Plaintiff is ordered to give notice of
this ruling.
