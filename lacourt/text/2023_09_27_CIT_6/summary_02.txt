**Significant Statements**

* "The demurrer is a pleading used to test the legal sufficiency of other pleadings. It raises issues of law, not fact, regarding the form or content of the opposing party's pleading." (Code Civ. Proc., § 422.10)
* "A demurrer can be used only to challenge defects that appear on the face of the pleading under attack; or from matters outside the pleading that are judicially noticeable." (Blank v. Kirwan, 39 Cal.3d at p. 318)
* "The face of the complaint includes material contained in attached exhibits that are incorporated by reference into the complaint, or in a superseded complaint in the same action." (Frantz v. Blackwell, 189 Cal.App.3d at p. 94)
