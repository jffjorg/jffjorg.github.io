The court has considered the motions for summary judgment and sanctions filed by Wilmington Savings Fund Society (Wilmington) and has denied both motions.

The court finds that there is a triable issue whether Andrea Brown obtained title to the property by fraud or forgery, and therefore, Wilmington's motion for summary judgment on its own claims for quiet title and declaratory relief is denied.

The court also finds that there is a triable issue whether Plaintiff has standing to sue for quiet title, and therefore, Wilmington's motion for summary judgment on Plaintiff's claims is denied.

The court further finds that Wilmington has not carried its threshold burden at summary judgment to show that it purchased the Property in good faith without actual or constructive notice of Plaintiff's claim, and therefore, Wilmington's motion for summary adjudication of its third cause of action for equitable subrogation is denied.

Finally, the court denies Wilmington's motion for sanctions, finding that Plaintiff's complaint is not frivolous and that Wilmington has not shown that Plaintiff or her attorney filed the suit for an improper purpose.
