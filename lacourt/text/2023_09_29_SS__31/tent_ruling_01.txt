  Case Number:  20STCV45610    Hearing Date:   September 29, 2023    Dept:  31 DEPT:   31 OSC DATE:  09/29/23 CASE NAME/NUMBER:  JOSE MARTIN ARENAS v JOSE MONTOYA/20STCV45610 REQUEST FOR COURT JUDGMENT AGAINST [DEFAULTING PARTY]:  JOSE MONTOYA RECOMMENDATION:  DENY for reasons stated below.  TENTATIVE  Plaintiff Jose Martin Arenas (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Jose Montoya dba A Imports & Domestic Auto Wrecking (“Defendant”) and Does 1 to 30 for injuries Plaintiff sustained on November 30, 2018 while working for Defendant. The complaint alleges that while Plaintiff was operating a forklift, the forklift came down on his wrist and arm. Plaintiff has dismissed Does 1 to 30.  The request for default judgment submitted on 8/30/2023 is DENIED for the following reasons:  • Plaintiff served a Statement of Damages to Defendant on 1/16/2023, but default was entered on 6/23/2021. Plaintiff must serve Defendant with a Statement of Damages before default is entered. (Hamm v. Elkin (1987) 196 Cal.App.3d 1343, 1345-46; See also CCP 425.11(c)-(d).) Therefore, the Court finds the underlying default VOID. Plaintiff must obtain a new entry of default. Once Plaintiff obtains a new entry of default and submits a new default package, Plaintiff must cure the defects noted below.  • Counsel did not sign CIV-100, pertaining to Items 4-6.    • Plaintiff submits a declaration in Spanish with a copy that has seemingly been translated to English. However, the English translation is not certified under oath by a qualified interpreter. (See. Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1110(g).)  • Plaintiff’s Request for Court Judgment calls for special damages of $20,858.65. Plaintiff’s declaration provides that he suffered lost wages of $12,000.00, and Plaintiff’s medical records show Plaintiff incurred past medical expenses of approximately $1,369.00. Plaintiff does not otherwise substantiate what the remaining $7,489.65 in special damages is for.   • To the extent that Plaintiff is seeking damages for future medical expenses, Plaintiff must clarify the amount requested for future medical expenses. Further, Plaintiff must provide competent evidence that he anticipates incurring additional medical expenses and supporting any amount requested.  •Given Plaintiff is requesting general damages of $500,000, Plaintiff must submit competent evidence explaining and supporting the request for general damages.   No later than ____________, Plaintiff is to submit a new entry of default. Failure to do so may result in the imposition of sanctions, including monetary sanctions and/or dismissal. The OSC re-entry of default is continued to ________.
