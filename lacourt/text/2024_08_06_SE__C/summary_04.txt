And here is the English-text summary:

The Judge's tentative ruling is as follows:

* The motion to strike language relating to punitive damages is granted with 20 days leave to amend, as the plaintiff has not alleged sufficient facts to support a claim for punitive damages against Foldcraft Co.
* The motion to strike the prayer for attorney's fees is denied, as CCP § 1794 allows buyers of consumer goods to recover attorney's fees if they prevail in an action for breach of an express or implied warranty.
* The motion to strike the prayer for restitution and disgorgement damages is granted with 20 days leave to amend, as the plaintiff has not alleged any unjust enrichment.
* The motion to strike the prayer for consequential damages is granted with 20 days leave to amend, as the plaintiff did not have a contractual agreement with Foldcraft Co.
* The motion to strike the Fourth and Fifth Causes of Action is denied, as these causes of action are not adequately plead.
* The motion to strike the word "premises" is denied, as the location of the restaurant where the incident occurred can be ascertained through the discovery process.
* The motion to strike language in the first cause of action is denied, as the grounds for a motion to strike must appear on the face of the pleading, or from matter which the court may judicially notice.
* The motion to strike the words "lethal" and "violent" is denied, as they are not used exclusively for a claim for punitive damages.
* The request to comply with CRC, Rule 2.112 is denied, as Foldcraft Co. provides no authority that this issue is properly addressed in a motion to strike.
