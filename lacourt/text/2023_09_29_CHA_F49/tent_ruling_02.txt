Motion
to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories 
 
SUMMARY
OF ACTION
Plaintiff
Andrey Rossius sought out treatment for a broken middle finger with defendants
Providence Health & Services, et al. Plaintiff alleges negligently provided
medical care lead to necrosis of the left middle finger and a subsequent
amputation. Plaintiff alleges the loss of a finger now prevents Plaintiff from
engaging in work as a dental implantologist.
 
On
June 16, 2021, Plaintiff filed a complaint for Medical Malpractice. On April
21, 2023, the case was reassigned from Department 31 in the Mosk Courthouse to
Department 49. 
 
On
July 7, 2022, Plaintiff dismissed Providence Health & Services, Providence
St. Joseph Health, and Providence.
 
RULING: Granted.
Defendant,
James Lee, M.D. moves to compel responses to Special Interrogatories (set one)
served on plaintiff Andrey Rossius. 
 
Defendant represents service of form interrogatories on July
12, 2022. [Declaration of Elaine Sun, ¶ 3, Ex. A.] According to Defendant, at
the time of the filing of the motion, no responses were received. [Id., ¶ 4.] 
 
The motion is unopposed. The court electronic filing system
shows no reply filed at the time of the tentative ruling publication cutoff. Dr.
Lee also filed a notice of non-opposition. The unopposed motion is granted. Plaintiff
Andrey Rossius is ordered to serve verified responses to special
interrogatories (set one) without objections within ten days. (Code Civ. Proc.,
§ 2030.290, subd. (a-b).) No sanctions requested.
 
Motions
to compel on calendar for October 5, 6, 9, 10, 23, 24, and 25, 2023.
 
Defendant to provide notice.
