TENTATIVE RULING
 
The Joint
Stipulation and Order to Deny the Claim of Exemption is GRANTED.
 
DISCUSSION
 
 On November 9, 2022, Petitioner Meyer,
Olson, Lowy & Meyers, LLP filed a Petition to Compel Binding Arbitration
and for Court Appointment of an Arbitrator. Petitioner alleges it entered into
a written contract with Respondent Tina Shenk to perform various legal services
for her in connection with
her dissolution of marriage action. 
 
On December
12, 2023, a judgment was entered in favor of Petitioner in the amount of $66,
815.79. 
 
On February 2, 2024 Respondent filed a Claim of Exemption, set to
be heard on May 22, 2024.
 
On May 17, 2024, the parties filed a joint stipulation stating
that they reached an overall agreement regarding Respondent’s Claim of
Exemption. The parties stipulate:
1.    
 In order for Petitioner to
receive the money the Sheriff is presently holding, the Sheriff requires an
Order that the Claim of Exemption is denied, 
2.    
 Based on the above, the
parties Stipulate that the Claim of Exemption filed by Respondent on February
2, 2024 is denied and all funds being held by the Los Angeles County Sheriff
shall be distributed to Friedman & Friedman, attorneys for Petitioner.
 
The Court grants the parties joint stipulation. 
 
CONCLUSION

 
The
Joint Stipulation and Order to Deny the Claim of Exemption is GRANTED.
