(1:30 PM)
Case:                           PCB Bank v. Sung
Light Clothing Inc., et al. (24STCV07010)
  
TENTATIVE RULING:
 
Plaintiff PCB Bank’s UNOPPOSED
application for right to attach order with respect to defendant Sung Light
Clothing, Inc. is GRANTED. 
 
Plaintiff PCB Bank’s UNOPPOSED
application for right to attach order with respect to defendant Sung C. Lee is
GRANTED. 
 
Pursuant to CCP §484.090, with respect to both applications,
the Court finds:
1)     
the claim is one upon which attachment may be issued;
2)     
plaintiff has established the probable validity of the
claim;
3)     
attachment is not sought for any purpose other than
recovery on the claim;
4)     
the amount to be attached is greater than zero.
 
On February 16, 2021, plaintiff PCB Bank, formerly known as
Pacific City Bank, and defendant Sung Light Clothing, Inc. (“Sung Light”)
entered into a written Promissory Note (“Note”), whereby Sung Light borrowed
$300,000 to be repaid in monthly installments. (Yang Decl. ¶¶ 8, 9 & Ex.
1.) Defendant Sung C. Lee signed a Commercial Guaranty Agreement, whereby Lee
guarantied Sung Light’s performance under the Note. (Yang Decl. ¶ 14 & Ex.
2.) 
 
Sung Light failed to make the payment due on December 16,
2023 and all payments due thereafter. (Yang Decl. ¶ 24.) A balance of
$153,090.96, comprised of $149,742.61 in principal, $1,214.52 in late charges,
and $2,133.83 in interest remains outstanding. (Yang Decl. ¶ 26.) 
 
Based on the foregoing, the applications will be granted in
the amount of $153,845.96, which includes estimated costs of $755. (Yang Decl.
¶ 25.)
 
No writ shall issue unless plaintiff has posted a bond in
the amount of $10,000 for each defendant. (CCP § 489.220.) The Court will sign
the proposed orders, electronically received March 27, 2024.
