**Case Information**

* Case Number: 23STLC05063
* Hearing Date: November 9, 2023
* Department: 85
* Parties:
	+ Plaintiff: Keys Auto Finance
	+ Defendant: Ryan Torre

**Summary**

* The plaintiff, Keys Auto Finance, seeks a writ of possession against the defendant, Ryan Torre, to recover a 2007 BMW 3 Series 335i Sedan, VIN WBAVB73547VF50895 (Vehicle).
* The court has read and considered the moving papers and renders the following tentative decision: grant the application for a writ of possession.

**Facts**

* The Vehicle was leased to Torre by Keys on May 6, 2022, under a written Contract.
* Torre breached the Contract by failing to make the monthly lease payment due on March 6, 2023, and failing to maintain physical damage insurance for the Vehicle.
* The outstanding balance owed is $12,436.66.
* The Vehicle's market value is $5,570, and its current listing price is $6,520.

**Law**

* A writ of possession is a provisional remedy in a cause of action for claim and delivery, also known as replevin.
* The court may issue a writ of possession if the plaintiff has established the probable validity of their claim to possession of the property and the undertaking requirements of CCP section 515.010 are satisfied.

**Disputes**

* The main dispute is over the defendant's breach of the Contract and the plaintiff's right to possess the Vehicle.

**Acts**

* The plaintiff seeks a writ of possession for the Vehicle against the defendant.
* The defendant has breached the Contract by failing to make the monthly lease payment and failing to maintain physical damage insurance.

**Agreements**

* The Contract between Keys and Torre requires Torre to make 42 lease payments of $456.91 from June 6, 2022, thereafter.
* The Contract also requires Torre to have physical damage insurance that will cover any loss of or damage to the Vehicle.

**Laws**

* CCP section 512.010(a) requires the plaintiff to make a written application for a writ of possession.
* CCP section 515.010(a) requires the undertaking to be in an amount not less than twice the value of the defendant's interest in the property or in a greater amount.
* CCP section 512.060(b) requires the plaintiff to establish probable cause to believe that the property is located at the private place.

**Significant Statements**

* The court finds that the plaintiff has established the probable validity of their claim to possession of the Vehicle.
* The court grants the application for a writ of possession and requires the plaintiff to file a proposed order for writ of possession within two court days.
* The re-delivery undertaking will be $6,520, and the levying officer may enter the private address at 365 Cliff Dr Apt 208, Pasadena, CA 91107, and any commercial or public address.
