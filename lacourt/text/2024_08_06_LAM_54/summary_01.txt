**Case Information**

* Case Number: 21STCV11951
* Hearing Date: August 6, 2024
* Department: 54
* Court: Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles

**Parties**

* Plaintiffs: Cyrus V. Godfrey, et al.
* Defendants: Erin Lareau, Eric H. Ahola, and Sisu Construction, Inc.

**Facts**

* Plaintiffs filed a First Amended Complaint (FAC) against Defendants on March 15, 2022, alleging causes of action for nuisance, negligence, nuisance - parties, and covenants, conditions, and restrictions (CC&Rs).
* The FAC alleges that Defendants operate a construction business out of Plaintiffs' neighbor Lareau's residence and use that residence in a manner that causes excessive noise and disruption.
* On April 12, 2024, the Court ordered a jury trial on the first three causes of action and a bench trial on the fourth cause of action.
* On May 3, 2024, the jury rendered a verdict in favor of Defendants on the first three causes of action.

**Intentions**

* Defendants move for judgment on the pleadings and/or to dismiss the fourth cause of action.
* Plaintiffs seek to enforce the CC&Rs against Defendants and obtain an injunction to stop the alleged wrongful conduct.

**Disputes**

* The parties dispute whether the CC&Rs are still enforceable and whether Defendants Ahola and Sisu Construction, Inc. can be bound by the CC&Rs.

**Acts**

* The FAC alleges that Defendants breached the CC&Rs by engaging in a business on the Lareau property and constructing structures not built and maintained for private residence purposes.

**Agreements**

* The CC&Rs impose restrictions on the property owners and their heirs, personal representatives, or assigns.
* The FAC does not allege that Ahola and Sisu Corporation are owners of the property or heirs, personal representatives, or assigns of Lareau.

**Significant Statements**

* "Courts can take judicial notice of the existence, content, and authenticity of public records and other specified documents, but do not take judicial notice of the truth of the factual matters asserted in those documents." [Dominguez v. Bonta (2022) 87 Cal.App.5th 389, 400]
* "A party who is damaged by a violation of the CC&R's may seek money damages." [Cutujian v. Benedict Hills Estates Assn. (1996) 41 Cal.App.4th 1379, 1385]
* "Issues adjudicated in earlier phases of a bifurcated trial are binding in later phases of that trial and need not be relitigated." [Arntz Contracting Co. v. St. Paul Fire & Marine Ins. Co. (1996) 47 Cal.App.4th 464, 487]
* "Where legal claims are first tried by a jury and equitable claims later tried by a judge, the trial court must follow the jury's factual determinations on common issues of fact." [Hoopes v. Dolan (2008) 168 Cal.App.4th 146]
