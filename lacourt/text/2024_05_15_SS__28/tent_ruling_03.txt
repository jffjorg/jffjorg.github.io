 Case Number:  22STCV13624    Hearing Date:   May 15, 2024    Dept:  28 Having
considered the moving papers, the Court rules as follows. 
BACKGROUND 
On
April 25, 2022, Plaintiff Xavier Munoz Negreros (“Plaintiff”) filed this action
against Defendants Abdullah Hassan (“Hassan”), Ariell Ilunga (“Ilunga”), and
Does 1-50 for strict liability: dog attack. 
On
October 23, 2023, the Court dismissed the action without prejudice when
Plaintiff did not appear for trial. 
On
February 16, 2024, Plaintiff filed a motion to set aside the dismissal to be heard
on May 15, 2024. 
PARTY’S REQUEST 
Plaintiff
asks the Court to set aside the dismissal. 
LEGAL STANDARD 
          Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b),
provides in part: 
“The court may, upon any terms as may be just, relieve
a party or his or her legal representative from a judgment, dismissal, order,
or other proceeding taken against him or her through his or her mistake,
inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect. Application for this relief shall
be accompanied by a copy of the answer or other pleading proposed to be filed
therein, otherwise the application shall not be granted, and shall be made
within a reasonable time, in no case exceeding six months, after the judgment,
dismissal, order, or proceeding was taken. . . .” 
(Code Civ. Proc., § 473,
subd. (b).) 
DISCUSSION 
Citing
an attorney departure and a technology malfunction, Plaintiff seeks an order setting
aside the October 23, 2023 dismissal under the discretionary relief provision
of Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b). 
The Court
finds that the dismissal was the result of mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or
excusable neglect.  The Court grants the
motion. 
CONCLUSION 
The
Court GRANTS Plaintiff Xavier Munoz Negreros’s motion for relief from the October
23, 2023 dismissal.  The Court vacates
the dismissal.

Court sets an OSC Re: Failure to File Proof of Service of Summons and Complaint
