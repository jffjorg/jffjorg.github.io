            On 2/3/2023, Plaintiffs Gal Moshe
Faigel (Faigel) and Ronit Malamud (Malmud) (collectively, Plaintiffs) filed
suit against California Automobile Insurance Company aka Mercury Insurance,
alleging: (1) breach of insurance contract; and (2) breach of covenant of good
faith and fair dealing. 
 
            Now, Defendant moves to compel responses
from Faigel to its Form Interrogatories (Set One), Special Interrogatories (Set
One), and Requests for Production (RFPs) (Set One), and to have its Requests
for Admission (RFAs) deemed admitted as to Faigel. Defendant also seeks
monetary sanctions in connection to these motions. 
 
            Defendant also moves to compel
responses from Malmud to its Form Interrogatories (Set One), Special
Interrogatories (Set One), and Requests for Production (RFPs) (Set One), and to
have its Requests for Admission (RFAs) deemed admitted as to Faigel. Defendant
also seeks monetary sanctions in connection to these motions.
 
Discussion

 
Plaintiff
Gal Mose Faigel:
 
I.                  
Form
Interrogatories (Faigel) 
 
            On 724/2023, Defendant served
Plaintiff Faigel with Form Interrogatories. Despite granting Plaintiff an
extension to respond, to date, no response has been served.
 
            Plaintiff did not oppose this
motion, and thus has not set forth any substantial justification for the
failure to respond.
 
            Based on the foregoing, Defendant’s
motion to compel responses to its Form Interrogatories is granted. Faigel is
sanctioned, jointly and severally with counsel, $700 per motion. ($350/hr x 2
hr.) 
 
II.              
Special Interrogatories
(Faigel)

RFPs
(Faigel)

granting Plaintiff an extension to respond, to date, no response has been

            Plaintiff did not oppose this

IV.           
RFAs
(Faigel)
 
On 7/24/2023, Defendant served Plaintiff Faigel with RFAs. 
 
In opposition, Plaintiff indicated that she served responses on
5/7/2024. (CCP § 2033.280, subd. (c).) While this moots Defendant’s motion,
Plaintiff’s failure to timely response still necessitated this motion. As such,
sanctions are mandatory.  
 
            Based on the foregoing, Defendant’s
motion to have it RFAs deemed admitted is moot. Faigel is sanctioned, jointly
and severally with counsel, $700 per motion. ($350/hr x 2 hr.)
