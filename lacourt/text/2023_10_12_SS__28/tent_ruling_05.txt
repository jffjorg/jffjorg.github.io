 Case Number:  22STCV05333    Hearing Date:   October 12, 2023    Dept:  28 Having reviewed Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority’s unopposed Motions to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories, Special Interrogatories, and Request for Production of Documents, the Court rules as follows.On April 7, 2022, Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority served Form Interrogatories, Set No. One, Special Interrogatories, Set No. One, and Request for Production of Documents, Set No. One, on Plaintiff Larry Rixter.On January 23, 2023, Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority served Special Interrogatories, Set No. Two,Responses were due on May 18, 2023. Plaintiff Larry Rixter has failed to provide timely responses. Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority now moves to compel code-compliant responses.The Court grants the motions to compel responses to the form and special interrogatories pursuant to Code of Civ. Proc. § 2030.290(b) and orders Plaintiff Larry Rixter to provide verified responses to the interrogatories compliant with Code of Civ. Proc. §§ 2030.210 (a) and 2030.220 without objections by November 11, 2023. The Court grants the motion to compel responses to the request for production and production of items pursuant to Code of Civ. Proc. § 2031.300 and orders Plaintiff Larry Rixter to provide verified responses pursuant to Code of Civ. Proc. § 2031.210 and to produce the documents, electronically stored information, and/or other things requested without objections by November 11, 2023.Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority requests monetary sanctions totaling $2,250.00.The Court awards sanctions pursuant to Code of Civ. Proc. § 2023.030 (a), Code of Civ. Proc. § 2030.290 (c) and Code of Civ. Proc. § 2031.300 (c) in the amount of $750.00, calculated as follows:Defendant requests $1,250.00 in sanctions for the motion to compel interrogatories, and $1,000.00 in sanctions for the motion to compel request for production. 5 hours were spent on the motion regarding interrogatories, while 4 hours were spent on the request for production. The Court notes both motions are substantially similar, unopposed and set to be heard concurrently. The Court awards sanctions totaling $750.00 across both motions, based on 3 hours of attorney’s work.The Court also notes that the motion to compel interrogatories should have been filed as three separate motions, one for each piece of discovery.The sanctions total $750.00 and are payable by Plaintiff Larry Rixter and his counsel by November 11, 2023.Moving party is to give notice of this ruling.
