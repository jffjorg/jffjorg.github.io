And here is a summary of the judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The court has reviewed the settlement and consent judgment between APS&EE, LLC (the plaintiff) and Ross Stores, Inc. (the defendant) regarding the sale of a nonstick frying pan that allegedly releases Perfluorooctanoic Acid (PFOA), a known carcinogen and reproductive toxicity substance. The plaintiff alleges that Ross Stores, Inc. violated Health & Safety Code section 25249.6 by selling the pan without proper warning on the label in compliance with Proposition 65.

The settlement provides for injunctive relief, warning requirements, payment of penalties and attorney fees. The court finds that the penalty of $4,000.00 is reasonable under the criteria and that the fees of $28,000.00 are reasonable. The court grants the motion for entry of the consent judgment and orders the defendant to provide a warning on the label and pay the civil penalty.

The court also notes that the settlement contains a general release, which the court finds is not a violation of the law. The non-jury trial remains set for April 1, 2025.
