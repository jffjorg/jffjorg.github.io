A tentative ruling has been made in the case of Lazy Dog Restaurants, LLC v. Slaters Restaurant Group, Inc., et al. (Case Number 24TRCV01324). The plaintiff, Lazy Dog Restaurants, LLC, has filed an application for a preliminary injunction against the defendant, Slaters Restaurant Group, Inc., et al., alleging that the defendant's construction work has caused excessive noise and water and debris intrusion onto its premises.

The court has granted the application for a preliminary injunction, in part. The defendant is ordered to prevent water intrusion or debris from emanating from its premises onto the premises of the plaintiff. The court has also ordered the plaintiff to post a bond in the sum of $35,000.00.

The court's decision is based on its finding that the plaintiff has adequately met its burden to demonstrate a likelihood that it will prevail on the merits of its case at trial. The court has also considered the interim harm that the plaintiff may suffer if an injunction is denied, compared to the harm that the defendant is likely to suffer if an injunction is granted.

The court has declined to order the defendant to stop operating its restaurant, as this would be potentially harmful to the defendant and would disrupt the status quo. The court has also noted that the resolution of this dispute will turn greatly on the credibility of the parties, documentary evidence, and the availability of competent third-party witnesses.
