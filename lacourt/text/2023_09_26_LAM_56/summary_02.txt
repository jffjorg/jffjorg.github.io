The tentative ruling is regarding a motion to compel compliance with a court order. The court has considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers. The case involves the sale of an allegedly defective vehicle manufactured by American Honda Motor Co., Inc. (Defendant). The plaintiff, Clifford DeJong, filed a motion to compel compliance with the court's May 8, 2023 order, which directed the Defendant to produce further responses to requests for production (RFPs) and provide statements of compliance. The Defendant served unverified supplemental responses, and the Plaintiff's counsel sent a meet and confer letter. The court observes that the Plaintiff's meet and confer effort was negligible. The court grants the motion in part, ordering the Defendant to produce any unproduced supplemental documents and provide statements of compliance that specify if/what documents have been withheld. The court denies the Plaintiff's and Defendant's requests for monetary sanctions.
