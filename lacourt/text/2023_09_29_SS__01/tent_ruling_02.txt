


[1]           A residential hotel is “any building
containing six or more guestrooms or efficiency units, as defined by Section
17958.1 [of the Health and Safety Code], intended or designed to be used, or
which are used, rented, or hired out, to be occupied, or which are occupied,
for sleeping purposes by guests, which is also the primary residence of those
guests, but does not mean any building containing six or more guestrooms or
efficiency units, as defined by Section 17958.1, which is primarily used by
transient guests who do not occupy that building as their primary residence.”  (Health & Saf. Code § 50519(b)(1).)
