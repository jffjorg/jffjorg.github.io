And here is a summary of the Judge's tentative ruling in English text:

The Judge is considering a motion by Defendant Dal-Tile Distribution, LLC to compel compliance with a deposition subpoena for production of documents by Third-Party Astonish, Inc. The Plaintiff, Jaime Blanco Maciel, alleges that he developed silicosis and other conditions due to his work as a fabricator, cutter, polisher, and installer of stone countertops. The Defendant subpoenaed records from Astonish, but Astonish failed to produce any responsive documents despite several timeline extensions. The Court finds good cause and grants the motion to compel, assuming that the Defendant can establish that it served the motion on Astonish. The Court also grants sanctions totaling $2,464.20, based on 10.5 hours of attorneys' work and the filing fee. The motion to compel is granted, and Astonish is ordered to produce the requested records within 30 days of the hearing. The Defendant is also ordered to pay sanctions within 30 days of the hearing.
