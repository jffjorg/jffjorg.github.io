**Issues**

**Facts**

* Plaintiff leased a 2020 Hyundai Tucson from KEYES, an authorized dealership, in December 2019.
* The lease agreement included an arbitration provision.
* The vehicle was stolen and declared a total loss by the insurance company.
* Plaintiff was informed that the pay-off amount was $24,905.00, exceeding the residual value.
* Plaintiff challenges the validity of Section 25 of the lease agreement, which altered the calculations and expected payments.

**Intentions**

* The defendants seek to compel arbitration of the dispute.
* The plaintiff seeks to litigate the dispute in court.

**Disputes**

* Whether an arbitration agreement exists between the parties.
* Whether the plaintiff assented to the arbitration agreement.
* Whether the arbitration agreement is enforceable.
* Whether the class waiver clause is enforceable.

**Acts**

* Plaintiff signed the lease agreement with the arbitration provision.
* Defendant did not explicitly cross-reference Section 25 of the lease agreement.

**Agreements**

* Lease agreement with arbitration provision.
* Arbitration provision states that disputes will be resolved through arbitration rather than litigation.

* Federal Arbitration Act (FAA)
* California law regarding arbitration agreements
