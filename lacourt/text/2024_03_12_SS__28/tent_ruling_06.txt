BACKGROUND 
On
October 2, 2023, Plaintiffs Hayden Ramirez, Emily Ramirez (“Emily Ramirez”), a
minor, by and through her guardian ad litem Erica Ramirez, Ben Ramirez (“Ben
Ramirez”), a minor, by and through his guardian ad litem Erica Ramirez, Arthur
Ramirez, and Guadalupe Ramirez filed this action against Defendants MNJ
Trucking, Inc., Ricardo Jose Andres, and Does 1-50 for motor
vehicle tort and general negligence. 
On
November 16, 2023, Plaintiffs filed a first amended complaint. 
On
January 5, 2024, Petitioner Erica Rodriguez (“Petitioner”) filed petitions to approve minors’
compromises to be heard on March 12, 2024.  On February 9, 2024, Petitioner filed amended
petitions. 
Trial
is scheduled for April 1, 2025. 
PETITIONER’S REQUESTS 
Petitioner
asks the Court to approve the compromises of the pending claims of Plaintiffs
Emily Ramirez and Ben Ramirez. 
LEGAL STANDARD 
“‘[W]ithout
trial court approval of the proposed compromise of the ward’s claim, the
settlement cannot be valid. [Citation.] [¶] Nor is the settlement binding [on
the minor] until it is endorsed by the trial court.’” (Pearson v. Superior
Court (2012) 202 Cal.App.4th 1333, 1338; see Prob. Code, §§ 3500, 3600, et
seq.; Code Civ. Proc., § 372.) 
To
obtain court approval of the settlement of a minor’s claims, the petitioner
must file a complete and “verified petition for approval of the settlement and
must disclose ‘all information that has any bearing upon the reasonableness of
the compromise.’ [Citations.]” (Barnes v. Western Heritage Ins. Co.
(2013) 217 Cal.App.4th 249, 256; see Cal. Rules of Court, rule 7.950.)
