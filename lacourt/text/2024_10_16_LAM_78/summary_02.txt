* Defendant(s): GOODSON REAL ESTATE CO., et al.

**Key Points**

* The Court sustains Hajoca's demurrer without leave to amend to the third and sixth causes of action for fraud and violation of B&P §17200.
* The Court sustains the demurrer to the eighth cause of action for accounting without leave to amend.
* The motion to strike punitive damages is moot.

**Facts**

* Hajoca furnished materials to Express and Mullem and opened an unauthorized charge account to GRE.
* Hajoca issued a Preliminary Notice in the amount of $150,000 for the materials, and fabricated/doctored invoices, delivery dates, and shipment dates.
* Cross-Complainants allege they were forced to rely on the material representations made by Hajoca.

**Legal Issues**

* The Court finds that Cross-Complainants have not pled with particularity a fraud claim against Hajoca.
* The Court holds that the UCL claims are insufficient because Cross-Complainants do not seek equitable relief and do not plead that money or property was ever obtained by Hajoca.
* The Court finds that the demurrer to the third cause of action for fraud is sustained without leave to amend.

**Orders**

* Hajoca's demurrer to the third and sixth causes of action is sustained without leave to amend.
* The motion to strike punitive damages is moot.
* The Court orders that notice be given to the parties.

**Intention**

* The Court intends to sustain Hajoca's demurrer without leave to amend and finds that the motion to strike punitive damages is moot.

**Dispute**

* The dispute centers on the sufficiency of the pleadings and the allegations of fraud and unfair business practices.

**Agreements**

* The parties are encouraged to meet and confer after reading this tentative ruling to see if they can reach an agreement.
