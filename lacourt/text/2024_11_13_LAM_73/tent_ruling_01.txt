I. BACKGROUND
 
On May 12,
2021, Plaintiff Claidin Davidoff the instant action against Defendants Ryan
Orojenilak and Metco Group, Inc. (collectively “Defendants”). Plaintiff filed a
First Amended Complaint alleging the following causes of action:
1.      Breach
of Partnership Agreement
2.      Abuse
of Legal Process
3.      Intentional
Infliction of Emotional Distress
4.      Fraud
5.      Unjust
Enrichment
6.      Promissory
Estoppel
 
The FAC alleges that in or around April 2019, Plaintiff and
Defendants, entered into an oral agreement to start a partnership or in the
alternative a joint venture in which the Parties would obtain and perform
construction work together and share the profits. (FAC, ¶ 12.) Both parties agreed that the profit from each
construction work would be divided equally amongst themselves. (FAC, ¶ 12.) Plaintiff alleges that Defendants did not
perform according to the Partnership Agreement and Plaintiff did not receive
any profit from any of the four projects that he completed with Defendants.
(FAC, ¶ 19.) The FAC further alleges that after
multiple attempts by Plaintiff to recover his share of the profits from
Defendants, Defendants informed Plaintiff that he may take Defendants' tools
stored in a storage unit, as a form of payment towards Plaintiff. (FAC, ¶ 21.) However, Defendants contacted the police and
made a false report that Plaintiff was stealing the tools from Defendants and
Plaintiff was arrested and charged. (FAC, ¶¶ 25-28.)
Plaintiff alleges Metco Group, Inc., is a California Corporation,
registered to Orojenilak, who is its sole owner. (FAC, ¶¶ 3-4.)
 
On
March 5, 2024, Plaintiff filed a request for entry of default judgment against
Defendant Orojenilak. Plaintiff sought
judgment in the total amount of $73,933.53, consisting of the amount demanded
in the complaint of $51,030.53, $22,288.00 in interest, and $615.00 in costs. On May 29, 2024, the Court granted
Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment
against Defendant Orojenilak.
 
On October 14, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant request
for entry of default judgment against Defendant Metco Group, Inc. 
 
II.DISCUSSION
 
A.     
Legal Standard
 
CCP § 585 permits entry of a judgment after a Defendant has
failed to timely answer after being properly served.  A party seeking
judgment on the default by the Court must file a Request for Court Judgment,
and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible
evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as
necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) a proposed form of
judgment; (6) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought;
(7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an
application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of
grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for
attorneys’ fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. 
(CRC Rule 3.1800.)

Here,
Plaintiff has complied with all requirements for a default judgment. Plaintiff
provided a declaration summarizing the case and attesting to his damages. Plaintiff
also submitted a declaration by his attorney, Armen Shaghzo, in support of the
interest calculation, attorney’s fees and requested costs. 
 
Since
Plaintiff has complied with the requirements, the Court GRANTS Plaintiff’s
request for default judgment against Defendant Metco Group, Inc.

entry of default judgment against Defendant Metco Group, Inc. is GRANTED.
