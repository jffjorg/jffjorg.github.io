Case No.: 20STCV05566 [CONSOLIDATED WITH CASE NO. 20STCV15361] Hearing Date: February 8, 2024   [TENTATIVE] RULING RE: PLAINTIFF THE ESTATE OF TRINA ALLEN’S MOTION TO DEEM REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION, SET ONE ADMITTED AS TO DEFENDANT SUPERIOR LOAN SERVICING AND IMPOSING MONETARY SANCTIONS Plaintiff The Estate of Trina Allen’s Motion to Deem RFAs Admitted as to Defendant Superior Loan Servicing and Imposing Monetary Sanctions is GRANTED.  Sanctions are imposed jointly and severally on Defendant Superior Loan Servicing and its Counsel of record in the amount of $677.50Sanctions are payable within 10 days after the date of this order.Moving party to provide notice and to file proof of service of such notice within five court days after the date of this order.  FACTUAL BACKGROUNDThis is an action for declaratory relief, contractual breach of good faith and fair dealing, an accounting, unfair business practices, rescission, breach of fiduciary duty, and injunctive relief arising from a loan Plaintiff entered into in April 2018.  Plaintiff alleges Defendants illegally, deceptively, or unjustly qualified Plaintiff for a loan which Defendants knew or should have known she would not normally qualify for or afford.On December 4, 2023, Plaintiff The Estate of Trina Allen filed the instant Motion to Deem RFAs Admitted against Superior Loan Servicing.  No opposition has been filed.DISCUSSIONMOTION TO DEEM RFAs ADMITTED A.  Legal StandardCCP §2033.280If a party to whom requests for admission are directed fails to serve a timely response, the following rules apply:(a) The party to whom the requests for admission are directed waives any objection to the requests, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product under Chapter 4 (commencing with Section 2018.010). The court, on motion, may relieve that party from this waiver on its determination that both of the following conditions are satisfied: (1) The party has subsequently served a response that is in substantial compliance with Sections 2033.210, 2033.220, and 2033.230. (2) The party's failure to serve a timely response was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect.(b) The requesting party may move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted, as well as for a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010).(c) The court shall make this order, unless it finds that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220. It is mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion. B.  Estate’s Requests for Admission properly deemed admitted against Defendant Superior Loan Servicing On September 15, 2023, Plaintiff served RFAs on Defendant Superior Loan Servicing.  (Motion, Chavez Dec., ¶3.)  No responses to the RFAs were ever received.  (Id. at ¶5.)  Pursuant to CCP §2033.280, the RFAs are properly deemed admitted against Superior Loan Servicing based on its failure to serve any responses to the RFAs.   C.  Estate entitled to mandatory sanctions As the prevailing party, Estate is entitled to mandatory sanctions pursuant to CCP §2033.280(c).  Estate requests sanctions against Defendant and its counsel in the amount of $677.50 based on 1.75 hours @ $350/hr and a $65 filing fee.  The amount is reasonable.  CONCLUSIONAccordingly, Plaintiff The Estate of Trina Allen’s Motion to Deem RFAs Admitted as to Defendant Superior Loan Servicing and Imposing Monetary Sanctions is GRANTED.  Defendant Superior Loan Servicing and its counsel are ordered to pay sanctions in the amount of $677.50.  DATED:  February 8, 2024  ________________________________                                                                Hon.
