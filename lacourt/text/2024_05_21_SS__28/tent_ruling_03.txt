 Case Number:  21STCV33238    Hearing Date:   May 21, 2024    Dept:  28 Having
considered the petitioning papers, the Court rules as follows.  
BACKGROUND 
On
September 8, 2021, Plaintiffs Sintia Marvely Garcia Samayon (“Garcia”) and Luna
Marvella Velasquez (“Luna Velasquez”) filed this action against Defendants Jose
Elias Jaime (“Defendant”) and Does 1-20 for negligence. 
On
March 8, 2023, the Court dismissed the action without prejudice.  On May 25, 2023, the Court granted
Plaintiffs’ motion to vacate the dismissal. 
On
July 5, 2023, Garcia filed a notice of settlement. 
On
September 20, 2023, the Court dismissed Garcia’s action with prejudice at
Garcia’s request. 
On April
24, 2024, Petitioner Jose Velasquez (“Petitioner”) filed a petition for
expedited approval of the compromise of the claims of minor Luna Velasquez (“Claimant”). 
No
trial date is currently scheduled. 
PETITIONER’S REQUEST 
Petitioner
asks the Court for expedited approval of the compromise of Claimant’s action. 
LEGAL STANDARD 
“‘[W]ithout
trial court approval of the proposed compromise of the ward’s claim, the
settlement cannot be valid. [Citation.] [¶] Nor is the settlement binding [on
the minor] until it is endorsed by the trial court.’” (Pearson v. Superior
Court (2012) 202 Cal.App.4th 1333, 1338; see Prob. Code, §§ 3500, 3600, et
seq.; Code Civ. Proc., § 372.) 
To
obtain court approval of the settlement of a minor’s claims, the petitioner
must file a complete and “verified petition for approval of the settlement and
must disclose ‘all information that has any bearing upon the reasonableness of
the compromise.’ [Citations.]” (Barnes v. Western Heritage Ins. Co.
(2013) 217 Cal.App.4th 249, 256; see Cal. Rules of Court, rule 7.950.) 
DISCUSSION      
The
petition contains the following information about the proposed settlement: 
PROPOSED
SETTLEMENT AMOUNT: $7,000.00 INJURIES:
cognitive issues, speech delay, “other specified trauma and stressor related
disorder” TOTAL
MEDICAL EXPENSES: $2,379.98 MEDICAL
EXPENSES TO BE REIMBURSED FROM SETTLEMENT PROCEEDS: $1,154.73 COSTS:
$585.85 ATTORNEY’S
FEES: $1,750.00, which is 25% of the gross settlement BALANCE
OF PROCEEDS: $3,509.42 
DISCUSSION: 
Section
13a(1) of the petition states that Claimant’s total medical expenses were $2,379.98. 
Section 13a(3) of the petition states that $1,225.25 of the medical expenses
were the subject of negotiated, statutory, or contractual reductions.  Section 13a(4) of the petition states that
the remaining $1,154.73 in medical expenses will be paid or reimbursed
from the settlement proceeds. 
Section
13c(2)(b) of the petition states that Medi-Cal has agreed to accept $154.73 in
reimbursement for its payment of medical expenses.  The attached letter from the Department of
Health Care Services states that Medi-Cal paid $206.30 in benefits and has
agreed to accept $154.73 in reimbursement. 
Attachment
13a states: “DHCS Final Lien (For TLC Pediatrics) bill is $154.73. GRC AC &
CS Advanced Cognitive and Clinical Solutions original bill is $2,225.25. Based
on the Settlement Agreement, current adjusted fee owed is $1,000.00.” 
Based
on the information set forth above, the Court grants the petition.  Petitioner asks that the net settlement
proceeds ($3,509.42) be deposited in an insured account at US Bank, 4350 Pacific
Coast, Torrance, CA 90505, subject to withdrawal only on authorization of the Court.
 The Court grants the request. 
CONCLUSION 
The
Court GRANTS the petition for expedited
approval of the compromise of Plaintiff Luna Velasquez’s action filed by
Petitioner Jose Velasquez on April 24, 2024. 
The Court orders that the net settlement amount be deposited in an
insured account at US Bank, 4350 Pacific
Coast, Torrance, CA 90505, subject to withdrawal only on authorization of the Court.

Court sets an OSC Re: Distribution of Net Settlement on July 22, 2024, at 8:30

The
Court sets an OSC Re: Dismissal of Case (Settlement) on July 22, 2024, at 8:30
a.m., in Department 28 of the Spring Street Courthouse.
