The Judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Clark Crozer and Karen Crozer vs. General Motors, LLC. The case arises from the Plaintiffs' purchase of a 2022 Chevrolet Bolt that allegedly has a defect. The Plaintiffs filed a Complaint against the Defendant, alleging a cause of action for a violation of the Song-Beverly Act.

The Defendant failed to produce its Person Most Qualified (PMQ) for deposition and failed to comply with the Court's orders. The Plaintiffs filed a motion to compel deposition and for monetary sanctions against the Defendant in the amount of $2,970.00. The Defendant argued that the Plaintiffs failed to meet and confer in good faith and that the requested materials are not relevant or discoverable.

The Judge found that the Defendant failed to comply with the Court's orders and ordered the Defendant to produce its PMQ for deposition within the next 30 days. The Judge also awarded sanctions in the amount of $2,970 to the Plaintiffs' counsel.

The Court's ruling is as follows: (1) the Plaintiffs' motion to compel deposition is granted; (2) the Defendant must produce its PMQ for deposition within the next 30 days; and (3) the Defendant and Defendant's counsel of record are ordered to pay sanctions in the amount of $2,970 to Plaintiffs' counsel within the next 30 days.
