The Court has granted the motion filed by Plaintiff Dylan Blaze Huerta for an order permitting the filing of an amended memorandum of costs and entering a new judgment. The motion is based on Plaintiff's claim that his counsel's mistake or excusable neglect resulted in the failure to submit a claim for prejudgment interest. The Court has found that counsel's mistake was inadvertent and excusable, and has exercised its discretion to grant relief under Code of Civil Procedure section 473(b). The Court has vacated the portion of its September 24, 2024 order denying prejudgment interest and has ordered Plaintiff to file a supplemental memorandum within three days of this order. Defendant may proceed to challenge prejudgment interest via a second motion to tax, if it chooses to do so, within the statutorily prescribed time following Plaintiff's supplemental filing.
