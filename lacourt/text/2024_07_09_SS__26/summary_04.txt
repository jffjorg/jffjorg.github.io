And here is the English-text summary:

This tentative ruling grants Plaintiff I.L.W.U. Credit Union's Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings against Defendant Andre T. Thomas. The motion is based on Plaintiff's Requests for Admissions, which effectively establish the truth of the allegations in the Complaint. The admissions demonstrate that Defendant executed the September 29, 2021, Loanliner Loan and Security Agreements and failed to make installment payments, resulting in an unpaid principal balance of $15,190.47. Defendant also failed to make payments on the Visa Credit Card Account, resulting in an amount due of $1,041.79. The Court takes judicial notice of the matters deemed admitted in Plaintiff's Motion to Deem Requests for Admission, Set One, Admitted, and concludes that Plaintiff has demonstrated that it served Defendant with Requests for Admissions that establish the truth of the allegations in the Complaint. As a result, Plaintiff is entitled to judgment as a matter of law, and Plaintiff is to file a proposed judgment within 20 days of this order.
