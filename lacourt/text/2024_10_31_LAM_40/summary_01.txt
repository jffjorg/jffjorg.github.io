And here is a summary of the tentative ruling in English text:

The Court has issued a tentative ruling on two motions to compel discovery responses: one for Form Interrogatories, Set One, and one for Request for Production of Documents, Set One. The Plaintiff, Pamela Cook, has alleged claims of FEHA Race/Color/National Origin/Ancestry Discrimination, FEHA Disability Discrimination, and other claims against the Defendant, University of Southern California, Fatima Manuao, Lorena (Last Name Unknown), and Does 1 to 100 inclusive.

The Defendant failed to serve timely responses to the discovery requests, and the Court has granted the Plaintiff's motions to compel discovery responses. The Defendant is ordered to serve fully compliant responses without objections within 20 days.

Additionally, the Court has granted the Plaintiff's request for sanctions against the Defendant and its counsel, John Giovannone, in the amount of $2,310.00 for the motion to compel discovery responses related to Form Interrogatories, and in the reduced amount of $1,410.00 for the motion to compel discovery responses related to Request for Production of Documents.

The Court has also ordered the Defendant to serve fully compliant responses without objections to the Request for Production of Documents, as well as the responsive documents, within 20 days.
