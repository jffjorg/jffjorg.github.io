 Case Number:  22STCV09437    Hearing Date:   October 4, 2023    Dept:  31 DEPT:   31 OSC DATE:  10/04/2023 CASE NAME/NUMBER:  22STCV09437 SHEENA PAHOYO HERNANDEZ vs SHLOMO KESHET REQUEST FOR COURT JUDGMENT AGAINST [DEFAULTING PARTY]:  SHLOMO KESHET RECOMMENDATION:    DENY for reasons stated below.  TENTATIVE  The request for default judgment submitted on August 29, 2023 is DENIED for the following reasons:  On JUD-100, Item 5(a), Plaintiff must state the name of defendant against whom judgment is to be entered against. This portion is blank. Plaintiff must also checkmark “Damages” on Item 6(a)(1).   On CIV-100, Item 1(d), Plaintiff must submit the name of the defendant whom Plaintiff is requesting court judgment against. Instead, Plaintiff placed Plaintiff’s own name.   Plaintiff must dismiss all other parties against whom Plaintiff is not seeking judgment, which includes all unnamed Doe defendants. (CRC 3.1800(a)(7).)  Plaintiff’s declaration in support of her application for default judgment is not signed under penalty of perjury, under the laws of the State of California, that her statements are true and correct. A penalty of perjury clause must be included above the signature block. Furthermore, Plaintiff does not substantiate her claim for damages with any support. Plaintiff must submit authenticated and admissible evidence in support of the damages sought. (CRC 3.1800(a)(2).)  Plaintiff’s attorney necessarily does not have personal knowledge of the event as a percipient witness, and may not attest to Plaintiff’s injuries of the incident. Furthermore, Plaintiff’s counsel’s declaration is not signed under penalty of perjury. The medical records attached to Plaintiff’s attorney’s declaration are not authenticated; authenticated records should be attached to Plaintiff’s declaration. Plaintiff must introduce admissible prima facie evidence of damages. (Johnson v. Stanhiser (1999) 72 Cal.App.4th 357.)  No later than ______________, Plaintiff is to submit a new default judgment package correcting these defects.  Failure to do so may result in the imposition of sanctions, including monetary sanctions and/or dismissal. The OSC re entry of default judgment is continued to ___________.
