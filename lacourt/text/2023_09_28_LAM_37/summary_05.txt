**Case Number:** 22STCV19061
**Hearing Date:** September 28, 2023
**Dept:** 37

**Case Summary:**

The case concerns a dispute between Plaintiff VJ Hirsch and Defendant Los Angeles Unified School District (LAUSD) regarding alleged discrimination, harassment, and retaliation in violation of the Fair Employment and Housing Act (FEHA). Plaintiff alleges that LAUSD failed to provide reasonable accommodations for his disabilities, engaged in a hostile work environment, and retaliated against him for exercising his rights under the FEHA.

**Tentative Ruling:**

The Court has reviewed the pleadings and supporting documents and hereby issues the following tentative ruling:

* The demurrer to the first cause of action (discrimination in violation of FEHA) is sustained without leave to amend.
* The demurrer to the second cause of action (harassment in violation of FEHA) is sustained without leave to amend.
* The demurrer to the third cause of action (retaliation in violation of FEHA) is sustained without leave to amend.
* The demurrer to the fourth cause of action (failure to prevent discrimination, harassment, and retaliation in violation of FEHA) is sustained without leave to amend.
* The demurrer to the fifth and sixth causes of action (failure to make reasonable accommodations and failure to engage in the interactive process in violation of FEHA) is denied.

**Background:**

Plaintiff, a math teacher, alleges that LAUSD discriminated against him based on his disabilities, harassed him, and retaliated against him for exercising his rights under the FEHA. Plaintiff alleges that he requested reasonable accommodations, including accommodations for his disabilities, but LAUSD failed to provide them.

**Key Issues:**

1. Whether Plaintiff's claims are time-barred based on the statute of limitations.
2. Whether Plaintiff has adequately alleged discrimination, harassment, and retaliation in violation of the FEHA.
3. Whether LAUSD engaged in a hostile work environment and whether Plaintiff was subjected to adverse employment actions.

**Ruling:**

The Court finds that:

* Plaintiff's claims based on the denial of accommodations in October 2019 are time-barred.
* Plaintiff has failed to adequately allege discrimination, harassment, and retaliation in violation of the FEHA.
* LAUSD did not engage in a hostile work environment and did not subject Plaintiff to adverse employment actions.

**Conclusion:**

The Court sustains the demurrer to the first, second, third, and fourth causes of action and denies the demurrer to the fifth and sixth causes of action. The case is set for a status conference on [insert date] to discuss the next steps.

**Significant Statements:**

* "To establish a prima facie case of discrimination under the FEHA, a plaintiff must show that he was a member of a protected class, he was qualified for the position he sought or was performing competently in the position he held, he suffered an adverse employment action, and some other circumstance suggests discriminatory motive."
* "To establish a prima facie case of harassment, a plaintiff must show that he was an employee, he was subjected to unwanted harassing conduct based on his protected status, the harassing conduct was severe or pervasive, a reasonable person in the employee's circumstances would have considered the work environment to be hostile or abusive, and the employee considered the work environment to be hostile or abusive."
