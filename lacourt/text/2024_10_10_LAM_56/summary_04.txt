**Case Information**

* Case Number: 24STCV10436
* Hearing Date: October 10, 2024
* Department: 56

**Parties**

* Plaintiff: Alejandro Melendrez
* Defendant: Joseph Melendrez, Does 1 to 10, et al.

**Facts**

* The case involves an intra-family dispute over the ownership of a piece of real property.
* Plaintiff Alejandro Melendrez alleges that he and Defendant Joseph Melendrez entered into several oral contracts regarding the property, which were breached.
* The alleged contracts include an agreement to purchase a residence, an agreement to transfer ownership of the property, and an agreement to modify a mortgage.
* Defendant demurs to all three causes of action, arguing that they are barred by the statute of limitations, the statute of frauds, and that the allegations in the complaint conflict with attached exhibits.

**Disputes**

* Statute of Limitations: Defendant argues that the statute of limitations bars all three causes of action.
* Statute of Frauds: Defendant argues that the alleged oral contracts are unenforceable because they concern the ownership of real property.
* Conflict between Complaint and Exhibits: Defendant argues that the allegations in the complaint conflict with the attached exhibits, which evidence the written Grant Deeds that provide for Defendant's ownership.

**Acts**

* Defendant demurs to all three causes of action.
* Plaintiff has not pled facts sufficient to state a cause of action for fraud.
* The Court has found that all three causes of action are barred by the statute of limitations and the statute of frauds.

**Intentions**

* Defendant intends to sustain the demurrer to all three causes of action.
* Plaintiff intends to assert his claims despite the bar by the statute of limitations and the statute of frauds.

**Money**

* Not applicable

**Roles**

* Judge: Hon. Holly J. Fujie
* Parties: Alejandro Melendrez (Plaintiff) and Joseph Melendrez (Defendant)

**Significant Statements**

* "The Court agrees that Plaintiff's allegations of oral agreements contradict the attached exhibits which evidence the written Grant Deeds which provide for Defendant's ownership."
* "The Court finds that in addition to constructive trust being barred as a cause of action due to the statute of frauds, it is also barred due to the statute of limitations."
* "The Court sustains Defendant's demurrer as to its entirety for all three causes of action, WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND."
