 Case Number:  24STLC04501    Hearing Date:   December 4, 2024    Dept:  26 Gupta
v. DiSalvo, et al.TENTATIVE RULING:
Plaintiff Sushant Gupta’s Motion
to Recuse Judicial Officer is DENIED.
ANALYSIS:
On June 21, 2024, Plaintiff Sushant Gupta (“Plaintiff”) filed the Complaint in this action against Defendants
Lee Ann DiSalvo (“Defendant DiSalvo”), Intuitive Healing Institute, LLC
(“Defendant IHI”), and Kristen Lahey (“Defendant
Lahey”). On August 7, 2024, Defendant Lahey filed a Motion to Deem
Plaintiff a Vexatious Litigant. Defendants DiSalvo and IHI filed their Motion
to Deem Plaintiff a Vexatious Litigant on September 4, 2024. 
On
October 1, 2024, Plaintiff voluntarily dismissed the action without prejudice. Plaintiff
filed the instant noticed motion to recuse judicial officer on October 7, 2024.
The Court took the Motions to Deem Plaintiff a Vexatious Litigant under
submission and then issued a ruling granting the Motions on October 14, 2024.
(Minute Order, 10/02/24.)
Plaintiff
filed a preemptory challenge to judicial officer pursuant to Code of Civil
Procedure section 170.6, which was denied by the Court on October 15, 2024. (Minute
Order, 10/15/24.) On November 25, 2024, the action was assigned to Judge Cindy
Pánuco.
In
light of the Court’s ruling on Plaintiff’s preemptory challenge to judicial
officer pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 170.6, which the Court
previously denied, and the assignment of the action to a new judicial officer
rendering the requested relief moot, the instant Motion to Recuse Judicial
Officer is DENIED.
