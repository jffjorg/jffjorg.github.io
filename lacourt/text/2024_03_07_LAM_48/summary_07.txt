The judge has issued a tentative ruling in the case of Jay Import Company, Inc. v. Pixior, LLC, et al. (Case No. 23STCV26971). The dispute arises from a Warehousing, Distribution & E-Commerce Fulfilment Service Agreement between the Plaintiff and Pixior, LLC. The agreement contains an arbitration clause and specifies that the parties have waived long-term storage fees.

The Plaintiff seeks to confirm a partial final award issued by an arbitrator, which prohibits Pixior from enforcing its warehouse lien and requires Pixior to allow the Plaintiff to retrieve its goods. The Defendant opposes confirmation, arguing that the award is not a proper "award" under California Code of Civil Procedure (CCP) § 1283.4, and that the arbitrator erred in finding irreparable harm and waiver of long-term storage fees.

The judge has granted the Plaintiff's petition to confirm the partial final award and denied the Defendant's motion to compel arbitration. The Court has also ordered a stay of proceedings pending arbitration.
