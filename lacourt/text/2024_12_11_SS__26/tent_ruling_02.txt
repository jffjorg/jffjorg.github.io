 Case Number:  23STLC03267    Hearing Date:   December 11, 2024    Dept:  26 Kim v. Perez et al.,TENTATIVE RULING:Ruling to depend on what happens at the hearing.ANALYSIS:On May 18, 2023, Plaintiff Don Kim (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against Defendant Jake Perez (“Defendant”). Plaintiff filed proof of personal service of the Summons and Complaint on September 20, 2023. Following Defendant’s failure to file a responsive pleading, the Court entered their default on November 22, 2023 and default judgment on December 7, 2023.Defendant filed the first Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment (“the first Motion”) on June 12, 2024. Plaintiff filed an opposition on August 2, 2024 and Plaintiff replied on August 8, 2024. Plaintiff filed a supplemental declaration on August 13, 2024. At the hearing on August 15, 2024, the Court took the first Motion under submission following oral argument from Plaintiff. (Minute Order, 08/15/24.) Thereafter, the Court denied the first Motion. (Ibid.)On August 19, 2024, Defendant filed the Second Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment(“the Renewed Motion”). Plaintiff filed an opposition on September 3, 2024 and Defendant replied on September 9, 2024. At the hearing on September 16, 2024, the Court continued the matter for further consideration of the declaration filed on August 19, 2024 and whether the requirements for a renewed motion have been satisfied. (Minute Order, 09/16/24.) The Renewed Motion was denied on November 7, 2024. (Minute Order, 11/07/24.)On November 25, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Notice of Settlement of Entire Case.DiscussionThe Notice of Settlement of Entire Case filed by Plaintiff on November 25, 2024 indicates that the parties reached a conditional settlement agreement and that a request for dismissal will be filed by December 20, 2024. (Notice of Settlement, filed 11/25/24, ¶1b.) Under Cal. Rules of Court rule 3.1385, “the court must vacate all hearings and other proceedings requiring the appearance of a party and may not set any hearing or other proceeding requiring the appearance of a party earlier than 45 days after the dismissal date specified in the notice, unless requested by a party.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1385(c)(3)(A).) However, the Court need not vacate a hearing on a motion for sanctions. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1385(c)(3)(B).)Plaintiff is to appear through counsel of record at the hearing and clarify whether the Motion for Sanctions is to proceed in light of the parties’ settlement agreement.ConclusionRuling to depend on what happens at the hearing.Court clerk to give notice.
