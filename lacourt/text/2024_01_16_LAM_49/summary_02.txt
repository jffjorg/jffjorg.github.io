Summary:

The court grants the defendant's motion to quash service of summons due to lack of personal jurisdiction. The plaintiff, Jose Gutierrez, brought an action against the defendant, Bokf, NA, alleging that the company used spyware on its website to collect personal data of visitors, in violation of the California Unauthorized Access to Computer Data Act. The defendant moved to quash service of summons, arguing that the court lacked personal jurisdiction over it. The court finds that the defendant's principal place of business is in Oklahoma, and there is no evidence that the defendant does substantial business in California. The court also notes that the plaintiff has not presented any evidence in support of its opposition. The court distinguishes the case from Stomp, Inc. v. NeatO, LLC, where the defendant had used a "highly commercial" website allowing Californians to purchase the company's products. The court recognizes that the alleged collection of Californian consumer data could somewhat change the analysis, especially if the defendant was to use that information for business purposes. Finally, the court notes that the case of Briskin v. Shopify, Inc. is persuasive, but as federal authority, is not binding on this court.
