Background
 
            On August 9, 2024, Plaintiff Marks
Capital, Inc. (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint against Defendant Harry Strauss
Zelnick alleging causes of action for: 
 
1.                 Violations
of California Corporate Securities Law of 1968; and 
2.                 Fraud.

 
On September 17, 2024, Plaintiff filed a First
Amended Complaint (“FAC”) against Defendants Harry Strauss Zelnick (“Zelnick”) and
B&C Retail Ventures, LLC (erroneously sued as Kairos Industries, LLC and collectively
with Zelnick as “Defendants”) alleging causes of action for: 
 
1.              
Violations of California Corporate Securities Law of 1968; 
2.              
Fraud; 
3.              
Sexual Harassment in Violation of Civ. Code §§51.9, 52; 
4.              
Sexual Harassment in Violation of Cal. Gov. Code § 12940 et seq.; 
5.              
Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress; 
6.              
Violations of Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200 et seq. (Unfair Competition
Law); and 
7.              
Wrongful Termination. 
 
On November 6 and 14, 2024, Damon Suden and Michael
Lynch filed their respective Applications to Appear Pro Hac Vice for Defendants.

 
On November 12, 2024, Defendants filed a Motion to
Compel Arbitration. On November 21, 2024, the court granted the parties’
Stipulation to Stay the Proceedings until a court ruling on Defendants’ Motion
to Compel Arbitration, without affecting the pending Applications to Appear Pro
Hac Vice.

Discussion
  
Counsel Applicants, Damon Suden and
Michael Lynch, seek admission to appear as counsel pro hac vice to
represent Defendants Harry Strauss Zelnick and B&C Retail Ventures, LLC and
to be associated with Counsel Rebecca Durrant. 
 
Counsel Applicants are members in good
standing of the State of New York and various federal courts. 
 
Counsel Applicants have appeared once in
the United States District Court, Northern District of California on November
2, 2023. 
 
Each Counsel Applicant has individually
provided proof that the $50.00 fee required to be admitted pro hac vice has
been paid to the State Bar of California and that the State Bar has been served
with their Application. 
 
Conclusion
 
1.              
Damon Suden’s
Application to Appear Pro Hac Vice is GRANTED.
2.              
Michael Lynch’s
Application to Appear Pro Hac Vice is GRANTED.
