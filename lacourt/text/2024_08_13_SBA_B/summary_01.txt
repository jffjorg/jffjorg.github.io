The Court has issued a tentative ruling on the demurrer and motion to strike filed by the defendant, American Honda Motor Co., Inc. in the case of Kaylee J. Martinez and Juan J. Martinez v. American Honda Motor Co., Inc.

The Court has ruled that the demurrer to the fifth cause of action for fraudulent concealment in the First Amended Complaint (FAC) is OVERRULED. The Court finds that the allegations are sufficient to support the elements at the pleading stage for fraudulent concealment.

The Court has also ruled that the motion to strike the prayer for punitive damages is DENIED. The plaintiff's allegations of fraudulent concealment by the defendant in connection with the sale of a certified pre-owned 2018 Honda CR-V are sufficient to support the cause of action.

The Court has ordered the defendant to file an answer within twenty days.
