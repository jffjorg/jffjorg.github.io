The requirement is also set forth in the Department 19 Courtroom Information link on the Los Angeles Superior Court website.  Courtesy copies are to be delivered to Department 19 by 4:00 p.m. on October 23, 2023.  Second, in support of its Motion for  Preliminary Injunction and in opposition to Cross-Complainant Tres A’s Motion  for Preliminary Injunction, Plaintiff lodged documents under seal without  obtaining leave to do so in violation of California  Rules of Court, rule 2.551(a). (CRC, rule 2.551(a)  [“A record must not be filed under seal without a court order.”].)  The Stipulated Protective Order clearly states that the parties shall follow CRC, rules  2.550 and 2.551 regarding including confidential materials/information in any motion. (See paragraph 17, page 9).  Only discovery motions can be filed without further sealing orders. (Id.) As such, the  Court also sets a  Motion to Seal Lodged Evidence for October 30, 2023, at 1:30 p.m. in  Department 19 of the Stanley Mosk Courthouse. The Court sets this hearing to  give Plaintiff Vicente Del Rio an opportunity to follow the procedures for  filing the records under seal and to make the requisite showing, (see Cal. R.  Ct., 2.550-2.551), or to refile the records in unredacted/unsealed form, or  withdraw the redacted/sealed material by October 16, 2023.  The memorandum  and supporting declaration for the motion to seal must be filed and served on or before October 16, 2023.  (See CRC, rule 2.551(b)(1) [“A party requesting that a record be filed  under seal must file a motion or an application for an order sealing the  record. The motion or application must be accompanied by a memorandum and a  declaration containing facts sufficient to justify the sealing.”].) Any opposition  must be filed and served on or before October 20, 2023. No reply brief  will be permitted, but the Court will hear oral argument responding to any  arguments raised in opposition, if applicable. Other than the briefing for the motion to seal, no additional briefing or supplemental papers for the motions for preliminary injunction will be allowed or considered.
