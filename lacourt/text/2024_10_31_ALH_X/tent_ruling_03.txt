 Case Number:  23AHCV01487    Hearing Date:   October 31, 2024    Dept:  X DEFENDANTS’ MOTIONS TO DEEM ADMISSIONS ADMITTEDRequests for Admission (Set One) (Motion No. 6697) Requests for Admission (Set One) (Motion No. 5309)Defendants Vartanian and Patatanyan separately move for orders to have matters in Requests for Admission (Set One) (6697) and Requests for Admission (Set One) (5309) served on Plaintiff Leus deemed admitted.Tentative RulingDefendant Vartanian’s motion for an order deeming the matters in the Requests for Admissions (Set One) (6697) is GRANTED.Defendant Patatanyan’s motion for an order deeming the matters in the Requests for Admissions (Set One) (5309) is GRANTED. AnalysisThis case arises from a motor vehicle accident which occurred on October 15, 2022, at the intersection of Baldwin Avenue and Stanford Drive in Arcadia, California.  Plaintiff has claimed personal injuries because of the accident.  (Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 3.)  The parties conducted initial written discovery.  (Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 4.)  On or about June 7, 2024, this Court relieved Plaintiff’s counsel, Logan Quirk, as attorney of record.  (Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 5.)On July 9, 2024, Defendants Vartanian and Patatanyan served by first-class mail their respective Requests for Admission, Set One (6697) and (5309) upon Plaintiff.  (RFA (6697) —Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. A; RFA (5309)—Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. A.)  Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivisions (b) and (c) allow the requesting party to file a motion requesting that the truth of any matters specified in the request for admissions be deemed admitted unless the party to whom the requests have been directed has served a response that is in substantial compliance before the hearing.  A response to requests for admissions is timely if provided within 30 days of service.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.250.)  A response was due on August 13, 2024.  (RFA (6697) —Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 7; RFA (5309) —Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 7.)  To date, Defendant has not received the discovery responses nor has Plaintiff requested an extension or sent any communication.  (RFA (6697) —Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 8; RFA (5309) —Petrosyan Decl., ¶ 8.)  No oppositions by Plaintiff have been filed. Defendants Vartanian and Patatanyan’s motions that the truth of the matters specified in the request for admissions (6697, 5309) be deemed admitted are GRANTED.Moving Party is to give notice.
