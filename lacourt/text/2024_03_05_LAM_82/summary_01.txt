**Summary**

* The Court is considering a motion for attorney fees and costs by intervenors Re-Imagine Los Angeles County Coalition and Thomas Newman.
* The motion is granted in part, with a total and reasonable amount of attorney fees incurred for the work performed in connection with the appeal of the judgment in the Petition for Writ of Mandate being $339,606.33.

**Facts**

* The Court adopts the Court of Appeal's summary of the case, which states that a coalition of County employee unions and two individuals filed a petition for a peremptory writ of mandate prohibiting the Board, the Los Angeles County Auditor, and the Los Angeles County Chief Executive Officer from enforcing Measure J, a charter amendment that requires the County to annually allocate at least 10% of its locally generated unrestricted revenues in the general fund to direct community investment and alternatives to incarceration.
* The Court of Appeal reversed the grant of the writ petition and directed the trial court to enter a new judgment denying the petition, and on July 31, 2023, modified that opinion.

**Disputes**

* The primary dispute is over the entitlement to attorney fees and costs.

**Acts**

* The intervenors filed a petition for a peremptory writ of mandate, which was granted by the trial court.
* The County Board of Supervisors and other respondents appealed the decision to the Court of Appeal, which reversed the grant of the writ petition and directed the trial court to enter a new judgment denying the petition.
