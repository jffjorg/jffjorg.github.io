DEPARTMENT 207 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23SMCV04908    Hearing Date: July 1, 2024    Dept: 207

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPARTMENT

207

HEARING DATE

July 1, 2024

CASE NUMBER

23SMCV04908

MOTION

Application for Writ of Attachment

MOVING PARTIES

Plaintiffs Gar Lee, Trustee of the Gar Yin Lee Charitable Remainder Unitrust; Acquisition Science; Zach Goldberg, Trustee of the Zach Goldberg Charitable Remainder Unitrust; and Advance the World

OPPOSING PARTY

none

 

MOTION

 

This case arises from a dispute concerning an alleged Ponzi scheme to defraud.  On October 17, 2023, Plaintiffs Gar Lee, Trustee of the Gar Yin Lee Charitable Remainder Unitrust; Acquisition Science; Zach Goldberg, Trustee of the Zach Goldberg Charitable Remainder Unitrust; and Advance the World (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) brought suit against Defendants Bruce Francis Majeski; Kristine Taylor (originally named as Kristine Taylor Appel); Ron Appel; Kristine Taylor Philanthropies; TLC Escrow Services, Inc.; Helpuloan, LLC aka Helpuloans aka Help U Loan aka Help U Loans; Sovereign Wealth Preservation, Inc.; Philanthropy House; My Philanthropies; B & B Economic Development, LLC; KAB Holdings, Inc.; Seagate Holdings, Inc.; CFO Advisor; and Orion House (collectively, “Defendants.”)

 

Plaintiffs now seek a writ of attachment against Defendants My Philanthropies (“MP”) and Bruce Majeski (“Majeski”).  The writ is unopposed.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

“Attachment is a prejudgment remedy that allows a creditor to have a lien on the debtor's assets until final adjudication of the claim sued upon[.]”  (Weil & Brown, Cal. Practice Guide: Civil Procedure Before Trial (The Rutter Group 2024) ¶¶ 9:853 et seq., p. 9II-118 et seq.)  “The creditor must follow statutory guidelines in applying for the attachment and establish a prima facie claim; and the court is required to make a preliminary determination of the merits of the dispute.”  (Ibid.)

 

“Except as otherwise provided by statute, an attachment may be issued only in an action on a claim or claims for money, each of which is based upon a contract, express or implied, where the total amount of the claim or claims is a fixed or readily ascertainable amount not less than five hundred dollars ($500) exclusive of costs, interest, and attorney’s fees.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 483.010, subd. (a).)

 

The requirements for obtaining a writ of attachment are: (1) the claim upon which the attachment is based must be one upon which an attachment may issue; (2) plaintiff has established the probable validity of the claim upon which the attachment is based; (3) the attachment is not sought for a purpose other than recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based; and (4) the amount to be secured by attachment is greater than zero.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 484.090, subd. (a).) 

 

            “The application shall be supported by an affidavit showing that the plaintiff on the facts presented would be entitled to a judgment on the claim upon which the attachment is based.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 484.030.)  The facts stated in any such affidavit or verified complaint must be stated with particularity.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 482.040.)

 

            “A claim has ‘probable validity’ where it is more likely than not that the plaintiff will obtain a judgment against the defendant on that claim.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 481.190.)

 

ANALYSIS

 

As a threshold matter, a notice of application and hearing for writ of attachment must be filed and served at least 16 court days prior to the hearing.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005.) 

 

Here, the Court finds that the proofs of service indicate that the moving papers were served on Ronald I. Appel, Esq., counsel for Defendants Ron Appel, Kristine Taylor, Kristine Taylor Philanthropies, and Seagate Holdings, Inc., but no other Defendants. 

 

However, the remaining Defendants are in default, and therefore no notice to them is required.  “The entry of a default terminates a defendant's rights to take any further affirmative steps in the  litigation until either its default is set aside or a default judgment is entered.”  (Devlin v. Kearny Mesa AMC/Jeep/Renault, Inc. (1984) 155 Cal.App.3d 381, 385.)  Further, the entry of default “[c]uts off the defendant's right to file pleadings and motions (other than a motion to set aside default under § 473), and it also cuts off the defendant's right to notices and the service of pleadings or papers.”  (Bailey v. Citibank, N.A. (2021) 66 Cal.App.5th 335, 347.) 

 

Having determined service is proper, the Court turns to the merits of the application.

 

1.     Claim Upon Which Attachment is Based

 

The first element is that the claim upon which the attachment is based be one upon which an attachment may be issued.  “[A]n attachment may be issued only in an action on a claim or claims for money, each of which is based upon a contract, express or implied, where the total amount of the claim or claims is a fixed or readily ascertainable amount not less than five hundred dollars ($500) exclusive of costs, interest, and attorney’s fees.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 483.010, subd. (a).)

 

Here, Plaintiffs claim for money is based on an express contract.  (Complaint at ¶¶ 211-226.)  Plaintiffs contend they have sustained damages in the amount of $1,840,000, which is the amount they invested under the contract.  (Manzo Affidavit at ¶ 3.)

 

Therefore, Plaintiffs have established the first element.

 

2.     Probable Validity of the Claim

 

The second element is that Plaintiffs have established the probable validity of the claim upon which the attachment is based.  Plaintiffs seek to attach the property of two defaulted Defendants, Majeski and MP , based on the breach of contract claim.

 

“To prevail on a cause of action for breach of contract, the plaintiff must prove (1) the contract, (2) the plaintiff's performance of the contract or excuse for nonperformance, (3) the defendant's breach, and (4) the resulting damage to the plaintiff.”  (Richman v. Hartley (2014) 224 Cal.App.4th 1182, 1186.) 

 

However, Plaintiffs have not provided evidence establishing the probable validity of this claim.  Instead, to support Plaintiffs’ assertion that the parties entered into a contract, Plaintiffs primarily cites allegations in the unverified Complaint.  (See Motion at pp. 4-5.)  But allegations in an unverified Complaint are not evidence.  And although Majeski and MP have defaulted, Plaintiffs must still demonstrate the probable validity of their claim.  For example, as to defaulted defendants, entry of a default judgment still requires proof.

 

The only evidence Plaintiff cites to is paragraph 7 of the Manzo Affidavit, which provides:

 

7. I am informed and believed that BRUCE MAJESKI failed to record the vast majority of fractionalized Trust Deeds on behalf of Plaintiffs which Plaintiffs believed in good faith they were purchasing. I have attached as Exhibit “4” hereto three true and correct copies of property profile reports for 251 Camellia Ln, Costa Mesa, CA 92627, 4445 Cartwright Avenue, Unit 207, North Hollywood, CA 91602, and 33582 Palo Alto St., Dana Point, CA 92629 – all of which Plaintiffs believed they had invested in. As the reports demonstrate – none of Plaintiffs were recorded on the deeds mentioned, but defendants and their companies appear numerous times.

 

(Manzo Affidavit at ¶ 7.)

 

            Thus, Plaintiffs have not established the probable validity of their breach of contract claim.[1]

 

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

 

            For the foregoing reasons, the Court denies Plaintiffs’ Application without prejudice. 

 

            Plaintiffs shall give notice of the Court’s ruling and file the notice with a proof of service forthwith.
