DEPARTMENT X LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Counsel wishing to submit on a tentative ruling must send an email to alhdeptx@lacourt.org advising the Court and all counsel of their intention to submit.  Unless all parties submit, the matter will still be called for hearing and may be argued by any appearing/non-submitting parties.  If all parties submit on the Court’s tentative ruling, counsel for moving party shall give notice of ruling unless otherwise ordered by the Court.  This may be done by incorporating verbatim the Court’s tentative ruling. 



Case Number: 22AHCV00356    Hearing Date: October 10, 2024    Dept: X

Plaintiff’s Motion (4) to Deem RFAs Admitted and Order to Show Cause Hearing

 

Order to Show Cause Hearing re: Striking Defendant Mile High’s Answer and Cross-Complaint

 

The Court notes that a substitution of attorney has been filed as to Defendant Mile High Cure Corp.  However, no substitution of attorney has been filed on behalf of Cross-Complainant Mile High Cure Corp.  In the absence of any such substitution filed prior to the OSC hearing, the latter will remain on calendar.

 

 

Plaintiff’s
Motions (4) to Deem RFAs Admitted; Requests for Sanctions

 

Plaintiff moves for an
order that the truth of the matters specified in the requests for admission be
deemed admitted against Defendants Mile High and Jeremy Caldwell.  

 

Code of Civil Procedure
section 2033.280, subdivision (b), provides that if a party fails to respond to
a request for admission, “[t]he requesting party may move for an order
that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in
the requests be deemed admitted”.  Unless the court determines that
the responding party “has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed
response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with
Section 2033.220, it must order the RFAs deemed admitted… But a responding
party’s service, prior to the hearing on the ‘deemed admitted’ motion, of
substantially compliant responses, will defeat a propounding party’s attempt
under section 2033.280 to have the RFAs deemed admitted.”  (St.
Mary v. Superior Court (2014) 223 Cal.App.4th 762, 775–776, citations
and footnote omitted.)

 

Each of Plaintiff’s four
motions is supported by the following facts: On December 6, 2023, Plaintiff
served (1) Requests for Admission, Set One—Caldwell; (2) Requests for Admission
of Genuineness of Documents, Set One—Caldwell; (3) Requests for Admission, Set
One—Mile High; and (4) Requests for Admission of Genuineness of Documents, Set
One—Mile High. (Motion (6681) Liberzon Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. 1; Motion (0774)
Liberzon Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. 1; Motion (9490) Liberzon Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. 1; Motion
(6972) Liberzon Decl.,  4, Exh. 1.)  Responses were due January 8, 2024.  Plaintiff provided at least three extensions
to Mile High and Caldwell.  (Motion
(6681) Liberzon Decl., ¶¶ 5-6; Motion (0774) Liberzon Decl., ¶¶ 5-6; Motion
(9490) Liberzon Decl., ¶¶ 5-6; Motion (6972) Liberzon Decl., 5-6.)  Plaintiff provides that Caldwell and Mile High
failed to produce any responses as of the filing of these four motions (August
27, 2024).  (Motion (6681) Liberzon
Decl., ¶ 7; Motion (0774) Liberzon Decl., ¶ 7; Motion (9490) Liberzon Decl., ¶
7; Motion (6972) Liberzon Decl., 7.)

 

Motion
re: Admissions to Defendant Mile High (No. 6972)



In opposition, Defendant Mile High provides it served objection-free responses
on October 7, 2024.  (Murrin Decl., ¶ 4.)  Because
Defendant has served responses prior to the hearing on this motion, Plaintiff’s
motion to deem admitted is denied as moot.  However, due to the necessity

Case Number: 23AHCV02200    Hearing Date: October 10, 2024    Dept: X

 

Motions to Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories and Responses to Production Demands

 

Defendants move to compel further responses to (1) five sets of Request for Production of Documents (Set One) and (2) five sets of Special Interrogatories (Set One) propounded upon Plaintiff.      

 

This litigation arises out of an alleged incident that occurred on January 18, 2023 between Plaintiff and City of Pasadena officers.  The complaint alleges assault and battery, negligence, violation of Civil Code sections 51, 51.7, 52, and 52.1, and false imprisonment.

 

The motions are granted as follows:

 

·       Special interrogatories from Officer Zamora: Granted as to Interrogatory Nos. 1, 2, 4, 13, 14, 15, 19, 23, 24, 26, 29, 32, 34, 35, 38, and 41.  Plaintiff’s response as to Interrogatory No. 18 is sufficient. 

·       Production demand requests from Officer Zamora: Granted as to Request Nos. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.

·       Special interrogatories from Officer Sandoval: Granted as to Interrogatory Nos. 1, 2, 4, 13, 14, 15, 19, 23, 24, 26, 29, 32, 34, 35, 38, and 41.  Plaintiff’s response as to Interrogatory No. 18 is sufficient. 

·       Production demand requests from Officer Sandoval: Granted as to Request Nos. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.

·       Special interrogatories from Officer Gutierrez: Granted as to Interrogatory Nos. 1, 2, 4, 13, 14, 15, 19, 23, 24, 26, 29, 32, 34, 35, 38, and 41.  Plaintiff’s response as to Interrogatory No. 18 is sufficient. 

·       Production demand requests from Officer Gutierrez: Granted as to Request Nos. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.

·       Special interrogatories from Officer Carrillo: Granted as to Interrogatory Nos. 1, 2, 4, 13, 14, 15, 19, 23, 24, 26, 29, 32, 34, 35, 38, and 41.  Plaintiff’s response as to Interrogatory No. 18 is sufficient. 

·       Production demand requests from Officer Carrillo: Granted as to Request Nos. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, and 8.

·       Special interrogatories from City: Granted as to Interrogatory Nos. 1, 15, 35, and 38.

·       Production demand requests from City: Granted as to Request Nos. 46, 47, 48, 49, 50, 51, 52, and 53.

 

Each answer in the response to written discovery must be “as complete and straightforward as the information reasonably available to the responding party permits. [¶] . . . If an interrogatory cannot be answered completely, it shall be answered to the extent possible.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.220, subd. (a), (b).)  Where the question is specific and explicit, an answer that supplies only a portion of the information sought is improper.  It is also improper to provide “deftly worded conclusionary answers designed to evade a series of explicit questions.”  (Deyo v. Kilbourne (1978) 84 Cal.App.3d 771, 783.)  Furthermore, where an interrogatory asks for the names of all witnesses to a particular event then known to the responding party, a response omitting the name of a known witness could subject the adversary to unfair surprise at trial and therefore may result in an order excluding that witness’ testimony.  (R & B Auto Ctr., Inc. v. Farmers Group, Inc. (2006) 140 Cal.App.4th 327, 356.)

 

In addition, Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.310, subdivision (a) provides:

 

On receipt of a response to a demand for inspection . . . , the demanding party may move for an order compelling further response to the demand if the demanding party deems that any of the following apply:

 

(1) A statement of compliance with the demand is incomplete.

(2) A representation of inability to comply is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive.

(3) An objection in the response is without merit or too general.

 

As Plaintiff’s responses to the subject discovery appear incomplete and are unverified, Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified further Code-compliant responses within 20 days of mailing of the Court’s notice of ruling.

 

Defendants request sanctions in the amount of $1,500 for each of the two motions ONLY IF Plaintiff fails to provide a verification for the previously served responses and further verified responses.  Accordingly, the Court defers ruling on Defendants’ request for sanctions.

Case Number: 23AHCV02396    Hearing Date: October 10, 2024    Dept: X


PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO SET ASIDE/VACATE DISMISSAL

The motion to set aside the dismissal entered on July 17, 2024 is GRANTED.

On October 16, 2023, Plaintiff filed the complaint in this action.

 

On January 3, 2024, the Court heard an OSC re: Failure to File Proof of Service, which was continued to March 6, 2024 and the Court instructed Plaintiff’s counsel to file proof of service prior to the new date.  (1/3/24 Minute Order.)

 

On March 6, 2024, the Court heard the OSC re: Failure to File Proof of Service and there was still no proof of service as to Defendant Rodriguez.  The Court scheduled an OSC re: $250 sanctions for Plaintiff’s Failure to Serve the Defendant for May 8, 2024.  The Court instructed Plaintiff’s counsel to file a Proof of Service, Declaration re: Due Diligence or Application & Order for Publication prior to the next court date.  (3/6/24 Minute Order.)

 

On July 17, 2024, the Court heard an OSC re: Sanctions of $250 for Plaintiff’s failure to serve the complaint and $250 for Plaintiff’s failure to appear on May 8, 2024.  There were no appearances.  The Court ordered the complaint dismissed without prejudice.  (7/17/24 Minute Order.)

 

On August 15, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel filed a motion to set aside the dismissal based on the grounds of mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect.  Plaintiff’s counsel failed to attend the Order to Show Cause for Failure to File Proof of Service hearing set on May 8, 2024, because Plaintiff’s counsel moved from Irvine to Santa Ana in the month of April.  (Hatch Decl., ¶ 7.)  A notice of change of address was filed on May 2024.  (Hatch Decl., ¶ 7.)

 

Plaintiff’s counsel declares that on or about March 13, 2024, Plaintiff attempted to serve Defendant at 12769 7th Street, in Chino, California and after one attempt the process server spoke with resident Johnathan Rodriguez who said Defendant moved to 14406 Aztec St., Sylmar, CA 91342.  (Hatch Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. 3 [Non-Service Report].)  On March 27 and April 6, 2024, Plaintiff attempted to serve Defendant five and seven times, respectfully.  (Hatch Decl., ¶¶ 5-6.)  The attempts to serve by publication resulted in publication dates of July 19, July 25, August 1, and August 8, 2024.  (Hatch Decl., ¶ 8; Exh. 7.)  Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 473, Plaintiff requests that the Court set aside the dismissal on July 17, 2024.

 

The motion is timely as it was filed on August 15, 2024, which is within six months of the dismissal on July 17, 2024.  The Court finds Plaintiff has met her burden under section 473(b) of showing that the dismissal was caused by her attorney’s mistake, inadvertence, and neglect.  Relief is mandatory.

 

The motion is, therefore. GRANTED.
