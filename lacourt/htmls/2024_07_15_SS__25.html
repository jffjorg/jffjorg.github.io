Case Number: 21STLC00181    Hearing Date: July 15, 2024    Dept: 25

HEARING DATE:   Mon., July 15, 2024                                        JUDGE /DEPT:        Mkrtchyan/25

CASE NAME:           State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company v. Nicole Abril

CASE NUMBER:     21STLC00181                                                 COMPL. FILED:      01-08-21

NOTICE:                   OK                                                                  DISPOSED:               02-09-23

 

           

PROCEEDINGS:     MOTION TO VACATE DISMISSAL, ENFORCE SETTLEMENT, AND ENTER JUDGMENT

 

MOVING PARTY:   Plaintiff State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company

RESP. PARTY:         None

 

MOTION TO VACATE DISMISSAL, ENFORCE SETTLEMENT, AND ENTER JUDGMENT

(CCP § 664.6)

 

 

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Plaintiff State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company’s Motion to Set Aside the Dismissal Entered on 02/03/2023, and to Enter Judgment is GRANTED. 

 

The Dismissal entered on February 3, 2023, is hereby set aside and vacated.

 

Judgment is entered for Plaintiff and against Defendant in the amount of $3,949.70 as follows: principal amount of $3,449.70 plus court costs of $500.

 

 

 

SERVICE: 

 

[   ] Proof of Service Timely Filed (CRC, rule 3.1300)                     OK

[   ] Correct Address (CCP §§ 1013, 1013a)                                      OK

[   ] 16/21 Court Days Lapsed (CCP §§ 12c, 1005(b))                       OK

 

OPPOSITION:          None filed as of July 11, 2024.                       [   ] Late                      [X] None

REPLY:                     None filed as of July 11, 2024.                       [   ] Late                      [X] None

 

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

            On January 8, 2021, Plaintiff State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) filed an action against Defendant Nicole Abril (“Defendant”) for subrogation, stemming from an automobile collision between Defendant, on the one hand, and an individual insured by Plaintiff’s automobile insurance policy, on the other hand.  (Compl.)  Plaintiff compensated the insured for claimed damages in the amount of $18,416.96 and filed the instant claim against Defendant for allegedly causing the damages.  (Ibid. pp. 2-3.)  On March 24, 2021, Defendant filed an Answer.

 

On February 2, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Stipulation for Dismissal with Reservation to Vacate and Enter Judgment Upon Breach (“Stipulation”), signed by both parties, to dismiss the action on the premise that Defendant would compensate Plaintiff for the settlement amount of $12,433.56.  On the same day, the Court dismissed the entire case without prejudice pursuant to the Stipulation.

 

On April 23, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Vacate Dismissal and Enter Judgment (“Motion”).  Plaintiff has submitted the motion on the moving papers and will not appear at the hearing.  (Mot. p. 2.)

 

No opposition has been filed.

 

II.                 Legal Standard & Discussion

 

CCP § 664.6, provides a summary procedure that enables judges to enforce a settlement agreement by entering a judgment pursuant to the terms of the parties’ settlement.  In particular, the statute provides:

 

(a) If parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside of the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement.  If requested by the parties, the court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement.

 

(b) For purposes of this section, a writing is signed by a party if it is signed by any of the following:

 

(1) The party.

(2) An attorney who represents the party.

(3) If the party is an insurer, an agent who is authorized in writing by the insurer to sign on the insurer's behalf.

 

CCP § 664.6(a)-(b) (emphasis added).

 

III.              Discussion

 

A. Retention of Jurisdiction

 

“‘[V]oluntary dismissal of an action or special proceeding terminates the court’s jurisdiction over the matter.’  (Conservatorship of Martha P. (2004) 117 Cal.App.4th 857, 867) [12 Cal.Rptr.3d 142.) ‘If requested by the parties,’ however, ‘the [trial] court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce [a] settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement.’ (§ 664.6, italics added.)”  (Mesa RHF Partners, L.P. v. City of Los Angeles (2019) 33 Cal.App.5th 913, 917.)  “‘Because of its summary nature, strict compliance with the requirements of section 664.6 is prerequisite to invoking the power of the court to impose a settlement agreement.’”  (Ibid. (quoting Sully-Miller Contracting Co. v. Gledson/Cashman Construction, Inc. (2002) 103 Cal.App.4th 30, 37).)

 

“A request for the trial court to retain jurisdiction under section 664.6 ‘must conform to the same three requirements which the Legislature and the courts have deemed necessary for section 664.6 enforcement of the settlement itself: the request must be made (1) during the pendency of the case, not after the case has been dismissed in its entirety, (2) by the parties themselves, and (3) either in a writing signed by the parties or orally before the court.’”  (Ibid. (quoting Wackeen v. Malis (2002) 97 Cal.App.4th 429, 440).)  “The ‘request must be express, not implied from other language, and it must be clear and unambiguous.’” (Ibid. (quoting Wackeen, supra, 97 Cal.App.4th at 440).)

 

Here, the parties signed a Stipulation for Dismissal with Reservation to Vacate and Enter Judgment Upon Breach (“Stipulation”) containing the parties’ agreement for the Court to retain jurisdiction pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure §664.6 to enforce the terms of the stipulation and enter judgment in the event of default.  (Stipulation ¶ 3.)  Prior to the dismissal of this action, the Stipulation was signed by the parties and submitted to the Court.  (Ibid. at p. 3.)  On the same day, the Court dismissed the entire case without prejudice pursuant to the Stipulation and expressly stated that it “shall retain jurisdiction pursuant to CCP § 664.6.”  (2-3-23 Order.)

 

The Court finds that the Stipulation complies with § 664.6 requirements and the Court has retained jurisdiction to enter judgment pursuant to the parties’ Stipulation in this action.

 

B. Entry of Judgment

 

The Stipulation Agreement filed on February 2, 2023, provides that Plaintiff and Defendant agreed to dismiss the action on the premise that Defendant would compensate Plaintiff for the settlement amount of $12,433.56.  (Stipulation ¶ 2.)  Pursuant to the Stipulation, Defendant would make an initial payment of $8,983.56 by June 30, 2022, and beginning on February 1, 2023, make monthly payments of $125, until the settlement was paid in full.  (Ibid.)

 

The Stipulation also provides that in the event Defendant fails to make timely payments, Plaintiff will mail a letter to Defendant regarding the default.  (Ibid. at ¶ 3.)  If Defendant does not remedy the default within fourteen (14) days of receiving notice, “then plaintiff will be entitled to have any dismissal in this action set aside and judgment entered for the settlement amount, minus credit for payments received, plus any costs associated with entering the judgment not to exceed $500.”  (Ibid.)  The declaration of Plaintiff’s counsel shall be sufficient proof for judgment to be entered.  (Ibid.) 

 

On April 23, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion for an order to vacate the dismissal and enter judgment.  (Memorandum p. 1.)  According to Plaintiff’s counsel, Defendant’s insurance carrier made a one-time payment of $8,983.86; however, Defendant failed to make any payments.  (Reese Decl. ¶¶ 4-5.)  Around March 12, 2024, Plaintiff mailed Defendant a letter regarding the missed payments; however, Defendant has not made any additional payments.  (Ibid. at ¶¶ 6-7, Ex. C.)

 

Plaintiff seeks to have the dismissal set aside and judgment entered in the amount of $3,949.70 as follows: $12,433.56 (settlement amount), minus $8,983.86 (payment made by Defendant’s insurance carrier), plus $500 (court costs).  (Ibid. at ¶ 8.)  The Court costs consist of $370.00 complaint filing fee, $80 service of process fee, and $60.00 motion fee.  (Ibid.)

 

The Court finds the Stipulation to be valid and enforceable under Code of Civil Proc. § 664.6.  Plaintiff provides evidence that Defendant defaulted on payments and has not made any additional payments after Plaintiff sent notice.  Thus, a valid and signed stipulation agreement was breached and the Court retained jurisdiction to enter judgment upon breach.

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s Motion to Set Aside Dismissal and Enter Judgment is GRANTED.  Dismissal entered on February 3, 2023, is vacated, and judgment is entered for Plaintiff and against Defendant for $3,949.70 as follows: principal amount of $3,449.70 plus court costs of $500.

 

IV.              Conclusion & Order

 

For the foregoing reasons,

 

Plaintiff State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company’s Motion to Set Aside Dismissal and Enter Judgment is GRANTED. 

 

The Dismissal entered on February 3, 2023, is hereby set aside and vacated.

 

Judgment is entered for Plaintiff and against Defendant in the amount of $3,949.70 as follows: principal amount of $3,449.70 plus court costs of $500.

 

 

The Moving party is ordered to give notice of this Court’s Ruling and to attach a copy of the Court's Tentative Ruling, as exhibit “A” to said notice.

BACKGROUND

 

On October 13, 2022, Plaintiff State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company (“Plaintiff) filed the Complaint alleging damages arising from an automobile collision against Defendants Zoila Yolanda Delcid; Aldo F. Medina Sr., and Does I through V.

None.

I.          Motion to be Relieved as Counsel

A.        Legal Standard

            Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362 (Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel) requires (1) notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client (made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-051)); (2) a declaration stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under CCP § 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under CCP § 284(1) (made on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-052)); (3) service of the notice of motion and motion and declaration on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and (4) the proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-053)).

Case Number: 23STLC03672    Hearing Date: July 15, 2024    Dept: 25

Hearing Date:                          Monday, July 15, 2024 

Case Name:                             Morales, et al. v. Austra Motors, Inc., et al. 

Case No.:                                23STLC03672 

Motion:                                   Motion for Order Lifting the Current Arbitration Stay and to Reopen this Action   

Moving Party:                         Plaintiffs Benito Morales and Lisa Morales 

Responding Party:                   Unopposed 

Notice:                                     OK 

 

 

Tentative Ruling:                    Plaintiffs Benito Morales and Lisa Morales’ Motion to Lift Stay is GRANTED.

 

 

BACKGROUND 

 

On June 8, 2023, Plaintiffs Benito Morales and Lisa Morales (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) filed this action against Defendants Austra Motors, Inc., Lentegrity, LLC, Hudson Insurance Company, and Does 1 through 40, inclusive, asserting causes of action for (1) violation of Consumers Legal Remedies Act (Civil Code § 1750 et seq), (2) violation of California Business and Professions Code § 17200, et seq., unlawful acts or practices; (3) violation of Civil Code §1632, (4) claim against surety, and (5) violation of Code of Civil Procedure §§ 1281.97 and 1281.99. 

 

On November 15, 2023, the Court granted Plaintiff’s unopposed Motion to Compel Arbitration. The Court ordered Plaintiffs, Defendant Austra Motors, Inc., and Defendant Lentegrity, LLC, to arbitrate their dispute with the American Arbitration Association. Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 1281.98, the Court also ordered Defendants to pay all American Arbitration Association arbitration fees they are obligated to pay upon arbitration.

The Court stayed the case pending binding arbitration to the entire action. (Minute Order 11/15/23.)

 

On May 5, 2024, Plaintiffs filed the instant Motion to Lift Stay. No opposition has been filed by Defendants as of July 11, 2024. Any opposition was required to have been filed and served at least nine court days prior to the hearing. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005, subd. (b).)

 

ANALYSIS  

 

Under section 3 of the Federal Arbitration Act, an action referring the parties to arbitration may be stayed so long as no party is in default. (9 U.S.C., § 3.)

 

Here, arbitration under the parties’ agreement is to be conducted pursuant to the Federal Arbitration Act, set forth at 9 U.S.C. section 1, et. seq. The Declaration of Heydari cites Exhibit 3 in support of this; however, Exhibit 3 does not support this as it is a letter from the American Arbitration Association (“the AAA”). Notwithstanding, the Court finds that arbitration is governed by the Federal Arbitration Act per the Retail Installment Sales Contract attached as Exhibit 2 (at pages 5-6) of the Declaration of Sadr in support of Plaintiffs’ motion to compel arbitration.

 

Further, the Court finds that Defendants are now in default with respect to the arbitration proceedings. Despite the Court’s November 15, 2023 order that Defendants make initial payments in accordance with the rules of the AAA, they have yet to do so. (Heydari Decl., ¶¶3-7; Exs. 1-3.) Around March 11, 2024, the AAA sent notice that it was closing the case due to non-payment of fees. (Id. at ¶7.)  

 

Based on Defendant’s default with respect to the arbitration proceeding, the Court finds it appropriate to reinstate this action.

 

CONCLUSION

 

Plaintiffs Benito Morales and Lisa Morales’ Motion to Lift Stay and Reopen Case is GRANTED.

 

Trial to be reset at today’s hearing.

 

The Moving party is ordered to give notice of this Court’s Ruling and to attach a copy of the Court's Tentative Ruling, as exhibit “A” to said notice.

 

 


Case Number: 24STCP00444    Hearing Date: July 15, 2024    Dept: 25

Hearing Date:                         Monday, July 15, 2024

Case Name:                             BELMONT SHORES MOBILE ESTATES v. PATRICK AUGUSTUS ROGERS; and DOES 1 through 10, inclusive

Case No.:                                24STCP00444

Motion:                                   Petition to Declare Mobile Home Abandoned   

Moving Party:                         Petitioner Belmont Shores Mobile Estates       

Responding Party:                   Unopposed  

Notice:                                    OK

 

Tentative Ruling:                    The Petition to Declare Mobile Home Abandoned is GRANTED.

 

Counsel for Petitioner is ordered to electronically submit a proposed form of Judgment for Abandonment within 10-days of this Court’s Ruling.

                                               

 

BACKGROUND

 

On February 13, 2024, Petitioner Belmont Shores Mobile Estates (“Petitioner”) filed the instant Petition to Declare Mobile Home Abandoned (the “Petition”) against Respondent Patrick Augustus Rogers (“Respondent”) and DOES 1 through 10, inclusive. Pursuant to Civil Code § 798.61, Petitioner seeks a declaration that the mobilehome located at 6258 Golden Sand Dr Space 259, Long Beach, California 90803 and described as a 2004 Charleston IV, Decal Number LBF8883, Serial Numbers CAHK01241931049364A/CAHK01241931049364B (the “Mobilehome”) is abandoned.

 

Also, on February 13, 2024, the Court issued a Notice of Hearing on Petition and set the hearing on the Petition for June 18, 2024. (02/13/24 Notice of Hearing.) Petitioner was ordered to give notice of the hearing by serving a copy of the notice on all parties to this action, and to file a proof of service thereof. (02/13/24 Notice of Hearing.)

 

On March 25, 2024, pursuant to a Request for Dismissal filed by Petitioner, Does 1 through 10 were dismissed.

 

On March 25, 2024, Sal Ortiz (“Ortiz”), who is the Regional Manager for Petitioner filed a Declaration and a Supplemental Declaration in support of the Petition.

 

Also, on March 25, 2024, Petitioner filed proof of service indicating that Respondent was served with the Petition and Notice of Hearing as to the Petition by posting the documents at 6258 Golden Sand Dr., Space 259, Long Beach, California 90803 (the “Premises”) and sending such documents by certified mail to such address on February 14, 2024.

 

Also, on March 25, 2024, Petitioner filed a Proof of Service of Notice of Belief of Abandonment indicating that Respondent was served with such document by certified mail and posting at the Premises on November 15, 2023.

 

On March 26, 2024, default was entered against Respondent.

 

On June 18, 2024, the Court continued the hearing on the Petition to July 15, 2024, so that the declaration and supplemental declaration of Mr. Ortiz could be served on Respondent. (06/18/24 Minute Order at pp. 5-6.)

 

On June 19, 2024, Petitioner served Respondent with the declaration and supplemental declaration of Mr. Ortiz. (See 06/20/24 Proof of Service of Declaration and Supplemental Declaration of Sal Ortiz.)

 

On June 20, 2024, Petitioner filed and served a Notice of Continuance and Tentative Ruling.

 

 

MOVING PARTY POSITION

 

            Petitioner prays for the Court to declare the Mobilehome abandoned. Petitioner also seeks: (1) the total amount of rent and other applicable charges due on the mobilehome site as of the date of judgment; (2) storage costs equal to the rent due and other applicable charges from the date of judgment up to the date of the sale of the Mobilehome or other disposition; (3) court costs; and (4) attorney’s fees pursuant to Civ. Code § 798.61.

 

OPPOSITION

 

            No opposition has been filed as of July 10, 2024.

 

REPLY

 

            No reply has been filed as of July 10, 2024.

 

ANALYSIS

 

I.          Petition for Declaration of Abandonment of Mobilehome   

A.                Legal Standard

An abandoned mobilehome means a mobilehome which: (1) is located in a mobilehhome park on a site for which no rent has been paid to the management for the preceding 60 days; (2) is unoccupied; (3) a reasonable person would believe it to be abandoned; and (4) is not permanently affixed to the land. (Civ. Code § 798.61(a)(1)(A)-(D).)

“After determining a mobilehome in a mobilehome park to be an abandoned mobilehome, the management shall post a notice of belief of abandonment on the mobilehome for not less than 30 days, and shall deposit copies of the notice in the United States mail, postage prepaid, addressed to the homeowner at the last known address and to any known registered owner, if different from the homeowner, and to any known holder of a security interest in the abandoned mobilehome. This notice shall be mailed by registered or certified mail with a return receipt requested.” (Civ. Code § 798.61(b).)

“Thirty or more days following posting pursuant to subdivision (b), the management may file a petition in the superior court in the county in which the mobilehome park is located, for a judicial declaration of abandonment of the mobilehome . . . [c]opies of the petition shall be served upon the homeowner, any known registered owner, and any known person having a lien or security interest of record in the mobilehome by posting a copy on the mobilehome and mailing copies to those persons at their last known addresses by registered or certified mail with a return receipt requested in the United States mail, postage prepaid.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 798.61(c)(1).)

“If, at the hearing, the petitioner shows by a preponderance of the evidence that the criteria for an abandoned mobilehome has been satisfied and no party establishes an interest therein at the hearing and tenders all past due rent and other charges, the court shall enter a judgment of abandonment, determine the amount of charges to which the petitioner is entitled, and award attorney’s fees and costs to the petitioner.” (Civ. Code § 798.61(d)(2).)

B.        Discussion  

Petitioner seeks a declaration that the Mobilehome is abandoned. (Pet. p. 3.) The Petition alleges that the Mobilehome is located at a mobilehome site for which no rent has been paid to the management for the 60 days preceding November 15, 2023. (Id., ¶ 5(c).) Petitioner reasonably believes that the Mobilehome has been abandoned. (Id., ¶ 5(d).) Petitioner alleges that the Mobilehome is unoccupied, and a reasonable person would believe it to be abandoned. (Id., ¶¶ 5(a)-(b).) The Notice of Belief of Abandonment was posted on the Mobilehome on November 15, 2023, and was sent certified mail on such date pursuant to CCP § 798.61(b). (Id., ¶ 5(c); Exh. 1.)  

In his declaration, Mr. Ortiz declares the following: Respondent is the registered owner of the Mobilehome. (Ortiz Decl., ¶ 4; Exh. 2.) The Mobilehome is currently unoccupied and has been unoccupied prior to November 15, 2023, which is the date the Notice of Belief of Abandonment was posted on the Mobilehome. (Ortiz Decl., ¶ 7; Exh. 1.) The Mobilehome is not currently affixed to the mobilehome space and is located in a mobilehome park on a site for which no space rent or storage has been paid to the management since March 2023. (Ortiz Decl., ¶ 8.) The monthly storage charges owed from April 1, 2023 through March 1, 2024, twelve months, is calculated at $1,425.00 per month based upon the former space rent Respondent was paying at the time the Mobilehome was abandoned. (Ortiz Decl., ¶ 9; Exh. 4.) The monthly storage charge is reasonable as local storage for a similarly sized mobilehome would exceed that amount if commercial space for a mobilehome storage was actually available. (Ortiz Decl., ¶ 10.) The total amount of storage charges owed is $17,100.00. (Ortiz Decl., ¶ 10.)

In his supplemental declaration, Mr. Ortiz declares that the Mobilehome is abandoned within the meaning of CCP § 798.61. (Ortiz Supp. Decl., ¶ 3.) No rent for the space has been paid to Petitioner since March 2023. (Ortiz Supp. Decl., ¶ 4.) A reasonable person would concur that no one is occupying the Mobilehome. (Ortiz Supp. Decl., ¶ 5.) Respondent has not been seen at the Premises and no one else has been seen or reported to be occupying the mobilehome. (Ortiz Supp. Decl., ¶ 5.) The Mobilehome has been unattended since October 2023 and his office has performed basic yard maintenance and upkeep. (Ortiz Supp. Decl., ¶ 6.) The Mobilehome is not permanently affixed to the space. (Ortiz Supp. Decl., ¶ 7.)

Petitioner has shown that the Mobilehome meets the definition of abandoned pursuant to CCP § 798.61(a)(1).

II.        Conclusion

           

            Based on the foregoing, the Court GRANTS the Petition to Declare Mobile Home Abandoned.

Moving Party:                         Defendant 3D Engineering & Manufacturing Inc.

Recommended Ruling:           Defendant 3D Engineering & Manufacturing Inc.’s Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Judgment Entered on 02/20/2024 is GRANTED.

 

The Court sets an Order to Show Cause Re: Dismissal (Settlement) for August 19, 2024 at 9:30 a.m.

 

Moving party is ordered to give Notice of this Court’s Ruling.  A responsive pleading or a Request for Dismissal is to be filed within the next 20 days.

 

 

 

BACKGROUND 

 

Defendant 3D Engineering & Manufacturing Inc. (“Defendant”) moves for an order setting aside and vacating the Judgment obtained in State Case number WC-CM-816672 and entered on February 20, 2024 against Defendant. To date, Plaintiff Juan Recalde (“Plaintiff”) has not opposed the motion.

 

ANALYSIS 

                                                                                                                  

“The court may, upon any terms as may be just, relieve a party or his or her legal representative from a judgment, dismissal, order, or other proceeding taken against him or her through his or her mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect. Application for this relief shall be accompanied by a copy of the answer or other pleading proposed to be filed therein, otherwise the application shall not be granted, and shall be made within a reasonable time, in no case exceeding six months, after the judgment, dismissal, order, or proceeding was taken.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 473, subd. (b).) 

 

Here, Defendant requests an order setting aside and vacating the Judgment entered on February 20, 2024 against Defendant. The motion is timely filed because Defendant filed the original motion on May 22, 2024, within six months of the Judgment. Further, Defendant provides its responsive pleading to the action as required by the Code of Civil Procedure. (Yurcich Decl., ¶ 4; Ex. “B.”) Defendant has demonstrated good cause exists to set aside and vacate the Judgment as it was entered through mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable negligent of Defendant’s prior counsel. Specifically, the Declaration of Adolfo Landeros, President of Defendant, provides that on March 7, 2023, Plaintiff agreed to settle this matter by executing a “Release of Claims" with Defendant for claim numbered WC-CM-816672. (Landeros Decl., ¶ 5; Ex. “C.”) Per the Release, Plaintiff agreed, in consideration of payment by Defendant in the amount of $15,231.14, to release Defendant and dismiss with prejudice his claim numbered WC-CM-816672. (Id.) Defendant’s prior counsel paid the amount of $15,231.14 directly to Plaintiff mistakenly instead of sending the payment to the Office of Labor Commissioner. (Landeros Decl., ¶ 6; Ex. “D.”) Plaintiff received and cashed the cashier's check numbered 1123446748 in the amount $15,231.14. (Landeros Decl., ¶ 7.) The award of the Labor Commissioner against Defendant on February 20, 2024 was based on mistake as this matter should have been dismissed by mutual agreement of the parties. (Landeros Decl., ¶ 7.)

 

CONCLUSION

 

In all, Defendant 3D Engineering & Manufacturing Inc.’s Motion to Set Aside/Vacate

Judgment Entered on 02/20/2024 is GRANTED.  

 

            The Court sets an Order to Show Cause Re: Dismissal (Settlement) for August 19, 2024 at 9:30 a.m.

 

Moving party is ordered to give Notice of this Court’s Ruling.  A responsive pleading or a Request for Dismissal is to be filed within the next 20 days.
