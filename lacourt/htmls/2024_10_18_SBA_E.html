DEPARTMENT E LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23TRCV04190    Hearing Date: October 18, 2024    Dept: E

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Southwest District

Torrance Dept. E

 

COLLIN J. BURT,

 

 

 

Plaintiff,

 

Case No.:

 

23TRCV04190

 

vs.

 

 

[Tentative] Sustained

 

 

US BANK, CLARISA ESPINOZA, et al.,

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Defendants.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Hearing Date:             October 18, 2024

 

Moving Parties:          Defendants US Bank and Clarisa Espinoza

 

Responding Party:      Plaintiff Colin J. Burt

 

HEARING: Demurrer to the Second Amended Complaint

 

            The Court considered the moving papers.

 

RULING

           

            The Court sustains the Demurrer without leave to amend.

 

BACKGROUND

 

           On December 21, 2023, Plaintiff Colin J. Burt filed the initial Complaint alleging causes of action common counts, grand larceny, and negligence  against Defendants US Bank and Clarisa Espinoza. Plaintiff sought compensatory damages and punitive damages in the amount of $100,000.

 

            On January 22, 2024, Defendants US Bank and Clarisa Espinoza filed the Demurrer to the initial Complaint.

           

            On January 31, 2024, Defendants filed the Amended Demurrer to the Initial Complaint.

           

On February 8, 2024, the Court sustained Defendants’ Demurrer with leave to amend.

 

On May 2, 2024, Plaintiff filed the First Amended Complaint.

 

On June 20, 2024, Defendants Filed the Demurrer to the First Amended Complaint.

 

            On July 19, 2024, the Court sustained Defendants’ Demurrer to the First Amended Complaint with leave to amend.

 

            On July 25, 2024, Plaintiff filed the operative “Amended Amended Complaint” (Second Amended Complaint) alleging causes of action for breach of contract, common counts, invasion of privacy, and defamation.

 

            On August 26, 2024, Defendants filed the Demurrer to the Second Amended Complaint.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

            A demurrer can be used only to challenge defects that appear on the face of the pleading under attack or from matters outside the pleading that are judicially noticeable. (Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318.) “To survive a demurrer, the complaint need only allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action; each evidentiary fact that might eventually form part of the plaintiff’s proof need not be alleged.” (C.A. v. William S. Hart Union High School Dist. (2012) 

53 Cal.4th 861, 872.) For the purpose of testing the sufficiency of the cause of action, the demurrer admits the truth of all material facts properly pleaded. (Aubry v. Tri-City Hospital Dist. (1992) 2 Cal.4th 962, 966-967.) A demurrer “does not admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law.” (Daar v. Yellow Cab Co. (1967) 67 Cal.2d 695, 713.) 

 

A pleading is uncertain if it is ambiguous or unintelligible. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (f).) A demurrer for uncertainty may lie if the failure to label the parties and claims renders the complaint so confusing defendant cannot tell what he or she is supposed to respond to. (Williams v. Beechnut Nutrition Corp. (1986) 185 Cal.App.3d 135, 139, fn. 2.) However, “[a] demurrer for uncertainty is strictly construed, even where a complaint is in some respects uncertain because ambiguities can be clarified under modern discovery procedures.” (Khoury v. Maly’s of California, Inc. (1993) 14 Cal.App.4th 612, 616.) 

 

 

DISCUSSION

 

Meet and Confer 

 

Before filing a demurrer, “the demurring party shall meet and confer in person or by telephone with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to demurrer for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer.” (Emphasis added, Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41, subd. (a).) 

 

            On August 2, 2024, Defendants attempted to meet and confer with Plaintiff via phone and email. However, Plaintiff did not respond prior to the filing of this Demurrer. (Howe Declaration ¶ 16; Exhibit M.) The Court finds this is sufficient to satisfy the meet and confer requirement.

 

Cause of Action 1 – Breach of Contract

 

            “To establish a cause of action for breach of contract, the plaintiff must plead and prove (1) the existence of the contract, (2) the plaintiff’s performance or excuse for nonperformance, (3) the defendant’s breach, and (4) resulting damages to the plaintiff.”  (Maxwell v. Dolezal (2014) 231 Cal.App.4th 93, 97-98 [internal citation omitted].) For a written contract, the plaintiff may “plead the legal effect of the contract rather than the price language.” (Ibid.) To “plead a contract by its legal effect, plaintiff must ‘allege the substance of its relevant terms. This is more difficult, for it requires a careful analysis of the instrument, comprehensiveness in statement, and avoidance of legal conclusions.’” (McKell v. Washington Mutual, Inc. (2006) 142 Cal.App.4th 1457, 1489.)  

 

            Defendants argue that Plaintiff's Second Amended Complaint fails to establish sufficient facts to support the first cause of action for Breach of Contract. (Opposition p. 6.) For a breach of contract claim to be valid, Plaintiff must demonstrate that a contract existed between him and Defendants, that he fulfilled his contractual obligations, that the conditions for Defendants’ performance were met, and that US Bank failed to perform its required duties, causing harm to Plaintiff. (Opposition p. 7.) However, Plaintiff did not allege that a contract was ever formed with US Bank, nor did he provide facts showing that any contract conditions were unmet or that US Bank breached any terms. (Opposition 7.)

 

            Plaintiff submitted no opposition to this motion.

 

            In the Complaint, Plaintiff states that an oral contract was created, under which Plaintiff agreed to pay back 100,000$ over an uncertain amount of time at an uncertain interest rate. (SAC p. 6.)  Plaintiff further alleges that Defendants have not made the agreed upon principal payments. (SAC p. 6.)

 

When ruling on a demurrer, the Court “does not admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law.” (Daar, supra, 67 Cal.2d 695, 713.) A demurrer to a breach of contract claim may be sustained when the terms are objectively uncertain. (Performance Plastering v. Richmond American Homes of California, Inc. (2007) 153 Cal.App.4th 659, 671.)

 

  Here, Plaintiff has pleaded no facts that show the essential terms of the purported contract, including the interest rate or duration of the loan. Instead, he merely concludes that a contract was created for a $100,000 loan without any defined interest rate or duration. (See, Daar, supra, 67 Cal.2d 695, 713; Performance Plastering, 153 Cal.App.4th 659, 671.) Therefore, the demurrer to the first cause of action for breach of contract is sustained with leave to amend.

           

Cause of Action 2 – Common Counts

 

            “Recovery in quantum meruit does not require a contract.” (Maglica v. Maglica (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 442, 449.) Instead, the party must show “the circumstances were such that ‘the services were rendered under some understanding or expectation of both parties that compensation therefor was to be made.” (Chodos v. Borman (2014) 227 Cal.App.4th 76, 96.) “The underlying idea behind quantum meruit is the law's distaste for unjust enrichment. If one has received a benefit which one may not justly retain, one should restore the aggrieved party to his or her former position by return of the thing or its equivalent in money.” (Maglica, supra, 66 Cal.App.4th at p. 449.)

 

            Defendants argue that Plaintiff's Second Amended Complaint fails to provide any facts or reasonable inferences suggesting that Plaintiff was expressly or impliedly requested to perform services for Defendants. (Opposition p. 8.)

 

            Plaintiff submitted no opposition to this motion.

 

            The Complaint states that Defendants became indebted to Plaintiff for money had and received by Defendants for the use and benefit of Plaintiff. (SAC p. 6.)

 

            Plaintiff has failed to allege any facts suggesting Plaintiff rendered any service for Defendants, for which he expected to be paid. (See, Chodos, supra, 227 Cal.App.4th 76, 96.) Therefore, the Court sustains the Demurrer to the second cause of action with leave to amend.

 

Cause of Action 3 – Invasion of Privacy

 

            “It is well settled that there are three elements of a cause of action predicated on tortious invasion of privacy. First, the disclosure of the private facts must be a public disclosure. Second, the facts disclosed must be private facts and not public ones. Third, the matter made public must be one which would be offensive and objectionable to a reasonable person of ordinary sensibilities.” (Sipple v. Chron. Publ’g Co. (1984) 154 Cal.App.3d 1040, 1045 [internal citations omitted].)

 

            Defendants argue that Plaintiff's complaint fails to provide any facts supporting a claim for invasion of privacy. (Opposition p. 8.) Plaintiff does not allege any facts suggesting he had a reasonable expectation of privacy in the information he voluntarily provided to the bank when applying for a loan. There is no claim of intrusion or disclosure of personal information nor any allegation of harm resulting from such an invasion. (Opposition p. 8-9.)

 

            Plaintiff submitted no opposition to this motion.

 

            The Complaint states that Plaintiff entered one of Defendant’s branches, requesting a loan to purchase a Tesla. He then was asked basic questions by Defendants about the loan.

 

            Therefore, because Plaintiff has alleged no facts that show Defendants disclosed any fact about Plaintiff, nor that Defendants intruded in any way on Plaintiff's privacy, the Court sustains the Demurrer to the third cause of action with leave to amend. (See, Sipple, supra, 154 Cal.App.3d 1040, 1045.)

 

Cause of Action 4 – Defamation

           

            The elements of a defamation claim are (1) a publication that is (2) false, (3) defamatory, (4) unprivileged, and (5) has a natural tendency to injure or causes special damage. (John Doe 2 v. Superior Ct. (2016) 1 Cal.App.5th 1300, 1312 [internal citations and quotations omitted].) The defamatory statement must specifically refer to, or be of and concerning, the plaintiff.” (Ibid.)

           

            Defendants argue that Plaintiff's Second Amended Complaint fails to provide any facts supporting a defamation claim. (Opposition p. 9-10.) Plaintiff does not allege that US Bank made any statements to a third party or that anyone interpreted a statement as defamatory. (Opposition p. 9-10.)

 

            Plaintiff submitted no opposition to this motion.

 

            The Complaint states that Plaintiff entered one of Defendant’s branches, requesting a loan to purchase a Tesla. He was then asked basic questions by the Defendants about the loan. The Complaint further alleges that Plaintiff made a physical advance toward Defendant and put her under citizen’s arrest.

 

            Plaintiff has pleaded no facts that show Defendants made any false publication about him. (See, John Doe 2, supra, 1 Cal.App.5th 1300, 1312.) Therefore, the Court sustains the Demurrer to the fourth cause of action without leave to amend. In deciding not to grant leave to amend, the Count notes that Plaintiff failed to correct the same deficiencies from the two prior complaints.
