Hearing Date:              February 2, 2024        

 

Moving Parties:          Defendants Phichkeang In, TK Donuts Inc., and TK Fam Investment LLC

Responding Party:       None  

Motion to Seal

 

            The court considered the moving papers. No opposition has been filed.

RULING

            The motion is GRANTED. The court ORDERS that the exhibits containing the unredacted social security number within defendants’ respective Requests for Judicial Notice in Support of Demurrer, filed on June 15, 2023 and October 20, 2023, are sealed.

At this time, no other records relating to this case are to be sealed.  No person other than the court is authorized to inspect the sealed records.  CRC Rule 2.551(e)(2), (3).   

The court directs the court clerk to comply with the requirements and procedures set forth in CRC Rule 2.551(e) and (f) with respect to the records the court has ordered to be filed under seal.  

BACKGROUND

            On October 18, 2021, Plaintiff Lidia Barba initiated this against defendants Peter Seav (“Seav”), Bunthoeurn Plong (“Plong”), Jane Doe and Does 1 through 50, alleging the following causes of action: (1) Discrimination in Violation of the FEHA (Gov’t Code §§ 12940, et seq.); (2) Harassment in Violation of the FEHA (Gov’t Code §§ 12940, et seq.); (3) Failure to Prevent Discrimination and Harassment in Violation of the FEHA (Gov’t Code §§ 12940, et seq.); (4) Sex-Based Discrimination in Violation of the Unruh Civil Rights Act (Civil Code § 51); (5) Sexual Harassment in Violation of the Unruh Civil Rights Act (Civil Code § 51); (6) Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress; (7) Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress; (8) Negligence; (9) Negligent Hiring and Supervision; and (10) Wrongful Constructive Termination in Violation of Public Policy.

            On May 20, 2022, plaintiff voluntarily dismissed Seav from the action.

            On January 18, 2023, plaintiff filed Amendments to Complaint, naming TK Fam Investment LLC (“TK Fam”) and TK Donuts, Inc. (“TK Donuts”) as defendants.

            On January 24, 2023, plaintiff filed an Amendment to Complaint, naming Phickheang In (“In”) as a defendant.

            On July 31, 2023, plaintiff filed the operative First Amended Complaint against defendants Plong, Inc. TK Donuts, Rose Donuts and Ice Cream TK Fam and Does 3 through 50, alleging the following causes of action: (1) Sexual Harassment in Violation of the FEHA; (2) Battery; (3) Assault; (4) Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress; (5) Sexual Assault and Intimidation by Violence (The Ralph Civil Rights Act of 1976); (6) Interference with Rights by Threat, Intimidation, or Coercion (Civil Code § 52.1); (7) Gender Violence (Civil Code § 52.4); (8) Failure to Prevent Harassment (Gov’t Code § 12940(k); (9) Constructive Discharge in Violation of Public Policy; (10) Negligent Hiring and Supervision; and (11) Violation of Business & Professions Code §§ 17200, et seq.

            On October 20, 2023, Defendants In, TK Donuts, and TK Fam filed their respective demurrers to the FAC, which are set to be heard on March 3, 2023.

            Now, Defendants In, TK Donuts, and TK Fam collectively move to retroactively seal a portion of an exhibit filed in each of their Requests for Judicial Notice on June 15, 2023 and October 20, 2023. No opposition has been filed.

 

LEGAL AUTHORITY

California Rules of Court (“CRC”), Rule 2.550(c) states:  “Unless confidentiality is required by law, court records are presumed to be open.”  But a party may move to seal records pursuant to Rules 2.550-2.551.  CRC Rule 2.551(b)(1) states:  “A party requesting that a record be filed under seal must file a motion or an application for an order sealing the record.  The motion or application must be accompanied by a memorandum and a declaration containing facts sufficient to justify the sealing.”  CRC Rule 2.550(d) states:  “The court may order that a record be filed under seal only if it expressly finds facts that establish:   

(1) There exists an overriding interest that overcomes the right of public access to the record; 

(2) The overriding interest supports sealing the record; 

(3) A substantial probability exists that the overriding interest will be prejudiced if the record is not sealed; 

(4) The proposed sealing is narrowly tailored; and 

(5) No less restrictive means exist to achieve the overriding interest.” 

 

DISCUSSION

            Defendants In, TK Donuts, and TK Fam requests the court to retroactively seal a portion of an exhibit filed in their respective Requests for Judicial Notice because this repeated exhibit inadvertently includes an unredacted social security number.  Motion at pp. 3-4; Takhsh Decl. ¶¶ 3-5.  In order to further protect this private information, the motion does not identify the specific exhibit but defendants’ counsel will provide this information on the day of the hearing.  Motion at pg. 3, fn. 1.

The court makes the following findings of facts that establish the requirements set forth in CRC Rule 2.550(d):  

(1) There exists an overriding interest in protecting disclosure of an individual’s private information, such as a social security number.  See Takhsh Decl. ¶ 3. 

(2) The overriding interest of protecting the individual supports sealing the records because such information is sensitive and private.   

(3) A substantial probability exists that the overriding interest of protecting this information will be prejudiced if the records are not sealed.  See CRC Rule 1.201.

(4) The proposed sealing is narrowly tailored to only include the social security number.  

(5) No less restrictive means exist to achieve the overriding interest because of the nature of the record to be sealed. 

The motion is therefore GRANTED.   The court orders defendant to file a duplicate copy of the exhibits with appropriate redactions that are consistent with this ruling within 5 days.

Defendant is ordered to give notice of ruling.
