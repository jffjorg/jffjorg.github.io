MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES TO SPECIAL INTERROGATORIES FROM PLAINTIFF DAN SACKHEIM

Defendant Paramount Pictures Corporation, Inc. (“Paramount”) moves the court for an order (1) compelling plaintiff Dan Sackheim (“Plaintiff”) to provide verified responses to Paramount’s Special Interrogatories, numbers 93, 94, 96, 97, 99, 100, 102, 103, 105, 106, 108, 109, 111, 112, 114, 115, 117, 118, 120, 121, 123, 124, 126, 127, 129, 130, 132, 133, 137 138, 142, 144, 150, 152, 154, 155, 157, and 158, and (2) awarding sanctions in favor of Paramount and against Plaintiff in the amount of $1,385.

First, the court denies Paramount’s motion to the extent that it requests that the court compel Plaintiff’s further responses because Paramount’s separate statement is defective.  (Mills v. U.S. Bank (2008) 166 Cal.App.4th 871, 893 [court has the discretion to deny a motion to compel further responses for failure to comply with separate statement requirements].)

A “separate statement must be full and complete so that no person is required to review any other document in order to determine the full request and the full response . . . .  The separate statement must include—for each discovery request . . . —the following: [¶¶] (2) The text of each response, answer, or objection, and any further responses or answers[.]”  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 3.1345, subd. (c)(2) [emphasis added].)  Here, Paramount’s separate statement does not include Plaintiff’s full responses to the Special Interrogatories and therefore is defective.  (Ibid.; See, e.g., Paramount Separate Statement, p. 2:17-23 [summarizing Plaintiff’s response to Special Interrogatory no. 93] and Skalsky Decl., Ex. C, pp. 2:11-3:3 [Plaintiff’s full response to Special Interrogatory no. 93].)

Second, the court denies Paramount’s motion to the extent that it requests that the court compel Plaintiff’s initial responses to its Special Interrogatories as moot because Plaintiff has presented evidence, in opposition, showing that he has since served supplemental, verified responses to the discovery at issue.  (Smith Decl., Ex. G.)

Third, the court finds that the circumstances presented make the imposition of sanctions unjust and therefore denies Paramount’s request for monetary sanctions against Plaintiff.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (c).)

MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES TO FORM INTERROGATORIES FROM DEFENDANT SKYDANCE TELEVISION, LLC

Plaintiff moves the court for an order (1) compelling defendant Skydance Television, LLC (“Skydance”) to provide further responses to Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, Set One, numbers 3.7, 15.1, 16.1, and 16.9, and (2) awarding sanctions in favor of Plaintiff and against Skydance in the amount of $5,469.

The court grants Plaintiff’s motion to compel Skydance’s further responses to Form Interrogatories, numbers 3.7, 15.1, 16.1, and 16.9, because Skydance’s answers to those interrogatories are evasive and incomplete.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (a)(1).)

The court finds that the circumstances presented make the imposition of sanctions unjust and therefore denies Plaintiff’s request for monetary sanctions against Skydance.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (d).)

MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES TO FORM INTERROGATORIES FROM DEFENDANT PLATINUM DUNES PRODUCTIONS

Plaintiff moves the court for an order (1) compelling defendant Platinum Dunes Productions (“Platinum Dunes”) to provide further responses to Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, Set Ones, numbers 3.7, 15.1, 16.1, and 16.9, and (2) awarding sanctions in favor of Plaintiff and against Platinum Dunes in the amount of $5,469.

The court grants Plaintiff’s motion to compel Platinum Dunes’ further responses to Form Interrogatories, numbers 3.7, 15.1, 16.1, and 16.9, because Platinum Dunes’ answers to those interrogatories are evasive and incomplete.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (a)(1).)

The court finds that the circumstances presented make the imposition of sanctions unjust and therefore denies Plaintiff’s request for monetary sanctions against Platinum Dunes.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (d).)

ORDER

            The court denies defendant Paramount Pictures Corporation’s motion to compel further responses to special interrogatories.

            The court grants plaintiff Dan Sackheim’s motion to compel further responses to form interrogatories from defendant Skydance Television, LLC as follows.

            Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300, the court orders defendant Skydance Television, LLC to serve further, full and complete answers to plaintiff Dan Sackheim’s Form Interrogatories, numbers 3.7, 15.1, 16.1, and 16.9, which comply with Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.210-2030.220, and 2030.250, within 20 days of the date of this order.

            The court denies plaintiff Dan Sackheim’s request for monetary sanctions against defendant Skydance Television, LLC.

            The court grants plaintiff Dan Sackheim’s motion to compel further responses to form interrogatories from defendant Platinum Dunes Productions as follows.

            Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300, the court orders defendant Platinum Dunes Productions to serve further, full and complete answers to plaintiff Dan Sackheim’s Form Interrogatories, numbers 3.7, 15.1, 16.1, and 16.9, which comply with Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.210-2030.220, and 2030.250, within 20 days of the date of this order.

            The court denies plaintiff Dan Sackheim’s request for monetary sanctions against defendant Platinum Dunes Productions.
