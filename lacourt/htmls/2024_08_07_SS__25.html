(CCP § 664.6)

 

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

The hearing on Plaintiff State Farm’s Motion to Vacate the Dismissal Entered on 11/02/2023, and Enter Judgment against Defendant Kyle Lapesarde is GRANTED. 

 

Judgment by Court on Stipulation is entered for Plaintiff and against Defendant Kyle Lapesarde in the TOTAL amount of $1,365.00

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

            On April 1, 2022, Plaintiff State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) filed an action against Defendants Kyle Lapesarde (“Kyle”) and Mary Lapesarde aka Mary J Lapesarde (“Mary”) (collectively “Defendants”) for subrogation, stemming from an automobile collision between Defendant Kyle, on the one hand, and an individual insured by Plaintiff’s automobile insurance policy, on the other hand.  (Compl.)  Defendant Kyle was alleged to have been operating the vehicle with the express or implied permission of Defendant Mary.  Plaintiff compensated the insured for claimed damages in the amount of $3,850.89 and filed the instant claim against Defendants for allegedly causing the damages.  (Ibid. pp. 2-3.)  On July 13, 2022, Defendants filed a joint Answer.

 

On September 28, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Stipulation for Dismissal with Reservation to Vacate and Enter Judgment Upon Breach (“Stipulation”), signed by both parties, to dismiss the action on the premise that Defendant Kyle would compensate Plaintiff for the settlement amount of $3,381.17.  On November 2, 2023, the Court dismissed the entire case without prejudice pursuant to the Stipulation.

 

On May 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Vacate Dismissal and Enter Judgment (“Motion”).  Plaintiff has submitted the motion on the moving papers and will not appear at the hearing.  (Mot. p. 2.)

 

No opposition has been filed.

 

II.                 Legal Standard & Discussion

 

CCP § 664.6, provides a summary procedure that enables judges to enforce a settlement agreement by entering a judgment pursuant to the terms of the parties’ settlement.  In particular, the statute provides:

 

(a) If parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside of the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement.  If requested by the parties, the court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement.

 

(b) For purposes of this section, a writing is signed by a party if it is signed by any of the following:

 

(1) The party.

(2) An attorney who represents the party.

(3) If the party is an insurer, an agent who is authorized in writing by the insurer to sign on the insurer's behalf.

 

CCP § 664.6(a)-(b) (emphasis added).

 

 

 

 

 

III.              Discussion

 

A. Retention of Jurisdiction

 

“‘[V]oluntary dismissal of an action or special proceeding terminates the court’s jurisdiction over the matter.’  (Conservatorship of Martha P. (2004) 117 Cal.App.4th 857, 867) [12 Cal.Rptr.3d 142.) ‘If requested by the parties,’ however, ‘the [trial] court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce [a] settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement.’ (§ 664.6, italics added.)”  (Mesa RHF Partners, L.P. v. City of Los Angeles (2019) 33 Cal.App.5th 913, 917.)  “‘Because of its summary nature, strict compliance with the requirements of section 664.6 is prerequisite to invoking the power of the court to impose a settlement agreement.’”  (Ibid. (quoting Sully-Miller Contracting Co. v. Gledson/Cashman Construction, Inc. (2002) 103 Cal.App.4th 30, 37).)

 

“A request for the trial court to retain jurisdiction under section 664.6 ‘must conform to the same three requirements which the Legislature and the courts have deemed necessary for section 664.6 enforcement of the settlement itself: the request must be made (1) during the pendency of the case, not after the case has been dismissed in its entirety, (2) by the parties themselves, and (3) either in a writing signed by the parties or orally before the court.’”  (Ibid. (quoting Wackeen v. Malis (2002) 97 Cal.App.4th 429, 440).)  “The ‘request must be express, not implied from other language, and it must be clear and unambiguous.’” (Ibid. (quoting Wackeen, supra, 97 Cal.App.4th at 440).)

 

Here, the parties signed a Stipulation for Dismissal with Reservation to Vacate and Enter Judgment Upon Breach (“Stipulation”) containing the parties’ agreement for the Court to retain jurisdiction pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure §664.6 to enforce the terms of the stipulation and enter judgment in the event of default.  (Stipulation ¶ 3.)  Prior to the dismissal of this action, the Stipulation was signed by the parties and submitted to the Court.  (Ibid. at p. 3.)  The Court dismissed the entire case without prejudice pursuant to the Stipulation and expressly stated that it “shall retain jurisdiction pursuant to CCP § 664.6.”  (11-2-23 Order.)

 

The Court finds that the Stipulation complies with § 664.6 requirements and the Court has retained jurisdiction to enter judgment pursuant to the parties’ Stipulation in this action.

 

B. Entry of Judgment

 

The Stipulation Agreement filed on September 28, 2023, provides that Plaintiff and Defendant Kyle agreed to dismiss the action on the premise that Defendant Kyle would compensate Plaintiff for the settlement amount of $3,381.17.  (Stipulation ¶ 2.)  Pursuant to the Stipulation, Defendant’s insurance, Farmers Insurance, would make an initial payment of $2,381.17 by September 28, 2023, and subsequently, Kyle would make two payments of $500, one on November 1, 2023, and one on February 1, 2023 .  (Ibid.)

 

The Stipulation also provides that in the event Defendant fails to make timely payments, Plaintiff will mail a letter to Defendant regarding the default.  (Ibid. at ¶ 3.)  If Defendant does not remedy the default within 14 days of receiving notice, “then plaintiff will be entitled to have any dismissal in this action set aside and judgment entered for the settlement amount, minus credit for payments received, plus any costs associated with entering the judgment not to exceed $500.”  (Ibid.)  The declaration of Plaintiff’s counsel shall be sufficient proof for judgment to be entered.  (Ibid.)

 

On May 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion for an order to vacate the dismissal and enter judgment against Defendant Kyle.  (Memo. p. 1.)  According to Plaintiff’s counsel, Defendant’s insurance carrier made a one-time payment of $2,381.17; however, Defendant failed to make any payments.  (Reese Decl. ¶ 4) Around April 29, 2024, Plaintiff mailed Defendant a letter regarding the missed payments; however, Defendant has not made any payments.  (Ibid. at ¶¶ 5-6, Ex. C.)

 

Plaintiff seeks to have the dismissal set aside and judgment entered against Defendant Kyle in the amount of $1,365 as follows: $3,381.17 (settlement amount), minus $2,381.17 (payment made by Defendant’s insurance carrier), plus $365 (court costs).  (Ibid. at ¶ 7.)  The court costs consist of $225 complaint filing fee, $80 service of process fee, and $60.00 motion fee.  (Ibid.)

 

The Court finds the Stipulation to be valid and enforceable under Code of Civil Proc. § 664.6.  Plaintiff provides evidence that Defendant defaulted on payments and has not made any additional payments after Plaintiff sent notice.  Thus, a valid and signed stipulation agreement was breached and the Court retained jurisdiction to enter judgment upon breach.

 

 

IV.              Conclusion & Order

 

For the foregoing reasons,

 

The hearing on Plaintiff State Farm’s Motion to Vacate the Dismissal Entered on 11/02/2023, and Enter Judgment against Defendant Kyle Lapesarde is GRANTED. 

 

Judgment by Court on Stipulation is entered for Plaintiff and against Defendant Kyle Lapesarde in the TOTAL amount of $1,365.00

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

On June 10, 2024, Petitioner Miami New Times, LLC (“Petitioner”) filed a Petition to Compel Answers to Questions Not Answered at Deposition (“Petition”) against Respondent Erik Laibe (“Respondent”).

 

On June 25, 2024, Scott D. Ponce, Esq. (“Ponce”) filed the instant Application to Appear Pro Hac Vice (“Application”) for Petitioner Miami New Times, LLC, in association with Counsel of Record Lindsay E. Catt (“Catt”).  No opposition has been filed.

 

On July 17, 2024, the Court found that the following cases, 24STCP01890 and 22STCP03790, are related within the meaning of California Rules of Court, rule 3.300(a). 22STCP03790 is the lead case.  Both cases were assigned to Commissioner Karine Mkrtchyan in Department 25 at Spring Street Courthouse.

II.                 Legal Standard

 

California Rules of Court rule 9.40 provides that an attorney in good standing in another jurisdiction may apply to appear as counsel pro hac vice in the State of California by filing a verified application together with proof of service by mail of a copy of the application and notice of hearing on all parties who have appeared in the case and on the State Bar of California at its San Francisco office, with payment of a $50.00 fee, so long as that attorney is not a resident of the State of California, and is not regularly engaged in substantial business, professional, or other activities in the State of California.  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40.)

 

“The application must state: (1) the applicant’s residence and office addresses; (2) the courts to which the applicant has been admitted to practice and the dates of admission; (3) that the applicant is a member of good standing in those courts; (4) that the applicant is not currently suspended or disbarred in any court; (5) the title of each court and cause in which the applicant has filed an application to appear as counsel pro hac vice in this state in the preceding two years, the date of each application, and whether or not it was granted; and (6) the name, address, and telephone number of the active member of the State Bar of California who is attorney of record in the local action.”  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40(d).)

 

III.              Discussion

 

Counsel Scott D. Ponce (“Ponce”) has submitted the instant verified Application to appear as counsel pro hac vice for Petitioner Miami New Times, LLC, in association with Counsel of Record Lindsay E. Catt.  (Ponce Decl. ¶ 12.)  He has also submitted Proof of Service indicating that Respondent and the State Bar of California have been served with the Notice, Application, and supporting documents by UPS overnight delivery and electronic transmission on June 2, 2024.  (App. p. 8.)

 

Counsel Ponce declares that he has been admitted to practice in several jurisdictions and has provided the dates of admission.  (Ponce Decl. at ¶¶ 3-4.)  He is a member of good standing in all jurisdictions and is “not currently suspended or disbarred in any court.”  (Ibid. at ¶¶ 5-7.)  Counsel is not regularly engaged in the practice of law or other business in the State of California; however, on March 15, 2023, he was admitted pro hac vice in a related case, Charlie Ehrlich v. Miami Times, LLC, 22STCP0370, in Los Angeles Superior Court.  (Ibid. at ¶¶ 8-9.)  He has provided his residence and home addresses, which are both located in the state of Florida.  (Ibid. at ¶¶ 1-2.)  Finally, Counsel provides the name, address, and telephone number of Petitioner’s attorney of record, Lindsay E. Catt, who is an active member of the State Bar of California.  (Ibid. ¶ 12.)  In her declaration, Catt states that she served a copy of this Application on the San Francisco offices of the State Bar California with the necessary fee.  (Catt Decl. ¶ 7.)

 

Counsel has complied with all requirements for the instant Application to be Admitted Pro Hac Vice.  Accordingly, Counsel Ponce’s Application to Appear Pro Hac Vice is granted.

 

 

IV.              Conclusion & Order

 

For the foregoing reasons,

 

Counsel Scott D. Ponce’s Application to Appear as Counsel Pro Hac Vice for the Petitioner is GRANTED.

 

The Court sets an Order to Show Cause Re:  Pro Hac Vice Status of Scott D. Ponce, Esq. / Pro Hac Vice Renewal Fees is set for 08/07/2025 at 9:30 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse.
