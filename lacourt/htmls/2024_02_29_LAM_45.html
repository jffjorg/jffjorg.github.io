Background

            This is a habitability case. Plaintiffs Isai Edgar Castellanos; Denise Babonoyaba; Jayleen Denise Castellanos, a minor by and through her guardian ad litem, Denise Babonyaba; Alazay Jazleen Castellanos, a minor by and through her guardian ad litem, Denise Babonyaba; Janey Lexine Castellanos, a minor by and through her guardian ad litem, Denise Babonyaba; Isai Edgar Castellanos Jr., a minor by and through her guardian ad litem, Denise Babonyaba filed this action on September 9, 2021 against defendant Potomac Gardens LLC. The Complaint alleges causes of action for (1) Breach of Implied Warranty of Habitability; (2) Breach of Statutory Warranty of Habitability; (3) Breach of the Covenant of Quiet Enjoyment; (4) Negligence; (5) Violation of Civ. Code § 1942.4; and (6) Private Nuisance.

            The Complaint alleges the following: Defendant Potomac Gardens LLC is the owner and manager of an apartment building located at 3868 Potomac Ave. Los Angeles, CA 90008. (Compl., ¶ 1.) Plaintiffs resided in Unit 9 of this apartment building. (Id. at ¶ 2.) Defendant failed to correct substandard conditions on the subject property, including garbage disposal deficiencies; electrical and plumbing deficiencies; lack of hot water, water leaks, and water damaged ceilings; floors and walls containing mold and mildew; rodent, insect, and pest infestations; security and safety deficiencies; storage deficiencies; refuse disposal

deficiencies; and loss of use of the premises for various periods of times from May 2017 to the present. (Id. at ¶¶ 8-9.)

            On August 25, 2023, Plaintiffs filed (1) a motion to compel the deposition of Defendant s agent, Sharishi Kobayashi; and (2) a motion to compel the deposition of TDI Properties, Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable. Defendant filed an omnibus opposition on January 29, 2024. Plaintiffs replied on January 29, 2024.

 

Legal Standard

CCP § 2025.450(a) states: “If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.”

The motion must be accompanied by a declaration showing a reasonable and good faith attempt to informally resolve each issue presented or, when the deponent fails to attend the deposition and produce the requested documents or things, the motion must be accompanied by a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance. (CCP § 2025.450(b)(2).)

Discussion

Merits of the Motions

Plaintiffs move to compel the depositions of (1) defendant Potomac Gardens LLC’s agent, Sharishi Kobayashi; and (2) TDI Properties, Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable. Plaintiffs contend they served Notices of Deposition, Defendant did not object to said depositions, and Plaintiffs followed up on multiple occasions regarding new dates but Defendant never responded.

Defendant contends Plaintiffs unilaterally noticed these depositions. Defendant argues they provided alternative dates, but Plaintiff refused to accept them..

The court finds Plaintiffs sufficiently show these motions should be granted. Plaintiffs’ counsel show they property noticed these depositions on July 24, 2023. (Diaz Decl. re Kobayashi and PMK (collectively, “Diaz Decls.”), ¶ 2, Exh. A.) On August 11, 2023, Plaintiffs’ counsel called Defendant’s counsel, who assured Plaintiff’s counsel that they would provide the availability of their clients. (Id. at ¶ 3.) Plaintiffs’ counsel emailed Defendant’s counsel on August 12, 2023 and August 18, 2023 to confirm the depositions of Kobayashi and TDI Properties, Inc.’s PMK, respectively. (Id. at ¶ 4.) Plaintiffs’ counsel received no response, so the depositions continued as noticed and the deponents failed to appear. (Id., Exh. B.)

Defendant contends they offered to provide alternative dates for the depositions multiple times, but Plaintiffs refused. However, the emails provided by Defendant do not appear to show Defendant actually ever gave any specific dates for the depositions. (See Harwell Decl., ¶¶ 3-4, Exhs. A-B.) Defendant’s counsel attests that she “offered to provide dates.” (Id. at ¶ 2.) The court finds that simply offering to provide dates, without actually providing any when Defendant had plenty of opportunities to do so, will not sustain Defendant’s burden and does not justify avoiding depositions. The court notes even the fact that a deposition was unilaterally noticed is not a legally recognized ground to object and avoid the deposition. It is the responding party’s burden to provide alternative dates for a deposition, which Defendant failed to do so in these matters.

The court therefore rules as follows:

The court GRANTS Plaintiff’s motion to compel Sharishi Kobayashi’s deposition. Sharishi Kobayashi’s deposition shall take place on the date, time, and location set forth above.

The court GRANTS Plaintiff’s motion to compel the deposition of TDI Properties, Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable. The deposition of TDI Properties, Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable shall take place on the date, time, and location set forth above.

 

Requests for Monetary Sanctions

Plaintiffs request $2,785 and $2,765 for the Kobayashi motion and the TDI Properties, Inc.’s PMK motion, respectively, as monetary sanctions against Defendant and its counsel of record. Plaintiffs’ counsel’s hourly rate is $750. (Diaz Decls., ¶ 6.) Plaintiffs request to recover three hours per motion and the court reporter fees ($535 for Kobayashi and $515 for TDI Properties, Inc.’s PMK).

The court finds Plaintiffs’ counsel’s hourly rate is reasonable, but the number of hours Plaintiffs seek is unreasonable. These are relatively uncomplicated motions that do not justify the amounts Plaintiffs seek. However, the court recognizes the substantial delay and costs Defendant created in failing to cooperate in discovery without sufficient justification. Plaintiffs seek to recover two hours for preparing the motion and one hour for reviewing opposition, preparing a reply, and attending hearing. (Diaz Decls., ¶ 6.) The court finds reducing Plaintiffs’ recoverable time to one hour for the work on the motion and 0.5 hours for attending hearing is reasonable. The court finds Plaintiffs’ requests for the court reporter fees are reasonable since the deponents’ failure to attend the depositions forced Plaintiffs to incur these costs. Accordingly, Plaintiffs’ total reduced recoverable amounts in attorney’s fees and costs are (1) $1,660 for the Kobayashi motion ([(1 hour + 0.5 hours) x $750] + $535); and (2) $1,640 for the TDI Properties, Inc.’s PMK motion ([(1 hour + 0.5 hours) x $750] + $515)

The court therefore rules as follows:

The court GRANTS Plaintiffs’ request for sanctions against Defendant and its counsel of record in the reduced amount $1,660 for the motion to compel Sharishi Kobayashi’s deposition. The court orders Defendant and its counsel of record, jointly and severally, to pay $1,660 Plaintiffs, through their counsel of record, within 20 days of the date of this motion.

The court GRANTS Plaintiffs’ request for sanctions against Defendant and its counsel of record in the reduced amount of $1,640 for the motion to compel the deposition of TDI Properties, Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable. The court orders Defendant and its counsel of record, jointly and severally, to pay $1,640 Plaintiffs, through their counsel of record, within 20 days of the date of this motion.

 

It is so ordered.

Background

            On April 5, 2023, Plaintiffs Delmorgan Group, LLC, a California limited liability company and Globalist Capital, LLC, a Delaware limited liability company (“Plaintiffs”) filed this operative First Amended Complaint against Defendant Australis Capital, Inc., a Nevada corporation and DOES 1 through 10, inclusive for Breach of Written Contract.

            On January 5, 2024, Polina L. Ross filed this instant Motion to be Relieved As Counsel. No opposition has been filed.

 

 

 

Legal Standard

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Code of Civ. Proc., § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)  

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e)). The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).)  

Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).) A motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) 

 

Discussion

            Attorney Polina L. Ross of Ross Legal Corporation declares an irreparable breakdown in the attorney-client relationship has occurred due to Defendant Australis Capital, Inc. breaching a material term of agreement with attorney regarding representation. Furthermore, Ross declares Defendant Australis Capital, Inc.’s conduct has made it unreasonably difficult to carry out representation. Moreover, Ross avers continued representation would likely result in violation of California Rules of Professional Conduct or the State Bar Act. As such, Ross avers she can no longer represent Defendant Australis Capital, Inc.

            Ross has stated a valid basis to be relieved as counsel. Furthermore, the MC-053 Form indicates the following hearing dates are scheduled in this action: (1) Defendant Australis Capital, Inc.’s Demurrer to Plaintiffs’ First Amended Complaint and Trial setting Conference set for March 13, 2024; (2) Plaintiffs’ Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Admissions, Set One set for March 15, 2024; (3)  Plaintiffs’ Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One set for March 22, 2024; and (4) Plaintiffs’ Motion to Compel Further Responses to Demand for Production of Documents, Set One set for March 22, 2024. Additionally, the MC-051 Form indicates this present motion was served on all parties that have appeared in this case. Lastly, Ross avers that Defendant Australis Capital, Inc. was served with this instant motion via mail at its last known address, confirmed within the past 30 days as current by conversation with Defendant Australis Capital, Inc. and reviewing the State of Nevada, Secretary of State website.

            Therefore, the motion to be relieved as counsel is GRANTED.

 

            It is so ordered.
