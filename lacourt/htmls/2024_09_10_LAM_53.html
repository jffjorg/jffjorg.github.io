[tentative] Order RE:

 

defendant’s motion to compel responses to requests for production of documents

 

 

MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant Sommer Ray Beaty          

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Unopposed

Motion to Compel Responses to Requests for Production of Documents

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this motion.  No opposition papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

Defendant Sommer Ray Beaty (“Defendant”) filed the pending motion to compel responses to requests for production of documents on June 6, 2024, moving the court for an order (1) compelling plaintiffs Anthony Aiello and Anthony’s Worldwide Productions, LLC (“Plaintiffs”) to serve responses to Defendant’s Requests for Production of Documents, Set One, and (2) awarding monetary sanctions in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiffs in the amount of $3,230.

On August 2, 2024, Defendant filed an ex parte application (set for hearing on August 12, 2024) requesting that the court shorten the time to hear, and advance the March 12, 2025 hearing on, Defendant’s motion to compel Plaintiffs’ responses to Defendant’s Requests for Production of Documents.  (Aug. 2, 2024 Ex Parte, p. 2:11-15.)  On August 12, 2024, the court issued an order (1) granting Defendant’s ex parte application, (2) advancing the hearing on Defendant’s motion from March 12, 2025 to September 10, 2024, and (3) ordering Defendant to give notice of the court’s ruling.  (Aug. 12, 2024 Minute Order, p. 1.)

Defendant has not filed a proof of service showing that Defendant served Plaintiffs with the court’s August 12, 2024 order.  Moreover, Plaintiffs did not file an opposition to Defendant’s motion.  Thus, it appears that Plaintiffs have not been given notice of the new hearing date on Defendant’s motion to compel their responses to her Requests for Production of Documents.

The court finds that it is appropriate, and therefore exercises its discretion, to continue the hearing on Defendant’s motion in order to ensure that Plaintiffs are given notice of the new hearing date on the motion and to, if desired, file an opposition to Defendant’s motion.

ORDER

            The court orders that the hearing on defendant Sommer Ray Beaty’s motion to compel responses to requests for production of documents is continued to October 9, 2024.

            The court orders defendant Sommer Ray Beaty (1) to give notice of this ruling to plaintiffs Anthony Aiello and Anthony’s Worldwide Productions, LLC, and (2) to file with the court a proof of service of the notice of ruling on plaintiffs Anthony Aiello and Anthony’s Worldwide Productions, LLC no later than September 12, 2024.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

 

DATED:  September 10, 2024

 

_____________________________

Robert B. Broadbelt III

Judge of the Superior Court

Seran Ng, D.M.D., Inc. v. Lu  20STCV49246

Ex Parte Application 9/10/24

Motion for New Trial

The court considered the moving and opposition papers filed in connection with this motion.  No reply papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiffs Amosun Akala, Daniel P. Stelzer, and Stacy Hayes (“Plaintiffs”) filed this action against defendant Los Angeles Unified School District (“Defendant”) on October 31, 2022, alleging seven causes of action for (1) failure to accommodate religious belief or observance; (2) discrimination based on religion; (3) harassment based on religion; (4) discrimination based on national origin; (5) harassment based on national origin; (6) retaliation; and (7) failure to prevent discrimination, harassment, and retaliation.

            On May 7, 2024, the court issued an order granting Defendant’s motion for summary judgment on Plaintiffs’ Complaint.  (May 7, 2024 Order, p. 20:1-3.)  Pursuant to that order, on July 1, 2024, the court entered judgment in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiffs.  (Judgment entered July 1, 2024, p. 2:11-14.)

            Plaintiffs now move the court for an order setting aside the court’s May 7, 2024 order and the July 1, 2024 judgment, and granting a new trial against Defendants.  Plaintiffs move for this relief on the grounds that (1) the evidence was insufficient to justify the court’s decision, (2) the decision is against law, and (3) the court made an error in law.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 657, subds. (6) [new trial may be granted based on “[i]nsufficiency of the evidence to justify the verdict or other decision, or the verdict or other decision is against law”], (7) [new trial may be granted based on “[e]rror in law, occurring at the trial and excepted to by the party making the application”]; Doe v. United Air Lines, Inc. (2008) 160 Cal.App.4th 1500, 1504 [“An order granting summary judgment is properly challenged by a motion for a new trial”].)  Specifically, Plaintiffs have based their motion for a new trial on the sole ground that, in a case pending in Department 24 of the Los Angeles Superior Court, Mitchell, et al. v. Los Angeles Unified School District (LASC Case No. 22STCV29869), the court denied Defendant’s motion for summary judgment.  (Mot., pp. 3:20-5:23.)  Plaintiffs assert that “[t]he ruling in Mitchell shows the error in the” court’s May 7, 2024 order in this action.  (Mot., p. 5:18.)  

            First, the court finds that the matter relied on by Plaintiffs is insufficient to show that the evidence presented by the parties in connection with Defendant’s motion for summary judgment was insufficient to justify the court’s order granting summary judgment in Defendant’s favor.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 657, subd. (6).)  Plaintiffs did not present adequate analysis or evidence showing that, upon weighing the evidence, the court “clearly should have reached a different verdict or decision.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 657.)  Moreover, while Plaintiffs have cited the ruling in the Mitchell case, the order made in Department 24 is not binding on this court.  (Harrott v. County of Kings (2001) 25 Cal.4th 1138, 1146 [“Trial court decisions are not precedents binding on other courts under the principle of stare decisis”]; Sargent Fletcher, Inc. v. Able Corp. (2003) 110 Cal.App.4th 1658, 1669 [“‘[a] written trial court ruling has no precedential value’”].)  The court further finds, after considering the evidence and arguments raised by the parties, that the evidence presented was sufficient to justify the court’s order granting Defendant’s motion for summary judgment.

            Second, the court finds that the matter relied on by Plaintiffs is insufficient to show that (1) the court’s order was against law, and (2) the court made an error in law.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 657, subds. (6), (7).)  While the court notes that Plaintiffs contend that the court erred in granting summary judgment in favor of Defendant because Department 24 denied a motion for summary judgment filed by Defendant in another action, as set forth above, the order issued in that department is not binding on this court.  (Harrott, supra, 25 Cal.4th at p. 1148; Sargent Fletcher, Inc., supra, 110 Cal.App.4th at p. 1669.)  Thus, Plaintiffs have not cited binding authority establishing that the court’s order was against law or that the court’s ruling was, as a matter of law, erroneous.  (Collins v. Sutter Memorial Hospital (2011) 196 Cal.App.4th 1, 17 [noting that a motion for a new trial based on a decision against law “‘is available mainly in two situations: (a) an improper verdict [citation]; (b) a defective statement of decision [citation]’”], 17-18 [a court may grant a new trial based on an error in law “if ‘its original ruling, as a matter of law, was erroneous’”].)

            Thus, for the reasons set forth above, the court finds that its May 7, 2024 order granting summary judgment in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiffs (1) is supported by sufficient evidence, (2) is not against law, and (3) is not erroneous as a matter of law.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 657, subds. (6), (7).)  The court therefore denies Plaintiffs’ motion for a new trial.

ORDER

            The court denies plaintiffs Amosun Akala, Daniel P. Stelzer, and Stacy Hayes’s motion for a new trial.

            The court orders defendant Los Angeles Unified School District to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

DISCUSSION

Now pending before the court are nine petitions seeking approval of the compromise of claims on behalf of nine minor claimants.  First, plaintiff and petitioner Yessica Koh seeks court approval of the settlements made on behalf of minor claimants Andrew Koh, Aileen Reyes, Brandon Hernandez, and Geovanny Reyes.  Second, plaintiff and petitioner Guadalupe Reyes seeks court approval of the settlements made on behalf of minor claimants Cindy Raigoza and Yazmyn Raigoza.  Third, plaintiff and petitioner Liliann Garcia seeks court approval of the settlements made on behalf of minor claimants Carlos Sanchez and Jonathan Sanchez.  Finally, plaintiff and petitioner Monique Martinez seeks court approval of the settlement made on behalf of minor claimant Irie Tapia.

The compromise of a minor’s disputed claim for damages is valid only after it has been approved, upon the filing of a petition, by the court.¿ (Prob. Code, § 3500.)¿ The petition must be verified by the petitioner, must contain a full disclosure of all information that has any bearing upon the reasonableness of the compromise, and must be prepared on Judicial Council form MC-350.¿ (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 7.950.)¿¿ 

Defendants Susan Koepke-Green, Kismet Enterprises LLC, Kismet Enterprises, LLC, Brian Koepke, COIG, LLC, and B&S Industries, LLC have agreed to pay a total of $478,000 to settle this action, of which $6,700 will be separately allocated to each minor claimant.  (Pets., MC-350, ¶¶ 10-11, 16.)  Of the $6,700 allocated to the minor claimants, $1,675 will be paid to counsel for attorney’s fees.  (Pets., MC-350, ¶¶13, 16.)  The remaining $5,025 will be deposited into insured accounts for the minor claimants, subject to withdrawal only on authorization of the court.  (Pets., MC-350, ¶ 18, subd. (b)(2).)

The court has reviewed the petitions and finds the settlements to be fair and reasonable, and in the best interests of minor claimants Andrew Koh, Aileen Reyes, Brandon Hernandez, Geovanny Reyes, Cindy Raigoza, Yazmyn Raigoza, Carlos Sanchez, Jonathan Sanchez, and Irie Tapia.  The court finds that the declaration of Jesus A. Rodriguez is sufficient to support the requests for $1,675 in attorney’s fees, which represents 25 percent of the gross settlement amount of $6,700.  (Pets., Attachment 13a, Rodriguez Decl., ¶¶ 4-5.)  

The court therefore grants the petitions.

The court grants plaintiff and petitioner Liliann Garcia’s (1) petition for approval of compromise of claim for minor claimant Carlos Sanchez, and (2) petition for approval of compromise of claim for minor claimant Jonathan Sanchez.
