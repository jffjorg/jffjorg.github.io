Case Number: 21STCV26615    Hearing Date: December 12, 2024    Dept: 73

12/12/2024 

Dept. 73 

Hon. Rolf Treu, Judge presiding 

 

YUN v. PICO BUILDING SUPPLY CO., et al. (21STCV26615) 

 

Counsel for Plaintiff:  Andrew Kim (Law Offices of Andrew Kim, APC) 

Counsel for Defendants: Thomas Gelini (Bennett, Gelini & Gelini)

 

PLAINTIFF’S MOTION TO SUBSTITUTE COUNSEL (filed 10/17/2024) 

 

TENTATIVE RULING 

 

Plaintiff’s Motion to Substitute Counsel is DENIED.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On July 19, 2021, Plaintiff Jung Won Yun (“Plaintiff” or “Mr. Yun”) filed this action against Defendants Pico Building Supply Corporation (erroneously sued as Pico Building Supply Co.) and Saviss Pico, LLC (“Defendants”).

 

Plaintiff brought this action with causes of action for negligence and premises liability for injuries resulting from a slip and fall that occurred on Defendant’s property. Plaintiff alleges that as he was exiting from the back yard of the property of Defendant Pico, he slipped and fell because “sand and dirt had been spilled from a big truck of Pico.” (Complaint p. 4, 5.)

 

On November 27, 2024, the Court granted Defendants’ Motion to Enforce Settlement.

 

On October 17, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Motion to Substitute Counsel in Form of Declaration, arguing:  

·       The Declaration filed states that it is the Declaration of Jung Won Yun.

·       The Declaration states that he requests the Court to substitute out his counsel

 

Plaintiff’s counsel, Andrew Kim, filed a declaration in opposition, arguing:

·       Kim states the Motion is not genuine and was prepared by an individual named Jin Ree who is claiming to have a claim to a portion of the settlement proceed in this case.

·       Kim states his client, Mr. Yun, never presented a substitution of attorney document to him for signature.

 

No reply was filed.

 

II.ANALYSIS

 

In Plaintiff Jung Won Yun’s Motion to Substitute Counsel, he submits a declaration requesting the Court substitute out his counsel, Andrew Kim. The declaration of Mr. Yun states that Mr. Kim refused to sign a substitution of attorney form. The declaration further states he decided to substitute Mr. Kim out after being disappointed by his work in the instant case and another case. The declaration also states that if the motion is granted he will re-start the settlement process.

 

Plaintiff’s counsel, Andrew Kim, filed a declaration in opposition stating the Motion is not genuine and was prepared by an individual named Jin Ree who is claiming to have a claim to a portion of the settlement proceed in this case. Mr. Ree’s email is on the top left-hand corner of the cover page of the motion and his signature is on the proof of service. The first page of the Declaration of Jung Won Yun states “I, Jin Ree, declare…” Mr. Kim states his client, Mr. Yun, never presented a substitution of attorney document to him for signature.

 

A reply was not filed addressing Mr. Kim’s arguments.

 

The Court does not find there are grounds to grant the motion. Accordingly, Plaintiff’s Motion to Substitute Counsel is DENIED.

 

III. CONCLUSION 

 

Plaintiff’s Motion to Substitute Counsel is DENIED.

Referee’s Motion for Order Directing the Issuance of a Writ of Possession is GRANTED.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On June 1, 2023, Plaintiff Sundo Hong (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against Defendant Sun Be Hong (“Defendant”).

 

This is an action for partition by sale of the commercial property at 6840 S. Crenshaw Blvd., Los Angeles, CA 90043, held equally as joint tenants by Plaintiff and Defendant. (FAC, ¶ 1.) Plaintiff alleges Defendant refused to voluntarily sell the real property in question or buy out the share of Plaintiff’s interest. (Ibid.)

 

On May 1, 2024, the Court entered interlocutory judgment to partition the property and to appoint Brandon Anand as the referee.  

 

On November 6, 2024, the Partition Referee filed a Motion for Order Directing the Issuance of a Writ of Possession.

 

No opposition was filed.

 

II.DISCUSSION

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

Partition is the procedure for segregating and terminating common interests in the same parcel of property. (Summers v. Superior Court (2018) 24 Cal.App.5th 138, 142.) The manner of partition may be in kind—i.e., physical division of the property—or by sale. (Id.) If the parties so stipulate, the court shall order a partition by sale with the proceeds divided among the parties in accordance with their interests in the property as determined in an interlocutory judgment. (Code Civ. Proc., § 872.820(a).)

The procedure of a partition actions is as follows: (1) determine whether the plaintiff has an interest in the property sufficient to maintain a partition action (CCP § 872.710); (2) determine whether the plaintiff has waived her right to partition (CCP § 872.710(b)); (3) entry of an interlocutory judgment of partition (CCP § 872.720(a)) and determination of the manner of partition; (4) appointment of a referee to divide or sell the property (CCP § 873.010(a)); (5) the referee sells the property (CCP §§ 873.670, 873.680); and (6) the referee delivers a report and the Court confirms the sale (CCP §§ 873.710, 873.720(b), 873.730(a) and 873.790(a)). 

 

B.      Discussion

 

The Court appointed referee, Brandan Anand, moves for an order directing the Clerk of the Court to issue a Writ of Possession for the property located at:

 

The auto repair building located on the corner of Crenshaw Boulevard and West 71st Street on the parcel commonly known as 6836 Crenshaw Blvd., Los Angeles, CA 90043-4667, also known as 6840 Crenshaw Blvd., Los Angeles, CA 90043-4667 (the “Property”).

 

On May 1, 2024, the Court entered interlocutory judgment to partition the Property and appointed Brandon Anand as the referee (hereinafter, “Referee”) to manage and sell the Property.  

 

The Property is a mixed-use lot consisting of four buildings, one auto repair building and three separate residential buildings. (Anand Decl., ¶ 2.) The auto repair building, located on the corner of Crenshaw Boulevard and West 71st Street, is currently occupied by a tenant operating a mechanic’s shop called Florence Transmission (the “Tenant”). (Ibid.) The Referee states that upon information and belief, the Tenant does not have a written lease agreement. (Ibid.)

 

Between July to September, 2024, Referee made multiple attempts to contact the Tenant, and the Tenant was non-responsive. (Anand Decl., ¶ 3.) On August 29, Referee sent a letter to the Tenant, via USPS Certified Mail, advising them that, pursuant to the Interlocutory Judgment, rent was to be paid to Referee. (Anand Decl., ¶ 4.) The Tenant remained non-responsive and failed to pay rent for September 2024. (Anand Decl., ¶ 5.)

 

On September 18, 2024, Referee posted a Three-Day Notice to Quit on the Tenant’s building at the Property, and the same notice was mailed to the Tenant on September 19, 2024. (Anand Decl., ¶ 6, Ex. 3.)

 

On October 21, 2024, Referee received a call from an individual named Miguel Esparza who represented that he was the owner of Florence Transmission. (Anand Decl., ¶ 8.) The Referee states he requested verification that rent was paid for September and October, but Mr. Esparza has failed to provide verification or a written lease. (Anand Decl., ¶ 8.) The Tenant failed to pay rent for October and November 2024. (Anand Decl., ¶ 7.)

 

The Referee argues removing Tenant at this point is necessary for multiple reasons: (1) the non-performing tenant is reducing the value of the Property; (2) Referee can lease the unit at market rate while selling the property, creating value for both owners, and at the same time, increase the value at sale; (3) Referee has a prospective Lessee that will be signing a lease for $3,600 per month; (4) a written lease is needed for multiple reasons, including that it will provide a better ability to show the property and ensure that it is not being maintained in a hazardous condition. (Anand Decl., ¶ 13.)

 

Therefore, the Referee requests an order directing the Clerk of the Court to issue a Writ of Possession for the Property, directing the Los Angeles County Sheriff to remove the Tenant and any other occupants from the Property and deliver possession to the Referee.

 

No opposition was filed. “A party who has not timely filed written opposition to a motion…may not be afforded an opportunity to offer oral argument at the hearing.” (Sexton v. Superior Court (1997) 58 Cal.App.4th 1403, 1410.) “The failure to file opposition creates an inference that the motion or demurrer is meritorious.”¿ (Id.)¿ “In such cases, the court may hear argument limited to a request for a continuance of the hearing in order to afford an opportunity for written opposition.” (Id.) “[T]he rule is patently intended to prevent the introduction of legal theories without prior notice to opposing counsel and the court.” (Id.)

 

The Interlocutory Order grants the Referee specific authority to terminate tenancies and take possession of the Property to ensure the highest bid for sale. (Anand Decl., Ex. 1 “Interlocutory Order” ¶¶ 8-9.)

 

The court in its discretion may appoint a referee for sale. (Code Civ. Proc., § 873.020.) The referee may perform any acts necessary to exercise the authority conferred by order of the court. (Code Civ. Proc., § 873.060.)

 

C.C.P. §872.120 provides, as follows: “In the conduct of the action, the court may hear and determine all motions, reports, and accounts and may make any decrees and orders necessary or incidental to carrying out the purposes of this title and to effectuating its decrees and orders.”

 

The Court finds that the Interlocutory Order and Code of Civil Procedure provide grounds for the relief requested.

 

Accordingly, Referee’s Motion for Order Directing the Issuance of a Writ of Possession is GRANTED.

 

III. CONCLUSION 

 

Referee’s Motion for Order Directing the Issuance of a Writ of Possession is GRANTED.
