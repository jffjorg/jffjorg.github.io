DEPARTMENT 8 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 24TRCV01108    Hearing Date: September 17, 2024    Dept: 8

Tentative Ruling¿

¿¿ 

HEARING DATE:                 September 17, 2024

¿¿ 

CASE NUMBER:                   24TRCV01108

¿¿ 

CASE NAME:                        Rosario Marcela Rivera Santos v. General Motors LLC, et al.

¿¿ 

MOVING PARTY:                 Plaintiff, Rosario Marcela Rivera Santos

 

RESPONDING PARTY:        Defendant, General Motors, LLC

 

TRIAL DATE:                        Not Set. 

¿¿ 

MOTION:¿                              (1) Motion to Compel GM’s Further Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One

                                                (2) Motion to Compel GM’s Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One

                                                (3) Motion to Compel GM’s Further Responses to Requests for Admission, Set One

 

Tentative Rulings:                  (1) –(3) – CONTINUED.  The Court will take action reasonably required to ensure that the meet-and-confer IN GOOD FAITH requirement of the Discovery Act is not ignored by both sides. It is not in good faith to give the responding party a single day deadline to respond to a lengthy meet-and-confer letter.  It is not in good faith for a responding to allow a month to pass after a meet-and-confer letter with no substantive response to the issues raised.   If the parties and their counsel intend to continue to congest the Court with discovery motions and objections to the meet and confer process, the Court can impose a case management order that mandates certain things both sides might find problematic.  But before the Court goes there, it will instead continue the hearing so Plaintiffs do not lose their 45-day clock -- and will order the parties to meet telephonically or in person and confer either in the second half or September or the whole month of October -- with a view towards narrowing the issues and discovery requests the Court must decide before a November return date. 

 

 

I. BACKGROUND¿¿ 

¿¿ 

A. Factual¿¿ 

On April 2, 2024, Plaintiff, Rosario Marcela Rivera Santos (“Plaintiff”) filed a Complaint against Defendant General Motors (“GM”) and DOES 1 through 10. The Complaint alleges causes of action for: (1) Violation of Song-Beverly Act – Breach of Express Warranty; (2) Violation of Song-Beverly Act – Breach of Implied Warranty; and (3) Violation of the Song-Beverly Act § 1793.2.

 

On May 6, 2024, Plaintiff propounded Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Requests for Admission, Set One.

 

On June 6, 2024, Plaintiff notes that Defendant GM responded asserting allegedly boilerplate objections that are not code-compliant. Plaintiff also notes that Defendant GM did not provide verifications in support of its responses until June 12, 2024. To date, Plaintiff contends that GM has not provided further responses to this discovery that could assist in Plaintiff’s allegation that GM willfully violated its obligations under the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act.

 

B. Procedural¿¿ 

            On July 29, 2024, Plaintiff filed its Motions to Compel Further Responses. On August 30, 2024, Defendant GM filed opposition briefs. On September 10, 2024, Plaintiff filed reply briefs.

 

II. ANALYSIS

¿ 

A.    Legal Standard

On receipt of a response to discovery requests, the party requesting may move for an order compelling further responses for interrogatories (Code Civ. Proc. 2030.300), requests for admission (Code. Civ. Proc. section 2033.290), and request for production (Code Civ. Proc. section 2031.310). “Unless notice of this motion is given within 45 days of the service of the verified response, or any supplemental verified response, or any specific later date to which the requesting party and the responding party have agreed in writing, the requesting party waives any right to compel further response to the requests for admission.” (Code Civ. Proc. section 2033.290, subd. (c).) 

B.    Discussion

 

                                     i.          Meet and Confer Efforts

 

            In the declaration of Gregory Sogoyan, Esq.’s (“Sogoyan Decl.”), counsel for Plaintiff, he notes that on July 25, 2024, he sent Defense counsel a letter attempting to meet and confer “in good faith” regarding Defendant’s deficient responses to Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Requests for Admission, Set One. (Sogoyan Decl., ¶ 19, Exhibit 7.) Sogoyan contends that the letter detailed GM’s deficient and boilerplate responses to Plaintiff’s interrogatories and honed in on Defendant’s responses laden with allegedly unsubstantiated boilerplate objections. (Sogoyan Decl., ¶ 19.) Further, Sogoyan notes that he detailed the various deficiencies that existed in GM’s discovery responses to Plaintiff’s justifiably relevant and discovery information sought to forward its claims that Defendant willfully violated the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act and requested responses BY JULY 26, 2024, i.e. one day later, and offered a responsive extension deadline in efforts to further meet and confer. Despite this, the Sogoyan notes that as of the date of filing Plaintiff’s motions, Defendant remains unresponsive despite Plaintiff’s July 25, 2025 meet and confer letter.

 

In GM’s opposition brief, it argues that Plaintiff failed to meet and confer in good faith prior to bringing this motion. GM contends that Plaintiff’s letter, rather than narrow the scope of the discovery requests at issue, took the position that each request was appropriate and necessary, and demanded that GM withdraw its objections and supplement its previous production.  

 

Here, neither party met and conferred in good faith. Plaintiff’s meet and confer letter was sent on July 25, 2024, the letter required responses only one day later, on July 26, 2024, and subsequently filed the Motions to Compel Further four days after the letter on July 29, 2024. The Court wonders how Plaintiff’s counsel believed GM’s counsel would be able to provide further written responses to 109 discovery interrogatories and/or requests. The Court also wonders how this could be perceived as being in good faith.  As of a month later, on August 30, 2024 when GM filed its Opposition to the instant motions, GM has apparently not spoke with Plaintiff’s counsel, had not prepared substantive or amended responses, or taken other steps to moot or resolve the discovery dispute.

 

Because of the present meet and confer issue between the parties, instead of ruling on the motions in today’s hearing, the Court CONTINUES this motion to a date available on the Court’s calendar in November of 2024.  The parties are ordered to meet and confer about the discovery requests in dispute, and ordered each to provide a status report with when and how they met and conferred including a description by discovery set and request number of the remaining interrogatories and requests at issue.
