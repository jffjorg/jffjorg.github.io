DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22VECV00297    Hearing Date: December 15, 2023    Dept: W

FEISTY GAL, INC., et al. v. MICHAEL KAISER, et al.

 

defendants michael kaiser and people’s care holdings, inc.’s motion for judgment on the pleading

 

Date of Hearing:        December 15, 2023                                      Trial Date:       April 8, 2024  

Department:              W                                                                    Case No.:        22VECV00297

 

Moving Party:            Defendants Michael Kaiser and People’s Care Holdings, Inc.

Responding Party:     Plaintiffs Feisty Gal, Inc. and Michelle Lander

Meet and Confer:      Yes. (Tovar Decl.)

 

BACKGROUND

 

Plaintiffs Feisty Gal, Inc. and Michelle Lander allege Defendants Michael Kaiser and People’s Care Holdings, Inc. sold Plaintiffs an unregistered franchise. Plaintiffs allege Defendants had several pre-sale omissions and post-sale omissions false and deceptive representations regarding the franchise status of A Right Place for Seniors International, LLC.

 

On March 2, 2022, Plaintiffs filed a complaint against Defendants asserting causes of action for (1) Violation of the California Franchise Investment Law - Sale of an Unregistered Franchise; (2) Violation of the California Franchise Investment Law - Failure to Disclose Materially Different Terms of Franchise Agreement; (3) Fraudulent Concealment; (4) Fraud; (5) Negligent Misrepresentation; (6) Breach of Contract; and (7) Unjust Enrichment.

 

Default was entered against Defendants on June 17, 2022. The default was vacated on December 15, 2022.

 

On December 22, 2022, Defendants filed a Cross-Complaint against Gemma Dela Cruz, A Right Place for Seniors, Inc., and A Right Place For Seniors International LLC for contractual indemnity, equitable indemnity, implied indemnity, and contribution. Gemma Dela Cruz and A Right Place for Seniors, Inc. were dismissed on March 3, 2023.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendants Michael Kaiser and People’s Care Holdings, Inc.’s Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings is DENIED.

 

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTIce

 

Defendants request this court take judicial notice court documents (See RJN, Exhs. 1-3.) The court GRANTS Defendants’ request. (Evid. Code §472(d).)

 

discussion

 

Defendants Michael Kaiser and People’s Care Holdings, Inc. move pursuant to Code of Civil

Procedure §438 for judgment on the pleadings as to the entire Complaint and all cause of action therein on the grounds Feisty Gal, Inc. may not represent itself. Defendants also move on the grounds Ms. Lander was not a party to the Franchise Agreement and therefore also lacks standing to bring or maintain the lawsuit.

 

A defendant’s motion for judgment on the pleadings may be made after the time to demur has expired and an answer has been filed. (CCP § 438(f).) A motion by a defendant may be made on the grounds that (1) the court “lacks jurisdiction of the subject of one or more of the causes of action alleged” or (2) the complaint or cross-complaint “does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action against that defendant.” (CCP § 438(c).)¿

 

“A motion for judgment on the pleadings has the same function as a general demurrer, and hence attacks only defects disclosed on the face of the pleadings or by matters that can be judicially noticed.” (Cloud v. Northrop Grumman Corp. (1998) 67 Cal.App.4th 995, 999.) “A motion for judgment on the pleadings is akin to a general demurrer; it tests the sufficiency of the complaint to state a cause of action.” (Wise v. Pacific Gas and Elec. Co. (2005) 132 Cal.App.4th 725, 738.)¿¿

 

Defendants first argue Defendants cannot proceed with discovery or its trial preparation so long as FGI remains unrepresented and therefore, the court should grant the motion without leave to amend and enter judgment in favor of Defendants and against Plaintiffs. Defendants contend Plaintiff FGI remains in pro per despite the fact that the court relieved its counsel on August 29, 2023 and defense counsel has repeatedly reminded Plaintiffs that FGI needed to retain new counsel. The court agrees FGI cannot proceed in pro per. “When a corporation seeks to appear without the benefit of counsel in other than small claims cases, it is the duty of the trial court to advise the representative of the corporation of the necessity to be represented by a licensed lawyer. If no such licensed representative is present, appearing for the corporation, the court may: (1) hear a motion for continuance; or (2) enter the corporation's default for nonappearance at trial. We conclude neither the parties nor the court can permit the corporation to appear and conduct a contested (other than small claims) trial in propria persona.” (Van Gundy v. Camelot Resorts, Inc. (1983) 152 Cal.App.3d Supp. 29, 31–32.) Defendants further argue because this entire lawsuit is based on the allegation that Plaintiff FGI entered into a written franchise agreement and Ms. Lander is not a party to the contract, the court must also grant the motion as to Ms. Lander.

 

Plaintiffs filed a belated opposition arguing the motion is now moot because Feisty Gal and Lander now have counsel to represent them. (Gordee Decl. ¶2.) Although ‘belated’, the court exercises its discretion to consider the late filed and served opposition.  (CRC 3.1300(d).)  Accordingly, the court agrees the motion is now moot. FGI is represented by counsel. Moreover, although Defendants further moved on separate grounds against Ms. Lander, the grounds were based on FGI not moving forward with the action.

 

Accordingly, Defendants’ Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings is DENIED. 


Case Number: 23VECV01229    Hearing Date: December 15, 2023    Dept: W

CYRUS MAZAHERIAN, et al. v. CAMERON MAZAHERIAN, et al.

 

MOTION TO STRIKE FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT

 

Date of Hearing:        December 15, 2023                          Trial Date:       None set.

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        23VECV01229

 

Moving Party:            Defendant Monika Mazaherian

Responding Party:     Plaintiffs Cyrus Mazaherian and Linda Terashita

Meet and Confer:      Yes. (Bloom Decl. ¶¶8-12.)

 

BACKGROUND

 

On March 20, 2023, Plaintiffs Cyrus Mazaherian and Linda Terashita filed a complaint against Defendants Cameron Mazaherian and Monika Mazaherian for breach of contract, unjust enrichment, money lent, money paid, constructive trust, judicial foreclosure, and declaratory relief. Plaintiffs allege they entered promissory note agreements with Defendants whereby Plaintiffs agreed to provide loans to Defendants at various times. Plaintiffs further alleged they have not received any of the monthly payments and moreover, Defendants are amidst a separation, which Plaintiffs believe  will cause a Default / anticipatory repudiation under all three promissory notes if reasonable and timely assurances are not made about payments of the promissory notes and the status of the secured interests.

 

On November 6, 2023, Plaintiffs filed a First Amended Complaint for breach of contract, unjust enrichment, money lent, money paid, and declaratory relief.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant’s Motion to Strike First Amended Complaint is DENIED.

 

discussion

 

Defendant Monika Mazaherian moves the court for an Order striking the First Amended Complaint of Plaintiffs Cyrus Mazaherian and Linda Terashita as a late filed pleading. Defendant Monika contends the deadline for Plaintiffs to file an amended complaint was October 24, 2023; however, Plaintiffs did not file a First Amended Complaint until November 6, 2023 – i.e., approximately two weeks after the Deadline for them to do so had passed.

 

“Under section 436: ‘The court may, upon a motion made pursuant to Section 435 [allowing motions to strike], or at any time in its discretion, and upon terms it deems proper: [¶] ... [¶] (b) Strike out all ... of any pleading not drawn or filed in conformity with ... an order of the court.’ (Italics added.) Indeed, ‘by virtue of its inherent power to prevent abuse of its processes’ (italics added), a trial court may strike an amended complaint ‘filed in disregard of established procedural processes,’ and may strike an amended pleading ‘because no request for permission to amend was sought.’ [Citation.]” (Leader v. Health Industries of America, Inc. (2001) 89 Cal.App.4th 603, 613.)

 

The court may dismiss a complaint as to a defendant when, after a demurrer to the complaint is sustained with leave to amend, the plaintiff fails to amend it within the time allowed by the Court and either party moves for dismissal. (CCP § 581(f)(2).)  If an amended pleading is filed after the time allowed, an order striking the amended pleading must be obtained by noticed motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 1010. (CRC Rule, 3.1320(i).)

 

The court sustained Monika’s demurrer as to all causes of action in the Complaint on September 18, 2023 and Monika’s counsel gave notice to Plaintiffs of the Court’s ruling by U.S. mail and e-mail on September 19, 2023. (Bloom Decl., Exh. 1.) Defendant Monika argues because time to amend after a demurrer runs from the service of notice of the decision or order, the deadline for Plaintiffs to file an amended complaint was October 24, 2023. (See CCP §472(b).) However, Plaintiffs did not file the amended complaint until approximately two weeks later on November 6, 2023. Defendant contends rather than filing the belated amended complaint, Plaintiffs should have sought permission to file the belated pleading. (See e.g., Leader v. Health Indus. of America, Inc. (2001) 89 Cal.App.4th 603, 612-13.)

 

In opposition, Plaintiffs argue the FAC was served six days early of the deadline. However, due to the inadvertent mistake of the Plaintiffs’ counsel’s office, the FAC was not filed at that time and was not filed until twelve days after the deadline set by the court. Plaintiffs’ counsel claims he prepared the FAC and then asked secretary Desiree Robison to file and serve the FAC on all parties. (Abramson Decl. ¶¶ 3-4, Ex. 1.) Mr. Abramson saw Ms. Robison’s timely email serve the FAC and mistakenly assumed that Ms. Robison has also filed the FAC with the Court, as was Ms. Robison’s and the firm’s custom and practice. (Abramson Decl. ¶¶ 3-5, Ex. 1; Robison Decl. ¶3-8.) Unfortunately, at this time, Ms. Robison was going through personal issues which resulted in Ms. Robison failing to timely file the FAC with the court. (Robison Decl. ¶¶4-5.) As a result, Plaintiffs contend pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 473 and 581, the court has discretion to relieve Plaintiffs of their counsel’s mistake and accept the FAC.

 

Monika, in reply, takes issue with Plaintiffs’ contention that there was actual service on Monika and moreover, Plaintiffs’ “concocted” excuse is not credible.

 

The court denies Defendant’s motion to strike. "A trial court's determinations to deny leave to file a belated amended pleading under section 473, subdivision (a)(1) [Citation], to strike a pleading under section 436 [Citation], and to dismiss an action under section 581, subdivision (f)(2) [Citation] are all reviewed for abuse of discretion.” (Leader, supra, 89 Cal.App.4th at p. 612.) The court uses its discretion in denying the motion to strike. Based on Plaintiff’s counsel’s testimony along with Ms. Robinson’s (both signed under penalty of perjury), the court finds mistake and/or excusable neglect pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 473. Defendants have not sufficiently shown they were prejudiced by the two-week delay. Moreover, as noted above, the decision to grant or deny a party's motion to dismiss is within the judge's discretion. (Gitmed, supra, 26 Cal.App.4th at p. 827.) Several courts have held a demurrer is a dismissal motion for purposes of mandatory relief under CCP § 473(b).) Accordingly, even if the court were to grant the motion to strike, Plaintiffs could then file for mandatory relief under Code of Civil Procedure section 473. “[I]f the prerequisites for the application of the mandatory provision of section 473, subdivision (b) exist, the trial court does not have discretion to refuse relief.” (Leader v. Health Industries of America, Inc. (2001) 89 Cal.App.4th 603, 612.) Because relief is mandatory, the court would set aside the stricken complaint.

 

Any arguments regarding the merits of the action can be addressed on demurrer, motion for judgment on the pleadings, summary judgment and the like.

 

Accordingly, Defendant’s Motion to Strike is DENIED.
