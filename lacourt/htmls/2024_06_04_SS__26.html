Case Number: 22STLC08613    Hearing Date: June 4, 2024    Dept: 26

Nissanoff v. Oscar Health Plan of Cal., et al.

MOTIONS TO COMPEL DEFENDANT’S FURTHER RESPONSES TO SPECIAL INTERROGATORIES

(CCP § 2030.300)



TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Plaintiff Jonathan Nissanoff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, is GRANTED. DEFENDANT OSCAR HEALTH PLAN OF CALIFORNIA IS TO SERVE FURTHER RESPONSES TO SPECIAL INTERROGATORIES, SET ONE, NOS. 1-20 TO PLAINTIFF WITHIN 20 DAYS’ NOTICE OF THIS ORDER.

 

 

ANALYSIS:

 

Plaintiff Jonathan Nissanoff (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action for common counts and breach of contract against Defendant Oscar Health Plan of California (“Defendant”) on December 27, 2022. Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint on February 14, 2023.

 

Plaintiff filed a “Motion to Compel Further Discovery Responses” on February 20, 2024. At the hearing on March 21, 2024, the Court noted that the motion improperly combined multiple motions into one and ordered that they be filed separately. Plaintiff filed the instant (1) Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One; and (2) Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One, on March 26, 2024. The Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One, was granted in part on May 23, 2024. (Minute Order, 05/23/24.) No opposition to the instant Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, has been filed to date.

 

Discussion

 

Notice of the motion to compel further must be given “within 45 days of service of the verified response, or any supplemental verified response, or any specific later date to which the requesting party and the responding party have agreed in writing,” otherwise, the propounding party waives any right to compel a further response. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (c).) Defendant’s discovery responses were served electronically on June 13, 2023. (Motion, Nissanoff Decl., ¶5.) Notice of the Motions was initially timely filed and served on July 23, 2023. (Id. at Exh. 4.)

 

Also, the Motion must be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (b)(1); 2031.310, subd. (b)(2).) The supporting declarations demonstrate an effort by Plaintiff to obtain further responses from Defendant after service of the original and supplemental responses. (Id. at Exh. 3.)

 

Finally, Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1345 requires all motions or responses involving further discovery contain a separate statement with the text of each request, the response, and a statement of factual and legal reasons for compelling further responses. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1345, subd. (a).) Alternatively, “the court may allow the moving party to submit a concise outline of the discovery request and each response in dispute.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.310, subd. (b)(3).) The Motion is accompanied by a separate statement. (Separate Statement, filed 03/26/24.)

 

Special Interrogatories, Set One

 

Plaintiff moves to compel Defendant’s further responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, Nos. 2-20. Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300 states in relevant part:

 

On receipt of a response to interrogatories, the propounding party may move for an order compelling a further response if the propounding party deems that any of the following apply:

 

(1) An answer to a particular interrogatory is evasive or incomplete.

. . . .

 

(3) An objection to an interrogatory is without merit or too general.

 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (a).)

 

Interrogatory No. 2 asks Defendant to describe how it takes into account the factors under Title 28 of the California Code of Regulations, Section 1300.71, subdivision (a)(3)(B) (“the Gould factors”) in determining the reasonable and customary value of out-of-network physician’s emergency medical services.

 

Defendant objected to the request as vague as to time and scope, ambiguous as to the terms “emergency medical services” and “noncontracted physician”, and seeking proprietary, confidential, and trade secret information. It then responds that it calculates the allowable amounts in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

 

If a timely motion to compel responses has been filed, the burden is on the responding party to justify its objections. (Fairmont Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (Stendell) (2000) 22 Cal.4th 245, 255 [citing Coy v. Superior Court (1962) 58 Cal.2d 210, 220-221].) Defendant, however, has not filed an opposition to the instant Motion justifying the objections. The Court also agrees that Defendant’s purported response that it calculates the allowable amounts in accordance with applicable laws and regulations is non-responsive. As Plaintiff points out, this does not provide any factual information regarding how Defendant takes into account the Gould factors when determining the usual, customary, and reasonable (“UCR”) rate. Furthermore, Defendant’s objection that certain terms are ambiguous is belied by its own use of those terms in its response. Finally, Defendant’s objection that the information sought is propriety, privileged or confidential was addressed in the Court’s ruling on Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Production, Set One. As the Court explained, Defendant offers no evidence to show that the information sought falls within the definition of a trade secret and the parties have a stipulated protective order in place.

 

Therefore, Plaintiff’s request to compel Defendant’s further responses to Interrogatory No. 2 is granted.

Interrogatory No. 11 asks Defendant to identify all professional claims it paid for emergency medical services within 4-and-a-half years of the date this action was filed. An additional objection by Defendant to this interrogatory is that it requires the preparation of a compilation, abstract, audit, or summary, in violation of Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.230. This statute does not prohibit an interrogatory that requires such a preparation, however. Rather, it states: “If an answer to an interrogatory would necessitate the preparation or the making of a compilation, abstract, audit, or summary of or from the documents of the party to whom the interrogatory is directed, and if the burden or expense of preparing or making it would be substantially the same for the party propounding the interrogatory as for the responding party, it is a sufficient answer to that interrogatory to refer to this section and to specify the writings from which the answer may be derived or ascertained.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.230.) Defendant has not shown that the burden of preparing such a compilation, etc. would be the same for it as for Plaintiff, nor has Defendant specified the writings from which the answer may be ascertained. This objection, therefore, is also without merit. Plaintiff’s request to compel Defendant’s further responses to Interrogatory No. 11 is granted.
