Case Number: 23TRCV03685    Hearing Date: May 3, 2024    Dept: B

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Southwest District

Torrance Dept. B

 

MIGUEL ROJAS MENDOZA,

 

 

 

Plaintiff,

 

Case No.:

 

 

23TRCV03685

 

vs.

 

 

[Tentative RULING

 

 

CITADEL SERVICING CORPORATION DBA ACRA LENDING, et al.,

 

 

 

Defendants.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Hearing Date:                          May 3, 2024

 

Moving Parties:                      (1), (2) Defendant Citadel Servicing Corp., (3) defendant MTC Financial Inc.

Responding Party:                  Plaintiff Miguel Rojas Mendoza

(1)   Demurrer

(2)   Motion to Strike

(3)   Demurrer and Joinder

 

            The court considered the moving, opposition and reply papers.

RULING

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND as to the first through seventh cause of action in the complaint.

The motion to strike is MOOT.

BACKGROUND

            On November 8, 2023, Miguel Rojas Mendoza (self-represented) filed a complaint against Citadel Servicing Corporation dba Acra Lending, MTC Financial Inc. dba Trustee Corps, and Yehudah Younessian for (1) wrongful foreclosure, (2)-(4) violations of Homeowners Bill of Rights, (5) cancellation and expungement of trustee’s deed upon sale, (6) violation of unfair competition law, Bus. & Prof. Code §17200, et seq., and (7) declaratory relief.

LEGAL AUTHORITY

Demurrer

When considering demurrers, courts read the allegations liberally and in context.  Taylor v. City of Los Angeles Dept. of Water and Power (2006) 144 Cal. App. 4th 1216, 1228.  “A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters.  Therefore, it lies only where the defects appear on the face of the pleading or are judicially noticed.”  SKF Farms v. Superior Court (1984) 153 Cal. App. 3d 902, 905.  “The only issue involved in a demurrer hearing is whether the complaint, as it stands, unconnected with extraneous matters, states a cause of action.”  Hahn v. Mirda (2007) 147 Cal. App. 4th 740, 747.

Strike

“The court may, upon a motion . . ., or at any time in its discretion, and upon terms it deems proper:  (a) Strike any irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading.  (b) Strike out all or any part of any pleading not drawn or filed in conformity with the laws of this state, a court rule, or an order of the court.”  CCP §436(b).

            CCP §431.10 states:  “(a) A material allegation in a pleading is one essential to the claim or defense and which could not be stricken from the pleading without leaving it insufficient as to that claim or defense. 

(b) An immaterial allegation in a pleading is any of the following:  (1) An allegation that is not essential to the statement of a claim or defense.  (2) An allegation that is neither pertinent to nor supported by an otherwise sufficient claim or defense.  (3) A demand for judgment requesting relief not supported by the allegations of the complaint or cross-complaint. 

(c) An ‘immaterial allegation’ means ‘irrelevant matter’ as that term is used in Section 436.”

The grounds for moving to strike must appear on the face of the pleading or by way of judicial notice.  CCP §437.

DISCUSSION

Demurrer

Defendants Citadel Servicing Corp. and MTC Financial Inc. dba Trustee Corps demur to the complaint and each of the causes of action on the grounds that they fail to state sufficient facts to constitute a cause of action and are uncertain.

The complaint alleges that the real property at 1 Sweetgrass Lane, Rolling Hills Estates, California owned by plaintiff by way of a deed of trust dated April 24, 2021.  Complaint, ¶11.  Citadel violated the Homeowners Bill of Rights Act in connection with processing loan modification applications and foreclosures.  Id., ¶14.  Around May 2022, plaintiff filed a complete loan modification package with Citadel.  Id., ¶18.  Citadel denied his loan modification and failed to give plaintiff the opportunity to appeal the denial.  Id., ¶19.  Citadel submitted a defective declaration to the trustee to begin non-judicial foreclosure process on July 19, 2022 while plaintiff’s loan modification was still under review.  Id., ¶20.  Plaintiff contacted an individual named Richard who works with loan modification to assist plaintiff, but Citadel was working behind the scenes in violation of the HBOR dual tracking.  Id., ¶21.  Plaintiff participated “in this charade of Citadel employees without being given a single point of contract and on October 31, 2022, in violation of HBOR, Citadel, through its third-party agent, Trustee Corps, filed a notice of trustee’s sale of December 6, 2022.  Id., ¶23.  Defendant Citadel sent a letter to plaintiff in April “that said a plan to pay $8527.78 a month.”  Id., ¶26.

In addition to the arguments made by defendants below, defendant Trustee Corps argues that as a non-judicial foreclosure trustee, Trustee Corps has a statutory privilege to record nonjudicial foreclosure documents under Civil Code §§2924(b), (d), and 47.  Further, Trustee Corps had no duty to plaintiff, which falls outside the foreclosure process, in originating or servicing plaintiff’s loan.  As alleged, the only actions taken by Trustee Corps were the recording of a notice of default on July 25, 2022 and a notice of sale on October 31, 2022, and conducting the foreclosure sale.

First cause of action for wrongful foreclosure

The elements of a cause of action for wrongful foreclosure are (1) the trustee or mortgagee caused an illegal, fraudulent, or willfully oppressive sale of real property pursuant to a power of sale in a mortgage or deed of trust; (2) the party attacking the sale (usually but not always the trustor or mortgagor) was prejudiced or harmed; and (3) in cases where the trustor or mortgagor challenges the sale, the trustor or mortgagor tendered the amount of the secured indebtedness or was excused from tendering.  Lona v. Citibank, N.A. (2011) 202 Cal. App. 4th 89, 104.  A complainant is required to do equity “by restoring to the defendant any value the plaintiff received from the transaction,” if they are to maintain a claim.  Fleming v. Kagan (1961) 189 Cal. App. 2d 791, 795.  “A valid and viable tender of payment of the indebtedness owing is essential to an action to cancel a voidable sale under a deed of trust.”  Karlsen v. Am. Sav. & Loan Assn. (1971) 15 Cal. App. 3d 112, 116.

Defendants argue that there are no allegations that the alleged foreclosure was not justified from the status of the loan and HBOR does not apply to the loan, property, or defendants (HBOR is discussed below).  Defendants also contend that plaintiff’s claim fails due to lack of prejudice because any procedural irregularity is not the cause of plaintiff’s injury.  Further, even if HBOR applies, “a mere violation of HBOR” does not render a sale void or voidable; and that a borrower’s remedies under the HBOR are limited to injunctive relief or damages depending on whether a trustee’s deed has been recorded.  “HBOR violations are not grounds for voiding and rescinding the trustee’s sale.”  Gilmore v. Wells Fargo Bank N.A. (N.D. Cal. 2014) 75 F. Supp. 3d 1255, 1265.  Defendants further point out that plaintiff has pled no procedural irregularities with the sale or that plaintiff has made a tender.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient for the reasons stated by defendants. Plaintiff has not shown how he can amend.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

Second cause of action for violations of Homeowners Bill of Rights, Civil Code §§2923.6 and 2924.18 (“dual-tracking”)

Civil Code §2923.6(c) states “[i]f a borrower submits a complete application for a first lien loan modification . . . a mortgage servicer . . . shall not record a notice of default or notice of sale, or conduct a trustee’s sale, while the complete first lien loan modification application is pending.”

Civil Code §2924.18(1) states:  “[i]f a borrower submits a complete application for a first lien loan modification offered by, or through, the borrower’s mortgage servicer at least five business days before a scheduled foreclosure sale, a mortgage servicer, trustee, mortgagee, beneficiary, or authorized agent shall not record a notice of default, notice of sale, or conduct a trustee’s sale while the complete first lien loan modification application is pending, and until the borrower has been provided with a written determination by the mortgage servicer regarding that borrower’s eligibility for the requested loan modification.”

Defendants argue that the allegations are insufficient because the allegation that “around May 2022” plaintiff submitted a “complete” loan modification to Citadel lacks specificity and is conclusory.  Further, defendants argue, any alleged “violation” of this code section is not material and thus not actionable.  To the contrary, defendants assert, plaintiff alleges that Citadel denied him a modification and later offered him a repayment plan, and that a notice of default was recorded when he was “trying to work with Citadel” and not when a complete application was under review.  Defendants further argue that plaintiff’s claim is barred by the safe harbor rule in that plaintiff alleges he was provided a repayment plan, which demonstrates that any HBOR violations were cured.

Defendants also argues that HBOR does not apply to the loan or property because plaintiff does not allege that the property is his principal residence or used for personal, family, or household purposes, as HBOR only applies to “owner-occupied” residential real property.  Civil Code §2924.15.  Defendants assert that the loan does not qualify for HBOR protections because HBOR only applies to senior liens secured by 1-4 unit residential properties, and plaintiff does not allege such.  Lastly, defendants argue that the HBOR claims do not apply to Citadel under Civil Code §2923.6(i), which provides that dual tracking prohibitions do not apply to lenders, who during its immediately preceding annual reporting year, have foreclosed on 175 or fewer residential properties.  Likewise, the HBOR sections cited below also do not apply.  See Request for Judicial Notice, Exh. 1.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient for the reasons stated by defendants.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

 

Third cause of action for violation of Homeowners Bill of Rights, Civil Code §2923.7

Civil Code §2923.7(a) states that “[w]hen a borrower requests a foreclosure prevention alternative, the mortgage servicer shall promptly establish a single point of contact and provide to the borrower one or more direct means of communication with the single point of contact.”  “[A] violation of 2923.7 is actionable only when that violation is material.”  Shupe v. Nationstar Mortg. LLC (E.D. Cal. 2017) 231 F. Supp. 3d 597, 603.

Defendants assert that plaintiff alleges that Citadel provided a SPOC, but that he was transferred numerous times and that representatives “gave Plaintiff the run around” but plaintiff also alleges that Citadel reviewed and denied the application and later offered him a repayment plan.  Defendants argue that plaintiff’s allegations “amount to, at best, a complaint about customer service quality which do not relate to any difficulties connected with loss mitigation efforts.”  Defendants further assert that a Notice of Default and Notice of Sale were not recorded when a complete application was pending.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient for the reasons stated by defendants.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

Fourth cause of action for violation of Civil Code §§2923.55 and/or 2923.5

Civil Code §2923.55(a) provides that “[a] mortgager servicer, mortgagee, trustee, beneficiary, or authorized agent shall not record a notice of default pursuant to Section 2924 until” certain conditions have been satisfied, including that “[a] mortgagor servicer shall contact the borrower in person or by telephone in order to assess the borrower’s financial situation and explore options for the borrower to avoid foreclosure . . . .” Civil Code §2923.55(b)(2).  Civil Code §2923.5(a)(2) states the same.

“The only remedy afforded by section 2923.5 is, however, a one-time postponement of the foreclosure sale before it happens.”  Lueras v. BAC Home Loans Servicing (2013) 221 Cal. App. 4th 49, 77.

Defendants point out that plaintiff does not allege that a declaration as required by Civil Code §2923.5(e) is not attached to the notice of default.  Defendants also argue that as a matter of law, plaintiff cannot state a claim under this section because plaintiff alleges that a foreclosure sale has already occurred.  Defendants also argue that the allegations are conclusory and that plaintiff does not identify how the documents are “fraudulent,” why Citadel would owe a fiduciary duty to a borrower, and how any of the documents are “void.”  Defendants further argue that any alleged violation was material as plaintiff alleges that discussions did occur, a review was completed, and he was offered a repayment plan.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient for the reasons stated by defendants.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

Fifth cause of action for cancellation and expungement of trustee’s deed upon sale

“To prevail on a claim to cancel an instrument, a plaintiff must prove (1) the instrument is void or voidable due to, for example, fraud; and (2) there is a reasonable apprehension of serious injury including pecuniary loss or the prejudicial alteration of one’s position.”  U.S. Bank National Association v. Naifeh (2016) 1 Cal. App. 5th 767, 778.

The complaint seeks cancellation of the deed of trust, substitution of trustee, notice of default, notice of sale, and trustee’s deed upon sale.  Complaint, ¶74.

Defendants argue that the allegations fail to articulate how or why any documents are void or voidable and thus subject to cancellation.  Defendants assert that while not clear, it appears that plaintiff’s claim is based entirely on the alleged HBOR violations, which, defendants argue, fail on several grounds as stated above.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient for the reasons stated by defendants.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

Sixth cause of action for violation of Bus. & Prof. Code §17200, et seq.

The purpose of the Unfair Business Practices Act (Bus. & Prof. Code §17000, et. seq.) is to “safeguard the public against the creation or perpetuation of monopolies and to foster and encourage competition, by prohibiting unfair, dishonest, deceptive, destructive, fraudulent and discriminatory practices by which fair and honest competition is destroyed or prevented.”  Bus. & Prof. Code §17001.  The Unfair Business Practices Act shall include “any unlawful, unfair or fraudulent business act or practice.”  Bus. & Prof. Code §17200.  The Unfair Business Practices Act is a tool with which to enjoin deceptive or sharp practices.  Samura v. Kaiser Foundation Health Plan, Inc. (1993) 17 Cal. App. 4th 1284, 1299, fn. 6.  A plaintiff alleging unfair business practices under these statutes must state with reasonable particularity the facts supporting the statutory elements of the violation.  Khoury v. Maly's of California, Inc. (1993) 14 Cal. App. 4th 612, 619.  

Defendants argue that a borrower who defaults on a loan before the defendant’s alleged wrongful acts does not have standing to sue under Section 17200 because the default caused the foreclosure proceedings, credit damage, late fees, etc., not the defendant’s conduct.  See Jenkins v. JP Morgan Chase Bank, N.A. (2013) 216 Cal. App. 4th 497, 522-523, disapproved on other grounds by Yvanova v. New Century Mortg. Corp. (2016) 62 Cal.4th 919, 929.  Thus, plaintiff lacks standing.  Defendants further argue that plaintiff fails to allege any wrongful conduct under the UCL.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient and conclusory.  Plaintiff does not allege sufficiently conduct that violated the UCL.  The allegations do not show unlawful, unfair, or fraudulent conduct, and he has not alleged with particularity.  Further, he lacks standing.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

            Seventh cause of action for declaratory relief

Defendants argue that plaintiff’s substantive claims fail and that there is no dispute as to the ownership of the property, that it was encumbered by the deed of trust under which plaintiff defaulted, and that Citadel had a right to foreclose.

The Court finds that the allegations are insufficient for the reasons stated by defendants.

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

 

Motion to Strike 

Defendant Citadel requests that the Court strike attorney’s fees in paras. 53, 62, and 91; any request for disgorgement, or restitution damages; any request for punitive damages; and the prayer. 

In light of the ruling on the demurrer, the motion is MOOT.

 

ORDER

The demurrer is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND as to the first through seventh cause of action in the complaint.

The motion to strike is MOOT.

Defendant Citadel is ordered to give notice of ruling.
