DEPARTMENT 56 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

DEPARTMENT 56 JUDGE HOLLY J. FUJIE, LAW AND MOTION RULINGS. The court makes every effort to post tentative rulings by 5.00 pm of the court day before the hearing. The tentative ruling will not become the final ruling until the hearing [see CRC 3.1308(a)(2)], and are also available in the courtroom on the day of the hearing [see CRC 3.1308(b)]. If the parties wish to submit on the tentative ruling and avoid a court appearance, all counsel must agree and choose which counsel will give notice. That counsel must 1) call Dept 56 by 8:30 a.m. on the day of the hearing (213/633-0656) and state that all parties will submit on the tentative ruling, and 2) serve notice of the ruling on all parties. If any party declines to submit on the tentative ruling, then no telephone call is necessary and all parties should appear at the hearing in person or by Court Call. Court reporters are not provided, and parties who want a record of motions and other proceedings must hire a privately retained certified court reporter.

Case Number: 21STCV24488    Hearing Date: May 14, 2024    Dept: 56

 

 

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

FOR THE COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES - CENTRAL DISTRICT

 

KYLE MADISON,

                        Plaintiff,

            vs.

 

MICHAEL THEODORE, ET AL.,

                                                                             

                        Defendants.                              

 

      CASE NO.:  21STCV24488

 

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

MOTION TO CONTINUE TO TRIAL

 

Date:  May 14, 2024

Time: 8:30 a.m.

Dept. 56

 

 

 

MOVING PARTY:                Plaintiff Kyle Madison

RESPONDING PARTY:        None as of May 9, 2024

 

            The Court has considered the moving papers.  No opposition has been filed as of May 9, 2024.

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises out of a dispute over the enforcement of a money judgment. The currently operative fourth amended complaint (the “4AC”) alleges: (1) statutory voidable transfers; and (2) common law voidable transfers.

Plaintiff filed the Motion to Continue Trial (the “Motion”) currently before the Court on April 17, 2024.  The Court advanced the hearing date on the Motion from July 1, 2024 to May 14, 2024 on April 22, 2024.  Plaintiff served Defendant with a Notice of Ruling on April 22, 2024.

 

DISCUSSION

            Legal Standard

            Code Civ. Proc. § 128(a)(8) provides that the court has the power to “amend and control its process and orders so as to make them conform to law and justice.” “[T]he power to determine when a continuance should be granted is within the discretion of the trial court.” (Color-Vue, Inc. v. Abrams (1996) 44 Cal.App.4th 1599, 1603.) A trial court has wide latitude in the matter of calendar control including the granting or denying of continuances. (Park Motors, Inc. v. Cozens (1975) 49 Cal.App.3d 12, 18.) 

 

            A party seeking a continuance of the date set for trial, whether contested or uncontested or stipulated by the parties, must make the request for a continuance by a noticed motion or ex parte application, with supporting declarations. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1332(b).) The party must make the motion or application as soon as reasonably practical once the necessity for the continuance is discovered. (Ibid.)

 

            Each request for a continuance must be considered on its own merits according to California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1332(c). The court may grant a continuance only on an affirmative showing of good cause requiring the continuance.  (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1332(c).) Good cause may be present where a party has not been unable “to obtain essential testimony, documents, or other material evidence despite diligent efforts” or there has been a “significant, unanticipated change in the status of the case as a result of which the case is not ready for trial.” (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1332(c)(6)-(7).)

 

            California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1332 sets forth a list of non-exhaustive factors to be analyzed when determining whether good cause for a trial continuance is present. A court considers factors such as: (1) the proximity of the trial date; (2) whether there was any previous continuance, extension of time, or delay of trial due to any party; (3) the length of the continuance requested; (4) the availability of alternative means to address the problem that gave rise to the motion or application for a continuance; (5) the prejudice that parties or witnesses will suffer as a result of the continuance; (6) if the case is entitled to a preferential trial setting, the reasons for that status and whether the need for a continuance outweighs the need to avoid delay; (7) the court’s calendar and the impact of granting a continuance on other pending trials; (8) whether trial counsel is engaged in another trial; (9) whether all parties have stipulated to a continuance; (10) whether the interests of justice are best served by a continuance, by the trial of the matter, or by imposing conditions on the continuance; and (11) any other fact or circumstance relevant to the fair determination of the motion or application. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1332(d).)

 

            Analysis

            Plaintiff asks that the Court continue the trial date and all discovery, motion cut-off and related dates for six months, from July 8, 2024 to January 8, 2025.  The Motion asserts that Plaintiff requires a trial continuance because: 1) he has been unable to obtain essential evidence despite diligent efforts; 2) discovery will not be completed sufficiently before the existing trial date to allow him to prepare for trial; 3) the case has taken longer than usual to prepare for trial; and 4) the interests of justice will be served by a continuance.  Plaintiff argues that his inability to complete discovery and prepare for trial is due to Defendants’ bad faith refusal to cooperate in discovery.  (Motion, Mink Dec., ¶¶11-16.)  Plaintiff’s counsel also has a time conflict with the current trial date, as he is also trial counsel on a case in Department 78 of this court that is set to begin on July 1, 2024.  (Id. at ¶17.)

           

            The current trial date is less than two months away and Plaintiff is seeking a short continuance of six months. This case was filed on July 2, 2021, so there is no issue with the five year rule for bringing cases to trial.  Trial has been continued four times since it was first set.  The Court cautions the parties that it expects cooperation in discovery and that the case will be ready for trial on the date it is setting through this order.  Counsel should note that this case will take precedence for trial before any other matter filed after this case, unless an order for preference has been granted.

 

            Although the parties have not stipulated to a continuance, no timely opposition has been filed. 

 

            In light of these facts, Plaintiff’s Motion to Continue Trial is GRANTED.

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

MOTION FOR ORDER GRANTING RELIEF FROM FAILING TO PROPERLY IDENTIFY AMENDED COMPLAINT, UNDER CCP 473 MISTAKES, INADVERTENCE, SURPRISE, AND EXCUSABLE NEGLECT

BACKGROUND

             Plaintiff was a tenant of Defendants for twenty years. Plaintiff alleges Defendants falsely misrepresented to him that other tenants were paying $500 a month in rent, and they would give him a $100 discount if he performed maintenance on the property.  Plaintiff alleges the other tenants were in fact paying $400 per month, and Defendants lied to him to so that he would perform maintenance on the property for free.

 

            On February 28, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint alleging (1) breach of oral contract; (2) fraud and deceit; (3) senior abuse and harassment; (4) illegal unit as residence; (5) IIED; and (6) breach of covenant of good faith and fair dealing.

            The Court notes that Plaintiff does not need relief from the clerk’s rejection of the “Second Amended Complaint.” Instead, the relief sought should be to file his “Amended Complaint” after the expiration of the 30 days leave granted by the Court after the sustaining of the demurrer.  Plaintiff may file a motion seeking this relief.  The motion that is currently on file is DENIED.

FOR THE COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES - CENTRAL DISTRICT

 

ROKIT WORLD, INC., et al.,

                        Plaintiffs,

            vs.

 

BANK OF AMERICA, N.A., et al.,

 

                        Defendants.

      CASE NO.: 23STCV25101

 

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: DEMURRER WITH MOTION TO STRIKE

 

Date: May 14, 2024

Time: 8:30 a.m.

Dept. 56

 

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendant Bank of America, N.A. (“Moving Defendant”)

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiffs Rokit World, Inc. and Jonathan Kendrick (collectively, “Plaintiffs”)

 

The Court has considered the moving papers, opposition, and reply. 

 

BACKGROUND

On October 13, 2023, Plaintiffs filed the original complaint (the “Complaint”) in the within case, alleging: (1) breach of implied contract of confidentiality; and (2) violation of California Financial Information Privacy Act.  On January 5, 2024, the Moving Defendant filed a demurrer to the Complaint. On January 23, 2024, Moving Defendant filed a notice of non-opposition to the demurrer to the Complaint.

On January 29, 2024, Plaintiffs filed a First Amended Complaint (“FAC”), without having received leave of Court to file an amended complaint and after the time to serve opposition to the demurrer had passed.

 

On January 31, 2024, the Court granted Moving Defendant’s Demurrer because it was unopposed. (Minute Order 1/31/24.) The Court then granted 20 days leave for Plaintiffs to amend their Complaint.   The time to file a valid FAC pursuant to the Court’s order was thus from January 31 through February 20, 2024.  Plaintiffs did not file a valid FAC pursuant to the Court’s January 31, 2024 order. 

 

On March 29, 2024, Moving Defendant filed the instant demurrer (the “Demurrer”) as to all four causes of action in the FAC and a motion to strike (the “Motion to Strike”) the FAC. On May 1, 2024, Plaintiffs filed their opposition to the Demurrer and Motion to Strike. On May 7, 2024, Moving Defendant filed its reply.

 

MEET AND CONFER

            Moving Defendant’s counsel states that he attempted to meet and confer over the course of several weeks and asked Plaintiffs’ counsel to schedule a phone call to no avail. (Austin Decl. ¶ 2.) Therefore, the meet and confer requirement has been met. 

 

JUDICIAL NOTICE

            Moving Defendant requests judicial notice of the following document:      

Exhibit 1: Superior Court for the County of Los Angles, Dept. 56, Judge Holly J. Fujie, tentative ruling dated July 31, 2024, Rokit World, Inc, et al. vs. Bank of America, N.A. (Case No. 23STCV25101).

 

            The Court notes that the tentative was made the ruling of the Court and grants judicial notice pursuant to Evidence Code section 453.

 

LEGAL STANDARD 

            Motions to strike are used to reach defects or objections to pleadings that are not challengeable by demurrer, such as words, phrases, and prayers for damages. (See Code Civ Proc., §§ 435-437.) A motion to strike can be made to strike irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading or to strike any pleading or part thereof not drawn or filed in conformity with the laws of this state, a court rule or order of the court. (§ 436.)

 

DISCUSSION

            Moving Defendant moves to strike Plaintiffs’ First Amended Complaint or in the alternative, Plaintiffs’ third and fourth causes of actions. (Mot. to Strike at p.1.) Plaintiffs argue that the Motion to Strike is MOOT because the Court granted leave to amend, and any defect has been cured. (Opp’n at pp. 2-3.)

 

            If Plaintiffs had filed the FAC before the date the opposition was due to the demurrer to the original Complaint, it would have been timely filed, but it was not.  Plaintiffs had until January 18, 2024 to file an opposition to that demurrer to the Complaint. Plaintiffs filed their FAC on January 29 – eleven days after it could legally be filed without leave to file from the Court. On January 31, 2024, the Court sustained the unopposed demurrer as to Plaintiffs’ Complaint, with 20 days leave to amend. (Minute Order 1/31/24.) Twenty days from the Court Order would be on or before Tuesday, February 20, 2024. Plaintiffs did not file their FAC either before January 18, 2024 or from January 31 through February 20, 2024. Therefore, the FAC was not filed in a procedurally proper manner and must be stricken.

 

            Accordingly, the Court GRANTS the Motion to Strike Plaintiffs’ FAC, with 20 days leave to file a valid FAC. Therefore, the Moving Defendant’s Demurrer is MOOT.

           

            In preparing the FAC that the Court has granted leave to file, the Court notes that it did not grant leave to Plaintiffs to add new causes of action to the Complaint in connection with the original demurrer.  As such, any FAC that Plaintiffs file in connection with this order should not include the additional causes of action alleged in the FAC filed on January 29, 2024.
