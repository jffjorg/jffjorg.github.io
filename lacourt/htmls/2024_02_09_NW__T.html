Case Number: 23VECP00442    Hearing Date: February 9, 2024    Dept: T

 

23VECP00442 LAW OFFICES OF ROSALINE L. ... vs VLADISLAV SHULIKO

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Judgment Debtor Vladislav Shuliko’s Claim of Exemption is GRANTED in Part and DENIED in Part.  The Levying Officer is ORDERED to release $1,500.00 to the Judgment Creditor Julia Gandelsman and to release the balance of $185.08 to the Judgment Debtor Vladislav Shuliko.

Introduction

Defendant/Judgment Debtor Vladislav Shuliko (Debtor) filed a claim of exemption as to a bank levy issued in favor of Plaintiff/Judgment Creditor Julia Gandelsman (Creditor). 

Discussion 

Per the Writ of Execution filed on October 19, 2023, the Los Angeles County Sheriff levied funds found in Debtor’s bank account in the amount of $1,685.08.  Funds in a natural person's deposit account may be exempt, and the judgment debtor may file a claim of exemption to release the funds from the execution levy. Funds in a judgment debtor’s deposit account are exempt to the extent necessary for the support of the judgment debtor.  (Code Civ. Proc., sec. 704.225.)  Debtor filed a Financial Statement asserting that the levied funds are exempt because they are necessary for his support.  Debtor’s Financial Statement asserts that he has “no income” yet incurs monthly expenses totaling $23,750.00.  Although the breakdown of expenses appears to be excessive because Debtor is single and has no spouse or dependents, the Court noted that Debtor spends $1,500.00 per month for “Entertainment”.   The Court does not find that the “Entertainment” expense is necessary for the Debtor’s support and the amount can be applied to payment of the judgment.

Debtor’s Claim of Exemption is GRANTED in Part and DENIED in Part.  The levying officer is ordered to release $1,500.00 to Creditor and the balance of $185.08 to Debtor.  

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.


Case Number: 23VECV03559    Hearing Date: February 9, 2024    Dept: T

23VECV03559 LINDA NGUYEN, AN INDIVIDUAL vs SKY ZONE

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Defendants Sky Zone, LLC and SZ Socal, LLC’s Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay the Action is CONTINUED to 3/14/2024 at 8:30 a.m. in Dept. T for further briefing from the parties to address the Court’s tentative ruling.  Plaintiff Linda Nguyen’s response is due 9 court days prior to the new hearing date.  Defendants Sky Zone, LLC and SZ Socal, LLC’s response is due 5 court days prior to the new hearing date. 

Introduction

            Defendants Sky Zone, LLC and SZ Socal, LLC dba Sky Zone Van Nuys, fka SZ Van Nuys Capital Partners, LLC erroneously named/served as 7741 Hayvenhurst, LLC (collectively, Defendants) moved to compel arbitration and stay Plaintiff Linda Nguyen’s (Plaintiff) action. Defendant requested attorneys’ fees and costs per the arbitration agreement and/or monetary sanctions per Code of Civil Procedure section 128.7 in the amount of $7,586.65.  Plaintiff’s Opposition requested monetary sanctions per Code of Civil Procedure section 128.7 in the amount of $4,800.00.    

            Discussion 

The Court is authorized to order the arbitration of a controversy if it determines that an agreement to arbitrate the controversy exists.  (Code Civ. Proc., sec. 1281.2.)  “Arbitration” is not expressly defined in the statute.  Arbitration may take many procedural forms.  However, a form of alternative dispute resolution is not considered an “arbitration” unless it has the following attributes:  “(1) a third party decision maker; (2) a mechanism for ensuring neutrality with respect to the rendering of the decision; (3) a decision maker who is chosen by the parties; (4) an opportunity for both parties to be heard, and (5) a binding decision. [Bolding added.]”  (Cheng-Canindin v. Renaissance Hotel Associates (1996) 50 Cal. App.2d 676, 684-685.)  Finality is a core component of arbitration.  (Moncharsh v. Heily & Blasé (1992) 3 Cal.4th 1, 10.) 

The Court notes that Defendant’s “arbitration” agreement and Defendant’s motion is expressly grounded on the “non-binding” nature of the arbitration.  Within Defendant’s arbitration agreement, the “non-binding” nature of the arbitration was expressly stated eleven separate times.  Because the agreement requires “non-binding” arbitration, the Court is not authorized to enforce the agreement per Code of Civil Procedure section 1281.2 because it is not a binding decision. 

In deciding a motion/petition to compel arbitration, trial courts must decide first whether an enforceable arbitration agreement exists between the parties, and then determine the second gateway issue whether the claims are covered within the scope of the agreement.  (Omar v. Ralphs Grocery Co. (2004) 118 Cal.App.4th 955, 961.)  Because Defendant’s agreement requires “non-binding” arbitration, Defendant failed to present facts showing the existence of an enforceable “arbitration” agreement.

The motion to compel arbitration and stay of the action is not persuasive and should be denied.   There is no legal authority which authorizes a court to order a case to non-binding arbitration in a motion to compel arbitration.

Because the legal authority cited by the Court is a new issue not raised in any of the parties’ briefs, the Court CONTINUES the hearing to allow proper notice and opportunity to be heard on the issue by all parties. 

            IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.

 


Case Number: 23VECV04806    Hearing Date: February 9, 2024    Dept: T

23VECV04806 TONY ROBERT TAWIL vs GALPIN MOTORS

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Defendant Galpin Motors, Inc.’s Demurrer to the Complaint is OVERRULED as to the first cause of action and  SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND as to the second cause of action.

Defendant Galpin Motors, Inc.’s Motion to Strike is DENIED in its entirety.

Defendant Galpin Motors, Inc. is ORDERED to file an Answer within 20 days.

Introduction

Defendant Galpin Motors, Inc. (Defendant) demurred to Plaintiff Tony Robert Tawil’s (Plaintiff) Complaint.  Defendant’s demurrer placed into issue the first cause of action (COA) for fraud and deceit and the second COA for breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing.  Defendant concurrently moved to strike the request for punitive damages and allegations referring to three online complaints by unknown persons not parties to this action (Compl. par. 47). 

Discussion 

As to the first COA for fraud, Defendant asserted that there are insufficient facts pled as to the misrepresentations and reasonable reliance.  To support the contention, Defendant relied upon the terms of the Retail Installment Sales Contract (RISC) attached as Exhibit A to the Complaint.  Defendant argued that facts in an attached exhibit take precedence over contradictory factual allegations within the body of the pleading.  Defendant asserted that the RISC expressly notified Plaintiff of the cash price, the markup, and the optional nature of the optional theft deterrent device.  Based upon the terms of the RISC, Defendant argued that there are insufficient facts to plead any misrepresentation or reasonable reliance.  However, Defendant’s argument is grounded on a misinterpretation of the allegations.  The fraud COA is based on the claim that the RISC is invalid due to the fraud.  Plaintiff’s requested relief under the fraud COA is to rescind the RISC.  (Compl. par. 65.)  Plaintiff does not allege that the RISC is valid and enforceable.  Defendant’s argument presumed that the RISC is valid.  Defendant did not correctly apply the rule that facts alleged in an attached exhibit take precedence over contradicting factual allegations in the body of the pleading.  The rule requires Plaintiff’s COA to plead that the contract is valid and enforceable (i.e., similar to a breach of contract claim in a collection case) but Plaintiff’s COA does not do that.   Defendant’s argument is unpersuasive.

The Demurrer to the first COA is OVERRULED. 

The Court noted that the Demurrer to the first COA cited to the pleading requirements of specificity and particularity.  However, Defendant did not present any argument as to this pleading requirement and/or how the Complaint’s allegations are deficient related to specificity or particularity.  A mere reference to the requirements of specificity and particularity is insufficient to present argument on how the pleading is deficient.  A judge in a civil case is not “'obligated to seek out theories [a party] might have advanced, or to articulate for him that which … [a party] has left unspoken.'” (Mesecher v. County of San Diego (1992) 9 Cal.App.4th 1677, 1686.)  The demurrer that merely references the pleading requirements of specificity and particularity is unpersuasive. 

The Demurrer to the first COA is OVERRULED. 

Defendant argued that the second COA for breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing failed to allege facts pleading that Plaintiff did not receive the benefits of the RISC (i.e., possession/ownership of the vehicle).  Plaintiff countered that the benefit of the bargain was the lower advertised price.  However, payment of money was not Plaintiff’s benefit of the bargain.  It was Defendant’s benefit of the bargain.  Payment of the purchase price was Plaintiff’s obligation under the RISC, irrespective of whether the price to be paid was the price on the RISC or the advertised price.  Plaintiff’s contention to dispute Defendant’s argument is not persuasive because Plaintiff’s argument is based upon a misinterpretation of the pleading element.  The demurrer argument is persuasive.

The demurrer to the second COA is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND. 

Defendant moved to strike Plaintiff’s request for punitive damages.  Defendant argued that there are insufficient facts alleged to support malice, oppression, or fraud.  Defendant’s argument again relied upon the terms of the RISC, which was not found to be persuasive as  reviewed above.  The factual allegations within the fraud COA are sufficient to support the request for punitive damages and the fraud element under punitive damages per Civil Code section 3294. 

The motion to strike the request for punitive damages is DENIED.

Defendant argued that the allegations regarding three online reviews by unknown persons are irrelevant because the online reviews are hearsay and cannot be verified.  However, the assertion of evidentiary objections at the pleading stage is improper.  At the pleading stage, allegations of fact are deemed true.  Arguing the veracity, authenticity, or speculative nature of a factual allegations is insufficient to argue irrelevance.  Plaintiff sufficiently provided that the factual allegations go to the pleading elements of intent to defraud and knowledge of the falsity.  The Court does not find Defendant’s argument persuasive and the factual allegations are shown to be relevant.

The motion to strike Complaint paragraph 47 is DENIED. 

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.
