DEPARTMENT 39 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 24STCV00274    Hearing Date: December 3, 2024    Dept: 39

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPARTMENT

39

HEARING DATE

December 3, 2024

CASE NUMBER

24STCV00274

MOTION

Motion to Stay Action

MOVING PARTY

Plaintiff George Stanley Donev

OPPOSING PARTY

Defendant Jared Smith

 

MOTION

Plaintiff Goerge Stanley Donev (“Plaintiff”) moves to stay this action because of a restraining order in Donev v. Smith (Super. Ct. L.A. County, 2023, No. 23STRO08197 (case No. 23STRO08197)).  Defendant Jared Smith (“Smith”) opposes the motion.   

ANALYSIS

“Trial courts generally have the inherent power to stay proceedings in the interests of justice and to promote judicial efficiency.”  (Freiberg v. City of Mission Viejo (1995) 33 Cal.App.4th 1484, 1489.) 

Plaintiff obtained a domestic violence order of protection against Defendant in case No. 23STRO08197.  The restraining order prohibits Defendant from contacting Plaintiff “directly or indirectly, by any means . . . ,” but permits Defendant to contact Plaintiff “through a lawyer or process server or another person for service of legal papers related to a court case . . . .”  The order is in effect until April 23, 2025.  (Declaration of George S. Donev, Exhibit B.) 

Plaintiff and Defendant are self-represented litigants in this action.  As the Court noted at the last proceedings in this matter, given this reality, if this case proceeds without some carve out for it in the protective order, Plaintiff and Defendant will interact in ways that violate that order.  Defendant has, for example, served at least one deposition subpoena and several sets of written discovery, all of which would require Plaintiff to interact with Defendant.  With respect to any deposition, whether it is conducted in person or taken remotely, such interaction is inevitable. Neither party has indicated he has returned to the court which issued the protective order to seek some modification of the order that would permit some limited contact for purposes of the parties participating in these proceedings.

In opposition, the Defendant argues the domestic violence protective order issued against him is on appeal.  It is not.  The Court of Appeal dismissed Defendant’s appeal on September 10, 2024.  Therefore, the protective order remains in full force and effect.

 Defendant also asserts Plaintiff desires a stay of this action because he wants to avoid discovery Defendant has propounded and because Plaintiff wants to proceed with an action in which the parties are involved in Hawaii.  Given remarks Plaintiff makes in his reply brief, Defendant’s contentions in this regard are on the mark.  To be clear, however, the only reason the Court finds a stay at this time is warranted is because of the practical impossibility of these two self-represented litigants being able to meaningfully participate in this action while a domestic violence protective order is in place, and that order has not been modified to permit them to communicate and otherwise interact with each other. 

Accordingly, the Court grants the motion to stay and schedules an order to show cause regarding the status of the domestic violence protective order on April 26, 2025. 

As the Court grants the motion to stay, the Court places the motion to quash service of summons and all other motions off calendar. 

Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of this order and to file proof of service of same.

 


Case Number: 24STCV23755    Hearing Date: December 3, 2024    Dept: 39

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPARTMENT

39

HEARING DATE

December 3, 2024

CASE NUMBER

24STCV23755

MOTION

Demurrer to Complaint

Motion to Strike Portions of Complaint

MOVING PARTY

Defendant American Honda Motor Co., Inc.

OPPOSING PARTY

Plaintiff China Nicholas

 

MOTION

Plaintiff China Nichols (“Plaintiff”) sued Defendant American Honda Motor Co., Inc. (“Defendant”) under the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act.  Defendant demurs to Plaintiff’s complaint, generally alleging the claims are time-barred and specifically contending the fifth cause of action for fraudulent inducement/concealment is susceptible to demurrer.  Defendant also moves to strike portions of the complaint.  Plaintiff opposes the motions. 

ANALYSIS

“It is black letter law that a demurrer tests the legal sufficiency of the allegations in a complaint.”  (Lewis v. Safeway, Inc. (2015) 235 Cal.App.4th 385, 388.)  In ruling on a demurrer, the court must “liberally construe[]” the allegations of the complaint.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 452.)  “This rule of liberal construction means that the reviewing court draws inferences favorable to the plaintiff, not the defendant.”  (Perez v. Golden Empire Transit Dist. (2012) 209 Cal.App.4th 1228, 1238.)

Any party, within the time allowed to respond to a pleading, may serve and file a motion to strike the whole pleading or any part thereof.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 435, subd. (b)(1); Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1322(b).)  On a motion to strike, the court may: (1) strike out any irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading; or (2) strike out all or any part of any pleading not drawn or filed in conformity with the laws of California, a court rule, or an order of the court.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 436, subd. (a)-(b); Stafford v. Shultz (1954) 42 Cal.2d ¶767, 782.)

Defendant generally argues Plaintiff’s claims are time-barred.  The statute of limitations on claims under the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act is four years.  (Com. Code., § 2725; Mexia v. Rinker Boat Co., Inc. (2009) 174 Cal.App.4th 1297, 1305-1306.)  However, a cause of action does not accrue “until the plaintiff discovers, or has reason to discover, the cause of action.”  (Fox v. Ethicon Endo-Surgery, Inc. (2005) 35 Cal.4th 797, 807.) 

In the instant case, Plaintiff alleges she bought the vehicle at issue more than four years prior to filing the complaint.  (Complaint, ¶ 6.)  However, Plaintiff contends she “discovered Defendant's wrongful conduct alleged herein shortly before filing this Complaint as the Subject Vehicle continued to exhibit symptoms of defects following Defendant's unsuccessful attempts to repair them.  However, Defendant failed to provide restitution pursuant to the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act.”  (Complaint, ¶ 23.)  For pleading purposes, these allegations suffice.  The Court cannot determine Plaintiff’s claims are time-barred as a matter of law.

Defendant specifically challenges Plaintiff’s fifth cause of action for fraudulent inducement.  A party must allege fraud with particularity.  “This means: (1) general pleading of the legal conclusion of fraud is insufficient; and (2) every element of the cause of action for fraud must be alleged in full, factually and specifically, and the policy of liberal construction of pleading will not usually be invoked to sustain a pleading that is defective in any material respect.”  (Wilhelm v. Pray, Price, Williams & Russell (1986) 186 Cal.App.3d 1324, 1331.)  To state a claim for promissory fraud, Plaintiff must allege specific factual circumstances beyond contract breach, which reflect Defendant’s contemporaneous intent not to perform.  (Hills Transportation Co. v. Southwest Forest Ind., Inc. (1968) 266 Cal.App.2d 702, 707.)  Plaintiff alleges Defendant knew the driver-assisting safety system in the vehicle Plaintiff purchased from Defendant had defects.  (Complaint, ¶¶ 45, 49.)  However, Plaintiff does not allege specific facts showing Defendant intended to sell Plaintiff a vehicle with a non-functional driver-assisting safety system at the time of the sale.  Based on Defendant’s challenge, and given Plaintiff’s pleading, Plaintiff must amend to address Defendant’s contentions.

Defendant also argues the economic loss rule bars the cause of action for fraud.   The economic loss rule does not apply to claims for fraud.  (See County of Santa Clara v. Atlantic Richfield Company (2006) 137 Cal.App.4th 292, 328 [while extensively interpreting the Robison Helicopter Company v. Dana Corp. (2004) 34 Cal.4th 979 case, the Court of Appeal reasoned “the California Supreme Court’s decision in Robinson precludes the application of the economic loss rule to any intentional affirmative fraud action where the plaintiff can establish that the fraud exposed the plaintiff to liability”  and “fraud itself is immune from application of the economic loss rule because fraud is particularly blameworthy and therefore unlike both contract causes of action and products liability causes of action”].)  Thus, Defendant’s argument is unfounded. 

As the Court sustains Defendant’s demurrer to the cause of action for fraud, the motion to strike the punitive damages allegations is moot.  However, the Court notes Plaintiff fails to allege facts to support an award of punitive damages.  To state a prima facie claim for punitive damages, a plaintiff must allege the elements set forth in the punitive damages statute, Civil Code section 3294.  (College Hospital, Inc. v. Superior Court (1994) 8 Cal.4th 704, 721 (College Hospital).)  Pursuant to Civil Code section 3294, a plaintiff must allege the defendant has been guilty of oppression, fraud, or malice.  (Civ. Code, § 3294, subd. (a).)  “Malice is defined in the statute as conduct intended by the defendant to cause injury to the plaintiff or despicable conduct which is carried on by the defendant with a willful and conscious disregard of the rights or safety of others.”  (College Hospital, supra, 8 Cal.4th at p. 725.)  “The mere allegation an intentional tort was committed is not sufficient to warrant an award of punitive damages.  Not only must there be circumstances of oppression, fraud or malice, but facts must be alleged in the pleading to support such a claim.”  (Grieves v. Superior Ct. (1984) 157 Cal.App.3d 159, 166, internal citations and footnotes omitted.) 

Plaintiff does not allege any specific facts constituting malicious conduct, which could support an award of punitive damages.  (See, e.g., Complaint, ¶ 64.)  Accordingly, at this point, Plaintiff has failed to allege facts to support an award of punitive damages. 

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Defendant’s demurrer to the fifth cause of action in Plaintiff’s complaint is sustained with leave to amend.  Sustaining the demurrer to the fifth cause of action renders the motion to strike moot.  Plaintiff is to file an amended complaint within 20 days of notice of this order. 

Defendant is ordered to provide notice of this order and to file proof of service of same.
