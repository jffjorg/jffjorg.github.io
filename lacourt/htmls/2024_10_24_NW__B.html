DEPARTMENT B LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Attorneys who elect to submit on these published tentative rulings, without making an appearance at the hearing, may so notify the Court by communicating this to the Department's staff before the set hearing time. See, e.g., CRC Rule 324(b).

Case Number: 20STCV25187    Hearing Date: October 24, 2024    Dept: B

SUPERIOR COURT OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

DEPARTMENT B

TENTATIVE RULING

YARI DE LEON, an individual, vs. MATTHEW FEDERGREEN, an individual. Case No.: 20STCV25187 Hearing Date: OCTOBER 24, 2024

Defendant Matthew Federgreen’s motion to compel the independent medical examination of Plaintiff Yari de Leon is granted. Defendant’s request for sanctions is also granted in the reduced amount of $1,772.67.

Defendant Matthew Federgreen (“Defendant”) moves to compel the independent medical examination (“IME”) of Plaintiff Yari de Leon (“Plaintiff”), contending that there is good cause for the motion because Plaintiff placed her physical injuries in controversy. (Notice of Motion, p. 5:11-17.)

“Unless otherwise limited by order of the court …, any party may obtain discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, that is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action …, if the matter either is itself admissible in evidence or appears reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence.” (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 2017.010.)

Where any party seeks to obtain discovery by a physical examination, the party shall obtain leave of the court. (CCP § 2032.310(a).) The Court shall grant the motion only for good cause shown. (CCP§ 2032.320(a).)

Meet and Confer

On May 17, 2024, defense counsel served a Demand for Physical Examination of Plaintiff on plaintiff’s counsel, to be conducted on July 8, 2024, by orthopedic surgeon Philip S. Yuan, M.D. (“Dr. Yuan”). (Babadjanian Decl. ¶ 6; Exh. D [Demand].) On June 14, 2024, the examination was re-noticed with an amended

date of July 15, 2024, to accommodate a change in Dr. Yuan’s schedule. (Ibid. at ¶ 7; Exh. E [amended Demand].) On July 8, 2024, defense counsel sent plaintiff’s counsel an email confirming Plaintiff’s attendance for the July 15th examination. (Ibid. at ¶ 9.) Plaintiff’s counsel replied that Plaintiff was unavailable on that date. (Ibid.; Exh. F [meet and confer email].) On July 16, 2024, defense counsel offered to withdraw any motion to compel Plaintiff’s examination if Plaintiff stipulated to a date and time regarding their availability for an IME. (Ibid. at ¶ 11; Exh. F.) Defense counsel received no response. (Ibid. at ¶ 13.) On July 24, 2024, defense counsel emailed plaintiff’s counsel for a final opportunity, giving Plaintiff’s counsel one day to confirm whether Plaintiff would appear for an August 19, 2024, examination. (Ibid. at ¶ 14.; Exh. I [July 24, 2024 email].) Accordingly, the Court finds that Defendant satisfies the meet and confer declaration requirement.

Separate Statement Requirements

A motion for “medical examination over objection” must be accompanied by a separate statement. (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1345(a).) Here, Plaintiff did not object to Defendant’s noticed IMEs. (Babadjanian Decl. ¶ 8.) Accordingly, a separate statement is not required.

Good Cause

Defendant contends that there is good cause to compel Plaintiff’s IME because Plaintiff placed her physical injuries in controversy. In her response to Form Interrogatories, Set One, Plaintiff claims injuries to her neck and back. (Exh. A [Plaintiff’s Response to Form Interrogatories, Set One] Nos. 6.2.) Plaintiff also contends that her neck and back pain is worsening. (Ibid. at No. 6.3.)

Plaintiff did not object to Defendant’s noticed IMEs or oppose this motion. Without an IME, Defendant cannot fully evaluate Plaintiff’s injuries and prepare a defense for trial. Accordingly, the Court finds that Defendant has shown good cause to compel Plaintiff's IME.

Required Specifications of the Proposed IME

A motion for an examination must specify the time, place, manner, conditions, scope, and nature of the examination, as well as the identity and specialty, if any, of the person or persons who will perform the examination. (CCP § 2032.310(b).)

Defendant provides a scheduled IME for November 4, 2024, at 9:00 a.m., to be conducted by Dr. Yuan, an orthopedic surgeon, at 2760 Atlantic Ave., Long Beach, CA 90806. (Notice of Motion p. 2:1-7.) Defendant states that the IME “will

be conducted for the purposes of determining, evaluating and assessing plaintiff’s physical condition and claims of injury in controversy in this action.” (Ibid. at 15-16.) The examination “will be consistent with the practices of orthopedic surgery in Southern California and consultations in connection with such practices.” (Ibid. at 22-23.) Finally, the exam may include the taking of x-rays, CT scans, or MRIs as the performing physician deems necessary. (Ibid. at 27-28.)

Accordingly, the Court finds that Defendant provides the required specifications of the proposed IME.

Sanctions

Defendant moves to impose monetary sanctions against Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s counsel, jointly and severally, in the amount of $2,589.54.

Here, sanctions are appropriate since Plaintiff’s failure to appear at the July 15, 2024, IME and failure to reschedule a subsequent examination constitutes a misuse of the discovery process under CCP § 2023.010.

The requested $2,589.54 consists of the following: .8 hours for meet and confer communications with opposing counsel; 7.5 hours to prepare this motion; 1.5 hours in anticipation for preparing a reply; 1 hour for attending this hearing; .3 hours for preparing a notice of ruling, all at the hourly rate of $160.17 per hour; a $61.65 filing fee; and a $750.00 non-appearance fee which defense counsel incurred for the July 15, 2024, IME. (Babadjanian Decl. ¶ 17.) Prior to the July 15, 2024, IME, Defendant put Plaintiff on notice of the $750.00 non-appearance fee. Accordingly, the Court finds this fee reasonable. However, as Plaintiff did not oppose this motion, and this motion largely involves a simple fact pattern, the Court reduces the amount of attorney work hours to six. Therefore, the Court imposes sanctions against Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s counsel in the reduced amount of $1,772.67.

Conclusion

For the reasons set forth above, the motion to compel Plaintiff’s independent medical examination is GRANTED. Sanctions are awarded against Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s counsel in the amount of $1,772.67.

HEARING DATE: Thurs., Oct. 24, 2024 JUDGE/DEPT: Moskowitz/Dep. B

CASE NAME: KAREN MARIA RODRIGUEZ, et al. vs EDWIN DE SANTIAGO, et al.

CASE NUMBER: 20STCV47354

PROCEEDINGS: EXPEDITED PETITION FOR APPROVAL OF MINOR’S COMPROMISE

MOVING PARTY: Petitioner Karen Maria Orozco, on behalf of minor Claimant Justin Yael Bustillo

RESP. PARTY: None

EXPEDITED PETITION FOR APPROVAL OF

MINOR’S COMPROMISE OF A DISPUTED CLAIM

(CCP § 372, CRC, rules 7.950, 7.950.5)

TENTATIVE RULING:

The Expedited Petition filed on behalf of minor Claimant Justin Yael Bustillo is DENIED.

ANALYSIS:

I. Background

On December 10, 2020, Plaintiff Justin Yael Bustillo (“Claimant”) by and through his

guardian ad litem Karen Maria Orozco (“Petitioner”) filed an action for general and motor vehicle negligence against Defendants Edwin de Santiago (“Edwin”), Jose de Santiago (“Jose”), and Does 1 to 100 (collectively “Defendants”). Petitioner was appointed as guardian ad litem for Claimant on February 1, 2021.

On April 20, 2023, Petitioner filed a Notice of Settlement.

On December 4, 2023, the Court approved a compromise of claim or action or disposition of proceeds of judgment for minor Claimant. However, on June 26, 2024, Petitioner filed a notice of errata, notifying that her name was incorrectly listed as Karen Maria Rodriguez on all prior filings. Petitioner also notified that she would file an amended minor’s compromise with her correct legal name, Karen Maria Orozco.

On June 6, 2024, the matter was assigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B of Van Nuys Courthouse East. Petitioner was ordered to give notice to all parties in this action

within 10 days of service of this notice and file proof of service thereof within 12 days of this notice. The Court notes that Petitioner has failed to file proof of service of the Notice of Case Reassignment.

On September 16, 2024, Petitioner filed the instant Expedited Petition to Approve Minor’s Compromise of Disputed Claim on behalf of Claimant.

II. Legal Standard

Court approval is required for all settlements of a minor’s claim. (Prob. Code §§ 3500, 3600, et seq.; CCP § 372.) “‘[W]ithout trial court approval of the proposed compromise of the ward’s claim, the settlement cannot be valid. [Citation.] Nor is the settlement binding [on the minor] until it is endorsed by the trial court.’” (Pearson v. Superior Court (2012) 202 Cal.App.4th 1333, 1338.) A minor, like Claimant, “shall appear either by a guardian or conservator of the estate or by a guardian ad litem appointed by the court in which the action or proceeding is pending, or by a judge thereof, in each case.” (CCP § 372(a)(1).) Alternatively, the petitioner may file a declaration demonstrating that he or she has a right to compromise the minor’s claim under Cal. Probate Code § 3500.

Regarding the substance of the Petition, to obtain court approval of the settlement of a minor’s claims, the petitioner must file a complete and “verified petition for approval of the settlement and must disclose ‘all information that has any bearing upon the reasonableness of the compromise.’ [Citations.]” (Barnes v. Western Heritage Ins. Co. (2013) 217 Cal.App.4th 249, 256; CRC Rule 7.950.) (Italics added.)

Under CRC Rule 7.950.5, an expedited petition for minor’s compromise without a scheduled hearing is permitted as long as Petitioner uses the required Judicial Council forms and meets certain additional conditions.

III. Discussion & Conclusion

Petitioner seeks court approval for a settlement under which Claimant would receive

$500.00. $100.00 in costs and $125.00 attorney’s fees will be deducted from Claimant’s settlement amount. (MC-350EX Items 14a. and 14b.)

The fee agreement between Petitioner and Petitioner’s counsel is reasonable as it represents a 25% contingency fee of the proposed $500 settlement. (MC-350EX Attachment 18a.) Plaintiff’s counsel represents that, due to lack of medical records kept by the treating clinic, Clinica Medica, Virgin de Guadalupe, Petitioner can only provide back to school notes as proof of Claimant’s full treatment. (Sapir Decl. ¶¶ 3-6; Attachment 9 [back to school note].)

Nonetheless, Petitioner still does not meet requirements under CRC 7.950.5 because they fail to disclose information that bears upon the reasonableness of the compromise. Notably, Petitioner and Plaintiffs Norma Leticia Perez and Jairo Abraham Orozco are to receive $10,000, $6,000, and $500, respectively. (See Item 12b.) Petitioner fails to provide a declaration or other admissible evidence pertaining to the reasonableness of Claimant’s settlement amount in relation to Plaintiffs’. Petitioner indicates that the reason for the apportionment is provided on Attachment 12, yet no Attachment 12 is furnished.

Additionally, although Defendant Jose Santiago answered Plaintiffs’ complaint, he is not participating in the compromise as required by CRC 7.950.5(7)(A).

Accordingly, the Expedited Petition filed on behalf of minor Claimant Justin Yael Bastillo is DENIED. Petitioner is put on notice that further deficient filings risk the incurrence of sanctions pursuant to CCP § 128.7 and CRC Rule 2.30.

Petitioner is ordered to give notice.

PROCEEDINGS: EXPEDITED PETITION FOR APPROVAL OF MINOR’S COMPROMISE

MOVING PARTY: Petitioner Sandra Riestra, on behalf of minor Claimant Matthias Barreno Riestra

RESP. PARTY: None

EXPEDITED PETITION FOR APPROVAL OF

MINOR’S COMPROMISE OF A DISPUTED CLAIM

(CCP § 372, CRC, rules 7.950, 7.950.5)

TENTATIVE RULING:

The Expedited Petition filed on behalf of minor Claimant Matthias Barreno Riestra is DENIED.

ANALYSIS:

I. Background

On June 17, 2022, Plaintiff Matthias Barreno Riestra (“Claimant”), by and through his

guardian ad litem Sandra Riestra (“Petitioner”), filed an action for general and motor vehicle negligence against Defendants Leonel Mayorquin (“Mayorquin Sr.”), Leonel Mayorquin Jr. (“Mayorquin Jr.”), and Does 1 to 30 (collectively “Defendants”). Petitioner was appointed as guardian ad litem for Claimant on June 20, 2022.

On June 5, 2024, the matter was assigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B of Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024. Petitioner was ordered to give notice to all parties in this action within 10 days of service of this notice and file proof of service thereof within 12 days of this notice. The Court notes that Petitioner has failed to file proof of service of the Notice of Case Reassignment.

On August 23, 2024, Petitioner filed the instant expedited petition for approval of compromise of claim or action or disposition of proceeds of judgment for minor.

II. Legal Standard

Court approval is required for all settlements of a minor’s claim. (Prob. Code §§ 3500, 3600, et seq.; CCP § 372.) “‘[W]ithout trial court approval of the proposed compromise of the ward’s claim, the settlement cannot be valid. [Citation.] Nor is the settlement binding [on the minor] until it is endorsed by the trial court.’” (Pearson v. Superior Court (2012) 202 Cal.App.4th 1333, 1338.) Western Heritage Ins. Co. (2013) 217 Cal.App.4th 249, 256; CRC Rule 7.950.) (Italics added.)

Under CRC Rule 7.950.5, an expedited petition for minor’s compromise without a scheduled hearing is permitted as long as Petitioner uses the required Judicial Council forms and meets certain additional conditions.

III. Discussion & Conclusion

As a preliminary matter, Petitioner used the incorrect form for their expedited petition.

Petitioner filed on MC-350, but an expedited petition must be filed on MC-350EX. (CRC Rule 7.950.5.) Petitioner’s proposed order indicates that Petitioner intended to file an expedited petition. (See MC-351, Item 1a.) The petition on form MC-350 cannot be approved since a non-expedited petition requires a hearing. (Prob. Code § 3505.)

Accordingly, the instant petition must be denied. Nonetheless, for Petitioner’s benefit, the Court chooses to analyze the proposed settlement’s merits.

Petitioner seeks court approval for a settlement under which Claimant would receive

$15,000.00. $147.47 will be deducted from Claimant’s settlement amount for medical expenses. (MC-350 Item 16b.) $5,000.00 will be deducted from Claimant’s settlement amount for attorney’s fees. (MC-350 Item 16c.) An additional $640.00 will be deducted from Claimant’s settlement amount for other expenses. (MC-350 Item 16d.) Accordingly, Claimant will receive a net settlement of $9,212.53.

Claimant is represented by counsel with a retainer agreement. (MC-350 Item 17a(2).) The retainer agreement is provided as Attachment 17(a) and supports the 33% contingency fee

sought. Additionally, Petitioner provides information and medical records that bear upon the reasonableness of the compromise. Claimant suffered severe head trauma from the motor vehicle accident.

However, no declaration was provided regarding the reasonableness of the settlement’s apportionment. Petitioner and Plaintiff Dylan Riestra are to receive $11,000 and $4,000 in settlement, respectively. (MC-350 Item 11b(5).) A declaration or other evidence regarding this apportionment is required. Additionally, Petitioner improperly completes Item 21, identifying herself as the Claimant.

Accordingly, the petition filed on behalf of minor Claimant Matthias Barreno Riestra is DENIED. The Court advises Petitioner that future deficient filings risk sanctions pursuant to CCP § 128.7 and CRC Rule 2.30.

Petitioner is ordered to give notice.
