DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.




 






Case Number: 21STCV07830    Hearing Date: May 8, 2024    Dept: 26

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Department 26

 

 

YOUNGQUAN HU and JINGHUA REN,

 

                        Plaintiffs,

            vs.

 

DAIMLER TRUCKS NORTH AMERICA, LLC, et al.,

 

                        Defendants.

 

 Case No.: 21STCV07830

 

 Hearing Date: May 8, 2024

 

[TENTATIVE] order RE:

DEFENDANT DAIMLER TRUCKS NORTH AMERICA, LLC’S MOTION TO STRIKE

 

 

 

Background

            On February 5, 2024, Plaintiffs Youngquan Hu and Jinghua Ren (“Plaintiff”) filed the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) against Defendants Daimler Trucks North America, LLC, a Delaware Limited Liability Company; XPO Logistics, LLC, a Delaware Limited Liability Company; American Alliance Logistics, Inc., a California Corporation; Popo Trucking, Inc. a California Corporation; GC Global Trucking, Inc. a California Corporation; Action Logistics, Inc., a California Corporation; Liang Ye, an individual; Ran Gao, an Individual; Werner Enterprises, Inc. a Nebraska Corporation; Fleet Truck Sales, Inc. a Nebraska Corporation (“Defendants”); and DOES 3 to 50, inclusive for: (1) Strict Product Liability; (2) Negligent Product Liability; (3) Negligence; and (4) Negligent Hiring, Supervision, or Training of Employee.

            On March 8, 2024, Defendant Daimler Trucks North America, LLC (“DTNA”) filed the instant Motion to Strike. On April 23, 2024, Plaintiffs filed an opposition. The reply was due on May 1, 2024. As of May 3, 2024, no reply has been filed.

 

Allegations in the Operative FAC

The FAC alleges as follows:

On or about March 20, 2020, Defendant XPO contracted with Sabert Corp. to transport plastic trays and lids from Sayreville, New Jersey to Riverside, California. (FAC ¶ 18.) Defendant XPO hired Defendant Alliance to drive the load. (FAC ¶ 19.) The load was transported in a 2016 Freightliner Cascadia, California License Plate No. XP65462, Vehicle Identification No.  VIN 3AKJGLD51GSGU1349, (hereafter the “SUBJECT VEHICLE”). (FAC ¶ 20.) The SUBJECT VEHICLE was owned by the Alliance Defendants but leased to Defendant Popo at the time of the accident. (FAC ¶¶ 21-22.)

On March 21, 2020, Plaintiff and Defendant Gao were co-driving the SUBJECT VEHICLE from Sayreville to Riverside. (FAC ¶ 23.) Plaintiff  and Defendant Gao were employed by Defendant XPO and the Alliance Defendants and were acting within the course and scope of their employment at the time of the accident. (FAC ¶ 24.) Further, Defendant Gao was driving westbound on the I-40 in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma when he lost control of the SUBJECT VEHICLE and collided with the wall on the right-hand side of the freeway, lost its trailer and then collided with the wall on the left-hand side of the freeway and stopped. (FAC ¶ 25.)  During the collision Plaintiff was sleeping in the lower sleeping bunk of the SUBJECT VEHICLE’s sleeper compartment, which was a tent style bunk restraint in use. (FAC ¶ 26.) Plaintiff moved laterally, from side to side, during the collision, struck his head, and was rendered quadriplegic.  (Id.)

Defendants Daimler, Werner, Fleet and DOES 3 through 24, inclusive, and each of them, (hereafter “THE PRODUCT DEFENDANTS”) knew that the SUBJECT VEHICLE was to be purchased and used without inspection for defects by the users of that vehicle including but not limited to Plaintiff. (FAC ¶ 30.) Specifically, “THE PRODUCT DEFENDANTS failed to make the sleeper compartment safe for the sleeping passenger in an accident.  Specifically, the sleeping passenger is completely unrestrained.  It is common knowledge and well known by the PRODUCT DEFENDANTS that an unrestrained passenger in a moving vehicle is at risk of serious bodily injury or death.  In spite of this THE PRODUCT DEFENDANTS failed to provide a sufficient occupant protection system for the sleeper compartment.” (FAC ¶ 32.)

 

Legal Standard

Motions to strike are used to reach defects or objections to pleadings that are not challengeable by demurrer (i.e., words, phrases, prayer for damages, etc.).  (See Code Civ. Proc., §§ 435-437.)  A party may file a motion to strike in whole or in part within the time allowed to respond to a pleading, however, if a party serves and files a motion to strike without demurring to the complaint, the time to answer is extended.  (See Code Civ. Proc., §§ 435(b)(1), 435(c).)

A motion to strike lies only where the pleading has irrelevant, false, or improper matter, or has not been drawn or filed in conformity with laws.  (See Code Civ. Proc., § 436.)  The grounds for moving to strike must appear on the face of the pleadings or by way of judicial notice.  (See Code Civ. Proc., § 437.)

 

Meet and Confer Requirement

Code of Civil Procedure section 435.5, subdivision (a) requires that “[b]efore filing a motion to strike pursuant to this chapter, the moving party shall meet and confer¿in person or by telephone¿with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to the motion to strike for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the motion to strike.” The parties are to meet and confer at least five days before the date the responsive pleading is due and if they are unable to meet the demurring party shall be granted an automatic 30-day extension.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 435.5(a)(2).)  The moving party must also file and serve a declaration detailing the meet and confer efforts.  (Id.¿at (a)(3).)¿ If an amended pleading is filed, the parties must meet and confer again before a motion to strike may be filed to the amended pleading.  (Id.¿at (a).) 

Here,  DTNA sufficiently met and conferred.  (Cosgrove Decl. ¶¶ 2-3, Ex. A.)

 

Discussion

DTNA moves to strike allegations of and prayer for punitive damages and prejudgment interest. Specifically, DTNA moves to strike paragraphs 41-43, and prayer at 21:8-11 as to the first and second causes of action. DTNA argues the FAC does not specifically plead any facts to support a claim for punitive damages because it is replete with conclusions and contentions of counsel, which are not allegations of fact that must be deemed true by the Court. Furthermore, DTNA contends the FAC merely characterizes its conduct as intentional, which is insufficient. DTAN also contends the FAC makes no effort to distinguish between the alleged acts of two, separate and distinct legal entities. Moreover, DTNA asserts Plaintiffs must plead specific facts constituting clear and convincing evidence that DTNA is guilty of fraud, malice or oppression that was ratified or approved ny an officer, director or managing agent of DTNA, which Plaintiffs did not do. Additionally, DTNA argues prejudgment interest is not available here because there must be facts alleged to establish oppression, fraud and/or malice under Civil Code section 3288 and prejudgment interest may not be awarded on claims for general, noneconomic damages for personal injury, physical, mental and emotional pain and suffering. Finally, DTNA contends Plaintiffs should not be granted leave to amend because they were granted leave to file the FAC years after this action was commenced, discovery was performed, and a trial date was set.

In opposition, Plaintiffs argue they have alleged specific facts evidencing that DTNA knew their truck’s sleeper compartment was dangerous to sleep in because the safety system was inadequate and left the occupant completely unrestrained; knew that people had been seriously injured in their trucks because the safety systems were inadequate; and continued to market the truck and actively encouraged people to sleep in the truck while driving across country, which established that DTNA was aware of the probable consequences of their conduct and willfully and deliberately failed to avoid those consequences. Plaintiffs also argue the allegations in the FAC clearly put DTNA on notice of the punitive damages claim against them. Moreover, Plaintiffs contend the cases cited by DTNA are distinguishable from this instant case because their allegations are much more detailed and rise to the level of conscious disregard. In addition, Plaintiff argue they have made sufficient allegations that DTNA’s officers, directors or managing agents ratified the wrongful conduct. Lastly, Plaintiff contend if the Court decides to grant the present motion, any alleged defects would easily be corrected with leave to amend.

 

Punitive Damages and Prejudgment Interest

California Civil Code section 3294 authorizes the recovery of punitive damages in non-contract cases where “the defendant has been guilty of oppression, fraud, or malice . . . .” (Civ. Code, § 3294(a).) “‘Malice’ means conduct which is intended by the defendant to cause injury to the plaintiff or despicable conduct which is carried on by the defendant with a willful and conscious disregard of the rights or safety of others.” (Id. at (c)(1).) “‘Oppression’ means despicable conduct that subjects a person to cruel and unjust hardship in conscious disregard of that person’s rights.” (Id. at (c)(2).) “‘Fraud’ means an intentional misrepresentation, deceit, or concealment of a material fact known to the defendant with the intention on the part of the defendant of thereby depriving a person of property or legal rights or otherwise causing injury.” (Id. at (c)(3).) Punitive damages thus require more than the mere commission of a tort. (See Taylor v. Superior Court (1979) 24 Cal.3d 890, 894-95.)

In order to survive a motion to strike a prayer for punitive damages, a plaintiff must plead ultimate facts showing its entitlement to such relief. (Clauson v. Superior Court (1998) 67 Cal.App.4th 1253, 1255 (Clauson).) Allegations, devoid of any factual assertions, are insufficient to support a conclusion that parties acted with oppression, fraud, or malice. (Smith v. Superior Court (1992) 10 Cal.App.4th 1033, 1042.) “In ruling on a motion to strike, courts do not read allegations in isolation.” (Clauson, supra, 67 Cal.App.4th at p. 1255.)

“‘Something more than the mere commission of a tort is always required for punitive damages. There must be circumstances of aggravation or outrage, such as spite or “malice,” or a fraudulent or evil motive on the part of the defendant, or such a conscious and deliberate disregard of the interests of others that his conduct may be called willful or wanton.’ [Citation.]” (Taylor, supra, 24 Cal.3d at 894–895, [italics omitted].) 

Similarly, “[i]n an action for the breach of an obligation not arising from contract, and in every case of oppression, fraud, or malice, interest may be given, in the discretion of the jury.” (Civ. Code, § 3288.)

 

Here, the FAC pleads sufficient facts to support Plaintiffs’ claims for punitive damages and prejudgment interest. The FAC alleges DTNA was aware that the sleeper compartment of their truck was not safe and causes occupants to suffer catastrophic injury in side impacts and rollover incidents  because it leaves the occupant unrestrained in a moving vehicle. (FAC 11:11-19.) The FAC further alleges despite this knowledge, DTNA continued to produce, market, and sell the subject vehicle. (FAC 11:20-23.) The FAC also alleges DTNA was aware of alternative designs that exists which would offer protection from lateral movement in a side impact or rollover accident. (FAC 11:24-28-12:1-19.) Taking these allegations as true, a jury could reasonably found that DTNA acted with conscious disregard as to the safety of others in continuing to produce, market, and design the subject vehicle with the purported defect.

 

Conclusion and ORDER

            Defendant Daimler Trucks North America, LLC’s Motion to Strike is DENIED.

Legal Standard

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Code Civ. Proc., § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)  

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(e)). The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(e).)  

Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(e).) A motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.)

 

Discussion

Counsel has filed the MC-051, MC-052, and MC-053 forms pursuant to California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362.

Counsel served Defendant Zhong by mail at his last known address, which Counsel states was confirmed within the past 30 days as current through Defendant Tang.

Counsel declares that representation cannot continue because the attorney-client relationship has been breached as Counsel is unable to contact or communicate with Defendant Zhong since late January 2024. Counsel further states he has tried to reach Defendant Zhong via email and telephone, but to no avail.

Counsel also filed proof of service indicating that the requisite forms were served on Defendant Zhong and all other parties who have appeared in this case. Furthermore, it appears from the court record that Defendant Zhong has filed a substitution of attorney.

Finally, the proposed order on form MC053 includes all the hearings scheduled in this matter as follows:

 

1.      Motion to be Relieved as Counsel set for 5/8/24;

2.      OSC Re: Dismissal & Hearing on Motion for Summary Judgment set for 5/22/24;

3.      Final Status Conference set for 9/11/24; and

4.      Jury Trial set for 9/23/24

Therefore, Counsel has complied with the requirements of California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362.

 

Conclusion and ORDER
