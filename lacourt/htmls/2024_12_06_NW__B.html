Attorneys who elect to submit on these published tentative rulings, without making an appearance at the hearing, may so notify the Court by communicating this to the Department's staff before the set hearing time. See, e.g., CRC Rule 324(b).

Case Number: 20STCV47432    Hearing Date: December 6, 2024    Dept: B

Ron a. rosen jonfaza VS 1875/1925 CENTURY PARK EAST COMPANY, et al.

 

MOTION TO COMPEL further RESPONSES TO form INTERROGATORIES, SET one, SPECIAL INTERROGATORIES, SET ONE, AND REQUEST for production of documents, set one, and request FOR MOnetary sanctions.

 

Date of Hearing:  December 6, 2024                          Trial Date:       June 30, 2025

Department:         B                                                    Case No.:         20STCV47432

 

Moving Parties:                       Defendants Pocrass & De Los Reyes, LLP, James Pocrass,                                         and Michael Devore

Responding Party:                   Plaintiff Ron A. Rosen Jonfaza

 

BACKGROUND

 

On December 10, 2020, Plaintiff Ron A. Rosen Jonfaza (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants 1875/1925 Century Park East Company (“Century Park”), Watt Properties, Inc. (“Watt Properties”), Pocrass & De Los Reyes, LLP (“Pocrass & De Los Reyes”), James Pocrass (“Pocrass”), Michael Devore (“Devore”), Abrams & Taheri LLP (“Abrams & Taheri”), and Does 1 to 50 alleging two causes of action: (1) general negligence; and (2) premises liability.

 

On June 6, 2024, the action was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

 

On October 17, 2024, Pocrass & De Los Reyes, Pocrass, and Devore (collectively “Defendants”) filed the instant motions to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Production of Documents, Set One (“Discovery Motions”). Defendants also request sanctions.

 

Plaintiff did not file an opposition.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendants’ Discovery Motions are GRANTED.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

Interrogatories

 

Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 2030.300 provides, in pertinent part, that “[o]n receipt of a response to interrogatories, the propounding party may move for an order compelling a further response if the propounding party deems that any of the following apply: (1) An answer to a particular interrogatory is evasive or incomplete. (2) An exercise of the option to produce documents under Section 2030.230 is unwarranted or the required specification of those documents is inadequate. (3) An objection to an interrogatory is without merit or too general.” (CCP § 2030.300(a).) 

 

Unless notice of a motion to compel further responses is given within 45 days of the service of the verified response, or any supplemental verified response, or on or before any specific later date to which the propounding party and the responding party have agreed in writing, the propounding party waives any right to compel a further response. (CCP § 2030.300(c).) 

 

Request for Production of Documents

 

CCP § 2031.310 provides, in pertinent part, that “[o]n receipt of a response to demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling, the demanding party may move for an order compelling further response to the demand if the demanding party deems that any of the following apply: (1) A statement of compliance with the demand is incomplete. (2) A representation of inability to comply is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive. (3) An objection in the response is without merit or too general.” (CCP § 2031.310(a).)

 

Unless notice of a motion to compel further responses is given within 45 days of the service of the verified response, or any supplemental verified response, or on or before any specific later date to which the propounding party and the responding party have agreed in writing, the propounding party waives any right to compel a further response. (CCP § 2031.310(c).) 

 

Under California law, the objecting party has the burden of justifying its objections when the propounding party requests that the Court order further responses. (Coy v. Superior Court (1962) 58 Cal.2d 210, 220-221.) Further, under CCP § 2023.010, it is a misuse of discovery to make unmerited objections. (CCP § 2023.010(e).) 

 

DISCUSSION

Meet and Confer Requirement

A motion to compel further responses shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration. (CCP § 2030.300(b); CCP § 2031.310(b).) Defendants initially propounded Discovery Motions upon Plaintiff on June 26, 2024. (Marchi Decl. ¶ 2; Exh. 1 [service]; Exh. 2 [discovery requests].) After various extensions agreed upon by both parties, Plaintiff served Defendants with responses to their Discovery Motions on September 26, 2024. (Ibid. at ¶ 7; Exh, 12 [service of responses]; Exh. 13 [responses].) However, these responses objected to Defendants’ discovery requests in their entirety. (Ibid. at ¶ 7; Exh. 13.)

 

On October 3, 2024, Defendants sent a meet and confer email to Plaintiff’s counsel, noting the objections and requesting that Plaintiff provide substantive, verified, and amended responses by October 10, 2024. (Ibid. at ¶ 8; Exh. 15 [October 3, 2024, email].) On October 10, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel called and requested an extension. (Ibid. at ¶ 9.) Defendants denied this request, noting that, due to previous extensions, Plaintiff had 106 days to provide substantive responses. (Ibid. at ¶¶ 9 and 11; Exh. 17 [October 10, 2024, email regarding counsels’ telephone conversation].)

 

To date, Defendants have not received Plaintiff’s supplemental responses to discovery or proof of service thereof. (Ibid. at ¶ 15.) Therefore, Defendants satisfy the meet and confer requirements of CCP §§ 2030.300(b) and 2031.310(b).

 

Form Interrogatories, Set One

Defendants move to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, in its entirety.

Defendants’ motion is granted.

 

Here, Plaintiff provided the same boilerplate objection to each and every one of Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories. The identical objections state as follow:

“Objection. Responding Party objects to this interrogatory as it seeks irrelevant

matter not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence in the subject lawsuit.”

(Marchi Decl. Exh. 13 [Plaintiff’s responses].)

 

Form interrogatories were created and approved by the Judicial Council to allow discovery into information regarding an individual’s general background information. A party responding to these interrogatories owes a duty to respond in good faith as best as possible. (Deyo v. Kilbourne (1978) 84 Cal.App.3d 771, 783.)

Here, Plaintiff failed to respond in a substantive manner. Therefore, Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified, objection-free, supplemental responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, in its entirety.

Special Interrogatories, Set One

Defendants move to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, in its entirety.

Defendants’ motion is granted.

Here, Plaintiff provided the same boilerplate objection to each and every one of Plaintiff’s Special Interrogatories. The identical objections state as follow:

“Objection. Responding Party objects to this interrogatory as it seeks irrelevant

matter not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence in the subject lawsuit. This request is overly broad and unduly burdensome in

scope and remote in its time span as to be excessively burdensome, oppressing,

and harassing. Greyhound Corp. v. Superior Court (1961) 56 Cal.2d 355.”

(Marchi Decl. Exh. 13 [Plaintiff’s responses].)

 

Here, Plaintiff failed to respond in a substantive manner. Therefore, Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified, objection-free, supplemental responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, in its entirety.

Request for Production of Documents, Set One

Defendants move to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One, in its entirety.

Defendants’ motion is granted.

Here, Plaintiff provided the same boilerplate objection to each and every one of Plaintiff’s Request for Production of Documents. The identical objections state as follow:

“Objection. This request is improper because it fails to designate

documents by specifically describing each individual item or by reasonably

particularizing each category of item in contravention of Code of Civil Procedure

§2031.030(c)(1). This request is overly broad and unduly burdensome in scope

and remote in its time span as to be excessively burdensome, oppressing, and

harassing. Greyhound Corp. V. Superior Court (1961) 56 Cal.2d 355. This request

is vague, ambiguous, and overbroad which calls into speculation what this

request means or is referring to. This request is objected to on the grounds that

it seeks documents that are protected by the anticipation of litigation privilege,

attorney/client privilege, and/or work-product rule. See e.g. Payless Drug Stores,

Inc. v. Superior Court (1976) 54 Cal.App.3d 988; Rodriguez v. McDonnell Douglas

Corp., (1978) Cal.App.3d 626, 647-648; Nacht & Lewis Architects, Inc. v. Superior

Court, (1996) 47 Cal.App.4th 214. Subject to and without waiving said

objections, Responding Party responds as follows: The documents requested are

equally available to Defendants.”

 

(Marchi Decl. Exh. 13 [Plaintiff’s responses].)

 

Defendants’ requests for production of documents are, by and large, specific and related to the underlying incident. Additionally, they are not equally available to Defendants as Plaintiff claims. Many of them relate to Plaintiff’s alleged injuries, economic damages, and contentions of that nature.

Plaintiff failed to respond in a substantive manner. Therefore, Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified, objection-free, supplemental responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One, in its entirety.

Monetary Sanctions

 

“The court may impose a monetary sanction against a party engaging in the misuse of the discovery process, or any attorney advising that conduct” under CCP § 2023.030(a). A court has discretion to fix the amount of reasonable monetary sanctions. (Cornerstone Realty Advisors, LLC v. Summit Healthcare Reit, Inc. (2020) 56 Cal.App.5th 771, 791.)

 

Here, Plaintiff failed to substantively respond to Defendants’ Discovery Motions without substantial justification, constituting a misuse of the discovery process. In fact, Plaintiff’s universal boilerplate objections to Defendants’ Discovery Motions have the practical effect of not responding at all. Therefore, monetary sanctions are appropriate.

 

Form Interrogatories, Set One

 

Defendants request monetary sanctions in the amount of $1,184.00 for their motion to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, representing 6.4 hours of work at an hourly rate of $185.00 (Marchi Decl. ¶¶ 16-19.) The Court reduces monetary sanctions to $925.00, representing five hours of work at an hourly rate of $185.00, as Plaintiff did not file an opposition.

 

Special Interrogatories, Set One

 

Defendants request monetary sanctions in the amount of $1,591.00 for their motion to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, representing 8.6 hours of work at an hourly rate of $185.00 (Marchi Decl. ¶¶ 16-19.) The Court reduces monetary sanctions to $1,295.00, representing seven hours of work at an hourly rate of $185.00, as Plaintiff did not file an opposition.

 

Request for Production of Documents, Set One

 

Defendants request monetary sanctions in the amount of $2,201.50 for their motion to compel Plaintiff’s further responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One, representing 11.9 hours of work at an hourly rate of $185.00 (Marchi Decl. ¶¶ 16-19.) The Court reduces monetary sanctions to $1,295.00, representing seven hours of work at an hourly rate of $185.00, seeing as Plaintiff did not file an opposition and the motion is largely identical to Defendants’ other motions to compel further.

 

Total Sanctions

 

Pursuant to CCP § 2023.050, Defendants also request sanctions in the amount of $1,000.00 since Plaintiff delayed discovery responses for 106 days before serving boilerplate objections to Defendants’ Discovery Motions in their entirety. (Motion to Compel Plaintiff’s Further Responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One, Marchi Decl. ¶ 19.)

 

CCP § 2023.050 provides, in pertinent part, that “a court shall impose a one-thousand dollar ($1,000) sanction, payable to the requesting party, upon a party, person, or attorney if, upon reviewing a request for sanction made pursuant to Section 2023.040, the court finds any of the following: (1) The party, person, or attorney did not respond in good faith to a request for the production of documents made pursuant to Section 2020.010, 2020.410, 2020.510, or 2025.210, or to an inspection demand made pursuant to Section 2031.010.” (CCP § 2023.050(a)(1).)

 

Here, Plaintiff did not respond in good faith to Defendants’ request for production of documents and inspection demand made pursuant to CCP § 2031.010. Therefore, the Court grants Defendants’ request for sanctions in the amount of $1,000.00 pursuant to CCP § 2023.050.

 

In total, for Plaintiff’s failure to substantively respond to Defendants’ Discovery Motions, Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s counsel, jointly and severally, is ordered to pay defense counsel sanctions in the sum amount of $5,540.00.

 

CONCLUSION

 

Based on the foregoing, Defendants’ Discovery Motions are GRANTED.

 

The Court ORDERS that Plaintiff provides verified, complete and code-compliant responses, without objections, to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Production of Documents, Set One, in their entirety, within 20 days of this hearing.

 

Additionally, Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s counsel, jointly and severally, are ordered to pay $5,540.00 in monetary sanctions to defense counsel within 20 days of this hearing.

Moving party is ordered to give notice.


Case Number: 22STCV06193    Hearing Date: December 6, 2024    Dept: B

Haoxian lai vs ip shun lee, et al.

 

MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

 

Date of Hearing:          December 6, 2024                  Trial Date:       April 11, 2025

Department:               B                                              Case No.:         22STCV06193

 

Moving Party:             Todd B. Becker, Esq.

Responding Party:       None  

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from the alleged February 2020 assault of Plaintiff Haoxian Lai (“Plaintiff”) by Defendant Ip Shun Lee (“Lee”). On February 18, 2022, Plaintiff brought suit against Defendants Lee, Fung Ying Yeung (“Yeung”), Ka Sing Chiang (“Chiang”),  and Does 1 to 50 (collectively “Defendants”) alleging three causes of action: (1) premises liability; (1) intentional tort (as to Lee); (2) general negligence (as to Yeung and Chiang); and (3) premises liability (as to Yeung and Chiang.)

 

On June 6, 2024, this action was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

 

On November 7, 2024, Todd B. Becker, Esq. (“Counsel”) filed the instant unopposed Motion to be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiff.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel filed by Todd B. Becker, Esq.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)   

 

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (CRC Rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (CRC Rule 3.1362(e)).  The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (CRC Rule 3.1362(e).)

 

Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (CRC Rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (CRC Rule 3.1362(e).) The Court has discretion on whether to allow an attorney to withdraw, and a motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) 

 

DISCUSSION

Counsel declares that there has been an irreconcilable breakdown of the attorney-client relationship. (MC-052 Item 2.) Counsel also represents that continued representation of Plaintiff may cause Counsel to violate the Rules of Professional Conduct or the State Bar Act. (Ibid.)

Counsel has filed and served Plaintiff with the required documents under CRC Rule 3.1362. Plaintif will not be prejudiced by Counsel’s relief as they have adequate time to find alternative representation. Therefore, Counsel’s motion to be relieved is granted.

CONCLUSION

 

The Court GRANTS the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel filed by Todd B. Becker, Esq., effective upon proof of service of the signed Order being filed with the court.  

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

Cout to sign the proposed order.
