DEPARTMENT 20 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


TENTATIVE RULINGS  


SUBMISSION INSTRUCTIONS

      STANLEY MOSK COURTHOUSE, DEPARTMENT 20 - JUDGE KEVIN C. BRAZILE

Counsel may submit on the tentative ruling without appearing at the hearing by emailing Dept. 20 as soon as possible after reviewing a posted tentative. Though the Court makes every effort to post tentatives at least a day ahead of the hearing, this cannot be guaranteed due to the volume of motions. The email address is smcdept20@lacourt.org. In the subject line include:

1) The name and number of the case;
2) The word "SUBMITTING" or “NOT SUBMITTING” in all caps; and
3) The date of hearing. 

In the body of the email include your name, contact information, and the party you represent (i.e. Defendant/moving party; Plaintiff/opposing party). Include all other parties on the email by "cc". Do not include any comments, questions or other information on your email.

PLEASE DO NOT call the court to submit on the tentative or to confirm receipt of your email.  If you follow the instructions above, you will receive an automatic reply to your email confirming receipt of your email. If all parties submit, the tentative ruling will become the final ruling after the hearing date, the court will sign applicable orders/judgments, if any, and the final ruling will be posted online with the minute order.   The moving party shall give notice of the final ruling. 

If you submit on the tentative, you must immediately notify the other side by email that you will not appear at the hearing. If you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion.

Tentative rulings are not invitations nor opportunities, to file further documents relative to the hearing in question.  No such document will be considered by the Court.

RULES ON USING EMAIL THE COURT


 


No ex-parte communications via email. Always copy all parties in all emails to Court.


Do not use email to file documents in Court. All documents must be filed in the Clerk’s office.  Emails are not part of the official Court record.  Do not use the email for any other purpose other than submitting/not submitting on tentative rulings or as ordered by the Court.  Do not "cc" the Court on emails among the attorneys, parties or others.  Do not use the email to ask questions regarding a case. For frequently asked questions go to the Court Information for Department 20 at www.lacourt.org.
  The Court will not respond to inappropriate emails.


 


WARNING: Inappropriate use or misuse of the Court’s email or violation of these or other rules may result in sanctions, including blocking receipt of emails by that sender, after the first misuse/violation.

On March 6, 2023, Plaintiff AMAG, Inc. filed the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) for (1) fraudulent conversion, (2) misrepresentation, (3) constructive fraud, (4) breach of fiduciary duty, and (5) declaratory relief.  Plaintiff alleges that Defendant Vlaze Media Networks, Inc. sought to avoid the judgment against it in case no. BC587406 by fraudulently transferring certain assets to the other Defendants or with the help of the other Defendants.  

On May 1, 2023, Anastasiia Arseneva filed the operative Second Amended Cross-Complaint (“SACC”) for (1) breach of written contract, (2) quantum meruit, (3) violation of Pen. Code §§ 528.5, 529, and (4) declaratory relief.

There are now 6 Motions to Compel Further Responses at issue.


  1. Motion -089

Arseneva seeks to compel further responses to her requests for admission, set two, propounded on Omar Zambrano.

The Motion is denied because Arseneva did not meet and confer in good faith.  Rather, she sent out a meet and confer letter and then filed the instant Motion the next day.  This is plainly insufficient.


  1. Motion -636

Arseneva seeks to compel further responses to her requests for production propounded on Zambrano.  

This Motion is untimely.  The Motion must be filed within 45 of receiving responses.  (CCP § 2031.310(c).)  Even taking into account the later email service date of January 24, 2024, for the subject responses, the deadline to file the instant Motion was March 13, 2024, when the Motion was actually filed much later on May 13, 2024.  Thus, the Motion is denied.


  1. Motion -391

Arseneva seeks to compel further responses to her requests for admission, set one, and form interrogatories from Zambrano.

The Motion is granted as to RFA nos. 19-20, 22-23 and 25 because it is not apparent that Zambrano ever actually supplemented his responses as promised.  A further response should be provided as Zambrano promised: “A reasonable inquiry concerning the matter in the particular request has been made, and that information know[n] or readily obtainable is insufficient to enable that party to admit the matter.”

The Motion is granted as to RFA no. 29 because the scope of discovery is broad and whether Cubas used multiple social security numbers might ultimately lead to admissible evidence with respect to the forgery claims against Cubas.

The Motion is denied as to RFA no. 31/34 which states: “Admit that YOU applied for a loan from All Star financial, which was guaranteed by Marc Cubas.”  Arseneva has failed to establish that a response could lead to admissible evidence.

The Motion is granted as to RFA no. 35, which states, “Admit that Anastasiia Arseneva paid for YOUR charges, for services, to Northwest Registered Agent LLC.”  The scope of discovery is broad and Zambrano’s arguments are irrelevant and/or seek to reframe the RFA.

The Form Interrogatories (“FIs”) all seek information about Arseneva’s alleged contracts.  The Motion is denied as to all FIs because Zambrano’s response denying the existence of any contract was sufficient, as was the objection about the term “incident” having no application to this matter.

In sum, the Motion is granted in part as set forth herein.  Further responses are to be provided within 20 days.  The Court declines to award sanctions.


  1. Motion -331

  1. Motion -727

Zambrano seeks to compel further responses to his special interrogatories (“SI”), set one, from Arseneva.  

The Motion is granted as to SI nos. 68-69, which seek the identity of Arseneva’s cell phone carrier and all documents that would show this.  The objections lack merit.

The Motion is granted as to SI nos. 70-76, which relate to Arseneva’s allegations that she has large tax liabilities due to Cross-Defendants’ fraudulent actions.  The objections lack merit.

  1. Motion -159

Arseneva seeks to compel further responses to her requests for production nos. 1-13 from Sydney Cubas.  Arseneva submitted a 108-page separate statement for these 13 requests.  

The requests at issue seek: (1) all documents and videos that show the subject office burglary; (2) all documents or videos that show that the burglary suspect was a woman that looks like Arseneva; (3) video surveillance of the burglary; (4) all telephone records of a report about the burglary to the police; (5) all telephone records of a report about the burglary to an insurance company; (6) all emails about the burglary; (7) all text messages about the burglary; (8) all hard drives or thumb drives having videos and matters pertaining to the burglary; (9)-(11) duplicative and vexatious requests for videos; (12) all communications about the burglary; and (13) all ESI that pertains to the burglary. 

In response, Sydney argues that she emailed her seven surveillance videos to Arseneva and that when Arseneva complained that she couldn’t open them, Sydney then delivered a thumb drive of all the videos.

In Reply, Arseneva, in typical unhelpful fashion, ignores the contention about a thumb drive and continues to complain about inaccessible emails.

Because a thumb drive was apparently delivered, the Motion is denied as to request nos. 1-3 and 9-13.

For request nos. 4-8, Sydney responded that: “RESPONDING PARTY conducted a diligent search and made reasonable inquiries in an attempt to comply with the request but is unable to produce any Documents in her possession, custody, or control that are responsive to this request. and RESPONDING PARTY will comply with this request. RESPONDING PARTY has never possessed any non-privileged Documents that are responsive to this request.”

For these requests, Sydney should indicate, if possible, the name and address of any natural person or organization known or believed by that party to have possession, custody, or control of that item or category of item.  (CCP § 2031.230.)  Also, if there are privileged documents, then Sydney should provide a general basis and explanation for the existence of the privilege.  

The Motion is granted in part as set forth herein.  Further responses are to be provided within 20 days.  The Court declines to award sanctions.

  1. Enforce Settlement

Defendant L.A. Live Rentals, LLC seeks to enforce its settlement with Plaintiff Juan Manuel Quiroz Canchola.  Defendant contends that Canchola and the other Plaintiffs agreed to a settlement but that only Canchola refuses to sign a long-form agreement, which precludes this action from being dismissed and the insurer from tendering payment under the settlement.  

The Court is authorized to enter judgment pursuant to a stipulated settlement.  (Code Civ. Proc. § 664.6.)  In reviewing a motion to enforce a settlement, the Court determines “whether the parties entered into a valid and binding settlement.”  (Hines v. Lukes (2008) 167 Cal.App.4th 1174, 1182.)  “A settlement is enforceable under section 664.6 only if the parties agreed to all material settlement terms.  [Citations.]  The court ruling on the motion may consider the parties’ declarations and other evidence in deciding what terms the parties agreed to, and the court’s factual findings in this regard are reviewed under the substantial evidence standard.  [Citations.]  If the court determines that the parties entered into an enforceable settlement, it should grant the motion and enter a formal judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement.”  (Id. at pp. 1182-1183; see also Osumi v. Sutton (2007) 151 Cal.App.4th 1355, 1357 [“Strong public policy in favor of the settlement of civil cases gives the trial court, which approves the settlement, the power to enforce it”].)

Because a valid settlement exists between the parties and the Court has retained jurisdiction (Dzharatanyan Decl., Exhibit A), the Motion to Enforce Settlement is granted.  (Code Civ. Proc. § 664.6.)
