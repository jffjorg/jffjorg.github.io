DEPARTMENT H LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23PSCV01379    Hearing Date: August 14, 2024    Dept: H

Plaintiff A.A., a minor by and through his Guardian Ad Litem, Sherry Gua’s Application for Default Judgment is DENIED without prejudice.

Background

In this dog bite case, Plaintiff A.A., a minor by and through his Guardian Ad Litem, Sherry Gua (“Plaintiff”), alleges that A.A. was bitten by a Labrador Retriever owned by neighbor Eileen Ortega (“Defendant”). The complaint asserts causes of action against Defendant and Does 1 through 10 for: (1) negligence; (2) common law strict liability based on known dangerous propensities; and (3) statutory strict liability.

Despite being served in June 2023, Defendant has not filed a responsive pleading or otherwise appeared in this case. On June 25, 2024, the Clerk entered default against Defendant, and Plaintiff has filed an application for default judgment against Defendant. A Case Management Conference is set for August 14, 2024.


Discussion

Plaintiff’s Application for Default Judgment is denied without prejudice. The following defects are noted:

1.       Plaintiff’s Statement of Damages (Galindo Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. B) lists $337,500.00 as the total damages amount, which is itemized as follows: (1) $12,500.00 for past medical expenses; (2) $25,000.00  for future medical expenses; (3) $250,000.00  for past pain, suffering, anxiety, disfigurement; and (4) $50,000.00  for future pain, suffering, anxiety and disfigurement. However, in the instant application, Plaintiff seeks $342,438.00 in total damages, which is comprised of $42,438.00 in special damages and $300,000.00 in general damages.

“The relief granted to the plaintiff, if there is no answer, cannot exceed that demanded in the complaint, in the statement required by Section 425.11 [i.e., statement of damages], or in the statement provided for by Section 425.115 [i.e., punitive damages statement] . . .” (Code Civ. Proc., § 580). 

2.       Absent additional information or documentation, the Court is not inclined to award anywhere near $300,000.00 in general damages for pain, suffering, anxiety, and disfigurement ($250,00.00 for past damages and $50,000.00 in future damages).

3.       Plaintiff has failed to complete that portion of Item 5a in the proposed judgment identifying Defendant.

4.       Plaintiff’s counsel attests, in the “declaration of nonmilitary status,” that she knows Defendant is not in the U.S. military because “Plaintiff’s representative has spoken with Defendant and knows she is not in the U.S. military.” This statement fails to identify how “Plaintiff’s representative” came to have such knowledge.

Accordingly, the Court denies Plaintiff’s application without prejudice and directs Plaintiff to submit a new default judgment packet that addresses the aforementioned defects. 

Case Number: 24PSCV01022    Hearing Date: August 14, 2024    Dept: H

Cross-Defendants Bell’s Lashes’ and Steven Zou’s Demurrer to Cross-Complaint is TAKEN OFF CALENDAR.

Background  

Plaintiff Madelynn Carranza (“Plaintiff”) alleges that she sustained severe burning, swelling, itching, and scarring to her eyebrow area due to the use of a defective product during a brow lamination procedure. On April 2, 2024, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting causes of action against Bell’s Lashes, Steven Doe, Lash Stuff LLC and Does 1-30 for (1) general negligence and (2) products liability.

On May 6, 2024, Lash Stuff LLC filed a cross-complaint, asserting causes of action against Bell’s Lashes, Steven Does, and Does 1-20 for (1) equitable indemnity; (2) contribution; (3) express indemnity; and (4) negligence. 

On May 7, 2024, Plaintiff filed an “Amendment to Complaint,” correcting the name of “Steven Doe” to “Steven Zou” (“Zou”). 

Bell’s Lashes and Zou filed
a demurrer to the third cause of action in Lash Stuff LLC’s cross-complaint on
June 25, 2024. Because the Court finds that Lash Stuff LLC filed a timely amended

Discussion 

Bell’s Lashes and Zou demur to the third cause of action (i.e., for Express Indemnity) in Lash Stuff LLC’s cross-complaint, on the basis that it “does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.” (Code of Civil Procedure § 430.10, subdivision (e).) The Court takes the demurrer hearing off calendar based on its conclusion that Lash Stuff LLC filed a timely amended cross-complaint under Code of Civil Procedure § 472. 

Code of Civil Procedure § 472 provides that “[a] party may amend its pleading once without leave of the court at any time before the answer, demurrer, or motion to strike is filed, or after a demurrer or motion to strike is filed but before the demurrer or motion to strike is heard if the amended pleading is filed and served no later than the date for filing an opposition to the demurrer or motion to strike.”

Here, the Court observes that while the demurrer was filed (and electronically served) on June 25, 2024, it should have been electronically served by June 21, 2024, in order to provide the requisite notice under Code of Civil Procedure § 1005, subdivision (b) and 1010.6, subdivision (a)(2)(B) (i.e., 16 court days (excluding the July 4, 2024 court holiday) plus 2 additional court days’ notice for electronic service). Had the demurrer been electronically served by June 21, 2025 (based on the original July 18, 2024 hearing date), the opposition would have been due on July 5, 2024. 

On July 8, 2024, Lash Stuff LLC filed a First Amended Cross-Complaint. On July 9, 2024, the Court issued a “Notice Re: Continuance of Hearing and Order,” resetting the July 18, 2024 hearing on the demurrer to August 14, 2024. Based on the rescheduled hearing, Lash Stuff LLC’s opposition would have been due August 1, 2024. However, Lash Stuff LLC had already filed and electronically served its amended cross-complaint before that date—on July 8, 2024.

Accordingly, because Lash Stuff LLC filed its First Amended Cross-Complaint within the time set forth in Code of Civil Procedure § 472, the Court vacates the hearing on the demurrer. The Case Management Conference remains scheduled for August 14, 2024.
