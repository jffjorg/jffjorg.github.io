DEPARTMENT B LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Attorneys who elect to submit on these published tentative rulings, without making an appearance at the hearing, may so notify the Court by communicating this to the Department's staff before the set hearing time. See, e.g., CRC Rule 324(b).

Case Number: 20STCV46821    Hearing Date: November 1, 2024    Dept: B

 

Hearing:              Friday, November 1, 2024

Re:                         ZOILA MONROY, et al. vs CITY OF GARDENA, et al. (20STCV46821)¿

 

 

Petitioner Zoila Monroy’s Petition for Approval of Compromise of Claim or Action or Disposition of Proceeds of Judgment for Minor or a Person with a Disability

 

Respondent: NO OPPOSITION

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Petitioner Zoila Monroy’s Petition for Approval of Compromise of Claim or Action or Disposition of Proceeds of Judgment for Minor or a Person with a Disability is GRANTED.

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from a motor vehicle collision in which Defendants City of Gardena, a municipal corporation (“Gardena”), Brandon Pastre (“Pastre”), an individual, and Does 1 to 50 (collectively, “Defendants”) negligently owned, maintained, entrusted, and operated a vehicle in a distracted manner, causing it to collide with Plaintiffs Zoila Monroy (“Petitioner”), Genesis Dubon (“Genesis”), and Kelly Dubon (“Kelly”) (collectively “Plaintiffs”) who were in a vehicle stopped at a red traffic light. As a result, Plaintiffs suffered injuries. (Complaint ¶¶11-14.) 

 

On December 7, 2020, Plaintiffs filed an action against Defendants for motor vehicle negligence.

 

On December 7, 2020, Petitioner Zoila Monroy (“Petitioner”) filed an application and order for appointment of guardian ad litem for Kelly Dubon, which was granted. On December 9, 2020, Petitioner filed an application and order for appointment of guardian ad litem for Genesis Dubon, which was granted.

 

On October 27, 2023, and November 7, 2023, Petitioner filed two Petitions to Confirm Minor’s Compromise with Special Needs Trust for Kelly and Genesis. The Court denied both petitions for medical record and filing deficiencies, including the failure to file an Order to Deposit Funds Into Blocked Account (MC-355) for both Claimants.

 

On November 27, 2023, Plaintiffs filed a notice of settlement.

 

On December 18, 2023, Petitioner again filed two Petitions to Confirm Minor’s Compromise with Special Needs Trust for Kelly and Genesis. The Court denied both petitions for medical record deficiencies.

 

On September 9, 2024, Petitioner filed the two instant Petitions for Approval of Compromise of Claim or Action or Disposition of Proceeds of Judgment for Minor or a Person with a Disability for Genesis and Kelly. Petitioner also filed two corresponding forms MC-355.

 

ANALYSIS

 

Petitioner seeks court approval for a settlement under which Genesis and Kelly would receive $7,500 and $6,000, respectively.

 

Legal Standard

 

An enforceable settlement of a minor’s or incompetent’s claim can only be consummated with court approval. (Prob. Code, §§ 2504, 3500, 3600 et seq.; Code Civ. Proc., § 372.) For this purpose, a petition for approval must be presented to the court and until it is granted, there is no final settlement. (Scruton v. Korean Air Lines Co. (1995) 39 Cal.App.4th 1596, 1603-1606.) Any settlement agreement therefore is voidable by the minor’s guardian ad litem. (Id., at p. 1606.) 

 

Probate Code section 3600, et seq., governs how the settlement proceeds are to be paid. Pursuant to Probate Code section 3601, the order shall approve payment of reasonable expenses from the settlement as follows: 

 

The court making the order or giving the judgment referred to in Section 3600, as a part thereof, shall make a further order authorizing and directing that such reasonable expenses (medical or otherwise and including reimbursement to a parent, guardian, or conservator), costs, and attorney’s fees, as the court shall approve and allow therein, shall be paid from the money or other property to be paid or delivered for the benefit of the minor or incompetent person. (Prob. Code, § 3601, subd. (a).) 

 

In cases pursuant to Probate Code section 3601, “unless the court has approved the fee agreement in advance, the court must use a reasonable fee standard when approving and allowing the amount of attorney’s fees payable from money or property paid or to be paid for the benefit of a minor or a person with a disability.” (Cal. Rules of Court 7.955, subd. (a)(1).) In doing so, “[t]he court must give consideration to the terms of any representation agreement made between the attorney and the representative of the minor or person with a disability and must evaluate the agreement based on the facts and circumstances existing at the time the agreement was made, except where the attorney and the representative of the minor or person with a disability contemplated that the attorney's fee would be affected by later events.” (Cal. Rules of Court 7.955, subd. (a)(2).)

 

 

Discussion

 

Petitioner seeks court approval for a settlement under which Genesis and Kelly would receive $7,500 and $6,000, respectively, out of a total settlement of $87,500. No costs or attorney’s fees will be deducted from either Claimant’s settlement amounts.

 

Having reviewed the petitions, the Court finds that the settlement is fair considering the risk of going to trial and the alleged injuries sustained by Plaintiffs. Petitioner has satisfied the requirements under CRC 7.950 by disclosing information that bears upon the reasonableness of the compromise. (Kelly MC-350 Attachment 8; Genesis MC-350 Attachment 8.) There is no fee agreement between Plaintiffs and Counsel as Counsel represented Plaintiffs pro bono. (Kelly MC-350 line 13a; Genesis MC-350 line 13a.) Petitioner’s settlement amount is reasonable given her injuries were more severe than the minors involved. (Kelly MC-350 Attachment 11(b)(3); Genesis MC-350 Attachment 11(b)(3).)

 

CONCLUSION

 

Based on the foregoing, the Court GRANTS the petition to confirm minor’s compromise. Court to sign orders and set OSC re Deposit into blocked account.

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiff Nicholas Alcantar’s request for default judgment against Defendants Down and Out Bar; Rudy Alberto Rayo, an individual; Jennifer Savedra, an individual; Lydia Rodriguez, an individual; jointly and severally is GRANTED.

           

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from an alleged January 26, 2019, assault. Plaintiff Nicholas Alcantar (“Plaintiff”) alleges that Defendants Rudy Alberto Rayo (“Rayo), Jennifer Savedra (“Savedra”), and Lydia Rodriguez (“Rodriguez”), punched and kicked him while at the Down and Out Bar, resulting in serious injury to Plaintiff.

 

On January 22, 2021, Plaintiff filed a complaint alleging four causes of action: (1) assault; (2) battery; (3) intentional infliction of emotional distress; and (4) negligence.

 

On June 6, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

 

On September 20, 2024, all Defendants entered default. Plaintiff then filed the instant request for default judgment against all Defendants, jointly and severally.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

Code of Civil Procedure § 585 permits entry of a default judgment after a party has failed to timely respond or appear. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 585.) A party seeking judgment on the default by the court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) declaration of nonmilitary status; (6) a proposed form of judgment; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorney’s fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1800.) 

 

ANALYSIS

 

Plaintiff seeks damages in the total amount of $12,122.69, representing $9,324.44 in general and special damages and $2,798.25.

 

Defendants have failed to timely respond or appear. Plaintiff’s default packet meets all statutory requirements under CCP § 585. Therefore, Plaintiff’s request for default judgment against Defendants Down and Out Bar; Rudy Alberto Rayo, an individual; Jennifer Savedra, an individual; Lydia Rodriguez, an individual; jointly and severally is GRANTED.

I.       BACKGROUND  

This case is a personal injury action. Plaintiff Mark Williams (“Plaintiff”) alleges that, on or about March 17, 2021, Defendants Maria Ewest (“Maria”) and Christopher Kevin Ewest (“Christopher”), in a road rage incident, called Plaintiff a racial slur, threatened bodily harm against Plaintiff and his family, and intentionally side-swiped Plaintiff’s vehicle. (First Amended Complaint “FAC” ¶¶ 9-14.) Plaintiff alleges physical and property damages as a result.

On January 31, 2022, Plaintiff filed a complaint against Defendants Maria, Christopher, and Does 3 to 50, alleging five causes of action: (1) assault; (2) battery; (3) negligence; (4) intentional infliction of emotional distress; and (5) negligent infliciton of emotional distress.

On June 5, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024. 

On October 4, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant notice of motion and motion for court issuance/seal of subpoenas.

On October 16, 2024, Defendants filed their opposition.

Plaintiff did not file a reply.

 

II.          LEGAL STANDARDS

       Except as provided in Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 2025.280(a), the process by which a nonparty is required to provide discovery is through a deposition subpoena. (CCP § 2020.010 et seq.) Thus, to require the attendance and testimony of a nonparty deponent, as well as his or her production of any document or tangible thing for inspection and copying, the party seeking discovery must serve on that deponent a deposition subpoena under CCP § 2020.010 et seq. (CCP § 2025.280(b).)

       Service of the deposition notice under CCP § 2025.220 is effective to require any deponent who is a party to the action or who is an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party to attend and testify at the deposition, as well as to produce any document or tangible thing for inspection and copying. (CCP § 2025.280(a); see also Waters v. Superior Court (1962) 58 Cal.2d 885, 895-896.)

         The attorney of record in an action or proceeding may sign and issue a subpoena to require attendance before the court in which the action or proceeding is pending, or at the trial of an issue therein, or on the taking of a deposition in an action or proceeding pending therein. The subpoena in such a case need not be sealed. (CCP § 1985(c).) The attorney of record may also sign and issue a subpoena duces tecum to require production of the matters or things described in the subpoena. (CCP § 1985(c).)

          The service of a subpoena generally is made (except on peace officers, firefighters, and specified technical analysts, discussed in CCP § 535.17(5)) by delivering a copy or ticket containing its substance to the witness personally. (CCP § 1987(a).)

           Personal service of any deposition subpoena is effective to require of any deponent who is a resident of California at the time of service: (1) personal appearance and testimony, if the subpoena so specifies; (2) any specified production, inspection, testing, and sampling; and (3) the deponent's attendance at a court session to consider any issue arising out of the deponent's refusal to be sworn, to answer any question, to produce specified items, or to permit inspection or photocopying, if the subpoena so specifies, or specified testing and sampling of the items produced. (CCP § 2020.220(c).)

           Service of the deposition subpoena is invalidated by a defect or failure to comply with any of these requirements, so that the custodian is not obliged to produce records. (CCP § 1985.3(k).) However, minor defects in the deposition subpoena are curable for “good cause,” as long as the rights of the consumer and custodian are preserved. (CCP § 1985.3(h).)

 

III.       DISCUSSION

Plaintiff moves to have subpoenas issued to the following persons or entities: (1) Los Angeles Police Department (“LAPD”); (2) Target Corp.; (3) JC Produce, Inc.; (4) Altagracia Sanguino; (5) Ruben Sanguino; and (6) the Sanguino Living Trust. (Notice of Motion, p. 3:3-5.) The Court addresses each request in subsequence.

 

Los Angeles Police Department

Plaintiff seeks to obtain the 911 telephone call recordings made to LAPD at the time of the underlying incident, as well as LAPD’s dashcam footage and a recorded interview with Plaintiff from their response thereof. (Williams Decl. ¶¶ 7-9.) Plaintiff provides a copy of their subpoena to the LAPD as Exhibit A. (Williams Decl. ¶ 13.) Defendants do not oppose Plaintiff’s subpoena to the LAPD.

The subpoena is substantially related to the underlying incident. Therefore, the Court issues and seals Plaintiff’s subpoena to the LAPD as provided in Exhibit A.

 

Target Corp.

Plaintiff seeks to issue a subpoena to Target Corp. to obtain footage of the persons and vehicle entering Target’s premises at 3535 S. La Cienaga Blvd., Los Angeles, CA, 90016, directly following the underlying incident. (Williams Decl. ¶¶ 8 and 9.) Plaintiff provides a copy of their subpoena to Target Corp. as Exhibit B. (Williams Decl. ¶ 14.) Defendants do not oppose Plaintiff’s subpoena to Target Corp.

The subpoena is substantially related to the underlying incident. Therefore, the Court issues and seals Plaintiff’s subpoena to Target Corp. as provided in Exhibit B.

 

JC Produce, Inc.

Plaintiff seeks to issue a subpoena to JC Produce, Inc., to produce the work schedule of Defendant Maria Ewest on the date of the underlying incident. (Williams Decl. ¶ 15.) Plaintiff provides a copy of their subpoena to JC Produce, Inc. as Exhibit C. (Williams Decl. ¶ 15.) Defendants do not oppose Plaintiff’s subpoena to JC Produce, Inc., in the limited respect as to Maria’s schedule on March 17, 2021.

The subpoena is substantially related to Maria’s involvement in the underlying incident. Therefore, the Court issues and seals Plaintiff’s subpoena to JC Produce, Inc. as provided in Exhibit C.

 

Altagracia Sanguino, Ruben Sanguino, and the Sanguino Living Trust

Plaintiff seeks to issue subpoenas to Altagracia Sanguino, an individual, Ruben Sanguino, an individual, and the Sanguino Living Trust, to obtain Defendant Christopher Kevin Ewest’s rental applications and, subsequently, information regarding his income. (Notice of Motion p. 4:6-12.) Plaintiff states this information is necessary to Plaintiff’s request for punitive damages. Plaintiff provides copies of these subpoenas as Exhibits D, E, and F. (Williams Decl. ¶¶ 16-18.)

These subpoenas seek improper discovery at this stage of litigation. “Unlike defendant's liability insurance, the net dollar value of his other assets, real and personal, before his liability has been established by judgment, is not the measure of the collectibility of any judgment which may be entered against him.” (Doakes v. Superior Court for Los Angeles County (1968) 257 Cal.App.2d 825, 832.) “[T]he pretrial disclosure of [personal assets] would be contrary to the public interest and would not be consistent with the policy of the law.” (Ibid.) Therefore, the Court denies issuance of subpoenas to Altagracia Sanguino, Ruben Sanguino, and the Sanguino Living Trust.

 

IV.    CONCLUSION

The Court issues and seals Plaintiff’s subpoenas to the Los Angeles Police Department, Target Corp., and JC Produce, Inc.

The Court denies issuance of Plaintiff’s subpoenas to Altagracia Sanguino, Ruben Sanguino, and the Sanguino Living Trust.

It is so ordered. Moving party to give notice.

Date of Hearing:          November 4, 2024                  Trial Date:       January 27, 2025

Department:               B                                              Case No.:         22STCV16662

 

Moving Party:             Hesam Yazdanpanah

Responding Party:       None  

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from a motor vehicle accident. On May 19, 2022, Plaintiffs Evan Pugrad (“Pugrad”), Trenton Hearin (“Hearin”), and Caroline Draper (“Draper”), filed a Complaint against Defendant Nancy Martinez (“Defendant”) and Does 1 to 50 alleging a single cause of action for motor vehicle negligence.

 

On June 5, 2024, this action was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz sitting in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East effective June 24, 2024.

 

On September 20, 2024, Hesam Yazdanpanah, Esq. (“Counsel”) filed and served the instant unopposed Motion to be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiffs.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel filed by Hesam Yazdanpanah, Esq.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc., § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)   

 

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (CRC Rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (CRC Rule 3.1362(e)).  The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (CRC Rule 3.1362(e).)

 

Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (CRC Rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (CRC Rule 3.1362(e).) The Court has discretion on whether to allow an attorney to withdraw, and a motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client.  (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) 

 

DISCUSSION

Counsel declares that their office has made multiple attempts to contact Plaintiffs. (MC-052 Item 2.) Counsel represents that their office has been unable to make any communication with them. (Ibid.)

Counsel has filed and served Plaintiffs with the required documents under CRC Rule 3.1362. Plaintiffs will not be prejudiced by Counsel’s relief as they have adequate time to find alternative representation. Therefore, Counsel’s motion to be relieved is granted.

CONCLUSION

 

The Court GRANTS the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel filed by Hesam Yazdanpanah, Esq. This order will take effect once Counsel files proof of service of this signed order on Plaintiffs.

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.
