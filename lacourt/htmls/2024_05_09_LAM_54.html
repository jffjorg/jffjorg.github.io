DEPARTMENT 54 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22STCV36742    Hearing Date: May 9, 2024    Dept: 54

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

 

A-Ju Realty, Inc., et al.,

 

 

 

Plaintiffs,

 

Case No.:

 

 

22STCV36742

 

vs.

 

 

Tentative Ruling

 

 

Kee Whan Ha, et al.,

Hearing Date: May 9, 2024

Department 54, Judge Maurice A. Leiter

(2) Motions to Enforce Subpoena

Moving Party: Defendants Kee Whan Ha, Hannam Chain USA Inc., Jeong Wan Koo, 1000 South Vermont, LLC and Newkoa, LLC

Responding Party: Plaintiffs A-Ju Realty, Inc. and Yun Sam Chung

 

T/R:      DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO ENFORCE SUBPOENA AGAINST DR. CHUNG IS GRANTED. DR. CHUNG IS ORDERED TO COMPLY WITH THE SUBPOENA WITHIN 20 DAYS OF NOTICE OF RULING.

 

DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO ENFORCE SUBPOENA AGAINST JOO HEE CHUNG IS DENIED.

 

DEFENDANTS TO NOTICE.

 

If the parties wish to submit on the tentative, please email the courtroom at SMCdept54@lacourt.org with notice to opposing counsel (or self-represented party) before 8:00 am on the day of the hearing.

 

The Court considers the moving papers, oppositions, and replies.

 

BACKGROUND

               

On February 7, 2023, Plaintiffs A-Ju Realty, Inc. and Yun Sam Chung filed the operative first amended complaint against Defendants Kee Whan Ha, Hannam Chain U.S.A., Inc., Jung Wan Koo, 1000 South Vermont, LLC and Newkoa, LLC, asserting 12 causes of action for breach of fiduciary duty, fraud, conversion, accounting and intentional infliction of emotional distress.

 

Plaintiff A-Ju Realty, Inc. was a 22.5% member of Defendant 1000 South Vermont, LLC, which owned certain real property in Koreatown, Los Angeles. Plaintiffs allege Defendant Ha, the manager of Vermont, represented that Vermont was going to develop the property and would require significant capital investment from Plaintiffs. Plaintiffs were not able to provide additional capital and instead agreed to a buyout of A-Ju’s 22.5% interest in Vermont for $3,000,000.00. Plaintiffs allege Defendants did not actually intend to develop the property, but rather intended to sell the property to a developer, which it later did for $40,000,000.00. Plaintiffs allege Defendants made these misrepresentations to induce Plaintiffs to sell the membership interest in Vermont and keep the profits of the sale for themselves.

 

ANALYSIS

 

CCP § 1987.1 provides, “[i]f a subpoena requires the attendance of a witness or the production of books, documents, electronically stored information, or other things before a court, or at the trial of an issue therein, or at the taking of a deposition, the court, upon motion reasonably made by any person described in subdivision (b), or upon the court's own motion after giving counsel notice and an opportunity to be heard, may make an order quashing the subpoena entirely, modifying it, or directing compliance with it upon those terms or conditions as the court shall declare, including protective orders.”

 

A. Motion to Enforce Subpoena against Dr. Christopher Kyoon Hee Chung

 

Defendants move to enforce a subpoena for medical records from Plaintiff Chung’s medical provider Dr. Christopher Kyoon Hee Chung. Defendants served the subpoena personally on March 9, 2024, with a production date of March 26, 2024. The subpoena seeks all medical records of Plaintiff Chung. Defendants assert the information is relevant to Plaintiff’s claims for emotional distress. In opposition, Plaintiff argues the subpoena is overbroad as to time and scope, but states Plaintiff will provide a medical authorization to allow Dr. Chung to produce the records.

 

As Plaintiff Chung has placed his emotional state at issue, the records are relevant. Dr. Chung must comply with the subpoena.

 

Defendant’s motion to enforce subpoena against Dr. Chung is GRANTED.

 

 

 

B. Motion to Enforce Subpoena against Joo Hee Chung

 

Defendants move to enforce a subpoena for deposition and production of documents on Plaintiff Chung’s ex-wife Joo Hee Chung. The subpoena seeks all medical records pertaining to Plaintiff, records of Plaintiff’s depression and bipolar disorder and all tax returns from 2011 to 2019. Defendants assert this information and information about Plaintiff’s divorce is relevant to Plaintiff’s claims for IIED and NIED. In opposition, Plaintiffs assert these requests are overbroad and harassing and seek privileged (by the tax privilege and martial communication privilege) information. Plaintiffs represent that they have offered to produce Ms. Chung for deposition on May 13, 2024.

 

The Court will not enforce the subpoena as written. Though Plaintiff has placed his emotional state at issue in this action, seeking medical and tax records from his ex-wife is unnecessary to conduct this litigation. Additionally, Plaintiff has agreed to produce Ms. Chung for deposition.

 

Defendant’s motion to enforce subpoena against Joo Hee Chung is DENIED.

“The court may order that a record be filed under seal only if it expressly finds facts that establish: (1) There exists an overriding interest that overcomes the right of public access to the record; (2) The overriding interest supports sealing the record; (3) A substantial probability exists that the overriding interest will be prejudiced if the record is not sealed; (4) The proposed sealing is narrowly tailored; and (5) No less restrictive means exist to achieve the overriding interest.”  (CRC Rule 2.550(d).)  “The motion or application must be accompanied by a memorandum and a declaration containing facts sufficient to justify the sealing.”  (CRC Rule 2.551(b)(1).) 

 

Plaintiffs move to seal Exhibit B attached to the previously-filed April 8, 2024 Declaration of Gerald S. Ohn, which was submitted by Defendants Kee Whan Ha, Hannam Chain U.S.A. Plaintiffs’ motion to seal is DENIED.

BACKGROUND

               

On February 7, 2023, Plaintiffs A-Ju Realty, Inc. and Yun Sam Chung filed the operative first amended complaint against Defendants Kee Whan Ha, Hannam Chain U.S.A., Inc., Jung Wan Koo, 1000 South Vermont, LLC and Newkoa, LLC, asserting 12 causes of action for breach of fiduciary duty, fraud, conversion, accounting and intentional infliction of emotional distress.

 

Plaintiff A-Ju Realty, Inc. was a 22.5% member of Defendant 1000 South Vermont, LLC, which owned certain real property in Koreatown, Los Angeles. Plaintiffs allege Defendant Ha, the manager of Vermont, represented that Vermont was going to develop the property and would require significant capital investment from Plaintiffs. Plaintiffs were not able to provide additional capital and instead agreed to a buyout of A-Ju’s 22.5% interest in Vermont for $3,000,000.00. Plaintiffs allege Defendants did not actually intend to develop the property, but rather intended to sell the property to a developer, which it later did for $40,000,000.00. Plaintiffs allege Defendants made these misrepresentations to induce Plaintiffs to sell the membership interest in Vermont and keep the profits of the sale for themselves.

 

ANALYSIS

 

A. Motion to Compel Further Deposition Testimony

 

“If a deponent fails to answer any question or to produce any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing under the deponent's control that is specified in the deposition notice or a deposition subpoena, the party seeking discovery may move the court for an order compelling that answer or production.” (CCP § 2025.480(a); see Stewart v. Colonial Western Agency, Inc. (2001) 87 Cal.App.4th 1006, 1015 n. 3 [the only proper basis to instruct a deponent to not answer a question is an objection based upon a privilege or manifestly irrelevant questions or questions designed only to harass.]) “If the court determines that the answer or production sought is subject to discovery, it shall order that the answer be given or the production be made on the resumption of the deposition.” (CCP § 2025.480(i).) “This motion shall be made no later than 60 days after the completion of the record of the deposition, and shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration.” (CCP § 2025.480(b).) 

 

Defendants move to compel further deposition answers from Plaintiff on his financial condition, emotional condition, and valuation of his shares of 1000 South Vermont as stated in his divorce financial disclosures. In opposition, Plaintiff asserts this information is irrelevant and private. The Court disagrees. Plaintiff has placed his financial condition, emotional condition, and valuation at issue in this action. Further responses are necessary.

 

Defendants’ motion is GRANTED. The Court declines to award sanctions as Plaintiff claims Defendants failed to meet and confer.

 

B. Motion to Compel Medical Record Authorization

 

Defendants move to compel Plaintiff to provide a medical record authorization for records from Dr. Christopher Kyoon Hee Chung. Defendants provide no authority, and the Court knows of no authority, that allows the Court to order Plaintiff to sign a medical authorization.

 

Defendants’ motion to compel medical record authorization is DENIED.

The Court considers the moving papers, oppositions, and reply.

 

Under CCP §1048(b), the court, in furtherance of convenience or to avoid prejudice, or when separate trials will be conducive to expedition and economy, may order a separate trial of any cause of action, including a cause of action asserted in a cross-complaint, or of any separate issue or of any number of causes of action or issues, preserving the right of trial by jury required by the Constitution or a statute of this state or of the United States. 

 

Defendant moves to sever Plaintiff Tracy Hark’s trial and Plaintiff Al Waller’s trial. Defendant asserts that Plaintiffs Hark and Waller’s allegations arise from separate incidents with separate employers. Defendant argues that a joint trial of both of Plaintiffs’ claims will confuse the jury and the Court. In opposition, Plaintiffs argue that separate trials are a waste of resources and time.

 

The Court finds that severance is appropriate. The respective Plaintiffs’ claims arise from distinct events that occurred over a year and a half apart and involve different employer entities. Neither Plaintiff witnessed the incidents giving rise to the other Plaintiff’s causes of action. Trying the actions together may confuse the jury and prejudice Defendants.

 

Defendant’s motion is GRANTED.
