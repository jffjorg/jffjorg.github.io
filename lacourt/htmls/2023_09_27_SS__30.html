DEPARTMENT 30 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

 PLEASE NOTE:    

The parties are encouraged to meet and confer concerning this tentative ruling to determine if there is an agreement to submit.  

Regardless of whether there is any such agreement, each party who wishes to submit must send an email to the Court at SSCdept30@LACourt.org indicating the party's intention to submit. 

Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the case number in the subject line of the email and in the body provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, or non-party.  

If a party submits but still intends to appear at the hearing, include the words "SUBMITS BUT WILL APPEAR" in the subject line of the email. 

If the Court does not receive emails from the parties indicating submission on this tentative ruling and there are no appearances at the hearing, the Court may, at its discretion, adopt the tentative as the final order or place the motion off calendar. 

Unless all the parties have submitted, the Court will hear argument from any party that appears at the hearing and wishes to argue. The Court may change its tentative as a result of the argument and adopt the changed tentative as the final order at the end of that hearing, even if all the parties are not present. 

Be advised that after the Court has posted/issued a tentative ruling, the Court has the inherent authority to prohibit the withdrawal of said motion and may adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the Court.     


Case Number: 19STCV00482    Hearing Date: September 27, 2023    Dept: 30

GISELLE LEE HAMILTON, AN INDIVIDUAL MINOR, et al. vs KFC CORPORATION, A DELAWARE CORPORATION

Petition to Confirm Minor's Compromise

Tentative:  The Petition to Approve Compromise of Disputed Claim of a Minor is CONTINUED to allow Petitioner to submit an amended form MC-350 containing Attachment 18b(2).  Judicial Assistant to calendar. Moving party to give notice.

Discussion:  

The Court has reviewed the Petition to Approve Compromise of Disputed Claim of a Minor. The Court finds that the settlement is reasonable. The Court also has considered the Declaration of Marc Strecker in support of the fee request. The Court finds that on the current record, and in consideration of the factors set forth in Cal Rules of Court, Rule 7.955(b), a fee of 25% of the gross settlement is reasonable. Petitioner proposes to deposit the funds in insured accounts in one or more financial institutions in this state, subject to withdrawal only on authorization of the court.  However, Petitioner does not provide the name, branch, and address of each depository. There is no Attachment 18b(2).



Case Number: 19STCV28264    Hearing Date: September 27, 2023    Dept: 30

RODY CASTROLL, AN INDIVIDUAL vs CALIFORNIA POLICE ATHLETIC FEDERATION, et al.

Motion to Deem Request for Admissions Admitted

Tentative: Defendant’s motion to deem RFAs admitted is GRANTED. The Court orders that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in Defendant's requests be deemed admitted. The request for sanctions is GRANTED.  Plaintiff is ordered to pay Defendant $ 621.65 in monetary sanctions within 30 days. Moving party is to give notice of this ruling.

Discussion:  

Defendant’s Counsel testifies that he propounded requests for written discovery on Plaintiff on November 30, 2022. (Sipprelle Decl., ¶ 3.) On January 25, 2023, Defendant’s Counsel sent Plaintiff a meet and confer letter inquiring after the late responses and numerous written correspondences to follow up. (Id., ¶ 4.) To date, Plaintiff has not served responses to Defendant’s discovery requests. (Id., ¶4.)

Because Plaintiff has provided no responses to Defendant’s discovery requests, Defendant’s motion to deem RFAs admitted is granted. The genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in Defendant’s motions are admitted.

Sanctions with respect to the motion to deem requests for admissions admitted are mandatory if a party to whom the requests for admissions have been directed failed to serve a timely response to the request for admission. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).)  Accordingly, the Court grants Defendant $ 621.65 in sanctions for 2 hours of attorney time at a rate of $280.00 per hour and $61.65 in filing fees.

CHUL HEE LIM vs KUNG SOON HAN, et al.

Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories
Motion to Compel Responses to Request for Production

Tentative Ruling:  The Motions to Compel re Form Interrogatories and Request for Production are granted.  Plaintiff is ordered to serve responses, without objections, within 20 days of date of notice of ruling.  Requests for sanctions are denied. Moving party to give notice.

Discussion: 

Defendant served Form Interrogatories, Set One, and a Request for Production on Plaintiff on November 4, 2022. (Jeong Decl. ¶ 2, Ex. 3 and 5.) To date, Defendant has not received any responses. (Id. at ¶ 5.) If a party to whom discovery is directed fails to serve a timely response, the propounding party may move for an order compelling responses. (Code Civ. Proc sections 2030.290(b), 2031.300(b)) Further, a party waives its objections to a discovery request when it does not serve a timely response to the request. (Code Civ. Proc. 2030.290(a), 2032.300(a)) Even if objections do not need to be verified, objections will be waived if the responding party “fails to file any response within the statutory time period.” Food 4 Less Supermarkets, Inc. v. Superior Court (1995) 40 Cal.App.4th 651, 658.  The Court declines to award sanctions as these motions are unopposed.  

Case Number: 22STCV02045    Hearing Date: September 27, 2023    Dept: 30

MARISOL SERRANO, et al. vs JOSE ADAN ALARCON GONZALEZ

Demurrer

Tentative Ruling:  Defendant’s demurrer is sustained with leave to amend within 20 days of notice of ruling.  Moving party to give notice.

Discussion:  

STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS

Defendant demurs to Plaintiff’s complaint on the grounds that Plaintiff’s claims are time-barred.

Pursuant to California Code of Civil Procedure section 335.1, “Within two years: An action for assault, battery, or injury to, or for the death of, an individual caused by the wrongful act or neglect of another.”

Here, Plaintiffs’ complaint alleges that Defendant struck Serrano on July 16, 2019. Plaintiff’s deadline to file his complaint was July 16, 2021. Plaintiff’s Complaint was originally filed on January 18, 2022, which falls after two years from the date of the alleged injury.

Judicial Council issued Emergency Rule 9 on April 6, 2020, which allowed the time to file the Complaint to be  tolled from April 6, 2020, until October 1, 2020 (179 days)  The Complaint, however, was filed beyond two years and 179 days, approximately a week. 

Defendant’s demurrer is sustained on this ground.

Bystander Damages

Defendant next argues that Mangram cannot recover damages because he is the boyfriend of Serrano and cannot recover as a bystander.

Because Plaintiff concedes this argument and has requested to dismiss Mangram, this issue is moot.

The Court will grant leave to amend. Plaintiff notes that it has special circumstances that may further toll the statute of limitations. Without ruling upon the merits of those contentions, the Court will allow Plaintiff to amend to allege any tolling that will be necessary to make this action viable. The Court notes that the Complaint has two different dates that  Plaintiff alleges that the incident occurred.  This should be corrected as well.

SOFIA JAMORA vs JAXSON HAYES

Motion to Compel Second Session of Plaintiff's Deposition

Tentative Ruling:  Defendant motion for an order compelling a second deposition of Plaintiff is granted, in part. The requests for sanctions are denied.  Moving party is to give notice of this ruling.

Discussion: 

Evidentiary Objections

Defendant objects to statements in Declaration of Waukeen McCoy submitted in support of Plaintiff’s opposition.

The first objection is SUSTAINED, and the second objection is OVERRULED.

Discussion

Defendant moves for an order compelling a second deposition of Plaintiff.

Plaintiff’s deposition occurred on June 2, 2023, and she was questioned about the text messages with her cousins. (Motion Baghdishyan Decl., ¶ 5.) On June 6, 2023, Defendant served Plaintiff with Requests for Production of Documents, Set Two, and on June 10, 2023, Plaintiff served responses to Defendant’s requests. (Id., ¶¶ 6,7.) The responses included 60 pages of copies of additional text messages between Plaintiff and her cousins. (Id., ¶¶ 4, 7.) Because Defendant received copies of additional text messages she did not receive prior to Plaintiff’s deposition, the court finds good cause for a second deposition, but only for the purpose of further questioning about the text messages.

The court does not find good cause for a second deposition for further questioning about video evidence or Plaintiff’s tax returns. On March 2, 2023, Defendant served Plaintiff with Requests for Production of Documents, Set One, and on May 15, 2023, Plaintiff served responses. (Id., ¶¶ 2,3.) The responses included screenshots of video files, which Defendant now argues show unexplained gaps in video footage. (Id., ¶ 10.) Thus, Defendant knew or should have known about these purported unexplained gaps prior to Plaintiff’s deposition. The Court does not find persuasive that one of Defendant’s counsel was on family leave from May 1, 2023, to July 24, 2023, and upon return, discovered these purported unexplained gaps. (Reply Baghdishyan Decl., ¶¶ 2, 3.) Regarding Plaintiff’s tax returns, Plaintiff has produced all responsive documents that are in the possession, custody, or control of Plaintiff. (Motion Baghdishyan Decl., ¶¶ 2,3, Exs. A, B.)

The court declines to award sanctions to either party.
