DEPARTMENT SEC LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

DEPARTMENT SE-C LAW & MOTION PROCEDURES ARE AS FOLLOWS: APPEARANCES: The Court will hear oral arguments on all matters at the scheduled time of hearing. If all counsel intend to submit on the Tentative Order and do not want oral argument, please advise the clerk, in Department “C”, by calling (562-345-3702). If all sides submit on the Tentative Order and the clerk is so advised, the Tentative Order will become the final order of the court and the prevailing party shall give written Notice of Ruling per CRC 3.1312. If the Moving and Responding parties do not agree to submit on the Tentative Order, the motion will be called as calendared for hearing. There is no need to contact Department “C”, as the matter will remain on calendar for hearing. If the Moving party does not call Department “C” to submit on the Tentative Order and there is no appearance by any party, then the motion(s), at the Court’s discretion, may be taken off calendar without ruling on the motion(s). ORDERS: The minute order reflecting the Court’s Order will constitute the final Order. No additional orders should be submitted to the Court for signature unless required by law or by the Court. Prevailing party shall give written Notice of Ruling per CRC 3.1312. Minute orders, which constitute the final Order of the Court, will only be sent to the parties via U.S. mail  for the following: OSC re: sanctions, OSC re: contempt or matters taken under submission after oral arguments or briefing. Counsel or parties may request copies of all other minute orders/final orders either at the clerk’s office or in writing. If a request is in writing, a self-addressed stamped envelope and the appropriate fee for copies shall be submitted.

Case Number: 22NWCV01452    Hearing Date: December 6, 2024    Dept: SEC

PALLIN v. PRECISION CONSTRUCTION GROUP, INC.

CASE NO.: 22NWCV01452

HEARING: 12/06/24


Plaintiff JONATHAN JACOB PALLIN’s Unopposed Motion for an Order to Show Cause Re: Contempt is DENIED without prejudice.

Court Clerk to give notice.

No Opposition filed as of December 2, 2024. Due by November 20, 2024. (CCP §1005(b).)

Plaintiff JONATHAN JACOB PALLIN (“Plaintiff”) moves the Court for an Order to Show Cause re: Contempt against Defendant JERRY SORRENTINO due to his “willful and continued defiance of the Court’s August 29, 2024 Order Compelling Defendant’s Responses to Demand for Production of Documents (“Set One”) by September 13, 2024, and Defendant and his counsel’s failure to jointly pay monetary sanctions in the amount of $633.75 by September 28, 2024…. [¶] Plaintiff is also seeking further award of attorneys’ fees and costs as a result of being required to bring motion.” (Notice. 2:6-14)

The Motion is unopposed.

“Disobedience of any lawful judgment, order, or process of the court” is an act of contempt of the authority of the Court. (CCP §1209(a)(5).) “When the contempt is not committed in the immediate view and presence of the court, or of the judge at chambers, an affidavit shall be presented to the court or judge of the facts constituting the contempt, or a statement of the acts by the referees or arbitrators, or other judicial officers.” (CCP §1222(a).)

The factual predicates to a finding of contempt are: (1) the rendition of a valid order; (2) actual knowledge of the order; (3) ability to comply; and (4) willful disobedience. (Conn. v. Sup. Ct. (1987) 196 Cal.App.3d 774, 784.)

The punishment for contempt is up to five days’ imprisonment and/or a fine of up to $1,000 for each contempt. (See CCP §1218(a).) In addition, a person who is subject to a court order as party to the action who is adjudged guilty of contempt for violating that court order may be ordered to pay the party initiating the contempt proceeding the reasonable attorney’s fees and costs incurred by that party in connection with the contempt proceeding. (Id.) Civil contempt proceedings are quasi-criminal in nature because of the penalties which may be imposed. (In re Kreitman (1995) 40 Cal.App.4th 750, 754.) Thus, “guilt must be

established beyond a reasonable doubt.” (Ross v. Superior Court of Sacramento County (1977) 19 Cal.3d 899, 913.)

The respondent must be formally notified of the contempt charge and of the time and place of the hearing; otherwise, the Court lacks jurisdiction to proceed. For this purpose, both the OSC and affidavit ordinarily must be served on respondent in a manner authorized for service of summons. (Cedars-Sinai Imaging Med. Group v. Sup. Ct. (2000) 83 Cal.App.4th 1281, 1286.) Personal service of the order to show cause on the party charged is a jurisdictional requirement for a contempt order. (See In re Meyer (1933) 131 Cal.App.41 43.)

Here, no Proof of Service has been separately filed, or attached in conjunction with the Moving Papers. Therefore, at this time, the Court lacks jurisdiction over Defendant Jerry Sorrentino for purposes of issuing an OSC Re: Contempt. The Motion is DENIED without prejudice. The Moving Party is not foreclosed from re-filing and properly serving this application, and then seeking ex parte relief to have the matter advanced to a sooner date, if necessary. The Court notes that no Motion for Sanctions (e.g., terminating, issue, or evidentiary) has been filed or scheduled with the Court.

Case Number: 23NWCV00237    Hearing Date: December 6, 2024    Dept: SEC

GIRON v. AMERICAN HONDA MOTOR CO., INC.

CASE NO.: 23NWCV00237

HEARING: 12/06/24


Plaintiffs MELISSA GIRON and RUDY GIRON’s Unopposed Motion for Summary Adjudication is GRANTED.

Moving Party to give notice.

No Opposition filed as of December 2, 2024. Due by November 21, 2024. (CCP §437c(b)(2).)

This “lemon law” action was filed by Plaintiffs MELISSA GIRON and RUDY GIRON (“Plaintiffs”) on January 24, 2023. On April 7, 2023, the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) was filed. Plaintiffs allege that AMERICAN HONDA MOTOR CO., INC. (“Defendant” or “Honda”) did not disclose and actively concealed defects involving Honda’s Transmission Defect affecting Plaintiffs’ 2017 Honda Pilot vehicle. (See e.g., FAC ¶¶20, 43.) Plaintiffs allege that Defendant Honda manufactured the Subject Vehicle. (FAC ¶13.)

Plaintiff’s FAC asserts the following causes of action: (1) Violation of Song-Beverly Act (Breach of Express Warranty); and (2) Fraudulent Inducement – Concealment.

On October 31, 2023, Defendant Honda filed its Answer to Plaintiffs’ FAC, Defendant’s Answer asserts a general denial and 28 affirmative defenses to the FAC. Honda alleges “that it possesses a qualified third-party dispute resolution process, thereby barring Plaintiffs from any civil penalty in this lawsuit.” (Answer to FAC ¶8.)

Plaintiff now moves for summary adjudication of the following issues:

(1) Plaintiffs are entitled to summary adjudication as to Defendant’s Fifth Affirmative Defense (Third Party Resolution Process), because Defendant does not have a qualified third-party dispute resolution process;

(2) Plaintiffs are entitled to summary adjudication as to Defendant’s Eighth Affirmative Defense (No Civil Penalty), because Defendant does not have a qualified third-party resolution process; and

(3) Plaintiffs are entitled to summary adjudication as to Defendant’s Eighth Affirmative Defense because willful and civil penalties are not barred by the existence of a qualified third-party dispute resolution process. Plaintiff argues that summary adjudication of these issues is proper because

“[t]here is no evidence that Honda maintains a qualified third-party dispute resolution process[.]” (Motion 2:24.)

“If the buyer establishes that the failure to comply was willful, the judgment may include, in addition to the amounts recovered under subdivision (a), a civil penalty which shall not exceed two times the amount of actual damages.” (Cal. Civ. Code §1794(c).) Under Cal. Civ. Code §1794(e), “if the buyer establishes a violation of paragraph (2) or subdivision (d) of Section 1793.2, the buyer shall recover damages and reasonable attorney’s fees and costs, and may recover a civil penalty of up to two times the amount of damages. [¶] If the manufacturer maintains a qualified third-party dispute resolution process which substantially complies with Section 1793.22, the manufacturer shall not be liable for any civil penalty pursuant to this subdivision.” (Cal. Civ. Code §1794(e)(2).)

In support of their Motion, Plaintiffs proffer the following evidence:

(1) Honda conceded in discovery that it does not have a certified third-party dispute resolution process. (UMF No. 3, Swanson Decl., ¶¶6-7.);

(2) The Department of Consumer Affairs (“DCA”) maintains a publicly available list of automakers for which it has certified their “qualified third-party dispute resolution process,” and Honda is not among them. (UMF No. 4.)

(3) The Program Chief for the DCA Arbitration Certification Program has attested in a signed affidavit that Honda has not had a certified dispute resolution program since 2013, that Honda’s National Center for Dispute Settlement Program is not certified by the DCA, that a qualified program must be certified by the DCA, and that Honda’s non-certified program has not been subject to any regulatory review or oversight by the DCA. (UMF Nos. 5-10.)

As indicated above, the Motion is unopposed. Based on the foregoing, the Court finds that there is no existence of disputed issues of material fact with regards to Plaintiffs’ ability to obtain civil penalties because Defendant fails to establish that it maintains a qualified third-party resolution process. Moreover, as argued in the Moving Papers, even if Defendant did have a third-party dispute resolution process, that would not be a shield to civil penalties under Cal. Civ. Code §1794(c) (which depends on a finding of willfulness).

The unopposed Motion for Summary Adjudication as to Honda’s Fifth and Eighth Affirmative Defenses is GRANTED.

FELLHOELTER v. CUNNINGHAM

CASE NO.: 23NWCV02387

HEARING: 12/06/24


I. Tina H. Abdolhosseini, Esq. and BD&J, PC’s unopposed motion to withdraw as counsel of record for Plaintiff DAVID FELLHOELTER is GRANTED.

II. Tina H. Abdolhosseini, Esq. and BD&J, PC’s unopposed motion to withdraw as counsel of record for Plaintiff MARICELA FELLHOELTER is GRANTED.

Moving Party to give notice.

No Oppositions filed as of December 3, 2024.

The Court finds that Counsel has met the procedural requirements, including proper notice to the client at the last known residence or business address of the client. C.R.C. 3.1362.

Here, Counsel’s declarations indicate that there has been a breakdown of the attorney-client relationship. Where the issue (or conflict) is between the lawyer and client(s), without implicating other parties, the court can rely on counsel’s sworn declaration absent any reason to doubt the representations.

There is an undisputed breakdown in attorney-client communications. It does not appear that Attorney Abdolhosseini continued representation would serve the interests of justice. The motions to withdraw are GRANTED.
