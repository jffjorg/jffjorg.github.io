DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22VECV01643    Hearing Date: June 17, 2024    Dept: W

FARZANEH TABIBIAn v. PROVIDENCE CEDARS-SINAI TARZANA MEDICAL CENTER, et al.

 

demurrer to the first amended complaint

 

Date of Hearing:        June 17, 2024                                    Trial Date:       None set.

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        22VECV01643

 

Moving Party:            Defendant McCarthy Building Companies, Inc.  

Responding Party:     Plaintiff Farzaneh Tabibian  

Meet and Confer:      Yes (Grossi Decl. ¶¶2-3.)

 

BACKGROUND

 

On October 18, 2022, Plaintiff Farzaneh Tabibian filed a complaint against Defendant Providence Cedars-Sinai Tarzana Medical Center and Tarzana Medical Center for negligence-premises liability. Plaintiff alleges on August 2, 2023, Plaintiff was at the Providence Cedars-Sinai Tarzana walking on a pathway that was under construction when she slipped and violently fell due the unmaintained, hazardous condition of the floor, including loose gravel. As a result of the incident, Plaintiff alleges she experienced immediate excruciating pain and discomfort. This violent impact caused an acute fracture on the fifth metatarsal. Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint on April 3, 2024, adding a cause of action for negligent hiring.

 

On June 29, 2023, Plaintiff named McCarthy Building Companies, Inc. as Doe 1. On July 21, 2023, Plaintiff dismissed the Providence Cedars-Sinai Tarzana Medical Center and Tarzana Medical Center defendants.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant’s Demurrer to the First Amended Complaint is OVERRULED.

 

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE

 

Defendant McCarthy Building Companies, Inc. requests this court take judicial notice of Plaintiff’s First Amended Complaint served on April 3, 2024 (RJN, Exh. A.) The court GRANTS Defendant’s request for judicial notice.  

 

DISCUSSION

 

Defendant McCarthy Building Companies, Inc. demurs to the to the complaint of Plaintiff Farzaneh Tabibian on the grounds the first cause of action for negligence-premises liability and second cause of action for negligent hiring fails to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action against Defendant and is uncertain, ambiguous and unintelligible.

 

A demurrer for sufficiency tests whether the complaint states a cause of action.¿ (Hahn v. Mirda (2007) 147 Cal.App.4th 740, 747.)¿When considering demurrers, courts read the allegations liberally and in context.¿ (Wilson v. Transit Authority of City of Sacramento (1962) 199 Cal.App.2d 716, 720-21.)¿In a demurrer proceeding, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or via proper judicial notice.¿(Donabedian v. Mercury Ins. Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994.)¿“A demurrer tests the pleading alone, and not on the evidence or facts alleged.” (E-Fab, Inc. v. Accountants, Inc. Servs. (2007) 153 Cal.App.4th 1308, 1315.) As such, the court assumes the truth of the complaint’s properly pleaded or implied factual allegations. (Id.) The only issue a demurrer is concerned with is whether the complaint, as it stands, states a cause of action. (Hahn, supra, 147 Cal.App.4th at 747.) Before filing a demurrer, demurring party is also required to meet and confer with the party who filed the pleading demurred to for the purposes of determining whether an agreement can be reached through a filing of an amended pleading that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer. (See CCP § 430.41.) 

 

Premises Liability

 

Defendant demurs to the first cause of action for premises liability on the grounds the complaint does not sufficiently allege “duty”, “breach of duty” or causation.

 

To establish premises liability, plaintiff must prove that the defendant breached a duty of care owed to the plaintiff that proximately caused injury and damages. “Those who own, possess, or control property generally have a duty to exercise ordinary care in managing the property in order to avoid exposing others to an unreasonable risk of harm.” (Annocki v. Peterson Enterprises, LLC (2014) 232 Cal.App.4th 32, 37.)  

 

Defendant argues Plaintiff alleges McCarthy was contracted to perform construction on the subject premises but fails to allege any other facts supporting the contention that McCarthy controlled the premises. Moreover, Plaintiff alleges no facts as to the specific location where the subject incident occurred and therefore, it is unclear who, if anyone, was working in that area, who owned it and who controlled it. As for causation, Defendant argues Plaintiff fails to allege facts that McCarthy’s conduct caused Plaintiff’s condition. Merely alleging McCarthy was performing construction at the subject does not sufficiently allege McCarthy’s conduct was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff’s injuries as there were other contractors onsite.

 

The court finds the allegations sufficient for a demurrer. The complaint alleges Defendant possessed, constructed, maintained, operated, inspected, performed, and planned construction work at the property and Defendant failed to coordinate and control the work being performed on said job site in a safe and proper manner, thereby creating a dangerous condition. This is sufficient to show duty and breach of duty. Moreover, Plaintiff’s allegations that Defendant’s negligence caused Plaintiff to fall and injure themselves is sufficient. Any ambiguities can be clarified under the discovery process.

 

Accordingly, Defendant’s demurrer to the first cause of action is OVERRULED.  

 

Negligent Hiring

 

Defendant demurs to the second cause of action for negligent hiring on the grounds Plaintiff fails to allege facts sufficient to constitute a claim for negligent hiring.

 

Under California law, an employer may be liable to a third person for negligently training, hiring, supervising, or retaining an unfit employee, but only when the employer knows, or has reason to know, that an employee presents a particular risk and the harm posed by that risk materializes.  (Doe v. Capital Cities (1996) 50 Cal. App. 4th 1038, 1042.) However, liability for negligent hiring and supervision “is based upon the reasoning that if an enterprise hires individuals with characteristics which might pose a danger to customers or other employees, the enterprise should bear the loss caused by the wrongdoing of its incompetent or unfit employees.” (Mendoza v. City of Los Angeles (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1333, 1339–1340 [78 Cal.Rptr.2d 525].) This tort applies “in factual settings where the plaintiff’s injury occurred in the workplace, or the contact between the plaintiff and the employee was generated by the employment relationship.” (Ibid.) 

 

Defendant argues the complaint assumes Defendant hired subcontractors without providing sufficient facts, fails to identify the subcontractors and employees at issue, fails to allege facts that the subcontractors and employees were incompetent or unfit, fails to allege Defendant knew or should have known that the subcontractors or employees were unfit and fails to allege that Defendant’s hiring was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff’s harm.

 

The court finds the complaint sufficiently facts to support a claim against Defendant for negligent hiring. The court finds it sufficient for plaintiff to allege that defendant knew that its employees or subcontractors were incompetent to perform their job and that the improper performance of the job led to an unmaintained and hazardous condition which caused injury to the plaintiff.  These allegations are sufficient. 

 

Uncertainty

 

Defendant demurs to the complaint on the grounds it is uncertain on its face. Specifically, that the allegations fail to apprise Defendant of the essential facts and allegations against it or adequately defend against all causes of action asserted.

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 430.10(f) provides that a pleading is uncertain if it is ambiguous and unintelligible. (See CCP § 430.10(f).) “A demurrer for uncertainty will be sustained only where the complaint is so bad that defendant cannot reasonably respond—i.e., he or she cannot reasonably determine what issues must be admitted or denied, or what counts or claims are directed against him or her.” (Weil & Brown, Civil Procedure Before Trial (The Rutter Group) § 7:85 (emphasis in original).)

 

Although the court finds the second cause of action fails to allege facts sufficient to state a claim against Defendant, the overall complaint is not so uncertain. Defendant could reasonably determine what issues must be admitted or denied and what claims are directed at them. Accordingly, the demurrer is OVERRULED on this basis.

 

 


Case Number: 23VECV01917    Hearing Date: June 17, 2024    Dept: W

HARGES FAMILY PARTNERSHIP, LP, et al. v. CHARTWELL ESCROW, INC.

 

defendant chartwell escrow, inc.’s motion to dismiss complaint for plaintiffs’ failure to obey court order re filing amended complaint after demurrer sustained with leave to amend

 

Date of Hearing:        June 17, 2024                                    Trial Date:       None Set  

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        23VECV01917

 

Moving Party:            Defendant Chartwell Escrow, Inc.

Responding Party:     No opposition

 

BACKGROUND

 

This is a breach of fiduciary duty action. Plaintiffs Harges Family Partnership, LP and Lorelei Harges, Trustee of the Harges Community Property Trust allege Plaintiffs entered into multiple agreements to sell seven pieces of real property to several buyers. Chartwell agreed to act as the escrow company for the transactions. Plaintiffs allege per Section C of the Addendum, the escrow holder, Chartwell, was supposed to release the deposits to the plaintiffs one business day after their receipt. However, Plaintiffs allege Chartwell never released any of the deposits to the plaintiffs and instead, Chartwell returned the deposits to the buyers.  

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant Chartwells Escrow, Inc.’s Motion to Dismiss Complaint for Plaintiffs’ Failure to Obey Court Order re Filing Amended Complaint After Demurrer Sustained With Leave to Amend is GRANTED

 

DISCUSSION

 

Defendant Chartwell Escrow, Inc. moves for an order of dismissal of the complaint of Plaintiffs’ Harges Family Partnership, LP, and Loreli Harges, Trustee of the Hargest Community Property Trust pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 581(f)(2) and California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1320(h).

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 581 provides that a court may dismiss the complaint as to that defendant when, after a demurrer is sustained with leave to amend, the plaintiff fails to amend the complaint within the time allowed by the court and either party moves for dismissal. (CCP § 581(f).) While dismissal under Code of Civil Procedure section 581(f) is discretionary, any such dismissal must be with prejudice. (Cano v. Glover (2006) 143 Cal.App.4th 326, 330.) “No cases have held that a dismissal pursuant to section 581, subdivision (f)(2) may be without prejudice. The right to dismiss without prejudice is expressly permitted by other subdivisions of section 581 but section 581, subdivision (f)(2) does not so provide.”¿(Id. at 229.)

 

Pursuant to California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1320, following a ruling on a demurrer, unless otherwise ordered, leave to answer or amend within 10 days is deemed granted.

 

Defendant Chartwell’s demurrer to all causes of action in the complaint was sustained on March 18, 2024, with 45 days leave to amend. Accordingly, Plaintiff had until May 2, 2024 to file their amended complaint. As of the date of this hearing, Plaintiff has not served or filed an amended pleading nor did they seek an extension before Defendant filed the present motion.

 

Such failure can be construed as a presumption that Plaintiff has no meritorious argument(s). (See Southern California Gas Co. v. Flannery (2016), 5 Cal. App. 5th 476, 483, fn.7 (lack of opposition waives arguments); Sexton v. Superior Court (1997), 58 Cal. App. 4th 1403, 1410.) Therefore, the Court GRANTS Defendant Chartwell’s motion and Plaintiff’s complaint against Defendant is DISMISSED WITH PREJUDICE.
