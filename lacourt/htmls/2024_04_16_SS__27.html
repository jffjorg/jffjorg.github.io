Hearing Date:           4/16/2024 at 1:30 p.m.

Case No./Name.:      20STCV44666 SARKIS AVAGYAN vs THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

Motion Name:           MOTION TO AMEND WITNESS LIST

Moving Party:           Plaintiff

Responding Party:    Defendant Jose Gabriel Martinez

Notice:                     Sufficient

 

Ruling:                               MOTION TO AMEND DENIED AS MOOT

 

On November 20, 2020, Plaintiff filed the present lawsuit, alleging he was a passenger in a Lyft vehicle involved in a multi-vehicle accident. The jury trial was recently continued to July 29, 2024. On February 21, 2024, Plaintiff and Defendant exchanged expert witness designations. Plaintiff designated Don Stevens as his intended accident reconstruction expert. On March 8, 2024, Plaintiff's counsel raised the need to augment Plaintiff’s expert designation due to Mr. Stevens’ unavailability.  On April 2, 2024, as reflected in the Notice of Ruling prepared by Defendant, the Defendant agreed “to allow Plaintiff to substitute a different accident and biomechanical expert in place of Plaintiff’s previously designated expert.” 

Accordingly, it appears to the Court that Defendant already agreed to allow a substitute expert for Don Stevens.  To the extent the parties need further communication with the Court regarding who that alternate expert will be, what he can opine on, and when a deposition of that expert will be taken, the Court will hear from the parties.

Case Number: 21STCV14872    Hearing Date: April 16, 2024    Dept: 27

Hon. Lee S. Arian

Department 27

Tentative Ruling

 

Hearing Date:           4/16/2024 at 1:30 p.m.

Case No./Name.:      21STCV14872 DESIDERIO SANDOVAL, et al. vs MARIA GUERRERO

Motion Name:           MOTION TO COMPEL COMPLIANCE AND FOR SANCTIONS

Moving Party:           Plaintiff

Responding Party:    Defendant Maria Guerrero

Notice:                     Sufficient

 

Ruling:                               MOTION TO COMPEL COMPLIANCE IS GRANTED IN PART AND DENIED IN PART

                               
REQUESTS FOR SANCTIONS ARE DENIED

 

Background

On April 20, 2021, Plaintiffs initiated this motor accident lawsuit. On November 3, 2021, they served their first set of Requests for Production on Defendant. On March 8, 2023, Plaintiffs filed a motion to compel initial responses to these requests. On September 15, 2023, Defendant provided responses to the discovery at issue without any objections, rendering Plaintiffs' motion to compel moot. On December 19, 2023, Defendant sought relief from waiver of objections, but the Court denied the motion. Defendant’s document production contained several redactions. For example, a significant portion of the ISO report was redacted. Plaintiffs now move the Court to compel Defendant to provide the unredacted document production and all related documents pertinent to the claim.

Legal Standard 

 

CCP section 2031.320 states in relevant part:  

 

If a party filing a response to a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling under Sections 2031.210, 2031.220, 2031.230, 2031.240 and 2031.280 thereafter fails to permit the inspection, copying, testing, or sampling in accordance with that party’s statement of compliance, the demanding party may move for an order compelling compliance. 

 

(CCP § 2031.320(a).)  

 

Analysis

 

As a preliminary matter, a motion for compliance under CCP 2031.320 does not require the parties to meet and confer or attend an informal discovery conference.

 

Defendant, in her responses to Plaintiff's Request for Production, Set One, served on September 15, 2023, stated that she would provide specific documents listed as Exhibits A through H in her document production. This includes Plaintiff's ISO reports and the declaration page of the applicable auto insurance policy.  Defendant raised no objections in her response to the Request for Production. Moreover, the Court has already denied Defendant's motion for relief from the waiver of objections. Consequently, Defendant is precluded from redacting any parts of the responses, regardless of any objections, including those based on privacy or privilege. Upon reviewing Defendant's document production, the Court found extensive redactions in the ISO report and other documents. Defendant is barred from making such redactions, having already waived all objections. Therefore, Defendant is ORDERED to produce the unredacted version of Defendant’s document production.

 

However, a motion for compliance under CCP 2031.320 only compels a party to comply with its stated commitments of document production. Thus, Plaintiff’s requests for all of Defendant’s documents related to the claim, including attorney files, billing records, insurance correspondence, status reports, and internal communications, can only be compelled if Defendant agreed to produce them in her responses. Upon examining Defendant's responses, the Court finds that Defendant was very specific about the documents she would produce, and it did not include attorney files, billing records, insurance correspondence, status reports, or internal communications. Therefore, Plaintiff’s request for the production of all of Defendant’s documents relating to the claim is DENIED.

 

The court finds that both Parties acted with substantial justification and therefore sanctions are DENIED.

Legal Standard

 

Continuances of trials are disfavored, but each request for a continuance must be considered on its own merits.  (CRC Rule 3.1332(c).)  The Court may grant a continuance only on an affirmative showing of good cause requiring the continuance.  (CRC Rule 3.1332(c).) The Court may look to the following factors in determining whether a trial continuance is warranted: (1) proximity of the trial date; (2) whether there was any previous continuance of trial due to any party; (3) the length of the continuance requested; (4) the availability of alternative means to address the problem that gave rise to the motion; (5) the prejudice that parties or witnesses will suffer as a result of the continuance; and (6) whether trial counsel is engaged in another trial.  (See generally CRC Rule 3.1332(d)(1)-(11).)  Additional factors for the Court to consider include: a party’s excused inability to obtain essential testimony, documents, or other material evidence despite diligent efforts; whether all parties have stipulated to a continuance; and any other fact or circumstance relevant to the fair determination of the motion or application.  (CRC Rule 3.1332(c), (d).)  

 

Factual Background and Analysis

On May 16, 2022, Plaintiff filed the present auto accident lawsuit alleging that she was injured by a vehicle operated by Defendant Yung Ho Kim, while he was purportedly in the course and scope of his employment with Defendant Uni Trans, LLC. On July 11, 2022, Defendant Yung Ho Kim filed his answer. Defendant Uni Trans, LLC, was only recently served and filed its answer on March 18, 2024. The current trial date is set for May 14, 2024. Defendant Uni Trans, LLC now moves the Court to continue the trial date for 10 months.

 

Given that Defendant Uni Trans, LLC's answer was only recently filed, its request for additional time to conduct discovery is reasonable. All parties have stipulated to the extension, with no opposition filed citing prejudice. Considering the age of the case, the absence of multiple prior continuances, the parties' stipulation, and the need for further discovery, the Court grants Defendant's motion to continue trial.

The new Trial Date is set for March __, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. The Final Status Conference is continued to March __, 2025, at 10:00 a.m. All other case-related deadlines will follow the new trial date.

Ruling:                     MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES TO REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION AND FOR SANCTIONS IS CONTINUED.

 

On August 29, 2022, Plaintiff Hector Hipolito filed the present case, 22STCV28139, alleging injuries from a work-related concrete pump accident against four defendants, including Defendant Catalina Pacific Concrete Company. On September 12, 2022, Everest National Insurance Company, the worker compensation insurer for Hipolito’s employer, filed a subrogation claim (Case No. 22STCV29697) against Defendants M2 Concrete Pumping and Catalina Pacific. On August 21, 2023, the Court issued a minute order declaring the two cases related, designating Hipolito’s case (22STCV28139) as the lead case but emphasizing that the cases are still considered independent of each other. Subsequently, Defendant Catalina in the lead case filed a motion to compel Everest to produce further responses to its Response to Defendant’s Request for Production No. 72. The Court's minute order dated August 21, 2023, clarifies that while the two cases are related, they remain independent of each other. Requests for the production of documents under CCP 2031.010 are applicable only to discovery between parties in the lawsuit. Consequently, a motion to compel further responses can only be directed at a party to the dispute. Since Everest is not a party in the lead case, a motion to compel further against Everest is therefore inappropriate.

However, the Court notes that Everest filed a motion for leave to intervene in the lead case on April 5, 2024. The court will continue the current motion to April 30, 2024, at 1:30 p.m. Everest may file a renewed opposition challenging the substance of the present motion if it chooses and Defendant may likewise file a reply, both on dates pursuant to Code.

¿ 

Ruling:                              MOTION TO COMPEL FORM INTERROGATORIES, SET ONE, AND FOR SANCTIONS IS GRANTED.

 

Background

On August 30, 2023, Plaintiff filed the present auto collision case. On October 10, 2023, Plaintiff served Defendant Boris Navarro with his Form Interrogatories (FROG), Set One. On December 12, 2023, Plaintiff sent Defendant's counsel, Hanger, Steinberg, Shapiro & Ash, courtesy copies of the discovery requests. From December 2023 to February 2024, the parties were in communication, and Plaintiff granted Defendant several extensions to provide the discovery responses, even allowing Defendant to preserve his objections. However, Defendant failed to provide the discovery response by the final deadline set for February 19, 2024, and Plaintiff now moves the court to compel Defendant to provide discovery responses to Plaintiff’s FROG, Set One.

 

Legal Standard

 

A plaintiff may make a demand for production of documents and propound interrogatories without leave of court at any time 10 days after the service of the summons on, or appearance by, the party to whom the demand is directed, whichever occurs first. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.020, subd. (b); Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.020, subd. (b).) The demand for production of documents is not limited by number, but the request must comply with the formatting requirements in Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.030. A party may propound 35 specially prepared interrogatories that are relevant to the subject matter of the pending action and any additional number of official form interrogatories that are relevant to the subject matter of the pending action. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.030, subd. (a)(1) - (a)(2).)

 

The party whom the request is propounded upon is required to respond within 30 days after service of a demand, but the parties are allowed to informally agree to an extension and confirm any such agreement in writing. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.260, subd. (a); Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.260, subd. (a); Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.270, subd. (a) - (b); Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.270, subd. (a) - (b).)

 

If a party fails to timely respond to a request for production or interrogatories, the party to whom the request is directed waives any right to exercise the option to produce writings under Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.230, and waives any objection, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300, subd. (a); Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (a).)¿¿The party propounding the discovery requests may move for an order compelling responses. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300, subd. (b); Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (b).) Unlike a motion to compel further discovery responses, a motion to compel initial discovery responses does not have any meet and confer requirements.¿¿¿¿ 

¿

Code of Civil Procedure section 2023.030, subdivision (a) provides, in pertinent part, that the court may impose a monetary sanction on a party engaging in the misuse of the discovery process to pay the reasonable expenses, including attorney’s fees, incurred by anyone as a result of that conduct. A misuse of the discovery process includes failing to respond or submit to an authorized method of discovery. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2023.010, subd. (d).)   A court has discretion to fix the amount of reasonable monetary sanctions. (Cornerstone Realty Advisors, LLC v. Summit Healthcare Reit, Inc. (2020) 56 Cal.App.5th 771, 791.)  

 

Analysis

 

Motion to Compel

 

It is undisputed that Defendant failed to serve an initial response, even one consisting solely of objections, within the statutory timeline. Defendant filed an opposition but did not show that a response was served prior to the hearing date. Therefore, the present motion is thereby GRANTED.

 

Sanctions

 

Sanctions are mandatory against the party, the attorney, or both whose failure to serve timely discovery responses necessitated Plaintiff to file the current motion. (CCP § 2030.290(c).) Defendant's counsel contends that they have not resolved a representation issue with Defendant's carrier and intend to move to be relieved as counsel. However, this does not excuse Defendant’s failure to provide responses for over six months, during most of which time Plaintiff has also been in communication with defense counsel. Defendant has requested sanctions in the amount of $1,250.00, which the Court finds reasonable. Given that Plaintiff’s counsel and Defendant’s counsel have been in communication since December 12, 2023, with Plaintiff agreeing to several extensions and even consenting to preserve Defendant’s objections, Plaintiff has been more than accommodating to Defendant. Thus, under the circumstances, the Court finds that Defendant did not act with substantial justification and GRANTS Plaintiff's request for sanctions. Defendant and his counsel are ORDERED, jointly and severally, to pay sanctions of $1,250.00 to Plaintiff within 20 days of today’s date.
