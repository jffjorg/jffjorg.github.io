DEPARTMENT 17 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22STCV35638    Hearing Date: January 11, 2024    Dept: 17

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

 

DEPARTMENT 17

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

IRIS AU

 

 

         vs.

 

ENZO ZELOCCHI, et al.

 

 Case No.:  22STCV35638  

 

 

 

 Hearing Date:  January 11, 2024

 

Defendant’s motion for a judgment on the pleadings is DENIED.

 

            On 11/9/2022, Plaintiff Iris Au (Plaintiff) filed suit against Enzo Zelocchi, Kenneth Childs (Childs), and David Do, alleging: (1) intentional infliction of emotional distress; (2) attempted extortion; (3) aiding and abetting attempted extortion; (4) false light; and (5) defamation per se. 

 

            Now, Defendant Childs (Defendant) moves for a judgment on the pleadings as to the three causes of action asserted against him.

 

Discussion 

 

            Defendant argues that Plaintiff cannot state a claim for the three causes of action asserted against him—IIED, attempted extortion, and aiding and abetting extortion—because Plaintiff has not alleged sufficient facts to support her allegations based on information and belief and because she has not alleged outrageous conduct.

 

            After review, the Court disagrees.

 

            Here, Plaintiff alleges that Defendants sent Plaintiff threatening messages under the pseudonym "$," in effort to extort money from her, and defamed her personal and professional reputation by publishing online false allegations asserting that Plaintiff is exploiting innocent people to finance an extravagant lifestyle. For example, Plaintiff alleges that Defendants sent Plaintiff a photo of her children, a link to a wallet, a demand for a transfer of $5,000,000, and a message stating, “Pay up or Death will rain from Above.” (Complaint ¶ 45.)

 

            Defendant here argues that Plaintiff only alleges that he participated in the publishing of the Ripoff Report, where an anonymous comment was left by someone using the alias “Lolo” stating: "Iris Au Iris Au Iris Rabaya Eyerize Adam IZA Ahmed Assad Faiq ZORT.COM ZORT INC VURG.COM DREAM AGENCY INC Stole my life savings and spent it on Birkin Bags, Jewlery [sic], and are STILL Funding their extravagant lifestyle all from scamming innocent people! Newport Beach California.” (Complaint ¶ 39.)

 

            Defendant argues that Plaintiff’s allegation that this comment was posted by him is based inadequately on information and belief, that the allegations cannot rise to the level of extreme, and that this allegation cannot support a claim for extortion or aiding and abetting extortion.

 

            However, first, “[p]laintiff may allege on information and belief any matters that are not within his personal knowledge, if he has information leading him to believe that the allegations are true.” (Pridonoff v. Balokovich (1951) 36 Cal.2d 788, 792.) Here, Plaintiff alleges that her boyfriend Iza and Zelocchi had a falling out over business, and that Zelocchi became enraged at Iza and engaged in a campaign to target him and his family. (Complaint ¶ 19.) Plaintiff alleges that Childs owns a company Paramount Investigative Services which Iza consulted with to obtain investigative services. Plaintiff alleges that Childs “double-crossed” Iza, and eventually began working in connection with Zelocchi. (Complaint ¶¶ 19-35.) At the pleadings stage, this is sufficient information to show why Plaintiff believes Childs to be the anonymous commentor, as well as a participant in the alleged extortion.

 

            Second, while Defendant argues that he is only alleged to have participated in the Ripoff Report, Plaintiff’s Complaint clearly alleges otherwise. Plaintiff alleges that Zelocchi and Childs were working in conjunction to extort money from Plaintiff through anonymous texts sent under “$” and by posting anonymous comments alleging Plaintiff was a scammer:

 

-         “Nearly immediately after filing a lawsuit vs Plaintiff, CHILDS provided substantial assistance and encouragement to ZELOCCHI’S extortion attempts by contacting and working with ZELOCCHI in the hopes of extorting millions from Plaintiff and IZA via threats from the anonymous group “$”.” [Complaint at ¶ 63].

 

-         “This fear was induced via threatening messages sent by Defendants using the name “$”, which threatened to ruin Plaintiff’s life and sent pictures of Plaintiff’s young children demanding $ 5 million dollars or else “Death will rain from above”.” [Complaint at ¶ 54]. • CHILDS filed a lawsuit against Plaintiff seeking $5,000,000 in damages. [Complaint at ¶ 32]. Only five days later, ZELOCCHI admitted that CHILDS and ZELOCCHI were conspiring with one another. [Complaint at ¶ 33]. Nearly immediately after Plaintiff was served with CHILDS Complaint, Plaintiff and IZA began receiving extortion demands from anonymous group identified as “$.” [Complaint at ¶ 34]. The timing of one such demand was made suspiciously within days of Plaintiff being served with CHILD’S lawsuit, and requested $5,000,000, which is the exact amount that is stated in CHILD’S Complaint as damages. [Complaint at ¶ 35].

 

In sum, Plaintiff has alleged facts which could show that Defendant here participated and aided in a scheme to extort Plaintiff, and posted anonymous defamatory comments on public websites. As set forth above, Plaintiff has set forth sufficient information to support her basis for believing Defendant here to be associated with the anonymous online comments and the anonymous “$” texter. Whether or not this conduct, in fact, rises to the level of extreme and outrageous conduct is a factual determination not properly made at this stage.

 

Based on the foregoing, Defendant’s motion for a judgment on the pleadings is denied.

 

  

It is so ordered.
