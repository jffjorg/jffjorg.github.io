DEPARTMENT 71 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


            All parties are urged to meet and confer with all parties concerning this tentative ruling to see if they can reach an agreed-upon resolution of their matter.  If you are able to reach an agreement, please notify the courtroom staff in advance of the hearing if you wish to submit on the tentative ruling rather than argue the motion by notifying the court by e-mailing the court at: SMCDept71@LACourt.org.  Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the Case Number in the Subject line.  In the body of the email, please provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, and whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, intervenor, or non-party, etc.

            Please be advised that if you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may still appear at the hearing and argue the matter, and the court could change its tentative based upon the argument.  Unless you receive a submission from all other parties in the matter, you should assume that others might appear at the hearing to argue.  If you submit, but still intend to appear, include the words "SUBMITS, BUT WILL APPEAR" in the Subject line.    

            If you elect to argue your matter, you are urged to do so remotely, via Court-Connect. If the moving party fails to appear and/or submit to the Court’s tentative ruling, the Court will take the  matter off calendar.
                          
            Note that once the Court has issued a tentative, the Court has the inherent authority not to allow the withdrawal of a motion and to adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the court.   
 
            If you submitted a courtesy copy of your papers containing media (such as a DVD or thumb drive), unless you request the return of the media in your papers, the court will destroy it following the hearing of your matter.

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

 

DEPARTMENT 71

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

RIGHT BROTHERS MANAGEMENT, LLC, 

 

         vs.

 

JOAO SILVERSTEIN, et al.

 Case No.:  19STCV45931

 

 

 

 

 Hearing Date:  July 31, 2024

 

Defendants BMI Group, Inc.’s, Mike Agazaryan’s, David Agazaryan’s, and Ara Baljian’s motion to bifurcate trial is denied.

The Court sets trial on October 7, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. and a Final Status Conference on September 26, 2024, at 9:30 a.m., both in Dept. 71. 

 

Defendants BMI Group, Inc. (“BMI”), Mike Agazaryan (“Mike”), David Agazaryan (“David”), and Ara Baljian (“Baljian”) (collectively, “Moving Defendants”) move for this Court to bifurcate the trial in this matter and hold a bench trial on the issue of contract interpretation before any jury is empaneled.  (Notice of Motion, pg. 2.)  Moving Defendants move on the grounds this Court has the power and authority to hold a bench trial as to the interpretation of the Binding Letter of Intent Re: Joint Venture for the operation of licensed commercial cannabis businesses in Lompoc, California (the “LOI”) so that the jury can be instructed as to the Court’s interpretation at the commencement of the trial.  (Notice of Motion, pg. 2.)  Moving Defendants argue bifurcating the trial in this matter will save substantial resources of the Court and the parties, and will promote convenience to the prospective jurors since virtually all of the issues involved in this action revolve around the terms of the LOI.  (Notice of Motion, pg. 2.)

 

Request for Judicial Notice

Moving Defendants’ 7/24/24 request for judicial notice of the Declaration of Dennis Bozanich (“Bozanich”) is denied.

 

Background

Moving Defendants filed the instant motion on March 14, 2024.  Plaintiff filed its opposition on July 17, 2024.  Moving Defendants filed their reply on July 24, 2024.

 

Motion to Bifurcate Trial

C.C.P. §598 provides, in part,

 

The court may, when the convenience of witnesses, the ends of justice, or the economy and efficiency of handling the litigation would be promoted thereby, on motion of a party, after notice and hearing, make an order, no later than the close of pretrial conference in cases in which such pretrial conference is to be held, or, in other cases, no later than 30 days before the trial date, that the trial of any issue or any part thereof shall precede the trial of any other issue or any part thereof in the case, except for special defenses which may be tried first pursuant to Sections 597 and 597.5. 

 

(C.C.P. §598.)

Additionally, Evidence Code §320 provides that “[e]xcept as otherwise provided by law, the court in its discretion shall regulate the order of proof.” “Under these provisions, trial courts have broad discretion to determine the order of proof in the interests of judicial economy.”  (Grappo v. Coventry Financial Corp. (1991) 235 Cal.App.3d 496, 504.)  Further, “[t]he statutory provisions for severance and separate trials (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 598, 1048) are not limited to the separate trial of a ‘cause of action’ but also authorize a separate trial of any ‘issue.’”  (American Motorists Insurance Co. v. Superior Court (1998) 68 Cal.App.4th 864, 872.)

When “ascertaining the intent of the parties at the time the contract was executed depends on the credibility of extrinsic evidence, that credibility determination and the interpretation of the contract are questions of fact that may properly be resolved by the jury.”  (City of Hope National Medical Center v. Genentech, Inc. (2008) 43 Cal.4th 375, 395 [holding that the issue of whether a DNA use requirement or patent infringement requirement applied to certain monies or licenses was properly decided by the jury because the decision involved conflicting extrinsic evidence].)

Here, a factual conflict exists to determine each party’s obligations under the LOI; therefore, extrinsic evidence is needed to interpret contract terms.  Specifically, the parties will need to present external evidence to interpret whether the LOI required BMI to provide a cultivation license beyond the temporary licenses, which expired the month after the parties entered into the joint venture agreement.  Defendants have argued that the LOI could not have contemplated anything beyond the temporary licenses because a renewal of their licenses were not guaranteed (i.e. the temporary label on the license on its own limited the term of the agreement) and that the both parties knew that a renewal of the licenses was uncertain.  (12/6/23 Opposition Memo to MSJ at 1:14-19, 13:15-22.)  Plaintiff will present testimony from Dennis Bozanich about how a license holder could reasonably expect to have its license renewed as long as it complied with all applicable regulations. Plaintiff will also present testimony from its principals that they had reasonable expectations based on their previous cultivation experience that the licenses would be renewed.  (Opposition, pg. 5.) 

Thus, the parties will present conflicting evidence as to the effect of the temporary permits on the meaning of the LOI, for which a jury will determine the credibility.  (Warner Construction Corp. v. City of Los Angeles(1970) 2 Cal.3d 285, 291 [holding that the trial court properly relegated the question of contract interpretation to the jury because it depended on the credibility of a witness’s interpretation of terms in the contract].)

Accordingly, Moving Defendants’ motion to bifurcate trial is denied.

 

          Conclusion

          Moving Defendants’ motion to bifurcate trial is denied.
