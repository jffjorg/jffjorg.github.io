This may be done by incorporating verbatim the court's tentative ruling. All hearings on law and motion and other calendar matters are generally NOT transcribed by a court reporter unless one is provided by the party(ies).

Case Number: 22STCV15693    Hearing Date: September 12, 2024    Dept: F47

Dept. F47

Date: 9/12/24

Case #22STCV15693

 

DEMURRER & MOTION TO STRIKE TO THE FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT

 

Demurrer & Motion to Strike filed 7/24/24.

Joinder filed 8/28/24.

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendants Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center, Inc. dba Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center (erroneously served as Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center, Inc., and as Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center); David Slavin and Daniel Hrenciuc

 

JOINING PARTY: Defendant Golden State Health Centers, Inc.

 

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff Melvin Rosales, individually and as heir and successor-in-interest to Enriqueta Raymond, deceased

 

NOTICE: ok

 

Demurrer is to the entire First Amended Complaint:

            1.  Wrongful Death (Elder Abuse – Welfare & Institutions Code 15600, et seq.)

            2.  Violations of Elder Abuse Act (Welfare & Institutions Code 15600, et seq. – Survival                 Claim)

            3.  Violations of Health & Safety Code 1430(b) (Survival Claim)

            4.  Wrongful Death (Negligence)

 

RELIEF REQUESTED IN MOTION TO STRIKE: An order striking portions of the complaint regarding attorneys’ fees and punitive damages.

 

RULING: The joinder is granted.  The demurrer is overruled.  The motion to strike is denied.  Answer(s) is/are due within 30 days.

 

SUMMARY OF FACTS & PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

This action arises out of the care and treatment provided to Enriqueta Raymond (Decedent) at Defendant Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center, Inc. dba Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center, erroneously served as Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center, Inc. and as Sylmar Health & Rehabilitation Center, (Sylmar Health/the Facility), a skilled nursing facility.  Plaintiff Melvin Rosales, individually and as heir and successor-in-interest to Enriqueta Raymond, deceased  (Plaintiff) alleges that Decedent was “admitted to the Facility for physical therapy, mental rehabilitation, and other limiting conditions.”  (FAC ¶30).  Additionally, Plaintiff alleges that  Decedent arrived at Sylmar Health “with critical needs arising from mental health irregularities and physical limitations.”  (FAC ¶33).  Defendant David Slavin (Slavin) is alleged to have worked for and served as an administrator for Sylmar Health and/or had custodial obligations with respect to Decedent.  (FAC ¶¶7-8).  Defendant Daniel Hrenciuc (Hrenciuc) is alleged to have worked for and served as the director of nursing at Sylmar Health and/or had custodial obligations with respect to Decedent.  (FAC ¶¶9-10). 

 

On 5/11/22, Plaintiff Melvin Rosales, Decedent’s son and successor-in-interest (Plaintiff), filed this action against Sylmar Health, Slavin, Hrenciuc, Golden State Health Centers, Inc. (Golden State) (collectively, Defendants) and nominal defendant Gail Swalley (Decedent’s daughter) alleging causes of action for: (1) Wrongful Death (Elder Abuse – Welfare & Institutions Code 15600, et seq.); (2) Violations of Elder Abuse Act (Welfare & Institutions Code 15600, et seq. – Survival Claim); (3) Violations of Health & Safety Code 1430(b) (Survival Claim) and (4) Wrongful Death (Negligence).  After Plaintiff’s counsel failed to respond to Defendants’ meet and confer efforts regarding issues Defendants’ had with the complaint, on 11/3/23, Sylmar Health, Slavin and Hrenciuc filed and served a demurrer to the entire complaint and motion to strike which sought to strike portions of the complaint regarding attorneys’ fees and punitive damages.  Golden State joined in the demurrer and motion to strike.  On 5/14/24, this Court granted the joinder, sustained the demurrer with 30 days leave to amend and placed the motion to strike off calendar as moot.  (See 5/14/24 Minute Order). 

 

On 6/17/24, Plaintiff filed the subject First Amended Complaint alleging the same four causes of action as alleged in the original complaint.  Again, after Plaintiff’s counsel failed to respond to Defendant’s meet and confer efforts, on 7/24/24, Sylmar Health, Slavin and Hrenciuc filed and served the instant demurrer to the entire First Amended Complaint on the grounds that the pleading fails to allege sufficient facts to state each cause of action and is uncertain.  (See Lee Decl.).  On the same date, Plaintiffs filed and served the instant motion to strike which seeks to strike portions of the First Amended Complaint regarding attorneys’ fees and punitive damages.  On 8/28/24, Golden State, represented by the same counsel as the other Defendants, filed and served a joinder in the demurrer and motion to strike.  No explanation is provided as to why the joinder was filed and served more than a month after the demurrer and motion to strike and less than 16 court days before the hearing date.  See CCP 1005(b).  On 8/29/24, Plaintiff filed and served oppositions to the demurrer and motion to strike.  On 9/6/24, Defendants filed and served untimely (by 1 court day) replies to the oppositions.  See CCP 1005(b).  Defendants provide no explanation or excuse for the untimely filing and service of the replies.  Despite the untimely service and filing, the joinder and replies were considered by the Court in ruling on the  demurrer and motion to strike.  See CRC 3.1300(d).

 

ANALYSIS

 

Defendants’ Request for Judicial Notice (RJN) is granted. 

 

Immunity

 

CCP 430.41(b) provides:

 

“A party demurring to a pleading that has been amended after a demurrer to an earlier version of the pleading was sustained shall not demur to any portion of the amended complaint, cross-complaint, or answer on grounds that could have been raised by demurrer to the earlier version of the complaint, cross-complaint, or answer.”

 

Defendants did not demur to the original complaint (which alleged the same causes of action based on the same underlying facts) on the ground they are immune from liability under the Federal Readiness and Emergency Preparedness Act (PREP Act) 42 U.S.C. 247d-6d, 247d-63 and California Government Code 8659.  (See Demurrer to original complaint filed 11/3/23, generally).  As such, Defendants may not raise the issue in this demurrer to the First Amended Complaint.  See CCP 430.41(b).

 

Even if the issue was properly raised at this time, Defendants have failed to establish that as a matter of law the PREP Act immunizes them from Plaintiff’s claims in this action. 

  

1st cause of action – Wrongful Death (Elder Abuse – Welfare & Institutions Code 15600, et seq.) & 2nd cause of action – Violations of Elder Abuse Act (Welfare & Institutions Code 15600, et seq. – Survival Claim)

 

An elder abuse claim is a statutory cause of action and, therefore, must be specifically pleaded.  See Carter (2011) 198 CA4th 396, 406-407, 410; Covenant Care, Inc. (2004) 32 C4th 771, 790.

 

To state a claim for elder abuse/neglect, under the Act, a plaintiff must allege facts establishing that the defendant: (1) had responsibility for meeting the basic needs of the elder or dependent adult, such as nutrition, hydration, hygiene or medical care; (2) knew of conditions that made the elder or dependent adult unable to provide for his or her own basic needs; (3) denied or withheld goods or services necessary to meet the elder or dependent adult’s basic needs, either with knowledge that injury was substantially certain to befall the elder or dependent adult (if the plaintiff alleges oppression, fraud or malice) or with conscious disregard of the high probability of such injury (if recklessness is alleged); (4) the neglect caused the elder or dependent adult to suffer physical harm, pain or mental suffering; and (5) facts constituting neglect and establishing the causal link between the neglect and the injury.  See Carter, supra at 406-407.   More recently, it has been held that “neglect” under the Act includes the “[f]ailure to protect from health and safety hazards” as set forth in the language of the statute.  Samantha B. (2002) 77 CA5th 85, 99; See also Fenimore (2016) 245 CA4th 1339, 1347-1348.

 

In the First Amended Complaint, Plaintiff alleges sufficient facts to support each of the elements enumerated in Carter against Sylmar Health, a skilled nursing facility; Golden State, its licensee/owner/operator; Slavin, its administrator; and Hrenciuc, its director of nursing (i.e., managing agents).  See Welfare & Institutions Code 15657(c); Civil Code 3294(b).  For example, Plaintiff alleges:

 

• Defendants engaged in fraudulent charting. (FAC ¶¶88(c), 104(c)).

 

• Defendants were required to monitor Decedent for dehydration and provide reminders and encouragement to Decedent when hydration was required. Defendants were aware that failing to do so or withholding reminders and/or encouragement placed Decedent at increased risk of dehydration. Despite this notice, Defendants disregarded Decedent’s dehydration risk, failed to monitor Decedent for signs and symptoms of dehydration, withheld fluids, and caused Decedent to become dehydrated for a prolonged period of time. Dehydration caused Decedent to become weak and compromised, exacerbating Decedent’s susceptibility to further illnesses and infections and eventually causing and contributing to Decedent’s death. (FAC ¶¶88(i)-(k), 104(i)-(k)).

 

• Defendants were required to provide Decedent with, at the very least, supervision with personal hygiene, bathing, and showers. Defendants repeatedly failed to provide hygienic care, including showers. When coupled with Decedent’s dehydration, Decedent developed a urinary tract infection that contributed to her death. (FAC ¶¶88(l)-(n), 104(l)- (n)).

 

• Defendants were required to assist with medications and supplements according to physicians’ orders. Defendants repeatedly failed to follow those orders and provide all prescribed medications and supplements. Withholding those medications and supplements further compromised Decedent’s health and caused Decedent to become vulnerable to complications, contributing to and causing Decedent’s death. (FAC ¶¶88(o)-(q), 104(o)-(q)).

 

• Defendants were required to protect Decedent from health and safety hazards, which included infection prevention control. Defendants failed to provide and withheld safe living conditions, causing Decedent to contract COVID-19 and contributing to Decedent’s death. (FAC ¶¶88(r)-(s), 104(r)-(s)).

 

• Defendants were ordered by Decedent’s physician to transport Decedent to the emergency room if Decedent’s oxygen saturation fell below a certain percentile. On more than one occasion, Defendants failed to transport Decedent to the emergency room when her oxygen saturation fell below the requisite percentile and withheld emergency treatment Decedent required. (FAC ¶¶88(v)-(cc), 104(v)-(cc)).

 

Similarly, Plaintiff alleges sufficient facts to support causation and the damage claims in the First Amended Complaint.  The First Amended Complaint includes facts regarding instances where Defendants’ alleged ongoing pattern of failing to provide Decedent with necessary care, monitoring and assistance led to physical conditions that left Decedent a weakened and immunocompromised state.  (See FAC ¶¶39, 42, 88(k), 88(n), 88(q), 104(k), 104(n), 104(q)).  Plaintiff has alleged that Defendants’ conduct, actions and/or omissions contributed to, caused and/or were a substantial factor in causing specific injuries alleged in the First Amended Complaint, and ultimately led to Decedent’s death.  (See (FAC ¶¶39, 41-42, 61, 62, 64, 88(c), 88(k), 88(m)-(n), 88(q), 88(jj), 90, 92, 104(c), 104(k), 104(m)-(n), 104(q), 104(jj), 106, 108).

 

3rd cause of action – Violations of Health & Safety Code 1430(b) (Survival Claim)/Violation of Residents’ Bill of Rights

 

Health & Safety Code 1430(b) provides for a civil remedy for patients and residents of a skilled nursing facility or intermediate care facility for violation of Patient Rights in Section 72527 of Title 22 of the Code of Regulations, or any other right provided for by federal or state law or regulation.  Section 72527 provides a list of 26 specific rights.

 

The facts alleged regarding elder abuse/neglect claims, which are incorporated into the 3rd cause of action, are also sufficient to support a claim for violation of Health & Safety Code 1430(b).  (See FAC ¶¶109, 122-123, 126).

 

4th cause of action – Wrongful Death/Negligence

 

The elements of a wrongful death cause of action are: (1) a wrongful act or neglect on the part of one or more persons that (2) causes (3) the death of another person.  Civil Code 377.60; Barrett (1990) 222 CA3d 1176, 1185-1186.   The elements of a negligence cause of action are: duty, breach, causation and damages.  County of Santa Clara (2006) 137 CA4th 292, 318. 

 

The facts alleged in the general allegations and the preceding causes of action, which are incorporated into the 4th cause of action, along with the specific allegations contained within the wrongful death/negligence claim are sufficient to state the claim.  (See FAC, generally, ¶¶131-137).

 

Motion to Strike

 

The same facts which sufficiently support the first three causes of action are sufficient to support the claims for punitive damages and attorney’s fees.   

 

CONCLUSION

 

The joinder is granted.  The demurrer is overruled and the motion to strike is denied.  Answer(s) is/are due within 30 days.
