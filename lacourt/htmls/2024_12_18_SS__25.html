Case Number: 22STLC07854    Hearing Date: December 18, 2024    Dept: 25

PROCEEDINGS:     MOTION TO VACATE DISMISSAL, ENFORCE SETTLEMENT, AND ENTER JUDGMENT AGAINST DEFENDANT [CCP 664.6]  

 

MOVING PARTY:   Plaintiff Farmers Insurance Exchange 

RESP. PARTY:         None

 

ENTER JUDGMENT PURSUANT TO STIPULATION

(CCP § 664.6)

 

TENTATIVE RULING:                                                                       

 

Plaintiff Farmers Insurance Exchange’s unopposed Motion to Vacate Dismissal, Enforce Settlement, and Enter Judgment Against Defendant is GRANTED.

 

Judgment on Stipulation is entered in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant Noah Dinard in the Total amount of $19,340.00.

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

 

SERVICE: 

 

[X] Proof of Service Timely Filed (CRC, rule 3.1300)                      OK

[X] Correct Address (CCP §§ 1013, 1013a)                                      OK

[X] 16/21 Court Days Lapsed (CCP §§ 12c, 1005(b))                       OK

 

OPPOSITION:          None filed as of November 25, 2024              [   ] Late                      [X] None

REPLY:                     None filed as of November 25, 2024              [   ] Late                      [X] None

 

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

On November 11, 2022, Plaintiff Farmers Insurance Exchange (“Plaintiff”) filed an action against Defendant Noah Dinard (“Defendant”) and Does 1-5 requesting damages of $24,265.01. (Compl., p. 1, Prayer.)

 

On March 2, 2023, Defendant filed an Answer.

 

On May 15, 2024, Plaintiff filed a “Stipulation for Entry of Judgment in the Event of Default” (the “Stipulation”).

 

On August 13, 2024, the Honorable Karine Mkrtchyan signed an order dismissing the action but retaining jurisdiction under Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6. (8/13/24 Order.)

 

On October 9, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Vacate Dismissal, Enforce Settlement, and Enter Judgment Against Defendant (the “Motion”). No Opposition was filed.  

 

On December 4, 2024, the Court held a hearing on the Motion, and Plaintiff’s Counsel and Defendant, proceeding pro se, appeared. The Court was inclined to grant Plaintiff’s Motion but found Plaintiff failed to file proof of service demonstrating the Judgment and Proposed Order were served on Defendant. Accordingly, the Court continued the hearing on the Motion to allow Plaintiff to file proper proof of service on Defendant and ordered Plaintiff to file proof of service within five days.

 

On December 4, 2024, Plaintiff filed an “Amended Proof of Service re: Motion to Set Aside Dismissal and Enter Judgment; Proposed Judgment and Proposed Order” (“Amended Proof of Service”) as well as Notice of the December 4, 2024, Ruling with Proof of Service attached.

 

Plaintiff’s Amended Proof of Service demonstrates Defendant and Defendant’s previous counsel were served by mail on December 4, 2024, with the “Notice of Motion and Motion to Set Aside Dismissal and Enter Judgment; Proposed Judgment; Proposed Order.”

 

The Court finds Plaintiff has now properly served Defendant with the Judgment and Proposed Order as well as Notice of Ruling.

 

II.                 Legal Standard & Discussion

 

Under Code of Civil Procedure, section 664.6: 

 
(a) If parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside of the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement. If requested by the parties, the court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement. 

 

(b) For purposes of this section, a writing is signed by a party if it is signed by any of the following: 

 

(1) The party. 

(2) An attorney who represents the party. 

(3) If an insurer is defending and indemnifying a party to the action, an agent who is authorized in writing by the insurer to sign on the party’s behalf. This paragraph does not apply if the party whom the insurer is defending would be liable under the terms of the settlement for any amount above the policy limits.” 

 

The Stipulation filed by Plaintiff was signed by Defendant on April 24, 2024, Defendant’s counsel on May 13, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel on May 14, 2024, and an authorized representative for Plaintiff on May 15, 2024. (5/15/24 Stipulation, p. 4.)

 

The Stipulation provides the matter would be settled for a total sum of $11,000. (5/15/24 Stipulation, ¶ 4.) The Stipulation states Defendant is to pay the total sum of $11,000, reflecting $5,000 paid by Defendant’s insurance carrier and $6,000 to be paid by Defendant as follows: (1) $500 down by June 15, 2024; (2) $75 per month, beginning July 15, 2024 and continuing on the fifteenth day of each month thereafter for a period of 24 months; (3) $100 per month, beginning July 15, 2026 and continuing thereafter until the balance is paid in full. (5/15/24 Stipulation, ¶ 2.)

 

The Stipulation further states that failure to make a timely payment constitutes a default and, in the event of default, “Defendant shall be permitted a fifteen-day grace period for payment of any installment due hereunder. Plaintiff shall give written notice to Defendant of his default; and, Defendant shall then be allowed an additional ten days from the date of said written notice to cure the particular default.” (5/15/24 Stipulation, ¶ 8.) In the event Defendant fails to cure the default, “Plaintiff may immediately cause Judgment to be entered pursuant to the terms set forth in this Stipulation for the full amount of the agreed upon judgment as set forth in Paragraph 1 less any monies paid to date of the breach; and shall also file a partial Satisfaction of Judgment for all sums previously paid pursuant to this Stipulation.”  (5/15/24 Stipulation, ¶¶ 6, 8.) Paragraph 1 of the Stipulation states: “The parties stipulate that Judgment shall be entered in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant in the sum of TWENTY-FOUR THOUSAND TWO HUNDRED AND SIXTY-FIVE DOLLARS ($24,265.00) plus costs of suit and prejudgment interest from the date of the loss less any monies paid to date of breach will be entered as set forth in paragraph 6 below.” (5/15/24 Stipulation, ¶ 1.)

 

The Court finds the Stipulation to be valid and enforceable under Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6. Plaintiff provides evidence that Defendant made partial payment – Defendant’s insurance carrier paid a total of $5,000. (Benson Decl., ¶ 5.) As of the date this Motion was filed, however, Plaintiff had received no further payments from Defendant. (Benson Decl., ¶¶ 5, 8-9.) Plaintiff also presents evidence of the default payment letter sent to Defendant on July 30, 2024 advising Defendant of the default and the remaining balance then due in the sum of $6,000. (Benson Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. 2.) Further, Plaintiff presents evidence that Plaintiff’s office emailed Defendant on or about August 20, 2024 requesting status of his past due payments; Plaintiff received no response. (Benson Decl., ¶ 7.)

 

Plaintiff requests that a judgment of $19,340.00, based on the principal amount of $24,265.00, less payments received in the amount of $5,000.00 plus filing costs of $75.00, be entered against Defendant. (Benson Decl., ¶ 10.)

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s unopposed Motion is GRANTED.

 

III.              Conclusion & Order

 

Plaintiff Farmers Insurance Exchange’s unopposed Motion to Vacate Dismissal, Enforce Settlement, and Enter Judgment Against Defendant is GRANTED.

 

Judgment on Stipulation is entered in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant Noah Dinard in the Total amount of $19,340.00.

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.


Case Number: 24STLC02909    Hearing Date: December 18, 2024    Dept: 25

PROCEEDINGS:     MOTION TO EXPUNGE LIS PENDENS AND MECHANIC’S LIEN

 

MOVING PARTY:   Defendant Luco Enterprises, LLC

RESP. PARTY:         Plaintiff SoCal Lien Solutions, LLC

 

MOTION TO EXPUNGE LIS PENDENS AND MECHANIC’S LIEN

(Code Civ. Proc., § 405.20, et seq.)

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Defendant Luco Enterprises, LLC’s Motion to Expunge Lis Pendens and Mechanic’s Lien is DENIED.  

 

Plaintiff’s request for Attorney’s Fees and Costs is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE. 

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

On April 19, 2024, Plaintiff SoCal Lien Solutions, LLC (“Plaintiff”) initiated this action against Defendants American Flower, LLC (“American Flower”), Luco Enterprises, LLC (“Luco Enterprises”), and Ti Properties, Inc. (“Ti Properties”) (“Defendants”) and Does 1-10 alleging causes of action for Services Rendered, Quasi Contract Recovery, and Enforcement of Mechanic’s Lien. (Compl., p. 1.)

 

On June 6, 2024, Plaintiff filed amendments to the Complaint to designate Doe 1 as IDM Endeavors, LLC (“IDM Endeavors”) and Doe 2 as Mortgage Vintage, Inc (“Mortgage Vintage”).

 

On June 6, 2024, American Flower, LLC filed an Answer. On June 24, 2024, Luco Enterprises filed an Answer.

 

On June 24, 2024, Luco Enterprises filed a Cross-Complaint against Plaintiff as assignee of Sky Country, Inc. dba Aztec Construction (“Aztec Construction”) and Roes 1-50.

 

On July 16, 2024, Mortgage Vintage filed an Answer, and IDM Endeavors filed an Answer.

 

On October 8, 2024, Luco Enterprises filed the instant “Motion to Remove Mechanic’s Lien Filed by Plaintiff SoCal Lien Solutions, LLC (CCP §§ 405.32, 405.23)” (the “Motion”).

 

On December 5, 2024, Plaintiff filed an Opposition.

 

On December 11, 2024, Luco Enterprises filed a Reply.

 

II.                 Procedural Issues

 

In its Motion, Luco Enterprises attaches proof of service demonstrating service by mail of the Motion on Plaintiff. However, Luco Enterprises fails to provide proof of service of the Motion on all parties in the action.

 

The Court also notes the Opposition and Reply proofs of service are defective as they too were not served on all parties in the action.

 

III.              Legal Standard & Discussion

 

Moving Party Position

 

Luco Enterprises moves for an order expunging the mechanic’s lien and lis pendens filed by Plaintiff on the following grounds: “Plaintiff cannot establish the probable validity of the claim under the facts of this case and, as such, the same must be expunged,” and “Plaintiff cannot establish the probable validity of the lis pendens under the facts of this case, and, as such, the same must be expunged.” (Motion, p. 3.) Luco Enterprises attaches the subject mechanic’s lien to its Motion. (Rippy Decl., ¶ 9, Exh. E.)

 

Luco Enterprises argues that Aztec Construction only performed 75 percent of the contracted work and overspent on the contract budget. (Motion, p. 6; Bowers Decl., ¶¶ 6-7; Rippy Decl., ¶¶ 6-9.) Thereafter, Aztec Construction filed a mechanics lien and lis pendens against the subject property, even though Luco Enterprises refused to further pay Aztec Construction because Aztec Construction “was requesting further draws in excess of funding to which it [was] entitled.” (Motion, p. 6.) Thus, Luco Enterprises argues the “filing of the mechanics lien was made in bad faith in an effort to squeeze further funds from Luco and its partners.” (Motion, p. 6.)

 

Finally, Luco Enterprises requests all reasonable fees and costs should Luco Enterprises be the prevailing party. (Motion, p. 6.)

 

Opposition

 

In Opposition, Plaintiff contends Luco Enterprises’ contention that the subject mechanic’s lien is invalid fails because there is no written memorialization of the alleged fixed contract price, and Plaintiff submits proof that Defendants have not made full payment to Aztec Construction for work performed. (Opp., pp. 2-3; Opp., Parikh Decl., ¶¶ 3-4, Exhs. A, B.) Specifically, Plaintiff contends Luco Enterprises cancelled payment to Aztec Construction “not for payments to Aztek having reached 75% of the budget, but due to Aztek being removed as contractor.” (Opp., Parikh Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. B.) To bolster this claim, Plaintiff attaches evidence of an email exchange between Aztek Construction, La Mesa Fund Control & Escrow, Inc. – “the fund controller handling construction loan draw requests for the construction loan made to Luco Enterprises” (Opp., Parikh Decl., ¶ 3) – and Mortgage Vintage, wherein La Mesa Fund Control and Escrow, Inc. stated: “We still have [a] request pending your approval. since the contractor is being removed etc. would you like us to void and cancel this request?” (Opp., Parikh Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. B.) Mortgage Vintage replied, “yes.” (Opp., Parikh Decl., ¶ 4, Exh. B.)

 

Finally, Plaintiff requests attorney fees under Code of Civil Procedure Section 405.38.

 

Reply

 

            In Reply, Luco Enterprises states the contract between the parties was verbal, meaning the terms of the contract can “only be determined by extrinsic writings, documentation, performance by the parties, and the funding agencies adopted by the parties during the contract execution.” (Reply, p. 2.) Accordingly, Luco Enterprises contends these factors evidence the state of performance and payments during the run of the contract, and the performance under the contract was incomplete when Aztec Constructions filed its last request for withdrawal under the budget. (Reply, p. 2.) Contrary to Plaintiff’s assertion, Luco Enterprises argues the budget is “indeed written evidence of the contracted price.” (Reply, p. 2.)

 

            In response to Plaintiff’s allegation that the last withdrawal request was denied at the direction of Luco Enterprises, Luco Enterprises denies the claim and states that determination was made by La Mesa Fund Control and Escrow, Inc., not Luco Enterprises. (Reply, pp. 2-3.) Luco Enterprises then reiterates its argument that Aztec Construction failed to perform under the contract and provides additional evidence that the replacement contractor who came in after Aztec Construction stated Aztec Construction’s work was performed only at 70 percent of competition and was rife with various examples of poor workmanship. (Reply, p. 3, Exh. A.)

 

            Finally, Luco Enterprises argues Plaintiff failed to meet its burden under Code of Civil Procedure section 405.32 to establish by a preponderance of the evidence the probable validity of the claim and its lien; Aztec Construction overdrew and underperformed; Plaintiff only presents evidence of a $9,800 draw request in its Opposition, which is substantially less than the amount requested in the Complaint and mechanic’s lien; and, due to Aztec Construction’s insufficient performance, Luco Enterprises had to have a new remedial contract in the amount of approximately $300,000. (Reply, pp. 4-5.)

 

Analysis

 

“At any time after notice of pendency of action has been recorded, any party, or any nonparty with an interest in the real property affected thereby, may apply to the court in which the action is pending to expunge the notice.… Evidence or declarations may be filed with the motion to expunge the notice.… The claimant shall have the burden of proof under Sections 405.31 and 405.32.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 405.30, emphasis added.) The “claimant” is the “party to an action who asserts a real property claim and records a notice of the pendency of the action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 405.1.) The court shall order the notice expunged if (1) the court “finds that the claimant has not established by a preponderance of the evidence the probable validity of the real property claim,” or (2) the Court “finds that the pleading on which the notice is based does not contain a real property claim.” (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 405.31-405.32.) The Court shall not order an undertaking to be given as a condition of expunging the notice where the Court finds the pleading does not contain a real property claim or the claimant has not established the probable validity of the real property claim. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 405.31-405.32.)

 

            As an initial matter, the Court notes a lis pendens regarding the subject mechanic’s lien has not been filed with the Court. Accordingly, the Court is unable to make a ruling regarding expunging a lis pendens when no lis pendens is before it. (Code Civ. Proc., § 405.30.)

 

            To the extent Luco Enterprises moves to expunge the mechanic’s lien described as “1667 Cota Ave, Long Beach, CA 90813 Los Angeles 7432-001-023” (Compl., ¶ 22; Motion, p. 3), the Motion is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 405.32 and 405.23; however, those statutes pertain to recording a notice of pendency of action (lis pendens) on which a real property claim is alleged. (See Code Civ. Proc., §§ 405.23, 405.32.) Luco Enterprises has not demonstrated that this statute allows the Court to expunge a mechanic’s lien.  

 

            Accordingly, Luco Enterprises’ Motion is DENIED.

 

Attorneys’ Fees

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 405.38 provides that, “[t]he court shall direct that the party prevailing on [a motion to expunge lis pendens] be awarded the reasonable attorney’s fees and costs of making or opposing the motion unless the court finds that the other party acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of attorney’s fees and costs unjust.”


            Here, Plaintiff is the prevailing party and is thus entitled to reasonable attorney fees and costs. (Code Civ. Proc., § 405.38.) However, Plaintiff provides no proof of fees and costs.

 

            Thus, Plaintiff’s request is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE.

 

IV.              Conclusion & Order

 

For the foregoing reasons, Luco Enterprises, LLC’s Motion to Expunge Lis Pendens and Mechanic’s Lien is DENIED.

 

Plaintiff’s request for Attorney’s Fees and Costs is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE. 

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.
