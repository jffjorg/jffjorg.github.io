DEPARTMENT 57 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Inform the clerk if you submit on the tentative ruling. If moving and opposing parties submit, no appearance is necessary.

Case Number: 20STCV15846    Hearing Date: May 8, 2024    Dept: 57

Plaintiffs have moved for terminating sanctions, or, alternatively, issue, evidentiary, or monetary sanctions against Defendant.  

The Court is declining to impose terminating or issue sanctions against Defendant. The asserted discovery misconduct of Defendant on which Plaintiffs' request for terminating or issue sanctions is based does not support the imposition of those extreme sanctions.

At the hearing, the Court will ask Plaintiffs' counsel why the asserted misconduct supports the imposition of the less draconian sanctions that Plaintiffs alternatively seek.

Case Number: 20STCV49949    Hearing Date: May 8, 2024    Dept: 57

DEFENDANTS MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT AND SUMMARY ADJUDICATION 

The Motion for Summary Judgment is DENIED.  Contrary to Defendants' position, the "entrustment exclusion" in the insurance policy at issue in this case does not bar coverage for Plaintiff Peter Marco LLC's asserted losses.  The Court's determination in this regard is based on  Freedman v. Queen Insurance Co. of America (1961) 56 Cal.2d 45.  The California Supreme Court's decision in Freedman forecloses as a matter of law Defendants' interpretation of the entrustment exclusion.  

Defendants' motion for Summary Adjudication as to five specified issues is GRANTED in part and DENIED in part.  

Issue 1  Entrustment Exclusion 

For the same reason that Defendant's Motion for Summary Judgment is DENIED, Defendants' Motion for Summary Adjudication as to Issue 1 is DENIED.

Issue 2   Lloyds Defendants' Duty to Defend

Defendants' Motion for Summary Adjudication as to Issue 2 is DENIED.  "A liability policy is presumed to include a defense duty unless the duty is excluded by clear and unambiguous language . . . ."  (Maryland Casualty Co. v. Nationwide Ins. Co. (1998) 65 Cal.App.4th 21, 30.)  This statement of the law is embodied in Civil Code Section 2778, which provides that "a defense obligation is implied in all indemnity agreements," including insurance agreements, unless the agreement clearly shows a contrary intention.  (Id. at p. 31.)   The insurance agreement at issue in this case does not clearly and unambiguously exclude the Lloyds Defendants' duty to defend the named insured, Peter Marco LLC, against liability to third parties. 

Issue 3  The Cause of Action Against Defendant Underwriters For Breach of the Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing 

Defendants' Motion for Summary Adjudication as to Issue 3 is DENIED.  There are disputed issues of material fact regarding the reasonableness of the Lloyds Defendants' denial of coverage for the asserted losses of Plaintiff Peter Marco LLC, and thus whether Defendants' coverage position breached the covenant of good faith and fair dealing.

Issue 4  The Availability of Punitive Damages Against the Defendant Underwriters

Defendants' Motion for Summary Adjudication as to Issue 4 is DENIED.  There are disputed issues of material fact regarding whether a jury could conclude by clear and convincing evidence that the Lloyds Defendants acted with malice, oppression, or fraud in denying coverage for Plaintiff Peter Marco LLC's asserted losses.

Issue 5  Dismissal of Plaintiff Peter Voutsas Because He is not a Named InsuredDefendants' Motion for Summary Adjudication as to Issue 5 is GRANTED.  Plaintiff Peter Voutsas is not
 a named insured for purposes of the asserted first party losses and liability to third parties from which this case arises.  Peter Marco LLC is the named insured for those purposes.  Voutsas is a named insured in the "Jeweler and Gemologist Professional Appraisal Endorsement" that is part of the policy in question.  But the asserted first party losses and liability to third parties from which this cases arises do not stem from an appraisal to which the Endorsement Applies.  The reference in the Endorsement to coverage for "owners," "executive officers," and "directors" of the insured, Peter Marco, LLC, does not extend coverage to Voutsas across the board.   That reference plainly is for the Endorsement only. 

PLAINTIFFS' MOTION FOR JUDGMENT ON THE PLEADINGS 

The motion for judgment on the pleadings as to the Defendants' Tenth, Eleventh, Twelfth, Fourteenth, and Fifteenth Affirmative Defenses is  DENIED.  The Court cannot determine from the face of the pleadings that those Affirmative Defenses are barred as a matter of law.  This is so notwithstanding the Court's ruling denying Defendants' Motion for Summary Adjudication as to the entrustment exclusion in the policy at issue in this case and the stipulated judgment in the related Lazar action against Peter Marco, LLC.    

The motion for judgment on the pleadings as to the Defendants' Thirteenth Affirmative Defense is GRANTED.  That Defense posits that the Lloyds Defendants had no duty under the policy in question to defend Plaintiff and named insured Peter Marco, LLC against asserted liability to third parties.  That defense is foreclosed as a matter of law.  "A liability policy is presumed to include a defense duty unless the duty is excluded by clear and unambiguous language . . . ."  (Maryland Casualty Co. v. Nationwide Ins. Co. (1998) 65 Cal.App.4th 21, 30.)  This statement of the law is embodied in Civil Code Section 2778, which provides that "a defense obligation is implied in all indemnity agreements," including insurance agreements, unless the agreement clearly shows a contrary intention.  (Id. at p. 31.)   The insurance agreement at issue in this case does not clearly and unambiguously exclude the Lloyds Defendants' duty to defend the named insured, Peter Marco LLC, against liability to third parties.  Thus, as a matter of law, the Lloyds Defendants have a duty to defend and their Thirteenth Affirmative Defense fails as a matter of law.

The Court will permit counsel for Defendant to refresh Plaintiff's recollection through the text messages that Defendant did not disclose to Plaintiff during to discovery even though the messages were responsive to Plaintiff's discovery requests.  The authority for this decision is Evans v. Hood Corp. (2016) 5 Cal.App.5th 1022, 1042, which observed that there is "no authority stating that a document used to refresh a witness's recollection must be disclosed in discovery . . .  To the contrary, Evidence Code section 771 . . . anticipates that a document used to refresh a witness's recollection will be produced at the hearing in which the witness is testifying . . . and need not be admissible, and indeed are inadmissible under Evidence Code section 771(b), except when proffered by the adverse party."    This decision is tentative in that the impeachment will be allowed today so that the trial can proceed expeditiously.  At the close of the trial, however, Plaintiff may argue that, notwithstanding Evans v .Hood,  the Court should strike all references to the text messages used to refresh Plaintiff's recollection and all testimony that follows the refreshing.   This is a bench trial, not a jury trial.  After the trial and in deciding the case, the Court will be able to disregard the text messages and related testimony if Plaintiff persuades the Court to strike the text messages and related testimony.

Case Number: 23STCV11642    Hearing Date: May 8, 2024    Dept: 57


The separate statements that Plaintiff filed in support of his three motions to compel Defendant Pop ID Inc. to provide further responses to discovery --  special interrogatories, form interrogatories, and request for production of documents -- that Plaintiff propounded are defective.  California Rule of Court 3.1345 requires separate statements in support of such motions to set for the text of the interrogatories and request that are the subject of the motions.  Plaintiff's separate statements do not comply with that requirement.   As a result of that void, the Court cannot ascertain to what it is that Plaintiff is seeking to compel further responses.   In response to that defect, the Court's recourse is the same one taken in connection with Defendant's discovery motions that were supported by separate statements that failed to comply with Rule 3.1345: like Defendant, Plaintiff will be given an opportunity to file and serve amended separate statements in support of his three motions, and as with Defendants' motions, Plaintiff's will have to be continued.
