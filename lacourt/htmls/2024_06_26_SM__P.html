DEPARTMENT P LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 20SMCV01208    Hearing Date: June 26, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Kadosh v. Allarie-Larson, et al., Case No. 20SMCV01208

Hearing date June 26, 2024

Defendant’s Motion to Set Aside Default Judgment

 

Defendant tenant seeks to set aside default in this failure to pay rent case. Defendant seeks relief under Code Civ. Proc. §473.5, asserting she did not have notice because she was not served and arguing default was fraudulently obtained.

 

Evidentiary Ruling Objections

Defendant’s Request for Judicial Notice of Proof of Service of Process filed by plaintiff on November 2, 2020 GRANTED as a court document per Evidence Code 452(d).

 

Plaintiff’s evidentiary objections to defendant’s declaration and supplemental declaration are not numbered consecutively (as required by CRC 3.1354), but the court rules as follows: Objections to Allarie’s Declaration: SUSTAINED as to paragraph 8 (hearsay). Other objections are OVERRULED. Objections to Allarie’s Supplemental Declaration: SUSTAINED as to paragraphs 7 (lines 6-9) (lacks personal knowledge) and 11 (irrelevant). Other objections are OVERRULED.

 

When a motion is made on grounds that default judgment is void for lack of service, the limitation period in Code of Civ. Proc., sec. 473.5 applies. Defendant must file the motion within two years after entry of default judgment (Trackman v. Kenney (2010) 187 Cal. App. 4th 175, 180-181; Code Civ. Proc. §473.5) and file a proposed responsive pleading.

 

Relief from default or default judgment can be sought at any time on the ground of extrinsic mistake. Olivera v. Grace (1942) 19 Cal.2d 570, 576; Sporn v. Home Depot USA, Inc. (2005) 126 Cal.App.4th 1294, 1300. To obtain relief, moving party must show: (1) a meritorious defense; (2) diligence in seeking relief; and (3) a satisfactory excuse for not presenting a defense to the original action. Rappleyea v. Campbell (1994) 8 Cal.4th 975, 982. Once the extrinsic fraud or mistake is discovered, a party must proceed diligently to seek relief. Id., at 983-984.

On September 3, 2020 plaintiff sued for past due rent. Process server Alexandra Melgoza substitute served the summons and complaint to “Chris Kim” on 9/14/20 at 2:41 PM at Postal Annex, defendant’s P.O. box location. Copies were also mailed. See 11/2/20 Proof of Service. Allarie did not file responsive pleadings. On November 2, 2020 plaintiff requested entry of default. Default was entered February 17, 2021 for $86,589.37.

 

On April 2, 2024 defendant moved to set aside per to Code Civ. Proc. §473.5, claiming lack of notice and that the default was obtained by fraud. A proposed answer was filed June 7, 2024.

 

A proof of service attested to by a registered process server creates a rebuttable presumption of service. Evid. Code §647; American Express Centurion Bank v. Zara¿(2011) 199 Cal.App.4th 383, 390. The party seeking to defeat service of process must present sufficient evidence to show that the service did not take place as stated. Palm Property Investments, LLC v. Yadegar¿(2011) 194 Cal.App.4th 1419, 1428.

Defendant argues she was not served by mail. Allarie Decl. ¶¶5-6. Per the proof of service, process server Melgoza states she served at defendant’s residence, but the address served was actually defendant’s P.O. box. Allarie Decl. ¶7. Based upon this discrepancy, defendant argues Melgoza’s declaration is fraudulent. Defendant states she does not know what happened to the documents served by Melgoza. Allarie Decl. ¶9. However, this is contradicted by Exh. 3 to Allarie’s declaration (filed 4/2/24), which states:

 

“In early December the elderly woman at my PO Box gave me a legal documents not in an envelope or folder, given to her by a process server. (FYI: I HAVE DECEMBER AS WHEN I RECEIVED THE CMC STATEMENT DOCUMENT IN MY NOTES. I KNOW THE PROCESS SERVER SAYS SEPTEMBER 14, 2020 WAS THE DATE SHE LEFT THE DOCUMENTS AT MY PO BOX SO MAYBE I MADE A MISTAKE? I WAS MOVING SO IT IS POSSIBLE I DIDN’T STOP BY MY PO BOX UNTIL DECEMBER?) I assumed it was for a hearing for a lawsuit. The paperwork said; “NOTICE OF CASE MANAGEMENT CONFERENCE”

 

Your Case Management Conference has been scheduled at the courthouse address shown above on: 03-02-2021 at 8:30am   Dept. P

 

I understood this to mean, I needed to show up at the SM Courthouse at 8:30am on the 2nd of March 2021. Allarie Decl. Exh. 3, p. 3.

 

This constitutes an admission that Allarie was in receipt of the summons and complaint by no later than December 2020. Defendant’s supplemental declaration (filed 5/7/24, para. 3) states:

 

In fact, I only first learned that I was being sued by KADOSH in September or possibly October of 2020 when the “Notice of Case Management Conference” was mailed to my PO Box. The Notice of Case Management Conference informed me of a hearing on March 2, 2021 for this lawsuit. It was then that I discovered that KADOSH was suing both me and my deceased husband, whom KADOSH knew had died (by suicide) in April of 2020. (emphasis added)

 

This acknowledges notice of the suit and CMC in September or October 2020. Allarie Supp. Decl. ¶3. A motion under Code Civ. Proc. §473.5 must be made within two years after entry of a default judgment, which occurred February 17, 2021.

 

Defendant was aware of the suit by late 2020 and the default by no later than March 1, 2021, when she called the court clerk to ask for directions to the March 2, 2021 CMC and was told the CMC was cancelled since default judgment had been entered. Allarie Supp. Decl. ¶8.

 

Based on the totality of the evidence, Allarie’s motion is untimely under Code Civ. Proc. §473.5. As default judgment was entered February 17, 2021, the motion would have to have been filed by February 17, 2023.

 

Evidence of a false proof of service constitutes grounds for a set-aside based on extrinsic fraud. County of San Diego v. Gorham (2010) 186 Cal.App.4th 1215, 1229. To be entitled to such equitable relief, defendant must meet the Rappleyea standard (discussed above).

 

At the court’s instruction, defendant provided a supplemental declaration regarding when and how she discovered the default judgment and whether she proceeded thereafter in a timely manner to seek relief. Defendant learned of the default from the court clerk on March 1, 2021. She filed her motion to set aside April 2, 2024, three years later. Defendant did not take reasonable steps to set aside promptly and was outside the two year statutory time period.

 

Defendant argues the process server’s affidavit was fraudulent because states the summons and complaint were served at defendant’s residence, but the address listed is defendant’s P.O. box location. This may be evidence of a mistake, but defendant proves no fraudulent intent. Further, she had actual notice (see discussion above).

 

Mailboxes at a private business can receive service. Code Civ. Proc., sec. 415.20. Defendant’s mailbox was at Postal Annex. See Allarie Decl. ¶7. By her own admission, she received notice of the CMC in “early December” from “the elderly woman at my PO Box” who gave her “legal documents . . . given to her by a process server.” Allarie Decl. Exh. 3, p. 3. There is no proof of fraud.

 

There is no basis on which the court can grant the requested relief. DENIED. 


Case Number: 22SMCV01410    Hearing Date: June 26, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Fallieras v. Subaru of America, Inc, et al., Case No. 22SMCV01410

Hearing date June 26, 2024

Cross-Complainant Subaru’s Demurrer to DCH Subaru’s Second Amended Cross-Complaint

 

Plaintiff Fallieras sued defendants Subaru of America, Inc. (SOA) and Thousand Oaks-S, Inc. dba DCH Subaru of Thousand Oaks (DCH) alleging various Song-Beverly claims against SOA and negligent repair against DCH only. SOA and DCH cross-complained against one another. Plaintiff’s claims were settled on 3/13/24.

 

Dealer DCH cross complained against SOA, seeking (1) express indemnity; (2) implied indemnity; (3) equitable indemnity and (4) declaratory relief. SOA’s demurrer to DCH’s first amended cross-complaint was sustained with leave to amend as to all causes of action on 1/16/24. DCH filed a second amended cross-complaint (SACC) on 2/7/24. SOA demurs again.

 

The only claim against DCH in the underlying action was for negligent repair. DCH tendered to SOA, which denied the tender. Via the SACC, DCH seeks indemnification from SOA for its own alleged negligence in repairing the vehicle. The SACC does not allege SOA was involved in repairing the vehicle. The SACC contains no new allegations that SOA is liable (or should be liable) for alleged negligence by DCH for its allegedly faulty repair of the vehicle or that the alleged problems with the vehicle were manufacturing or design defects for which SOA might be responsible under the Song-Beverly theories.

 

DCH’s Request for Judicial Notice

DCH requests the court take judicial notice of (a) SOA’s cross-complaint filed November 20, 2023 (Cross-Comp. RJN, Exh. A), and (b) the Notice of Settlement between Fallieras and SOA filed March 13, 2024 (Cross-Comp. RJN, Exh. B). GRANTED. Evid. Code §§452-453.

 

Implied Indemnity

Implied contractual indemnity is not based upon equitable considerations, but on a contractual relationship between indemnitee and indemnitor from which is implied an obligation by indemnitor to assume and pay foreseeable damages assessed against the indemnitee as a result of indemnitor's breach of contract. Bear Creek Planning Com. v. Title Ins. & Trust Co. (1985) 164 Cal.App.3d 1227, 1239.

 

In the SACC, DCH still fails to allege any basis for SOA’s implied contractual indemnity for DCH’s allegedly negligent repairs on the vehicle. SUSTAINED without leave to amend.

 

Equitable Indemnity

“Equitable indemnity, which requires no contractual relationship, is premised on a joint legal obligation to another for damages; it is subject to allocation of fault principles and comparative equitable apportionment of loss.” C.W. Howe Partners Inc. v. Mooradian (2019) 43 Cal.App.5th 688, 700, quotation marks omitted. “The elements of a cause of action for equitable indemnity are (1) a showing of fault on the part of the indemnitor and (2) resulting damages to the indemnitee for which the indemnitor is equitably responsible.” Id.

 

The same reasoning applies. DCH now alleges, without a factual basis, that SOA proximately caused the problems, and DCH has a right to indemnity. SACC, para. 8. The SACC still fails to allege a basis for equitable indemnity owed by SOA to DCH for DCH’s allegedly negligent repair to the vehicle. SUSTAINED without leave to amend.

 

Declaratory Relief

“To qualify for declaratory relief, a party would have to demonstrate its action presented two essential elements: (1) a proper subject of declaratory relief, and (2) an actual controversy involving justiciable questions relating to the party’s rights or obligations.” Jolley v. Chase Home Finance, LLC (2013) 213 Cal.App.4th 872, 909.

 

SOA argues the SACC is uncertain as to whether the alleged defects were SOA’s manufacturing defects (for which SOA might be responsible) or caused by DCH’s (allegedly) negligent repairs, so SOA’s refusal to accept DCH’s tender demand presents an actual controversy. However, even with the allegations of paras. 19-22, DCH fails to sufficiently demonstrate the negligent repair claim is derivative and stems from the Song-Beverly claims. SUSTAINED without leave to amend.

 

Apportionment of Fault/Contribution

“[T]he dichotomy between [contribution and indemnity] is more formalistic than substantive,” and “‘[[i]ndemnity] is only an extreme form of contribution.’” American Motorcycle Ass’n. v. Superior Court (1978) 20 Cal.3d 578, 591, fn. 3; see Prince, 45 Cal.4th at 1162, fn. 7 [“[c]ontribution and indemnity are related doctrines, but contribution ‘“presupposes a common liability which is shared by the joint tortfeasors on a pro rata basis”’”]. “‘At the heart of the doctrine [of equitable indemnity] is apportionment based on fault.’” Heritage Oaks Partners v. First American Title Ins. Co. (2007) 155 Cal.App.4th 339, 348.

This is not a recognized cause of action. There is no basis alleged under which SOA is liable to DCH. SACC, paras. 11-13. SUSTAINED without leave to amend.
