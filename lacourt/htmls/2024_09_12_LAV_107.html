DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23VECV01576     Hearing Date: September 12, 2024    Dept: 107

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

Hamid Kohan Darvish,

Plaintiff,

 

      

   v.

 

Khelika Bell et al.

 

Defendants.

 

   Case Number             Department

23VECV01576                     107

 

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

 

Motion for Judgment on the PLeadings

 

 

BACKGROUND

 

On April 11, 2023, Hamid Kohan Darvish (decedent) filed suit against defendants Khelika Bell (Bell), El Camino Real Charger High School (El Camino), Friends of ECR (ECR), and the City of Los Angeles (the City; collectively defendants). On May 23, 2024, the estate of Hamid Kohan Darvish and Sarah Kohan Darvish (Kohan Darvish; collectively plaintiffs) filed the operative first amended complaint for damages. The Decedent appears to allege a claim for negligence against all defendants while Kohan Darvish asserts a claim for loss of consortium.

 

Plaintiffs alleged that on November 6, 2022, at 5600 North Valley Circle Boulevard at Woodland Hills, the Decedent was on his way to the local high school for a carnival. When Decedent tried to cross the street at Valley Circle Blvd, Bell struck him with her vehicle. Bell was distracted by the lights of the carnival. The Decedent suffered severe injuries which he eventually succumbed to. Defendants El Camino, ECR and the City were the owners of the grounds where the carnival was located as well as the organizers and operators of the event. They created the unsafe conditions when they failed to provide safe access to El Camino, created a substantial increase in local traffic and failed to mitigate any safety hazards caused by the event.

 

 

On August 1, 2024, the City filed a Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings as to the complaint filed by Kohan Darvish on the grounds that plaintiff failed to comply with the claims presentation requirement of the California Tort Claims Act. The Plaintiffs did not file an opposition.

 

On August 29, 2024, the court denied plaintiff’s motion for leave to file a second amended complaint.

[Tentative] Ruling

 

 The motion for judgment on the pleadings is granted with leave to amend.

 

DISCUSSION

Legal Standard:

A party may move for judgment on the pleadings on the grounds that “[t]he complaint does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action against that defendant.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 438, subd. (c)(1)(B)(ii).)

Analysis

The issue is whether Kohan Darvish has complied with the claims presentation requirement.

Generally, no suit for money or damages may be brought against a public entity until a written claim, known as a government claim, is presented to and rejected by that entity. (Gov. Code §§ 905 [listing exceptions not relevant here], 945.4 [requiring presentation of claims]; see also Munoz v. State of California (1995) 33 Cal.App.4th 1767, 1776.) The failure to timely file a proper government claim is fatal to the maintenance of a civil action against a public entity. (Hernandez v. City of Stockton (2023) 90 Cal.App.5th 1222, 1230–31.)

Here, there are three grounds for granting the motion. First, Kohan Darvish does not plead compliance with the claims presentation requirement anywhere in the FAC. Second, defendant submits the declaration of Michael Valdivia who states he is a is a duly appointed Deputy City Clerk for the City of Los Angeles. (Valdivia Decl., ¶1.) He is charged with the filing of all claims against the City and is familiar with its records. He provides that after a diligence search, defendant Kohan Darvish did not serve a claim for damages. (Valdivia Decl., ¶2) Third, Kohan Darvish brings her claim in her individual capacity, not on behalf of the estate, and thus, must present her own separate claim from decedent. (See Lewis v. City and County of San Francisco (1971) 21 Cal.App.3d 339, 342 [heirs could not rely on decedents claims letter for wrongful death claim].)

Thus, the motion is granted because plaintiff has not complied with the claims presentation requirement.

Leave to Amend

The question remains whether the court should grant leave to amend. Defendant argues that plaintiff cannot correct the deficiency of the claims presentation requirement because the time to file a claim has lapsed.

Claims for death or personal injury must be presented “not later than six months after the accrual of the cause of action.” (Gov. Code, §911.2.) “When a claim that is required by Section 911.2 to be presented not later than six months after the accrual of the cause of action is not presented within that time, a written application may be made to the public entity for leave to present that claim.” (Gov. Code § 911.4, subd. (a).) The application must be presented within a reasonable time not to exceed one year after the accrual of the cause of action. (Gov. Code § 911.4, subd. (b).)

It is unclear what type of claim plaintiff brings, e.g., loss of consortium and/or wrongful death. “The cause of action for loss of consortium ‘arises out of the bodily injury to the spouse who can no longer perform the spousal functions. It is the loss of conjugal fellowship, affection, society and companionship which gives rise to the cause of action.’ ” (Leonard v. John Crane, Inc. (2012) 206 Cal.App.4th 1274, 1288.) A “claim for loss of consortium could not come into existence until the defendant's negligent conduct causes the plaintiff actual and appreciable harm.” (Id. at p. 1289.) The claim for wrongful death, necessarily accrues on the date of the death.

Here, on November 6, 2022, decedent was gravely injured, and Kohan Darvish’s cause of action for loss of consortium accrued. (See Leonard, supra, 206 Cal.App.4th at p. 1288.) Accounting for the six months claims presentation requirement, her time to file a claim expired on May 6, 2023. On July 8, 2023, decedent passed away and Kohan Darvish’s claim for wrongful death accrued. She had until January 8, 2024, to file a claim. Assuming Kohan Darvish had some kind of grounds for a late application for the later claim, she had until July 8, 2024, to file a claim for wrongful death. (See Gov. Code, §911.4.)

The City submits a declaration from Valdivia dated May 28, 2024, stating that Kohan Darvish had failed to submit a claim. That still leaves 40 days during which Kohan Darvish may have timely applied to file a late claim. The court cannot conclude based on the evidence that Kohan Darvish did not timely remedy the claims presentation issue.

Accordingly, the motion is granted with 30 days leave to amend.

 

CONCLUSION

 

The motion is granted with 30 days leave to amend.

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.
