DEPARTMENT 207 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: SC127147    Hearing Date: January 26, 2024    Dept: 207

TENTATIVE RULING - REVISED

 

DEPARTMENT

207

HEARING DATE

January 18, 2024 and January 26, 2024

CASE NUMBER

SC127147

MOTION

Motion to Bifurcate/OSC re Consolidation

MOVING PARTY

Cross-Defendant Railcraft USA (2010), Inc. (erroneously sued as Railcraft International Inc. dba Railcraft USA, Inc.)

OPPOSING PARTIES

Cross-Complainant PPF AMLI 4242 Via Marina, LP

Cross-Defendant Advanced Geosolutions, Inc.

 

MOTION

 

            The Court issued an Order to Show Cause why all related cases should not be consolidated for trial, and set an Order to Show Cause hearing on January 26, 2024. 

 

In the meantime, Cross-Defendant Railcraft USA (2010), Inc. (erroneously sued as Railcraft International Inc. dba Railcraft USA, Inc.) moved to sever and bifurcate all railing claims, or in the alternative, to continue trial.  The parties have filed statements with their respective positions regarding consolidation and bifurcation for trial.

 

PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

            Main Case & Cross-Complaints – SC127147

 

This case began on February 28, 2017, when Plaintiff Advanced Geosolutions, Inc. (“AGI”) brought suit against Defendant Western National Builders (“WNB”) and AMLI 4242 Via Marina LLC (“AMLI”).  Defendant Fidelity and Deposit Company of Maryland (“Fidelity”) was added as a defendant on November 15, 2023.

 

AMLI cross-complained against AMLI and AGI on May 30, 2017, adding Cross-Defendant True Line Railing (“True Line”) to the action via Roe amendment on August 18, 2021, but True Line’s motion to dismiss for failure to serve within three years was granted on January 18, 2022.

 

WNB also cross-complained against AGI on May 31, 2017, and added the following Cross-Defendants via Roe Amendments on November 14, 2018: Boudreau Pipeline (“Boudreau”), David Evans and Associates dba Hall & Foreman, Hall & Foreman (“Hall & Foreman”), Geotechnical Professionals (“Geotechnical”), and R3 Contractors (“R3”).  WNB added True Line as another Cross-Defendant via Roe Amendment on August 17, 2021, but True Line was voluntarily dismissed on March 10, 2022.  Fidelity was added as a cross-defendant on August 5, 2022 via the filing of the second amended cross-complaint.  Griffin Dewatering was added as a cross-defendant via Roe Amendment on October 23, 2023.

 

Additional cross-complaints were also brought by:

 

1.      Geotechnical against AGI on February 5, 2019

2.      Boudreau against Geotechnical on February 7, 2019 (replacing Geotechnical with WNB by amended cross-complaint on September 11, 2019, and adding Griffin Dewatering via Moe Amendment on June 20, 2023)

3.      R3 against WNB on March 8, 2019 (adding Griffin Dewatering via Moe Amendment on August 30, 2021)

4.      R3 against AGI on April 23, 2019

5.      Griffin Dewatering against MOES 1 through 100 on February 14, 2022

6.      True Line against WNB on November 21, 2023 

 

Rockwell Drywell Case & Cross-Complaints – 21STCV20436

 

On June 1, 2021, Rockwell Drywall filed suit against WNB and AMLI, adding Fidelity as a defendant on July 22, 2022.  Rockwell voluntarily dismissed its complaint on December 1, 2023.

 

Despite Rockwell’s voluntarily dismissal, there are still two active cross-complaints within the Rockwell matter.  WNB cross-complained against AMLI, adding True Line via Roe Amendment on February 1, 2022, and Railcraft International Inc. dba Railcraft USA, Inc. (“Railcraft”) via Roe Amendment on September 25, 2023. 

 

AMLI also cross-complained against WNB within the Rockwell matter.

SUMMARY OF THE PARTIES’ RESPECTIVE POSITIONS

 

WNB requests that all cases be consolidated for discovery and case management purposes, but that the cases be severed/bifurcated for trial as follows: the lead case and sewer claims shall be tried first after a 90-day continuance to allow the parties to complete expert depositions before trial.  Then, the railing claims should be tried separately via the Rockwell case, the emergency lighting claims should be tried separately, and the mechanics lien collection cases should be tried separately from the sewer claims, the railing claims, and the emergency lighting claims.

 

Boudreaux does not oppose bifurcation/severance of the railing issues, but primarily seeks a trial continuance of at least 90 days.

 

AMLI also requests that the disputes be grouped into four bifurcated trials: (1) sewer; (2) railings; (3) emergency lighting; and (4) subcontractor claims, but explicitly requests that the sewer claims be tried first, followed by the railings and/or emergency lighting trials, under the theory that once it becomes clear that WNB’s liability exceeds the amount of the retention, the mechanic’s lien cases should resolve easily, as the only issue will be the amount of payment from WNB to each subcontractor, potentially avoiding mechanic’s lien trials altogether.

 

Six subcontractors with mechanic’s lien/collection cases oppose consolidation, instead seeking a resolution of their claims which are otherwise unrelated to the sewer, railing, or emergency lighting claims: Jem, Hale, Dino’s, Royal, Mon Arc, and Sound-Crete.  Regency Lighting also takes the position that its case is a simple collection case, however Regency Lighting’s claims appear related to the emergency lighting issues.

 

Railcraft’s primary concern is that the railing claims are relatively new and require more time to prepare for trial, including completion of non-expert and expert discovery.

 

AGI does not oppose a trial continuance, so long as there is a commensurate agreement to extend the five year deadline, however AGI opposes severing Railcraft’s claims, because AGI wants to ensure that Railcraft and the other railing and lighting defendants pay their fair share of AMLI’s consequential damages.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Court’s Authority re Consolidation/Bifurcation

 

Code of Civil Procedure Section 598 provides:

 

The court may, when the convenience of witnesses, the ends of justice, or the economy and efficiency of handling the litigation would be promoted thereby, on motion of a party, after notice and hearing, make an order, no later than the close of pretrial conference in cases in which such pretrial conference is to be held, or, in other cases, no later than 30 days before the trial date, that the trial of any issue or any part thereof shall precede the trial of any other issue or any part thereof in the case, except for special defenses which may be tried first pursuant to Sections 597 and 597.5. The court, on its own motion, may make such an order at any time.

 

Similarly, Code of Civil Procedure section 1048 provides:

 

(a) When actions involving a common question of law or fact are pending before the court, it may order a joint hearing or trial of any or all the matters in issue in the actions; it may order all the actions consolidated and it may make such orders concerning proceedings therein as may tend to avoid unnecessary costs or delay.

 

(b) The court, in furtherance of convenience or to avoid prejudice, or when separate trials will be conducive to expedition and economy, may order a separate trial of any cause of action, including a cause of action asserted in a cross-complaint, or of any separate issue or of any number of causes of action or issues, preserving the right of trial by jury required by the Constitution or a statute of this state or of the United States.

 

Per Local Rule 3.3, subdivision (g), “Cases may not be consolidated unless they are in the same department.  A motion to consolidate two or more cases may be noticed and heard after the cases, initially filed in different departments, have been related into a single department, or if the cases were already assigned to that department.”  (Super. Ct. L.A. County, Local Rules, rule 3.3(g)(1).)  Once the Court relates the cases, the Court may consolidate the actions and order a joint trial on matters that “involve[e] a common question of law or fact.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1048, subd. (a).)

 

Here, the Court makes its ruling only as to those cases that have already been related and are currently pending in Department 207. 

 

5-Year Deadline

 

            Code of Civil Procedure section 583.310 requires “[a]n action shall be brought to trial within five years after the action is commenced against the defendant.”

 

In computing the time within which an action must be brought to trial pursuant to this article, there shall be excluded the time during which any of the following conditions existed:

 

(a)   The jurisdiction of the court to try the action was suspended.

 

(b)   Prosecution or trial of the action was stayed or enjoined.

 

(c)   Bringing the action to trial, for any other reason, was impossible, impracticable, or futile.

 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 583.340.)

 

The parties may extend the time within which an action must be brought to trial pursuant to this article by the following means:

 

(a)   By written stipulation.  The stipulation need not be filed but, if it is not filed, the stipulation shall be brought to the attention of the court if relevant to a motion for dismissal.

 

(b)   By oral agreement made in open court, if entered in the minutes of the court or a transcript is made.

 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 583.330.)

 

On July 24, 2020, the Court signed Case Management Order #1, pursuant to which discovery was phased and the parties then involved in the litigation (AGI, AMLI, WNB, R3, Hall & Foreman, Geotechnical, and Boudreau) purported to stipulate to extend the five-year deadline to bring cases to trial by the time from the date of the first Case Management Order to the date Phase II of discovery begins, however, the version of the stipulation submitted to (and signed by) the Court is not signed by any of the parties.  

 

The Court notes that a partial stay of discovery is insufficient to extend the five-year deadline pursuant to subdivision (b).  (See Warner Bros. Entertainment Inc. v. Superior Court (2018) 29 Cal.App.5th 243, 255.)

 

On June 7, 2022, the Court signed the parties’ signed stipulation and case management order, which again contained a clause waiving the five-year deadline for the period of time from the enactment of the first Case Management Order to the enactment of the second Case Management Order.  It was signed by AGI, AMLI, WNB, Hall & Foreman, Geotechnical, Boudreau, R-3, Hale Glass, Jem, Royal, Rockwell, Pacific, LDI, Griffin Dewatering, Dino’s, Silverline, and True Line.

 

Further, Emergency Order 10 extends the five-year deadline by an additional six months, to cases that were ongoing during the COVID-19 pandemic, which interrupted court operations.

 

The parties have slightly differing calculations for the current 5-year deadline.  Western National and Boudreau believe the deadline is July 9, 2024.  (See WNB’s Notice of Partial Joinder at p. 4; Boudreau’s Notice of Limited Joinder at p. 2.)  By contrast, AGI contends the deadline is July 11, 2024.  (See AGI’s Opposition at p. 7.)

 

The original complaint was filed on February 28, 2017.  Therefore, by operation of the original 5-year rule, to bring the case to trial “within five years,” the case was required to be brought to trial on or before February 27, 2022.  That period was extended by an additional 6 months by virtue of Emergency Order 10, making the new deadline August 27, 2022.  Then, by stipulation of the parties, the deadline was further extended by the 683 days between July 24, 2020 when CMO # 1 was entered and June 7, 2022, when CMO # 2 was entered (including July 24, 2020, but excluding June 7, 2022), making the current deadline to bring the case to trial July 10, 2024. 

 

Without the parties stipulating to extend the 5-year deadline, the Court finds the current deadline to be determinative as to when the various claims shall be tried, especially the sewer claims.

 

Consequential Damages

 

The Court finds AGI’s consequential damages argument for postponing the mechanic’s lien cases unrelated to the sewer, railing, or emergency lighting claims until all other claims have first been resolved to be unavailing.  As the Court has previously held, the subcontractors are generally entitled to speedy payment of their valid claims, and AMLI and WNB may not withhold such payments based solely on the dispute between them.  (See Aug 10, 2023 Minute Order [granting summary judgment in favor of Silverline and summary adjudication in favor of Pacific].)  Moreover, an allocation of the consequential damages would need to occur regardless of whether the trials are severed and phased or held together.  The Court sees no reason why proportionate consequential damages cannot be tried separately.

 

However, as a practical matter, especially in light of the impending five-year deadline regarding the sewer claims, as well as the fact that the trial related to the sewer claims may be designated long-cause, the Court and parties must tend to the matter of setting the sewer-related claims for trial first.

 

Consolidation

 

The Court also notes that the three cases that have been related, but not yet consolidated, 22SMCV00819 (Sound-Crete), 22STCV25157 (Lonestar), and 22SMCV01315 (Regency). 

 

As such, on the Court’s own motion, these actions shall be consolidated with SC127147 for discovery and case management purposes only under Code of Civil Procedure section 1048.  

 

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

 

Having reviewed the parties’ respective positions, the Court determines that judicial economy will be best served by structuring the cases as follows:

 

·         The sewer claims, along with the associated mechanic’s liens, (“Sewer Claims”) shall be severed and consolidated for trial as set on March 11, 2024 with a Final Status Conference set on February 23, 2024. 

 

Due to the practical concerns with referring the Sewer Claims for a long cause trial to Department 1, the Court cannot further continue the current trial date and ensure that the case will be brought to trial prior to the current 5-year deadline of July 10, 2024. 

 

In preparation for the Final Status Conference, parties shall comply with the Long Cause Trial Guidelines.  All trial related papers in conformance with the Long Cause Trial Guidelines shall be filed no later than five (5) court days before the Final Status Conference.  Upon confirmation that the parties have complied with the Long Cause Trial Guidelines and the Court’s determination that Sewer and Lighting Claims are ready for trial, the Court will order the parties to file a “Notice of Long Cause Submission to Department 1,” and lodge a courtesy copy of the notice directly in Department 1 at the Stanley Mosk Courthouse (Supervising Judge Michelle Williams Court).  Thereafter, Department 1 will set a non-appearance case review to determine if parties have complied with the Long Cause Trial Guidelines and are ready for the long cause trial.

 

·         The railing claims, along with the associated mechanic’s liens, (“Railing Claims”) shall be severed and consolidated for trial.  The Court sets a Trial Setting Conference regarding the Railing Claims on March 1, 2024 at 8:30 A.M. in Department 207.  Parties associated with the Railing Claims shall meet and confer, and prepare a joint report regarding trial readiness, including detailed information regarding the status of discovery and the time estimate for a jury trial.  The joint report shall be filed with the Court on or before February 23, 2024. 

 

·         The emergency lighting claims, along with the associated mechanic’s liens, (“Lighting Claims”) shall be bifurcated and consolidated for trial.  The Court sets a Trial Setting Conference regarding the Lighting Claims on March 1, 2024 at 8:30 A.M. in Department 207.  Parties associated such mechanic’s liens shall meet and confer, and prepare a joint report regarding trial readiness, including detailed information regarding the status of discovery and the time estimate for a jury trial.  The joint report shall be filed with the Court on or before February 23, 2024.

 

·         All other mechanic’s liens (not associated with the Sewer, Lighting or Railing claims) shall be severed and consolidated for trial.  The Court sets a Trial Setting Conference regarding such mechanic’s liens on March 1, 2024 at 8:30 A.M. in Department 207.  Parties associated such mechanic’s liens shall meet and confer, and prepare a joint report regarding trial readiness including detailed information regarding the status of discovery and the time estimate for a jury trial.  The joint report shall be filed with the Court on or before February 23, 2024. 

 

With respect to the Railing and Lighting Claims, the Court anticipates, subject to further consideration,  that the deadline(s) for non-expert and expert discovery shall correspond with the new trial date(s) when set at the upcoming trial setting conferences. 

 

With respect to all other claims and mechanic’s liens, the discovery deadline set forth in the operative Case Management Order stands.
