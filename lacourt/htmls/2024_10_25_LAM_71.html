DEPARTMENT 71 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

        All parties are urged to meet and confer with all parties concerning this tentative ruling to see if they can reach an agreed-upon resolution of their matter.  If you are able to reach an agreement, please notify the courtroom staff in advance of the hearing if you wish to submit on the tentative ruling rather than argue the motion by notifying the court by e-mailing the court at: SMCDEPT71@lacourt.org. Do not click on the email address, either copy and paste it or type it into your email.  Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the Case Number in the Subject line.  In the body of the email, please provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, and whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, intervenor, or non-party, etc.


            Please be advised that if you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may still appear at the hearing and argue the matter, and the court could change its tentative based upon the argument.  Unless you receive a submission from all other parties in the matter, you should assume that others might appear at the hearing to argue.  If you submit, but still intend to appear, include the words "SUBMITS, BUT WILL APPEAR" in the Subject line.    


            If you elect to argue your matter, you are urged to do so remotely, via Court-Connect. If the moving party fails to appear and/or submit to the Court’s tentative ruling, the Court will take the  matter off calendar.
                          
            Note that once the Court has issued a tentative, the Court has the inherent authority not to allow the withdrawal of a motion and to adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the court.   
 

            If you submitted a courtesy copy of your papers containing media (such as a DVD or thumb drive), unless you request the return of the media in your papers, the court will destroy it following the hearing of your matter.

Barboza v. Howard Management

20STCV48250

Hearing on Order to Show Cause re Entry of Judgment

 

            On August 19, 2024, the Court granted Defendant’s unopposed motion to enforce the settlement entered into between it and Plaintiff on Augst 4, 2024.  Pursuant to the settlement, Defendant agreed that Plaintiff would be paid $195,000.  The Court ordered Defendant to submit a judgment within 10 days and set a hearing on an Order to Show Cause regarding the entry of the judgment on September 25, 2024.

            At the September 25, 2025,  hearing on the Order to Show Cause, Plaintiff did not appear, only Defendant’s counsel did.  The Court continued the hearing on the Order to Show Cause to October 25, 2024.

            Defendant filed a proposed judgment on October 8, 2024, along with a Declaration from Christina Newell, Esq. re her attempts to communicate with Plaintiff re same.   On October 10, 2024, Plaintiff filed a” Request for Purposed Order” in which she outlines her objections to the settlement. 

            The Court finds that the Judgment in this matter should simply be entered in favor of Plaintiff and against the settling Defendants in the amount of $195,000.  The settling Defendants can then issue checks paying the judgment in accord with the identified liens.

BLACKBURN HOLDINGS, LLC, 

 

         vs.

 

JEFFREY WEISS, et al.

 Case No.:  23STCV04271

 

 

 

 Hearing Date:  October 25, 2024

 

Cross-Complainant Robert Yager’s motion to compel further responses from Cross-Defendant Blackburn Holdings, LLC to his Request for Production of Documents (Set One) is granted.   Blackburn is to produce responsive documents within 20 days of this ruling.

Cross-Complainant Robert Yager’s request for sanctions is granted against Cross-Defendant Blackburn Holdings, LLC, and its counsel of record, Robert Gentino, jointly and severally, in the reduced amount of $1,868.91, payable within 20 days of this ruling.

Cross-Complainant Robert Yager’s unopposed motion to compel further responses from Cross-Defendant Blackburn Holdings, LLC to his Form Interrogatory No. 15.1 is granted.  Blackburn is ordered to provide Code-compliant responses to FROG 1, Request No. 15.1, within 20 days of this ruling.

Cross-Complainant’s request for sanctions is granted against Blackburn Holdings, LLC, and its counsel of record, Robert Gentino, jointly and severally, in the reduced amount of $2,168.91, payable within 20 days of this ruling.

 

Cross-Complainant Robert Yager (“Yager”) (“Cross-Complainant”) moves to compel further responses from Cross-Defendant Blackburn Holdings, LLC (“Blackburn”) (“Cross-Defendant”) to his Request for Production of Documents (Set One) (“RFP 1”) and requests sanctions against Blackburn and its attorney of record, Robert Gentino, in the amount of $3,908.91.  (Notice Motion RFP 1, pgs. 1-2; C.C.P. §§2023.010, 2023.030, 2031.290, 2031.300, 2031.320.)

Cross-Complainant moves unopposed to compel further responses from Blackburn to his Form Interrogatories (Set One) (“FROG 1”) Request No. 15.1 and requests sanctions against Blackburn and its attorney of record, Robert Gentino, in the amount of $3,968.91.  (Notice Motion FROG 1, pgs. 1-2; C.C.P. §§2023.010, 2023.030, 2030.300.)

 

Meet and Confer

On June 5, 2024, the parties engaged in an IDC before this Court, regarding Plaintiff’s RFP 1 and FROG 1, Request No. 15.1 and the issues were deemed resolved.  (6/5/24 Minute Order; 6/4/24 Defendant’s Notice of Outcome Re IDC.)

 

1.     Motion to Compel Further RFP 1

Having reviewed Cross-Complainant’s Motion to Compel Further Responses from Blackburn to Cross-Complainant’s RFP 1, the Court rules as follows:

          On April 12, 2024, Cross-Complainant served RFP 1 on Blackburn.  (Decl. of Riley ¶5, Exh. 2.)  Blackburn’s response was due on or before May 14, 2024.  On May 14, 2024, Blackburn served its untimely response that was unaccompanied by a proof of service and contained a single objection to the entire set of production.  (Decl. of Riley ¶6, Exh. 3.)

On July 16, 2024, Blackburn served its further verified response, which failed to produce documents requested.  (Decl. of Riley ¶12, Exh. 8.)  On July 27, 2024, Blackburn served a second supplemental response that also failed to produce documents in response to RFP 1.  (Decl. of Riley ¶15, Exh. 11.)  As of the date of this motion no production has been made.  (Decl. of Riley ¶16.)  Cross-Complainant now moves to compel further responses. 

          Cross-Complainant’s motion to compel further responses from Blackburn to RFP 1 is granted.  Blackburn failed to produce responsive documents in accordance with its statement of compliance.  Blackburn also waived all objections by failing to timely respond to Cross-Complainant’s RFP 1.  Blackburn is ordered to produce all responsive documents within 20 days of this ruling.

Cross-Complainant requests monetary sanctions in the amount of $3,908.91 against Blackburn and its counsel of record, Robert Gentino (“Gentino”).  In Blackburn’s untimely opposition, Gentino declares that “[o]n August 14, 2024, I received an email from Mr. Riley with no attachments and only a Dropbox Link” and “I have never given consent to Mr. Riley, nor any other counsel, to serve court filings by Dropbox due to my limited Dropbox storage.”  (Decl. of Gentino ¶¶1-2.)  The Court questions why Plaintiff’s counsel continues to serve Gentino via Dropbox links after Gentino explicitly asked for documents to not be delivered in this manner, and further why Plaintiff’s counsel cannot simply attach the documents as a PDF attachment to an email.  However, Gentino demonstrates that he took no proactive measures for two months to receive a copy of the PDF document.

          The Court awards sanctions against Blackburn and its counsel pursuant to C.C.P. §§2023.010(d), 2023.010(e), and 2031.310(h) in the reduced amount of $1,868.91, calculated as follows:

([2.5 hours to prepare instant motion + 0.5 hour to attend hearing on motion] x $600.00) + $68.91 filing fee = $1,868.91

The sanctions are payable, jointly and severally, by Blackburn and its attorney, Robert Gentino, within 20 days of this order.

Moving Party is to give notice of this ruling.

 

2.     Motion to Compel Further FROG 1

Having reviewed Cross-Complainant’s Motion to Compel Further Responses from Blackburn to Cross-Complainant’s FROG 1, Response No. 15.1, the Court rules as follows:

          On April 12, 2024, Cross-Complainant served FROG 1 on Blackburn.  (Decl. of Riley ¶5, Exh. 2.)  Blackburn’s response was due May 14, 2024.  On May 28, 2024, Blackburn served its untimely response.  (Decl. of Riley ¶6, Exh. 3.)  Blackburn’s response did not include a proof of service or separate responses as to each affirmative defense as required by the Code.  (See Decl. of Riley ¶6, Exh. 3.) 

On July 16, 2024, Blackburn served its supplementary response to FROG 1, Request No. 15.1, and did not include separate responses as to each affirmative defense as required by the Code.  (See Decl. of Riley ¶12, Exh. 8.) 

On July 27, 2024, Blackburn served a second supplemental response that still lacks separate responses as to each affirmative defense.  (See Decl. of Riley ¶15, Exh. 11.)  Cross-Complainant now moves to compel further responses. 

          Cross-Complainant’s motion to compel further Code-compliant responses from Blackburn to FROG 1, Request No. 15.1 is granted.  Blackburn waived all objections by failing to timely respond to Cross-Complainant’s FROG 1.  Blackburn is ordered to provide Code-compliant responses to FROG 1, Request No. 15.1, within 20 days of this ruling.

Cross-Complainant requests monetary sanctions in the amount of $3,968.91 against Blackburn and its counsel of record, Robert Gentino.  In light of the fact that the instant motion is unopposed, the Court declines to grant Cross-Complainant’s request for fees in anticipation of reviewing an opposition and drafting a reply.

          The Court awards sanctions against Blackburn and its counsel pursuant to C.C.P. §§2023.010(d), 2023.010(e), and 2030.300(d) in the reduced amount of $2,168.91, calculated as follows:

([3.0 hours to prepare instant motion + 0.5 hour to attend hearing on motion] x $600.00) + $68.91 filing fee = $2,168.91

The sanctions are payable, jointly and severally, by Blackburn and its attorney, Robert Gentino, within 20 days of this order.

Moving Party is to give notice of this ruling.

TENTATIVE RULING

 

BANK OF HOPE, 

 

         vs.

 

GREEN MASTERS BUILDING SERVICE, INC., et al.

 Case No.:  23STCV31463

 

 

 

 

 Hearing Date:  October 25, 2024

 

The Court denies Plaintiff’s default judgment packet. 

The Court sets a hearing on an order to show cause why the complaint should not be dismissed and/or Plaintiff’s counsel sanctioned $250 for failing to enter default judgment (California Rule of Court, rule 3.110(h)) on November 26, 2024, at 8:30 AM in Department 71 at Stanley Mosk Courthouse for the following reasons:

1.     No interest calculation provided.  The proposed judgment indicates that interest is sought at a rate of 15%.  Breach of contract claims have a default prejudgment interest rate of 10% (unless a different rate is specified in the contract).  (See Civ. Code §3289(b); Cal. Const., art. XV, §1.)  Plaintiff fails to demonstrate why the interest rate is 15% for a breach of contract claim, and where the contract provides for a 15% interest rate.
