County of Los Angeles

Department 50

                        Plaintiffs,

            vs.

38551 AND 38661 11th STREET EAST LLC, et al.,

 

                        Defendants.

22STCV34281

September 23, 2024

Hearing Time:

10:00 a.m.

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

 

DEFENDANTS’ MOTION TO CONTINUE TRIAL AND RELATED DATES

 

 

Background

On October 25, 2022, Plaintiffs Kristina Sanders, Cassandra Robinson, Sarah Sims, Giselle Moreno, and Tiannah Jojola filed this action against Defendants 38551 and 38661 11th Street East LLC, Pinkal Jogani, and Ariel Ramirez (collectively, “Defendants”). The Complaint alleges ten causes of action.  

Defendants now move for a trial continuance “on the grounds that defense counsel is not available for trial on October 16, 2024 due to conflicts, the current trial date and related cut-offs are rapidly approaching and defendants desire to continue trial in order to participate in mediation/engage in settlement discussions, to complete necessary discovery including the depositions of parties and witnesses, or alternatively have sufficient time to prepare for trial prior to the discovery cut-off.” Defendants “request the trial date and all discovery, motion and other deadlines related to the trial be continued from October 16, 2024 to February 17, 2025 or a date thereafter convenient for the Court.” The motion is unopposed.  

Discussion

            As an initial matter, the Court notes that on September 4, 2024 the Court issued a minute order in this matter providing, inter alia, as follows:

 

“The Ex Parte Application TO CONTINUE TRIAL AND RELATED DATES OR ALTERNATIVELY ADVANCE THE HEARING ON DEFENDANTS’ MOTION filed by 38551 AND 38661 11th STREET EAST LLC, Pinkal Jogani, Ariel Ramirez on 08/15/2024 is Granted.

 

Pursuant to the request of moving party, the Non-Appearance Case Review trial readiness and exhibit binders must be lodged in Department 50 by 4:00p.m. scheduled for 09/30/2024 is continued to 04/03/25 at 04:00 PM in Department 50 at Stanley Mosk Courthouse.

 

Pursuant to the request of moving party, the Final Status Conference scheduled for 10/04/2024 is continued to 04/04/25 at 02:00 PM in Department 50 at Stanley Mosk Courthouse.

 

Pursuant to the request of moving party, the Jury Trial scheduled for 10/16/2024 is continued to 04/16/25 at 09:30 AM in Department 50 at Stanley Mosk Courthouse.

 

All dates and deadlines to be based off the new trial date.”

In light of the foregoing, Defendants’ instant motion to continue trial and related dates is moot.  

Conclusion

Based on the foregoing, Defendants’ motion to continue trial and related dates is denied as moot.

Defendants are ordered to give notice of this ruling.

 

DATED:  September 23, 2024                                                          

________________________________

Hon. Teresa A. Beaudet

Judge, Los Angeles Superior Court

Background

Plaintiff Torrance Flite Park, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed this action on January 18, 2024 against Defendants Donald Orval Shaw and Advanced Tactics, Inc. (jointly, “Defendants”).

On April 4, 2024, default was entered against Donald Orval Shaw. On April 9, 2024, default was entered against Advanced Tactics, Inc.

Defendants now move “for an order setting aside the default [sic] entered against them…” No opposition to the motion was filed.

Discussion

Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b) provides in pertinent part:

 

“The court may, upon any terms as may be just, relieve a party or his or her legal representative from a judgment, dismissal, order, or other proceeding taken against him or her through his or her mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect. Application for this relief shall be accompanied by a copy of the answer or other pleading proposed to be filed therein, otherwise the application shall not be granted, and shall be made within a reasonable time, in no case exceeding six months, after the judgment, dismissal, order, or proceeding was taken.”

 “[B]ecause the law strongly favors trial and disposition on the merits, any doubts in applying section 473 must be resolved in favor of the party seeking relief from default.” (Elston v. City of Turlock (1985) 38 Cal.3d 227, 233 [negative treatment on other grounds].) Where the party in default moves promptly to seek relief, and no prejudice to the opposing party will result from setting aside the default, “very slight evidence will be required to justify a court in setting aside the default.” (Ibid.)

In support of the motion, Defendants submit the Declaration of Donald Shaw, the owner of Advanced Tactics. (Shaw Decl., ¶ 2.) Mr. Shaw states that he “was also its Chief Executive Officer until in or about late 2023, which [sic] Christoper Anderson was hired to serve as a consultant and fill that role.” (Shaw Decl., ¶ 2.) Defendants also submit the Declaration of Christopher Anderson, who states that he “was installed into the role of Chief Executive Officer for purposes of addressing a number of issues Advanced Tactics was then facing, including the issues raised in this suit.” (Anderson Decl., ¶ 4.)

In his declaration, Mr. Anderson states that “[p]rior to and after the filing of this suit, I was engaged in numerous conversations with Hazzan Izad, the owner of Torrance Flite Park, LLC. Within these communications, Mr. Izad and I made a variety proposals and counterproposals to resolve the dispute.” (Anderson Decl., ¶ 5.) Mr. Anderson states that “even after the suit was served, I continued to negotiate with Mr. Izad. During these conversations, I was led by Mr. Izad to believe the parties would be able to reach an agreement that would result in the lawsuit being dropped. Ms. Izad told me, in substance, he had authorized the filings of the suit solely as a backstop in order to help keep our negotiations on track.” (Anderson Decl., ¶ 7.)

In his supporting declaration, Mr. Shaw states that “[d]ue to our belief the issues with Plaintiff would be resolved, we made the mistake of directing our efforts and attention primarily to the negotiations with Mr. Izad and the consolidation of the facilities we were using…It was not my intent to allow a default or default judgment to be entered against either me or Advanced Tactics. Rather, throughout, we were working to resolve the issues with Torrance Flite Park so that the lawsuit would be dismissed.” (Shaw Decl., ¶¶ 8-9.)

            Mr. Anderson states that “I now understand that the deadline to file a response to the lawsuit was April 1, 2024. Torrance Flite apparent [sic] first attempted to obtain a default on April 2, 2024, without any warning. To wit, though I had been in contact with Mr. Izad, he never provided the courtesy of letting me know that Torrance Flite would attempt on the first possible day to obtain entry of defaults.” (Anderson Decl., ¶ 12.) Mr. Shaw states that “[u]pon learning that the responsive pleading deadline had passed and that Torrance Flite Park was attempting obtain a default, we immediately retained counsel…Advanced Tactics and I retained the Maloney Firm at approximately the close of business on April 9, 2024 and completed the retention of the firm the next day, April 10, 2024. The Maloney Firm filed Answers for both Mr. Shaw and Advanced Tactics on April 10, 2024.” (Shaw Decl., ¶¶ 11-12.)

            Mr. Shaw states that “[i]n sum, Advanced Tactics and I missed the deadline to file responsive pleadings because their [sic] efforts were focused on attempting to resolve this dispute directly with Plaintiff’s principal, Mr. Izad. I did not intend to allow a default to be taken, nor did I allow the defaults to be taken for strategic or other purposes. Rather, I simply made the honest mistake of believing the negotiations with Mr. Izad had been fruitful, directing their efforts to complying with requests made within those negotiations. Had I realized that a default would be taken, I and Advanced Tactics would have changed course, abandoned the negotiations, and directed their efforts to locating counsel at an earlier date.” (Shaw Decl., ¶ 13.)

In light of the foregoing, the Court finds that Defendants have shown that their defaults were taken as a result of their “mistake” or “surprise.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b).) In addition, Defendants lodged an Answer to Complaint with the instant motion. Further, the instant motion was filed on August 14, 2024, less than six months after Defendants’ defaults were entered on April 4, 2024 and April 9, 2024.

Based on the foregoing, and in light of the lack of any opposition, the Court finds that Defendants have demonstrated good cause under Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b) for the Court to set aside the defaults entered against them.   

Conclusion

Based on the foregoing, Defendants’ motion for an order setting aside the defaults is granted. The default entered against Donald Orval Shaw on April 4, 2024 is ordered set aside. The default entered against Advanced Tactics, Inc. on April 9, 2024 is ordered set aside.  

Defendants are ordered to file their Answer to Complaint within 10 days of the date of this Order.¿ The Court sets a case management conference on December 4, 2024 at 10 a.m. in Dept. 50.

Defendants are ordered to give notice of this Order.

SPECIALLY APPEARING DEFENDANTS’ AMENDED MOTION TO QUASH SUMMONS

 

Background

Plaintiff Shiloh Parker (“Plaintiff”) filed this action on March 27, 2024 against Defendants Haley Freiberger and Kyle Freiberger. The Complaint alleges causes of action for

(1) “fraud by intentional misrepresentation,” (2) “fraud by negligent misrepresentation,”

(3) “breach of good faith and fair dealing covenant,” (4) violation of Business and Professions Code section 17200, et seq., and (5) breach of oral contract.[1]

“Specially Appearing Defendants” Haley Freiberger and Kyle Freiberger (jointly, the “Freibergers”) now move for an order quashing the service of the Summons and Complaint. Plaintiff opposes.

            Discussion

A.             Procedural Issues

As an initial matter, in the reply, the Freibergers assert that Plaintiff’s opposition is procedurally defective in multiple ways.   

First, the Freibergers note that the opposition was untimely filed and served. Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 1005, subdivision (b), “[a]ll papers opposing a motion so noticed shall be filed with the court and a copy served on each party at least nine court days, and all reply papers at least five court days before the hearing.” In addition, “[a]ny period of notice, or any right or duty to do any act or make any response within any period or on a date certain after the service of the document, which time period or date is prescribed by statute or rule of court, shall be extended after service by electronic means by two court days…” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1010.6, subd. (a)(3)(B).)

Here, Plaintiff’s opposition to the instant motion was filed on September 12, 2024. The proof of service filed with Plaintiff’s opposition indicates that the opposition was served by facsimile, e-mail, and mail on September 12, 2024. However, the hearing on the instant motion was set for September 23, 2024.[2] Nine court days prior to September 23, 2024 is September 10, 2024. Two court days prior to September 10, 2024 is September 6, 2024. As set forth above, Plaintiff’s opposition was served by, inter alia, electronic transmission after September 6, 2024, on September 12, 2024. The Freibergers note that they were accordingly “left…with two court days to respond to [Plaintiff’s] lengthy Opposition.” (Reply at p. 3:10-11.) The Court notes that five court days prior to the September 23, 2024 hearing is September 16, 2024 (the deadline to file a reply under Code Civ. Proc., § 1005, subd. (b)).  

Moreover, the Freibergers note that Plaintiff’s opposition well exceeds the applicable page limit. Pursuant to California Rules of Court, rule 3.1113, subdivision (d), “[e]xcept in a summary judgment or summary adjudication motion, no opening or responding memorandum may exceed 15 pages. In a summary judgment or summary adjudication motion, no opening or responding memorandum may exceed 20 pages. No reply or closing memorandum may exceed 10 pages. The page limit does not include the caption page, the notice of motion and motion, exhibits, declarations, attachments, the table of contents, the table of authorities, or the proof of service.” (Emphasis added.) Here, Plaintiff’s memorandum of points and authorities in support of the opposition consists of 24 pages. The Freibergers assert that this is “unfair to the Specially Appearing Defendants who are left with only ten (10) pages on Reply to respond to Plaintiff’s improperly lengthy Opposition.” (Reply at p. 3:16-18.) Moreover, Plaintiff does not appear to cite any Court order authorizing her to include additional pages in her opposition. 

The Freibergers also assert that “in her Opposition, Plaintiff includes the…Motion to Strike that she withdrew and…that was vacated and asks the Court to grant it. See Opposition, pgs. 10-20, 30. Plaintiff’s inclusion of the previously vacated Motion to Strike is improper and in direct contradiction to the Court’s orders at the August 12, 2024, hearing.” (Reply at p. 3:22-25.) However, the Court’s August 12, 2024 minute order in this matter provides, inter alia, that “Plaintiff withdraws the Motion to Strike and will address the issues raised therein via the opposition to the Motion to Quash.”

In any event, in light of the untimeliness of Plaintiff’s opposition and the fact that the opposition well exceeds the page limit set forth in California Rules of Court, rule 3.1113, subdivision (d), the Court continues the hearing on the instant motion.

Conclusion

In light of the foregoing issues, the Court continues the hearing on the Freibergers’ motion to quash to ____________, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. in Dept. 50.

On or before ____________, 2024, Plaintiff is to file amended opposition papers that comply with the page number requirement set forth in California Rules of Court, rule 3.1113, subdivision (d). Courtesy copies shall be delivered to Dept. 50. No new evidence or new argument is to be submitted in Plaintiff’s amended opposition papers.

On or before ____________, 2024, the Freibergers may file a new reply brief that will supersede the previously filed reply. Courtesy copies of any new reply brief shall be delivered to Dept. 50.
