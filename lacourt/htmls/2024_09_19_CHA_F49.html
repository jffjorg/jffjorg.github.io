DEPARTMENT F49 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Counsel wishing to submit on a tentative ruling may inform the clerk or courtroom assistant in North Valley Department F49, 9425 Penfield Ave., Chatsworth, CA 91311, at (818) 407-2249.  Please be aware that unless all parties submit, the matter will still be called for hearing and may be argued by any appearing/non-submitting parties. If the matter is submitted on the court's tentative ruling by all parties, counsel for moving party shall give notice of ruling. This may be done by incorporating verbatim the court's tentative ruling. The tentative ruling may be extracted verbatim by copying and pasting, as unformatted text, from the Los Angeles Superior Court’s website, http://www.lasuperiorcourt.org.
All hearings on law and motion and other calendar matters are generally NOT transcribed by a court reporter unless one is provided by the party(ies). 


Case Number: 23CHCV00262    Hearing Date: September 19, 2024    Dept: F49

Dept. F49

Date: 9/19/24

Case Name: Taylor Peacock and Austin Smith v. Ford Motor Company, AutoNation Ford Valencia, and Does 1 through 10

Case No. 23CHCV00262

 

LOS ANGELES SUPERIOR COURT

NORTH VALLEY DISTRICT

DEPARTMENT F49

 

SEPTEMBER 19, 2024

 

MOTION TO DEEM ADMITTED OR IN THE ALTERNATIVE TO COMPEL PLAINTIFF AUSTIN SMITH TO PROVIDE RESPONSES, WITHOUT OBJECTION, TO REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION, SET ONE; REQUEST FOR SANCTIONS [1839]

Los Angeles Superior Court Case No. 23CHCV00262

 

Motion filed: 5/6/24

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendant AutoNation Ford Valencia

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff Austin Smith

NOTICE: OK.

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order from this Court to deem admitted, or in the alternative, to compel Plaintiff Austin Smith to provide responses, without objection, to Requests for Admission, Set One, within 15 days, and imposing monetary sanctions against Plaintiff Austin Smith and his attorney of record, in the amount of $2,102.50.

 

TENTATIVE RULING: The motion is GRANTED IN PART. The request for monetary sanctions is GRANTED IN PART.

 

BACKGROUND

 

Plaintiffs Taylor Peacock (“Peacock”) and Austin Smith (“Smith”) (collectively “Plaintiffs”) brought this action under Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act (Lemon Law), Consumer Legal Remedies Act, and Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act over alleged defects in their 2020 Ford Expedition (the “Subject Vehicle”), which was manufactured by Defendant Ford Motor Company (“FMC”). Plaintiffs entered into a warranty contract with Defendant FMC on or about December 19, 2020.

 

On January 31, 2023, Plaintiffs initiated this action. Subsequently, on May 26, 2023, the Department F47 Court sustained a demurrer filed by Defendant FMC on April 10, 2023.

 

On June 23, 2023, Plaintiffs filed their First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) against Defendants FMC and AutoNation Ford Valencia (“AutoNation Ford”), and Does 1 through 10, alleging the following causes of action: (1) Violation of Subdivision (d) of Civil Code Section 1793.2, (2) Violation of Subdivision (b) of Civil Code Section 1793.2, (3) Violation of Subdivision (a)(3) of Civil Code Section 1793.2, (4) Breach of Implied Warranty of Merchantability (Civ. Code §§ 1791.1, 1794, 1795.5), (5) Fraudulent Inducement – Concealment, (6) Negligent Repair, (7) Violation of Consumer Legal Remedies Act (Civ. Code § 1750 et seq.), and (8) Violation of the Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act. The First through Fifth, and Seventh through Eighth Causes of Action are alleged against Defendant FMC. The Sixth Cause of Action is alleged against AutoNation Ford. Subsequently, on December 5, 2023, the Department F47 Court sustained FMC’s demurrer to the Third and Fifth Causes of Action.

 

On May 6, 2024, AutoNation Ford filed the instant Motion to Deem the Requests for Admission, Set One, Admitted. (the “Motion”).

 

Subsequently, on September 5, 2024, Smith filed his Opposition to the Motion, and AutoNation Ford replied on September 12, 2024.

 

ANALYSIS

 

“Any party may obtain discovery . . . by a written request that any other party to the action admit the genuineness of specified documents, or the truth of specified matters of fact, opinion relating to fact, or application of law to fact. A request for admission may relate to a matter that is in controversy between the parties.”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.010.)¿ “Within 30 days after service of requests for admission, the party to whom the requests are directed shall serve the original of the response to them on the requesting party, and a copy of the response on all other parties who have appeared ....”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.250, subd. (a).)¿

 

If a party to whom request for admissions is served fails to provide a timely response, the party to whom the request was directed waives any objections, including based on privilege or the work product doctrine. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (a).) The requesting party can move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the request be deemed admitted, as well as for monetary sanctions. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (b).) The court shall issue this order unless the party to whom the request was made serves a response in substantial compliance prior to the hearing on the motion. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).)¿

 

A.    Motion to Deem Requests for Admission, Set One, Admitted

 

AutoNation Ford’s counsel (“Counsel”) attests that on March 7, 2024, Smith was served with the first set of Requests for Admission. (Geraghty Decl. ¶ 3, Ex. “2.”) Having not received any responses by the due date, Counsel sent Plaintiff’s counsel a written meet and confer letter on April 24, 2024, requesting Plaintiff Smith to serve his responses, without objection, by April 29, 2024. (Id. ¶ 5, Ex. “3.”) On May 6, 2024, Counsel attempted to contact Plaintiff’s counsel via telephone, but was unsuccessful.  (Id. ¶ 6.) Despite these efforts, no responses had been received from Plaintiff Smith at the time of the filing of the Motion. (Id. ¶ 8.) Subsequently, Plaintiffs’ counsel served verified discovery responses to AutoNation Ford on August 28, 2024. (Sanaia Decl. ¶ 8.)

 

Based on the above records, the Court determines that Smith failed to serve a timely response, thereby waiving any objection to the interrogatories, including those based on privilege or the protection for work product, pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (a).

 

Nevertheless, under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (a), the court, on motion, may relieve that party from this waiver on its determination that both of the following conditions are satisfied: (1) The party has subsequently served a response that is in substantial compliance with Sections 2033.210, 2033.220, and 2033.230. (2) The party’s failure to serve a timely response was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect.

 

Here, Plaintiff Smith’s counsel contends that she was not aware of outstanding discovery responses, until August 26, 2024, when the opposition to the Motion became due. (Sanaia Decl. ¶¶ 6, 10.) Plaintiffs made a good faith attempt to immediately respond to Defendant’s Discovery Requests. (Id. ¶ 7.) “The reason for this delay is due to a calendaring error. Upon further investigation, we noticed the deadline to serve Discovery Responses was not properly calendared. Calendaring Department of Plaintiffs’ Counsel is responsible for making sure that due dates and deadlines are calendared, and that Plaintiffs’ attorneys are made aware of these deadlines. Because of this inadvertent and excusable mistake made by the Calendaring Department, Plaintiffs failed to respond to Defendant’s discovery requests in a timely manner.” (Id. ¶ 9.)

 

Established case law holds that errors such as misreading a calendar do not qualify as excusable neglect absent extraordinary circumstances. (See Garcia v. Hejmadi (1997) 58 Cal.App.4th 674). Similarly, simple carelessness, lack of diligence, or neglect without reasonable justification is not excusable. (See Huh v. Wang (2007) 158 Cal.App.4th 1406 [attorney’s failure to respond to discovery due to “press of business” did not constitute excusable neglect.]) Additionally, AutoNation Ford’s counsel sent a written meet and confer letter on April 24, 2024, alerting that “[a]s of today’s date, my office has not received Plaintiffs’ discovery responses.” (Geraghty Decl. Ex. “3.”) Furthermore, the Motion papers were served upon Plaintiff’s counsel’s law firm on May 6, 2024. (5/6/2024 Proof of Service.) Consequently, the Court finds Plaintiff’s argument that the delay was solely due to calendaring errors to be unpersuasive.

 

Accordingly, the Court finds that Plaintiff’s failure to serve a timely response was not the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect. Therefore, Plaintiff is not relieved from the waiver of objections.

 

Based on the above, the Court GRANTS IN PART the Motion, deeming Plaintiff’s objections asserted in his responses dated August 28, 2024, waived.

 

Nonetheless, the Court finds the Plaintiff’s responses, although untimely, were served before the hearing on the Motion, and are in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220.

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (c), provides in pertinent part, “The court shall make this order [to deem admitted], unless it finds that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220.”

 

Therefore, the Court DENIES IN PART the Motion as to deem the Requests for Admission, Set One, admitted.

 

A.    Monetary Sanctions

 

“It is mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).)

 

Although the Court has denied in part the Motion to deem Requests for Admission, Set One, admitted, it finds that the mandatory sanctions under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (c) are still applicable in this case.

 

Given that the Motion shares significant similarities with another concurrently filed motion, the Court determines the total and reasonable amount of attorney’s fees and costs incurred for the work performed in connection with the Motion to be $435.00, calculated based on a reasonable hourly rate of $250.00 for 1.5 hours reasonably spent plus a $60.00 filing fee.

 

Therefore, the Court GRANTS IN PART Defendant AutoNation Ford’s request for monetary sanctions.

 

CONCLUSION

 

Defendant AutoNation Ford Valencia’s Motion to Deem Admitted Requests for Admission, Set One, is GRANTED IN PART and DENIED IN PART. Specifically, Plaintiff Austin Smith’s objections, asserted in her responses dated August 28, 2024, are deemed waived. However, the genuineness and truth of the matters specified in the Requests for Admission, Set One, are not deemed admitted.

 

Defendant AutoNation Ford Valencia’s Request for Monetary Sanctions is GRANTED IN PART.

 

Plaintiff Austin Smith and his attorney of record are ordered to jointly and severally pay $435.00 to Defendant AutoNation Ford Valencia within 20 days.

 

Moving party to give notice.
