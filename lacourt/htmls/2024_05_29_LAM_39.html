Case Number: 23STCP00345    Hearing Date: May 29, 2024    Dept: 39

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPT:

39

HEARING DATE:

May 29, 2024

CASE NUMBER:

23STCP00345

MOTION: 

Motions to Compel Discovery Responses

MOVING PARTY:

Judgment Creditor Jacksonville Jaguars, LLC

OPPOSING PARTY:

None

 

MOTIONS

            Judgment Creditor Jacksonville Jaguars, LLC (“Judgment Creditor”) moves in two separate motions to compel responses from Judgment Debtor Fuelster Technologies, Inc. (“Judgment Debtor”) to Special Interrogatories, set one (“SROG”) and Requests for Production, set one (“RPD”).  

ANALYSIS

Judgment Creditor served the SROG and RPD on Judgment Debtor on July 7, 2023 by mail.  Judgment Debtor’s responses were thus due by no later than August 11, 2023.  As of the filing date of these motions, Judgment Debtor has not responded to the discovery.  Accordingly, the motions to compel responses to the SROG and RPD are granted pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 2030.290 and 2031.300, respectively.  Judgment Debtor is ordered to serve verified responses to Judgment Creditor’s SROG and RPD without objections, within 30 days of service of this order.

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Judgment Creditor’s motions to compel responses to the SROG and RPD are granted.  Judgment Debtor is to serve verified responses, without objections, within 30 days of notice of this order. 

Judgment Creditor is ordered to provide notice of this order and file proof of service of same.


Case Number: 23STCV00514    Hearing Date: May 29, 2024    Dept: 39

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPARTMENT

39

HEARING DATE

May 29, 2024

CASE NUMBER

23STCV00514

MOTION

Motion for Leave to File Amended Complaint

MOVING PARTY

Plaintiff 58th St Industrial LLC

OPPOSING PARTY

None

 

MOTION

Plaintiff 58th St Industrial LLC (“Plaintiff”) moves for leave to amend its complaint.  The unopposed motion is denied without prejudice.

 

ANALYSIS

Under Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (a)(1), “The court may, in furtherance of justice, and on any terms as may be proper, allow a party to amend any pleading or proceeding by adding or striking out the name of any party, or by correcting a mistake in the name of a party, or a mistake in any other respect; and may, upon like terms, enlarge the time for answer or demurrer. The court may likewise, in its discretion, after notice to the adverse party, allow, upon any terms as may be just, an amendment to any pleading or proceeding in other particulars; and may upon like terms allow an answer to be made after the time limited by this code.” 

Judicial policy favors resolution of all disputed matters between the parties and, therefore, leave to amend is liberally granted.  (Berman vs. Bromberg (1986) 56 Cal.App.4th 936, 945.)  “[I]t is an abuse of discretion for the court to deny leave to amend where the opposing party was not misled or prejudiced by the amendment.”  (Kittredge Sports Co. v. Superior Court (1989) 213 Cal.App.3d 1045, 1048.)

Under California Rules of Court, rule 3.1324, a motion to amend a pleading must include a copy of the proposed amendment and state the allegations that the moving party proposes to add or delete.  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1324, subd. (a)(1) & (2).)  Rule 3.1324 also requires the moving party to provide a declaration specifying the effect of the amendment; why the amendment is necessary and proper; when the facts giving rise to the amended allegations were discovered; and the reasons why the request for amendment was not made earlier.  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1324, subd. (b).)

Plaintiff has submitted the Declaration of Sasha Struthers (“Counsel”) in support of the motion.  However, in that declaration Counsel does not adequately address the factors set forth in rule 3.1324.  Accordingly, the Court denies the motion without prejudice, as procedurally improper. 

Plaintiff is to give notice of this order and file proof of service of concerning it.

Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings

Defendant Oxford Grand LLC

 

MOTION

Plaintiff Heidy Garcia (“Plaintiff”) sued Defendant Oxford Grand LLC (“Defendant”) regarding an apartment Plaintiff rents from Defendant.  Plaintiff moves for judgment on the pleadings.  Code of Civil Procedure section 438 provides Plaintiff may move for judgment on the pleadings if “the complaint states facts sufficient to constitute a cause or causes of action against the defendant and [defendant’s] answer does not state facts sufficient to constitute a defense to the complaint.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 438, subd. (c)(1)(A).)  Fatal to Plaintiff’s motion is her attachment of documents representing extrinsic evidence which the Court cannot consider in ruling on a motion for judgment on the pleadings.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 438, subd. (d).)  Accordingly, the Court denies the motion.

The Court directs Defendant to file notices of related cases in Garcia v. Esmeralda (Super. Ct. L.A. County, 2023, No. 23STCV17481) and Garcia v. LA Rentals (Super. Ct. L.A. County, 2023, No. 23STCV09093) as required.  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.300, subds. (b), (f).)

Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of this order and file proof of service of same.
