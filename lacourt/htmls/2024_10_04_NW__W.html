DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 20STCV09343    Hearing Date: October 4, 2024    Dept: W

OTONIEL MARTINEZ v. SUMITOMO RUBBER

NORTH AMERICA, INC., et al.

 

plaintiff’s motion for order granting relief from potential waiver of trial by jury

 

Date of Hearing:          October 4, 2024                      Trial Date:       March 17, 2025

Department:               W                                             Case No.:         20STCV09343

 

Moving Party:             Plaintiff Otoniel Martinez

Responding Party:       Defendants Sumitomo Rubber North America, Inc. and Jacob Koo

 

BACKGROUND

 

On March 6, 2020, Plaintiff Otoniel Martinez filed a complaint against Defendants Sumitomo Rubber North America, Inc., Jacob Koo, and EAN Holdings, LLC for motor vehicle negligence and general negligence. On January 15, 2021, Plaintiff dismissed EAN Holdings, LLC.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Plaintiff’s Motion for Relief from Jury Waiver is GRANTED.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Plaintiff Otoniel Martinez moves the court for an order granting relief from Plaintiff’s waiver of a jury trial in this matter pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 631(c), (e), and (g).

 

A party can secure their right to a jury trial by timely demanding a jury trial and posting jury fees. (See CCP § 631 (b), (f)(4).) Code of Civil Procedure section 631 provides that “[a]t least one party demanding a jury on each side of a civil case shall pay a nonrefundable fee of one hundred fifty dollars ($150), unless the fee has been paid by another party on the same side of the case.” (CCP § 631(b).) The fee must be paid before the date scheduled for the initial case management conference in the action. (CCP § 631(c).) If no case management conference is scheduled in a civil action, the fee shall be due no later than 365 calendar days after the filing of the initial complaint. (CCP § 631(c)(2).) A party waives jury trial by failing to timely pay the fee, unless another party on the same side of the case has paid that fee.¿(CCP § 631(f)(5).)¿¿ 

¿ 

“The court may, in its discretion upon just terms, allow a trial by jury although there may have been a waiver of a trial by jury.” (CCP § 631(g).) “A trial court abuses its discretion as a matter of law when relief has been denied where there has been no prejudice¿to the other party or to the court from an inadvertent waiver.” (Tesoro del Valle Master Homeowners Assn. v. Griffin¿(2011) 200 Cal.App.4th 619, 638.) Policy favors the court using its discretion to proceed with a jury trial. (Bishop v. Anderson¿(1980) 101 Cal.App.3d 821, 823.)

 

Plaintiff argues they inadvertently believed the jury trial had been demanded when the initial complaint was filed on March 6, 2020 or shortly after it was filed and now seek relief from any potential waiver. Here, the court finds that Plaintiff did waive a trial by jury. First, the initial complaint was filed on March 6, 2020, and a CMC was not scheduled.  Thus, Plaintiffs had until March 6, 2021 to pay the jury fee -- one year after the initial filing of the complaint. Plaintiff concedes the jury fee was not paid until September 25, 2023. Plaintiff contends confusingly that prior to making the payment, Plaintiff made a demand for a jury trial in his case management conference statement filed on July 30, 2024, which could not occurred as the CMC statement was filed a year after fees were posted.   Nonetheless, Plaintiff contends there would be no prejudice as all parties have been aware of Plaintiff’s intention for a jury trial for more than a year, and the parties have worked together to continue the trial several times.    

 

Defendants Sumitomo Rubber North America, Inc. and Jacob Koo oppose the motion on the grounds Plaintiff has not met their burden of explaining to this court how the waiver occurred and why relief should be provided. Specifically, Plaintiff has not explained why fees were suddenly posted in July 2023, and why counsel declares that the waiver was realized until July 2024. In other words, Plaintiff has not explained why jury fees were paid 3.5 years later and the instant motion was made 1.5 years after Plaintiff first posted the fees. Moreover, any lack of prejudice does not stand alone as a basis to relief a jury waiver. Defendants contend this case has been pending for years without mention of Plaintiff seeking relief from the waiver of a jury trial.

 

While it is true that it took Plaintiff three and a half years to notice that jury fees had not been paid, counsel for Plaintiff has provided a declaration that they believed the jury fees were posted when the complaint was first filed in 2020. (Hernandez Decl. ¶3.) It was not until a review of the court’s register of actions that Plaintiff’s counsel learned Plaintiffs had inadvertently failed to demand a jury trial and had inadvertently failed to timely post jury fees in this case. (Hernandez Decl. ¶3.) Plaintiff has demonstrated preparedness for a jury trial and there is no prejudice to the court or Defendants in granting relief. Furthermore, it is well-established that when doubt exists as to relief, the court must resolve that doubt in favor of the party seeking jury trial. 

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s Motion for Relief from Jury Waiver is GRANTED.


Case Number: 20VECV01512    Hearing Date: October 4, 2024    Dept: W

CHUCK PETERS vs EQUINOX FITNESS CENTURY CITY, inc.

 

defendant EQUINOX FITNESS CENTURY CITY, INC.’S MOTION TO SET ASIDE DEFAULT AND DEFAULT JUDGMENT  

 

Date of Hearing:        October 4, 2024                                Trial Date:       None Set

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        20VECV01512

 

Moving Party:            Defendant Equinox Fitness Century City, Inc.   

Responding Party:     Plaintiff Chuck Peters  

 

BACKGROUND

 

On December 18, 2020, Plaintiff Chuck Peters filed a 32 cause of action complaint against Defendant Equinox Fitness Century City, Inc. Plaintiff alleges when Defendant closed their location where Plaintiff worked out, he was deprived of the benefits of his membership.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant’s Motion to Set Aside Default and Default Judgment is GRANTED.

 

ANALYSIS

 

Defendant Equinox Fitness Century City, Inc. moves the court for an Order setting aside the default and default judgment taken against Defendant and also requests that the demurrer and motion to strike filed on behalf of Defendant filed in another action be deemed filed upon the granting of this motion.

 

Relief from default or default judgment can be sought at any time on the ground of extrinsic mistake.  (Olivera v. Grace (1942) 19 Cal.2d 570, 576; Sporn v. Home Depot USA, Inc. (2005) 126 Cal.App.4th 1294, 1300.) This is a form of equitable relief, rather than statutory relief, and is based on a finding by the court that the judgment is void. (See Trackman v. Kenney (2010) 187 Cal.App.4th 175, 180-81 [contrasting CCP § 473(d) and extrinsic fraud and mistake]; Manson, Iver & York v. Black (2009) 176 Cal.App.4th 36, 47 [“After the six-month period for statutory relief has passed, the court may still grant relief on equitable grounds, including extrinsic fraud or mistake.”].)  

 

There are three essential requirements to obtain relief on this ground: (1) a meritorious defense; (2) diligence in seeking relief once the default or default judgment is discovered; and (3) a satisfactory excuse for not presenting a defense to the original action. (Rappleyea v. Campbell (1994) 8 Cal.4th 975, 982.) The statutory time limits on relief under CCP §§ 473(b) and 473.5 do not apply, but once the purported extrinsic fraud or mistake is discovered, a party is expected to proceed diligently to seek relief. This requirement is inextricably intertwined with prejudice to the plaintiff. (Id., at 983-984.)   

 

Defendant argues extrinsic mistakes and fraud both exist so as to warrant this court setting aside the default and default judgment entered against Defendant. Moreover, Plaintiff failed to provide notice to Defendant’s counsel regarding his intent to file the default that was entered by this court in violation of the principles set forth in Lasalle v. Vogel (2019) 36 Cal.App.5th 127. First, Defendant contends no where does the proof of service indicate that the Civil Case Cover Sheet Addendum and Statement of Location was served with the summons and complaint. Moreover, no Statement of Damages were ever personally served upon Defendant nor did the clerk require proof of service of a Statement of Damages. On top of that, Defendant contends Plaintiff actually never served Defendant. As a result of the lack of service and communication, Defendant believed Plaintiff abandoned this action like he abandoned his prior action against Defendant in Los Angeles County Superior Court case number YC071910.

 

As for the extrinsic fraud, Defendant argues Plaintiff processed a default utilizing a proof of service of the summons and complaint that he knew was not valid based upon prior discussions with counsel for Defendant and the court. The proof of service was served on someone who is not an agent for service of process and moreover, Plaintiff did not complete the Notice and Acknowledgment of Receipt as advised.

 

In opposition, Plaintiff submits a declaration of the purported process server who served the summons and complaint to Kevin Lutz, the corporate officer and authorized employee of GLK Corporate/Search, Inc. in Sacramento.

 

The court finds Defendant has sufficiently demonstrated extrinsic mistake and/or fraud. Kevin Lutz is not listed as the registered agent for service of process. (Exh. 3.) Even if he was, there is no evidence Defendants were served with the Civil Case Cover Sheet Addendum as required. There is also no evidence the Statement of Damages was ever served. In addition, Plaintiff submitted a proof of service which he was advised was improper. Moreover, once learning that a default and default judgment had been entered, Defendant immediately moved to set aside them.

 

Accordingly, Defendant’s Motion to Set Aside Default and Default Judgment is GRANTED.   The court finds that Defendant has never been properly served.
