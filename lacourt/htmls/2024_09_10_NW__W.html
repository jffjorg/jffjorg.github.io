DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV44845    Hearing Date: September 10, 2024    Dept: W

CARLOS ARITA vs 7-ELEVEN INC

 

plaintiff’s motion for leave to amend the third amended complaint and file fourth amended complaint

 

Date of Hearing:        September 10, 2024                   Trial Date:      None set

Department:              W                                                   Case No.:      21STCV44845

 

Moving Party:            Plaintiff Carlos Arita  

Responding Party:     No opposition  

 

BACKGROUND

 

This is personal injury action involves an alleged slip-and-fall incident that occurred on September 11, 2020, at a 7-Eleven franchise store located in Van Nuys, CA (the “Subject Incident”). 

 

Plaintiff Carlos Josue Arita filed the instant action’s Complaint on December 9, 2021 and filed the First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) on December 20, 2021, against defendants 7-Eleven Inc. (“Defendant”) and Does 1 to 100. On February 17, 2023, Plaintiff added S and R Luthra Inc. (dba 7-Eleven as Doe 1, Suresh Kumar Luthra as Doe 2, and Saroj Luthra as Doe 3. Plaintiff dismissed 7-Eleven, Inc. on March 21, 2023.

 

The instant action is related to 22STCV28081 (Carlos Arita v. Saros Luthra) and 22STCV28062 (Carlos Arita v. Suresh Kumar Luthra).

 

On May 4, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Second Amended Complaint. The SAC alleges negligence and premises liability. Plaintiff added back 7-Eleven, Inc. on December 18, 2023. On May 13, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Third Amended Complaint after seeking leave to amend. Plaintiff now seeks leave to file a Fourth Amended Complaint.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Plaintiff’s Motion for Leave to Amend the 3rd Amended Complaint and File Fourth Amended Complaint is GRANTED.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Plaintiff moves the court for an order granting leave to amend the Third Amended Complaint and file a Fourth Amended Complaint pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 473(a)(1), 576, and California Rules of Court, Rule 3.134.  While Plaintiff counsel filed a notice of errata on 8/27 asserting that Plaintiff did not file their third amended complaint and P is actually seeking to file a 2nd amended complaint, this does not appear to be correct. The original complaint was filed 12/9/21, another complaint was filed on 8/29/22 (as a matter of right), another complaint was filed on 5/4/23 via ex parte application on 4/11/23, and the final one filed 5/13/24, that was granted via motion on 4/17/24.

 

Leave to amend is permitted under Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (a) and section 576. The policy favoring amendment and resolving all matters in the same dispute is “so strong that it is a rare case in which denial of leave to amend can be justified. . ..” “Although courts are bound to apply a policy of great liberality in permitting amendments to the complaint at any stage of the proceedings, up to and including trial [citations], this policy should be applied only ‘where no prejudice is shown to the adverse party . . .. [citation]. A different result is indicated ‘where inexcusable delay and probable prejudice to the opposing party’ is shown. [Citation].” (Magpali v. Farmers Group (1996) 48 Cal.App.4th 471, 487.)  

 

A complainant may obtain leave from the trial court to amend his pleading beyond the number of amendments allowed under Code of Civil Procedure section 472 (a) by filing a noticed motion.¿ (CRC Rule 3.1324.)¿ The motion must be accompanied by a declaration stating: (1) the effect of the amendment; (2) why the amendment is necessary and proper; (3) when the facts giving rise to the amended allegations were discovered; and (4) why the request was not made earlier.¿ (CRC Rule 3.1324 (b).)¿The motion must also include (1) a copy of the proposed and numbered amendment, and (2) specifications by reference to pages, paragraphs, and lines of the allegations that would be deleted and/or added. (CRC Rule 3.1324(a).) 

 

Plaintiff seeks leave to amend to allege causes of action for False Imprisonment and Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress. Plaintiff’s new counsel believes that there are facts sufficient to support these claims, which relate to the facts already existing in the current complaint. Moreover, the current action is still in the pleading stage and the parties have not completed the discovery process. As such, Defendant will not be prejudiced by the addition of the new causes of action.

 

The motion is unopposed. Plaintiff has substantially complied with California Rules of Court, rule 3.1324 by submitting a proposed copy of the proposed amended complaint. Plaintiff has also submitted a separate declaration.

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s Motion for Leave to Amend the 3rd Amended Complaint and File Fourth Amended Complaint is GRANTED.

 


Case Number: 23VECV02049    Hearing Date: September 10, 2024    Dept: W

NICHOLAS VILCHIS vs MICHELLE KARASICK, et al.

 

plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses to demands for production, set one from defendant on the move, inc. and request for sanctions in the amount of $4,000.00

 

Date of Hearing:        September 10, 2024                         Trial Date:       None set

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        23VECV02049

 

Moving Party:            Plaintiff Nicholas Vilchis

Responding Party:     No opposition

Meet and Confer:      Yes. (Koppelman Decl. ¶¶8-10.)

 

BACKGROUND

 

On May 10, 2023, Plaintiff Nicholas Vilchis filed a complaint against Defendants Michelle Karasick, Matthew Vasquez, Socal Self Storage-Colorado Boulevard LP (“SSS-COLORADO”), Socal Self Storage LLC (“SSS”), and On The Move Inc. for 1. Negligence/Negligence Per-Se; 2. Premises Liability; and 3. Negligent Hiring, Retention & Supervision. Plaintiff alleges while in a parking lot owned and operated by SSS and SSS-Colarado, Defendant Karasick caused her truck to crash into a dolly nearby, which was positioned in front of Plaintiff, causing Plaintiff to fall over and sustain severe neck injuries.

 

On August 4, 2023, On The Move, Inc. filed a cross-complaint against SSS and SSS-Colorado for equitable indemnity, implied indemnity, contribution, and express indemnity.  

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Plaintiff Nicholas Vilchils’ Motion to Compel Further Responses to Demands for Production, Set One from Defendant On the Move, Inc. is GRANTED.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Plaintiff Nicholas Vilchis moves this court for an order compelling Defendant On The Move, Inc. to provide further responses to Plaintiff’s Demands for Production, Set One on the grounds Defendant has served responses that are insufficient under the Code of Civil Procedure and applicable case law. Plaintiff also seeks an award of monetary sanctions against Defendant and its counsel of record, Jacobsen & McElroy PC., in the amount of $4,561.65.

 

A motion to compel further responses to a demand for inspection or production of documents (“RFP”) may be brought based on: (1) incomplete statements of compliance; (2) inadequate, evasive or incomplete claims of inability to comply; or (3) unmerited or overly generalized objections.  (CCP § 2031.310(c).) To prevail, a party moving for an order compelling further responses to a document production demand must first offer facts demonstrating “good cause justifying the discovery sought by the demand.”  (CCP § 2031.310(b)(1).)  This burden “is met simply by a fact-specific showing of relevance.”  (TBG Ins. Servs. Corp. v. Superior Court (2002) 96 Cal.App.4th 443, 448.)

 

Plaintiff asserts good cause exists for further responses as the demands were drafted with the aim of obtaining discoverable documents that are relevant to the subject action. Moreover, Defendant waived objections by failing to timely respond to discovery. According to Plaintiff, Defendant received four extensions to respond to Plaintiff’s discovery requests – with responses eventually due on January 26, 2024 (Koppelman Decl. ¶¶4-6.) Defendant, however, did not issue responses until March 22, 2024.

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff seeks to compel Defendant to withdraw all objections to Document Demand numbers 1-5, 8-9, 13-25, 30-48, 52, 54-55, and also remove the “nonprivileged” language from the response. Plaintiff contends as answered, Defendant’s responses leave open the possibility that responsive documents do exist, but Defendant refuses to produce them based on privilege. Alternatively, Defendant must identify any withheld document in a privilege log.

 

Plaintiff also seeks further responses for Demands for Production. Nos. 26, 27, 28, 29, 44, 49, 50 and 51, claiming they are deficient. (See Sep. Statement.) Each of these responses assert only objections based on vagueness, overly broad, unduly burdensome and seeks documents based on attorney-client privilege and/or work product.

 

The court finds good cause for further responses. Defendant has waived objections by failing to timely respond. (Pelton v. Delta Packaging Prods. (2008) 165 Cal.App.4th 1568, 1577 n.13; Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pac. Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal. App. 4th 390, 404.) Parties cannot add late objections after service of initial discovery responses, absent a court order pursuant to a motion for relief from waivers. (CCP § 2031.300(a) [“The court, on motion, may relieve that party from this waiver, based upon conditions that response in substantial compliance was served, and the failure was result of mistake, inadvertence or excusable neglect.” Emphasis added].) Moreover, the requests are not vague or overly broad.

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff Nicholas Vilchils’ Motion to Compel Further Responses to Demands for Production, Set One from Defendant On the Move, Inc. is GRANTED in its entirety.  Further responses without objections to be served within 30 days.

 

Sanctions

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300(d) provides that for mandatory monetary sanctions “against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel a further response to interrogatories, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.”  (CCP § 2030.300(d).)

 

Plaintiff seeks $4,561.65 in monetary sanctions against Defendant and its counsel of record Jacobsen & McElroy PC for having to bring the instant motion. Plaintiff seeks six hours meeting and conferring and preparing and revising the instant Motion to Compel, an additional 2 hours preparing the reply brief and 1 hour attending the hearing on the matter. Counsel’s hourly rate is $500.00. Counsel also seeks $61.65 for bringing the instant motion.

 

The court GRANTS the request for sanctions in the reduced amount of $2,061.65 (3 hours preparing the motion, 1 hour attending the hearing, and the filing fee). The court will not award sanctions for Plaintiff’s statutory obligation to meet and confer. Moreover, no opposition was filed.  Sanctions payable within 30 days.
