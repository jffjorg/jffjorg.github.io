DEPARTMENT 73 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23STCV27674    Hearing Date: December 16, 2024    Dept: 73

12/16/2024 

Dept. 73 

Hon. Rolf Treu, Judge presiding 

 

PUIG v. GOODPEOPLE ROASTING LLC, et al. (23STCV27674)

 

Counsel for Plaintiff/moving party:  Scott Ernest Wheeler (Law Office of Scott Ernest Wheeler)

Counsel for Defendants/ opposing party: N/A

 

REQUEST FOR ENTRY OF DEFAULT JUDGMENT (filed 9/30/2024 & 10/08/2024) 

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment against Defendants Goodpeople Roasting LLC, GP Group LLC and Carlos Herrera is DENIED without prejudice.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On November 13, 2023, Plaintiff Eric Puig brought this action individually and on behalf of all other aggrieved employees against Defendants Goodpeople Roasting LLC and GP Group LLC.

 

On March 11, 2024, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) alleging the following causes of action:

1.      Willful Misclassification;

2.      Failure to Provide Required Meal Periods;

3.      Failure to Provide Required Rest Periods;

4.      Failure to Pay Overtime Wages;

5.      Failure to Pay Minimum Wages;

6.      Failure to Pay All Wages Due to Discharged and Quitting Employees;

7.      Failure to Furnish Accurate Itemized Wage Statements;

8.      Failure to Maintain Required Records;

9.      Failure to Reimburse Employees for Business Expenses;

10.  Penalties Under the Labor Code Private Attorneys General Act

 

The FAC alleges the following. Defendants own and operate one or more coffee bars, that also operate as restaurants, bars, and Marijuana dispensaries, in Southern California. (FAC, ¶ 1.) Plaintiff, along with other non-exempt employees of Defendants, worked and performed duties and tasks at these coffee bars and additionally, received wages from Defendants. (Ibid.) Defendants implemented common policies and practices within their coffee bars that have resulted in systematic violations of California’s wage and hour laws. (FAC, ¶ 2.) Plaintiff brings this representative action to recover civil penalties relating to systematic violations of California wage and hour laws by Defendants.

 

On April 25, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Doe Amendment to his First Amended Complaint naming an additional Defendant, Carlos Herrera, as Doe 1.

 

Default against Defendants, Goodpeople Roasting LLC and GP Group LLC was entered on June 25, 2024. Default against Defendant, Carlos Herrera was entered on August 15, 2024.

 

On September 30, 2024 and October 8, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant request for entry of default judgment against Defendants Goodpeople Roasting LLC, GP Group LLC and Carlos Herrera.

 

On October 8, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed Does 1 through 50 without prejudice.

 

II. ANALYSIS

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

 CCP § 585 permits entry of a judgment after a Defendant has failed to timely answer after being properly served.  A party seeking judgment on the default by the Court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) a proposed form of judgment; (6) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorneys’ fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties.  (CRC Rule 3.1800.) 

 

B.      Application

 

SUBMITTED: CRC 3.1800 

 

1.      Use of JC Form CIV-100                                                                          Yes

2.      Dismissal or judgment of non-parties to the judgment                  Yes 

3.      Declaration of non-military status for each defendant                          Yes

As an initial matter, Plaintiff’s request for civil penalties under PAGA does not appear to be proper.

 

A “ ‘[PAGA] action to recover civil penalties “is fundamentally a law enforcement action designed to protect the public and not to benefit private parties.” ’ ”  (Iskanian v. CLS Transportation Los Angeles (2014) 59 Cal.4th 348, 381.)  Labor Code section 2699, subdivision (i) requires that 75 percent of the civil penalties be distributed to the Labor and Workforce Development Agency and 25 percent to the aggrieved employees.  

 

The PAGA plaintiff does not receive the full 25 percent share of the civil penalties; rather, it must be shared by all aggrieved employees. (Moorer v. Noble L.A. Events, Inc. (2019) 32 Cal.App.5th 736, 738 [affirming trial court’s denial of default judgment because PAGA plaintiff refused to comply with order to distribute 25 percent of the civil penalties to 23 other aggrieved employees on a pro rata basis].)  

 

Here, Plaintiff is seeking penalties pursuant to PAGA only on behalf of himself. Plaintiff states that penalties for this claim total one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) with seventy-five percent (75%) of that penalty, or seven hundred and fifty dollars ($750), to be paid to the Labor & Workforce Development Agency (LWDA) and Plaintiff will receive the remaining twenty-five percent (25%) or two hundred and fifty dollars ($250). (Puig Decl., ¶ 27.)

 

However, a plaintiff seeking individual penalties and not on behalf of other employees, is not allowed.  “[T]he PAGA statute provides that plaintiffs can recover penalties through a civil action brought ‘on behalf of himself or herself and other current or former employees.’”  (Lab. Code, § 2699, subd. (a), italics added.)  “[A] PAGA claim may not be brought solely on the employee’s behalf, but must be brought in a representative capacity.”  (Williams v. Superior Court (2015) 237 Cal.App.4th 642, 649; see also (Kansupda v. Baptista Delivery Services., LLC¿(N.D.Cal. Mar. 31, 2022, No. 4:18-cv-02133-KAW) 2022 U.S.Dist.Lexis 111673, *23-24.) Therefore, Plaintiff’s request for PAGA penalties is improper.

 

In addition, the Court notes that Plaintiff does not appear to provide sufficient information as to his claimed damages. For example, Plaintiff seeks reimbursement for expenditures. In the brief filed in support of Plaintiff’s request for default judgment, Plaintiff states:

 

“Defendants forced Plaintiff to pay for expenses without receiving reimbursement for such expenditures, including but not limited to, costs associated with use of Plaintiffs personal cellphone and maintenance of his work uniform provided to him by Defendants. (See Declaration of Eric Puig, ¶ 18.) The business expenses incurred by Plaintiff during his employment with Defendants totals one hundred and fifty dollars ($150.00). To date, Plaintiff has not been reimbursed by Defendants for the expenditures incurred during his employment.”

 

In Plaintiff’s declaration he vaguely states: “Defendants required me to use my own personal cell phone for work-related purposes. I was also required to maintain my own uniform. I was never reimbursed by Defendants for these expenses that I personally incurred.” (Puig Decl., ¶ 18.)

 

However, Plaintiff does not specify how he calculated the $150 business expenses he incurred or provide any receipts of these expenses.

 

“Plaintiffs in a default judgment proceeding must prove they are entitled to the damages claimed.” (Barragan v. Banco BCH (1986) 188 Cal.App.3d 283, 302, citing Code Civ. Proc., § 585 & Taliaferro v. Hoogs (1963) 219 Cal.App.2d 559, 560.)  If evidence is presented by declaration, the facts must be shown to be “within the personal knowledge of the affiant and shall be set forth with particularity, and each affidavit shall show affirmatively that the affiant, if sworn as a witness, can testify competently thereto.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 585, subd. (d).) Here, Plaintiff has not provided sufficient information.

 

The Court also notes that Plaintiff seeks entry of default judgment against Defendants Goodpeople Roasting LLC, GP Group LLC and Carlos Herrera. Plaintiff added Defendant, Carlos Herrera, as Doe 1. However, on October 8, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed Does 1 through 50 without prejudice. Therefore, it appears Plaintiff dismissed Defendant, Carlos Herrera, as Doe 1.

 

Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment is DENIED without prejudice.

 

III. DISPOSITION

 

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment against Defendants Goodpeople Roasting LLC, GP Group LLC and Carlos Herrera is DENIED without prejudice.
