Case Number: 24STLC04319    Hearing Date: September 30, 2024    Dept: 25

Hearing Date:                         Monday, September 30, 2024

Case Name:                             JUAN ARIAS IBARRA v. SOCAL CARS, INC., et al.

Case No.:                                24STLC04319

Motion:                                   Plaintiff Juan Arias Ibarra’s Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay Proceedings as to SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services, LLC; and Request for Defendants to Pay Fees and Costs Related to Arbitration

Moving Party:                         Plaintiff Juan Arias Ibarra

Responding Party:                   None

Notice:                                    OK

 

Tentative Ruling:        Plaintiff Juan Arias Ibarra’s Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay Proceedings as to SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services, LLC is GRANTED in part, as follows:

 

 Plaintiff’s Request for an Order Requiring SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services to Pay Costs Related to Arbitration is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s Request for an Order Requiring Defendants to Pay Plaintiffs’ Attorney’s Fees Related to Arbitration is DENIED.

 

            The action is entirely stayed pending completion of arbitration. 

 

The Court sets status update as follows:

 

Post Arbitration Status Conference is set for June 30, 2025, at 9:30 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse.

  Counsel are ordered to file a JOINT Declaration/Report Re:  Status of Arbitration Proceedings at least five (5) court days prior to 06/30/2025. 

The Non-Jury Trial scheduled for 12/15/2025 at 8:30 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse, remains on calendar as currently scheduled.

 

 

 

BACKGROUND

 

On June 17, 2024, Plaintiff Juan Arias Ibarra (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint against Defendants SoCal Cars, Inc., Westlake Services, LLC DBA Westlake Financial Services, and Hudson Insurance Company and DOES 1 through 40, inclusive, (“Defendants”) for 1) Violation of Consumers Legal Remedies Act, Civil Code § 1750 et seq; 2) Violation of California Business and Professions Code § 17200, et seq.; 3) Unlawful Acts or Practices; 4) Violation of Civil Code §1632; 5) Claim Against Surety; 6) Violation of Code of Civil Procedure §§'s 1281.97 & 1281.99. Specifically, Plaintiff alleges that they purchased a used vehicle from Defendant SoCal Cars, Inc. (“Seller”). Plaintiff contends that seller made representations as to the condition of the vehicle, but the vehicle had one open recall and was in a major accident that caused structural damage after substandard repair. Defendant Westlake Services (“Holder”) was assigned the contract of sale and accepted the payments under the contract. Plaintiff alleges that they are entitled to a right of action against Defendant Hudson Insurance Company (“Surety”) as to Seller’s bond. Plaintiff seeks damages limited to $35,000 and rescission of the contract and restitution of amounts paid, as well as attorney’s fees.

 

MOVING PARTY POSITION

 

            On July 25, 2024, Defendant filed the instant Motion to Compel Arbitration on the basis that their contract for the sale of the subject vehicle contains an arbitration provision.

 

           

OPPOSITION

 

            No Opposition was filed.  

 

           

ANALYSIS

 

I.          Legal Standard & Discussion

Under Code of Civil Procedure section 1281.2, a court may order arbitration of a controversy if it finds that the parties have agreed to arbitrate that dispute. Because the obligation to arbitrate arises from contract, the court may compel arbitration only if the dispute in question is one in which the parties have agreed to arbitrate. (Weeks v. Crow (1980) 113 Cal.App.3d 350, 352.) Since arbitration is a favored method of dispute resolution, arbitration agreements should be liberally interpreted, and arbitration should be ordered unless the agreement clearly does not apply to the dispute in question. (Id. at p. 353; Segal v. Silberstein (2007) 156 Cal.App.4th 627, 633.) 

 

A. Existence of a Valid Arbitration Agreement

The right to compel arbitration exists unless the court finds that the right has been waived by a party’s conduct, other grounds exist for revocation of the agreement, or where a pending court action arising out of the same transaction creates the possibility of conflicting rulings on a common issue of law or fact. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1281.2(a)-(c).)

 

¿ The party moving to compel arbitration must establish the existence of a written arbitration agreement between the parties. (Code of Civ. Proc. § 1281.2.)  “With respect to the moving party's burden to provide evidence of the existence of an agreement to arbitrate, it is generally sufficient for that party to present a copy of the contract to the court. (See Condee v. Longwood Management Corp. (2001) 88 Cal.App.4th 215, 218, 105 Cal.Rptr.2d 597 (Condee ); see also Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1330 [“A petition to compel arbitration or to stay proceedings pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 1281.2 and 1281.4 must state, in addition to other required allegations, the provisions of the written agreement and the paragraph that provides for arbitration. The provisions must be stated verbatim or a copy must be physically or electronically attached to the petition and incorporated by reference”].) Once such a document is presented to the court, the burden shifts to the party opposing the motion to compel, who may present any challenges to the enforcement of the agreement and evidence in support of those challenges. [Citation]” (Baker v. Italian Maple Holdings, LLC (2017) 13 Cal.App.5th 1152, 1160.)

 

Here, Plaintiff seeks to compel arbitration of their claims as to two of three defendants, SoCal Cars and Westlake Services, based on the contract for sale of the subject vehicle, which includes an agreement to arbitrate all claims (“the Agreement”). (Mot., Ibarra Decl., Exh. 1.) Plaintiff argues that the contract requires arbitration if elected so by the consumer. In support of the existence of an arbitration agreement, Plaintiff submits evidence of a copy of the contract for sale of the subject vehicle, which includes the arbitration agreement. (Mot., Ibarra Decl., Exh. 1.)  The Agreement contains provisions allowing Plaintiff or defendants to arbitrate any claim or dispute relating to the sale of the vehicle. (Ibid.) The pertinent provision reads:

 

 

 

“ARBITRATION PROVISION

1.      EITHER YOU OR WE MAY CHOOSE TO HAVE ANY DISPUTE BETWEEN US DECIDED BY ARBITRATION AND NOT IN COURT OR BY JURY TRIAL.

Any claim or dispute, whether in contract, tort, statute or otherwise (including the interpretation and scope of this Arbitration Provision, and the arbitrability of the claim or dispute), between you and us or our employees, agents, successors or assigns, which arises out of or relates to your credit application, purchase or condition of this vehicle, this contract or any resulting transaction or relationship (including any such relationship with third parties who do not sign this contract) shall, at your or our election, be resolved by neutral, binding arbitration and not by a court decision... You may choose the American Arbitration Association, 1633 Broadway, Floor 10, New York, NY 10019 (www.adr.org); or any other organization to conduct the arbitration subject to our approval…

ANY HOLDER OF THIS CONSUMER CREDIT CONTRACT IS SUBJECT TO ALL CLAIMS AND DEFENSES WHICH THE DEBTOR COULD ASSERT AGAINST THE SELLER OF GOODS OR SERVICES OBTAINED PURUSANT HERETO OR WITH THE PROCESS HEREOF. "

(Mot., Ibarra Decl., Exh. 1.)

 

Here, Plaintiff has met his burden of demonstrating the existence of a valid arbitration agreement by presenting a copy of the agreement to the Court. Plaintiff has established that the agreement binds Seller SoCal Cars, Inc. as the agreement lists Socal Cars, Inc. as the Seller/Creditor. Further, Plaintiff has established that the agreement binds Holder Westlake Services based on the language directed at holders of consumer credit contracts. The burden now shifts to Defendant to challenge the existence or enforceability of the agreement. However, Defendants did not oppose this motion, and thus do not challenge the existence or enforceability of the arbitration agreement.

 

Therefore, a valid arbitration agreement exists between Plaintiff and Defendants SoCal Cars, Inc., and Westlake Services.

 

Accordingly, the Court GRANTS Plaintiff Juan Arias Ibarra’s Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay Proceedings as to SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services.

 

B. Attorney’s Fees and Costs Related to Arbitration

A court may award attorneys' fees, and costs, incurred in relation to statutorily authorized judicial proceedings regarding arbitration, and the arbitrator makes the determination in connection with the arbitration proceedings. Corona v. Amherst (2003) 107 Cal.App.4th 701, 707; DiMarco v. Chaney (1995) 31 Cal.App.4th 1809, 1816-1817; Carole Ring & Assocs. v. Nicastro (2001) 87 Cal.App.4th 253, 260-262; Ajida Technologies, Inc. v. Roos Instruments, Inc. (2001) 87 Cal.App.4th 534, 552, fn. 11.

 

Here, Plaintiff seeks an order directing Defendants to pay all of Plaintiff’s reasonable attorney’s fees and costs related to arbitration. On June 30, 2023, Plaintiff sent a demand for arbitration to Defendants SoCal Cars and Westlake Services. (Mot., Sadr Decl., Exh. 1.) Plaintiff filed a Demand for Arbitration with The American Arbitration Association (“AAA”). (Mot., Sadr Decl., Exh. 4.) On February 5, 2024, AAA notified the parties that SoCal Cars failed to comply with AAA’s policies and removed themselves from the Consumer Clause Registry, such that AAA declined to administer this claim. (Ibid.) On May 14, 2024, AAA notified the parties that Defendant Westlake Services failed to pay the $900 administrative fees to AAA and requested payment by May 29, 2024. (Mot., Sadr Decl., Exh. 5.)  On May 30, AAA declined to administer this claim as payment was not received. (Mot., Sadr Decl., Exh. 6.)

 

As to costs, Plaintiff has agreed to arbitrate their claims with AAA or JAMS, per the agreement which states that the consumer may choose AAA “or any other organization to conduct the arbitration, subject to our approval…” (Mot., Ibarra Decl., Exh. 1.) The arbitration provision states, “we will pay your filing, administration, service or case management fee and your arbitrator or hearing fee all up to a maximum of $5,000, unless the law or the rules of the chosen arbitration organization require us to pay more…” (Mot., Ibarra Decl., Exh. 1.) Based on the foregoing, Plaintiff has established 1) their right to arbitrate their claims against SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services with either AAA or JAMS and 2) that SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services are obligated to pay the administrative fees associated with the arbitration.

 

As to attorney’s fees, the agreement states that “each party shall be responsible for its own attorney, expert and other fees, unless awarded by the arbitrator under applicable law.” (Mot., Ibarra Decl., Exh. 1.) Thus, Plaintiff shall be responsible for their own attorney’s fees in connection with arbitration. Although the Court may award attorneys' fees, and costs, incurred in relation to statutorily authorized judicial proceedings regarding arbitration, Plaintiff did not include a request for attorney’s fees and costs for the preparing and filing of this motion.

 

Accordingly, the Court GRANTS Plaintiff’s Request for an Order Requiring Defendants to Pay All Costs Related to Arbitration.

 

The Court DENIES Plaintiff’s Request for an Order Requiring Defendants to Pay Plaintiffs’ Attorney’s Fees Related to Arbitration.

 

 

II.        Conclusion

           

            Plaintiff Juan Arias Ibarra’s Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay Proceedings as to SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services, LLC is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s Request for an Order Requiring SoCal Cars, Inc. and Westlake Services to Pay Costs Related to Arbitration is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s Request for an Order Requiring Defendants to Pay Plaintiffs’ Attorney’s Fees Related to Arbitration is DENIED.

 

            The action is entirely stayed pending completion of arbitration.
