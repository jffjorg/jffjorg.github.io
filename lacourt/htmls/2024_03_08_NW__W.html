DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22VECV00452    Hearing Date: March 8, 2024    Dept: W

MELINDA BONENE V. ASHKAN KERAKHSHAN

 

DEFENDANT KERAKHSHAN’S MOTION TO VACATE TRIAL AND FOR OTHER RELIEF

 

Date of Hearing:        March 8, 2024                                               Trial Date:       March 15, 2024

Department:              W                                                                    Case No.:        22VECV00452

 

Moving Party:            Defendant

Responding Party:     Plaintiff Bonene

 

BACKGROUND

 

This is an action for breach of contract, defamation and invasion of privacy brought by a tenant against her former landlord.  The case has been pending since March 30, 2022.  Trial was set on October 28, 2022 for June 12, 2023 and then continued multiple times to its current date of March 15, 2024.

 

[TENTATIVE] RULING:

 

The request to vacate the trial date is denied; request to file a cross-complaint is denied.

 

DISCUSSION

 

At any time during the course of the lawsuit, the court retains power to permit the

defendant to file a compulsory cross-complaint. Code Civ. Proc., § 426.50. Where the proposed cross-complaint arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences as the plaintiff’s claim, leave to file must be granted as long as defendant is acting in good faith. Id. (specifying that the court “shall” grant leave as long as defendant is acting in “good faith”); see also, Silver Organizations Ltd. v. Frank (1990) 217 Cal.App.3d 94, 98–99 (noting that, even on the “eve of trial,” leave to file compulsory cross-complaint is mandatory absent bad faith).

 

            The court finds no good cause to grant the request to vacate the trial date seven days prior to trial so as to allow defendant to file a cross-complaint.  Defendant has been aware of this case and the pending trial date for over a year but has taken no steps to seek leave to file a cross-complaint.  Instead, he has taken meritless actions to try to delay the trial, including filing an untimely challenge to this court and pursuing a baseless appeal.  There is no justification for the defendant’s delay in bringing a cross-complaint, when he was aware of whatever grievances he may have had with plaintiff from the very outset of this litigation.  The court finds this request to be made in bad faith, in order to prejudice plaintiff and cause her further expense and delay in resolving this matter.   

 


Case Number: 23VECV00538    Hearing Date: March 8, 2024    Dept: W

WESLEY SU v. AVANTGARDE SENIOR LIVING

 

Demurrer with motion to strike to the second amended complaint

 

Date of Hearing:        March 8, 2024                                   Trial Date:       None Set.

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        23VECV00538

 

Moving Party:            Defendant AvantGarde Senior Living dba AvantGarde Senior Living of Tarzana

Responding Party:     Plaintiff Wesley Su  

Meet and Confer:      Yes. (Dayal Decl. ¶¶2-3.)   

 

BACKGROUND

 

On February 6, 2023, Plaintiff Wesley Su filed a complaint against Defendant AvantGarde Senior Living dba AvantGarde Senior Living of Tarzana for (1) Negligence; (2) Violations of The Elder and Dependent Adult Civil Protection Act [Welf. & Instit. Code §15600 Et Seq.]; (3) Violations of Resident Rights [Health & Safety Code §1430(B)]; and (4) Wrongful Death. Plaintiff filed an amended complaint on July 14, 2023, removing the third cause of action for Violations of Resident Rights. Plaintiff filed a second amended complaint on November 27, 2023.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant AvantGarde Senior Living dba AvantGarde Senior Living of Tarzana’s Demurrer is OVERRULED; the Motion to Strike is GRANTED, in part.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Defendant AvantGarde Senior Living dba AvantGarde Senior Living of Tarzana demurs to Plaintiff’s Second Amended Complaint on the grounds the Second Cause of Action for Elder Abuse pursuant to Welfare and Institutions Code section 15600 et. seq. fails to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.

 

Defendant AvantGarde argues while Plaintiff has included new allegations in the SAC, Plaintiff still fails to set forth particularized factual allegations demonstrating any recklessly neglectful conduct on the part of this Defendant’s staff relative to the care and treatment provided to Decedent which caused or contributed to Decedent’s claimed injuries. Defendant AvantGardge further contends this is not an elder abuse case. This case is, at best, about whether this Defendant was negligent in providing care and services to Decedent. The fact that Decedent was a resident of a residential care facility for the elderly does not automatically create a cause of action for elder abuse.

 

The court finds the new allegations sufficient to support a claim for elder abuse for the purposes of a demurrer. Plaintiffs have sufficiently alleged AvantGarde denied or withheld goods or services necessary to meet Mr. Wang’s basic needs by allowing Mr. Wang on several occasions to stand from a chair and ambulate on his own, which was contrary to the protocols put in place to prevent him from falling. Moreover, the amended complaint sufficiently alleges Defendant’s conduct arose to the level of recklessness. “Recklessness involves ‘”deliberate disregard” of the “high degree of probability” that an injury will occur’ and ‘rises to the level of a “conscious choice of a course of action ... with knowledge of the serious danger to others involved in it.” ’” (Carter v. Prime Healthcare Paradise Valley LLC (2011) 198 Cal.App.4th 396, 405.) The amended complaint alleges Defendant wholly disregarded the fall prevention protocols that Defendants knew were specifically outlined in Mr. Wang’s medical chart. Taking these as true, Plaintiffs have alleged Defendant knew it must provide a certain type of care on a daily basis but provided it sporadically including prior instances to the fall at issue. Accordingly, a reasonable jury could conclude Defendant’s conduct was beyond simple or even gross negligence.

 

Lastly, the court had previously found Plaintiffs’ allegations sufficient to plead corporate ratification. Although the complaint did not provide the specific identity of particular employees, officers and agents of AvantGarde in Paragraph 36 (now Paragraph 38), it is established that this particular identification is not a strict requirement under Civ. Code §3294 (b).

 

Accordingly, Defendant AvantGarde’s demurrer is OVERRULED.

 

Motion to Strike

 

AvantGarde moves to strike portions of the second amended complaint including Plaintiffs’ claim for punitive damages, attorney’s fees, enhanced relief or treble damages, and that Defendant’s managing agents were involved.

 

As noted above, the court has found Plaintiffs have sufficiently alleged recklessness on part of Defendant and that Defendant’s officer, director and/or managing agent was involved. Accordingly, this point is moot. Next, the Elder Abuse Act allows for attorney fees, so the motion to strike is denied as to that request.

 

With respect to the claim for treble damages under Civil Code Section 3345, that section provides for treble damages in an action  “to redress unfair or deceptive acts or practices or unfair methods of competition” brought on behalf of a senior citizen or disabled person (both applicable here), “whenever a trier of fact is authorized by a statute to impose either a fine, or a civil penalty or other penalty, or any other remedy the purpose or effect of which is to punish or deter.”  Without deciding whether the conduct alleged here could be deemed “unfair or deceptive,” the court concludes that the remedy under Section 3345 is unavailable because the elder abuse act, on which the claim for treble damages under Section 3345 is based, does not provide for a “fine, or a civil penalty.”  See Clark v. Superior Court (2010) 50 Cal.4th 605.)  While it does allow for punitive damages, this court does not believe the legislature intended by the passage of Section 3345 to treble punitive damages awarded by the trier of fact. 

 

Accordingly, the motion to strike is GRANTED, in part, striking the request for treble damages.
