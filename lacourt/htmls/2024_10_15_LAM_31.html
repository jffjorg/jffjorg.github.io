DEPARTMENT 31 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Counsel may submit on the tentative ruling by emailing Dept. 31 before 8:30 the morning of the hearing. The email address is smcdept31@lacourt.org. Please do not call the court to submit on the tentative. Please do not submit to the tentative ruling on behalf of the opposing party. Please do not e-mail the Court if you plan to appear and argue.

In deciding whether to submit on the tentative ruling or attend the hearing and present oral argument, please keep the following in mind:

The tentative rulings authored by this court reflect that the court has read and considered all pleadings and evidence timely submitted to the court in connection with the motion, opposition, and reply (if any). Because the pleadings were filed, they are part of the public record.

Oral argument is not an opportunity to simply regurgitate that which a party set forth in its pleadings. Nor, is oral argument an opportunity to "make a record" when there is no court reporter present and the statements and arguments of counsel are already part of the record because they were set forth in the pleadings. Finally, simply because a party or attorney disagrees with the court's analysis and ruling or is not satisfied with it does not necessarily warrant oral argument when no new arguments will be articulated.

If you submit on the tentative, you must immediately notify all other parties email that you will not appear at the hearing. If you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motions. If all parties to the motion submit, this tentative ruling will become the final ruling after the hearing date and it will be memorialized in a minute order. This tentative ruling is not an invitation, nor an opportunity, to file further documents relative to the hearing in question. No such document will be considered by the Court.

**Tentative rulings on Motions for Summary Judgment will only be available for review in the courtroom on the day of the hearing.


Case Number: 21STCV35840    Hearing Date: October 15, 2024    Dept: 31

Tentative Ruling

 

Judge Kerry Bensinger, Department 31

 

 

HEARING DATE:     October 15, 2024                               TRIAL DATE:  N/A

                                                          

CASE:                         Richard Mucha v. Jeff Bankston

 

CASE NO.:                 21STCV35840

 

 

PETITION TO APPOINT CLERK OF THE COURT ELISOR

 

MOVING PARTY:               Plaintiff/Judgment Creditor Richard Mucha

 

RESPONDING PARTY:     Defendant/Judgment Debtor Jeff Bankston

 

 

I.          INTRODUCTION

 

            On January 9, 2021, Plaintiff Richard Mucha (Mucha or Plaintiff) filed this action against Defendant Jeff Bankston (Bankston or Defendant) for defamation, malicious prosecution, abuse of process, and infliction of emotional distress.  

 

Defendant defaulted.  On September 8, 2022, the court entered default judgment against Defendant and in favor of Plaintiff in the total sum of $255,000.

 

            On August 22, 2023, the court issued a Writ of Possession ordering Bankston to transfer possession of tangible assets to Mucha, including (1) 2001 Ford-350 with Mississippi license plate NOTNOWW and VIN 1FTWW32571ED71916 (2001 Ford), (2) 2002 Ford f-350 with Mississippi license plate WONTON and VIN 1FTWW32F52EA966645 (2002 Ford), and (3) a 1965 Ford Mustang California license plate STCSHFT and VIN 5409K677490 (1965 Mustang).  Bankston initially agreed to sign over title of the vehicles but reneged.  With respect to the 2002 Ford, Bankston reported the titles lost or fraudulently altered.   As to the 2001 Ford, Bankston claimed to have sold the vehicle to a John N. Chipolla.  Chipolla denied having purchased the vehicle and Bankston thereafter reported the 2001 Ford stolen.  And, as to the 1965 Mustang Bankston accused Mucha of grand theft auto.

   

On August 30, 2024, Mucha filed this Petition to Appoint Clerk of the Court Elisor to sign documents transferring title of the foregoing vehicles from Bankston to Mucha.

 

On October 4, 2024, Bankston filed handwritten documents entitled “Emergency Stay Order to Stop Court Elisor From Transferring Defendants Name on Vehicle Titles to Plaintiff”;  “Rebuttal to Plaintiffs Court Brief Including But Not Limited To CCP 128(a)(4) and 664.6(a)”; “Emergency Appeal to Stop the Transfer of Defendants Name on Vehicle Titles Registration to Plaintiff Case is On Appeal ….”    

 

Mucha did not file a reply.

 

II.        DISCUSSION & LEGAL STANDARD

 

            Mucha requests that the court appoint the court clerk as an elisor to sign documents on behalf of Bankston authorizing the transfer Bankston’s 2001 Ford, 2002 Ford, and 1965 Mustang to Mucha.  Mucha argues the appointment is necessary because Bankston has frustrated the transfer of these vehicles by falsely reporting they were stolen.

 

In its common legal meaning, “an elisor is a person appointed by the court to perform functions like the execution of a deed or document.”  (Blueberry Properties, LLC v. Chow¿(2014) 230 Cal.App.4th 1017, 1020 (Blueberry Properties).)  The court may use its discretionary powers to appoint the court clerk as an¿elisor¿to sign a recalcitrant seller’s name to all documents necessary to effectuate a court’s order.  (See¿Code Civ. Proc., § 128, subd. (a)(4); Blueberry Properties, 230 Cal.App.4th at pp. 1020-1021; see also Hood v. Gonzales (2019) 43 Cal.App.5th 57, 83 [concluding that the trial court acted within its discretion in appointing the court clerk as elisor to endorse a settlement check]; Venice Canals Resident Home Owners Assn. v. Superior Court (1977) 72 Cal.App.3d 675, 679 [“The inherent power of the trial court to exercise reasonable control over litigation before it, as well as the inherent and equitable power to achieve justice and prevent misuse of processes lawfully issued is well established.”].) 

 

Here, the appointment of an elisor is warranted.  The court entered a default judgment in Plaintiff’s favor.  Thereafter, the court issued a Writ of Possession ordering Bankston to transfer possession of tangible assets to Mucha in satisfaction. (Writ of Possession, 8/25/23.)  Bankston has not complied.   

 

Bankston’s various versions of an opposition lack merit.  Bankston revisits issues that were already decided.  He challenges Judge Orozco’s rulings as well as Judge Goorvitch’s rulings.  He also claims this court has already ruled on the matter, which is not the case as that is the purpose of this hearing.  Much of Bankston’s “oppositions” are incomprehensible or at least inapposite to this proceeding.  Bankston fails to present a meritorious argument in opposition to the motion.   

      

IV.       CONCLUSION

 

            The Petition is GRANTED.  Mucha filed a proposed order, however, that order was rejected by the clerk’s office.  Mucha is ordered to file a new proposed order within two court days. 

           

            The clerk of the court to give notice.

CASE:                                Douglas Lalonde v. Val-Pro, Inc., et al.

 

CASE NO.:                 22STCV17243

 

 

MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE A SECOND AMENDED COMPLAINT

 

MOVING PARTY:               Plaintiff Douglas Lalonde

 

RESPONDING PARTY:     Defendants Val-Pro, Inc., et al.

 

 

I.          BACKGROUND

 

            On June 25, 2022, Plaintiff Douglas Lalonde (Douglas or Plaintiff) filed a complaint against Defendants, Val-Pro-Inc. dba Valley Fruit & Produce Company (Val-Pro), Carrie Lalonde, and Donald J. Lalonde, arising from business disputes and wage and hour violations.  The First Amended Complaint, which is the operative pleading, was filed on February 6, 2023.

 

            On April 4, 2023, the parties attended a mediation.  The parties purportedly reached a settlement. 

 

            On April 25, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Notice of Settlement.  The Notice indicated that the settlement was conditional and that a request for dismissal would be filed no later than June 26, 2023. 

 

            On October 11, 2023, Defendants filed a Motion to Dismiss Pursuant to Settlement.

On January 29, 2024, the court denied the motion.

 

            On May 8, 2024, Val-Pro filed a complaint against Douglas in Los Angeles County Superior Court (Case No. 24STCV11574), alleging causes of action for (1) breach of written contract, (2) account stated, (3) open book account, (4) conversion, and (5) violation of Penal Code § 496. 

 

            On July 10, 2024, Defendants filed a Notice of Related Case. 

 

            On August 2, 2024, the court found this case and Case No. 24STCV11574 were not related.

 

On September 16, 2024, Douglas filed a Cross-Complaint in Case No. 24STCV11574 which alleges the same causes of action Douglas asserts in this action, including the following six additional causes of action: (1) financial elder abuse, (2) declaratory relief re management of Val-Pro, (3) appointment of a provisional director of Val-Pro under Corporations Code section 308, (4) declaratory relief regarding management and control over L&L Properties, LLC, (5) declaratory relief regarding the inapplicability of any formula for determining the value of Plaintiff’s shares in Val-Pro, and (6) accounting.  

 

Plaintiff now seeks leave to file the Second Amended Complaint.  Plaintiff seeks to add the six additional causes of action identified above.

 

Defendants filed an opposition.  Plaintiff replied.

 

II.        DISCUSSION & LEGAL STANDARD

 

Plaintiff is not entitled to an order granting leave to file the proposed Second Amended Complaint.

 

In ruling on a motion for leave to amend a pleading, the court does not consider the merits of the proposed amendment, because “the preferable practice would be to permit the amendment and allow the parties to test its legal sufficiency by demurrer, motion for judgment on the pleadings or other appropriate proceedings.”¿ (Kittredge Sports Co. v. Superior Court (1989) 213 Cal.App.3d 1045, 1048.)¿ While the court may deny leave to amend where the proposed amendment is insufficient to state a valid cause of action or defense, such denial is most appropriate where the insufficiency cannot be cured by further amendment—i.e., where the statute of limitations has expired or the insufficiency is established by controlling caselaw. (California Casualty Gen. Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (1985) 173 Cal.App.3d 274, 280-281, disapproved on other grounds in Kransco v. American Empire Surplus Lines Ins. Co. (2000) 23 Cal.4th 390.)¿¿ 

 

Here, denial for leave to amend is proper because there is no dispute Plaintiff seeks leave to add the same six causes of action alleged in his Cross-Complaint in Case No. 24STCV11574.  That case is still pending in Department 49.  Accordingly, Plaintiff improperly seeks to allege the very same causes of action asserted in another pending action.  This is expressly prohibited by Code of Civil Procedure section 430.10, subdivision (c).  A further amendment would therefore be insufficient to cure this defect.  Having failed to address this argument, Plaintiff concedes the point. 

 

Based on the foregoing, leave to amend is not warranted.  Having so concluded, the court does not address Defendants’ remaining arguments.

 

III.       CONCLUSION

 

            The motion is DENIED.

CASE:                         Neil H. Cogan v. Automobile Club of Southern California, et al.

 

CASE NO.:                 23STCV20030

 

 

MOTION TO STRIKE PORTIONS OF THE THIRD AMENDED COMPLAINT

 

MOVING PARTY:               Defendants Automobile Club of Southern California and Interinsurance Exchange of the Automobile Club

 

RESPONDING PARTY:     Plaintiff Neil H. Cogan

 

 

            This is a breach of contract action.  Plaintiff Neil H. Cogan purchased homeowners’ insurance from Automobile Club of Southern California (“Auto Club”), Interinsurance Exchange of the Automobile Club (“Interinsurance”), and American Automobile Association.  During the policy period, a patio wall at Plaintiff’s residence collapsed due to poor weather conditions.  Plaintiff submitted a claim.  Four claims adjusters reviewed the claim.  The first, second, and third claims adjusters determined there was coverage.  However, more than five months after the collapse occurred, the fourth claims adjuster determined the collapse of the patio wall was either not covered because 1) the wall collapse or 2) the patio was not covered under the policy. Plaintiff alleges the denial was erroneous because the fourth claims adjuster ignored relevant weather reports, overruled contemporaneous decisions of the three prior claims adjusters, and hired a civil engineer to justify the false conclusion that the collapse took place on March 1, 2023 when the weather was mild.  Plaintiff also alleges the civil engineer trespassed onto Plaintiff’s property.

 

Plaintiff commenced this action on August 21, 2023.  Plaintiff is self-represented.  On November 1, 2023, Plaintiff filed the First Amended Complaint (FAC) against Defendants Auto Club, Interinsurance, and American Automobile Association, alleging causes of action for (1) Breach of Contract, (2) Breach of Implied Covenant of Good Faith & Fair Dealing; Violation of Unfair Insurance Practices Act & Ins. Code Sections 785, 790, (3) Fraudulent Business Practice: Violation of Unfair Competition Law, Business and Professions Code Section 17200, (4) Breach of the Unruh Civil Rights Act; Failure to Protect Against Discrimination, and (5) Tortious Breach of Land and Privacy.  Attached as Exhibit A to the FAC were various documents including, but not limited to, a renewal offer, renewal bill, renewal declarations, building ordinance or law coverage disclosure, and residential insurance disclosure.  The policy was not included.

            On March 13, 2024, Plaintiff filed the Second Amended Complaint (SAC). The SAC alleges causes of action for (1) Breach of Contract, (2) Breach of Implied Covenant of Good Faith & Fair Dealing; (3) Negligent Misrepresentation; (4) Intentional Misrepresentation; (5) Violation of Unfair Insurance Practices Act & Ins. Code Sections 785, (6) Tortious Trespass to Land, and (7) Tortious Trespass to Chattels. 

 

            Defendants filed a demurrer and motion to strike the punitive damages request in the SAC.  The court sustained the demurrer in part and granted the motion to strike punitive damages from the second and sixth causes of action.  Leave to amend was granted solely to allow Plaintiff to file and serve a Third Amended Complaint to remove the objectionable causes of action and requests for punitive damages. 

 

            On August 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed the Third Amended Complaint (TAC).  The TAC seeks punitive damages.

CASE:                         JMF Pershing Square, LLC v. Home Furniture International, LLC

 

CASE NO.:                 24STCV04321

 

 

MOTION TO ENTER JUDGMENT FOR POSSESSION AND MONEY JUDGMENT PURSUANT TO STIPULATION

 

MOVING PARTY:               Plaintiff JMF Pershing Square, LLC

 

RESPONDING PARTY:     No opposition

 

           

I.          BACKGROUND          

 

            This case is a commercial unlawful detainer (“UD”) action by Plaintiff JMF Pershing Square, LLC (“Plaintiff”) against Defendant Home Furniture International, LLC (“HFC” or “Defendant”) for possession of residential real property located at 448 S. Hill Street, Suite 1101-1112, in Los Angeles (the “Property”). 

 

On February 21, 2024, Plaintiff filed a verified UD complaint against Defendant. 

 

On March 1, 2024, Defendant filed its answer to Plaintiff’s UD complaint. 

 

On March 6, 2024, Plaintiff filed a motion for summary judgment against Defendant pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 1170.7 and 437c. 

 

On March 19, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed Amouyal from this action without prejudice.

 

            On March 20, 2024, the court granted the unopposed motion for summary judgment.

 

            On March 28, 2024, Plaintiff filed an amendment to the complaint naming Abraham Amouyal (Amouyal) as Doe 2.  On the same day, the court entered judgment in Plaintiff’s favor as follows: 

 

1.      For possession of the property located at 448 S. Hill Street, Suite 1101-1112, Los Angeles, California 90013;

2.      For forfeiture of the lease agreement;

3.      For back rent in the amount of $65,831.27;

4.      For daily damages from March 1,2024 through March 20,2024, totaling $11,533.20 (20 days at $576.66 per day ($17,300.00/30 days));

5.      Attorney's fees per motion; and

6.      Costs per memorandum.

 

On June 21, 2024, Plaintiff filed this Motion to Enter Judgment for Possession and Money Judgment Pursuant to Stipulation.[1]

 

The motion was heard on September 10, 2024.  In advance of the hearing, the court issued a tentative ruling noting Amouyal had not been served with the motion.  The court directed Plaintiff to file another proposed judgment.

 

On September 11, 2024, Plaintiff filed another proposed judgment.  The proof of service attached to the proposed judgment shows Amouyal was served with the motion.

 

The court rules as follows.   

 

II.        LEGAL STANDARD

 

            Code of Civil Procedure section 187 states: “[w]hen jurisdiction is, by the Constitution or this Code, or by any other statute, conferred on a Court or judicial officer, all the means necessary to carry it into effect are also given; and in the exercise of this jurisdiction, if the course of proceeding be not specifically pointed out by this Code or the statute, any suitable process or mode of proceeding may be adopted which may appear most conformable to the spirit of this code.” 

 

“Section 187 contemplates amending a judgment by noticed motion.  [Citations.]  The court is not required to hold an evidentiary hearing on a motion to amend a judgment, but may rule on the motion based solely on declarations and other written evidence. [Citation.]” (Highland Springs Conference & Training Center v. City of Banning (2016) 244 Cal.App.4th 267, 280.)  “In the interests of justice, the greatest liberality is to be encouraged in the allowance of amendments brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 187.” (Wells Fargo Bank, N.A. v. Weinberg (2014) 227¿Cal.App.4th 1, 7 (cleaned up).) 

 III.            DISCUSSION 

Plaintiff moves to amend the judgment entered in its favor and against Defendant and Amouyal to reflect the following:

1.      Entry of a money judgment in favor of plaintiff and against defendants, jointly and severally, in the amount of $111,967.61 ($129,882.61 for principal minus $20,000.00 in payments plus $2,025.00 for attorney's fees and $60.00 for costs);

2.       For forfeiture of the lease agreement.

In support, Plaintiff provides a stipulation executed by Plaintiff, HFC, and Amouyal, on April 1, 2024.  Under the stipulation, HFC and Amouyal agreed “that a judgment may be rendered against them, jointly and severally, for money judgment for $129,882.61, plus reasonably attorney’s fees and costs, less payments made by defendants, if defendants fail to do any of the following:

[¶]

B.        Pay plaintiff $10,000.00 on or before April 15, 2024 . . . and

C.        $10,000.00 per month on or before the 1st day of each month commencing May 1, 2024 and continuing on or before the 1st day of each month thereafter, until the $129,882.61 is paid in full.”

(Horowitz Decl., Ex. 1, Stipulation.)

            HFC and Amouyal failed to make the $10,000.00 payments due on April 15 or May 1.  HFC and Amouyal then requested to pay $10,000.00 and agreed to vacate the Property on May 31,2024 and Plaintiff agreed to postpone the April 15, 2024, payment to the end of the repayment period if vacated by May 31, 2024. HFC and Amouyal made a payment of $10,000.00 on May 24, 2024, but did not vacate the Property on May 31, 2024, and did not make the June 1, 2024, payment.  (See Rosenbaum Decl., ¶ 11, Ex. 2.)

Based on the foregoing, the Court finds that Plaintiff is entitled to an order amending the judgment in favor of Plaintiff and against HFC and Amouyal.  Further, Plaintiff filed a proposed judgment and attaches a proof of service showing counsel for Amouyal was served with notice of this judgment.     

 IV.            CONCLUSION  

The unopposed motion is GRANTED. 

Plaintiff to give notice.
