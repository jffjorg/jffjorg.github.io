Case Number: 20STCV07031    Hearing Date: June 18, 2024    Dept: T

Motion to be Admitted Pro Hac Vice

Lisa F. Moore, Esq. (“Applicant”) seeks admission to appear as counsel pro hac vice to represent Belcalis Marlenis Almanzar, known professionally and sued as Cardi B, (“Defendant”) in this action.

DISCUSSION

Applicable Law

An out-of-state lawyer, not admitted to practice in California, may be admitted pro hac vice—i.e., to appear and take part in a particular action only. Applications for such admission are generally heard as law and motion matters.  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40.)  
To be eligible for admission pro hac vice: (1) the applicant must be admitted to practice before a U.S. court, or the highest court of any state or territory; (2) he or she must not be a California resident, nor regularly engaged in practice or other business here; and (3) a member of the California Bar must be associated as attorney of record in the case. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40(a).) A verified application must be filed stating (1) the applicant's residence and office addresses; (2) the court or courts in which he or she is admitted to practice and the dates of admission; (3) that he or she is currently in good standing in such courts; (4) that he or she has not been suspended or disbarred in any court; (5) the title and case number of any California action in which he or she has applied to appear pro hac vice during the preceding two years, the date of each application, and whether or not it was granted; and (6) the name, address and telephone number of the active member of the State Bar who is attorney of record. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40(d)—filing fee must accompany application.)

Copies of the application must be served by mail upon all parties who have appeared in the action, and also upon the State Bar at its San Francisco office at least 16 court days before the hearing (notice governed by Cal. Civ. Proc. § 1005). A fee (currently $50) must be paid to the State Bar.  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40.)

Application to Facts

Applicant establishes that she is licensed in the State of Georgia and State of Texas, and is in good standing. Applicant provides the list of courts she is admitted to practice and each date of admission. Applicant has not filed one prior application to appear pro hac vice in California in the past two years. Applicant further declares she is not a California resident and does not regularly work in California.  Licensed California attorney, Peter Anderson, Esq., is associated as counsel in this case. Counsel of record tendered payment to the State Bar and declares that the moving papers had been served on the State Bar. The Court finds the requirements of Cal. Rules of Court, rule 9.40 have been satisfied. 

CONCLUSION

Based on the foregoing, the Motion to be Admitted Pro Hac Vice is GRANTED. Defendant to give notice.

Case Number: 21STCV45711    Hearing Date: June 18, 2024    Dept: T

Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Default

Tentative Ruling:  Granted

BACKGROUND

Plaintiffs April Ascencio (“Ascencio”) and Julian Gonzalez filed this action against defendants Susanne Lorraine Tisdall (“Tisdall”) and Does 1 to 20 for injuries arising from a motor vehicle collision.  On January 10, 2024, Tisdall filed a cross-complaint against Ascencio and Roes 1 to 25 for indemnification, apportionment of fault, and declaratory relief.  On March 5, 2024, Tisdall obtained Ascencio’s default.  On May 24, 2024, Ascencio filed the instant motion to vacate the default entered against her pursuant to Cal. Civ. Proc. 473(b). Any opposition was due on or before June 5, 2024. No opposition has been filed to date.

DISCUSSION

Applicable Law

Cal. Civ. Proc. 473(b) provides that an application for relief must be made no more than six months after entry of the judgment, dismissal, order or other proceeding from which relief is sought and must be accompanied by an affidavit of fault attesting to the mistake, inadvertence, surprise or neglect of the moving party or its attorney. (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b); English v. IKON Business Solutions (2001) 94 Cal.App.4th 130, 143.)

Application to Facts

Under the mandatory relief provision, judges must vacate dismissals, default entries, and default judgments “whenever (1) an application is made no more than six months after entry of judgment, (2) the application is accompanied by an attorney's sworn affidavit attesting to his or her mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or neglect, and (3) the attorney's mistake, inadvertence, surprise or neglect in fact caused the dismissal or entry of default.” (Benedict v. Danner Press (2001) 87 Cal.App.4th 923, 927 (citing CCP §473(b)).)  
Here, the application was timely made within six months after default. Ascencio is represented by The JLF Firm as a plaintiff to this action, but Ascencio is also represented by Martinez, Dieterich, & Zarcone Legal Group (“MDZL”) as a cross-defendant. Ascencio’s counsel of MDZL declares that the cross-complaint filed against Ascencio was served on the JLF Firm. The JLF Firm notified Ascencio’s insurance carrier of the cross-complaint, but no response was received. Thereafter, default was entered against Ascencio, and MDZL was notified of the default. The insurance carrier then notified MDZL of the default, and on April 29, 2024, Ascenscio gave permission to MDZL to represent her in defending against the cross-complaint. Ascencio’s counsel avers that it was a mistake that default was entered against her, which could have been prevented had the legal representatives promptly addressed the cross-complaint. 

No parties oppose the motion. The Court finds that counsel’s straightforward admission of fault is sufficient to set aside the dismissal. (State Farm Fire & Casualty Co. v. Pietak (2001) 90 Cal.App.4th 600 609-610 [In order to qualify for mandatory relief, an affidavit from an attorney must be a straightforward admission of fault.].) 
CONCLUSION

The motion to set aside default under Cal. Civ. Proc. 473(b) is GRANTED.

Moving party is ordered to give notice.
