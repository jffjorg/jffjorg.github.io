DEPARTMENT T LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV13045    Hearing Date: August 9, 2024    Dept: T

Motion to Compel Deposition

Moving Party: Defendant Elia B. Depalacios 
Responding Party: Plaintiff Diane Nina Rendon 
Tentative Ruling: Granted

BACKGROUND

This matter arises out of a trip and fall incident that occurred on April 23, 2020, at or near Defendant’s Elia B. Depalacios (“Defendant”) property located at 3455 San Gabriel River Parkway, Baldwin Park, California. On April 26, 2021, Plaintiff Diane Nina Rendon (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint in Los Angeles County Superior Court, alleging negligence and premises liability against multiple defendants, including Defendant.

On March 28, 2024, Defendant served her Notice of Taking of Deposition by Oral Examination of Plaintiff. Plaintiff objected on April 3, 2024, based upon the fact that Plaintiff had been deposed on June 23, 2023, and should not be deposed twice. However, Defendant had not yet appeared in the case on June 23, 2023.  It was not until over four months later, that Defendant made her first appearance in this action by way of her Answer to Defendant City of Baldwin Park’s Cross-Complaint on October 30, 2023. 

On April 10, 2024, after receiving Plaintiff’s objection to the deposition, counsel for Defendant emailed counsel for Plaintiff asking for availability to schedule a telephonic meet and confer to discuss the objections contained in Exhibit B, Plaintiff’s objections to Defendant’s Notice of Deposition. On April 16, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel responded, in part: “The Plaintiff's position is that your clients do not get a second bite of the apple, for their failure to answer after being served. Had they answered timely, all parties would have been included at the deposition which has already been taken on June 23, 2023.”

MOVING PARTY POSITION

Defendant argues she is entitled to depose Plaintiff and needs to take Plaintiff’s deposition to adequately prepare for trial. Defendant argues they met and conferred in good faith, but Plaintiff’s refusal to appear for a deposition forces them to file this motion.
 
OPPOSITION

Plaintiff argues that issue preclusion bars Defendant from claiming plaintiff has failed to appear for examination, because Plaintiff has already been deposed once. (Plaintiff’s opposition p. 3.) 

REPLY

Defendant argues that they had not yet made an appearance in the case by the time Plaintiff was initially deposed, and that Defendant needs to depose Plaintiff in order to adequately prepare for trial. Defendant also argues Plaintiff’s objection to the deposition notice is inadequate and waived pursuant to Code Civ. Proc §2025.410, which provides, in pertinent part: “that the party served with the notice of a deposition waives any defect unless that party serves a written objection at least three calendar days prior to the date the deposition is scheduled.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 2025.410.)

APPLICABLE LAW

“If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent's attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 2025.450 (a).)  

The motion “shall set forth specific facts showing good cause justifying the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice” and it “shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 2025.450 (b)(1) – (b)(2).)  

Code of Civil Procedure § 2025.610(a) provides: “Once any party has taken the deposition of any natural person, including that of a party to the action, neither the party who gave, nor any other party who has been served with a deposition notice pursuant to Section 2025.240 may take a subsequent deposition of that deponent.” 

ANALYSIS

Defendant properly served Plaintiff a Notice of Deposition.  Defendant also had made sufficient efforts to meet and confer with Plaintiff, reaching out and attempting to schedule a call.  

Defendant was not served with the notice of deposition.  As such, Defendant is not precluded from taking Plaintiff’s deposition under Code of Civil Procedure § 2025.610(a) and is entitled to depose Plaintiff.  Further, the Court agrees with Defendant’s position she would be prejudiced in her ability to prepare for trial without taking Plaintiff’s deposition.  

Plaintiff’s issue preclusion and estoppel arguments are not applicable.  

RULING

Defendant's Motion to Compel Deposition of Plaintiff is GRANTED.

Case Number: 21STCV20591    Hearing Date: August 9, 2024    Dept: T

Motion for Summary Judgement 

Moving Party: Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority, A Public Entity; Kenny's Auto Service, a California Corporation (Defendants)
Responding Party: N/A
Tentative Ruling: Grant

BACKGROUND

This is a personal injury action arising from a car accident on August 7, 2020. Plaintiff Margarita Beltran's (“Plaintiff”) asserted causes of action for Motor Vehicle Negligence and General Negligence against Defendants Kenny's Auto Service and Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority (“LAMTA”) (collectively “Defendants”). 

Plaintiff failed to respond to Defendant’s Requests for Admission (“RFAs”) and did not appear for her noticed deposition. On October 9, 2023, the Court granted Defendant's Motion to Deem Requests for Admissions Admitted.  Facts deemed admitted include, in part, that Defendants are not negligent, Plaintiff suffered no damages, and Defendants neither caused nor contributed to the plaintiff’s injuries. 

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE

Judicial notice may be taken of “[r]ecords of (1) any court of this state or (2) any court of record of the United States or of any state of the United States.” (Evid. Code § 452(d).) Here, Defendant requests that the Court take judicial notice of Defendant KAS’ Requests for Admission and the Court’s October 9, 2023 Order Granting Defendant’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted.  The request is granted in its entirety.

 MOVING PARTY POSITION

Defendants argue that Plaintiff is not able to establish one or more elements of her causes of action and that no triable issue of material fact exists, thus entitling Defendants to judgment as a matter of law.  In particular, Defendants contend that the facts deemed admitted defeat central elements of the negligence cause of action, thus entitling Defendants to summary judgment.

OPPOSITION

No opposition was filed to this motion.

APPLICABLE LAW 

Summary judgment is proper “if all the papers submitted show that there is no triable issue as to any material fact and that the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.”  (Code Civ. Proc. §437c(c).)  The moving party bears the initial burden of production to make a prima facie showing that there are no triable issues of material fact.  (Aguilar v. Atlantic Richfield Co. (2001) 25 Cal.4th 826, 850.)  A defendant moving for summary judgment must show either (1) that one or more elements of the cause of action cannot be established or (2) that there is a complete defense to that cause of action.  (Id. at §437c(p).)  A defendant may discharge this burden by furnishing either (1) affirmative evidence of the required facts or (2) discovery responses conceding that the plaintiff lacks evidence to establish an essential element of the plaintiff's case. If a defendant chooses the latter option, he or she must present evidence “and not simply point out that plaintiff does not possess and cannot reasonably obtain needed evidence….”  (Aguilar, supra, 25 Cal.4th at 865-66.)

Once the moving defendant has discharged its burden as to a particular cause of action, however, the plaintiff may defeat the motion by producing evidence showing that a triable issue of one or more material facts exists as to that cause of action.  (Id. at §437c(p)(2).)  On a motion for summary judgment, the moving party's supporting documents are strictly construed and those of his opponent liberally construed, and doubts as to the propriety of summary judgment should be resolved against granting the motion.  (D’Amico v. Board of Medical Examiners (1974) 11 Cal.3d 1, 21.)

With respect to negligence a “plaintiff must show that defendants owed [him or] her a legal duty, that they breached the duty, and that the breach was a proximate or legal cause of [his or] her injuries.” (Ericson v. Fed. Express Corp. (2008) 162 Cal. App. 4th 1291, 1299–300.) 

ANALYSIS 

While Defendants’ motion for summary judgment is unopposed, they must still meet the initial burden of making a prima facie showing that there is no triable issue of material fact. In this case, Defendants meet this burden. Defendants make a prima facie showing that Plaintiff cannot meet essential elements of the negligence cause of action. Specifically, the facts in the RFAs deemed admitted establish that Defendants were not negligent, that they neither caused nor contributed to Plaintiff’s injuries, and that Plaintiff was not injured.  These facts meet Defendants’ burden in establishing no triable issue of fact  pertaining to breach, causation, and damages. As a result, Defendants make a prima facie showing that there is no triable issue of material fact. 

Defendants, having met their initial burden, thus shift responsibility to Plaintiff to show that a triable issue of one or more material facts exists as to that cause of action.  Plaintiff filed no opposition to Defendants’ motion for summary judgement, and thus failed to raise any triable issue of material fact with respect to her causes of action for negligence.  Defendants are therefore entitled to summary judgment.

RULING

In light of the foregoing, Defendants’ motion for summary judgment is GRANTED.
