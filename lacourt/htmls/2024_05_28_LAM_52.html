DEPARTMENT 52 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Please notify Department 52 via email at smcdept52@lacourt.org and indicate that the parties are submitting on the tentative ruling. Please provide the attorney's name and represented party. Please notify the opposing side via email if submitting on the Court's tentative ruling.



Case Number: 22STCV18057    Hearing Date: May 28, 2024    Dept: 52

Plaintiff Danielle Schlichter’s Motion to Set Aside Judgment

            Plaintiff Danielle Schlichter moves to set aside the judgment and enter an amended judgment including prejudgment interest.  Plaintiff did not file any proof of service of the motion on defendant The Skid Row Housing Trust.  Defendant is entitled to notice of the motion.

            Plaintiff also did not submit a proper proposed amended judgment.  Plaintiff’s proposed judgment is merely a table of figures from a spreadsheet.  To obtain an amended judgment, plaintiff must serve on defendant and file with the court (1) a renewed motion to amend the judgment, and (2) a proposed amended judgment on Judicial Council Form JUD-100 with item 6.a(2)—the amount of prejudgment interest—filled out and with the amended total in item 6.a(6).

            Plaintiff’s motion is denied without prejudice.

Ilan N. Rosen Janfaza’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel for Defendant Phu Nguyen

Ilan N. Rosen Janfaza of Law Offices of Ilan N. Rosen Janfaza, A.P.C. moves to be relieved as counsel for defendant Phu Nguyen dba PN Diamond Repair.  The motion meets all requirements under California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362. 

Janfaza’s motion to be relieved as counsel for defendant Phu Nguyen is granted.  The court will sign the proposed order on Judicial Council form MC-053.  The order will be effective upon filing proof of service of the signed order on defendant Phu Nguyen.

Case Number: 23STCV18814    Hearing Date: May 28, 2024    Dept: 52

Plaintiff Marcelino Diaz’s Motion to Compel Defendant Accountable Health Care IPA to Provide Further Responses to First Set of Inspection Demands and First Set of Form Interrogatories

Inspection Demands

Plaintiff Marcelino Diaz moves to compel defendant Accountable Health Care IPA (Accountable) to provide further responses to inspection demands Nos. 1-12.  A party demanding documents may move to compel further responses if “[a] statement of compliance with the demand is incomplete,” “[a] representation of inability to comply is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive,” or “[a]n objection in the response is without merit or too general.”  (CCP § 2031.310(a).)

Plaintiff shows good cause for further responses to these demands.  Accountable responded with incomplete statements of compliance.  A statement of compliance must state the inspection “will be allowed either in whole or in part, and that all documents or things in the demanded category that are in the possession, custody, or control of that party and to which no objection is being made will be included in the production.”  (CCP § 2031.220.) 

Accountable also objected to the demands without adequately identifying documents being withheld or the factual basis for the objections.  When a party objects, it must “[i]dentify with particularity any document, tangible thing, land, or electronically stored information falling within any category of item in the demand to which an objection is being made.”  (CCP § 2031.240(b)(1).)  If it objects based on privilege or attorney work product, the response must also “provide sufficient factual information for other parties to evaluate the merits of” these objections, “including, if necessary, a privilege log.”  (CCP § 2031.240(c)(1).)

Accountable filed an opposition to this motion asserting only that it expected to provide supplemental responses before the hearing.  Plaintiff filed a reply indicating Accountable has not done so.  The court will therefore order Accountable to serve further responses.

Form Interrogatories

Plaintiff Marcelino Diaz also moves to compel defendant Accountable Health Care IPA to provide further responses to form interrogatories Nos.  3.1, 4.1, 12.1, 14.1, 14.2, 15.1, and 16.1.  A party may move to compel further responses to interrogatories when an answer “is evasive or incomplete” (CCP § 2030.300(a)(1) or an objection “is without merit or too general” (CCP § 2030.300(a)(3)).

Accountable gave evasive and incomplete responses to these interrogatories.  For example, it responded to No. 4.1 by objecting, then stating, “To be supplemented.”  That does not answer the question.

For Nos. 12.1, parts of 14.1, and 16.1 , others, Accountable stated it “has insufficient information by which to respond.”  Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.220(c) provides, “If the responding party does not have personal knowledge sufficient to respond fully to an interrogatory, that party shall so state, but shall make a reasonable and good faith effort to obtain the information by inquiry to other natural persons or organizations.”  “If a person cannot furnish details, he should set forth the efforts made to secure the information.  He cannot plead ignorance to information which can be obtained from sources under his control.”  (Deyo v. Kilbourne (1978) 84 Cal.App.3d 771, 782.)  Accountable did not set forth any efforts made to secure the information from sources under its control.

Again, Accountable’s opposition asserts only that it expected to provide supplemental responses before the hearing.  Plaintiff filed a reply indicating Accountable has not done so.  The court will therefore order Accountable to serve further responses.

Disposition

Plaintiff Marcelino Diaz’s motion to compel defendant Accountable Health Care IPA to provide further responses to inspection demands is granted.  Defendant Accountable Health Care IPA is ordered to serve further verified responses to inspection demands Nos. 1-12, including a privilege log, within 30 days.  Defendant shall produce all additional responsive documents concurrently with its written responses.  Defendant shall identify which documents respond to which demands for inspection.

Plaintiff Marcelino Diaz’s motion to compel defendant Accountable Health Care IPA to provide further responses to form interrogatories is granted.  Defendant Accountable Health Care IPA is ordered to serve further verified responses to form interrogatories – general, employment, Nos.  3.1, 4.1, 12.1, 14.1, 14.2, 15.1, and 16.1 within 30 days.            


Case Number: 23STCV23403    Hearing Date: May 28, 2024    Dept: 52

Plaintiff Jessica Carlson’s Motions to Compel Further Responses to (1) Form Interrogatories, (2) Special Interrogatories, and (3) Requests for Production

Plaintiff Jessica Carlson moves to compel defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group (Acuity) to further respond to form interrogatories, special interrogatories, and requests for production. 

A party may move to compel further responses to interrogatories when an answer “is evasive or incomplete” (CCP § 2030.300(a)(1) or an objection “is without merit or too general” (CCP § 2030.300(a)(3)).  A party demanding documents may move to compel further responses if “[a] statement of compliance with the demand is incomplete,” “[a] representation of inability to comply is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive,” or “[a]n objection in the response is without merit or too general.”  (CCP § 2031.310(a).)

Acuity made meritless objections in response to all disputed discovery requests.  “[I]f a timely motion to compel has been filed, the burden is on responding party to justify any objection.”  (Fairmont Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (2000) 22 Cal.4th 245, 255.)  Acuity only attempts to justify one objection: that it should not have to respond to this discovery because (a) defendants moved to compel arbitration, and (b) after the court denied the motion, defendants moved to stay the action pending appeal. 

The initial objection was legitimate at the time.  Code of Civil Procedure section 1281.4 provides, “If an application has been made … for an order to arbitrate a controversy … and such application is undetermined, the court in which such action or proceeding is pending shall, upon motion of a party to such action or proceeding, stay the action or proceeding until the application for an order to arbitrate is determined.”  Acuity reasonably refused to respond to discovery until the court ruled on defendants’ motion to compel arbitration.  But the court denied the motion on April 10.  Moving to stay the action pending appeal does not stay the action in the period between filing that motion and the court ruling on it.  Acuity cannot indefinitely delay its discovery obligations. 

Except for objections to the requests for production based on attorney-client privilege and attorney work product, Acuity’s objections to plaintiff’s discovery requests are overruled.

In each motion, plaintiff moves for $7,500 in sanctions against Acuity.  The court exercises its discretion not to impose sanctions against Acuity.  It acted with substantial justification in objecting to discovery on the basis that defendants had moved to compel arbitration. 

Disposition

            Plaintiff Jessica Carlson’s motion to compel defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group to further respond to form interrogatories is granted.  Defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group is ordered to serve further verified responses without objections to form interrogatories Nos. .1, 3.1-3.7, 4.1, 4.2, 12.1-12.7, 13.1, 13.2, 14.1 within 30 days.

            Plaintiff’s motion to compel defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group to further respond to special interrogatories is granted.  Defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group is ordered to serve further verified responses without objections to special interrogatories Nos. 1 and 2 within 30 days.

Plaintiff’s motion to compel defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group to further respond to requests for production is granted.  Defendant California Clinic Management, LLC dba Acuity Eye Group is ordered to serve further verified responses to requests for production Nos. 1-99, including a privilege log, within 30 days.  Defendant must make no objections except those based on attorney-client privilege or the work product doctrine.  Defendant shall produce all responsive documents concurrently with its written responses.  Defendant shall identify which documents respond to which demands for inspection.


Case Number: 23STCV28309    Hearing Date: May 28, 2024    Dept: 52

Defendants’ Motion to Reopen Discovery and Compel Site Inspection

Defendants Clan Co, Alison Wattles, Frank Wattles, and Marion Wattles move to reopen discovery and compel a site inspection.  Defendants show good cause to do so. 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2024.050, subdivision (b) provides:

In exercising its discretion to grant or deny [a motion to reopen discovery], the court shall take into consideration any matter relevant to the leave requested, including, but not limited to, the following:

(1) The necessity and the reasons for the discovery.

(2) The diligence or lack of diligence of the party seeking the discovery or the hearing of a discovery motion, and the reasons that the discovery was not completed or that the discovery motion was not heard earlier.

(3) Any likelihood that permitting the discovery or hearing the discovery motion will prevent the case from going to trial on the date set, or otherwise interfere with the trial calendar, or result in prejudice to any other party.

(4) The length of time that has elapsed between any date previously set, and the date presently set, for the trial of the action.

Inspecting the stairs is highly necessary.  Plaintiff alleges they are unsafe and that defendants refused to repair them.  (Comp., ¶ 14.)  Plaintiff put the condition of the stairs directly at issue in this action.

Defendants show minimal diligence.  They state they retained an expert to inspect the staircase, but he belatedly said he would not testify at trial.  (Montgomery Decl., ¶¶ 5-6.)  They do not, however, show any attempt to inspect the stairs until May 7, 2024.  (Id., ¶ 9, Ex. B.) 

Permitting this discovery is not likely to prevent the case from going to trial as set.  Defendants estimate that inspecting the stairs will take less than two hours and estimate they can repair the stairs over two days of work.  (Montgomery Decl., ¶¶ 16-17.)

Finally, the trial in this case was set for June 5, 2024, after plaintiff successfully moved for trial preference.  The court later continued the trial to June 26.  Moving for trial preference substantially reduced the parties’ time to complete discovery. 

After considering all relevant factors, the court finds good cause to reopen discovery solely to permit defendants to inspect the stairs at the subject property.  Despite defendants’ lack of diligence, the discovery they seek is both important enough and narrow enough to justify reopening discovery for that purpose. 

Plaintiff’s objections to defendants’ demand for site inspection are overruled. 

Disposition

            Defendants Clan Co, Alison Wattles, Frank Wattles, and Marion Wattles’s motion to reopen discovery and compel site inspection is granted.  The court hereby reopens discovery solely for defendants’ site inspection of the subject property.  Plaintiff is ordered to permit defendants to inspect the stairs at the subject property.  The parties shall meet and confer no later than May 31 to select a time and date for the site inspection.
