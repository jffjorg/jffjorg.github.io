Case Number: 19VECV01817    Hearing Date: April 23, 2024    Dept: W

FARZIN MALY, ET AL. V. BRETT C HARRISON, ET AL.

 

MOTIONS TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

 

Date of Hearing:          April 23, 2024                                      Trial Date:       None set.  

Department:               W                                                         Case No:          19VECV01817

 

Moving Party:             Christie Gaumer, Counsel for Farzin Maly and Bahareh Mohammadzadeh Moghadam

Responding Party:       None

Notice:                         Proper

 

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

 

On December 20, 2019, Plaintiffs Farzin Maly and Bahareh Mohammadzadeh Moghaddam filed a complaint against Defendants Brett C. Harrison aka Brett Christian Harrison; BCH Enterprises, LLC; Tryston Harrison; Re/Max Olson & Associates; David Thomas Lloyd; Center Street Lending Fund IV Spe, LLC; Bagnard Company, LLC; CT Lien Solutions; Mario Simone; The Turoci Firm, Inc.; BM Stewardship Inv., LLC; Prominence Capital Partners, LLC, and Charles Ockwell.

 

On May 24, 2023, Plaintiffs filed a third amended complaint alleging causes of action for (1) Breach of Contract; (2) Specific Performance; (3) Fraud; (4) Conversion; (5) Violation of Penal Code section 496; (6) Quiet Title; (7) Breach of Fiduciary Duties; and (8) Negligence. Plaintiff also named XQB Management, Inc. and Twisted River Capital, LLC as additional defendants.

 

On April 30, 2021, XQB Management, Inc., Tryston Harrison, and David Thomas Lloyd filed a cross-complaint against BCH Enterprises, LLC and Brett C. Harrison asserting causes of action for indemnification, apportionment of fault, and declaratory relief.

 

TENTATIVE RULING: GRANT

 

Counsel contends there has been an irreparable breakdown of the working relationship between clients and counsel, and an actual conflict of interest between counsel and clients. New counsel is supposed to substitute but has not done so.

 

Counsel has complied with the requirements of CRC Rule 3.1362 by filing forms MC-051 and MC-052 and lodging with the Court a copy of the proposed order on form MC-053. Counsel has personally served client with copies of the motion papers.

 

Trial is set for September 16, 2024. There is currently a motion for summary judgment set for June 3, 2024.  Plaintiffs’ opposition to that motion is due on May 20, 2024.

 

 

Accordingly, the Motions to be Relieved as Counsel are GRANTED. 


Case Number: 24VECV01022    Hearing Date: April 23, 2024    Dept: W

JOHN S BIRKE v. MICHELE MARTIN BIRKE, et al.

 

MOTION BY DEFENDANT MICHELE MARTIN birke FOR AN ORDER THAT THE COURT EXERCISE THE DISCRETION AFFORDED IT BY GOV’T. CODE § 68636(b) AND ORDER A HEARING BE HELD TO DETERMINE WHETHER PLAINTIFF JOHN S. BIRKE WAS ENTITLED TO HIS FEE WAIVER IN THIS ACTION

 

Date of Hearing:        April 23, 2024                                     Trial Date:       None set.

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        24VECV01022

 

Moving Party:            Defendant Michele Martin Birke

Responding Party:     Plaintiff John S. Birke   

 

BACKGROUND

 

On April 10, 2024, Plaintiff John S. Birke filed a First Amended Complaint against Defendants Michele Martin Birke, Susan Goldstein, and Lewitt, Hackman, Shapiro, Marshall & Harlan for 1. Intentional Interference with Expected Inheritance; and 2. Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress.

 

On April 15, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed their first cause of action. 

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant Michele Martin Birke’s Motion for an Order that the Court Exercise the Discretion Afforded it by Gov’t. Code § 68636(B) and Order a Hearing be Held to Determine Whether Plaintiff John S. Birke was Entitled to His Fee Waiver in this Action is granted.

 

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE

 

Defendant Michele Martin Birke requests this court take judicial notice of the complaint filed in this action on March 4, 2024 and the Order on Court Fee Waiver entered in this action on March 8, 2024. The court GRANTS Defendant’s request for judicial notice.

 

analysis

 

Defendant Michele Martin Birke move for an order that the court exercise the discretion afforded it by Government Code section 68636(b) and order a hearing be held to determine whether Plaintiff John S. Birke was entitled to his fee waiver in this action.

 

Government Code sections 68631 and 68636 allows a Court to reconsider the issuance of a fee waiver.¿ “Under circumstances set forth in Section 68636, the court may reconsider the initial fee waiver and order the fee waiver withdrawn for future fees and costs or deny the fee waiver retroactively.” (Gov. Code, § 68631.)  

 

Government Code section 68636, subdivision (b) provides, “If, before or at the time of final disposition of the case, the court obtains information, including information derived from the court file, suggesting that a person whose fees and costs were initially waived is not entitled to a fee waiver . . .¿ the court may require the person to appear at a court hearing by giving the applicant [1] no less than 10 days’ written notice of the hearing and [2] the specific reasons why the initial fee waiver might be reconsidered.” (Gov. Code, § 68636(b).)¿  

 

Subdivision (b) further provides “[t]he court may require the person to provide reasonably available evidence, including financial information, to support his or her eligibility for the fee waiver, but shall not require submission of information that is not related to the criteria for eligibility and application requirements set forth in Sections 68632 and 68633.” (Gov. Code, § 68636(b).)¿  

 

“If the court obtains information suggesting that a litigant whose fees and costs were initially waived is obtaining court services in bad faith, or for an improper purpose such as to harass or cause unnecessary delay, or to needlessly increase the costs of litigation, the court may give notice that the litigant is required to appear at a court hearing to consider whether limitations should be placed on court services for which fees were initially waived.” (Gov. Code, § 68636(f).)¿ 

 

“In conducting a hearing under subdivision (b) . . .¿ if the court determines that the person was not entitled to the initial fee waiver at the time it was granted, the court may order the waiver withdrawn retroactively. The court may order the person to pay to the court immediately, or over a period of time, all or part of the fees that were initially waived. The court shall give the person a minimum of 10 court days to begin paying the full or partial fees.” (Gov. Code, § 68636(d).)¿ 

 

Plaintiff’s Fee Waiver Request is confidential. Defendant makes the motion on the grounds if the court determines Plaintiff sought fee waiver under box (c) of paragraph 5 and did not make appropriate disclosure of the $204,500.00 distributed to him by The Charles L. Birke Inter Vivos Trust in paragraph 10 of the FW-001 form, Defendant submits the information provided to the court by this motion suggests that Plaintiff is not entitled to his fee waiver.

In opposition, Plaintiff argues Defendant made the motion (1) to cause unnecessary delay in the proceedings; (2) to divert Plaintiff John Birke’s resources; (3) to waste the Court’s resources; (4) to subject Plaintiff to vex, harass, and annoy Plaintiff; and (6), to abrogate the confidentiality of the fee waiver process by compelling Plaintiff to reveal to Michele Birke his private, confidential financial information. Plaintiff further contends Defendant does not have authority to make such a motion. Regardless, Plaintiff states he completed the fee waiver application truthfully and accurately but if the court chooses, he is willing to appear confidentially before the court to answer the court’s questions about the fee waiver, but asks the court not to allow Defendant, her attorney, and third parties not to be privy to the hearing if the court determines one is necessary.

 

The court will set the matter for hearing no less than 10 days from the date of this order.  Plaintiff is ordered to present records sufficient to establish the basis for his eligibility under Government Code Section 68631 and 68636.  The hearing shall be confidential, as required by Section 68633(f).
