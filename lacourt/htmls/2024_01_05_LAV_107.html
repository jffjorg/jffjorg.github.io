DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21VECV01412    Hearing Date: January 5, 2024    Dept: 107

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

UDI GANNOT, ET AL,

Plaintiffs.

 

   v.

 

LIOR EDRI, ET AL.

 

Defendants.

 

 

 

   Case Number             Department

21VECV01412                  107

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

 

MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS the motion to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff NRG Builders, Inc. (“NRG”) filed by Daniel Krishel (“Krishel”).

 

Given that Plaintiff NRG is a corporation, on its own motion, the Court sets an OSC re: Plaintiff NRG retaining new counsel for Friday, February 9, 2024 at 8:30 AM in this department.

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from alleged wrongful actions concerning a business agreement. On October 15, 2021, Plaintiffs Udi Gannot and NRG Builders, Inc. (“NRG”) (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) filed a Complaint against Defendants Lior Edri, Hila Edri, Keystone Remodeling, Inc., TLV Marketing, Inc., Isabelle Maadi, Ofir Attal, Yaniv Lanczet, Mos Property Preservation, Inc. dba LMX Builders, Oren Kirshenboim, KLK Marketing LLC, Rozalin Zafarty, and Does 1 to 20, alleging causes of action for: (1) Intentional Inference with Contractual Relations, (2) Intentional Interference with Prospective Economic Advantage, (3) Negligent Interference with Prospective Economic Advantage, (4) Fraud, (5) Negligence, (6) Breach of Oral Contract, (7) Breach of Fiduciary Duty, (8) Conversion, (9) Accounting, (10) Usurpation of Corporate Opportunity, (11) Unfair Competition, (12) Conspiracy to Breach Fiduciary Duty, and (13) Aiding and Abetting.  

 

On November 29, 2023, counsel for Plaintiff NRG, Daniel Krishel (“Krishel”) filed a motion to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff NRG. The motion is unopposed.

 

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Code of Civ. Proc., § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)   

 

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e)).   The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).)

 

Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).) The Court has discretion on whether to allow an attorney to withdraw, and a motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client.  (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) 

 

 

DISCUSSION

 

Counsel has filed the requisite forms pursuant to CRC Rule 3.1362. Krishel declares that Plaintiff NRG is “unable to comply with the terms of the [retainer] agreement and additional atty-client conflict of interest exists.” (Form MC-052.) Krishel further declares that Plaintiff NRG’s “principal, Plaintiff Udi Gannot has not signed [a] substitution of attorney form and now represents himself in pro per; [and] [the] motion is filed because [the] corporation is not able to represent itself in pro per.” (Form MC-052.)  

 

In light of the facts above, the Court GRANTS Krishel’s motion to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff NRG.

 

As a corporation, however, Plaintiff NRG cannot appear in this case without an attorney because “a corporation, unlike a natural person, cannot represent itself before courts of record in propria persona, nor can it represent itself through a corporate officer, director or other employee who is not an attorney. It must be represented by licensed counsel in proceedings before courts of record.” (CLD Construction, Inc. v. City of San Ramon (2004) 120 Cal.App.4th 1141, 1146 [citation omitted].)

 

For that reason, Krishel is to notify Plaintiff NRG of this fact, and of the setting on the Court’s own motion of an OSC re: Plaintiff NRG retaining new counsel for Friday, February 9, 2024 at 8:30 AM in this department.

 

CONCLUSION

 

The motion to be relieved as counsel filed by Krishel is GRANTED.

 

Krishel is ordered to give notice.

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: Defendant Home Depot U.S.A., Inc.’s MOTION TO COMPEL RESPONSES TO SPECIAL INTERROGATORIES, FORM INTERROGATORIES, AND REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION OF DOCUMENTS

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS Defendant Home Depot’s: (1) motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Defendant Home Depot’s Special Interrogatories, Set One (the “Special Interrogatories Motion”); (2) motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Defendant Home Depot’s Form Interrogatories, Set One (the “Form Interrogatories Motion”) and (3) motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Set One of Defendant Home Depot’s Request for Production of Documents (the “RPD Motion”) (collectively, the “Motions”).

 

Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified, complete, and code-compliant responses, without objection, to Set One of Defendant Home Depot’s Special Interrogatories, Form Interrogatories, and Request for Production of Documents within 20 days of the date of notice of this order.

Plaintiff is ordered to pay monetary sanctions in the total amount of $600.00 to Defendant Home Depot within 30 days of the date of notice of this order. This amount represents total monetary sanctions as to the Special Interrogatories Motion, Form Interrogatories Motion, and the RPD Motion.

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from a motor vehicle collision that occurred November 24, 2020. On November 15, 2022, Plaintiff Patricia Ann Mendez (“Plaintiff”) filed a Complaint against Defendants Dong Hai Le, Home Depot U.S.A., Inc. (“Home Depot”), and Does 1 to 50, alleging a cause of action for negligence.  

 

 

On April 12, 2023, after hearing, the Court granted Carlos A. Gomez’s motion to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff.

 

On April 14, 2023, Carlos A. Gomez filed a Proof of Service of the order granting the motion to be relieved as counsel. The Proof of Service shows that Plaintiff was served with the order granting the motion to be relieved as counsel on April 14, 2023 via mail.

 

On May 18, 2023, Defendant Home Depot filed an Answer to the Complaint.

 

On November 29, 2023, Defendant Home Depot filed and served an unopposed motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Defendant Home Depot’s Special Interrogatories, Set One (the “Special Interrogatories Motion”), an unopposed motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Defendant Home Depot’s Form Interrogatories, Set One (the “Form Interrogatories Motion”), and an unopposed motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Set One of Defendant Home Depot’s Request for Production of Documents (the “RPD Motion”) (collectively, the “Motions”). The Motions each request monetary sanctions against Plaintiff in the amount of $1,000.00. The proofs of service attached to the Motions show that the Motions were served on Plaintiff on November 29, 2023.

 

On January 2, 2024, after hearing, the Court continued the hearing on Special Interrogatories Motion and RPD Motion due to Plaintiff not being present “and in an abundance of caution as to correct notice to that party of advancing the 1/3/24 motion to this date.” (01/02/24 Minute Order.) The Court continued the hearing on such motions to January 5, 2024. (01/02/24 Minute Order.)

 

The Motions are unopposed. Any opposition was required to have been filed and served at least nine court days prior to the hearing. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005, subd. (b).)

 

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

“Within 30 days after service of interrogatories, the party to whom the interrogatories are propounded shall serve the original of the response to them on the propounding party, unless on motion of the propounding party the court has shortened the time for response, or unless on motion of the responding party the court has extended the time for response.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.260, subd. (a).)  If the party to whom interrogatories are directed fails to serve a timely response, “[t]he party to whom the interrogatories are directed waives any right to exercise the option to produce writings under Section 2030.230, as well as any objection to the interrogatories, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (a).) “The party propounding interrogatories may move for an order compelling response to the interrogatories.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (b).) 

“Any party may obtain discovery . . . by inspecting, copying, testing, or sampling documents, tangible things, land or other property, and electronically stored information in the possession, custody, or control of any other party to the action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.010, subd. (a).) Where a party fails to timely respond to demand for inspection, copying, testing or sampling “[t]he party to whom the demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling is directed waives any objection to the demand, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300, subd. (a).) Code Civ. Proc. § 2031.300(b) provides that “[t]he party making the demand may move for an order compelling response to the demand.”

Code Civ. Proc. § 2023.010(d) provides that a misuse of the discovery process is “[f]ailing to respond or to submit to an authorized method of discovery.” Code Civ. Proc. § 2023.010(h) states that a misuse of the discovery process includes “[m]aking or opposing, unsuccessfully and without substantial justification, a motion to compel or limit discovery.” A court has discretion to “impose a monetary sanction against a party engaging in the misuse of the discovery process or any attorney advising such conduct” under Code Civ. Proc. § 2023.030(a). A court has discretion to fix the amount of reasonable monetary sanctions. (Cornerstone Realty Advisors, LLC v. Summit Healthcare Reit, Inc. (2020) 56 Cal.App.5th 771, 791.)

 

DISCUSSION

As to monetary sanctions, the Court exercises its discretion and GRANTS IN PART Defendant Home Depot’s request for monetary sanctions against Plaintiff in the reasonable amount of $200.00. Such amount represents 1 hour of work on the Special Interrogatories Motion. The Court reduces the hours given the simplicity of the motion, the fact that the Motions are similar, and the fact that the Motions are unopposed. COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE A CROSS-COMPLAINT

 

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS Defendant’s motion for leave to file a cross-complaint for indemnification and apportionment of fault against Four Seasons Gardening & Tree Service (the “Motion”).

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from a slip and fall at an apartment complex that occurred on August 21, 2022. On December 19, 2022, Plaintiff Stanislav Polyvyan (“Plaintiff”) filed a Complaint against Defendants Jones & Jones Management Group, Inc. dba Jones & Jones Communities (“Defendant”) and Does 1 to 20, alleging a cause of action for Premises Liability. Plaintiff alleges that “he slipped on the first step from the top of the subject stairway that had water pooled on the tread and running down the stairway due to the sprinklers located near the subject stairway.” (Complaint, p. 5.)

 

On January 26, 2023, Defendant filed an Answer to the Complaint.

 

On September 29, 2023, Defendant filed and served the instant unopposed motion for leave to file a cross-complaint (the “Motion”). Defendant seeks to file a cross-complaint for indemnification and apportionment of fault against Four Seasons Gardening & Tree Service.  

 

On November 1, 2023, the Court issued a Notice re: Continuance of Hearing and Order which continued the hearing on the Motion from December 27, 2023, to January 5, 2024.

 

On November 2, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Proof of Electronic Service as to the Notice re: Continuance of Hearing and Order.

 

On November 21, 2023, Plaintiff filed an Amendment to Complaint which substituted Four Seasons Landscape Masters, LLC dba Four Seasons Gardening & Tree Service for Doe 1. On December 20, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Proof of Service of Summons which indicated that Four Seasons Landscape Masters, LLC dba Four Seasons Gardening & Tree Service was served with the summons and complaint on December 13, 2023, by substituted service.

 

The Motion is unopposed. Any opposition to the Motion was required to have been filed and served at least nine court days prior to the hearing. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005, subd. (b).)

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

Code Civ. Proc., § 428.50, subd. (a) provides that “[a] party shall file a cross-complaint against any of the parties who filed the complaint or cross-complaint against him or her before or at the same time as the answer to the complaint or cross-complaint.” “Any other cross-complaint may be filed at any time before the court has set a date for trial.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 428.50, subd. (b).) “A party shall obtain leave of court to file any cross-complaint except one filed within the time specified in subdivision (a) or (b). Leave may be granted in the interest of justice at any time during the course of the action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 428.50, subd. (c).)

 

Under Code Civ. Proc. § 428.10(a) “[a] party against whom a cause of action has been asserted in a complaint or cross-complaint may file a cross complaint setting forth . . . [a]ny cause of action he has against any of the parties who filed the complaint or cross-complaint against him.” (K.R.L. Partnership v. Superior Court (2004) 120 Cal.App.4th 490, 498.) Code Civ. Proc. § 428.10(b) allows a party to file a cross-complaint alleging “[a]ny cause of action he has against a person alleged to be liable thereon, whether or not such person is already a party to the action, if the cause of action asserted in his cross-complaint (1) arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences as the cause brought against him.” “[A] related cause of action is a cause of action which arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences as the cause of action which the plaintiff alleges in his complaint.” (K.R.L. Partnership v. Superior Court, supra, 120 Cal.App.4th 490, 498.) “Cross-complaints for comparative equitable indemnity would appear virtually always transactionally related to the main action.” (Time for Living, Inc. v. Guy Hatfield Homes/All American Development Co. (1991) 230 Cal.App.3d 30, 38.)  

 

DISCUSSION

 

In support of the Motion, Kelly W. Migoya (“Migoya”) declares that she is counsel for Defendant. (Migoya Decl., ¶ 1.) Defendant filed its Answer to the Complaint on January 26, 2023. (Id., ¶ 3.) According to the contract between Defendant and Four Seasons Gardening & Tree Service (“Four Seasons”), Four Seasons maintains the sprinkler system. (Id., ¶ 4; Exhibit B.) In light of Plaintiff’s contention that the sprinklers caused the water to be pooled on the stairs, counsel determined that it is necessary to file a cross-complaint for indemnification and apportionment of fault against Four Seasons. (Id., ¶ 5.) A copy of the proposed cross-complaint is attached to counsel’s declaration in support of the Motion. (Id., ¶ 6 and Exhibit C.)  

 

The Court finds that it is appropriate to allow Defendant to file the proposed cross-complaint. Defendant seeks indemnity against Four Seasons and therefore the cross-complaint is related to the main action under Time for Living, Inc. v. Guy Hatfield Homes/All American Development Co., supra, 230 Cal.App.3d 30, 38. Moreover, the Motion is unopposed which leads to the inference that the Motion has merit. (Sexton v. Superior Court (1997) 58 Cal.App.4th 1403, 1410.)

 

CONCLUSION

 

The Motion is therefore GRANTED. Moving party is ordered to file the proposed cross-complaint—which is attached as Exhibit C to the declaration of Migoya in support of the Motion—within five days of the date of this order so that it becomes an operative pleading in this action.

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.
