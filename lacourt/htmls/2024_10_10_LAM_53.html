Order RE:

 

substitution of attorney

 

 

On August 12, 2024, the court issued an order on two Orders to Show Cause set by the court, regarding (1) whether the answer of defendant James Jones (“Defendant”) should be stricken, and (2) the status of the conservatorship of the Estate of James Jones.

In the court’s August 12, 2024 order, the court noted that Leslie Bushnell (“Bushnell”) submitted (1) the “Durable Power of Attorney for Management of Property and Personal Affairs,” in which Defendant appointed Bushnell as Defendant’s attorney in fact and granted her the authority to take any actions with respect to, inter alia, any claim asserted by or against Defendant, and (2) a copy of a settlement agreement executed by Bushnell, Defendant, Claudia Moody Jones, and Nicholas Jones, in which the parties agreed that Bushnell (or, in the alternative, Nicholas Jones) would retain the rights and powers assigned to her in the Durable Power of Attorney, limited to those necessary to act on behalf of Defendant in any litigation, which Bushnell attested was approved by the Probate Court in her declaration.  (Aug. 12, 2024 Order, pp. 3:19-26, 5:9-17.) 

At the August 12, 2024 hearing on the Orders to Show Cause, attorney Vahidi appeared, asserting that Vahidi was representing Defendant.  Vahidi claimed that the Durable Power of Attorney is not valid, while Bushnell claimed that Defendant is not competent to make decisions about his estate, and that she retains authority to act on Defendant’s behalf in this action pursuant to the Durable Power of Attorney.

Thereafter, on August 12, 2024, Steven S. Vahidi filed a “Substitution of Attorney – Civil” form on behalf of Defendant, asserting that Steven S. Vahidi has substituted in as counsel to represent Defendant.  (MC-050, ¶¶ 1-2.)

It appears to the court that Vahidi may not have the authority to represent Defendant in this action since the Durable Power of Attorney remains in effect with regard to Bushnell’s authority to act on behalf of Defendant in litigation. 

The court therefore sets for hearing on December 13, 2024, at 10:00 a.m., an Order to Show Cause (1) whether the Durable Power of Attorney granting Leslie Bushnell the authority to act on defendant James Jones’s behalf in prosecuting or defending litigation has been revoked or determined by a court to be invalid and, if not, (2) whether the Substitution of Attorney – Civil form filed by Steven S. Vahidi on behalf of defendant James Jones should be stricken because Steven S. Vahidi did not have authority to sign and file it on behalf of defendant James Jones.

Any response to the Order to Show Cause shall be filed and served no later than nine court days before the hearing on the Order to Show Cause.

            The court directs the clerk to give notice of this ruling (1) to plaintiff Law Firm of Fox and Fox, (2) to defendant James Jones, (3) to attorney Steven S. Vahidi, (4) to Leslie Bushnell, and (5) to Claudia Moody Jones.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

 

DATED:  October 10, 2024

MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant Airgas USA, LLC

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Plaintiff Joe Ledesma

Motion to Compel Plaintiff’s Deposition and for Sanctions

The court considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with this motion.

DISCUSSION

Defendant Airgas USA, LLC (“Defendant”) moves the court for an order (1) compelling plaintiff Joe Ledesma (“Plaintiff”) to appear for and testify at deposition, and (2) imposing issue, evidentiary, terminating, or monetary sanctions in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiff.

“If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action . . . , without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination . . . , the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (a).)

The court finds that Defendant has (1) submitted a meet and confer declaration that complies with Code of Civil Procedure section 2016.040, and (2) shown that Plaintiff has failed to appear for deposition and to provide Defendant’s counsel with his availability to appear for deposition.  The court notes that Plaintiff has argued, in his opposition, that his counsel offered the deposition date of July 31, 2024, but that Defendant instead filed this motion as a bad faith tactic to punish Plaintiff.  (Opp., p. 5:22-24.)  The court disagrees.  Plaintiff’s counsel offered this date “if [counsel was] not in trial,” advising Defendant’s counsel further that “this date might not be available.”  (Ehsani-Nia Decl., Ex. 15, PDF p. 233 [emphasis added].)  Thus, Plaintiff did not provide Defendant with a date certain on which he would appear for deposition. 

The court therefore grants Defendant’s motion to compel Plaintiff’s deposition.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subds. (a), (b)(2).)

The court grants Defendant’s request for monetary sanctions against Plaintiff.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (g)(1).)  The court finds that $2,250 (5 hours x $450 hourly rate for counsel) is a reasonable amount of sanctions to impose against Plaintiff in connection with this motion.  (Ehsani-Nia Decl., ¶¶ 30-31.)

The court denies Defendant’s requests for terminating, issue, and evidence sanctions against Plaintiff because the court finds that compelling Plaintiff to appear for deposition and awarding monetary sanctions in Defendant’s favor is sufficient to remedy the harm caused by Plaintiff’s failure to appear and provide dates for his deposition.  (Lopez v. Watchtower Bible & Tract Society of New York, Inc. (2016) 246 Cal.App.4th 566, 604 [sanctions should be tailored to the harm caused by withheld discovery].)

The court denies Plaintiff’s request for monetary sanctions in his favor and against Defendant.

ORDER

            The court grants defendant Airgas USA LLC’s motion to compel plaintiff’s deposition for sanctions as follows.

            The court orders plaintiff Joe Ledesma to attend and testify at a deposition to be taken by counsel for defendant Airgas USA LLC on ___________________, 2024, at 10:00 a.m., to be conducted by a videoconference platform selected by defendant Airgas USA, LLC.

            The court orders plaintiff Joe Ledesma to pay monetary sanctions to defendant Airgas USA, LLC in the amount of $2,250 within 30 days of the date of this order.

            The court denies all other relief requested in defendant Airgas USA LLC’s motion.

            The court orders defendant Airgas USA, LLC to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE



            The court denies plaintiff Emilio Reyes’s
request for judicial notice because the Third Amended Complaint is not relevant
to the court’s disposition of this motion. 
(Malek Media Group LLC v. AXQG Corp. (2020) 58 Cal.App.5th 817,
825 [“Any matter to be judicially noticed must be relevant to a material
issue”].)



DISCUSSION



Plaintiff Emilio Reyes (“Plaintiff”) moves the court for an order (1) compelling
defendant Los Indios de San Gabriel, Inc. (“Defendant”), to serve further,
verified responses to Plaintiff’s Requests for Production of Documents, Set
One, numbers 53-54, 85-87, 111-117, 127-128, and 149-170, and (2) awarding
monetary sanctions in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant in the amount of
$339.06.



The court finds that the discovery motion cutoff date has passed and
therefore denies Plaintiff’s motion. 
(Code Civ. Proc., § 2024.020, subd. (a).)



Except as otherwise provided, “any party shall be entitled as a matter
of right to complete discovery proceedings on or before the 30th day, and to
have motions concerning discovery heard on or before the 15th day, before the
date initially set for the trial of the action.”  (Code Civ. Proc., §
2024.020, subd. (a).)  “Thus, if a party properly notices a discovery
motion to be heard on or before the discovery motion cutoff date, that party
has a right to have the motion heard.  By negative implication, a
party who notices a discovery motion to be heard after the discovery
motion cutoff date does not have a right to have the motion heard.”  (Pelton-Shepherd
Industries, Inc. v. Delta Packaging Products, Inc. (2008) 165 Cal.App.4th
1568, 1586 [emphasis in original].) 



On March 6, 2023, the court set this case for trial on May 15,
2024.  (March 6, 2023 Minute Order, p.
1.)  On May 2, 2024, the court (1)
continued trial, pursuant to the request of Plaintiff, from May 15, 2024 to
July 10, 2024, and (2) ordered that “[a]ll discovery cut-off and discovery
motion cut-off dates, and deadlines for the exchange of information concerning
expert trial witnesses, shall be based on the date initially set for trial of this
action (5/15/24) and shall not be reopened or based on the new trial date,
except that Defendants may take the deposition of Plaintiff Emilio Reyes
pursuant [to] the Court’s 5/1/24 order.” 
(May 2, 2024 Minute Order, pp. 1-2.)  On June 27, 2024, the court continued trial
from July 10, 2024 to July 17, 2024 on the court’s own motion.  (June 27, 2024 Minute Order, p. 2.)  The court did not reopen discovery or extend
the discovery and discovery motion cutoff dates in that order.  (Ibid.)  Thereafter, on July 15, 2024, the court
issued a minute order (1) continuing trial from July 17, 2024 to October 16,
2024, and (2) ordering that “the discovery cut-off and discovery motion cut-off
dates are only extended with respect to the deposition of Plaintiff, Emilio
Reyes.”  (July 15, 2024 Minute Order, p.
1.)



Thus, the court finds that the discovery motion cutoff date in this
action was on April 30, 2024, i.e., 15 days before the date initially set for
trial (May 15, 2024).  (Code Civ. Proc.,
§ 2024.020, subd. (a).)  Because
Plaintiff (1) noticed this motion for hearing after the discovery motion cutoff
date, and (2) did not file a motion requesting leave to have this motion
concerning discovery heard pursuant to section 2024.050, the court finds that
Plaintiff does not have a right to have this motion heard.  (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2024.020, subd. (a),
2024.050 [court “shall” take into consideration the factors in exercising its
discretion to grant or deny a motion for leave to reopen discovery]; Pelton-Shepherd
Industries, Inc., supra, 165 Cal.App.4th at p. 1586.)



The court denies Defendant’s request for monetary sanctions against
Plaintiff.



ORDER



            The court denies plaintiff Emilio
Reyes’s motion to compel further responses to requests for production of
documents.



            The court orders defendant Los
Indios de San Gabriel, Inc. to give notice of this ruling.



IT IS SO ORDERED.
