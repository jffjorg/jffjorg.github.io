DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

If you desire to submit on the tentative ruling, you may do so by e-mailing Dept. 26 at the Spring Street Courthouse until the morning of the motion hearing.

The e-mail address is SSCdept26@lacourt.org

The heading on your e-mail should contain the case name, number, hearing date, and that you submit. The message should indicate your name, contact information, and the party you represent. Please note, the above e-mail address is to inform the court of your submission on the tentative ruling. All other inquiries will not receive a response.

If there are no appearances by either side and no submission on the Court's tentative ruling, the matter will be placed OFF CALENDAR. 

Due to overcrowding concerns of COVID-19, all parties shall make every effort to schedule a remote appearance via LACourtConnect (https://my.lacourt.org/laccwelcome) for their next hearing. The parties shall register with LACourtConnect at least 2 hours prior to their scheduled hearing time.

Case Number: 23STLC01716    Hearing Date: November 26, 2024    Dept: 26

X-Tream Cleaning Service, Inc. v. Royal Case No. 23STLC01716

TENTATIVE RULING:             

Plaintiff X-Tream Cleaning Service, Inc.’s Motion to Compel Responses to Request for Admission, and Request for Sanctions, is DENIED. 

ANALYSIS:

On March 14, 2023, Plaintiff X-Tream Cleaning Service, Inc. filed the instant action for breach of contract against Defendant Royal Personal Training (“Defendant”). On February 21, 2024, the Court granted Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Sanctions. (Minute Order, 02/21/24.) Plaintiff filed a Motion to Compel Discovery Responses and Request for Sanctions, which came for hearing on October 22, 2024. The Court noted a number of defects with the Motion to Compel, including improperly filing a single discovery motion to compel responses with respect to four separate sets of discovery and failing to attach copies of the discovery requests served on Defendant. (Minute Order, 10/22/24.)

Plaintiff filed a supplemental declaration on October 22, 2024 and additional notices of motion demonstrating that these defects have been corrected.

Discussion

Plaintiff moves to compel Defendant’s responses to Request for Admission, Set Two, pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (b). (Notice of Motion, p. 1:24-25.) This statute, however, does not permit the Court to compel responses to Requests for Admission. Instead, it allows the Court to deem the requests for admission admitted. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (b) [“The requesting party may move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted.”].)

Also, as noted in the Court’s order dated November 25, 2024, the number of discovery requests propounded by Plaintiff exceeds the number permitted by Code of Civil Procedure section 94, subdivision (a).

Conclusion

Therefore, Plaintiff X-Tream Cleaning Service, Inc.’s Motion to Compel Responses to Request for Admission, and Request for Sanctions, is DENIED.

GMC Asset, LLC’s Petition to Release Property from Mechanic’s Lien is DENIED.  

ANALYSIS:

Petitioner GMC Asset, LLC (“Petitioner”) filed the instant Petition for Release of Mechanic’s Lien against Respondent K-Tech Construction, Inc. (“Respondent”) on October 28, 2024. No response to the Petition has been filed to date.

Discussion

Petitioner moves to have the mechanic’s lien recorded against their property by Respondent released. Under Civil Code section 8484 the petition for release order must be verified by the petitioner and allege the following: (a) The date of recordation of the claim of lien. A certified copy of the claim of lien shall be attached to the petition. 

(a) The date of recordation of the claim of lien. A certified copy of the claim of lien shall be attached to the petition.

(b) The county in which the claim of lien is recorded.

(c) The book and page or series number of the place in the official records where the claim of lien is recorded.

(d) The legal description of the property subject to the claim of lien.

(e) Whether an extension of credit has been granted under Section 8460, if so to what date, and that the time for commencement of an action to enforce the lien has expired.

(f) That the owner has given the claimant notice under Section 8482 demanding that the claimant execute and record a release of the lien and that the claimant is unable or unwilling to do so or cannot with reasonable diligence be found.

(g) Whether an action to enforce the lien is pending.

(h) Whether the owner of the property or interest in the property has filed for relief in bankruptcy or there is another restraint that prevents the claimant from commencing an action to enforce the lien.

(Civil Code, § 8484.) The Petition is defective in a number of respects. First, the pleading is not verified by an officer of the corporation, as required by Code of Civil Procedure section 446. Instead, the verification is merely signed by an unnamed “authorized agent” of Petitioner. (Pet., p. 5.) Second, the Petition does not show that Petitioner has not filed for relief in bankruptcy or there is another restraint that prevents Respondent from commencing an action to enforce the lien, as required by Civil Code section 8484, subdivision (h).

Civil Code section 8486, subdivision (b) requires service of the Petition and Notice of Hearing to “be made in the same manner as service of summons, or by certified or registered mail, postage prepaid, return receipt requested . . . .” (Civ. Code, § 8486, subd. (b).) Petitioner has filed a compliant proof of substitute service of the Petition and the Notice of the Hearing Date. (Proof of Substitute Service, filed 11/06/24.) This complies with the statute for service of process.

The Petition also seeks attorney’s fees and costs in connection with the instant proceeding under Civil Code section 8488, subdivision (c) but does not indicate the amount sought or provide proof of the same. (Pet., Prayer, ¶¶3-4.)

Conclusion 

Therefore, GMC Asset, LLC’s Petition to Release Property from Mechanic’s Lien is DENIED.

Loan Asset Issuer LLC Series M 2 v. Islam, et al. Case. No. 24STLC00518

TENTATIVE RULING:

Plaintiff Loan Asset Issuer LLC Series M 2’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted is GRANTED.

ANALYSIS:

On August 30, 2024, Plaintiff Loan Asset Issuer LLC Series M 2 (“Plaintiff”) served Requests for Admissions on Defendant Asif Islam (“Defendant”). (Motion, D’Anna Decl., Exh. A.) To date, Defendant has not served verified responses to the Requests for Admission. (Id. at ¶3.) Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted (“the Motion”) on October 17, 2024. No opposition has been filed to date.

Plaintiff moves for an order deeming the requests for admission served on Defendant admitted pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (b). There is no requirement for a prior meet and confer effort before a motion to deem requests for admission can be filed.

(Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.) Further, the motion can be brought at any time after the responding party fails to provide the responses. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.) Due to Defendant’s failure to timely serve verified responses and resultant waiver of objections, Plaintiff is entitled to an order deeming the Requests for Admission admitted. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.)

Conclusion

Plaintiff Loan Asset Issuer LLC Series M 2’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted is GRANTED.

Case Number: 24STLC01233    Hearing Date: November 26, 2024    Dept: 26

Munoz v. Treystman, et al., Case No. 24STLC01233

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendants Mark S. Treystman, DDS and New Century Dental Group’s Demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

Defendants Mark S. Treystman, DDS and New Century Dental Group’s Motion to Strike Portions of the Complaint is PLACED OFF CALENDAR AS MOOT.

Demurrer

ANALYSIS:

On February 16, 2024, Plaintiff Sandra Munoz (“Plaintiff”), in propria persona, filed the Complaint in this action against Defendants Mark S. Treystman, DDS (erroneously sued as Dr. Mark Treystman of New Century Dental Group (“Defendant Treystman”) and New Century Dental Group (“Defendant New Century Dental”). On October 9, 2024, Defendants filed the instant Demurrer to, and Motion to Strike Portions of, the Complaint. No opposition has been filed to date.

Allegations in the Complaint

The Complaint makes the following allegations, set forth at paragraph GN-1. In the summer of 2014, Plaintiff experienced severe pain on tooth #19 and consulted with Defendants. After taking an x-ray and examining the tooth, Defendant Treystman advised getting a root canal procedure done immediately. Defendant Treystman completed the procedure in the Summer of 2014 and informed Plaintiff that it went “perfectly.” Over the years, Plaintiff experienced minor discomfort in the area around the tooth and jaw but in February of 2023, experienced excruciating pain on that same tooth, #19. After examination and x-rays on February 13, 2023 with endodontists Dr. Sohn and Dr. Allyson Won of NYU Dentistry, Plaintiff was diagnosed with Symptomatic Apical Periodontitis and advised a root canal retreatment on tooth #19. On February 14, 2023, Dr. Sohn began the treatment. During the procedure, she shared her discovery of a separated instrument that was present in Plaintiff’s mesio-lingual canal from the original procedure done by Defendant Treystman. Dr. Sohn was unable to bypass the instrument and also noticed pus drainage from the messio-bucal canal. Dr. Sohn attempted to use the gentlewave machine to continue cleaning out the bacteria, but stopped because Plaintiff started experiencing unbearable pain. Dr. Sohn could not complete the root canal due to being unable to bypass the instrument.

Plaintiff was informed that apicoectomy surgery would eventually be needed to properly clean the infection and extract the instrument in order to save the tooth. Plaintiff was prescribed antibiotics) on February 16, 2023, to help with the pain and swelling that continued, and to help keep the infection at bay. At the end of September of 2023, Plaintiff began feeling awful pain on tooth #19 once again. On October 2, 2023, Plaintiff went in for an apicoectomy surgery consultation with Dr. Rabitsky and Dr. Pierre Wohlgemuth, of the same clinic at NYU Dentistry, who agreed that Plaintiff would be a good candidate for the surgery. Plaintiff alleges that during this visit and the next week, she experienced the worst pain she has ever felt. On October 3, Plaintiff was prescribed antibiotics to keep the bacteria at bay until surgery on November 16, 2023.

Demurrer to Complaint

The Demurrer is accompanied by a meet and confer declaration as required by Code of Civil Procedure section 430.41. (Meet and Confer Decl., filed 09/25/24.) Defendants demur on the grounds that the Complaint fails to allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action. (Citing Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (e).) Specifically, that the Complaint demonstrates it was brought after expiration of the statute of limitations. The statute of limitations for a healthcare malpractice action is set forth at Code of Civil Procedure section 340.5: “the time for the commencement of action shall be three years after the date of injury or one year after the plaintiff discovers, or through the use of reasonable diligence should have discovered, the injury, whichever occurs first. In no event shall the time for commencement of legal action exceed three years unless tolled for any of the following: (1) upon proof of fraud, (2) intentional concealment, or (3) the presence of a foreign body, which has no therapeutic or diagnostic purpose or effect, in the person of the injured person.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 340.5.)

The Complaint alleges that Plaintiff discovered the cause of her tooth pain was the separated instrument that was present in Plaintiff’s mesio-lingual canal from the original procedure done by Defendant Treystman during the procedure performed by Dr. Sohn on February 14, 2023. From that date, Plaintiff had one year to bring an action for negligence.

It is said the statutory “foreign body” exception only tolls the three-year outside limitation period and not the one-year “discovery” period. (Hills v. Aronsohn, supra, 152 Cal.App.3d at p. 761, 199 Cal.Rptr. 816.) What this means, of course, is that the “foreign body” exception in section 340.5 lifts the three-year outside limit entirely if a non-therapeutic “foreign body” has been left inside a patient. It gives that patient an unlimited time to discover or to use reasonable diligence to discover the presence of the “foreign body.” But once the patient discovers or through reasonable diligence would have discovered the “foreign body” and its role in her injury she only has one year to file her lawsuit against the practitioners responsible for leaving the “foreign body” inside her. That is, the patient is not free to completely ignore the statute of limitations just because she finds out her doctor left something inside her body which is causing her harm. She must still act within one year of her discovery the “foreign body” exists and is the negligent cause of her injury. (Ashworth v. Memorial Hospital (1988) 206 Cal.App.3d 1046, 1058.) Whether calculated by the calendar year, or the number of days in a year (365), the last day for Plaintiff to file the instant action was Tuesday, February 14, 2024. The instant action, however, was not filed until Thursday, February 16, 2024. Based on the action being filed two days past the statute of limitations, as evidenced by the allegations in the Complaint, the action is barred by the statute of limitations.

Defendants’ demurrer to the Complaint, therefore, is sustained.

Motion to Strike

Defendants move to strike the portion of the Complaint where Plaintiff alleges an entitlement to punitive damages. The Motion is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 436, on the grounds that the Court may “strike out any irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 436, subd. (a).) As with the Demurrer, Defendants have filed the required meet and confer declaration required by Code of Civil Procedure section 435.5. (Motion, Yu Decl., ¶3 and Exh. B.) In light of the ruling on the demurrer, however, the Motion to Strike is moot.

Leave to Amend

Leave to amend must be allowed where there is a reasonable possibility of successful amendment, however, the burden is on the complainant to show the Court that a pleading can be amended successfully. (Goodman v. Kennedy (1976) 18 Cal.3d 335, 348.) As Plaintiff has not filed an opposition to the demurrer or motion to strike, leave to amend is denied.

Conclusion

Defendants Mark S. Treystman, DDS and New Century Dental Group’s Demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

Defendants Mark S. Treystman, DDS and New Century Dental Group’s Motion to Strike Portions of the Complaint is PLACED OFF CALENDAR AS MOOT.

Moving party to give notice.
