DEPARTMENT M LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

ALERT

Due to Coronavirus, please consider appearing by phone for Department M cases.

 

Department M strongly encourages the use of  LA CourtConnect* for ALL hearings, without need for prior approval, unless live testimony by a witness is required.

 

The contact information for LA CourtConnect* is:

 

 

 https://lacourt.portalscloud.com/VCourt/

 

 

*Parties with a fee waiver on file may be eligible to appear at no/reduced cost


Dept. M issues tentative rulings in many, but not all motion hearings. There is no set time at which tentatives are posted. Please do not call the staff to inquire if a tentative will be posted. 

If parties are satisfied with the ruling, parties may submit on the tentative. However, if an opposing party does not submit, they will be permitted to argue. Please check with the other side before calling the courtroom to submit. The staff does not keep track of which parties submitted and which did not, so please do not ask. 

If a matter is also a scheduling hearing (CMC, TSC, OSC etc) an appearance is still required even if a party submits on the tentative ruling.

Hearing Date:                         October 23, 2023

 

Moving Parties:                      Cross-defendant Derek McLay

Responding Party:                  Cross-complainant Redondo Riviera Associates

Motion for Summary Judgment

 

            The court considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers.

RULING

            The motion for summary judgment is CONTINUED to ___________ to allow moving party to file the declaration of Kurt Baker in support of the motion for summary judgment. 

BACKGROUND

            On February 5, 2019, Kurt Baker, individual dba as Riviera Fitness, LLC and Dustin Holcomb, ind. dba as Riviera Fitness, LLC filed a complaint against Redondo Riviera Associates, LLC for (1) fraud, (2) breach of contract, (3) breach of covenant of good faith and fair dealing, and (4) rescission.

            On April 8, 2019, plaintiffs filed a FAC for (1) fraud-concealment, (2) fraud-false representation, (3) breach of contract, (4) breach of covenant of good faith and fair dealing, (5) gross negligence, and (6) rescission.

            On June 17, 2019, the parties stipulated, and the court ordered that plaintiffs were to file a second amended complaint.  The SAC alleges causes of action for (1) fraud-concealment, (2) fraud-false representation, (3) negligent misrepresentation, (4) breach of contract, (5) breach of covenant of good faith and fair dealing, (6) gross negligence, and (7) rescission.

            On September 5, 2019, the court overruled defendant’s demurrer to SAC as to the 4th and 5th causes of action and sustained with leave to amend as to the 6th cause of action.

            On September 25, 2019, Redondo Riviera Associates, LLC filed a cross-complaint against Kurt Baker, Dustin Holcomb, and Derek McLay for breach of contract.

            On December 31, 2019, the court sustained with leave to amend cross-defendant McLay’s demurrer to the cross-complaint because he was not a party to the lease and not a signatory to the lease.

            On January 22, 2020, Redondo Riviera Associates filed a FACC for breach of contract.

            On June 29, 2020, the court sustained with leave to amend Derek McLay’s demurrer to FACC.

            On July 24, 2020, Redondo Rivera Associates filed a SACC for breach of contract.

            On September 29, 2020, the court overruled McLay’s demurrer to the SACC.

            On February 9, 2022, the court denied McLay’s motion for summary judgment as to the SACC finding that there was a triable issue of material fact as to whether McLay was a partner with Baker and Holcomb in a partnership.

            On April 20, 2022, the court denied defendant Redondo Riviera’s motion for summary adjudication as to the 3rd, 4th, and 5th causes of action and granted it as to the 6th cause of action.

            On June 21, 2022, the court denied without prejudice plaintiffs’ motion for leave to file a TAC.

            On September 26, 2022, the case was reassigned to Dept. 8 in Inglewood.

            On October 24, 2022, the case was reassigned to Dept. M.

            On January 24, 2023, the court granted plaintiffs’ motion for leave to file a TAC to add Riviera Fitness, LLC as a separate plaintiff.  Plaintiffs filed the TAC.

            On May 17, 2023, at the hearing on defendant’s demurrer, the parties stipulated that a Fourth Amended Complaint may be filed.

            On May 30, 2023, plaintiffs filed a Fourth Amended Complaint.

LEGAL AUTHORITY

The purpose of a motion for summary judgment or summary adjudication “is to provide courts with a mechanism to cut through the parties’ pleadings in order to determine whether, despite their allegations, trial is in fact necessary to resolve their dispute.”  Aguilar v. Atlantic Richfield Co. (2001) 25 Cal. 4th 826, 843.  “Code of Civil Procedure section 437c, subdivision (c), requires the trial judge to grant summary judgment if all the evidence submitted, and ‘all inferences reasonably deducible from the evidence’ and uncontradicted by other inferences or evidence, show that there is no triable issue as to any material fact and that the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.”  Adler v. Manor Healthcare Corp. (1992) 7 Cal. App. 4th 1110, 1119.

“On a motion for summary judgment, the initial burden is always on the moving party to make a prima facie showing that there are no triable issues of material fact.”  Scalf v. D. B. Log Homes, Inc. (2005) 128 Cal. App. 4th 1510, 1519.  A defendant moving for summary judgment or summary adjudication “has met his or her burden of showing that a cause of action has no merit if the party has shown that one or more elements of the cause of action . . . cannot be established, or that there is a complete defense to the cause of action.”  CCP § 437c(p)(2).  “Once the defendant . . . has met that burden, the burden shifts to the plaintiff . . . to show that a triable issue of one or more material facts exists as to the cause of action or a defense thereto.” CCP § 437c(p)(2). 

“When deciding whether to grant summary judgment, the court must consider all of the evidence set forth in the papers (except evidence to which the court has sustained an objection), as well as all reasonable inferences that may be drawn from that evidence, in the light most favorable to the party opposing summary judgment.” Avivi v. Centro Medico Urgente Medical Center (2008) 159 Cal. App. 4th 463, 467; CCP §437c(c).

DISCUSSION

Cross-defendant Derek McLay requests summary judgment in favor of McLay and against cross-complainant Redondo Riviera Associates, LLC on the grounds that no triable issue of material fact exists as McLay was not a party to the lease or partner in a partnership that was a party to the lease and that McLay is entitled to a judgment as a matter of law.

The court notes that moving party cites to the declaration of Kurt Baker and that the opposition filed evidentiary objections.  The declaration of Derek McLay was filed twice.  The declaration of Kurt Baker was not filed.

Accordingly, the motion is CONTINUED to allow moving party to file the Baker declaration.

LEGAL AUTHORITY

“The function of the receiver is to aid the court in preserving and managing the property involved in a particular lawsuit for the benefit of those to whom it can ultimately be determined to belong.”  City of Sierra Madre v. SunTrust Mortgage, Inc. (2019) 32 Cal. App. 5th 648, 656.

            “[C]ourt rulings on receivership matters are afforded considerable deference on review.   Such deference is the rule, even where the court confirms extraordinary action by the receiver. . . .”  City of Santa Monica v. Gonzalez (2008) 43 Cal.4th 905, 931 (citation omitted). Thus, “[w]here there is no evidence of fraud, unfairness, or oppression, the court has wide discretion in approving the receiver's proposed actions.”  County of Sonoma v. U.S. Bank (2020) 56 Cal. App. 5th 657, 671.

DISCUSSION

Stephen J. Donell, Receiver

3.     The Statement of Claim must be actually received by the Receiver on or before 5:00 pm PDT on the date that is 30 days after the date of service of a notice of entry of the order establishing a claims resolution procedure (the "Claims Deadline"). If the date that is 30 days after the date of service of such notice of entry is a Saturday, Sunday or court holiday, the Claims Deadline shall be 5:00 pm PDT on the next day that is not a Saturday, Sunday or court holiday.

5.     The Receiver shall notify all parties of his receipt of a Statement Claim within three business days of his receipt of such Statement of Claim (“Receiver Claim Receipt Date”). Notice shall be made via email to all parties.

6.     Any party may within 30 days after the Receiver Claim Receipt Date submit to the Receiver and serve on the other parties a written statement supporting or opposing a Statement of Claim submitted by any other party. Such statement shall be accompanied by all documents or other evidence supporting the party’s position and by a short statement of the facts and legal authorities supporting the party’s position.

BACKGROUND

            On September 22, 2022, plaintiffs Robert W. Mathews, by and through his attorney in fact, Norbert Rock, and Norbert Rock filed a complaint against Palm Consulting Services, Inc. and Amity Care Inc. for (1) to void all contracts entered into by Mathews or on his behalf executed after July 17, 2020 due to legal incapacity, (2) to void all contracts entered into by Mathews or on his behalf executed after July 17, 2020 due to undue influence, (3) rescission and revocation of power of attorney, (4) declaratory relief, (5) specific performance of oral contract for visits and phone contact, and (6) elder financial abuse.

LEGAL AUTHORITY

CCP § 438 states, in relevant part:  “(b)(1) A party may move for judgment on the pleadings. . . . (c)(1) The motion provided for in this section may only be made on one of the following grounds:  (A) If the moving party is a plaintiff, that the complaint states facts sufficient to constitute a cause or causes of action against the defendant and the answer does not state facts sufficient to constitute a defense to the complaint.  (B) If the moving party is a defendant, that either of the following conditions exist: . . . . (ii) The complaint does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action against that defendant.” 

A motion for judgment on the pleadings “has the purpose and effect of a general demurrer.”  Smiley v. Citibank (s.D.), N.A. (1995) 11 Cal. 4th 138, 146 (citation omitted).  “[T]he trial court generally confines itself to the complaint and accepts as true all material facts alleged therein.  As appropriate, however, it may extend its consideration to matters that are subject to judicial notice.  In this, it performs essentially the same task that it would undertake in ruling on a general demurrer.”  Id. (citations omitted).

DISCUSSION

            Defendant Palm Consulting Services, Inc. requests that the court enter judgment on the pleadings in its favor on the 1st, 2nd, 4th, and 6th causes of action in the complaint on the ground that the complaint fails to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause or causes of action against defendant.

            The complaint alleges that plaintiff Robert Mathews currently resides in Manhattan Beach, was born in 1932, and suffers from ill health.  He has resided most of his life in Germany.  Complaint, ¶3.  Plaintiff Norbert Rock resides in Germany.  He is the attorney in fact for Mathews, pursuant to a duly authorized, durable power of attorney, executed in Germany, pursuant to German law, on February 12, 2016.  This power of attorney was certified for international use on October 23, 2020.  Rock is the son of the best friend of Mathews and his deceased wife and has maintained a very close, personal relationship with Mathews since he was a youth.  Id., ¶4.  Defendant Palm Consulting is the holder of a subsequently executed power of attorney executed in California on June 16, 2021.  Id., ¶6.

            The complaint further alleges that after Mathews’ wife deceased, Mathews continued to maintain a strong, fraternal relationship with Rock, as a de facto stepson.  Id., ¶13.  Mathews announced plans to visit California.  He requested that Rock maintain his estate interests in Germany, both while Mathews was present in Germany and while he was visiting California.  To that end, Mathews executed a power of attorney and health care proxy in favor of Rock in February 2016.  The POA is “durable” and of the highest authenticity in Germany, sanctified in the German courts and certified for international use in October 2020.  It can only be revoked by a valid declaration of the principal, and where the principal is incompetent, only invalidated or terminated by a formal order from a German court.  Id., ¶14.  Rock managed Mathews’ affairs in Germany and corresponded with him, both while Mathews was abroad in California and when he was in Germany.  Id., ¶15.

            The complaint further alleges that Mathews had an accident wherein he slipped and fell, striking his head, on July 17, 2020, while in California, necessitating his hospitalization.  Mathews was diagnosed with dementia while hospitalized, and subsequently, while at Santa Monica Health Care Center on December 8, 2020.  Id., ¶16.  Mathews subsequently attempted to negotiate transactions concerning his accounts at Wells Fargo bank wherein he maintained extensive cash accounts totaling more than $1 million.  Id., ¶17.  Wells Fargo bank determined that Mathews may have been, or was not competent, and referred him to their recommended attorney, Peter Goldberg, who in turn referred Mathews to defendant Palm Consulting Services.  Id., ¶18.  Notwithstanding that Mathews maintained full time health insurance policy in Germany, including long term care and nursing care insurance, and notwithstanding that Wells Fargo bank refused to allow Mathews to execute transactions concerning his own assets, due to their concern about his mental competency, Goldberg persuaded Mathews to sign an attorney-client fee agreement and, thereafter, various testamentary documents and referred Mathews to Palm Consulting Services who then improperly had Mathews sign their agreement for consulting services and “durable power of attorney for management of property and personal affairs” on June 16, 2021.  Id., ¶19.

            The complaint further alleges that in March 2020 and throughout this time, the US borders were closed to international visitors due to Covid-19 pandemic and Rock could not visit Mathews until November 2021.  Id., ¶20.  Although Rock had been conversing with Mathews by telephone every Saturday, the phone service/access was also terminated in 2021, leaving rock no choice but to come to the US when the border re-opened.  Although Rock was initially turned away when he came to physically visit Mathews at his town home in Manhattan Beach, on November 16, 2021, he was eventually permitted to visit Mathews, but only with multiple other persons present, and only once he insisted.  Then in March 2022, when Rock arrived, there was a note on the door informing Rock that he would not be permitted to visit with or communicate by telephone with Mathews any longer.  Id., ¶21.  Mathews, by this attorney in fact, is informed and believes that he has been prevented, against his will, from going outside of his home or communicating with outside parties, including Rock.  Mathews is further informed and believes that he has executed a last will and testament, bequeathing his estate to third parties of which he has no familiarity or knowledge, and no natural interest in benefitting; that he has had a health care agreement executed in favor of “Amity Care Services Inc.”; that his pension payments from Germany have been routed to Palm Consulting Services and Amity Care Services and Mathews meanwhile is being held captive in his Manhattan Beach townhome.  Id., ¶22.

            Under the 1st cause of action to void all contracts due to legal incapacity, plaintiffs allege that the German POA is still legally viable and authorizes Rock to act on Mathews’ behalf for all purposes; the POA was never revoked or terminated by competent declaration; and the POA can only be terminated by a German court of competent jurisdiction.  Id., ¶25.  Mathews was diagnosed with “unspecified dementia,” rendering him legally incapacitated, unable to execute any legal documents and susceptible to undue influence.  Id., ¶26.  Amity is a party to a caregiver agreement entered into on September 11, 2020, at a time Mathews was incapacitated.  It is null and void as Mathews was without legal capacity to enter into the agreement.  Id., ¶27.  All other legal agreements entered into after July 17, 2020, including the Durable Power of Attorney for Management of Property and Personal Affairs in favor of Palm executed on June 16, 2021, any attorney client agreements executed between Mathews and Goldberg, wills and trusts and other testamentary and non-testamentary documents prepared on Mathews behalf and consulting agreements with Palm and any other purported contracts executed by Mathews after July 17, 2020 are legally void due to Mathews’ incapacity.  Id., ¶28.

            The 2nd cause of action alleges the same and alleges undue influence.

            The 4th cause for declaratory relief alleges that a true dispute exists as to whether the POA executed in favor of Palm on June 16, 2021 is legally valid; a dispute also exists as to whether plaintiff’s preexisting POA in favor of Rock, executed in February 2016 remains valid and enforceable.  Id., ¶42. 

            The 6th cause of action for elder abuse alleges that defendants took advantage of Mathews as a result of his disabled state.  Id., ¶51.  Defendants profited thereby at plaintiff’s disadvantage.  Id., ¶52.

            The complaint alleges that plaintiff(s) Rock, and Mathews by and through his attorney in fact Norbert Rock, have standing to bring this action pursuant to Probate Code §§4540 and 4541.  Complaint, ¶24.

            Probate Code §4540 states in part, “Subject to Section 4503, a petition may be filed under this part by any of the following persons:  (a) The attorney-in-fact. . . .” 

Under Probate Code §4541, “A petition may be filed under this part for any one or more of the following purposes:

(a) Determining whether the power of attorney is in effect or has terminated. . . .

(d) Declaring that the authority of the attorney-in-fact is revoked on a determination by the court of all of the following:

(1) The attorney-in-fact has violated or is unfit to perform the fiduciary duties under the power of attorney.

(2) At the time of the determination by the court, the principal lacks the capacity to give or to revoke a power of attorney.

(3) The revocation of the attorney-in-fact’s authority is in the best interest of the principal or the principal’s estate. . . .

(f) Compelling a third person to honor the authority of an attorney-in-fact.”

            Under Probate Code §4542, “A proceeding under this part is commenced by filing a petition stating facts showing that the petition is authorized under this part, the grounds of the petition, and, if known to the petitioner, the terms of the power of attorney.”

            See also Probate Code §4130(a), “If a principal grants inconsistent authority to one or more attorneys-in-fact in two or more powers of attorney, the authority granted last controls to the extent of the inconsistency.”  See Probate Code §§810-813 as to legal mental capacity.

            The matter puts in dispute the principles and subject of probate court related matters, including, in particular, Probate Code §§4130, 810 et seq., and 4540 and 4541. 

            Thus, in accordance with LA Court Rule 3.2, the court refers the matter to Dept. 1 for review, consideration, and determination by the supervising judge of civil as to whether the matter should be assigned to the probate court.  Probate Department of the Superior Court are courts of general jurisdiction having all the same power and authority as provided to the Superior Court.

            The motion is thus CONTINUED to allow for Dept. 1 determination.

RULING

            The motion is CONTINUED to ______________ to allow defendants to file their opposition and for the court to consider.

BACKGROUND

            On September 28, 2022, plaintiff Philadelphia Indemnity Insurance Company filed a complaint against Kaladar Co., Inc. dba Cal Auto Net and Mehdi Omrani for breach of contract.

LEGAL AUTHORITY

“Upon the filing of the complaint or at any time thereafter, the plaintiff may apply pursuant to this article for a right to attach order and a writ of attachment by filing an application for the order and writ with the court in which the action is sought.”  CCP § 484.010.

The application shall be executed under oath and must include:  (1) a statement showing that the attachment is sought to secure the recovery on a claim upon which an attachment may be issued; (2) a statement of the amount to be secured by the attachment; (3) a statement that the attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based; (4) a statement that the applicant has no information or belief that the claim is discharged or that the prosecution of the action is stayed in a proceeding under the Bankruptcy Act (11 U.S.C. section 101 et seq.); and (5) a description of the property to be attached under the writ of attachment and a statement that the plaintiff is informed and believes that such property is subject to attachment.  CCP § 484.020.

“The application [for a writ of attachment] shall be supported by an affidavit showing that the plaintiff on the facts presented would be entitled to a judgment on the claim upon which the attachment is based.”  CCP § 484.030.

The court shall issue a right to attach order if the court finds all of the following:

(1)   The claim upon which the attachment is based is one upon which an attachment may be issued.

(2)   The plaintiff has established the probable validity of the claim upon which the attachment is based.

(3)   The attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based.

(4)   The amount to be secured by the attachment is greater than zero.

CCP § 484.090.

            “A claim has ‘probable validity’ where it is more likely than not that the plaintiff will obtain a judgment against the defendant on that claim.”  CCP § 481.190.

DISCUSSION

            Plaintiff Philadelphia Indemnity Insurance Company seeks a right to attach order and an order for issuance of writ of attachment against defendants Kaladar Co., Inc. dba Cal Auto Net and Mehdi Omrani for $93,031.75, which includes $3000 in costs and $15,000 in attorney’s fees.

            The complaint alleges that on May 6, 2013, defendants executed a Motor Vehicle Dealer Bond Application and Indemnity Agreement in favor of plaintiff.  On April 25, 2016, plaintiff made, executed, and delivered a dealer surety bond in the sum of $50,000 to the California Department of Motor Vehicles under Vehicle Code §11710.  On June 2021 and November 2021, defendants, as indemnitors, were required to hold plaintiff harmless and indemnify plaintiff against any claim made against the bond but defendants did not do so.  Multiple claims were filed against plaintiff by alleged victims under the bond arising from alleged misconduct by defendants concerning the sale of motor vehicles.  Plaintiff gave defendants notice of each claim and demanded that defendants furnish facts to support a defense, to defend or pay the claims and hold plaintiff harmless.  Plaintiff remitted payment in the penal sum of the bond as follows:  $40,000 to Vonda Morris on June 4, 2021 and $10,000 to Noe Flores Gutierrez on November 17, 2021.  Plaintiff remitted attorney’s fees and costs in the sum of $13,856.96 for defense of the bond.  Plaintiff suffered damages for breach of the agreement in the sum of $63,856.96 with interest plus $14,367.81 in collection agency fees.

            This motion is based on a claim for breach of a Motor Vehicle Dealer Bond Application and Indemnity Agreement.  To establish a claim for breach of contract, a plaintiff must prove: (1) existence of a contract, (2) plaintiff’s performance or excuse for nonperformance, (3) defendant’s breach of the contract, and (4) damages incurred by plaintiff as a result of the breach.  Durell v. Sharp Healthcare (2010) 183 Cal. App. 4th 1350, 1367.

            Plaintiff submitted a declaration from Robin Wilcox, Claim Examiner, Surety Division and custodian of records for plaintiff, which states that plaintiff is an admitted surety insurer and furnishes bonds and undertakings to parties who have executed an express indemnity agreement.  Among the bonds typically furnished by plaintiff are license bonds including bonds in the penal sum of $50,000 required by auto dealers under Vehicle Code §11710.  The purpose of said bonds is to protect victims of misconduct committed by auto dealers.  Robin Wilcox decl., ¶1.  On April 14, 2016, defendants executed an Indemnity Agreement in favor of plaintiff.  The general indemnity agreement (Complaint, Exh. A) required that defendants indemnify and hold plaintiff harmless against any claim brought against plaintiff by an alleged victim of misconduct committed by Kaladar Co., Inc. dba Cal Auto Net.  The indemnity agreement covers all losses incurred by plaintiff.  The agreement authorized plaintiff to decide whether to pay, compromise, or appeal any claim against a bond.  Para. 6 of the indemnity agreement states:  “Principal agrees that Philadelphia have the exclusive right to decide whether to pay, compromise, or appeal any claim against a Bond.”  Id., ¶2, Exh. A.  On April 25, 2016, plaintiff made, executed and delivered a dealer surety bond in the sum of $50,000 to the DMV.  Id., ¶3.  Thereafter, defendants obtained a DMV license and commenced doing business as a motor vehicle dealer in Fullerton, California.  Id., ¶4. 

            Plaintiff also presents evidence that it received three claims against the bond from consumers who purchased allegedly defective used vehicles from Kalador.  The claimants, Vonda Morris, Jorge A. Corcio, and Noe Flores Gutierrez, filed civil actions against plaintiff.  In response to providing defendants with notice of the first claim (Corcio), the attorney for Kaladar responded to plaintiff.  When the second claim came in (Morris), plaintiff sent email notice to Kaladar’s attorney and also mailed noticed to defendants because plaintiff had no email address for defendants.  In response to that notice, Kaladar’s attorney responded to plaintiff.  The Gutierrez claim was the third claim and was received after the Morris claim was settled and closed.  Id., ¶5.  Kaladar’s attorney asserted to plaintiff that defendants had defenses to the claims including the contention that the vehicles were sold “as is” and that the sales agreements were subject to binding arbitration.  The sale of vehicles “as is,” however, cannot be asserted to avoid a dealer’s liability to buyers of used vehicles that have defects which have not been expressly disclosed.  Also, an arbitration clause in a motor vehicle contract cannot be asserted by a license bond surety to compel a consumer to arbitrate his/her claim against plaintiff.  Plaintiff was willing to permit defendants to assert their alleged defenses as counsel for plaintiff in the Vonda Morris action.  Id., ¶6.   Plaintiff tendered defense of the Vonda Morris action to defendants by email on March 2, 2020, demanding that the tender of defense agreement be signed and returned by March 7, 2020.  Defendants signed and returned the tender of defense on March 13, 2020.  The tender acceptance was late, and plaintiff had retained its own counsel to defend the Morris action.  Plaintiff is unaware of any steps taken by defendants to resolve the Corcio action or the Gutierrez action.  Id., ¶7.  Plaintiff’s attorney
