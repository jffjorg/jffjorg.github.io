DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23VECV00079    Hearing Date: February 8, 2024    Dept: 107

 

 

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

RITA ZETINO,

 

Plaintiff.

 

   v.

DAVIT ANDREASYAN,

 

Defendant.

 

 

 

   Case Number             Department

  23VECV00079                107

 

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: Motion to be Relieved as Counsel

                 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Counsel’s motion to be relieved as counsel is GRANTED.  

 

 

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

 

On January 9, 2023, Plaintiff Rita Zetino filed this motor vehicle negligence action against Defendant Davit Andreasyan.

 

On January 8, 2024, Joshua Zokaeem, Esq, counsel for Plaintiff Rita Zetino, filed the instant motion to be relieved as counsel. The motion is unopposed.

 

ANALYSIS

 

California Rule of Court rule 3.1362 (Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel) requires (1) notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client (made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-051)); (2) a declaration stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(1) (made on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-052)); (3) service of the notice of motion and motion and declaration on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and (4) the proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-053)).

 

The motion satisfies the procedural requirements. The notice of motion and motion, declaration, and proposed order are on the appropriate Judicial Council forms and addressed to the client. (CRC, rule 3.1362(a), (c), (e).) The proof of service indicates service on the client and all parties. Counsel represents he served the client at her last known address. However, he was unable to confirm this address after making the following efforts: mailing the motion papers to her last known address, return receipt requested; calling her last known telephone number or numbers; and commissioning the services of a private investigator. Based on these efforts, the Court finds that counsel has satisfied the service requirements. (CRC, rule 3.1362(d).)

 

The court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client, and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.)

 

Here, counsel states that “[t]here has been a significant breakdown in the attorney-client relationship, which has significantly impeded counsel's ability to effectively prosecute this case and represent Plaintiff's interest.” Further, trial in this matter is set for September 24, 2024, thus there is no prejudice to the client from withdrawal.

 

Accordingly, the motion is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s counsel is ordered to serve a copy of the order granting leave to withdraw on the client and all parties who have appeared in the action or proceeding. Counsel will be relieved upon filing proof of service on the client of the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel--Civil (Judicial Council form MC-053).

 

Plaintiff’s counsel is ordered to give notice of the ruling on the motion to be relieved. Counsel for Plaintiff is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days.

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: Motion to be Relieved as Counsel

                 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Counsel’s motion to be relieved as counsel is GRANTED. 

 

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

 

 

Plaintiffs’ attorney Bleau Fox, a Professional Law Corporation (“Counsel”) moves to be relieved as counsel.

 

Background

 

Plaintiffs filed this action on August 22, 2023, stating claims for (1) negligence, (2) premise liability, (3) unsafe conditions, and (4) intentional infliction of emotional distress.

 

Counsel filed these motions on January 10, 2024. No opposition was filed.

 

Legal Standard

 

An attorney in an action may be changed at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon the order of the court, upon the application of either client or attorney, after notice from one to the other. (CCP § 284(2)).  Rules of Court, rule 3.1362 requires:

 

1.         Notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client (made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to be Relieved as Counsel Civil form (MC-051));

2.         A declaration stating in general terms, and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney client relationship, why a motion under CCP §284(2), is brought instead of filing a consent under CCP §284(1) (made on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel Civil form (MC-052));

3.         Service of the notice of motion and motion and declaration on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and

4.         The proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel Civil form (MC-053)).

 

The motion should be denied if withdrawal would prejudice the client.  (Rules Prof. Conduct, Rule 3-700(A)(2); Weil & Brown, Cal. Prac. Guide: Civ. Pro. Before Trial, 9:385.2 (2008); 1 Witkin Cal. Pro., Attorneys §72 (2008).)  The motion should be denied if it will cause undue delay in the proceeding or cause injustice.  (Mandell v. Superior Court, (1977) 67 Cal.App.3d 1, 4.)  The determination whether to grant or deny an attorney’s motion to withdraw as counsel of record lies within the sound discretion of the trial court, having in mind whether such withdrawal might work an injustice in the handling of the case.  (Lempert v. Superior Court (2003) 112 Cal.App.4th 1161, 1173.)  A breakdown in the attorney-client relationship (or personality clash) is grounds for withdrawal.  (Estate of Falco (1987) 188 Cal.App.3d 1004, 1014.) An attorney is not limited to withdrawing from a case for cause and may withdraw when withdrawal can be accomplished without undue prejudice to the client.  (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 26 Cal. Rptr. 2d 554, 559.)   

 

Discussion

 

The motion satisfies the procedural requirements. Specifically, as it relates to notice under CRC Rule 3.1362(a), a notice of motion and motion to be relieved as counsel under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) must be directed to the client and must be made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-051). Here, that is complied with and the hearing date on the notices is correct (February 13, 2024). Next, under the requirement of a Declaration under CRC Rule 3.1362(c), the motion to be relieved as counsel must be accompanied by a declaration on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-052). The declaration must state in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(1).

The declaration in this case provides three reasons: (1) a “breakdown in attorney-client communication,” (2) failure by client to “cooperate with his counsel,” and (3) failure by client to “fulfil his financial obligations.” The reasons provided are identical on both declarations. (The declaration Form MC-052 lists the wrong hearing date.) Furthermore, under the service requirement pursuant to CRC Rule 3.1362(d) and LASC Local Rule 4.35, the notice of motion and motion, the declaration, and the proposed order must be served on the client and on all other parties who have appeared in the case. The notice may be by personal service or mail. If the notice is served on the client by mail under Code of Civil Procedure section 1013, it must be accompanied by a declaration stating facts showing that either:

 

(1)The service address is the current residence or business address of the client; or

(2)The service address is the last known residence or business address of the client and the attorney has been unable to locate a more current address after making reasonable efforts to do so within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved.

 

As used in this rule, "current" means that the address was confirmed within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved. Merely demonstrating that the notice was sent to the client's last known address and was not returned is not, by itself, sufficient to demonstrate that the address is current. If the service is by mail, Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b) applies.

 

When an attorney files a motion to withdraw as attorney of record for a fiduciary, service must be made by citation. The citation must be served in the manner provided in Code of Civil Procedure sections 415.10 or 415.30. If the fiduciary resides outside of California, service may also be made in the manner provided in Code of Civil Procedure section 415.40.  (LASC Local Rules, Rule 4.35.)

 

In the current case, the declaration states that the motion was served at the client's current electronic address, which was confirmed within 30 days before the filing of the motion. It attaches (to the Notices of Motion) an attachment purporting to prove that the electronic mailing address was confirmed on January 9, 2024, but that attachment shows an email from a Sergey Vershinin, who is not party to this case, and does not explain any relationship between Sergey Vershinin and either Plaintiff.

 

Also, as it pertains to the requirement of a proposed order and CRC Rule 3.1362(e), the proposed order relieving counsel must be prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-053) and must be lodged with the court with the moving papers. The order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. If no hearing date is presently scheduled, the court may set one and specify the date in the order. After the order is signed, a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case. The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. In this case, the hearing date on proposed orders is incorrect. The proposed orders have the date of hearing therefore as February 9, 2024, but it is actually set for February 13, 2024. The proposed orders also do not list the Case Management Conference set for February 13, 2024, though they do list the prior Case Management Conference. Trial is not yet set.

 

The court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client, and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) Here, no trial date has been set and there is no prejudice to either side.

 

Accordingly, the motion is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s counsel is ordered to serve a copy of the order granting leave to withdraw on the client and all parties who have appeared in the action or proceeding. Counsel will be relieved upon filing proof of service on the client of the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel--Civil (Judicial Council form MC-053).

 

Plaintiff’s counsel is ordered to give notice of the ruling on the motion to be relieved. Counsel for Plaintiff is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days.

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: Application for Writ of Possession (CCP 512.010)  

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

TENTATIVE RULING: The Applications for Writ of Possession are GRANTED.

 

Procedural History

 

On November 28, 2023, Plaintiff Transport Funding, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed this breach of contract action (the “Complaint”) against Defendants LHC Trucking Inc. (“Buyer”) and Tigran Ghukasyan (“Ghukasyan” and, together with Buyer, “Defendants”) relating to the sale of 3 Volvos. Plaintiff alleges that Defendants failed to make installment payments and are in possession of the vehicles.

 

On November 30, 2023, Plaintiff filed an application for a writ of possession (the “Applications for Writs of Possession”) against each Defendant, along with proposed orders for writs of possession (the “Proposed Orders”). Plaintiff seeks a writ of possession against Defendants Buyer”) over the following property:

 

(1) 2021 Volvo VNL860, VIN 4V4NC9EH5MN271830 (“Truck #1”),

(2) 2021 Volvo VNL860, VIN 4V4NC9EH9EH9MN271832 (“Truck #2)[NOTE: This VIN appears to be incorrect. All VINs in North America are 17 digits long, but this one is 20. The correct VIN is written in Agreement #1/Complaint Exh. “5”: 4V4NC9EH9MN271832. All of Plaintiff’s filings have this typo, including both Applications for Writs of Possession and both Proposed Orders], and

 

 (3) 2021 Volvo VNL860 VIN 4V4NC9EH0MN271833 (“Truck #3” and, together with Truck #1 and Truck #2, the “Collateral” and, each, a “Truck”),

 

On January 2, 2024, Plaintiff served the Defendants by substituted service and on January 16, 2024, Plaintiff filed proofs of such service.

 

The applications are unopposed.

 

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

 

The following facts are drawn from the Complaint (as defined below).

 

On January 26, 2022, Buyer and Plaintiff’s assignor, Arrow Truck Sales, Inc. (“Seller”) entered a Conditional Sale Contract (“Agreement #1”) for the sale of two commercial trucks, Truck #1 and Truck #2, from Seller to Buyer in exchange for 60 monthly installments of $5,594.73. Simultaneously, Ghukasyan executed a Continuing Guaranty (“Guaranty #1”) of Buyer’s obligations under Agreement #1. The same day, Seller assigned its rights and interest in Agreement #1, Truck #1, and Truck #2 to Plaintiff. Plaintiff perfected its interest in Truck #1 and Truck #2 by obtaining Certificates of Title therefor from the State of California.

 

On March 7, 2022, Buyer and Plaintiff’s assignor, Arrow Truck Sales, Inc. (“Seller”) entered another Conditional Sale Contract (“Agreement #2” and, together with Agreement #1, the “Agreements”) for the sale of another commercial truck, Truck #3, from Seller to Buyer for $185,351.22, to be paid in 54 monthly payments of $3,432.43 commencing on April 22, 2022. (Newton Decl., ¶3, Exh. 1.) 3. Simultaneously, Ghukasyan executed a Continuing Guaranty (“Guaranty #2” and, together with Guaranty #1, the “Guaranties”) of Buyer’s obligations under Agreement #2. The same day, Seller assigned its rights and interest in Agreement #2 and Truck #3 to Plaintiff. Plaintiff perfected its interest in Truck #3 by obtaining a Certificate of Title from the State of California.

 

Buyer made payments to Plaintiff per the Agreements for about a year and a half. (Id. at ¶6.)

 

In September 2023, Buyer failed to make its monthly payment under Agreement #2, and in October 2023, Buyer failed to make its monthly payment under Agreement #1. Plaintiff accelerated the balances due under the Agreements as permitted by each of the Agreements. (Id. at ¶7.)  Plaintiff has made demands to Defendants that they deliver the Collateral to it, but Defendant has not done so. Plaintiff now seeks to recover from Defendants possession of the Collateral, the full balances due under the Agreements, late fees, interest at an annual rate of 18%, costs, and attorney’s fees. There is now due, owing and unpaid from Defendant to Plaintiff the sum of $115,675.32, including late charges of $686.48 and interest on the principal amount at the rate of 18% per annum. (Id. at ¶8.)

 

 

Summary of Applicable Law

 

“Upon the filing of the complaint or at any time thereafter, the plaintiff may apply pursuant to this chapter for a writ of possession by filing a written application for the writ with the court in which the action is brought.”  (CCP § 512.010(a).)

           

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 512.010(b), the application must be submitted under oath and include:

 

(1) A showing of the basis of the plaintiff's claim and that the plaintiff is entitled to possession of the property claimed. If the basis of the plaintiff's claim is a written instrument, a copy of the instrument shall be attached.

 

(2) A showing that the property is wrongfully detained by the defendant, of the manner in which the defendant came into possession of the property, and, according to the best knowledge, information, and belief of the plaintiff, of the reason for the detention.

 

(3) A particular description of the property and a statement of its value.

 

(4) A statement, according to the best knowledge, information, and belief of the plaintiff, of the location of the property and, if the property, or some part of it, is within a private place which may have to be entered to take possession, a showing that there is probable cause to believe that such property is located there.

 

(5) A statement that the property has not been taken for a tax, assessment, or fine, pursuant to a statute; or seized under an execution against the property of the plaintiff; or, if so seized, that it is by statute exempt from such seizure.

 

Before the hearing on the Writ of Possession, the Defendant must be served with (1) a copy of the summons and complaint; (2) a Notice of Application and Hearing; and (3) a copy of the application and any affidavit in support thereof.  (CCP § 512.030.)

 

“The writ will be issued if the court finds that the plaintiff's claim is probably valid and the other requirements for issuing the writ are established.”  (CCP § 512.040(b).)  “A claim has ‘probable validity’ where it is more likely than not that the plaintiff will obtain a judgment against the defendant on that claim.”  (CCP § 511.090.) 

 

Prior to the issuance of a writ of possession, the Plaintiff must file an undertaking “in an amount not less than twice the value of the defendant's interest in the property or in a greater amount.”  (CCP § 515.010(a).) 

 

Analysis

1.         Notice

 

Notice appears proper. 

 

2.         Basis of Plaintiff’s Claim

 

Plaintiff seeks a writ of possession based on its claims for (1) breach of written agreement, (2) claim & delivery, (3) conversion, (4) goods sold and delivered, and (5) breach of guaranty.

 

Plaintiff attaches copies of the Agreements and the Guaranties. Each Agreement includes a provision that, in the event of a default, which includes a failure by Byer to “pay when due any amount owed by it to [Plaintiff],” Plaintiff may exercise its “right to require Buyer to assemble the Collateral and deliver it  to [Plaintiff] at a place to be designated by [Plaintiff] which is reasonably convenient to [Plaintiff], and to lawfully enter any premises where the Collateral may be without judicial process and take possession thereof.” (Newton Decl. Exh. “1” ¶¶ F(a), G(v), Exh. “5” ¶¶ F(a), G(v).)

 

3.         Wrongful Detention

 

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 512.010(b)(2), the application must include “a showing that the property is wrongfully detained by the defendant, of the manner in which the defendant came into possession of the property, and, according to the best knowledge, information, and belief of the plaintiff, of the reason for the detention.”

 

Buyer entered, and Ghukasyan guaranteed, the Agreements, giving Plaintiff the right to immediate possession of the Collateral upon default. (Newton Decl. ¶¶ 3, 10, 13, 20, Exh. “1” ¶¶ G(v), Exh. “5” ¶¶ G(v).) Per the Agreements, Defendants took immediate possession of the Collateral. (Newton Decl. ¶¶ 3, 5, 13, 15.) Defendants subsequently defaulted. (Newton Decl. ¶¶ 6, 16.) Defendants refused to return the Collateral per the Agreements. (Newton Decl. ¶ 25.)

 

4.         Description and Value of Property

 

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 512.010(b)(3), the application must include a particular description of the property and a statement of its value.

 

The application particularly describes the Collateral by make, model, and VIN, and states the value of each Truck per the JD Power Guide. (Newton Decl. ¶¶ 28–31.) We note, however, that the VIN for Truck #1 is incorrectly written on Plaintiff’s filings, including on both Applications for Writs of Possession and on both Proposed Orders.

 

5.         Statutory Statements

 

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 512.010(b)(4)-(5), the application must include:

 

(4) A statement, according to the best knowledge, information, and belief of the plaintiff, of the location of the property and, if the property, or some part of it, is within a private place which may have to be entered to take possession, a showing that there is probable cause to believe that such property is located there.

 

(5) A statement that the property has not been taken for a tax, assessment, or fine, pursuant to a statute; or seized under an execution against the property of the plaintiff; or, if so seized, that it is by statute exempt from such seizure.

 

Plaintiff specifically states the addresses of two locations where it believes the Collateral is located. (Newton Decl. ¶ 23.) Both locations appear to be private real property, but Plaintiff does not make any showing of its belief that the Collateral is located there.

 

The Collateral is not being seized pursuant to statute or under execution. (Newton Decl. ¶ 26.)

 

6. Undertaking

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 515.010 requires an undertaking to be filed before the writ issues in the amount of “not less than twice the value of the defendant’s interest in the property.”

 

Since the balance owed under the Agreements ($298,839.64, being the sum of $183,164.32 owed under Agreement #1 and $115,675.32 owed under Agreement #2) is greater than the value of the Collateral ($222,000.00, being the product of three Trucks at $74,000.00 each), Defendants have no interest in the Collateral. (Newton Decl. ¶¶ 8, 18, 29, 31.) Therefore, no undertaking is required to be posted by Plaintiffs.

 

Conclusion

 

The Applications for Writ of Possession are GRANTED.
