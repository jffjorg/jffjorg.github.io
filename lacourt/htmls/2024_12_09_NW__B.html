Case Number: 20STCV37624    Hearing Date: December 9, 2024    Dept: B

MICHAEL MORRIS Plaintiff, vs. SUPERIOR COURT OF CALIFORNIA COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES, A MUNICIPAL ENTITY, ET AL. Defendants. CASE NO: 20STCV37624 [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES’S DEMURRER TO PLAINTIFF’S SECOND AMENDED COMPLAINT Dept. B 8:30 a.m. December 9, 2024

I. BACKGROUND

On October 1, 2020, Plaintiff Michael Morris (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint against Defendants Superior Court of California County of Los Angeles (“Superior Court”), the County of Los Angeles (“County”), and Does 1 to 50 (collectively “Defendants”) alleging two causes of action: (1) premises liability; and (2) general negligence. Plaintiff alleges that, on September 17, 2019, due to Defendants’ negligence, Plaintiff suffered injuries when he sat on a chair which collapsed underneath him at the Van Nuys Courthouse.

On May 24, 2022, the County filed a demurrer to Plaintiff’s complaint alleging that the complaint failed to allege a statutory basis for liability against the County as required by the

Government Claims Act. The Court sustained the County’s demurrer with leave to amen on August 10, 2022.

On August 16, 2022, Plaintiff filed their first amended complaint (“FAC”) alleging one cause of action for premises liability pursuant to Cal. Gov. Code §§ 815.2(a), 820(a), 835 and 840.2.

On November 17, 2022, the Superior Court filed a demurrer to Plaintiff’s FAC, alleging that the FAC failed to state facts sufficient to support a cause of action against the Superior Court since the Superior Court does not own or control the subject premises. The Court sustained the Superior Court’s demurrer with leave to amend on January 13, 2023.

On January 24, 2023, Plaintiff filed their second amended complaint (“SAC”) with additional facts.

On February 23,2023, the County filed its Answer to the SAC.

On June 5, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B of Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

On July 17, 2024, the County filed the instant demurrer to Plaintiff’s SAC.

On November 20, 2024, Plaintiff filed their opposition.

On December 2, 2024, the County filed their reply.

II. LEGAL STANDARD

A demurrer is a pleading that may be used to test the legal sufficiency of the factual allegations in a complaint. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 430.10.) There are two types of demurrers: general demurrers and special demurrers. (See McKenney v. Purepac Pharmaceutical Co. (2008) 167 Cal.App.4th 72, 77.)

General demurrers can be used to attack pleadings for failure to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action or for lack of subject matter jurisdiction. (CCP § 430.10(e); McKenney, 167 Cal.App.4th at 77.) Such demurrers can be used only to challenge defects that appear on the face of the pleading or from matters outside the pleading that are judicially noticeable; evidence or extrinsic matters are not considered. (CCP §§ 430.30 and 430.70; Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318; Donabedian v. Mercury Ins. Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994.) For the purpose of testing the sufficiency of the cause of action, the Court admits “all material facts properly pleaded” and “matters which may be judicially noticed,” but does not consider contentions, deductions, or conclusions of fact or law. [Citation].” (Blank, 39 Cal.3d at 318.) It gives these facts “a reasonable interpretation, reading it as a whole and its parts in their context.” (Ibid.). At the pleading stage, a plaintiff need only allege ultimate facts sufficient to apprise the defendant of the factual basis for the claim against him. (Semole v. Sansoucie (1972) 28 Cal. App. 3d 714, 721.) The face of the complaint includes exhibits attached to the complaint. (Frantz v. Blackwell (1987) 189 Cal.App.3d 91, 94.) "If facts appearing in the exhibits contradict those alleged, the facts in the exhibits take precedence." (Holland v. Morse Diesel Intern., Inc. (2001) 86 Cal.App.4th 1443, 1447.) Special demurrers can be used to attack the pleadings on grounds that the pleading is uncertain, ambiguous, and unintelligible, or in a contract case, for failure to allege whether a contract is oral or written. (CCP § 430.10(f).)

CCP § 430.41 requires that “[b]efore filing a demurrer pursuant to this chapter, the demurring party shall meet and confer in person or by telephone with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to demurrer for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer.” (CCP § 430.41(a).) The parties are to meet and confer at least five days before the date the responsive pleading is due.

(CCP § 430.41(a)(2).) Thereafter, the demurring party shall file and serve a declaration detailing their meet and confer efforts. (CCP § 430.41(a)(3).)

When a demurrer is sustained, the Court determines whether there is a reasonable possibility that the defect can be cured by amendment. (Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318.) When a plaintiff “has pleaded the general set of facts upon which his cause of action is based,” the court should give the plaintiff an opportunity to amend his complaint, since plaintiff should not “be deprived of his right to maintain his action on the ground that his pleadings were defective for lack of particulars.” (Reed v. Norman (1957) 152 Cal.App.2d 892, 900.) Generally, the court will allow leave to amend on at least the first try, unless there is absolutely no possibility of overcoming the issue. (See Angie M. v. Superior Court (1995) 37 Cal.App.4th 1217, 1227 [“Denial of leave to amend constitutes an abuse of discretion unless the complaint shows on its face it is incapable of amendment. (Citation.) Liberality in permitting amendment is the rule, if a fair opportunity to correct any defect has not been given."].)

III. DISCUSSION

Evidentiary Objections

Plaintiff’s December 3, 2024, objection to the reply declaration of Gary Bacio (“Bacio”) is overruled. Plaintiff misstates Bacio’s declaration. Baccio does not raise new matters or facts in their reply papers as Plaintiff claims; instead, it replies directly to Plaintiff’s arguments made in opposition of the County’s demurrer.

Untimeliness

As a preliminary matter, the County’s demurrer is untimely. Plaintiff filed their SAC on January 24, 2023. The County filed their demurrer on July 17, 2024, a year and a half after Plaintiff’s filing. Demurrers must be filed within 30 days after service of the complaint which is subject to demur. (CCP § 430.40(a).) Therefore, the County’s demurrer is overruled as untimely. Moreover, the County Answered the SAC on February 23, 2023, Nonetheless, for both parties’ benefit, the Court now chooses to discuss the motion’s substance.

Meet and Confer Requirement

The County fails to provide a meet and confer declaration as required by CCP § 430.41.(a). Therefore, the County’s motion is further denied on procedural grounds.

County’s Argument

The County argues that Plaintiff’s SAC fails to allege facts sufficient to constitute causes of action against the County because Plaintiff failed to file a claim for damages as required by Gov. Code § 945.4.

Under Gov. Code § 945.4, no personal injury suit for money or damages may be brought against a government entity until a written claim is presented to and acted upon by the Government Claims Program (“GCP”). (Gov. Code § 945.4.) The claim must be presented no later than six months after accrual of the cause of action. (Gov. Code § 911.2(a).) However, a claimant may apply to the GCP for late claim relief. (Gov. Code § 911.4.) If claimant’s application is denied, then they may petition the court for an order for relief from the presentation requirement of § 945.4. (Gov. Code § 946.6.)

Here, the County relies on the declaration of Katherine Medina (“Medina”), Deputy Clerk of the Board of Supervisors for the County of Los Angeles, to support its claim that

Plaintiff failed to file a claim of damages pursuant to Gov. Code § 945.4. (See Motion; Medina Decl. ¶¶ 1-3.) However, evidence and extrinsic matters cannot be considered for the purposes of ruling on a demurrer. (CCP §§ 430.30 and 430.70; Blank, supra, 39 Cal.3d at 318; Donabedian, supra, 116 Cal.App.4th at 994.) The Court may only consider the face of the pleading or matters outside of the pleading that are judicially noticeable. (Ibid.) Here, the Medina declaration is not a judicially noticeable matter. Further, the SAC at paragraph 9 states that Plaintiff has complied with applicable claims statutes. Therefore, on both procedural and substantive grounds, the County’s demurrer is overruled.

IV. CONCLUSION

The County’s demurrer is OVERRULED.

MUTIAT MAJEKODUNMI, Plaintiff, vs. ROBERT HAMANOTO, Defendant. Case No.: 21STCV01327 Hearing Date: DECEMBER 9, 2024

Defendant Robert Hamamoto’s motion to compel the independent medical examination of Plaintiff Mutiat Majekodunmi is granted. Defendant’s request for sanctions is also granted in the reduced amount of $2,103.18.

Defendant Robert Hamamoto (“Defendant”) moves to compel the independent medical examination (“IME”) of Plaintiff Mutiat Majekodunmi (“Plaintiff”), contending that there is good cause for the motion because Plaintiff placed her physical injuries in controversy. (Motion p. 5:11-20.)

“Unless otherwise limited by order of the court …, any party may obtain discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, that is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action …, if the matter either is itself admissible in evidence or appears reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence.” (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 2017.010.)

Where any party seeks to obtain discovery by a physical examination, the party shall obtain leave of the court. (CCP § 2032.310(a).) The motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under CCP § 2016.040. (CCP § 2032.310(b).) The Court shall grant the motion only for good cause shown. (CCP§ 2032.320(a).)

Meet and Confer

On May 21, 2024, defense counsel served notice of a Demand for Physical Examination of Plaintiff on plaintiff’s counsel, to be conducted on August 6, 2024, by orthopedic surgeon Michael P. Weinstein, M.D. (“Dr. Weinstein”). (Babadjanian Decl. ¶ 7; Exh. E [Demand].) Defense counsel sent Plaintiff’s counsel email reminders regarding the noticed examination on July 30, August 1, and August 3, 2024. (Ibid. at ¶ 9; Exh. F [email correspondences].) Plaintiff’s counsel did not respond. (Ibid.) Plaintiff failed to appear for the August 6, 2024, examination. (Ibid. at ¶ 10.) On August 21, 2024, Defense counsel sent Plaintiff’s counsel a meet and confer letter regarding the same. (Ibid. at 12; Exh. H [email].) Plaintiff’s counsel stated they had no authorization to enter into a stipulation to facilitate Plaintiff’s examination. (Ibid.) Accordingly, the Court finds that Defendant satisfies the meet and confer declaration requirement.

Separate Statement Requirements

A motion for “medical examination over objection” must be accompanied by a separate statement. (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1345(a).) Here, Plaintiff did not object to Defendant’s noticed examination. Accordingly, a separate statement is not required.

Good Cause

Defendant contends that there is good cause to compel Plaintiff’s independent medical examination (“IME”) because Plaintiff placed her physical injuries in controversy. In response to questioning at her February 7, 2024, deposition, Plaintiff claims injuries to her head, neck, lower back, shoulders, left hand and wrist, knees, and ankles. (Babadjanian Decl. ¶ 4; Exh. A [Plaintiff’s depo.] at 14:24-15:1, 33:3-25, 68:15-21, 72:13-73:10, 74:20-76:25,14:13-23, 35:6-10, and 36:7-12.)

Plaintiff did not object to Defendant’s noticed IME or oppose this motion. Without an IME, Defendant cannot fully evaluate Plaintiff’s injuries and prepare a defense for trial. Accordingly, the Court finds that Defendant has shown good cause to compel Plaintiff's IME.

Required Specifications of the Proposed IME

A motion for an examination must specify the time, place, manner, conditions, scope, and nature of the examination, as well as the identity and specialty, if any, of the person or persons who will perform the examination. (CCP § 2032.310(b).)

Defendant provides a scheduled IME for September 17, 2024, or alternatively on such date and time as Dr. Weinstein is available, depending on the hearing of the instant motion and Dr. Weinstein’s availability. (Motion p. 2:5-9.) The IME will take place at 360 San Miguel Drive, Suite 701, Newport Beach, CA 92660. (Ibid. at 10-11.) Defendant states that the IME “will be conducted for the purposes of determining, evaluating and assessing plaintiff’s physical condition and claims of injury in controversy in this action.” (Ibid. at 18-19.) The examination “will be consistent with the practices of orthopedic surgery in Southern California and consultations in connection with such practices.” (Ibid. at 25-26.) Finally, the exam may include the taking of x-rays, CT scans, or MRIs as the performing physician deems necessary. (Ibid. At 3:1-2.)

Accordingly, the Court finds that Defendant provides the required specifications of the proposed IME.

Sanctions

The Court declines to award discovery sanctions as Plaintiff’s counsel advised moving party that there was no authority to stipulate to an IME prior to the noticed examination and leave of Court is otherwise required pursuant to a showing of good cause.

Conclusion

For the reasons set forth above, the motion to compel Plaintiff’s independent medical examination is GRANTED. The parties are ordered to meet and confer to arrange a mutually convenient date and time to conduct the IME within the next 45 days.

Case Number: 21STCV32188    Hearing Date: December 9, 2024    Dept: B

NATAN TIPS, A MINOR, BY AND THROUGH HIS GAL CHELSEA HILES, ET AL. VS DOE DRIVER, ET AL.

MOTION FOR REMOVAL OF CURRENT GUARDIAN AD LITEM AND APPROVAL FOR FILING A NEW APPLICATION AND ORDER FOR APPOINTMENT OF GUARDIAN AD LITEM

Date of Hearing: December 9, 2024 Trial Date: None

Department: B Case No.: 21STCV32188

Moving Party: Plaintiffs Natan Tips, by and through his guardian ad litem Chelsea Hiles, and Chelsea Hiles

Responding Party: None

BACKGROUND

This action arises from an alleged October 2019 motor vehicle accident. On August 31, 2021, Plaintiffs Natan Tips (“Natan”), a minor by and through his guardian ad litem Chelsea Hiles, and Chelsea Hiles (“Hiles”) sued Defendants Doe Driver (“Driver”), Salvador Flores, (“Flores”), and Does 1 to 20 (collectively “Defendants”) alleging one cause of action for motor vehicle negligence.

On September 1, 2021, Hiles was approved as guardian ad litem for Natan.

On April 9, 2024, a request for order approving compromise of disputed claim or pending action or disposition of proceeds of judgment for minor or person with a disability (“minor’s comp.”) was granted on behalf of Natan.

On June 5, 2024, this action was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

On November 13, 2024, Plaintiffs filed the instant motion requesting the removal of Hiles as guardian ad litem and the appointment of a new guardian ad litem thereof. Plaintiffs also request leave to file a new expedited minor’s comp. petition.

[TENTATIVE] RULING

The Court GRANTS the motion for removal of current guardian ad litem and approval for filing a new application and order for appointment of guardian ad litem. The Court also grants leave for Plaintiffs to file an amended expedited minor’s comp. petition.

LEGAL STANDARD

Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 372 provides that a minor must appear in litigation by a guardian or conservator of his estate or a guardian ad litem appointed by the court. A guardian ad litem is an officer of the court appointed to represent a minor in litigation, and to protect and promote the minor's interests. Like any other officer of the court (receiver, conservator, referee, etc.), a guardian ad litem is subject to court supervision; should a guardian ad litem take an action inimical to the legitimate interests of the minor, the court retains the supervisory authority to rescind or modify the action taken. (Regency Health Services, Inc. v. Superior Court (1998) 64 Cal.App.4th 1496, 1501-1502.)

A guardian ad litem's removal, as well as his appointment, is also under the control of the court in which the case is pending. (Estate of Anna M. Hathaway v. James A. White (1896) 111 Cal. 270, 271.) Nevertheless, subject to the court's ultimate supervision, the guardian has the authority and the duty to control the litigation for the benefit of the minor and to act on the minor's behalf in the litigation. In making the determination concerning the appointment of a particular guardian ad litem, the court must consider whether the minor and the guardian have divergent interests. (Code Civ. Proc. § 372(b)(1)(D); Regency Health Services, Inc., supra, 64 Cal.App.4th at 1503.)

DISCUSSION

The entire Tips-Hiles family has relocated to Israel. (Motion p. 2:20-21.) Upon relocation to Israel, CHASE Bank unilaterally closed the account that Hiles chose to open a blocked account with. (Motion p. 3:3-6.) Additionally, the family thought that they would visit the U.S. before the end of 2024, therefore expecting to deposit the funds into a blocked account by October 4, 2024. (Motion p. 2:21-24.) However, due to the regional conflict, the family has informed Plaintiff’s counsel that leaving Israel, even temporarily, is challenging. (Ibid. at 24-26.) Therefore, Hiles was unable to deposit the settlement funds into a blocked account by the Court-ordered deadline of October 4, 2024.

Hiles has contacted a family friend and U.S. resident, Samuel Lovitch (“Lovitch”), to act as the new guardian ad litem. (Motion p. 4:22-23.) Lovitch has agreed to serve as the new guardian ad litem upon appointment. Lovitch has no financial interest or claim against the current guardian ad litem or minor and will not benefit from the settlement outcome. (Ibid. at p. 5:1-2.)

Finally, since the initial minor’s comp. was approved and signed in April 2024, Plaintiffs’ counsel has secured a second settlement for Natan from his parents’ underinsured motorist coverage with State Farm Mutaul Automobile Insurance Company. (Motion p. 6:11-13.) Plaintiffs would like to incorporate this settlement into an amended petition, consolidating State Farm’s settlement with Defendant Flores’s settlement. (Ibid. at 13-14.)

CONCLUSION

The Court finds Plaintiffs’ requests both reasonable and in the best interest of Natan. Therefore, the Court GRANTS Plaintiffs’ motion for removal of current guardian ad litem Chelsea Hiles.

Additionally, the Court grants leave to file a new guardian ad litem application and an amended expedited petition to approve compromise of a disputed claim or pending action or disposition of judgment for a minor or a person with a disability.
