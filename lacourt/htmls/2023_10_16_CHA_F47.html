Counsel wishing to submit on a tentative ruling may inform the clerk or courtroom assisant in North Valley Department F47, 9425 Penfield Ave., Chatsworth, CA 91311, at (818) 407-2247.  Please be aware that unless all parties submit, the matter will still be called for hearing and may be argued by any appearing/non-submitting parties. If the matter is submitted on the court's tentative ruling by all parties, counsel for moving party shall give notice of ruling. This may be done by incorporating verbatim the court's tentative ruling. The tentative ruling may be extracted verbatim by copying and specially pasting, as unformatted text, from the Los Angeles Superior Court’s website, http://www.lasuperiorcourt.org. All hearings on law and motion and other calendar matters are generally NOT transcribed by a court reporter unless one is provided by the party(ies).

Case Number: 21CHCV00576    Hearing Date: October 16, 2023    Dept: F47

Dept. F47

Date: 10/16/23

Case #21CHCV00576

 

MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES

(Requests for Production of Documents, Set 3)

 

Motion filed on 6/9/23.  Corrected (with electronic bookmarks) Motion filed 10/10/23.

 

MOVING PARTY: Plaintiff Ruben Ramirez

RESPONDING PARTY: Defendant Fresh Start Healthy School Meals, Inc.

NOTICE: ok

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order compelling Defendant Fresh Start Healthy School Meals, Inc. to provide further responses and documents to Plaintiff’s Requests for Production of Documents, Set 3.  Additionally, Plaintiff requests an award of sanctions against Defendant Fresh Start Healthy School Meals, Inc. and Defendant’s counsel in favor of Plaintiff in the amount of $4,560.00.

 

RULING: The motion is granted as set forth below. 

 

SUMMARY OF FACTS & PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

This action arises out of Plaintiff Ruben Ramirez’s (Plaintiff) employment as a truck driver with/for Defendants: (1) Juan Carlos Saucedo (Saucedo), (2) K12 School Services (K12),

(3) Fresh Start Healthy School Meals (Fresh Start Healthy) and (4) Fresh Start Meals, Inc. (Fresh Start Meals).  On 8/2/21, Plaintiff filed this action against Defendants for: (1) Unpaid Overtime; (2) Unpaid Minimum Wages; (3) Non-compliant Wage Statements; (4) Wages Not Timely Paid During and Upon Separation of Employment; (5) Unpaid Business Expenses; (6) Unpaid Meal Period Premiums; (7) Unpaid Rest Period Premiums; (8) Violation of California Business & Professions Code§§ 17200, et seq.; and (9) Failure to Provide Employment Records in Violation of Labor Code§§ 1198.5 & 432.7.

 

On 12/23/22, Saucedo, whom Plaintiff contends co-owns, controls and is the Chief Executive Officer of the entity defendants, testified that he is not the owner of Fresh Start Meals.  Rather, Saucedo claimed he purchased the assets, but not the liabilities, of Fresh Start Meals. 

 

In order to investigate Saucedo’s relationship with Fresh Start Meals, including any purchase of the entity and the relationship between the three entity defendants, Plaintiff served discovery on Defendants.

 

On 1/30/23, Plaintiff served Fresh Start Healthy with Requests for Production of Documents, Set 3.  (Roshanian Decl., Ex.A).  On 4/3/23, after being granted extensions of time to respond, Fresh Start Healthy served responses to the discovery.  (Id., Ex.B).  On 4/13/23, Plaintiff sent a meet and confer letter regarding what it perceived to be deficient responses.  (Id., Ex.C).  During the meet and confer process, it was ultimately agreed that Fresh Start Healthy would have until 5/19/23 to provide supplemental/further responses and Plaintiff’s deadline to file a motion for further responses would be extended until 6/13/23.  (Id., Ex.D-F).  On 5/18/23, Fresh Start Healthy’s counsel filed and served a motion to be relieved as counsel which was denied without prejudice on 9/21/23.  (Roshanian Decl.; 9/21/23 Minute Order).  Fresh Start Healthy did not serve supplemental responses as promised.  (Roshanian Decl.).  On 5/22/23, Plaintiff inquired as to whether further responses would be served or whether motions should be filed.  (Id., Ex.G).  Fresh Start Healthy did not respond.  Id.

 

Therefore, on 6/9/23, Plaintiff filed and served the instant motion seeking an order compelling  Fresh Start Healthy to provide further responses and produce documents to Plaintiff’s Requests for Production of Documents, Set 3.  Additionally, Plaintiff requests an award of sanctions against Fresh Start Healthy and its  counsel in favor of Plaintiff in the amount of $4,560.00.  Fresh Start Healthy has not filed an opposition or any other response to the motion. 

 

ANALYSIS

 

By way of this motion, Plaintiff seeks to compel further responses and/or the production of documents in relation to Requests 31, 32, 33, 34, 36, 37, 38, 39, 41, 42, 43, 44, 46, 47 and 48.  (See Plaintiff’s Separate Statement).

 

CCP 2031.310(a) provides:

 

“On receipt of a response to a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling, the demanding party may move for an order compelling further response to the demand if the demanding party deems that any of the following apply:

(1) A statement of compliance with the demand is incomplete.

(2) A representation of inability to comply is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive.

(3) An objection in the response is without merit or too general.”

 

In response to Request 31, Fresh Start Healthy makes objections and then states that without waiving the objections: “Documentation will be provided in the event the Plaintiff is able to provide a full explanation regarding exactly how the nature of this Responding Party's clients pertain in any manner to this dispute regarding wage and hour issues.”  This last sentence appears to be an objection based on relevance.  In response to Request 33, Fresh Start Healthy refers to its response to Request 31.  If a timely motion to compel further responses is filed, the responding party has the burden to justify any objection.  See Fairmont Insurance Co. (2000) 22 C4th 245, 255; Coy (1962) 58 C2d 210, 220-221.  Fresh Start Healthy has failed to meet its burden of justifying the objections made in response to these requests.    

 

In response to Requests 32, 34, 36, 37, 38,  and 44, Fresh Start Healthy asserts various objections and then states without waiving such objections, no responsive documents exist.  Fresh Start Healthy has failed to meet its burden of justifying the objections made in response to these requests.  See Fairmont Insurance Co., supra; Coy, supra.  Additionally, Fresh Start Healthy’s response/conclusion that “no such documents exist” is insufficient.  CCP 2031.230 provides:

 

“A representation of inability to comply with the particular demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling shall affirm that a diligent search and a reasonable inquiry has been made in an effort to comply with that demand. This statement shall also specify whether the inability to comply is because the particular item or category has never existed, has been destroyed, has been lost, misplaced, or stolen, or has never been, or is no longer, in the possession, custody, or control of the responding party. The statement shall set forth the name and address of any natural person or organization known or believed by that party to have possession, custody, or control of that item or category of item.”

 

In response to Requests 41 and 43, Fresh Start Healthy asserts unsupported objections and then states “[s]o far as is known to this Responding Party, no such documents exist” and indicates that a search is being conducted and all relevant documents will be produced.  Again, K12 has failed to meet its burden of justifying its objections.  See Fairmont Insurance Co., supra; Coy, supra.   Fresh Start Healthy’s response that no documents exist fails to comply with the requirements set forth in CCP 2031.230 noted above.  Fresh Start Healthy has failed to produce any documents from its claimed diligent search. 

 

In response to Requests 39, 42, 47 and 48, Fresh Start Healthy states “[s]o far as is known to this Responding Party, no such documents exist” and indicates that a search is being conducted and all relevant documents will be produced.  Fresh Start Healthy’s response that no documents exist fails to comply with the requirements set forth in CCP 2031.230 noted above.  Fresh Start Healthy has failed to produce any documents from its claimed diligent search. 

 

In response to Request 46, Fresh Start Healthy makes an unsupported objection based on relevance and then states that without waving the objection: “No such documents are or would be available or accessible to this Responding Party even after a diligent search and requests submitted to third parties, for example.”  The response is unclear.  If Fresh Start Healthy is claiming that no responsive documents exist, its response must comply with CCP 2031.230.  

 

Plaintiff is entitled to an award of sanctions against Fresh Start Healthy and its counsel of record for their failure to comply with their discovery obligations.  CCP 2023.010(d), (e); CCP 2023.030(a); CCP 2030.310(h).  However, the Court finds that the $4,560 requested to be excessive.  The Court awards $1,560 in sanctions (2 hours to prepare the motion + 1 hour to appear x $500/hour + $60 filing fee).  The Court finds this motion is substantially similar to the motion heard on 10/9/23 as to K12.  Therefore, the time was adjusted accordingly.     

 

CONCLUSION

 

The request to compel further responses is granted.  The request for sanctions is granted in the reduced amount of $1,560.00.  Further responses are due and sanctions are payable within 30 days.

MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE CROSS-COMPLAINT

 

Motion filed on 9/19/23.

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendant SiteOne Landscape Supply, LLC

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff L.A. Tire Tech, Inc.

NOTICE: ok

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order granting Defendant SiteOne Landscape Supply, LLC leave to file a cross-complaint. 

 

RULING: The motion is granted.  The trial date will be continued. 

 

SUMMARY OF FACTS & PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

Plaintiff L.A. Tire Tech, Inc. (Plaintiff) is an on-site fleet maintenance, repair, roadside service, tire company and provides safety inspections and certifications required by clients and customers, such as Defendant SiteOne Landscape Supply, LLC (Defendant).

 

Defendant is a supplier of landscape, hardscape, irrigation, and related supplies providing supplies to business owners, contractors and others in the landscaping business or needing landscape/hardscape or irrigation supplies. Defendant’s operations, to which Plaintiff provided services, were generally divided into two different divisions, commonly referred to as the hardscape and irrigation divisions.  Each division was managed by separate individuals and each division had vehicles and equipment assigned to it that required the fleet services of Plaintiff. 

 

Defendant operated a fleet of approximately 158 commercial trucks, trailers, forklifts, yard equipment that were used in its business.  Plaintiff provided Defendant with a full range of fleet maintenance, inspection, and repair services from brakes, to engines, tires, alignment, and total

vehicle service, maintenance, inspection, and repair.  Commercial trucks and vehicles of the nature utilized by Defendant require regular maintenance and certification to operate on state highways, which were included in the services provided by Plaintiff.  Plaintiff’s services were typically provided on-site at Defendant’s various business locations including emergency roadside services when required, for which Plaintiff was available 24/7. 

 

This action involves the alleged unpaid charges for services provided by Plaintiff to Defendant’s  Hardscape Division.  Plaintiff contends that at the time that Defendant’s hardscape division terminated Plaintiff’s services, the total amount of all invoices for services performed and which allegedly remain unpaid is approximately $236,000.

 

On 12/17/21, Plaintiff filed this action against Defendant for: (1) Goods and Services Rendered (Common Count), (2) Account Stated (Common Count), (3) Open Book Account (Common Count) and (4) Breach of Oral Contract.  On 3/7/22, Plaintiff filed its operative First Amended Complaint for: (1) Goods and Services Rendered (Common Count), (2) Open Book Account (Common Count) and (3) Breach of Oral Contract.  On 3/18/22, Defendant answered the First Amended Complaint. 

 

On 9/19/23, Defendant filed and served the instant motion seeking an order granting Defendant leave to file a cross-complaint against Plaintiff and Gerard J. Neglio for: (1) Breach of Contract and (2) Express Indemnity.  Plaintiff has opposed the motion and Defendant has filed a reply to the opposition. 

 

ANAYLSIS

 

CCP 428.10 provides:

 

“A party against whom a cause of action has been asserted in a complaint or cross-complaint may file a cross-complaint setting forth either or both of the following:

(a) Any cause of action he has against any of the parties who filed the complaint or cross-complaint against him. Nothing in this subdivision authorizes the filing of a cross-complaint against the plaintiff in an action commenced under Title 7 (commencing with Section 1230.010) of Part 3.

(b) Any cause of action he has against a person alleged to be liable thereon, whether or not such person is already a party to the action, if the cause of action asserted in his cross-complaint (1) arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences as the cause brought against him or (2) asserts a claim, right, or interest in the property or controversy which is the subject of the cause brought against him.”

 

If a defendant fails to file a cross-complaint against the plaintiff at the time of the filing of its answer, the defendant must seek leave of court to do so.  See CCP 428.50; CCP 426.50.  If defendant’s claims against the plaintiff are related to the subject matter of the complaint (arise out of the same transaction or occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences), they must be raised in a cross-complaint or they are waived.  CCP 426.10(c); CCP 426.30.

 

The Court finds that the claims in Defendant’s proposed cross-complaint are related to the causes of action Plaintiff asserts against Defendant in the First Amended Complaint and, therefore, the cross-complaint is compulsory.  As such, Defendant may seek leave to assert such claims “at any time during the course of the action” and leave must be granted if the moving party acted in good faith.  Id.  Further, such leave is to be liberally granted to avoid forfeiture of causes of action.  Id.; Silver Organizations Ltd. (1990) 217 CA3d 94, 98.

 

Plaintiff has not made a sufficient showing that Defendant has not acted in good faith.  As such, leave must be granted.    

 

However, the Court finds that the current 1/29/24 trial date and corresponding cut-offs dates will not allow Plaintiff and cross-defendant Gerard Neglio sufficient opportunity to challenge the cross-claims and/or conduct discovery on any new issues.  Therefore, the trial date will be continued.

 

CONCLUSION

 

The motion is granted.  The trial date will be continued.   

 

Defendant is ordered to separately file its cross-complaint today, 10/16/23, with all exhibits referenced therein attached.  The Court notes that the copy of the Cross-Complaint attached to the declaration of Nicholas J. Schuchert as Exhibit 4 alleges that the Agreement which is the subject of the cross-claims is attached to the cross-complaint as Exhibit A.  (See Schuchert Decl., Ex.4 – proposed Cross-Complaint ¶18).  However, there is no Exhibit A attached to the cross-complaint attached as Exhibit 4 to the declaration.
