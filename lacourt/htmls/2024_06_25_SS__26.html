Weiner v. Century Property Management Investments, Inc., et al

MOTION TO AMEND COMPLAINT / SUBSTITUTE ESTATE AS DEFENDANT


TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Plaintiff Samara Weiner’s Motion for Leave to Amend Instanter the Complaint on its Face to Substitute Estate of Michael Koomer as Defendant is DENIED.

 

 

ANALYSIS:

 

Plaintiff Samara A. Weiner (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action for recovery of COVID-19 rental debt against Defendants Century Property Management & Investment, Inc. (“Defendant Century Property”) and Deion Gerhard (“Defendant Gerhard”) on July 27, 2022. Defendants Century Property and Gerhard filed an answer and cross-complaint against Plaintiff, Art Koomer, and Michael Koomer on November 23, 2022. Arthur Koomer and Michael also filed cross-complaints against unnamed cross-defendants on May 18, 2023 and June 30, 2023, respectively. On July 26, 2023, Plaintiff added Art Koomer (“Defendant Art Koomer”) and Michael Koomer (“Defendant Michael Koomer”) to the Complaint as doe defendants.

 

On December 18, 2023, the Court granted Defendants Century Property and Gerhard’s Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings with respect to the Complaint with leave to amend. (Minute Order, 12/18/23.) On February 5, 2024, the Court granted the same Defendants’ ex parte application and dismissed Plaintiff’s Complaint against them with prejudice. (Minute Order, 02/05/24.) On April 22, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion for Leave to Amend Instanter the Complaint on its Face to Substitute Estate of Michael Koomer as Defendant.

 

Following Plaintiff’s Motion for Reconsideration, the Court vacated the dismissal and gave Plaintiff leave to file a First Amended Complaint. (Minute Order, 04/23/24.) Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint on May 12, 2024.

 

Discussion

 

The Motion is not brought pursuant to any legal authority or supporting evidence, as required by the California Rules of Court. “The memorandum must contain a statement of facts, a concise statement of the law, evidence and arguments relied on, and a discussion of the statutes, cases, and textbooks cited in support of the position advanced.” (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1113(b).) Indeed, Plaintiff’s failure to provide a memorandum as required by the Rule is an “admission that the [request] is without merit and cause for its denial.” (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1113(a), (b); In re Marriage of Falcone & Fyke (2012) 203 Cal.App.4th 964, 976.) The Court cannot grant the requested relief without a supporting memorandum.

 

Furthermore, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint that already names Michael C. Argan, as Trustee of the Michael D. Koomer Revocable Trust Dated July 9, 2007, and does not name Defendant Michael Koomer.

 

Conclusion

 

Therefore, Plaintiff Samara Weiner’s Motion for Leave to Amend Instanter the Complaint on its Face to Substitute Estate of Michael Koomer as Defendant is DENIED.

Qualitas Ins. Co. v. Conde, et al

MOTION FOR ORDER TO DEPOSIT INTERPLEADER FUNDS, FOR DISCHARGE OF STAKEHOLDER, RESTRAINING ORDER AND ATTORNEY’S FEES

(CCP §§ 386, 386.5)

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Plaintiff Qualitas Insurance Company’s Motion to Deposit by Stakeholder is GRANTED. PLAINTIFF IS TO DEPOSIT FUNDS OF $4,717.50 WITHIN 30 DAYS OF THIS ORDER AND IS AWARDED ATTORNEY’S FEES OF $5,282.50.

 

TRIAL / HEARING ON DISBURSEMENT OF FUNDS REMAINS SET FOR NOVEMBER 5, 2024 AT 8:30 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY OCTOBER 7, 2024, DEFENDANTS, CLAIMANTS, OR OTHER INTERESTED PARTIES MAY FILE AND SERVE CLAIMS TO THE INTERPLEADER FUNDS.

 

 

ANALYSIS:

 

On October 28, 2022, Plaintiff Qualitas Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) initiated this interpleader action against Defendants Julli A. Conde (“Defendant Conde”) and Richard M. Lester, a law corporation (“Defendant Lester”). Defendant Lester answered on December 9, 2022. The Court entered default against Defendant Conde on April 21, 2023. 

 

Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Deposit Funds on February 8, 2024. The Motion came for hearing on April 30, 2024 and was continued to allow Plaintiff to file a supplemental declaration. (Minute Order, 04/30/24.) Plaintiff filed a supplemental declaration on May 2, 2024. To date, no opposition to the Motion has been filed.

 

Discussion

 

The Complaint alleges that this action arises from a dispute over a settlement in a personal injury action. (Compl., ¶¶2-6.) Defendant Lester represented Defendant Conde in the underlying action and asserts a $4,000.00 attorney’s fees lien on the $10,000.00 settlement. (Ibid.) Plaintiff, Defendant Conde’s insurer concerning the motor vehicle accident that gave rise to the personal injury settlement, allegedly cannot resolve the dispute over the insurance money. (Id. at ¶12.) Plaintiff alleges no interest in the insurance money and upon its deposit seeks to be discharged from liability in this action. (Id. at ¶¶13-16.) Plaintiff also requests an order that it can recover its reasonable costs and attorney’s fees. (Id. at ¶15.)

 

Interpleader is a procedure whereby a person holding money or personal property to which conflicting claims are being made by others, or may be made, can join the adverse claimants and force them to litigate their claims among themselves. (Code Civ. Proc., § 386, subd. (b).) Hancock Oil Co. v. Hopkins (1944) 24 Cal.2d 497, 508 (i.e., an escrow-holder who receives conflicting demands from the parties to the escrow regarding the funds or documents he or she holds); City of Morgan Hill v. Brown (1999) 71 Cal.App.4th 1114, 1122.)

 

Once the stakeholder’s right to interplead is established and he or she deposits the money or personal property in court, he or she may be discharged from liability to any of the claimants. This enables the stakeholder to avoid multiplicity of actions, and the risk of inconsistent results if each of the claimants were to sue him or her separately. (Cantu v. Resolution Trust Corp. (1992) 4 Cal.App.4th 857, 874; City of Morgan Hill, supra, 71 Cal.App.4th at 1122.)

 

“An interpleader action is traditionally viewed as two suits: one between the stakeholder and the claimants to determine the stakeholder's right to interplead, and the other among the claimants to determine who shall receive the funds interpleaded ... As against the stakeholder, claimants may raise only matters which go to whether the suit is properly one for interpleader; i.e., whether the elements of an interpleader action are present.” (State Farm Fire & Cas. Co. v. Pietak (2001) 90 Cal.App.4th 600, 612.)

 

The stakeholder may seek reimbursement for its costs and reasonable attorneys’ fees incurred. 

(UAP-Columbus JV 326132 v. Nesbitt (1991) 234 Cal.App.3d 1028, 1036.) The court may order payment thereof out of the funds deposited by the stakeholder. (Ibid.) Ultimately, such payment may be charged to one or more of the adverse claimants in the final judgment. (Code Civ. Proc., § 386.6.) Finally, the Court may issue an “order restraining all parties to the action from instituting or further prosecuting any other proceeding in any court in this state affecting the rights and obligations as between the parties to the interpleader until further order of the court.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 386, subd. (f).)

 

Plaintiff’s request for an order depositing the interpleader funds and to be discharged from liability on the Complaint in interpleader is proper. Both Defendants have been served and have either answered or are in default. Substantively, Plaintiff cannot determine the validity of the conflicting demands that have been made and has no interest in the funds. (Motion, Heath Decl., ¶3; Hartman Decl., ¶4.) Upon deposit of the funds to the court, Plaintiff may be discharged from further liability. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 386, 386.5.) Furthermore, Plaintiff is entitled to recover the fees and costs expended bringing this interpleader action. (Code Civ. Proc., § 386.6, subd. (a).)

 

Plaintiff seeks an award of attorney’s fees in the amount of $9,422.50 based on 36.9 hours of attorney time. (Motion, Supp. Yuen Decl., ¶¶6-7.) 9.3 hours were billed by a paralegal at $125.00 per hour and the remaining time was billed at $300.00 per hour. (Ibid.) The hourly rate billed for the attorney work was reasonable in light of the prevailing rate for limited jurisdiction work in the area. However, the amount of time spent was not reasonable. Although the action was filed in October 2022, nothing except a request for entry of default was filed by Plaintiff until the instant Motion in March 2024. Plaintiff also applied ex parte to continue the trial date, which was set for the following month. Had the instant Motion been filed earlier, no ex parte application would have been required. Nor was it necessary to have two attorneys and a paralegal work on a straightforward interpleader action. A single attorney and paralegal would have sufficed. The attorney hours, therefore, are reduced by 13.8 hours in the amount of $4,140.00. Plaintiff is awarded fees of $5,282.50.

 

Finally, Plaintiff’s request for an offset of the $10,000.00 from any judgment awarded to Defendants is denied.

 

Conclusion

 

Plaintiff Qualitas Insurance Company’s Motion to Deposit by Stakeholder is GRANTED. PLAINTIFF IS TO DEPOSIT FUNDS OF $4,717.50 WITHIN 30 DAYS OF THIS ORDER AND IS AWARDED ATTORNEY’S FEES OF $5,282.50.

Case Number: 23STLC02832    Hearing Date: June 25, 2024    Dept: 26

 

  

Kohansameh v. Habesyan, et al.

DEMURRER

(CCP § 430.10, et seq.)

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Defendant Hudson Insurance Company’s Demurrer to the Complaint is CONTINUED TO JULY 29, 2024 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY JULY 15, 2024, DEFENDANT HUDSON INSURANCE COMPANY IS TO FILE AND SERVE A SUPPLEMENTAL DECLARATION SETTING FORTH DEPARTMENT A’S RULING ON THE NOTICE OF RELATED CASE AND ADDRESSING THE BASIS FOR THIS COURT TO EXERCISE ITS DISCRETION TO CONSIDER THE UNTIMELY DEMURRER.

 

 

ANALYSIS:

 

On April 28, 2023, Plaintiff Lida Kohansameh (“Plaintiff”) filed the Complaint in this action against Defendants Serob Habesyan (“Defendant Habesyan”), Arch Stone Design Showroom, Inc. (“Defendant Arch Stone”), and Noho Tile Center (“Defendant Noho”). On May 24, 2023, Plaintiff added Defendant Hudson Insurance Company (“Defendant Hudson”) via doe amendment. Defendant Hudson was served with the Summons and Complaint on May 26, 2023 but did not file the instant Demurrer to the Complaint until more than a year later, on May 30, 2024. No opposition has been filed to date.  

 

Discussion

 

A demurrer to a complaint or cross-complaint must be filed within 30 days after service of the complaint or cross-complaint, unless extended by stipulation or court order. (Civ. Proc. Code, § 430.40, subd. (a).) The Court has discretion to consider an untimely demurrer but must do so when it is in furtherance of justice and on any terms as may be proper. (Jackson v. Doe (2011) 192 Cal.App.4th 742, 749 [citing Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (a)(1).) Here, Defendant Hudson offers no basis for bringing the instant Demurrer so late to demonstrate that an exercise of the Court’s discretion is warranted.

 

Furthermore, Defendant Hudson filed a Notice of Related Case with respect to The Sangari And Rechner Family Trust, et al. v. Habesyan, et al., LASC Case No. 24BBCV00278 (“Case No. 24BBCV00278”) on May 30, 2024. Case No. 24BBCV00278 was filed in February 2024 and assigned to an unlimited jurisdiction court, Department A in the Burbank Courthouse. As the judge with the earliest filed unlimited civil case, the judge in Department A in the Burbank Courthouse “must determine whether the cases should be ordered related and assigned to his or her department.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.300(h)(1)(B) [emphasis added] [applying to situations “[w]here the cases listed in the notice include both unlimited and limited civil cases”].)  A case management conference is scheduled for Case No. 24BBCV00278 in Department A on July 1, 2024 at 9:00 am.

 

Therefore, the Court continues the hearing on the instant Demurrer pending Department A’s ruling on the Notice of Related Case. This will also provide Defendant Hudson an opportunity to file a supplemental brief explaining why the Court should consider the untimely Demurrer, if this action is deemed not related to Case No. 24BBCV00278.

 

Conclusion

 

Defendant Hudson Insurance Company’s Demurrer to the Complaint is CONTINUED TO JULY 29, 2024 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY JULY 15, 2024, DEFENDANT HUDSON INSURANCE COMPANY IS TO FILE AND SERVE A SUPPLEMENTAL DECLARATION SETTING FORTH DEPARTMENT A’S RULING ON THE NOTICE OF RELATED CASE AND ADDRESSING THE BASIS FOR THIS COURT TO EXERCISE ITS DISCRETION TO CONSIDER THE UNTIMELY DEMURRER.

MOTION TO DEEM REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION ADMITTED;

REQUEST FOR SANCTIONS

(CCP § 2033.280)

Defendant Discover Bank’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted and Request for Sanctions is CONTINUED TO AUGUST 13, 2024 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. ANALYSIS:

The case was then transferred to Department 26 in the Spring Street Courthouse and Defendant was ordered to give notice of the June 25, 2024 hearing date. (Ibid.)

 

Defendant, however, has not filed a notice of new hearing date or proof of service of the same.

 

Conclusion
