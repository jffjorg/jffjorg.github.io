Case Number: 21STCV33539    Hearing Date: September 26, 2024    Dept: 27

Hon. Lee S. Arian, Dept 27 

 

MOTION TO CONTINUE

Hearing Date: 9/26/24¿ 

CASE NO./NAME: 21STCV33539 KEVIN MCELRATH vs LOS ANGELES COUNTY METROPOLITAN TRANSIT AUTHORITY et al.

Moving Party: Defendant LOS ANGELES COUNTY METROPOLITAN TRANSIT AUTHORITY

Responding Party: Plaintiff¿ 

Notice: Sufficient¿ 

Ruling: MOTION TO CONTINUE IS GRANTED.

 

On September 10, 2021, Plaintiff filed the present case. At the time Defendant’s present motion to continue the trial was filed, the trial was set for October 1, 2024. The original reason for the continuance was that Plaintiff listed 47 non-retained experts, and there was not enough time to conduct all of the depositions. On September 4, 2024, the court granted Defendant’s ex parte application to continue the trial and extended the trial date by 30 days. The trial is currently set for October 31, 2024. On September 13, 2024, the court granted Defendant's motion for a protective order, and Plaintiff was ordered to amend his expert disclosure to list only the non-retained experts that Plaintiff expects will testify at trial. On September 18, 2024, Plaintiff reduced its expert disclosure to 13 non-retained experts.

Despite Plaintiff reducing his non-retained experts from 47 to 13 and the court extending the trial date by 30 days, Defendant argues that it still needs more time to subpoena and depose the 13 non-retained experts, along with the retained experts. Plaintiff’s non-retained experts were disclosed on September 18, 2024, with the expert discovery deadline set for October 16, 2024, leaving a little less than one month in between. The court finds that this timeframe, while very tight, is sufficient to conduct depositions of the 13 non-retained experts. Regarding the seven retained experts who were disclosed on July 8, 2024, Defendant alleges that none have been deposed by either party. The parties have had more than three months to depose the retained experts, and they were not subject to the motion for protective order. Thus, the need for additional time to conduct retained expert’s deposition is not particularly well-taken.

Additionally, Defense counsel has a five-year case set for trial on October 24, 2024 (19STCV11651) and will be occupied with the trial preparation for that case and is not available to take Plaintiff’s expert depositions around that time period.

Considering the unavailability of defense counsel and the remaining expert depositions to be taken, the court grants a continuance of only 30 days, given that a prior continuance was recently granted. The parties should consider the trial date set herein as firm.

The new Trial Date is set for December 2, 2024, at 8:30 a.m. The Final Status Conference is continued to November__, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. With the exception of expert discovery, all other case-related deadlines will not follow the new trial date. 
 
PLEASE TAKE NOTICE:

 

If a party intends to submit on this tentative ruling, the party must send an email to the court at sscdept27@lacourt.org with the Subject line “SUBMIT” followed by the case number.  The body of the email must include the hearing date and time, counsel’s contact information, and the identity of the party submitting.

 

Unless all parties submit by email to this tentative ruling, the parties should arrange to appear remotely (encouraged) or in person for oral argument.  You should assume that others may appear at the hearing to argue.

 

If the parties neither submit nor appear at hearing, the Court may take the motion off calendar or adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the Court.  After the Court has issued a tentative ruling, the Court may prohibit the withdrawal of the subject motion without leave.

Ruling: MOTION TO AUGMENT EXPERT WITNESS DESIGNATION IS DENIED

 

Legal Standard

 

The court shall grant leave to augment or amend an expert witness list only if all of the following conditions are satisfied:

 

(a)   The court has taken into account the extent to      which the opposing party has relied on the list of expert witnesses.

(b) The court has determined that any party opposing the motion will not be prejudiced in maintaining that party’s action or defense on the merits.

(c)   The court has determined either of the following: 
 

(1)   The moving party would not in the exercise of reasonable diligence have determined to call that expert witness or have decided to offer the different or additional testimony of that expert witness. 
 

(2) The moving party failed to determine to call that expert witness, or to offer the different or additional testimony of that expert witness as a result of mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect, and the moving party has done both of the following: 
 

(A) Sought leave to augment or amend promptly after deciding to call the expert witness or to offer the different or additional testimony. 
 

(B) Promptly thereafter served a copy of the proposed expert witness information concerning the expert or the testimony described in Section 2034.260 on all other parties who have appeared in the action. 
 

(d)   Leave to augment or amend is conditioned on the moving party making the expert available immediately for a deposition under Article 3 (commencing with Section 2034.410), and on any other terms as may be just, including, but not limited to, leave to any party opposing the motion to designate additional expert witnesses or to elicit additional opinions from those previously designated, a continuance of the trial for a reasonable period of time, and the awarding of costs and litigation expenses to any party opposing the motion.

 

(Code of Civ. Proc. § 2034.620.)

 

Discussion

This case arises from an incident on May 27, 2021, in which a motor vehicle collided with Ami Dorian (Plaintiff), who was bicycling across a crosswalk. The trial is set for October 16, 2024, and the deadline for expert discovery is October 1, 2024. Plaintiff alleges injuries to various body parts, including her lower back, which has an alleged 13-millimeter disc extrusion. On September 6, 2024, during the deposition of Dr. Bahman Shamloo, MD, Plaintiff's pain management physician, Plaintiff's counsel contends he learned for the first time that a health care provider recommended that Plaintiff consult a spine surgeon regarding future treatment options for her lower back.

On the same day, Defendant's expert witness, Dr. Arya Shamie, asserted during his expert deposition that Plaintiff's lower back injuries are unrelated to the crash and that surgical options are not reasonable. On September 12, 2024, Plaintiff consulted with Anthony Virella, MD, a certified neurosurgeon, to explore her spine surgical options. Dr. Virella, who is not a retained expert but a treating physician, was not included in Plaintiff's initial expert disclosure. Plaintiff now moves the court to augment its expert disclosure to include Dr. Virella as a non-retained expert.

Plaintiff argues that subsection (a), concerning the extent to which the opposing party has relied on the list of expert witnesses, is satisfied based on the assertion that Defendant’s expert medical doctors concluded that Plaintiff’s lower back injuries are unrelated to the crash only as of September 6, 2024. Additionally, the need for potential lower back surgical recommendations was discovered concurrently by both Defendant’s and Plaintiff’s counsel during the deposition of Dr. Shamloo on that same date. The Court is unclear how this demonstrates Defendant’s alleged non-reliance on Plaintiff’s expert witness list remains unclear.

 

Subsection (b) addresses whether there will be prejudice to a party in maintaining their action or defense on the merits. Plaintiff argues that this subsection is satisfied because there are no new injuries unknown to Defendants. The testimony of Dr. Virella, which concerns surgical options for existing injuries, is consistent with issues already presented in the case.

However, expert discovery is scheduled to close on October 1, 2024, leaving only three calendar days to complete Dr. Virella’s deposition. There are logistical challenges to consider. It is uncertain if Dr. Virella can be available on such short notice, defense counsel only has a limited amount of time to prepare for the deposition, and additional time is required to prepare the transcript for Defendant’s experts to review before the trial on October 16, 2024. Given these constraints, the Court finds that amending the expert witness list just a few days prior to the close of expert discovery does indeed pose a risk of prejudice to Defendant.

Subsection (c) concerns whether Plaintiff, in the exercise of reasonable diligence, would not have determined to call an expert witness sooner. Plaintiff argues that this subsection is satisfied because the necessity for surgical treatment options became apparent only after the deposition of Dr. Shamloo on September 6, 2024.

However, an April 25, 2024, referral form for Plaintiff with the insurance company listed as the Law Office of Ray Kermani, recommending a spine surgeon for an evaluation of Plaintiff's lower back for potential surgery dated (Defendant’s Exhibit C, Bates Stamp 91), along with similar medical documents of diagnostic, treatment, and management plan showing the same referral (Defendant’s Exhibit C, Bates Stamp 95) exist. This referral form reflects documented consideration for surgery several months before Dr. Shamloo’s September 6, 2024, deposition. The Court finds that Plaintiff did not act with reasonable diligence.

Thus, the motion is DENIED.

MOTION FOR RELIEF FROM WAIVER OF JURY TRIAL

Hearing Date: 9/26/24¿ 

CASE NO./NAME: 22STCV15937 MIREYA B. DE LA ROSA GALLEG.OS vs TRUKMAN ENTERPRISES, INC. et al.

Moving Party: Plaintiff 

Responding Party: Unopposed

Notice: Sufficient¿ 

Ruling: MOTION FOR RELIEF FROM WAIVER OF JURY TRIAL IS GRANTED. 

 

Legal Standard 

 

“The right to a trial by jury as declared by Section 16 of Article I of the California Constitution shall be preserved to the parties inviolate.”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 631(a).)¿ “In civil cases, a jury may only be waived pursuant to subdivision (f).”¿ (Id.)¿ CCP section 631(f) provides that a party waives trial by jury by (1) failing to appear at the trial; (2) written consent filed with the clerk or judge; (3) oral consent, in open court, entered in the minutes; (4) failing to announce that a jury is required, at the time the cause is first set for trial, if it is set upon notice of stipulation, or within five days after notice of setting if it is set without notice or stipulation; (5) failing to timely pay the fee described in subdivision (b), unless another party on the same side of the case has paid that fee; or (6) failing to deposit with the clerk or judge, at the beginning of the second and each succeeding day’s session, the sum provided in subdivision (e).¿(Id., § 631(f).) 

 

“The court may, in its discretion upon just terms, allow a trial by jury although there may have been a waiver of a trial by jury.”¿ (Id., § 631(g).)¿ “The trial court should grant a motion for relief of a jury waiver ‘unless, and except, where granting such a motion would work serious hardship to the objecting party.’” (Mackovska v. Viewcrest Road Properties LLC (2019) 40 Cal.App.5th 1, 10 (quoting Boal v. Price Waterhouse & Co. (1985) 165 Cal.App.3d 806, 809).)¿ “When there is doubt about whether to grant relief from a jury trial waiver, the court must resolve that doubt in favor of the party seeking a jury trial.”¿(Id.) 

 

“In a motion for relief from waiver of a jury trial, the crucial question is whether the party opposing relief will suffer any prejudice if the court grants relief.”¿ (Mackovska, supra, 40 Cal.App.5th at 10.)¿ “‘‘The prejudice which must be shown from granting relief from the waiver is prejudice from the granting of relief and not prejudice from the jury trial.’’”¿ (Id. (quoting Massie v. AAR Western Skyways, Inc. (1992) 4 Cal.App.4th 405, 411).)¿ “‘The mere fact that trial will be by jury is not prejudice per se.’”¿ (Id. (quoting Johnson-Stovall v. Superior Court (1993) 17 Cal.App.4th 808, 811).)¿ “Denying relief where the party opposing the motion for relief has not shown prejudice is an abuse of discretion.”¿ (Id.)

 

Discussion

On May 13, 2022, Plaintiff filed the present case. According to Code of Civil Procedure section 631(c)(2), jury fees must be paid no later than 365 calendar days after the complaint is filed, which set the deadline for paying the jury fee by May 13, 2023. However, the jury fee was not paid until May 10, 2024. Plaintiff’s counsel submitted a declaration stating that he believed the jury fees had been paid on time. It was only upon a recent review of the register of actions for this case that counsel realized the fees had not been paid as presumed, which was an inadvertent oversight. Plaintiff paid the jury fees upon learning of the oversight and now seeks relief from the waiver of the right to a jury trial. A jury trial was initially demanded by Plaintiff, and both parties have proceeded under the assumption that a jury trial would be held. Defendant did not file an opposition alleging prejudice. Given that Defendant has not claimed any prejudice from this delay and considering the unintentional nature of the oversight, the present motion is granted.

MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT

Hearing Date: 9/26/24¿ 

CASE NO./NAME: 22STCV24225 PATRICK RAYFIELD vs SAFEWAY INC.

Moving Party: Defendants KEHE DISTRIBUTORS, LLC and THE VONS COMPANIES, INC.

Responding Party: Plaintiff¿ 

Notice: Sufficient¿ 

Ruling: MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT IS DENIED

 

This is a personal injury case wherein Plaintiff, Patrick Rayfield, claims he was injured on August 2, 2020, while shopping at the Pavilions Store located at 727 9 N. Vine Street in Los Angeles, California.

On February 1, 2022, Plaintiff filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy. Under 11 U.S.C. § 541, when a debtor files for bankruptcy, all assets, including legal claims such as personal injury lawsuits, become part of the bankruptcy estate. The bankruptcy trustee becomes the real party in interest for any legal claims. Although the bankruptcy was discharged on May 16, 2022, Plaintiff’s failure to list the personal injury lawsuit in the bankruptcy schedules meant the claim remained part of the bankruptcy estate, under the trustee’s control, despite the discharge. On July 27, 2022, Plaintiff filed the personal injury case in his individual capacity, though the trustee retains control over the claim.

Defendants now move the court for summary judgment on the grounds that Plaintiff lacks standing to pursue this case and that this case should be barred due to judicial estoppel.

In Plaintiff's opposition, he acknowledges that he potentially lacks standing in the present personal injury case due to his inadvertence in not including the case in his original bankruptcy schedule. However, he highlights his efforts to obtain standing by reopening his bankruptcy case. On May 20, 2024, Plaintiff filed a motion to reopen his bankruptcy case. The motion was granted by the Bankruptcy Court on May 22, 2024. The court's order allowed Plaintiff to amend the bankruptcy schedules A/B and C and instructed the US Trustee to appoint a new Chapter 7 trustee, Brad Krasnoff, for the case. (Exhibit 1, Bral Decl. ¶ 5.)

On June 22, 2024, the newly appointed trustee, Brad Krasnoff, filed the amended schedules with the Bankruptcy Court. These amendments included the personal injury claim that had been previously omitted.  (Exhibit 2, Bal Decl. ¶ 6.) As of June 27, 2024, when the declaration was filed, the bankruptcy petition is still open and pending.

In Defendants’ reply, Defendants decided not to pursue the judicial estoppel argument, stating, "Although unnecessarily delayed, it appears that Plaintiff has remedied the judicial estoppel issue. However, Plaintiff still lacks standing to bring the suit." (Reply at pg. 5.) As of July 3, 2024, when the reply was filed, Plaintiff had not requested leave to amend the complaint to substitute the real party in interest, the bankruptcy trustee, in the present case. Defendant argues that Plaintiff still lacks standing to pursue the present case.

On September 24, 2024, Plaintiff's counsel filed a declaration indicating that after reopening the bankruptcy case to amend the schedules, the trustee, Brad Krasnoff, investigated and ultimately decided not to pursue any claims related to the personal injury action. The trustee's decision not to pursue the claim suggests that the personal injury claim was abandoned pursuant to 11 U.S. Code § 554, thereby granting Plaintiff standing in the present case.

Furthermore, an email from the bankruptcy court providing an update on the case states "Since it appears that no further matters are required that this case remain open, or that the jurisdiction of this Court continue, it is ordered that the Trustee is discharged, bond is exonerated, and the case is closed." This indicates that the case has been discharged without the need for the bankruptcy court to retain limited jurisdiction to oversee the administration of the personal injury case. The court finds that Plaintiff has provided sufficient evidence to raise a triable issue of fact regarding standing. Because Defendant concedes that Plaintiff has remedied the judicial estoppel issue, and that is the only other issue raised in the motion, the motion for summary judgment is denied.

MOTION TO DEEM RFA ADMITTED AND REQUESTS FOR SANCTIONS

Hearing Date: 9/26/24¿ 

CASE NO./NAME: 22STCV37039 MARICELA MUNOZ, et al. vs RODNEY DEWAYNE MARSHALL

Moving Party: Defendant Rodney Marshall

Responding Party: Unopposed

Notice: Sufficient¿ 

Ruling: MOTION TO DEEM RFA ADMITTED AND REQUESTS FOR SANCTIONS ARE GRANTED. 

Legal Standard   

“Any party may obtain discovery . . . by a written request that any other party to the action admit the genuineness of specified documents, or the truth of specified matters of fact, opinion relating to fact, or application of law to fact. A request for admission may relate to a matter that is in controversy between the parties.”  CCP § 2033.010.  “Within 30 days after service of requests for admission, the party to whom the requests are directed shall serve the original of the response to them on the requesting party, and a copy of the response on all other parties who have appeared ....”  CCP § 2033.250(a).   

            If a party to whom a request for admissions is served fails to provide a timely response, the party to whom the request was directed waives any objections, including based on privilege or the work product doctrine. CCP § 2033.280(a). The requesting party can move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the request be deemed admitted, as well as for monetary sanctions. CCP § 2033.280(b). The court shall issue this order unless the party to whom the request was made serves a response in substantial compliance prior to the hearing on the motion. CCP § 2033.280(c). Unlike a motion to compel further discovery responses, a motion to deem RFA admitted does not have any meet and confer requirements. 

 

Discussion

On May 7, 2024, Defendant Rodney Dewayne Marshall served Plaintiffs Alejandro Chavez and Marcela Munoz with his Requests for Admission, Set One. The responses from Plaintiffs were originally due on June 11, 2024. As of August 27, 2024, when the present motion was filed, Plaintiffs have not provided any responses, nor have they contacted Defendant’s counsel to request an extension.

It is undisputed that Plaintiffs did not serve discovery responses by the statutory deadline. No oppositions were filed, nor were any documents submitted to the court indicating that Plaintiffs have served the responses prior to the hearing date. The court grants the present motions; therefore, the contents of Defendant Rodney Marshall’s Requests for Admission Set One are deemed admitted against Plaintiffs Alejandro Chavez and Marcela Munoz.

Defendant seeks sanctions in the amount of $869 for each motion. Since sanctions are mandatory and the court finds that Plaintiffs did not act with substantial justification, sanctions are granted. However, due to the simplicity of the issues and the absence of any opposition, the court reduces sanctions to $1000 for both motions. Plaintiffs and their attorney are jointly and severally liable for this amount, payable to Defendant within 20 days of today.

MOTION TO COMPEL DEPOSTION AND REQUESTS FOR SANCTIONS 

Hearing Date: 9/26/24¿ 

CASE NO./NAME: 23STCV15482 SANTA ROMERO SANTILLIAN vs EXTERIOR DESIGNS & LANDSCAPE CONSTRUCTION INC.

Moving Party: Plaintiff Santa Romero Santillian

Responding Party: Unopposed

Notice: Sufficient¿ 

Ruling: MOTION TO COMPEL DEPOSTION AND REQUESTS FOR SANCTIONS ARE DENIED

 

Legal Standard 

Any party may obtain any discovery of information, documents, land, property, or electronically stored information so long as the discoverable matter is not privileged, is relevant to the subject matter and can lead one to admissible evidence.¿(Code Civ. Proc. § 2017.010.)

Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450¿provides in pertinent part the following:¿¿¿¿¿¿ 

“(a) If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent's attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.¿¿¿¿

Discussion

On June 22, 2024, Plaintiff Santa Romero Santillian served Defendants Ronald Boren and Exterior Designs & Landscape Construction Inc. with a First Amended Notice of Deposition, setting depositions for August 1 and August 2, 2024, respectively. Defendants served their objections to Plaintiff’s First Amended Notices of Deposition on July 25, 2024. On July 26, 2024, Plaintiff attempted to meet and confer with Defendants, requesting deposition dates by August 2, 2024, and indicated that a motion to compel would be filed otherwise.

The Court finds Plaintiff’s meet and confer attempt insufficient. The objections raised by Defendants were not limited to the deposition dates but also included each request for production and deposition topics for Exterior Design’s PMK, none of which were addressed in the meet and confer email. Typically, an email or a letter alone is considered inadequate for the purposes of meeting and conferring; the court usually requires the parties to either meet or have a phone call to thoroughly discuss the discovery issues.  Plaintiff sent only an email after the objections were served and failed to discuss the contents of the objections. Thus, the motion is denied without prejudice. The Parties are ordered to meet and confer on the objections. Sanctions are denied as the motions are denied.

¿ Legal Standard¿ 

¿¿ 

California Rules of Court, rule 3.350, subdivision (a) states in relevant part:¿ 

¿ 

(1) A notice of motion to consolidate must:¿ 

¿ 

(A) List all named parties in each case, the names of those who have appeared, and the names of their respective attorneys of record;¿ 

(B) Contain the captions of all the cases sought to be consolidated, with the lowest-numbered case shown first; and¿ 

(C) Be filed in each case sought to be consolidated.¿ 

¿ 

(2) The motion to consolidate:¿ 

¿ 

(A) Is deemed a single motion for the purpose of determining the appropriate filing fee, but memorandums, declarations, and other supporting papers must be filed only in the lowest-numbered case;¿¿¿¿ 

(B) Must be served on all attorneys of record and all non-represented parties in all of the cases sought to be consolidated; and¿ 

(C) Must have a proof of service filed as part of the motion.¿ 

¿ 

(Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.350, subd. (a)). Also, the consolidation statute, Code of Civil Procedure section 1048, states in relevant part:¿ 

¿ 

(a)¿ When actions involving a common question of law or fact are pending before the court, it may order a joint hearing or trial of any or all the matters in issue in the actions; it may order all the actions consolidated and it may make such orders concerning proceedings therein as may tend to avoid unnecessary costs or delay.¿ 

¿ 

(b)¿ The court, in furtherance of convenience or to avoid prejudice, or when separate trials will be conducive to expedition and economy, may order a separate trial of any cause of action, including a cause of action asserted in a cross-complaint, or of any separate issue or of any number of causes of action or issues, preserving the right of trial by jury required by the Constitution or a statute of this state or of the United States.¿ 

¿ 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 1048, subd. (a).)¿ 

¿ 

The granting or denial of the motion to consolidate rests in the sound discretion of the trial court and will not be reversed except upon a clear showing of abuse of discretion. (See Fellner v. Steinbaum (1955) 132 Cal.App.2d 509, 511.)¿ 

¿ 

Los Angeles County Court Rules, Rule 3.3(g) states, “Cases may not be consolidated unless they are in the same department. A motion to consolidate two or more cases may be noticed and heard after the cases, initially filed in different departments, have been related into a single department, or if the cases were already assigned to that department.”¿¿¿ 

¿¿ 

Discussion¿ 

¿ 

Plaintiff seeks to consolidate Case No. 23STCV15401 YAFA HAKIM vs BEVERLY HILLS REHABILITATION CENTRE, LLC, et al. with Case No. 24STCV04025 SAID HAKIM vs BEVERLY HILLS REHABILITATION CENTRE, LLC, et al. On August 22, 2024, the court denied Plaintiff's motion to consolidate filed in 23STCV15401 for failing to comply with California Rules of Court, rule 3.350. Subsequently, Plaintiff filed the present motion to consolidate in 24STCV04025; however, it still does not comply with California Rules of Court, rule 3.350 as previously outlined by the court in its August 22, 2024 ruling.  

 

First, a notice of motion to consolidate must be filed in each case sought to be consolidated. The notice of the present motion was not filed in 23STCV15401. Second, memorandums, declarations, and other supporting papers must be filed only in the lowest-numbered case; however, they were filed in the higher-numbered case, 24STCV04025. Furthermore, the motion to consolidate must be served on all attorneys of record and all non-represented parties in all of the cases sought to be consolidated.  Numerous parties who were previously served with the summons and complaint were not served with the present motion. Thus, the motion is denied.

MOTION TO COMPEL CLAIMANT’S IME 

Hearing Date: 9/26/24 

CASE NO./NAME: BS170239 DANIEL GEOULLA VS CALIFORNIA AUTOMOBILE INSURANCE COMPANY 

Moving Party: Respondent California Automobile Insurance Company 

Responding Party: Claimant

Notice: Sufficient¿ 

Ruling: Respondent’s proposed changes to the parameters of the IME is DENIED

The present motion concerns Claimant Daniel Geoulla’s second independent medical examination (IME) with Dr. Weinstein. On July 11, 2024, the court ordered the parties to meet and confer regarding the scope of the examination. By July 22, 2024, the parties entered into a stipulated order, outlining the following terms:

  • Dr. Michael Weinstein is permitted to re-examine the claimant.
  • The examination will take place in person at Dr. Weinstein's office located at 360 San Miguel Suite 701, Newport Beach, CA 92660.
  • The examination will adhere to the scope described in Dr. Weinstein’s letter dated July 11, 2024, attached as Exhibit A.
  • The examination will not exceed 30 minutes, including both the clinical examination and history taking.
  • Dr. Weinstein will prepare a report of his findings and conclusions within 20 days of the examination, which will be forwarded by CAIC's counsel to B &D Law Group immediately upon receipt.

On August 7, 2024, Respondent served its DME notice to Claimant. On August 27, 2024, Claimant responded to the DME notice, outlining certain statutory parameters of the examination. On the same day, Respondent's attorney proposed a few changes to these parameters, with two changes now in dispute:

1.  Respondent requests that if there is an observer or attorney recording the examination on behalf of the Claimant, then the doctor or someone on his staff should also be entitled to record.

2.  If an observer or attorney records the exam on behalf of the Claimant, the Claimant should provide a copy of the audio recording to the Respondent within 20 days.

Claimant’s second IME is scheduled for October 23, 2024, and the parties now wait for the court’s decision on the scope of the IME before it is taken.

Under CCP 2032.510, the attorney for the examinee, or that attorney's representative, is permitted to attend and observe any physical examination conducted for discovery purposes and to record stenographically or by audio technology any spoken words during the examination. The statute does not grant Respondent the right to record the IME or obtain Claimant’s recording of the IME. Respondent has provided no legal authority for these two requests. Instead, Respondent appeals to equity, arguing that if Claimant is allowed to record, so should the Respondent.

This overlooks the power imbalance between the examinee and examiner. The examiner holds control over how the examination is to be conducted and also the final output of the process, the medical report. This control can potentially lead to discrepancies between what occurs during the examination and what is reported. Therefore, the examinee is given the opportunity to record the independent medical examination (IME) to safeguard their interests. It effectively serves as a check on the power held by the examiner. The Court does not find good cause to grant Respondent’s proposed changes to the parameters of the IME and accordingly denies the motion.
