DEPARTMENT F43 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22CHCV00728    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: F43

Dept. F43

Date: 5-22-24

Case #22CHCV00728, Albert Gonzales vs. Daniel Torres, et al.

Trial Date: 7-15-24

 

MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES

 

MOVING PARTY: Plaintiff Abel Gonzalez

RESPONDING PARTY: Defendant Daniel Torres

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

Plaintiff is requesting further responses from Defendant for Special Interrogatory Nos. 25-34 from Special Interrogatories, Set Two.

 

RULING: Parties are ordered to meet and confer and submit a joint statement of remaining issues.

 

SUMMARY OF ACTION AND ANALYSIS

Plaintiffs Abel Gonzalez, Albert Gonzalez, Jr., and Albert Gonzalez (Plaintiffs) were shareholders of Pueblo Restaurant, Inc., (Pueblo) with Defendant Daniel Torres (Defendant). Plaintiffs have alleged that Defendant took control of the business and paid out funds to himself and failed to pay the other shareholders.

 

On April 3, 2023, Plaintiff Abel Gonzalez (Plaintiff) served his special interrogatories on Defendant. On May 10, 2023, the Court appointed a receiver to oversee Pueblo. Defendant claimed in his initial responses to discovery that the appointment of this receiver prevented him from accessing the relevant documents at Pueblo (though the Court would note that the receiver was appointed after the 30-day deadline to respond to discovery).

 

On May 1, 2023, Defendant filed motions for protective orders rather than respond to the discovery. These motions were denied on August 30, 2023. On September 1, 2023, Plaintiff then gave Defendant, at Defendant’s request, 60 days to respond to the discovery at issue.

 

On October 31, 2023, Defendant served his responses to the special interrogatories. For Special Interrogatory Nos. 25-34, his answers were boilerplate objections that claimed that Pueblo alone had access to the documents sought.

 

Plaintiff sent meet and confer letters to Defendant on November 1, 2023, and November 9, 2023, but received no response from Defendant. Plaintiff filed this motion to compel on November 15, 2023. Plaintiff also requested sanctions in the amount of $1,660.00 with his motion. This motion is set for hearing on May 22, 2024.

 

Over 5 months after Plaintiff filed this motion, Defendant served supplemental responses on April 30, 2024. Plaintiff claims that some of these responses were substandard. The Court would note that each of these supplemental responses state that Defendant conducted a reasonable search and diligent inquiry and was not able to locate documents responsive to Plaintiff’s request. Typically, this is a sufficient response when a party claims to not have responsive documents. Plaintiff met and conferred with Defendant regarding these supplemental responses on May 3, 2024.

 

On May 7, 2024, Defendant provided further supplemental responses to Special Interrogatory Nos. 25, 28, 31, and 34. With these further responses, he also provided some documents. Plaintiff points out in his reply that this appears to contradict Defendant’s April 30 responses, which stated that he could not locate any documents.

 

Defendant filed his opposition to Plaintiff’s motion on May 9, 2024. Defendant argues in his opposition that he did not have access to any of the documents after the receiver was appointed for Pueblo, and that the receiver was able to locate the files. He also argues that he has previously provided Plaintiff with the location of the documents. Finally, he argues that sanctions are not warranted against him and that Plaintiff should instead be sanctioned for bringing this motion.

 

Plaintiff filed his reply on May 13, 2024. It is not clear from Plaintiff’s reply whether any of Defendant’s responses to the special interrogatories still require supplementation. Plaintiff requests that the Court grant the motion, but Plaintiff does not say which responses require further supplementation.

 

Because the Court is unable to tell which responses Plaintiff believes still require supplementation, the Court orders the parties to meet and confer and file a joint statement of remaining issues by a date to be set by the Court. The joint statement should briefly describe the matters in dispute, followed by Plaintiffs’ arguments, then Defendant’s arguments.

 

ORDER  

1. The parties are ordered to conduct a meaningful meet and confer. 

 

2. The parties shall submit a joint statement of the remaining issues as described above. The format should be as follows: the parties should recite the specific discovery request at issue, followed by the moving party’s statement of why it should be compelled, followed by the opposing party’s statement of why it should not be compelled. Case Number: 23CHCV01341    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: F43

Dept. F43

Date: 5-22-24

Case #23CHCV01341 , Wvistano Daniel Perez vs. FCI Lender Services, Inc., et al.

Trial Date: N/A

 

MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER DISCOVERY RESPONSES

 

MOVING PARTY: Plaintiff Wvistano Daniel Perez

RESPONDING PARTY: Defendants FCI Lender Services, Inc., Puerto Loreto, LLC, and California TD Specialists

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

An Order compelling Defendants’ further responses to Plaintiff’s discovery requests

 

RULING: Motion is denied.

 

SUMMARY OF ACTION

Plaintiff Wvistano Daniel Perez (Plaintiff) served requests for production of documents, requests for admissions, form interrogatories, and special interrogatories on Defendants FCI Lender Services, Inc., Puerto Loreto, LLC, and California TD Specialists. Defendants served their responses on January 19, 2024. Plaintiff argues that Defendants responses were incomplete and improper. Plaintiff’s counsel met and conferred with Defendants’ counsel on February 22, 2024. Plaintiff’s counsel was told that supplemental discovery would be provided by Loreto on February 29, 2024, by FCI on March 7, 2024, and California TD on March 14, 2024. Plaintiff did not receive the supplemental discovery.

 

Plaintiff filed his motion to compel discovery responses on March 26, 2024. Rather than filing separate motions for each discovery set for each Defendant, Plaintiff filed them all in one motion. Plaintiff did not include a separate statement with the motion to compel further responses. Plaintiff also seeks sanctions in the amount of $3,310.00 for the motion.

 

Defendants argue in their opposition that Plaintiff’s motion, if it is a motion to compel further responses, was untimely because it was filed more than 45 days after responses were served. Next, Defendants argue that Plaintiff’s motion is improper because Plaintiff failed to file a separate statement. Finally, Defendants argue that monetary sanctions should be awarded against Plaintiff for bringing this motion.

 

Plaintiff argues in her reply that Defendants’ responses were unverified so the 45-day limit does not apply. Plaintiff also argues that monetary sanctions would be unwarranted.

 

ANALYSIS

Plaintiff has labeled this as a motion to compel further responses. Plaintiff did not file a separate statement pursuant to Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1345, which is required for motions to compel further responses. “Any motion involving the content of a discovery request or the responses to such a request must be accompanied by a separate statement.” (CRC Rule 3.1345(a); see Miller v. U.S. Bank (2008) 166 Cal.App.4th 871, 892-893.) Individual separate statements are especially needed where the moving party combines multiple discovery motions into one motion, as Plaintiff did here. (See Miller, 166 Cal.App.4th at 893.)

 

Because Plaintiff did not filed a separate statement, Plaintiff’s motion to compel further discovery responses is denied.

 

Sanctions

No sanctions will be awarded at this time.

 

ORDER

1.      Plaintiff’s motion to compel further discovery responses is denied.


Case Number: 23CHCV02873    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: F43

Dept. F43

Date: 5-22-24

Case #23CHCV02873 , Protective Insurance Company vs. Angel On My Shoulder, Inc.

Trial Date: N/A

 

MOTION FOR LEAVE TO AMEND DEFENDANT’S NAME IN JUDGMENT

 

MOVING PARTY: Plaintiffs Plaintiff Protective Insurance Company

RESPONDING PARTY: No response has been filed.

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

Plaintiff requests leave to amend the judgment to add another name for Defendant.

 

RULING: Motion is continued.

 

SUMMARY OF ACTION

Judgment was entered against Defendant Angel On My Shoulder, Inc. (Defendant) on December 5, 2023. Plaintiff Protective Insurance Company (Plaintiff) filed this motion to amend Defendant’s name in the judgment after Plaintiff’s counsel discovered, through a search of Defendant’s business records, that Defendant also goes by Angeles On My Shoulder, Inc. The evidence that Plaintiff’s counsel has submitted in support of this motion is his own declaration that he discovered this information and that he is informed that Defendant uses both names interchangeably. (Booska Decl., ¶ 2.)

 

Plaintiff seeks to amend Defendant’s name on the judgment so that it reads “ANGEL ON MY SHOULDER, INC., A CALIFORNIA CORPORATION aka ANGELES ON MY SHOULDER, INC., A CALIFORNIA CORPORATION.”

 

No opposition has been filed.

 

ANALYSIS

Courts have held that CCP § 187 gives trial courts jurisdiction to add additional judgment debtors. (See McClellan v. Northridge Park Townhome Owners Ass’n, Inc. (2001) 89 Cal.App.4th 746, 752.) Judgments can be “‘amended to add additional judgment debtors on the grounds that a person or entity is the alter ego of the original judgment debtor. [Citations.] This is an equitable procedure based on the theory that the court is not amending the judgment to add a new defendant but is merely inserting the correct name of the real defendant. [Citations.] “Such a procedure is an appropriate and complete method by which to bind new individual defendants where it can be demonstrated that in their capacity as alter ego of the corporation they in fact had control of the previous litigation, and thus were virtually represented in the lawsuit.”’” (Id.) Successor corporations may also be added to a judgment in a similar manner. (Id. at 753.)

 

Plaintiff contends in its motion that what it is not attempting to go so far as to add an additional judgment debtor or an alter ego entity or a successor corporation. Instead, Plaintiff is simply trying to add an additional name for the judgment debtor.

 

The Court is concerned that Plaintiff has not provided sufficient evidence that Angeles On My Shoulder is simply an “aka” of Angel On My Shoulder. The declaration of Plaintiff’s attorney states that he discovered this through Defendant’s business records, but does not specify which business records or whether he found the information on the Secretary of State’s website or someplace else.

 

On the off chance that Angeles On My Shoulder is a totally separate and unrelated entity, the Court will request more information on how Plaintiff’s attorney came by this information. The Court will continue the hearing and request supplemental information, with dates for both to be set at the hearing on this motion.

 

Moving party to give notice.
