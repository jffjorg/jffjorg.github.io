DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23VECV01842    Hearing Date: December 15, 2023    Dept: 107

[Tentative] Ruling

 

 

Plaintiff's default judgment application is valid in all respects except Plaintiff did not file a brief summary of the case as required by California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1800(a)(1). Plaintiff's default judgment application is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE.

 

 

Procedural History:

 

            On April 27, 2023, Plaintiffs Herbert Fair and Kelly Fair (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) Gonzalez filed a complaint against Adrian Banuelos; Imperial Xtreme, Inc. (aka Imperial Xtreme Builders); Imperial Xtreme Builders, Inc.; Elizabeth Banuelos; Gd Construction Group, Inc., A California Corporation; Dillon Reggie; and Does 1-50 (collectively, “Defendants”) for a defective construction job. Plaintiffs allege the following causes of action: (1) Fraud – Intentional Misrepresentation; (2) Fraud – Concealment; (3) Fraud – False Promise / Promissory Fraud; (4) Negligent Misrepresentation; (5) Conversion; (6) Breach Of Contract; (7) Breach Of Implied Covenant Of Good Faith And Fair Dealing; (8) Negligence; (9) Unjust Enrichment; (10) Statutory Violation Of Business & Professions Code §§7025-7034; and (11) Unfair Business Practices. (Complaint p.1)

 

            On August 24, 2023, the Court scheduled the OSC re: entry of default judgment to December 15, 2023 at 8:30 am. On October 30, 2023, the case was reassigned from Judge Salkin to Judge Harmon. On December 4, 2023, Plaintiffs filed the default judgment application.

 

Analysis:

 

The governing rule is CRC Rule 3.1800 which lays out the requirements for default judgments. In pertinent part, the rule dictates that a party must use form CIV-100 and file the following documents with the clerk: (1) Except in unlawful detainer cases, a brief summary of the case identifying the parties and the nature of plaintiff's claim; (2) Declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) Interest computations as necessary; (4) A memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) A declaration of nonmilitary status for each defendant against whom judgment is sought; (6) A proposed form of judgment;

(7) A dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment against specified parties under Code of Civil Procedure section 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) Exhibits as necessary; and (9) A request for attorney fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties.

 

                         

(1) Except in unlawful detainer cases, a brief summary of the case identifying the parties and the nature of plaintiff's claim;

 

Here, Plaintiffs did not include a separate statement with a brief summary identifying the parties and nature of the plaintiffs’ claims. However, they did provide information in the Declarations. (Dec. Fair ¶1-24; Dec. Lake ¶1-24.)

 

(2) Declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested;

 

Here, Plaintiffs provided David Lake’s Declaration and Kelly Fair’s Declaration in support of the request for entry for default judgment. Thus, this requirement is satisfied.

 

(3) Interest computations as necessary;

 

“A person who is entitled to recover damages certain, or capable of being made certain by calculation, and the right to recover which is vested in the person upon a particular day, is entitled also to recover interest thereon from that day, except when the debtor is prevented by law, or by the act of the creditor from paying the debt.” (Civ. Code, § 3287, subd. (a).)

 

Here, Plaintiffs seek $103,094.18 in judgment in the CIV-100 form, Declarations, and JUD-100 form. (CIV-100 form Item 2(f); JUD-100 form Item 6; Dec. Fair ¶27; Dec. Lake ¶27.)

 

“If a contract entered into after January 1, 1986, does not stipulate a legal rate of interest, the obligation shall bear interest at a rate of 10 percent per annum after a breach. [¶]

 

Here, Plaintiffs’ attorney provided interest computations of 10%. (Dec. Lake ¶23.)

 

“Damages are certain or capable of being made certain by calculation, or ascertainable, for purposes of the statute if the defendant actually knows the amount of damages or could calculate that amount from information reasonably available to the defendant.” (Collins v. City of Los Angeles (2012) 205 Cal.App.4th 140, 150–151.)

 

Here, the accrual date that Plaintiff relies upon is the anticipated date of judgment, December 1, 2023. (Dec. Fair ¶23.)

 

The test for determining certainty under section 3287(a) is whether the defendant knew the amount of damages owed to the claimant or could have computed that amount from reasonably available information. (Code of Civil Procedure 3287 (a).)

 

Here, Plaintiffs calculate their principal based on the $72,747.87 they paid Defendants. (Dec. Lake ¶22-23; Exhibit E Spreadsheet.) Plaintiffs argue that Defendants violated Business & Professions Code sections 7025-7034, 7114, and 7125.2. (Dec. Lake ¶22.) Thus, Defendants must disgorge all of the compensation they received from Plaintiffs ($72,747.87) pursuant to Business & Professions Code sections 7025-7034, 7114, and 7125.2 (the “Disgorgement Sum”). The total interest amount so far amounts to $6,754.11. (Id.) For a total of $79,501.98. (Dec. Lake ¶22-23; Exhibit E Spreadsheet.)

(9) A request for attorney fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties.

 

                        Attorney Fees are only available if allowed by statute or agreement.  (CRC 3.1800(a)(9).)  A request for attorney fees must be accompanied by a declaration stating that the fees were calculated in accordance with the fee schedule as per Local Rule 3.214. Where a request for attorney fees is based on a contractual provision the specific provision must be cited.  (Local Rule 3.207.) 

            Code of Civil Procedure section 1029.8 states that “[a]ny unlicensed person who causes injury or damage to another person as a result of providing goods or performing services for which a license is required under … Division 3 (commencing with Section 5000) … of the Business and Professions Code … shall be liable to the injured person for treble the amount of damages assessed in a civil action in any court having proper jurisdiction. … The additional damages provided for in subdivision (a) shall not exceed ten thousand dollars ($10,000).”

 

            Here, Plaintiff seeks attorneys’ fees in the amount of $1,890 against Defendants. Additionally, pursuant to 102.98, request $10,000.00, which is the limit imposed by Code of Civil Procedure section 1029.8 as to treble damages. (Dec. Lake ¶26-27.)
