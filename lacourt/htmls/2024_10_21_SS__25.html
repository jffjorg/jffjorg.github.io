Case Number: 22STLC08512    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: 25

Hearing Date:                         Monday, October 21, 2024

Case Name:                             Ely Sandoval v. William Zimmer

Case No.:                                22STLC08512

Motion:                                   Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Dismissal

Moving Party:                         Plaintiff Ely Sandoval

Responding Party:                   None

Notice:                                    Not OK

 

Tentative Ruling:       Plaintiff Ely Sandoval’s Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Dismissal is CONTINUED to DECEMBER 4, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse, to allow plaintiff to file proof of service of the moving papers in compliance with Code of Civil Procedure § 1005 (b).

 

At least 16 court days before the next scheduled hearing, Plaintiff must file and serve supplemental papers addressing the issues discussed herein. 

           

Failure to do so may result in the motion being placed off calendar or denied.   

 

 

 

 

BACKGROUND

 

On December 21, 2022, Plaintiff Ely Sandoval (Plaintiff) filed a Complaint against Defendant William Zimmer (Defendant) alleging one cause of action for Motor Vehicle Negligence related to a motor vehicle collision on January 2, 2021 involving Plaintiff and Defendant at or near the vicinity of 18561 Soledad Canyon Road, City of Santa Clarita, County of Los Angeles, State of California 91351.

 

On June 20, 2024, after failure of both Plaintiff and Defendant to appear at the scheduled hearing, the Court dismissed Plaintiff’s Complaint without prejudice.

 

On September 24, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Dismissal.

 

Plaintiff has not complied with Code of Civil Procedure § 1005 (b).

 

MOVING PARTY’S POSITION

 

Plaintiff moves for an order vacating the dismissal entered on June 20, 2024 pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure § 473(b).  Plaintiff contends that the failure to appear at the June 20, 2024 hearing was due to Plaintiff’s counsel making an inadvertent calendaring error as to the hearing date.  Plaintiff’s counsel argues that this error resulted because his law office had been heavily affected by Covid-19 and suffered a major break-in, including damage to computers that interfered with his ability to timely calendar and locate files for this case. 

 

No opposition has been received.

 

 

 

 

 

 

ANALYSIS

 

A.    Legal Standard

 

Judges must vacate dismissals, default entries, and default judgments “whenever (1) an application is made no more than six months after entry of judgment, (2) the application is accompanied by an attorney's sworn affidavit attesting to his or her mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or neglect, and (3) the attorney's mistake, inadvertence, surprise or neglect in fact caused the dismissal or entry of default.”¿ (Benedict v. Danner Press (2001) 87 Cal.App.4th 923, 927, Wagner v. Wagner (2008) 162 Cal.App.4th 249, 258.)

 

B.     Discussion

 

The motion was timely filed on September 24, 2024, within the six-month statutory period.

 

Plaintiff’s counsel Frank Canter (“Counsel”) has established by his declaration that the June 20, 2024 dismissal was entered due to his excusable neglect in failing to properly calendar. Counsel noted that this calendaring error occurred because Covid-19 heavily affected his law office and a major break-in occurred at his law office in September 2023, including damage to computers that interfered with his ability to timely calendar and locate files for this case. Plaintiff’s resulting failure to appear caused the June 20, 2024 dismissal. (Decl. Canter ¶¶ 15-18). Therefore, pursuant to the mandatory provision of the Code of Civil Procedure § 473, subdivision (b), the court finds that Plaintiff Ely Sandoval has established a right to relief.

However, plaintiff has not complied with Code of Civil Procedure § 1005 (b). Under C.C.P. §1005(b), “all moving papers ... shall be served and filed at least sixteen (16) court days before the hearing."

 

Here, plaintiff has not filed proof of service in compliance with Code of Civil Procedure § 1005 (b).

 

Conclusion

           

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff’s Motion to Set Aside Dismissal is CONTINUED TO DECEMBER 4, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse, to allow plaintiff to file proof of service of the moving papers in compliance with Code of Civil Procedure § 1005 (b).

 

At least 16 court days before the next scheduled hearing, Plaintiff must file and serve supplemental papers addressing the issues discussed herein.  

 

Failure to do so may result in the motion being placed off calendar or denied.   

 

Plaintiff is ordered to give notice.

 

 

 


Case Number: 23STLC02316    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: 25

 

Hearing Date:                         Monday, October 21, 2024

Case Name:                             The City of Los Angeles, Acting by and through The Los Angeles Department of Water and Power vs. Marcus Cordell Jones Jr. and DOES 1-10, inclusive

Case No.:                                23STLC02316

Recommended Ruling:           GRANT Defendant Counsel’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel.

 

The Court's Ruling and Attorney's relief as Counsel of record for client is not effective until Proof of Service of the Order signed by the Court upon the client is filed in this action.  Until then, counsel continues to be counsel of record.  Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(e).

 

"Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil" is electronically signed by the Court and filed herein this date for reference. 

 

 

BACKGROUND

 

On April 7, 2024, Plaintiff City of Los Angeles, Acting by and through The Los Angeles Department of Water and Power, (Plaintiff) filed a Complaint against Defendant Marcus Cordell Jones Jr. and Does 1 through 10 (Defendant) alleging one cause of action for Automobile Negligence.

 

On July 17, 2024, Defendant filed an Answer.

 

On August 22, 2024, Defendant’s counsel Kerry M. McMahon filed the instant motion to be relieved as counsel.

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

 

An order relieving counsel from representing Defendant Marcus Cordell Jones Jr.

 

ANALYSIS

 

A.    Legal Standard

 

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other.¿¿ (Code of Civ. Proc., § 284,¿subd. (b).)¿ An attorney is permitted to withdraw where conflicts between the attorney and client make it unreasonable to continue the representation.¿ (See Cal. Rules of Prof. Conduct 3-700(C)(1).)¿ “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court¿(1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(e)). Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(d).)¿The court may delay effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court.¿ (Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(e).)

 

B.     Discussion

 

Defendant’s counsel Kerry M. McMahon, Esq. (“Counsel”) moves to be relieved because of a breakdown in communication with the client, Defendant Marcus Cordell Jones Jr. (“Client”). (McMahon Decl. in Support of Motion to be Relieved as Counsel.) Client has become unresponsive to Counsel’s communications, has been unreachable by telephone or written correspondence for several months and as of August 22, 2024, has remained unresponsive. (Ibid.) Counsel has also employed a private investigator to obtain Defendant’s current contact information to no avail. (Ibid.) Thus, Counsel has tried to reach the client but his unresponsiveness has made it unreasonable to proceed with the representation. Counsel filed the requisite application (MC-051) and proposed order (MC-053) using the appropriate forms. (Motion to be Relieved as Counsel.) Counsel has sent a copy of the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel of Record for Defendant to Defendant’s last known address, including Proof of Service. (McMahon Decl. in Support of Motion to be Relieved as Counsel.) Counsel has also electronically served the motion and supporting declaration to counsel for Plaintiff City of Los Angeles. (McMahon Decl. in Support of Motion to be Relieved as Counsel.)

 

Therefore, Counsel substantially complied with the California Rules of Court and has consistently served requisite documents on all parties and on Client.

 

Conclusion

           

Accordingly, the court GRANTS the Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiff.

 

The Court finds Counsel has complied with the requirements of Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362.

 

The Court's Ruling and Attorney's relief as Counsel of record for client is not effective until Proof of Service of the Order signed by the Court upon the client is filed in this action.  Until then, counsel continues to be counsel of record.  Cal. Rules of Court 3.1362(e).

 

"Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil" is electronically signed by the Court and filed herein this date for reference.

MOTION TO ENFORCE SETTLEMENT

TENTATIVE RULING:                                                                       

 

Plaintiff BFS Group of California, LLC’s unopposed Motion for Order for Judgment Pursuant to Stipulation is CONTINUED to DECEMBER 4, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse, to allow Plaintiff time to serve all parties.

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

On August 15, 2023, Plaintiff BFS Group of California, LLC, formerly known as BMC West, LLC, (“Plaintiff”) filed an action against Defendants Global Home Design & Development, Inc. (“Global Home”), Zhirayr Jerry Lusikyan (“Lusikyan”), individually and dba Global Home, Narine Fstkchyan, individually and as trustee of the Narine Separate Property Trust, Harut Fstkdayam, Hagop Fstkdayam, and the North River Insurance Company (“Defendants”) for breach of contract; quantum meruit; account stated; foreclosure of Mechanic’s Lien; enforcement against on Mechanic Lien’s Lien Release Bond; Recovery on Contractor’s License Bond; and Recovery on Personal Guaranty.

 

The Complaint alleges that on or around January 23, 2023, Plaintiff and Defendants Global Home and Lusikyan entered into a written contract Credit Application (“Contract”), “the purpose of which was to allow Global Home and Lusikyan to purchase building materials from Plaintiff on a credit basis for various projects for which Global Home and Lusikyan would perform construction services, including the private work of improvement of their property located at 6525 Varna Avenue, Van Nuys, CA 91401.”  (Complaint, ¶ 15.)  Plaintiff alleges that on or about March 9, 2023, Global Home and Lusikyan breached their Contract by failing to pay the sum then due and owing to Plaintiff in the amount of approximately $23,771.45, and by requiring Plaintiff to provide equipment and materials without compensation. (Complaint, ¶ 18.) 

 

On October 12, 2023, default was entered against Defendants Global Home and Lusikyan (individually and dba Global Home).

 

On November 29, 2023, default was entered against Defendant Harut Fstkdayam.

 

On June 25, 2024, Plaintiff’s dismissed the action as to The North River Insurance Company only. (6/25/24 Request for Dismissal.)

 

On July 12, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Motion for Order for Judgment Pursuant to Stipulation. On August 15, 2024, the Court denied the motion without prejudice, finding that Plaintiff failed to attach the fully executed stipulation and default notice referenced in the accompanying declarations. (8/15/24 Order.)

 

On August 15, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motion for Order to Enter Judgment Against the Defendants Pursuant to Stipulation (the “Motion”), which includes the fully executed stipulation and default notice referenced in the accompanying declarations. (Motion, Exhs. A, B.)

 

No Opposition was filed.  

 

II.                 Legal Standard

 

Under Code of Civil Procedure, section 664.6: 

 
(a) If parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside of the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement. If requested by the parties, the court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement. 

 

(b) For purposes of this section, a writing is signed by a party if it is signed by any of the following: 

 

(1) The party. 

(2) An attorney who represents the party. 

(3) If an insurer is defending and indemnifying a party to the action, an agent who is authorized in writing by the insurer to sign on the party’s behalf. This paragraph does not apply if the party whom the insurer is defending would be liable under the terms of the settlement for any amount above the policy limits.” 

 

III.              Discussion

 

Plaintiff moves for an order for judgment pursuant to the stipulation against Defendants. The Stipulation for Future Entry of Judgment (the “Stipulation”) filed by Plaintiff was signed by Defendants Global Home and Zhirayr Jerry Lusikyan, individually and dba Global Home, and Plaintiff on March 26, 2024. (Motion, Exh. A, p. 5.) Accordingly, the Court considers the Motion as brought by Plaintiff and against Defendants Global Home and Lusikyan, individually and dba Global Home, only.

 

The Stipulation provides the matter would be settled for a total sum of $38,270.24 to be paid by Defendants at a rate of $4,252.25 per month, beginning April 30, 2024 and continuing each month thereafter until December 31, 2024. (Motion, Exh. A, ¶¶ 1-2.) The Stipulation provides specific monthly deadlines for each installment payment as well as the specific mode of payment. (Motion, Exh. A, ¶¶ 2, 4.) Additionally, the Stipulation states there is no grace period for the installment payments. (Motion, Exh. A, ¶ 3.)

 

The Stipulation further provides that failure to make a timely payment constitutes a default and, in the event of default, the unpaid balance will become immediately due, and Plaintiff’s counsel may immediately appear ex parte, without providing notice in order to obtain judgment. (Motion, Exh. A, ¶ 6.) The Stipulation then states “the Court may then issue the Judgment, which shall describe the amount due, and which shall permit [Plaintiff] to immediately enforce the terms thereof and collect the sums owed from [Defendants], including $38,130.24 less payments received.” (Motion, Exh. A, ¶ 6.)

 

Here, the Court finds the Stipulation to be valid and enforceable under Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6. Plaintiff submitted evidence Plaintiff and Defendants Lusikyan and Global Home entered into a Stipulation on or about March 26, 2024. (Motion, Exh. A, p. 5.) Pursuant to the Stipulation, Defendants agreed to pay Plaintiff $38,130.24 less payments received in the event of default, which could be proven solely by a declaration from Plaintiff’s counsel. (Motion, Exh. A, ¶ 6.) Plaintiff submitted a declaration from Plaintiff’s counsel pursuant to the Stipulation. (Oh Decl.) While Plaintiff dismissed The North River Insurance Company from the action on June 25, 2024, the Court retained jurisdiction on the matter as this was only a partial dismissal and the action against Defendants Lusikyan and Global Home is still before this Court. (See Casa De Valley View Owner’s Assn. v. Stevenson (1985) 167 Cal.App.3d 1182, 1192.) Plaintiff submitted evidence Defendants failed to perform under the Stipulation as promised, and Plaintiff’s counsel attempted to contact Defendants regarding the default to no avail. (Oh Decl. ¶ 4; Motion, Exh. B.)

 

  Further, Plaintiff provides evidence that The North River Insurance Company issued a payment of $7,500 “for the full amount of the penal sum of the Contractors’ License Bond,” and Plaintiff “applied this partial payment towards reducing the principal amount owed by Defendants.” (Oh Decl., ¶ 6; Manley Decl., ¶ 8.) As of the date this Motion was filed, however, Plaintiff had not received any payments from Defendants or any communication stating Defendants will pay any amount. (Manley Decl., ¶¶ 6-7; Oh Decl., ¶¶ 3, 5.) Accordingly, Plaintiff submitted evidence Defendants breached the Stipulation, and Plaintiff is entitled to an order enforcing the Stipulation and entering judgment against Defendants Lusikyan and Global Home Design & Development, Inc.

 

Plaintiff requests that a judgment of $30,770.24, based on the principal amount of $38,270.24 less payment received in the amount of $7,500, be entered against Defendant. (Motion, p. 6; Manley Decl., ¶¶ 8-9; Oh Decl., ¶ 6.) However, the Court notes Plaintiff’s calculation for the Judgment differs slightly from the calculation listed in the definite terms of the Default section of the Stipulation, which states that the Judgment shall be “$38,130.24 less payments received.”  (Motion, Exh. A, ¶ 6.)

 

However, Plaintiff’s motion does not satisfy the Notice Requirements of Code of Civil Procedure § 1005(b) since Hagop Fstkdayam and Narine Fstkdayam have not been served with the Motion. 

 

IV.              Conclusion & Order

 

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff’s unopposed Motion for Order for Judgment Pursuant to Stipulation is CONTINUED to DECEMBER 4, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. in Department 25 of the Spring Street Courthouse, to allow Plaintiff time to serve all parties.

Case Number: 24TRCV00017    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: 25

Hearing Date:                         Monday, October 21, 2024

Case Name:                             Creditors Adjustment Bureau Inc. v. The Times Media Distributing Inc. et al

Case No.:                                24TRCV00017

 

Motions:                                 Motion To Compel Requests for Production of Documents and Request for Sanctions

 

                                                Motion To Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Sanctions

 

Moving Party:                         Plaintiff Creditors Adjustment Bureau Inc.

Responding Party:                   Defendant The Times Media Distributing Inc.

Notice:                                    OK

 

Tentative Ruling:                    Plaintiff Creditors Adjustment Bureau Inc.’s Motion to Compel Requests for Production of Documents and Motion to Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One are DENIED AS MOOT.

 

The Court GRANTS Monetary Sanctions IN THE REDUCED total amount of $750.00.   Defendant’s counsel is ordered to pay Plaintiff $750 within 30 days of this Court’s order.  

 

 

BACKGROUND

 

            On January 3, 2024, Plaintiff Creditors Adjustment Bureau filed the Complaint against Defendants The Times Media Distributing, Inc. aka The Times Media Distributing Inc dba TDS Delivery Services and Does 1-10 for breach of contract, open book account, account stated, and reasonable value.

 

            On August 2, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant Motions to Compel Responses to Requests for Production of Documents, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Monetary Sanctions.  On October 11, 2024, at 3:25 pm, Defendant filed its oppositions. On October 14, 2024, Plaintiff filed its replies.

           

MOVING PARTY POSITION

 

            Plaintiff moves for an order compelling Defendant to serve responses to the Plaintiff’s Request for Production of Documents, Set One and Special Interrogatories, Set One which were served on May 17, 2023. Plaintiff also requests sanctions in the amount of $1,560.00 for each motion.

 

 

OPPOSITION

 

            Defendant argues that it has served responses to Plaintiff’s Special Interrogatories and Plaintiff’s Production of Documents rendering these motions moot. Further, Defendant argues that sanctions should be denied because there was substantial justification as the client’s principal’s son was hospitalized and was unable to help defense counsel in preparing the necessary response. Moreover, in August 2024, due to defense counsel’s error and the transferring from unlimited to limited jurisdiction, counsel lost track of the discovery responses.

 

REPLY

 

            Plaintiff argues that Defendant’s opposition is untimely and should be rejected. Moreover, Plaintiff argues that service of overdue responses does not render the motion moot and monetary sanctions are warranted.

 

ANALYSIS

 

I.          Motion to Compel Initial

            Plaintiff moves for an order compelling Defendant to serve responses to the Plaintiff’s Request for Production of Documents, Set One and Special Interrogatories, Set One which were served on May 17, 2023. Plaintiff also requests sanctions in the amount of $1,560.00 for each motion.

 

A.                                        Legal Standard

 

A party must respond to interrogatories and requests for production of documents within 30 days after service. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.260, subd. (a); Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.260, subd. (a).) If a party to whom interrogatories or requests for production of documents are directed does not provide timely responses, the requesting party may move for an order compelling responses to the discovery. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (b); Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300, subd. (c).) The party also waives the right to make any objections, including one based on privilege or work-product protection. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (a); Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300, subd. (a).) No meet and confer efforts are required before filing a motion to compel responses to the discovery. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290; Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300; Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 411.)

 

Code of Civil Procedure, Section 203 l .300(a) states that the failure to serve a timely response to this discovery results in a waiver of any objections, including ones based on attorney-client privilege or work product. Furthermore, C.C.P. Section 2031.300 states as follows: (b) The party making the demand may move for an order compelling response to the inspection demand. ( c) The court shall impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel a response to an inspection demand, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust. If a party then fails to obey an order compelling a response, the court may make those orders that are just, including the imposition of an issue sanction, and evidence sanction, or a terminating sanction under Chapter 7 ( commencing with Section 2023.010). Additionally, Code of Civil Procedure, Section 2023.010 states, in pertinent part, as follows: Misuses of the discovery process include, but are not limited to, the following: (d) Failing to respond or submit to an authorized method of discovery. C.C.P. Section 2023.030 provides for monetary sanctions where the court finds misuse of discovery.

 

1.                  Timeliness

 

            Under C.C.P. §1005(b), “all moving papers ... shall be served and filed at least sixteen (16) court days before the hearing." Furthermore, "[a]ll papers opposing a motion so noticed shall be filed with the court and a copy served on each party at least nine court days, and all reply papers at least five court days before the hearing.”

 

            Here, Defendant filed its opposition on October 11, 2024, which was six court days before the hearing. Thus, the opposition was untimely by three court days. However, the Court in its discretion will consider the opposition.

 

2.                  Merits

 

            On May 17, 2024, Plaintiff served Special Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Production and Inspection of Documents, Set One on Defendant The Times Media Distributing, Inc. aka The Times Media Distributing Inc dba TDS Delivery Services by mail and email. (Aposhian Decl. ¶ 3.) Responses were due on June 21, 2024. (Id.) Defendant sought a two-week extension. (Aposhian Decl. ¶ 4.) The deadline for the discovery request was moved to July 8, 2024. (Id.) On July 7, 2024, Defendant's counsel asked for an additional (2) two-week extension. On July 11, 2024, Plaintiff responded to Defendant's counsel that discovery responses are overdue, and all objections are waived. However, Plaintiff partially granted Defendant's extension request and demanded that Defendant send objection-free responses by close of business on July 17, 2024, otherwise discovery motions would be filed. (Id.) On July 11, 2024, Defendant's counsel emailed Plaintiff informing Plaintiff that the responses would be received on July 15, 2024. On the same day, Plaintiff again reiterated that if timely and meaningful responses were not received by July 17, 2024, Plaintiff would move forward with Motions to Compel. (Id. at ¶ 5.) Plaintiff waited an additional two (2) weeks beyond the due date to allow Defendant to provide responses.

 

            However, on October 11, 2024, Defendant filed its verified responses to both discovery requests. (Simons Decl. ¶ 6; Ex. A; Aposhian Decl. ¶ 3.) Although Plaintiff argues that the responses are insufficient, this is beyond the scope of these Motions to Compel Initial. Nevertheless, Plaintiff may file a Motion to Compel Further. 

 

            Accordingly, the Court DENIES the Motions to Compel Defendant’s Responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One and Special Interrogatories, Set One as MOOT.

 

 

3.                  Sanctions

 

Plaintiff requests monetary sanctions be awarded in the amount of $1,560.00 for each Motion to Compel in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant and Defense Counsel, jointly, for misuse of discovery.

 

Although Defendant argues that the delay was caused by its client’s principal’s unavailability and attorney mistake, monetary sanctions are warranted against Defense Counsel only. For instance, Defense Counsel failed to notify Plaintiff’s counsel of the principal’s unavailability since July 2024 even though he had requested multiple extensions and agreed to submit the responses on July 15, 2024.  Defense Counsel waited until six days before the hearing to inform Plaintiff about the prior unavailability.

 

Nevertheless, the Court finds the amount requested, $1,560.00 for each Motion to Compel to be excessive because they are nearly identical and straightforward. Thus, the Court GRANTS sanctions in a reduced amount to $750 in total.

 

III.       Conclusion

 

            In all, the Court DENIES the Motion to Compel Requests for Production of Documents and Motion to Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One as MOOT.

 

 The Court GRANTS Monetary Sanctions in the reduced total amount of $750. Defendant’s counsel is ordered to pay Plaintiff $750 within 30 days of this Court’s order.

 

Moving party is to give notice of ruling.
