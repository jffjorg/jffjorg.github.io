DEPARTMENT 30 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

 PLEASE NOTE:    

The parties are encouraged to meet and confer concerning this tentative ruling to determine if there is an agreement to submit.  

Regardless of whether there is any such agreement, each party who wishes to submit must send an email to the Court at SSCdept30@LACourt.org indicating the party's intention to submit. 

Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the case number in the subject line of the email and in the body provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, or non-party.  

If a party submits but still intends to appear at the hearing, include the words "SUBMITS BUT WILL APPEAR" in the subject line of the email. 

If the Court does not receive emails from the parties indicating submission on this tentative ruling and there are no appearances at the hearing, the Court may, at its discretion, adopt the tentative as the final order or place the motion off calendar. 

Unless all the parties have submitted, the Court will hear argument from any party that appears at the hearing and wishes to argue. The Court may change its tentative as a result of the argument and adopt the changed tentative as the final order at the end of that hearing, even if all the parties are not present. 

Be advised that after the Court has posted/issued a tentative ruling, the Court has the inherent authority to prohibit the withdrawal of said motion and may adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the Court.     


Case Number: 21STCV12948    Hearing Date: December 14, 2023    Dept: 30

TINNA JONSDOTTIR vs HONGSHI SUN, et al.

TENTATIVE

The motion to be relieved as counsel for Defendant, Hongshi Sun, is CONTINUED. Counsel for Defendant has submitted all of the mandatory judicial council forms (i.e., MC-051, MC-052, and MC-053). However, the papers have not been served on Defendant. (See CRC Rule 3.1362(d).) In addition, the proposed order and declaration have outdated hearing dates listed. (See CRC Rule 3.1362(e).) As a result, counsel is ordered to file a new proposed order, and declaration, list updated hearing dates, and serve Defendant with the notice of motion, motion and declaration.

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

Case Number: 22STCV00377    Hearing Date: December 14, 2023    Dept: 30

JASON MOTA, AN INDIVIDUAL vs RONALD SMOTHERS, AN INDIVIDUAL

TENTATIVE

Defendant Ronald Smothers’ motions to compel Plaintiff to provide responses to form interrogatories, special interrogatories, and requests for production for documents are GRANTED.

Plaintiff Jason Mota is ordered to provide responses without objection to Defendant’s request for form interrogatories, special interrogatories, and request for production within 20 days of this order.

Defendant’s request for sanctions is DENIED.

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

Discussion

On March 1, 2023, Defendant served Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Production of Documents, Set One, on Plaintiff. (Mullen Decl., ¶ 2; Exh. A.) On May 16, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel served unverified Responses. (Id., Exh. B.) No verified responses were provided.

As Defendant properly served discovery requests and Plaintiff has failed to serve verified responses, the Court finds Defendant is entitled to a court order directing Plaintiff to provide verified responses without objections to the discovery requests served on Plaintiff. Therefore, the motions are granted.

Defendant also requests sanctions. Code of Civil Procedure §§ 2030.290(c) and 2031.300(c) allow for sanctions when the motion was unsuccessfully opposed. Here, as the motions were not opposed, the request for sanctions is denied.

Defendant’s request for sanctions under CCP sections 2023.010 for the alleged misuse of discovery is also denied, as "sections 2023.010 and 2023.030 do not independently authorize the trial court to impose monetary sanctions for misuse of discovery.” (City of Los Angeles v. PricewaterhouseCoopers, LLC (2022) 84 Cal.App.5th 466, 504.)

SOFIA JAMORA vs JAXSON HAYES

TENTATIVE

Defendant Jaxson Hayes’ motion or sanctions is GRANTED in part. Plaintiff’s counsel Waukeen McCoy is ordered to pay monetary sanctions in the amount of $720, to be paid within 30 days of this order.  Moving party to give notice.

Legal Standard

Code of Civil Procedure section 2025.430 provides: “If the party giving notice of a deposition fails to attend or proceed with it, the court shall impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) against that party, or the attorney for that party, or both, and in favor of any party attending in person or by attorney, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” (CCP § 2025.430.)

Discussion

Plaintiffs served Defendant with a Notice of Deposition of Officer Robert Tamate on September 6, 2023, noticing the deposition of Officer Tamate for September 21, 2023. (Baghdishyan Decl., ¶ 2; Exh. A). On September 21, 2023, defense counsel appeared at the time and place designated for the deposition expecting the deposition to go forward. (Id., ¶ 3.) However, the door was locked and no one answered the door despite several knocks. (Id.) Defense counsel waited for approximately thirty minutes but no one opened the door. (Id.) While waiting in the hallway, defense counsel sent an email to Plaintiff’s counsel stating that he was at the location, but no one appeared to be behind the locked door. (Id.) The same email also asked whether the deponent was served. (Id.) Neither Plaintiff’s counsel responded to the email until the following day. (Id., ¶ 4, Exh. C.)

Officer Tamate was indeed served by Plaintiff’s process servers and had arrived at the location of the deposition and was similarly met with a locked and unanswered door. (Baghdishyan Decl., ¶ 5.) To Defendant’s knowledge, neither Plaintiff nor her counsel ever arrived at the deposition site on the scheduled date. (Id., ¶ 3.)

Defendant argues monetary sanctions are appropriate because Plaintiff noticed the deposition of Officer Tamate, did not cancel it, and failed to attend and proceed with it.

In opposition, Plaintiff’s counsel argues that he tried to confirm with the process serving company prior to the date his deposition was noticed that Officer Tamate had in fact been served with the notice of deposition. (McCoy Decl., Exh. C.) The process serving company responded that they would contact the server regarding their attempts and update the portal with information once it was received. Consequently, Plaintiff's counsel did not issue any notice to the parties about proceeding with the deposition. Unbeknownst, to Plaintiff's counsel, Officer Tamate had in fact been served on September 8, 2023. (McCoy Decl., Exh. B). It was only six days past the scheduled deposition date that the process serving company finally provided Plaintiff's counsel with the proof of service. (See McCoy Decl., Exh. D).                

The Court finds sanctions against Plaintiff’s counsel are warranted. Plaintiff’s counsel noticed the deposition of Officer Tamate, did not cancel it, and failed to attend and proceed with it. While Plaintiff’s counsel has explained why he believed the deposition would not proceed, he failed to notify defense counsel of this, resulting in a waste of defense counsel’s time in attending the deposition. As a result, Plaintiff’s counsel has not provided any justification for failing to inform defense counsel that the deposition would not proceed as noticed. However, the Court finds the amount requested is unreasonable for this simple and straightforward motion. Further, the time spent on the preparation of the deposition has not been wasted, as the next time the deposition is set, defense counsel will likely spend less than half the time preparing due to his previous preparation. As a result, the Court will impose sanctions against Plaintiff’s counsel Waukeen McCoy in the amount of $720 ($660 per hour for one hour, plus $60 filing fee) to be paid within 30 days of this order. Plaintiff’s request for sanctions for having to oppose this motion is denied, as this motion is being granted, and because the notice of the opposition did not request sanctions. (See CCP section 2023.040.)
