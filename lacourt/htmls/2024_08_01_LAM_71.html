DEPARTMENT 71 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


            All parties are urged to meet and confer with all parties concerning this tentative ruling to see if they can reach an agreed-upon resolution of their matter.  If you are able to reach an agreement, please notify the courtroom staff in advance of the hearing if you wish to submit on the tentative ruling rather than argue the motion by notifying the court by e-mailing the court at: SMCDept71@LACourt.org.  Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the Case Number in the Subject line.  In the body of the email, please provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, and whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, intervenor, or non-party, etc.

            Please be advised that if you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may still appear at the hearing and argue the matter, and the court could change its tentative based upon the argument.  Unless you receive a submission from all other parties in the matter, you should assume that others might appear at the hearing to argue.  If you submit, but still intend to appear, include the words "SUBMITS, BUT WILL APPEAR" in the Subject line.    

            If you elect to argue your matter, you are urged to do so remotely, via Court-Connect. If the moving party fails to appear and/or submit to the Court’s tentative ruling, the Court will take the  matter off calendar.
                          
            Note that once the Court has issued a tentative, the Court has the inherent authority not to allow the withdrawal of a motion and to adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the court.   
 
            If you submitted a courtesy copy of your papers containing media (such as a DVD or thumb drive), unless you request the return of the media in your papers, the court will destroy it following the hearing of your matter.  

Case Number: 22STCV34320    Hearing Date: August 1, 2024    Dept: 71

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

 

DEPARTMENT 71

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

MARKELLA THANOU, et al., 

 

         vs.

 

CEDARS-SINAI MEDICAL CENTER.

 Case No.:  22STCV34320

 

 

 

 Hearing Date:  August 1, 2024

 

Defendant Cedars-Sinai Medical Center’s demurrer to Plaintiffs Markella Thanou’s and Amalia Thanou’s first amended complaint is sustained without leave to amend as to the 1st (as to the survival action only), 2nd, and 3rd causes of action.

Defendant Cedars-Sinai Medical Center’s unopposed motion to strike is granted.

Plaintiff Markella Thanou’s unopposed motion for trial preference is granted.  The Court sets the trial of this matter on November 25, 2024, at 10:00 a.m. and the Final Status Conference on November 15, 2024, at 9:30 a.m.  Both are in Dept. 71.

Defendant’s unopposed motion to compel Plaintiff to provide verified responses to its Form Interrogatories (Set One) is granted.  Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified responses to Defendant’s FROGs compliant with C.C.P. §§2030.210(a) and 2030.220 without objections within 10 days.

Defendant’s unopposed motion to compel Plaintiff to provide verified responses to its Special Interrogatories (Set One) is granted.  Plaintiff is ordered to provide verified responses to Defendant’s SROGs compliant with C.C.P. §§2030.210(a) and 2030.220 without objections within 10 days.

Defendant’s unopposed motion to deem the truth of the matters admitted and the genuineness of the documents specified therein in Defendant’s Request for Admissions (Set One) served on Plaintiff is granted.

 

Defendant Cedars-Sinai Medical Center (“CSMC”) (“Defendant”) demurs to Plaintiff Markella Thanou’s (“Markella”) (“Plaintiff”) first amended complaint (“FAC”).  (Notice of Demurrer, pg. 1.)  Defendant also filed a motion to strike portions of Plaintiff’s FAC.  (Notice of MTS, pgs. 2-3.)

Plaintiff moves for an order granting her trial preference.  (Notice Trial Preference, pg. 1; C.C.P. §§ 36(a), (c), (e), (f); CRC, Rule 3.1335.)

Defendant moves unopposed to compel Plaintiff to provide responses to its Form Interrogatories (Set One) (“FROG”).  (Notice of MTC, pg. 2.) [1]

Defendant moves unopposed to compel Plaintiff to provide responses to its Special Interrogatories (Set One) (“SROG”).  (Notice of MTC, pg. 2.)

Defendant moves unopposed for an order deeming the truth of all matters specified in Request for Admissions (Set One) (“RFA”).  (Notice of RFA, pgs. 1-2.)

 

Background

This action arises out of Defendant’s diagnosis, treatment, and care of Demetrios Fotinatos (“Fotinatos”) (“Decedent”).  (See FAC ¶8.)  Plaintiff alleges Defendant failed to take reasonable care in performing Decedent’s admission to the hospital, for which he should have never been admitted, and subsequently treating him with Xanax, on or about July 24, 2021, when he was over the age limit for being given Xanax and Defendant was told that he had an adverse reaction to Xanax and he was not supposed to take it.  (FAC ¶10.)  Plaintiff alleges that prior to Decedent’s admission, he would walk three miles a day, and he became a zombie after being in Defendant’s care and never recovered to be how he was before being admitted.  (FAC ¶10.)  Plaintiff alleges as a result of Defendant’s negligence, Decedent suffered injuries to his person and was transferred to rehab where he was misfed while lying down, which ultimately resulted in his death on October 24, 2021.  (FAC ¶11.)  Plaintiff alleges as a result of Defendant’s negligence, he was subjected to continuous and unimaginable pain and suffering and being unnecessarily subjected to take prescribed strong medication because of the failure to diagnose him properly.  (FAC ¶11.)  Plaintiff alleges Decedent’s health deteriorated after that and ultimately resulted in his death.  (FAC ¶11.)  Plaintiff alleges Decedent sustained various losses, including but not limited to pain, suffering, emotional distress.  (FAC ¶11.)

A.   Defendant’s Demurrer

Summary of Demurrer

Defendant demurs to Plaintiffs’ causes of action for wrongful death and survivor action and professional negligence on the basis the causes of action fail to allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action and are uncertain.  (Demurrer, pg. 2; C.C.P. §§430.10(e), (f).)  Defendant demurs to Plaintiff’s cause of action for loss of consortium on the basis it is uncertain.  (Demurrer, pg. 2; C.C.P. §430.10(f).)

 

          Meet and Confer

Before filing a demurrer pursuant to this chapter, the demurring party shall meet and confer in person, by telephone, or by video conference with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to demurrer for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer.  (C.C.P. §430.41(a), emphasis added.)  A declaration must be filed with a demurrer regarding the results of the meet and confer process.  (C.C.P. §430.41(a)(3).)

Defendant’s counsel declares that on May 19, 2024, he attempted to meet and confer with Plaintiff’s counsel by email.  (Decl. of Moulin ¶13.)  Defendant’s counsel declares that Plaintiff’s counsel never responded to his meet and confer letter.  (Decl. of Moulin ¶14.)  Defendant’s counsel’s declaration is not sufficient under C.C.P. §430.41(a) because it does not state that he met and conferred with Plaintiff’s counsel confer in person, by telephone, or by video conference.  However, the failure to sufficiently meet and confer is not grounds to overrule or sustain a demurrer.  (CCP § 430.41(a)(4); Dumas v. Los Angeles County Board of Supervisors (2020) 45 Cal.App.5th 348, 355; Olson v. Hornbrook Community Services District (2019) 33 Cal.App.5th 502, 515.)  Accordingly, the Court will consider the instant demurrer.

 

Legal Standard

“[A] demurrer tests the legal sufficiency of the allegations in a complaint.” (Lewis v. Safeway, Inc. (2015) 235 Cal.App.4th 385, 388.)  A demurrer can be used only to challenge defects that appear on the face of the pleading under attack or from matters outside the pleading that are judicially noticeable.  (See Donabedian v. Mercury Insurance Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994 [in ruling on a demurrer, a court may not consider declarations, matters not subject to judicial notice, or documents not accepted for the truth of their contents].)  For purposes of ruling on a demurrer, all facts pleaded in a complaint are assumed to be true, but the reviewing court does not assume the truth of conclusions of law.  (Aubry v. Tri-City Hospital District (1992) 2 Cal.4th 962, 967.)

         

          Failure to State a Claim

          Wrongful Death and Survival Action (1st COA)

          A cause of action for wrongful death requires the following elements: (1) a wrongful act or neglect on the part of one or more persons; (2) causing the death of another.  (Norgart v. Upjohn Co. (1999) 21 Cal.4th 383, 404, citing CCP §377.60.) 

The statute of limitations for a survival action, “[i]n an action for injury or death against a health care provider based upon such person’s alleged professional negligence, the time for the commencement of action shall be three years after the date of injury or one year after the plaintiff discovers, or through the use of reasonable diligence should have discovered, the injury, whichever occurs first.” (C.C.P. §§377.20(a), 340.5.)

The statute of limitations for Plaintiff’s wrongful death cause of action of expired one year after decedent’s death on October 24, 2021.  Thus, any survival claim on behalf of Decedent had to be filed by or before October 24, 2022.  Plaintiff’s initial Complaint did not allege a survival claim on behalf of Decedent.  Plaintiff’s initial Complaint alleged a wrongful death claim on behalf of Plaintiff.  A cause of action for wrongful death is distinct from a survival action under C.C.P. §377.34.  (Quiroz v. Seventh Ave. Center (2006) 140 Cal.App.4th 1256, 1264 [“The damages recoverable in [wrongful death] are expressly limited to those not recoverable in a survival action under Code of Civil Procedure section 377.34.”].) Therefore, a survival action does not relate back to a timely filed wrongful death action.  (Id. at pgs. 1278-1279.)  Here, the FAC’s cause of action for survival is time-barred as a matter of law because it cannot relate back to the initial cause of action only for wrongful death. Plaintiff’s initial Complaint alleges Decedent was transferred to rehab, where he was “miss-fed while lying down, which ultimately resulted in his death on October 24, 2021.”  (Complaint ¶GN-1.)  The initial Complaint does not allege that Defendant caused Decedent’s death, but rather conduct at the rehab facility caused Decedent’s death.

          Accordingly, Defendant’s demurrer to Plaintiff’s 1st cause of action for wrongful death and survival action is sustained without leave to amend as to the survival action only.

 

Professional Negligence (2nd COA)

“The elements of a cause of action for medical malpractice are: (1) a duty to use such skill, prudence, and diligence as other members of the profession commonly possess and exercise; (2) a breach of the duty; (3) a proximate causal connection between the negligent conduct and the injury; and (4) resulting loss or damage.”  (Lattimore v. Dickey (2015) 239 Cal.App.4th 959, 968.)

“On causation, the plaintiff must establish ‘it is more probable than not the negligent act was a cause-in-fact of the plaintiff’s injury.’ ‘A possible cause only becomes ‘probable’ when, in the absence of other reasonable causal explanations, it becomes more likely than not that the injury was a result of its action.’ ‘[C]ausation in actions arising from medical negligence must be proven within a reasonable medical probability based on competent expert testimony, i.e., something more than a “50-50 possibility.”’ ‘[T]he evidence must be sufficient to allow the jury to infer that in the absence of the defendant’s negligence, there was a reasonable medical probability the plaintiff would have obtained a better result.’” (Belfiore-Braman v. Rotenberg (2018) 25 Cal.App.5th 234, 247, internal citations omitted.)

“The Medical Injury Compensation Reform Act (MICRA) contains numerous provisions effecting substantial changes in negligence actions against health care providers, including a limitation on noneconomic damages, elimination of the collateral source rule as well as preclusion of subrogation in most instances, and authorization for periodic payments of future damages in excess of $50,000. While in each instance the statutory scheme has altered a significant aspect of claims for medical malpractice, such as the measure of the defendant’s liability for damages or the admissibility of evidence, the fundamental substance of such actions on the issues of duty, standard of care, breach, and causation remains unaffected.”  (Flowers v. Torrance Memorial Hospital Medical Center (1994) 8 Cal.4th 992, 999.)

Plaintiff failed to allege a duty of care owed by Defendant to Plaintiff; Plaintiff merely alleges a duty of care owed by Defendant to Decedent.  An essential element of a cause of action for medical negligence is a physician-patient relationship giving rise to a duty of care.  (Mero v. Sadoff (1995) 31 Cal.App.4th 1466, 1471.)  A third party does not ordinarily have standing to bring a claim asserting a violation of someone else’s rights.  (Powers v. Ohio (1991) 499 U.S. 400, 410; People ex rel. Becerra v. Superior Court (2018) 29 Cal.App.5th 486, 500-501.)  Plaintiff failed to assert survivor claims in her initial Complaint, which would have given her standing to assert a cause of action for medical negligence.  (C.C.P. §377.30; Grant v. McAuliffe (1953) 41 Cal.2d 859; Brenner v. Universal Health Services of Rancho Springs, Inc. (2017) 12 Cal.App.5th 589.) 

Accordingly, Defendant’s demurrer to Plaintiff’s 2nd cause of action is sustained without leave to amend.

 

Loss of Consortium (3rd COA)

A cause of action for loss of consortium requires the following elements: (1) a valid and lawful marriage between the plaintiff and injured party, at time of injury; (2) tortious injury to the plaintiff’s spouse; (3) loss of consortium suffered by plaintiff; and (4) loss proximately caused by defendant’s act.  (Vanhooser v. Superior Court (2012) 206 Cal.App.4th 921, 927.)

Plaintiff alleges she and Decedent were lawfully married at all times relevant to this action.  (FAC ¶42.)  Plaintiff alleges as a result of the aforementioned injuries and death of Decedent, Plaintiff has been deprived of her spouse’s companionship, affection, love, sexual relations, conjugal fellowship, physical assistance in maintaining the family home and comfort, which deprivation has caused, and continued to cause Plaintiff to suffer depression, emotional distress, loss of earning capacity, past, present, and future, and other injuries, the full extent of which has not yet been ascertained, but which will be stated according to proof at trial.  (FAC ¶43.)

Plaintiff fails to allege her loss was proximately caused by Defendant’s act because she alleges Decedent’s death resulted from his transfer to rehab, where he was mis-fed lying down.  (FAC ¶11.)  Further, Plaintiff was not granted leave to amend her initial Complaint to add the instant cause of action.

Accordingly, Defendant’s demurrer to Plaintiff’s 3rd cause of action is sustained without leave to amend.

 

Conclusion

Defendant’s demurrer to Plaintiffs’ Complaint is sustained without leave to amend as to the 1st (as to the survival action only), 2nd, and 3rd causes of action.

Moving Party to give notice.

 

B.    Defendant’s Motion to Strike

Legal Standard

C.C.P. §436 provides that the Court may, upon a motion made pursuant to C.C.P. §435, or at any time within its discretion and upon terms it deems proper, “strike out any irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading.”  (C.C.P. §436(a).)

 

Summary of Motion

Defendant moves to strike the following portions of the FAC: (1) any and all references to Plaintiff as “Successor-in-Interest” to the Estate of Demetrios Fotinatos as reflected on Page 1:18, Pg 1, ¶1:1, Pg. 2, ¶¶2:1-11, Pg. 13:14-15; (2) Any and all references to Plaintiff as the “representative of the other statutory beneficiaries” including as a representative of Amalia Thanou, as reflected in Pg 1, ¶2:28; (3) Any and all references to “Plaintiff the Estate of Demetrios Fotinatos” as reflected on Pg. 2, ¶¶2:1-11; (4) Amalia Thanou (62) Stepdaughter, as reflected on Pg. 3, ¶6(b); (5) Step-daughter Amalia Thanou, as reflected on Pg. 3, ¶7:5; (6) References to CACI No. 400, as reflected on Pg. 4, ¶13:10-18. (7) References to CACI 3921, as reflected on Pg. 5, ¶19; (8) References to loss of consortium “suffered by decedent’s family” as reflected on Pg. 7, ¶22:4; (9) References to punitive damages, malice, oppression, despicable conduct, fraud, as reflected on Pgs. 8-9, ¶30:24-21; (10) References to punitive damages, as reflected on Pg. 10, ¶¶31-33:10-18; (11) References to CACI 400, as reflected on Pgs. 10-11, ¶35:23-2; (12) References to CACI 3920, as reflected on Pg. 11, ¶41:20-28; (13) References to “newly signed Assembly Bill 35” as referenced on Pg. 12, ¶¶44-45:13-28; (14) Prayer for general damages “in the amount of the jurisdictional $500,000 maximum, according to proof,” as reflected on Pg. 13, ¶49, Pg. 15, ¶50:6-7, and Pg. 16:1-2; (15) References to “Brandon Derdak, and Ashley Derdak” on Pg. 14:4; and (16) References to non-economic damages in the amount of $500,000 as referenced on Pg. 14:19-20.  (MTS, pgs. 2-3; C.C.P §§425.13, 435, 436.)

 

Successor-in-Interest Allegations

A survival action may be brought by a successor-in-interest to the Estate and is subject to the applicable statute of limitations of the underlying action.  (C.C.P. §377.20(a).)

A newly pled cause of action for survival does not relate back to the filing of the original complaint for wrongful death; Plaintiff’s the survival action is time-barred as a matter of law, as stated in the demurrer.  (Quiroz, 140 Cal.App.4th at pgs. 1278-1279.)

The FAC purports to substitute in (1) Plaintiff Markella Thanou “as successor in interest” to the Estate of Demetrios Fotinatos (“Estate”), and (2) the Estate, in an attempt to plead the survival cause of action on behalf of the Estate and beneficiaries of the Estate.  A new plaintiff cannot be joined after the statute of limitations has run where that plaintiff seeks to enforce an independent right. (Id.; Branick v. Downey Saving & Loan Association (2006) 39 Cal.4th 235; Diliberti v. Stage Call Corp. (1992) 4 Cal.App.4th 1468, 1471.)  The new plaintiff, Markella Thanou as successor in interest and the Estate, seeks to vindicate a survival claim. Therefore, neither the survival claim, nor the newly substituted plaintiff relates back to the filing of the initial Complaint and are time-barred.

Inclusion of Plaintiff as successor in interest and the Estate in the text of the FAC is irrelevant, false, and improper and not in conformity with the law.  The prayer for damages the FAC also seeks “funeral and burial expenses paid by Plaintiffs . . . Brandon Derdak, and Ashley Derdak.”  (FAC, Pg. 14:3-5.)  These people are not Plaintiffs in this action, nor are they identified anywhere in the FAC, and their inclusion in the FAC is irrelevant, false, and improper and not in conformity with the law.

Accordingly, Defendant’s motion to strike portions of the FAC pertaining to successors-in-interest is granted.

 

CACI Jury Instructions

The FAC references and quotes CACI jury instructions throughout the text of the FAC. The text of CACI jury instructions is irrelevant and improper and not filed in conformity with the law.

Accordingly, Defendant’s motion to strike the FAC’s references and quotes to CACI jury instructions is granted.

 

Civil Code §3333.2

The FAC references A.B. 35, various history regarding this bill, and the current version of Civil Code §3333.2 that went into effect for cases filed on or after January 1, 2023.  Civil Code §3333.2 is not retroactive.

Civil Code §3333.2 states in pertinent part: “(g) This section shall be deemed effective as of, and shall apply to all cases filed . . . on or after, January 1, 2023.”  (Civ. Code §3333.2(g).)

Plaintiff’s initial complaint was filed on October 25, 2022, when the previous version of Civil Code §3333.2 was in effect. The FAC’s references to A.B. 35, various history regarding A.B. 35, the version of Civil Code § 3333.2 that went into effect on January 1, 2023, and demands for noneconomic damages under the current version of Civil Code §3333.2 are irrelevant, false, improper and not filed in conformity with the law.

Accordingly, Defendant’s motion to strike the FAC’s references to A.B. 35 is granted.

 

Punitive Damages

C.C.P. § 425.13 states, in part:

(a)      In any action for damages arising out of the professional negligence of any health care provider, no claim for punitive damages shall be included in a complaint or other pleading unless the court enters an order allowing an amended pleading that includes a claim for punitive damages to be filed. The court may allow the filing of an amended pleading claiming punitive damages on a motion by the party seeking the amended pleading and on the basis of the supporting and opposing affidavits presented that the plaintiff has established that there is a substantial probability that the plaintiff will prevail on the claim pursuant to Section 3294 of the Civil Code . . .

 

(b)      For the purposes of this section, “health care provider” means any person licensed or certified pursuant to Division 2 . . . of the Business and Professions Code.

 

(C.C.P. §425.13(a)-(b).)

Where the facts which give rise to injuries and damages stem from the alleged professional negligence of a health care provider, the MICRA statutes apply.  (See Business & Professions Code §6164; Civil Code §§3333.1, 3333.2; C.C.P. §§340.5, 364, 667.7, 1295.)

Hospitals are “health care providers” under the MICRA statutes. A hospital’s alleged negligent treatment of a patient is professional negligence. (Flowers v. Torrance Memorial Hospital Medical Center (1994) 8 Cal.4th 992, 998.)

The allegations of the FAC stem from the manner in which Defendant provided medical care to Decedent.  Therefore, Plaintiff must obtain court authorization by way of a noticed motion, prior to pleading punitive damages. Plaintiff failed to follow the mandatory procedures before alleging punitive damages.

Accordingly, Defendants’ motion to strike Plaintiff’s references to punitive damages, malice, fraud, oppression, and despicable conduct from the FAC is granted. 

 

Conclusion

Defendants’ motion to strike is granted.

Moving Party to give notice.

 

C.    Plaintiff’s Motion for Trial Preference

Legal Standard

Parties who are 70 years of age or older (or who reach that age during pendency of the action) may be entitled to preference if they establish to the court’s satisfaction that: (1) they have a “substantial interest in the action as a whole”; and (2) their health is “such that a preference is necessary to prevent prejudicing the party’s interest in the litigation.”  (C.C.P. §36(a), (c)(2).)

The preference motion must be supported by a “declaration of the moving party” (or presumably, the moving party’s attorney) “that all essential parties have been served with process or have appeared.”  (C.C.P. §36(c)(1).)

Trial priority is not mandatory and absolute merely because one of the parties is age 70. The court has discretion to determine the extent of that party’s interest and find as a matter of fact the risk posed of that party’s death or incapacity if trial is delayed.  (C.C.P. § 36(a).)

 

Discussion

          Plaintiff’s motion and supporting declaration demonstrates Plaintiff is entitled to a motion for trial preference.  Plaintiff’s supplementary declaration submits admissible evidence from her medical provider stating that she is currently 94 years old.  (Supp.-Decl. of Thanou ¶6, Exh. A.)

Plaintiff’s attorney declares she is currently in ill health as she suffers numerous illnesses such as congestive heart failure, chronic kidney disease, Hypertension, has a pacemaker, has Hyperlipidemia, and suffers from Atrial fibrillation, for which she is currently on various medications.  (Decl. of Opia-Enwemuche ¶6.)  Plaintiff’s attorney declares on information and belief that Plaintiff has a grave heart ailment and now has Cirrhosis of the liver, due to her heart failure.  (Decl. of Opia-Enwemuche ¶8.)  Plaintiff’s attorney’s evidence supporting these facts would sufficiently supplement admissible evidence of Plaintiff’s advanced age.

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s motion for trial preference is granted.

 

Conclusion

Legal Standard

California Rule of Court rule 3.1362 (Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel) requires (1) notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client (made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-051)); (2) a declaration stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(1) (made on the Declaration in Support of Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-052)); (3) service of the notice of motion and motion and declaration on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and (4) the proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on the Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-053)).

The court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client, and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice.  (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.)

 

Discussion

Counsel has submitted completed MC-051, MC-052, and MC-053 forms. Counsel has provided a declaration stating Plaintiff no longer responds to counsel’s attempts at communication, does not follow advice or suggests, and there no longer exists a level of trust necessary to continue representation.  (See Decl. of Fradkin.)  Counsel has indicated that Caldera was served with copies of the motion papers filed with the declaration at Caldera’s last known address and has confirmed within the past 30 days that the address is current by telephone.  (See Decl. of Fradkin ¶3.)

 

Conclusion

Caldera’s counsel’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel is granted.

Counsel will be relieved upon filing proof of service on their client of the Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (Judicial Council form MC-053).
