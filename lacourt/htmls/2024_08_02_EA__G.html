Case Number: 23PSCV03141    Hearing Date: August 2, 2024    Dept: G

Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority and Metro Gold Line Foothill Extension Construction Authority’s Demurrer to Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint

Respondent: Plaintiffs Alicia Olivares, Stephanie Watkin, Amaris Violet Gutierrez, Abel Valentino Gutierrez, and Angelina Victoria Gallegos

Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority and Metro Gold Line Foothill Extension Construction Authority’s Motion to Strike Punitive Damages from Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint

Defendants Kiewit Corporation, Kiewit-Parsons, Kiewit Infrastructure West Co., and Parsons Construction Group, Inc.’s Demurrer to Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint

Respondent: Plaintiffs Alicia Olivares, Stephanie Watkin, Amaris Violet Gutierrez, Abel Valentino Gutierrez, and Angelina Victoria Gallegos

Defendants Kiewit Corporation, Kiewit-Parsons, Kiewit Infrastructure West Co., and Parsons Construction Group, Inc.’s Motion to Strike Punitive Damages from Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint

Respondent: Plaintiffs Alicia Olivares, Stephanie Watkin, Amaris Violet Gutierrez, Abel Valentino Gutierrez, and Angelina Victoria Gallegos

TENTATIVE RULING

Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority and Metro Gold Line Foothill Extension Construction Authority’s Demurrer to Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint and Motion to Strike Punitive Damages from Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint are CONTINUED to a date to be determined at the hearing in Department G (Pomona).

Defendants’ counsel is also ordered to meet and confer with Plaintiffs’ counsel regarding the Demurrer and Motion to Strike and to file a supplemental declaration describing such meet and confer efforts at least nine (9) court days before the next scheduled hearing.

Defendants Kiewit Corporation, Kiewit-Parsons, Kiewit Infrastructure West Co., and Parsons Construction Group, Inc.’s Demurrer to Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint is SUSTAINED without leave to amend as to Plaintiff Stephanie Watkin’s causes of action and with twenty (20) days leave to amend as to Plaintiffs Angelina Victoria Gallegos and Abel Valentino Gutierrez’s fifth and sixth causes of action.

Defendants Kiewit Corporation, Kiewit-Parsons, Kiewit Infrastructure West Co., and Parsons Construction Group, Inc.’s Motion to Strike Punitive Damages from Plaintiffs’ Second Amended Complaint is deemed MOOT.

BACKGROUND

This is a wrongful death action arising from a motor vehicle collision. In November 2022, Alberto J. Gutierrez was the passenger of a 2006 GMC Sierra that was travelling eastbound on Route 66 in Glendora near the intersection of Hunters Trail. As a result of the allegedly narrow construction of the road, Alberto Gutierrez’s truck was forced off the road by a vehicle in the other lane and collided with a wall on the southside of the roadway. Alberto Gutierrez was killed after the impact caused the truck to flip over.

On October 10, 2023, Plaintiffs Alicia Olivares, individually and as successor in interest to Alberto Gutierrez; Stephanie Watkin, individually and as successor in interest to Alberto Gutierrez; Amaris Violet Gutierrez, by and through Watkin as guardian ad litem; Abel Valentino Gutierrez, by and through Watkin as guardian ad litem; and Angelina Victoria Gallegos, individually and as successor in interest to Alberto Gutierrez (collectively, Gutierrez Plaintiffs) filed a complaint against Defendants City of Glendora (the City), Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority (LACMTA), the Metro Gold Line Foothill Extension Construction Authority (Foothill Gold Line), Kiewit Corporation (Kiewit), Fernando Vasquez Barrera, and Does 1-200, alleging the following causes of action: (1) negligence, (2) dangerous condition of public property, (3) negligence per se, (4) loss of consortium, (5) wrongful death, and (6) survival.

On December 8, 2023, the Gutierrez Plaintiffs amended the Complaint to replace Doe 1 with Defendant Kiewit-Parsons and Doe 2 with Defendant Kiewit Infrastructure West Co. (Kiewit West). On December 11, 2023, the Gutierrez Plaintiffs amended the Complaint to replace Doe 3 with Defendant Parsons Construction Group Inc. (Parsons).

On February 2, 2024, the Gutierrez Plaintiffs filed a First Amended Complaint (FAC) against the same defendants alleging the same causes of action.

On February 20, 2024, LACMTA filed a cross-complaint against Vasquez Barrera and Does 1-10, alleging causes of action for (1) indemnification, (2) apportionment of fault, and (3) declaratory relief. On the same day, Foothill Gold Line also filed a cross-complaint against Vasquez Barrera and Does 1-10, alleging causes of action for (1) indemnification, (2) apportionment of fault, and (3) declaratory relief.

On March 6, 2024, Kiewit, Kiewit-Parsons, Kiewit West, and Parsons (collectively, Kiewit-Parsons Defendants) filed a cross-complaint against Vasquez Barrera and Roes 1-50, alleging causes of action for (1) equitable indemnity, (2) implied indemnity, (3) comparative fault, and (4) declaratory relief.

On April 22, 2024, the Court sustained the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ demurrer to the FAC in part with leave to amend.  On May 13, 2024, the Gutierrez Plaintiffs filed a Second Amended Complaint (SAC) against the same defendants alleging the same causes of action.

On June 14, 2024, LACMTA and Foothill Gold Line filed one of the present demurrers and motions to strike. On the same day, the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants also filed one of the present demurrers and motions to strike. Prior to filing on May 23, 2024, the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ counsel attempted to meet and confer telephonically with the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ counsel and was unable to reach a resolution. (Fersch Decl., ¶ 3.)

A hearing on the present demurrers and motions to strike are set for August 2, 2024, along with a case management conference.

LACMTA AND FOOTHILL GOLD LINE’S DEMURRER

LACMTA and Foothill Gold Line demur to the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ fourth cause of action (­loss of consortium), fifth cause of action (­wrongful death), and sixth cause of action (­survival). For the following reasons, the court finds parties did not adequately meet and confer.

Legal Standard

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 430.41, subdivision (a), prior to filing a demurrer, “the demurring party shall meet and confer in person or by telephone with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to demurrer for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer.” This section further provides that “the demurring party shall identify all of the specific causes of action that it believes are subject to demurrer and identify with legal support the basis of the deficiencies.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41, subd. (a)(1).)

While Code of Civil Procedure section 430.41, subdivision (a)(4) makes clear failing to meet and confer is not grounds to overrule a demurrer, courts “are not required to ignore defects in the meet and confer process” and if the court determines “no meet and confer has taken place, or concludes further conferences between counsel would likely be productive, it retains discretion to order counsel to meaningfully discuss the pleadings with an eye toward reducing the number of issues or eliminating the need for a demurrer, and to continue the hearing date to facilitate that effort.” (Dumas v. Los Angeles County Bd. of Supervisors (2020) 45 Cal.App.5th 348, 355 & fn. 3.)

Discussion

In this case, LACMTA and Foothill Gold Line’s counsel filed a declaration stating counsel attempted to meet and confer by sending multiple emails to the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ counsel. Because emails are not the proper means of meeting and conferring and counsel fails to describe any attempt at telephonic communication, the court finds these meet and confer attempts to be inadequate. Accordingly, the court finds a continuance of the hearing on LACMTA and Foothill Gold Line’s demurrer and motion to strike is appropriate.

KIEWIT-PARSONS DEFENDANTS’ DEMURRER

The Kiewit-Parsons Defendants demur to the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ fourth cause of action (­loss of consortium), fifth cause of action (­wrongful death), and sixth cause of action (­survival). For the following reasons, the court SUSTAINS their demurrer in its entirety.

Legal Standard

A party may demur to a complaint on the grounds that it “does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (e).) A demurrer tests whether the complaint states a cause of action. (Hahn v. Mirda (2007) 147 Cal.App.4th 740, 747 (Hahn).) When considering demurrers, courts accept all well pleaded facts as true. (Fox v. JAMDAT Mobile, Inc. (2010) 185 Cal.App.4th 1068, 1078.) In a demurrer proceeding, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or via proper judicial notice. (Donabedian v. Mercury Ins. Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994.) “A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters. Therefore, it lies only where the defects appear on the face of the pleading or are judicially noticed.” (SKF Farms v. Superior Court (1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 902, 905.) “The only issue involved in a demurrer hearing is whether the complaint, as it stands, unconnected with extraneous matters, states a cause of action.” (Hahn, supra, at p. 747.)

Loss of Consortium (Fourth Cause of Action)

The Kiewit-Parsons Defendants argue Watkin’s fourth cause of action for loss of consortium fails to plead sufficient facts to state a claim because Watkin lacks standing. The court agrees.

Legal Standard

A cause of action for loss of consortium “has four elements: ‘(1) a valid and lawful marriage between the plaintiff and the person injured at the time of the injury; [¶] (2) a tortious injury to the plaintiff's spouse; [¶] (3) loss of consortium suffered by the plaintiff; and [¶] (4) the loss was proximately caused by the defendant’s act.’” (LeFiell Manufacturing Co. v. Superior Court (2012) 55 Cal.4th 275, 284-285, quoting Hahn v. Mirda (2007) 147 Cal.App.4th 740, 746, fn. 2.) Because “a cause of action for loss of consortium ‘must be narrowly circumscribed,’” courts refuse to recognize standing for cohabiting couples who are unmarried, even when their relationship parallels that of a married one. (Elden v. Sheldon (1988) 46 Cal.3d 267, 277-278, quoting Borer v. American Airlines, Inc. (1977) 19 Cal.3d 441, 444.)

With regards to domestic partnerships, “the Legislature has granted legal recognition comparable to marriage both procedurally and in terms of the substantive rights and obligations granted to and imposed upon the partners” when those partners qualify for and register their partnership pursuant to the Domestic Partner Act. (Koebke v. Bernardo Heights Country Club (2005) 36 Cal.4th 824, 845, citing Fam. Code, § 297.5, subd. (a).) A domestic partnership is established “when both persons file a Declaration of Domestic Partnership with the Secretary of State” and meet the requirements for a domestic partnership. (Fam. Code, § 297, subd. (a).)

Discussion

In previously sustaining the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ demurrer to this cause of action, the court found the FAC failed to establish Watkin had standing because it did not allege Watkin was in a domestic partnership with Alberto Gutierrez that was registered with the California Secretary of State. (4/22/2024 Ruling, p. 4.) The SAC does not cure this deficiency as it also fails to allege Watkin and Alberto Gutierrez’s domestic partnership was registered with the California Secretary of State and instead alleges it was registered with the County of Los Angeles. (SAC, ¶ 64.) Registration of a domestic partnership with the county does not satisfy the requirements of the Domestic Partner Act. (See Velez v. Smith (2006) 142 Cal.App.4th 1154, 1167 [declining to allow same-sex couple to pursue dissolution of their domestic partnership pursuant to the Domestic Partner Act when they registered their partnership with the county and not the state].)

Accordingly, because Watkin lacks standing as Watkin was not in a registered domestic partnership pursuant to the Domestic Partner Act, the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ demurrer to this cause of action is SUSTAINED without leave to amend.

Wrongful Death and Survival (Fifth and Sixth Causes of Action)

The Kiewit-Parsons Defendants contend the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ fifth cause of action for wrongful death and sixth cause of action for survival fail to plead sufficient facts to state a claim because Watkin, Gallegos, and Abel Gutierrez lacks standing. The court agrees.

Legal Standard

Wrongful Death

Code of Civil Procedure section 377.60, subdivision (a) establishes that a wrongful death action may be brought on the behalf of a decedent by the decedent’s personal representative or by the following individuals:

“The decedent’s surviving spouse, domestic partner, children, and issue of deceased children, or, if there is no surviving issue of the decedent, the persons, including the surviving spouse or domestic partner, who would be entitled to the property of the decedent by intestate succession. If the parents of the decedent would be entitled to bring an action under this subdivision, and the parents are deceased, then the legal guardians of the decedent, if any, may bring an action under this subdivision as if they were the decedent’s parents.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 377.60, subd. (a).)

In addition, an action may be brought by an individual on the behalf of the decedent “if they were dependent on the decedent, the putative spouse, children of the putative spouse, stepchildren, parents, or the legal guardians of the decedent if the parents are deceased.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 377.60, subd. (b)(1).) A “‘putative spouse’ means the surviving spouse of a void or voidable marriage who is found by the court to have believed in good faith that the marriage to the decedent was valid.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 377.60, subd. (b)(2).) A “‘domestic partner’ means a person who, at the time of the decedent's death, was the domestic partner of the decedent in a registered domestic partnership established in accordance with subdivision (b) of Section 297 of the Family Code.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 377.60, subd. (f)(1).)

Survival

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 377.30, a survival action may be brought by the decedent’s personal representative or successor in interest. For the purposes of this provision, successor in interest is defined as “the beneficiary of the decedent’s estate or other successor in interest who succeeds to a cause of action or to a particular item of the property that is the subject of a cause of action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 377.11.)

Discussion

Watkin’s Standing

The SAC alleges Watkin is Alberto Gutierrez’s registered domestic partner. (SAC, ¶ 2.) But as noted above, Watkin’s domestic partnership is not recognized by the Domestic Partner Act as Watkin fails to allege the partnership was registered with the California Secretary of State. Thus, the court finds Watkin lacks standing to bring a wrongful death and survival action.

Gallegos’ Standing

The SAC alleges Gallegos is one of Alberto Gutierrez’s surviving children. (SAC, ¶ 3.) The SAC also alleges Gallegos was Alberto Gutierrez’s stepchild and that Gallegos was dependent on Alberto Gutierrez. (SAC, ¶ 3-4.) Pursuant to Probate Code section 6454, a stepchild can be a beneficiary of the foster parent or stepparent if the following requirements are met:

“(a) The relationship began during the person’s minority and continued throughout the joint lifetimes of the person and the person’s foster parent or stepparent. 

(b) It is established by clear and convincing evidence that the foster parent or stepparent would have adopted the person but for a legal barrier.” (Prob. Code, § 6454.) 

In previously sustaining the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ demurrer to this cause of action, the court found the FAC failed to meet these requirements as it merely alleges Gallegos is the stepchild of Alberto Gutierrez without establishing if Gallegos was legally adopted, if Gallegos was legally dependent on Alberto Gutierrez, and if Gallegos’ relationship with Alberto Gutierrez met the requirements of Probate Code section 6454. (4/22/2024 Ruling, p. 5.)

Here, while the SAC adds the conclusory allegation that Gallegos was “dependent” on Alberto Gutierrez, it fails to allege compliance with the requirements of Probate Code section 6454, even while doing so for the other children. (SAC, ¶ 3, 4, 71, 79.) While the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ opposition includes additional facts about Alberto Gutierrez and Gallegos’ relationship, the Gutierrez Plaintiffs failed to properly include them in the SAC. (Opp., p. 7:28-8:13.) Thus, the SAC fails to establish Gallegos has standing.

Abel Gutierrez’s Standing

The SAC alleges Abel Gutierrez is one of Alberto Gutierrez’s surviving children. (SAC, ¶ 3.) But it is unclear if Abel Gutierrez is Alberto Gutierrez’s biological child or stepchild. While the use of “child” without any qualification would generally suggest a biological or adopted child, the SAC uses the same term to describe Alberto Gutierrez’s stepchildren, including Gallegos and Amaris Gutierrez. (SAC, ¶ 3-4.) Thus, the court finds the SAC needs to specify if Abel Gutierrez is Alberto Gutierrez’s biological child or if Abel Gutierrez is a stepchild who meets the requirements in Probate Code section 6454.

Thus, the SAC fails to establish Abel Gutierrez has standing.

Conclusion

Accordingly, the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ demurrer to these causes of action is SUSTAINED without leave to amend as to Watkin and with leave to amend as to Gallegos and Abel Gutierrez.

CONCLUSION

Based on the foregoing, LACMTA and Foothill Gold Line’s demurrer and motion to strike are CONTINUED to a date to be determined at the hearing in Department G (Pomona). LACMTA and Foothill Gold Line’s counsel is also ordered to meet and confer with the Gutierrez Plaintiffs’ counsel regarding the Demurrer and Motion to Strike and to file a supplemental declaration describing such meet and confer efforts at least nine (9) court days before the next scheduled hearing.

Furthermore, the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ demurrer to the SAC is SUSTAINED without leave to amend as to Watkin’s causes of action and with twenty (20) days leave to amend as to Gallegos and Abel Gutierrez’s fifth and sixth causes of action.

Based on the analysis above, the Kiewit-Parsons Defendants’ motion to strike punitive damages is deemed MOOT.
