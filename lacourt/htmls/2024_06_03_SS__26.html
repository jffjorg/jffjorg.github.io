The heading on your e-mail should contain the case name, number, hearing date, and that you submit. The message should indicate your name, contact information, and the party you represent. Please note, the above e-mail address is to inform the court of your submission on the tentative ruling. All other inquiries will not receive a response.

If there are no appearances by either side and no submission on the Court's tentative ruling, the matter will be placed OFF CALENDAR.

Case Number: 21STLC02170    Hearing Date: June 3, 2024    Dept: 26

 

Davidson v. Rite Aid Corp., et al.

MOTION TO AMEND JUDGMENT

(CCP § 187)


TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Judgment Creditor Lisa Davidson’s Motion to Amend Judgment is CONTINUED TO AUGUST 5, 2024 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY JULY 8, 2024, JUDGMENT CREDITOR IS TO FILE A PROOF OF SERVICE DEMONSTRATING SERVICE OF ALL THE MOTION PAPERS ON JUDGMENT DEBTOR. FAILURE TO DO SO MAY RESULT IN THE MOTION BEING DENIED.

 

 

ANALYSIS:

 

On March 15, 2021, Plaintiff Lisa Davidson (“Judgment Creditor”) filed the instant action against Defendant Rite Aid Corporation (“Judgment Debtor”). Default judgment was entered against Judgment Debtor on March 7, 2022.

 

On April 8, 2024, Judgment Creditor filed the instant Motion to Amend Judgment. No opposition has been filed to date.

 

Discussion

 

As an initial matter, the Motion to Amend Judgment is not accompanied by a proof of service demonstrating service of the Motion papers or notice of the hearing date on Judgment Debtor. Failure to give notice of a motion is not only a violation of the statutory requirements but of due process. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005; Jones v. Otero (1984) 156 Cal.App.3d 754, 757.)

 

Regarding the merits, Judgment Creditor moves pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 187 to add “Thrift Payless, Inc.” as an additional judgment debtor.

 

“Under section 187, the trial court is authorized to amend a judgment to add additional judgment debtors.... As a general rule, ‘a court may amend its judgment at any time so that the judgment will properly designate the real defendants.’.... Judgments may be amended to add additional judgment debtors on the ground that a person or entity is the alter ego of the original judgment debtor.... ‘Amendment of a judgmentrb to add an alter ego “is an equitable procedure based on the theory that the court is not amending the judgment to add a new defendant but is merely inserting the correct name of the real defendant...

 

(Greenspan v. LADT, LLC (2010) 191 Cal.App.4th 486, 508 [citing Hall, Goodhue, Haisley & Barker, Inc. v. Marconi Conf. Center Bd. (1996) 41 Cal.App.4th 1551, 1554-1555].) Alter ego liability exists when there is such “unity of interest” and ownership that the individuality or separateness of the corporation has ceased to exist (or never existed in the first place). (Mesler v. Bragg Management Co. (1985) 39 Cal.3d 290, 300.) Also, “the facts of the case must be such that adherence to the “fiction” of the corporation's “separate existence” would sanction a fraud or promote injustice.” (Ibid.)

 

Judgment Creditor moves on the grounds that Thrifty Payless, Inc. is Judgment Debtor’s business license name for the location where the incident occurred. The Motion cites the supporting declaration of Judgment Creditor’s attorney, Perrin Davidson and attached exhibits. (Motion, p. 3:9-15.) The exhibits demonstrate that the Sherrif’s Department would not serve the writ of execution to enforce the judgment because the business license name and judgment debtor name did not match. (Motion, Perrin Decl., ¶¶3-4 and Exhs. B-C.) Judgment Creditor verified that the business license name at the subject location is “Thrifty Payless, Inc.” (Id. at Exh. D.)

 

However, the hearing must be continued due to lack of service of the moving papers and notice of hearing on Judgment Debtor.

Case Number: 22STLC05983    Hearing Date: June 3, 2024    Dept: 26

  

Banda, Jr. v. Vernis, et al.

MOTION FOR RECONSIDERATION

(CCP § 1008(b))

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Defendant Lloyd Vernis’ Motion for Reconsideration of the ruling denying the Renewed Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment is GRANTED. UPON RECONSIDERATION, HOWEVER, THE RENEWED MOTION TO VACATE DEFAULT AND DEFAULT JUDGMENT PURSUANT TO CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE SECTION 473.5 IS DENIED.



 

                                                                                                                               

ANALYSIS:

 

On September 14, 2022, Plaintiff Antonio E. Banda, Jr. (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against Defendant Lloyd Vernis (“Defendant”). Following Defendant’s failure to file a responsive pleading, the Court entered default on November 1, 2022 and default judgment on January 4, 2023.

 

Defendant filed the first Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment on January 12, 2024, which the Court denied on February 8, 2024. (Minute Order, 02/08/24.) Defendant then filed a Renewed Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment (“the Renewed Motion”) on February 21, 2024. The Court denied the Renewed Motion on March 25, 2024. (Minute Order, 03/25/24.)

 

Defendant now brings the instant Motion for Reconsideration, filed on April 3, 2024. Plaintiff filed an opposition on May 21, 2024.

 

Discussion

 

Defendant moves for reconsideration of the Court’s ruling on the Renewed Motion pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 1008. When a motion has been denied in whole or in part, the moving party may apply again for the same relief at a later time only upon “new or different facts, circumstances or law.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1008, subd. (b); see Graham v. Hansen (1982) 128 Cal.App.3d 965, 969-970.)

 

The Court denied the first Motion to Vacate on the grounds that it was brought under Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b) but was untimely. (Minute Order, 02/08/24.) Defendant argues that the Court did not realize the Renewed Motion to Vacate was brought on different statutory grounds from the first Motion to Vacate. The Court finds there was confusion regarding whether the first Motion to Vacate was denied without prejudice to Defendant bringing a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 473.5, which is grounds for reconsideration. The Court, therefore, will reconsider its ruling on the Renewed Motion to Vacate by addressing the merits.

 

The Renewed Motion to Vacate is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 473.5, which states:

 

When service of a summons has not resulted in actual notice to a party in time to defend the action and a default or default judgment has been entered against him or her in the action, he or she may serve and file a notice of motion to set aside the default or default judgment and for leave to defend the action.  The notice of motion shall be served and filed within a reasonable time, but in no event exceeding the earlier of: (i) two years after entry of a default judgment against him or her; or (ii) 180 days after service on him or her of a written notice that the default or default judgment has been entered.

 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 473.5, subd. (a).) Additionally, the motion “shall be accompanied by an affidavit showing under oath that the party’s lack of actual notice in time to defend the action was not caused by his or her avoidance of service or inexcusable neglect. Finally, the party shall serve and file with the notice a copy of the answer, motion, or other pleading proposed to be filed in the action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 473.5, subd. (b).)

 

It is difficult to find that the Motion was brought within a reasonable time. Defendant declares he only learned of this action upon his appearance at the judgment debtor examination on October 18, 2023. (Motion, Vernis Decl., ¶5.) Service of the judgment debtor examiantion, however, was made to the same address as the Summons and Complaint, request for entry of default, and request for default judgment. (Proof of Personal Service, filed 10/24/22, ¶4; Request for Entry of Default, filed 10/24/22, ¶6; Request for Default Judgment, filed 12/02/22, ¶6; Proof of Service, filed 09/19/23, ¶3.) Without an explanation for this discrepancy in notice, it strains credulity to find that Judgment Debtor was unaware of any of the papers served prior to the Application and Order for Appearance and Examination.

 

Furthermore, the Motion is not supported by an adequate declaration. Defendant does not show under oath that his lack of actual notice was not caused by avoidance of service or inexcusable neglect. (Motion, Vernis Decl., ¶5.) Simply making an “adament” statement that Defendant was not served is insufficient under the express requirements of the statute. Therefore, Defendant has not demonstrated that he is entitled to relief under Code of Civil Procedure section 473.5.

 

Conclusion

 

Defendant Lloyd Vernis’ Motion for Reconsideration of the ruling denying the Renewed Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment is GRANTED. UPON RECONSIDERATION, THE RENEWED MOTION TO VACATE DEFAULT AND DEFAULT JUDGMENT IS DENIED UNDER CODE OF CIVIL PROCEDURE SECTION 473.5.

Case Number: 23STLC02299    Hearing Date: June 3, 2024    Dept: 26

 

Consolidated Electrical Distributors, Inc. v. Harness Power, Inc., et al.

MOTION TO DEEM REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION ADMITTED;

REQUEST FOR SANCTIONS

(CCP § 2033.280)


TENTATIVE RULING:  

           

Defendants/Cross-Complainants Valerie J. Berry and Billie Grace Berry’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted and Request for Sanctions is GRANTED. DEFENDANT/CROSS-DEFENDANT ERIC LARS LARSEN IS ORDERED TO PAY SANCTIONS OF $425.80 TO MOVING PARTY’S COUNSEL WITHIN 20 DAYS’ SERVICE OF THIS ORDER.

 

ANALYSIS:

 

On February 21, 2024, Defendants/Cross-Complainants Valerie J. Berry and Billie Grace Berry (erroneously sued as William R. Berry) (“the Berrys”) served Requests for Admissions on Defendant/Cross-Defendant Erik Lars Larsen (“Larsen”). (Motion, Marcum Decl., Exh. A.) To date, Larsen has not served responses to the Requests for Admission. (Id. at ¶6.) The Berrys filed the instant Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted (“the Motion”) on April 5, 2024. No opposition has been filed to date.

 

There is no requirement for a prior meet and confer effort before a motion to deem requests for admission can be filed. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.) Further, the motion can be brought at any time after the responding party fails to provide the responses. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.) Due to Larsen’s failure to timely serve verified responses and resultant waiver of objections, the Berrys are entitled to an order deeming the Requests for Admission admitted. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.)

 

The Court is required to award sanctions under the moving statute. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).) While properly noticed, the sanctions sought are excessive. Under a lodestar calculation, sanctions are granted against Larsen in the amount of $425.80 based on one hour of attorney time billed at $345.00 per hour, plus costs of $80.80. (Motion, Marcum Decl., ¶8.)

 

Conclusion

 

Defendants/Cross-Complainants Valerie J. Berry and Billie Grace Berry’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted and Request for Sanctions is GRANTED. DEFENDANT/CROSS-DEFENDANT ERIC LARS LARSEN IS ORDERED TO PAY SANCTIONS OF $425.80 TO MOVING PARTY’S COUNSEL WITHIN 20 DAYS’ SERVICE OF THIS ORDER.

 

 

Moving party to give notice.
