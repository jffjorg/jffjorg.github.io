MOVING PARTY:                Defendant Nicholas Fuentes

 

RESPONDING PARTIES:    Plaintiffs Reza Safaie and Aaron Trucking, Inc

(1)   Motion to Set Aside Default and/or Default Judgment

MOVING PARTY:                Defendant Old Earl Scheib Paint and Body

 

RESPONDING PARTIES:     Plaintiffs Reza Safaie and Aaron Trucking, Inc.

(2)   Motion to Set Aside Default and/or Default Judgment

The court considered the moving and non-opposition papers filed in connection with each motion.  No reply papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiffs Reza Safaie and Aaron Trucking, Inc. (“Plaintiffs”) filed this action against defendants Nicholas Fuentes (“Fuentes”) and Old Earl Scheib Paint and Body (“Old Earl”) (collectively, “Defendants”) on September 11, 2023.

On November 7, 2023, the clerk entered the defaults of Defendants.

Now pending before court are two motions filed by Defendants to set aside the defaults entered against them.  First, on January 8, 2024, defendant Fuentes filed his motion to set aside the default entered against him on November 7, 2023.  Second, on February 2, 2024, defendant Old Earl filed its motion to set aside the default entered against it on November 7, 2023.  In the interest of efficiency, the court discusses the two pending motions together.

On February 6, 2024, Plaintiffs filed a “Notice of Non-Opposition to Motion to Set Aside Entry of Default, and Plaintiffs’ Request for Court to Set Aside Entry of Default as to Both Defendants [and] for Plaintiffs to File First Amended Complaint.”  In that notice, Plaintiffs state that they “do not oppose the Motion, and request that the Court set aside the entry of default[s]” entered against Defendants on November 7, 2023.  (Non-Opp., p. 2:12-14.)  Plaintiffs further request that the court grant them leave to file their First Amended Complaint (1) to cure certain defects with the pleading, and (2) to add additional allegations and causes of action against Defendants.

Because Plaintiffs have expressly agreed to set aside the defaults entered against Defendants, the court grants Defendants’ motions to set aside their defaults.

The court also finds that it is in furtherance of justice to permit Plaintiffs to amend their Complaint and therefore grants Plaintiffs’ request for leave to file a First Amended Complaint.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (a)(1).)

ORDER

            The court grants defendant Nicholas Fuentes’s motion to set aside default and/or default judgment.

            The court orders that the default of defendant Nicholas Fuentes entered on November 7, 2023 is set aside.

            The court grants defendant Old Earl Scheib Paint and Body’s motion to set aside default and/or default judgment.

            The court orders that the default of defendant Old Earl Scheib Paint and Body entered on November 7, 2023 is set aside.

            The court grants plaintiffs Reza Safaie and Aaron Trucking, Inc. 15 days leave to file a First Amended Complaint.

            The court orders defendants Nicholas Fuentes and Old Earl Scheib Paint and Body to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

 

DATED:  February 21, 2024

MOVING PARTY:                 Plaintiff Emilio Reyes           

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Defendant Lorraine Ann Escobar

Motion to Enforce Order of December 13, 2022 and/or for an Order to Show Cause re: Contempt

The court considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with this motion.

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE

The court grants plaintiff Emilio Reyes’s requests for judicial notice.  (Evid. Code, § 452, subd. (d).)

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff Emilio Reyes (“Plaintiff”) moves the court for an order (1) enforcing the court’s December 13, 2022 order, or, in the alternative, setting an Order to Show Cause re: why defendant Lorraine Ann Escobar (“Defendant”) should not be held in contempt pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 128, 1209, and 1212, and (2) imposing monetary sanctions against Defendant in the amount of $1,500, payable to the court, pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 177.5.

“Every court shall have the power to do all of the following: [¶¶] (4) To compel obedience to its judgments, orders, and process, and to the orders of a judge out of court, in an action or proceeding pending therein.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 128, subd. (a)(4).)  The court also has the power “to impose reasonable monetary sanctions, not to exceed fifteen hundred dollars ($1,500), notwithstanding any other provision of law, payable to the court, for any violation of a lawful court order by a person, done without good cause or substantial justification.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 177.5.)

On June 4, 2021, the court issued an order granting Defendant’s special motion to strike each of the six causes of action alleged in Plaintiff’s First Amended Complaint.  (June 4, 2021 Order, p. 2.)  Thereafter, on August 12, 2022, the Court of Appeal (1) reversed the court’s order granting Defendant’s special motion to strike, and (2) ordered that Plaintiff is entitled to recover costs incurred in connection with the appeal of Defendant’s motion.  (Aug. 12, 2022 Order, pp. 2, 33.)  The Court of Appeal issued its remittitur on October 21, 2022.  Within 40 days after issuance of the remittitur, on October 24, 2022, Plaintiff filed and served his Memorandum of Costs on Appeal, claiming $582.59 in costs from Defendant.  (APP-013 filed Oct. 24, 2022, p. 1; Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 8.278, subd. (c)(1).)  Defendant did not file a motion to tax or strike costs within 15 days after service of the cost memorandum.  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rules 8.278, subd. (c)(2), 3.1700, subd. (b)(1).)  On December 13, 2022, the court issued a minute order (1) stating that the parties conferred regarding the Court of Appeal’s remittitur, (2) stating that the court informed Plaintiff to file his memorandum of costs, (3) stating that Plaintiff informed the court that he filed his memorandum of costs on October 24, 2022, and (4) awarding, upon review of the document, “costs to Plaintiff Emilio Reyes against Defendant Lorraine Ann Escobar in the amount of $582.59.”  (Dec. 13, 2022 Minute Order, p. 2.)

            First, the court notes that it appears that Plaintiff has a money judgment against Defendant in the amount of $582.29.  As set forth above, the Court of Appeal awarded costs to Plaintiff as to his appeal of Defendant’s special motion to strike, and Plaintiff thereafter timely filed with the court and served on Defendant his cost memorandum.  Thus, it appears that the December 13, 2022 order awarding costs to Plaintiff in the amount of $582.29 constitutes a money judgment in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant.[1]  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 8.278, subd. (c)(3) [“An award of costs is enforceable as a money judgment”]; Rostack Investments, Inc. v. Sabella (2019) 32 Cal.App.5th 70, 78 [the award of costs on appeal “represents an independent judgment, enforceable by any available means”] [internal quotations omitted] [emphasis in original].) Plaintiff’s remedy for nonpayment of those costs, therefore, is to enforce that money judgment pursuant to the Enforcement of Judgments Law (Code Civ. Proc., § 680.010 et seq.).  The court thus denies Plaintiff’s request that the court enforce the December 13, 2022 order pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 128, 1209, and 1212. 

            Second, the court denies Plaintiff’s alternative request that the court set an Order to Show Cause against Defendant for contempt because (1) contempt proceedings are considered quasi-criminal due to the potential punishments, including imprisonment, but (2) the court may not order the imprisonment of a person in a civil action for debt.  (In re M.R. (2013) 220 Cal.App.4th 49, 57 [contempt proceedings are considered quasi-criminal]; Code Civ. Proc., §§ 1218, subd. (a) [court may fine or imprison person guilty of contempt], 501; Cal. Const., art. I, § 10 [“A person may not be imprisoned in a civil action for debt or tort”].)

            Third, the court denies Plaintiff’s request to impose sanctions in favor of the court and against Defendant in the amount of $1,500.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 177.5.)

ORDER

            The court denies plaintiff Emilio Reyes’s motion to enforce the order of December 13, 2022 and/or for an order to show cause re: contempt against defendant Lorraine Ann Escobar.

            Pursuant to (1) the Court of Appeal’s August 12, 2022 Opinion and October 21, 2022 remittitur, and (2) the court’s December 13, 2022 minute order, the court orders that defendant Lorraine Ann Escobar shall pay costs to plaintiff Emilio Reyes as to Reyes’s appeal of Escobar’s special motion to strike in the total amount of $582.59.

The court orders plaintiff Emilio Reyes to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
