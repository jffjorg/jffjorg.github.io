DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22VECV02019    Hearing Date: January 22, 2024    Dept: 107

 

 

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

 

PATRICIA ANN MENDEZ,

 

Plaintiff.

 

      

   v.

DONG HAI LE, ET AL.,

Defendants.

 

 

   Case Number             Department

22VECV02019             107

 

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: MOTION TO HAVE REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION DEEMED ADMITTED OR IN THE ALTERNATIVE COMPELLING RESPONSES TO THE REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION, SET ONE

 

 

 

                              

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

BACKGROUND

 

This is an automobile collision case. On November 15, 2022, Plaintiff Patricia Ann Mendez (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Dong Hai Le (“Le”), Home Depot U.S.A., Inc. (“Home Depot”), and Does 1 to 50, inclusive, asserting one cause of action for negligence.

 

The Complaint alleges the following. On or about November 24, 2020, Plaintiff was driving a motor vehicle on the I-405 Freeway near Seal Beach, California, when Defendant Le, driving in an unsafe manner and at high speed, collided with Plaintiff’s vehicle, causing Plaintiff to sustain injuries. At the date and time of the incident, Le was driving a vehicle owned, operated, and maintained by Home Depot as its agent and/or employee.

 

On November 29, 2023, Home Depot filed the instant motion, seeking an order “deeming [its] Requests for Admission, Set One admitted, or in the alternative, ordering Plaintiff to respond to the Requests for Admission, Set One, without objections, and for monetary sanctions against Plaintiff in the amount of $1,000.00.” (Notice of Motion, p. 3:20-23.)

 

As of January 17, 2024, no opposition to the motion has been filed.

 

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS Defendant Home Depot U.S.A., Inc.’s motion. The truth of the matters specified in the defendant’s Request for Admissions, Set One, are deemed admitted. Plaintiff Patricia Ann Mendez is ordered to pay the defendant sanctions of $600.

 

 

DISCUSSION

 

If a party to whom requests for admission are directed fails to serve a timely response, a “party may move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted, as well as for a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with section 2023.010).” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (b).)

 

Home Depot moves for an order deeming the truth of the matters specified in its Request for Admissions, Set One (“RFAs”) admitted.

Defense counsel testifies as follows in support of the motion. On May 30, 2023, the defendant propounded the RFAs on Plaintiff, and responses were due on July 5, 2023. (Motion, Declaration of Sarah G. Denis (“Denis Decl.”), ¶¶ 4-5; Exhibit 2 – a copy of the RFAs.) Plaintiff did not request or receive any extensions to serve the responses. (Denis Decl., ¶ 6.) On August 30, 2023, counsel sent Plaintiff a meet and confer letter to Plaintiff, requesting the responses. (Denis Decl., ¶ 7.) However, as of the date counsel signed her declaration on November 29, 2023, Home Depot had not received the responses. (Denis Decl., ¶ 8.)

The RFAs at issue asked the defendant to admit the following.

1.     Admit that the Defendant, Dong Hai Le, was not an employee of Home Depot at the time of the INCIDENT (‘INCIDENT’ refers to the facts and circumstances giving rise to YOUR (‘YOU’ or ‘YOUR’ means Responding Party) Complaint).

2.     Admit that Defendant, Dong Hai Le, was not acting in the course and scope of his employment for Home Depot at the time of the INCIDENT.

3.     Admit that YOU do not have any evidence to support YOUR claim that Defendant, Dong Hai Le, was an employee of Home Depot.

“The court shall make [an] order [admitting the truth of any matters specified in the RFAs], unless it finds that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).)

Here, there is no evidence that Plaintiff has served, before the hearing on the instant motion, a proposed response to the RFAs.

Accordingly, the request to deem the truth of the matters specified in the RFAs admitted is granted.

“It is mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 2033.280, subd. (c).)

Here, Home Depot seeks sanctions of $1,000 against Plaintiff. The amount consists of 2 hours defense counsel spent on the moving papers and 3 hours she expected to spend reviewing the opposition, preparing a reply, and attending the hearing, a total of 5 hours at his billing rate of $200 per hour. (Denis Decl., ¶ 9.)

The Court finds the requested sanctions excessive because Plaintiff did not file any opposition, and no reply was filed.

Accordingly, the Court will impose sanctions of only $600 against Plaintiff. That amount represents 3 hours of defense counsel’s time at her billing rate of $200 per hour.

CONCLUSION

 

Defendant Home Depot U.S.A., Inc.’s motion is GRANTED.

 

The truth of the matters specified in the defendant’s Request for Admissions, Set One, are deemed admitted.

 

Plaintiff Patricia Ann Mendez is ordered to pay the defendant sanctions of $600.
