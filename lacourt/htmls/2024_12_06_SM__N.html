DEPARTMENT N LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22SMCV00044    Hearing Date: December 6, 2024    Dept: N

TENTATIVE RULING

Plaintiff Gulf Coast Bank & Trust Company’s Motion for Summary Adjudication is GRANTED in the amount of $662,007.39.

Plaintiff Gulf Coast Bank & Trust Company shall prepare, serve, and submit a proposed judgment as per statute.

Plaintiff Gulf Coast Bank & Trust Company to give notice.

REASONING

Plaintiff Gulf Coast Bank & Trust Company, as successor-in-interest to Bank of America, N.A. (“Plaintiff”) moves for summary adjudication of the first cause of action for breach of written agreement and second cause of action for breach of guaranty on the grounds that Defendant Gailani Dental Group, LLC (“the LLC”) breached the subject agreement, and Dunia Gailani (“Gailani”) failed to meet her obligations pursuant to her personal guaranty. Defendants Gailani Dental Group, LLC and Dunia Gailani (“Defendants”) oppose the motion on the ground that Plaintiff did not provide evidence authenticated with personal knowledge. Defendants also argue that Plaintiff sued the wrong party because the loan was issued to Dunia Gailani, DDS, Inc., the LLC did not sign the loan agreement, and Gailani guaranteed only the obligations of Dunia Gailani, DDS, Inc., not the LLC.

Request for Judicial Notice
Plaintiff requests judicial notice of the State of California Secretary of State Domestic Stock Corporation Certificate of Dissolution filed February 16, 2021. Plaintiff’s request is GRANTED pursuant to Evidence Code section 452, subdivision (c).

Legal Standard
The purpose of a motion for summary judgment or summary adjudication “is to provide courts with a mechanism to cut through the parties’ pleadings in order to determine whether, despite their allegations, trial is in fact necessary to resolve their dispute.” (Aguilar v. Atl. Richfield Co. (2001) 25 Cal.4th 826, 843.) 

“On a motion for summary judgment, the initial burden is always on the moving party to make a prima facie showing that there are no triable issues of material fact.” (Scalf v. D. B. Log Homes, Inc. (2005) 128 Cal.App.4th 1510, 1519.) A defendant moving for summary judgment or summary adjudication “has met his or her burden of showing that a cause of action has no merit if the party has shown that one or more elements of the cause of action . . . cannot be established, or that there is a complete defense to the cause of action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c, subd. (p)(2).) “Once the defendant . . . has met that burden, the burden shifts to the plaintiff . . . to show that a triable issue of one or more material facts exists as to the cause of action or a defense thereto.” (Ibid.) “If the plaintiff cannot do so, summary judgment should be granted.” (Avivi v. Centro Medico Urgente Med. Ctr. (2008) 159 Cal.App.4th 463, 467 (Avivi).)

“Code of Civil Procedure section 437c, subdivision (c), requires the trial judge to grant summary judgment if all the evidence submitted, and ‘all inferences reasonably deducible from the evidence’ and uncontradicted by other inferences or evidence, show that there is no triable issue as to any material fact and that the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.” (Adler v. Manor Healthcare Corp. (1992) 7 Cal.App.4th 1110, 1119.) “[T]he court must consider all of the evidence set forth in the papers (except evidence to which the court has sustained an objection) . . . in the light most favorable to the party opposing summary judgment.” (Avivi, supra, 159 Cal.App.4th at p. 467; see also Code Civ. Proc., § 437c, subd. (c).)

Evidentiary Objections
Defendants objected to Plaintiff’s evidence on the ground that Joel Daste, an officer for Plaintiff, lacked personal knowledge related to this loan because Plaintiff had nothing to do with the loan at the time the relevant events occurred. All of the undisputed material facts provided by Plaintiff to support its motion were based on the declaration of Joel Daste. The Court sustained Defendants’ objections and required Plaintiff to submit a supplemental brief and declaration authenticating the evidence. On November 25, 2024, Plaintiff submitted the declaration of Tamara J. Laughinghouse, Director at Bank of America, and the Court finds that the subject records have now been sufficiently authenticated. Thus, Defendants’ objections are OVERRULED.

Analysis
Plaintiff provides evidence that on February 7, 2018, Defendant Dunia Gailani, DDS, Inc. (“Gailani Inc.) executed a Finance Agreement to Plaintiff’s predecessor-in-interest, pursuant to which Plaintiff’s predecessor-in-interest agreed to make a term loan to Gailani Inc. for business purposes in the amount of $1,070,828. (Pl.’s UMF Nos. 1-3.) Gailani Inc. promised to pay the loan in accordance with a repayment schedule, and Plaintiff’s predecessor-in-interest was advised that Defendant Dunia Gailani had set up a limited liability company for her dental practice for use of the loan, thereby substituting Gailani Dental Group LLC (“Gailani Dental”) as the borrower. (Pl.’s UMF Nos. 4, 5.) On February 19, 2013, Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest and Gailani Dental executed the repayment schedule for the permanent loan in the principal amount of $1,070,828, and Gailani Dental was to repay the principal amount of $1,070,828.00 plus interest thereon at the rate of 5.40% per annum. (Pl.’s UMF No. 6.) The final terms for the permanent loan were set forth in the repayment schedule, were in supplement to and not in replacement of the initial agreement, and were merged into and became part of the agreement. (No. 7.) Except as modified by the repayment schedule, all other terms and conditions of the agreement remained unchanged and in full force and effect. (Ibid.) Plaintiff also provides evidence that Defendant Dunia Gailani executed a guaranty of Gailani Dental’s indebtedness to Plaintiff, existing at the time of execution of the guaranty and thereafter. (Pl.’s UMF No. 21.)

Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest provided all consideration and performed all of the terms and conditions of the agreement required to be performed by it and in particular by providing to Defendants all money that was the subject of the agreement. (Pl.’s UMF No. 8.) The terms of the loan documents provide that Gailani Dental shall notify of any change in the location of its business location, and it shall not relocate the collateral, as defined in the agreement, without prior permission, it shall comply with financial reporting requirements as requested by Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest, and it shall further maintain all required licenses and not suffer any suspension of its operations by any governmental entity. (Pl.’s UMF No. 9.)

On or about July 1, 2021, Gailani Dental breached the terms of the loan documents by failing to provide the financial documents requested by Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest pursuant to the terms of the loan documents, its failure to respond to Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest’s inquiries, Gailani Dental’s moving of the collateral, and its suspension by the California Secretary of State and Franchise Tax Board. (Pl.’s UMF No. 10.) On August 1, 2021, Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest notified Defendants of the defaults and provided 30 days for Defendants to cure said defaults. (Pl.’s UMF No. 11.) On September 1, 2021, Plaintiff s predecessor-in-interest sent a follow-up demand letter to Defendants, advised them that they had failed to cure their defaults as set forth in the demand letter, and accordingly accelerated the balance due pursuant to the terms of the loan documents and imposed the default rate of interest. (Pl.’s UMF No. l2.) Gailani Dental is suspended by the California Secretary of State and Franchise Tax Board. (Pl.’s UMF No. 13.) Defendants are also in monetary default for failing to repay the loan since October 20, 2021. (Pl.’s UMF No. 14.) On October 24, 2023, Bank of America sold all its rights in connection with the loan at issue in this case to Gulf Coast Bank and Trust Company. (Pl.’s UMF No. 15.) As of July 9, 2024, the amount owed by Defendants to Plaintiff is $662,007.39, consisting of $577,239.74 in principal and $84,767.65 in interest, with further interest accruing at the rate of $86.59 per day. (Pl.’s UMF No. 16.)

This evidence allows Plaintiff to meet its burden of showing that Defendants are liable for breach of the agreement and breach of guaranty. (See Oasis West Realty, LLC v. Goldman (2011) 51 Cal.4th 811, 821 [to establish a cause of action for breach of contract, Plaintiff must be able to establish “(1) the existence of the contract, (2) plaintiff’s performance or excuse for nonperformance, (3) defendant’s breach, and (4) the resulting damages to the plaintiff”]; Gray1 CPB, LLC v. Kolokotronis (2011) 202 Cal.App.4th 480, 486 [the elements of cause of action for breach of guaranty are “1) there is a valid guaranty, 2) the borrower has defaulted, and 3) the guarantor failed to perform under the guaranty”].)

In opposition, Defendants argue that Plaintiff sued the wrong party because the loan was issued to Gailani Inc., Gailani Dental did not sign the loan agreement, and Dunia Gailani guaranteed only the obligations of Gailani Inc., not Gailani Dental. As to whether Plaintiff sued the right entity, the repayment schedule, specifically the final disbursement, modified the borrower to be Gailani Dental, and Gailani Inc. was terminated before that document was signed. (Mot., Khatchadourian Decl. ¶ 6, Ex. 3, at Ex. 2; Req. for Judicial Notice.) It follows that Gailani Dental is then liable as the borrower under the agreement, and Gailani guaranteed the obligations of Gailani Dental. Given that Defendants provide no other evidence or argument to dispute Plaintiff’s evidence, there is no triable issue of material fact as to either claim. Accordingly, Plaintiff Gulf Coast Bank & Trust Company’s Motion for Summary Adjudication is GRANTED in the amount of $662,007.39. Plaintiff Gulf Coast Bank & Trust Company shall prepare, serve, and submit a proposed judgment as per statute.

TENTATIVE RULING

Plaintiff Teresa Zarate Gomez’s Motion to Compel Further Deposition of Defendant American Honda Motor Co., Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable and Production of Documents and Request for Sanctions is DENIED.

Plaintiff Teresa Zarate Gomez to give notice. 

REASONING

Plaintiff Teresa Zarate Gomez (“Plaintiff”) moves the Court for an order (1) compelling the further deposition of Defendant American Honda Motor Co., Inc. (“Defendant”)’s Person Most Knowledgeable (“PMK”) witness and compelling Defendant to produce a PMK witness for all
Matters of Examination requested in Plaintiff’s Notice of PMK Deposition and (2) compelling
Defendant to produce all responsive documents requested in Plaintiff’s Notice of PMK
Deposition within ten (10) calendar days. Plaintiff argues that she was effectively precluded from taking the deposition of Defendant’s PMK because Defendant failed to produce responsive documents. Defendant opposes the motion on the ground that Plaintiff was provided a meaningful opportunity to depose the PMK, the PMK answered all questions that were asked, and Plaintiff sought to depose the PMK on issues unrelated to the issue identified in the Notice of Deposition.

“Any party may obtain discovery . . . by taking in California the oral deposition of any person, including any party to the action. The person deposed may be a natural person, an organization such as a public or private corporation, a partnership, an association, or a governmental agency.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.010.) An oral deposition of a party may be taken after service of a deposition notice. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2025.210, 2025.220.) “If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (a).)

In the complaint, Plaintiff alleges that her 2019 Honda Odyssey suffered from transmission system defects. (Compl. ¶¶ 8, 10.) In the Fourth Amended Notice of Deposition, Plaintiff sought information related to transmission defects, among other documents. (Mot., Enav Decl. ¶ 16, Ex. 2.) In response, Defendant produced 1,517 pages of responsive documents, but Plaintiff now seeks documents relating to a specific recall relating to the fuel pump, which was not identified in the Notice of Deposition. Plaintiff argues that Defendant should have sought a protective order to limit the scope of the deposition notice, but there is no basis to conclude a protective order was necessary as to issues not identified in the deposition notice. Further, because Plaintiff did not meet and confer with Defendant as to the missing documents, instead identifying certain documents in the reply, she failed to allow Defendant to produce any documents that Plaintiff believes exists and were sought in the Notice of Deposition but were not produced. Plaintiff seeks information as to a fuel pump, which has not been placed at issue in the complaint, nor was it identified in the Notice of Deposition. For that reason, Plaintiff Teresa Zarate Gomez’s Motion to Compel Further Deposition of Defendant American Honda Motor Co., Inc.’s Person Most Knowledgeable and Production of Documents and Request for Sanctions is DENIED.

Case Number: 23SMCV02418    Hearing Date: December 6, 2024    Dept: N

TENTATIVE RULING

Defendant Victor Lossi’s Motion to Quash Subpoena/Subpoena Duces Tecum of Google Documents is DENIED.

Plaintiff Wilshire Quinn Income Fund Reit, LLC’s Request for Monetary Sanctions is GRANTED in the reduced amount of $600, payable by Defendant Victor Lossi and defense counsel to Plaintiff Wilshire Quinn Income Fund Reit, LLC and Plaintiff’s counsel within thirty (30) days of entry of this order.

Defendant Victor Lossi to give notice. 

REASONING

Defendant Victor Lossi (“Lossi”) moves the Court for an order quashing a subpoena issued by Plaintiff Wilshire Quinn Income Fund Reit, LLC (“Plaintiff”) to Google seeking documents and records of Defendant’s personal email and account with Google. Defendant states in the motion that he and his counsel had not been served with the subpoena and are only aware of it because of an email from Google referencing the subpoena. Reference to the email makes clear that the subpoena was issued as to a different case, Los Angeles Superior Court Case No. 22STCV41098 (Beck v. Wilshire Quinn Income Fund Reit). (Mot., Lossi Decl. ¶ 6, Ex. 1.) The cases are not related or consolidated. Defendant provides no evidence of a subpoena having been issued to Google as to the present action. The Court will not issue a ruling in this action as to the merits of any subpoena issued as to a different action. For this reason, Defendant Victor Lossi’s Motion to Quash Subpoena/Subpoena Duces Tecum of Google Documents is DENIED.

Code of Civil Procedure section 2023.030, subdivision (a), provides that “[t]he court may impose a monetary sanction ordering that one engaging in the misuse of the discovery process, or any attorney advising that conduct, or both pay the reasonable expenses, including attorney’s fees, incurred by anyone as a result of that conduct . . . unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” The Court finds that monetary sanctions are warranted here given that Defendant and defense counsel should have exercised diligence in filing this motion in the correct action, but the Court reduces the monetary sanctions award to discount any discussion in the opposition as to the merits of the motion, as this was not necessary work performed by counsel. Accordingly, Plaintiff Wilshire Quinn Income Fund Reit, LLC’s Request for Monetary Sanctions is GRANTED in the reduced amount of $600, which represents one half-hour spent drafting an opposition to the motion and one half-hour spent appearing at the hearing on this motion at the rate of $600, payable by Defendant Victor Lossi and defense counsel to Plaintiff Wilshire Quinn Income Fund Reit, LLC and Plaintiff’s counsel within thirty (30) days of entry of this order.
