DEPARTMENT X LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Counsel wishing to submit on a tentative ruling must send an email to alhdeptx@lacourt.org advising the Court and all counsel of their intention to submit.  Unless all parties submit, the matter will still be called for hearing and may be argued by any appearing/non-submitting parties.  If all parties submit on the Court’s tentative ruling, counsel for moving party shall give notice of ruling unless otherwise ordered by the Court.  This may be done by incorporating verbatim the Court’s tentative ruling. 



Case Number: 22AHCV01309    Hearing Date: September 17, 2024    Dept: X

Motion to Compel Deposition of Plaintiff's Employee


Plaintiff moves to compel the deposition of Defendant E&T Foods, Inc.’s employee.  Plaintiff’s motion to compel the deposition of Banuelos is DENIED without prejudice.  Plaintiff’s request for sanctions is DENIED.

 

This premises liability case arises from an alleged slip-and-fall that occurred on August 31, 2021 at Baja Ranch Market.  Plaintiff sought to complete the deposition of Defendant’s employee.  Following meet and confer efforts, it was agreed that the employee’s deposition would occur on June 28, 2024.  (Ramos Decl., Ex. 2.)  However, on June 27, 2024, the day prior to the deposition, then defense counsel’s office advised Plaintiff’s counsel that the deposition could not proceed as defense counsel was being substituted out.  (Ramos Decl., Ex. 3.)

 

On July 9, 2024, Defendant filed a substitution of attorney, replacing his former attorney Robert T. Bergsten with a new attorney, Greg Stone.  (Substitution of Attorney, filed 7/9/24.)  The next day, on July 10, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to compel the deposition.

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2025.450 provides the authority for moving to compel the deposition of a party-deponent: “If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action ... without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination ... the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony[.]”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (a).)  A motion seeking to compel a deposition must also contain either a meet and confer declaration, or a declaration “stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (b).)

 

Here, while Plaintiff may be entitled to depose Defendant’s employee, there is no evidence to support Plaintiff’s attempt to informally resolve the issue with new counsel after the nonappearance and prior to the filing of the motion.  Plaintiff attests that meet and confer attempts were lost as defense counsel failed to produce the employee for deposition.  (Ramos Decl., ¶ 8.)  Yet, nothing is stated concerning any efforts he made with new counsel to depose the witness after his failure to appear at his duly noticed deposition.  The absence of a meet and confer is evidenced by the fact that the motion was filed one day after new counsel substituted in the matter.  (See, Leko v. Cornerstone Building Inspection Service (2001) 86 Cal.App.4th 1112, 1124 [failure to consider rescheduling depo and instead filing motion to compel resulted in sanctions against deposing counsel for filing unnecessary motion].)

 

In the absence of any evidence showing a meet and confer with opposing counsel after the failure of Defendant’s employee to attend his deposition, Plaintiff’s motion is denied as is his request for sanctions.

Moving Party is to give notice.

Case Number: 23AHCP00074    Hearing Date: September 17, 2024    Dept: X

Demurrers and Motion to Strike

 

Real Parties (Defendants: Raising Cane’s Restaurants, L.L.C., Raising Cane’s USA, L.L.C., and Chapman W. Lew) and City Defendants separately demurred to the first cause of action in the Third Amended Complaint (“TAC”).  Defendants move to strike portions of the TAC.

 

The Demurrers are OVERRULED as moot considering the August 23, 2024 Request for Dismissal filed by Plaintiff.  The motion to strike is also denied as moot.

 

This case arises out of Plaintiff Atlantic Square, LLC’s (“Plaintiff”) claim that Raising Cane’s Restaurant, L.L.C.’s (“Raising Cane’s”) operation of the drive-through causes dangerous conditions on the road and blocks access to Plaintiff’s own parking lot.  Plaintiff filed a TAC on November 29, 2023, alleging 5 causes of action for (1) writ of mandate, (2) public nuisance, (3) private nuisance, (4) nuisance per se, (5) violation of California Business and Professions Code section 17200, et seq. (“UCL”), and (6) declaratory relief.

 

On August 23, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Request for Dismissal.  Plaintiff requested the following be dismissed with prejudice:

1) First Cause of Action against all parties.

2) The following City Defendants (but no other parties) from all remaining causes of action:

a. CITY OF MONTEREY PARK;
b. CITY COUNCIL OF CITY OF MONTEREY PARK;
c. PLANNING COMMISSION OF THE CITY OF MONTEREY PARK;
d. RON BOW, an individual, in his capacity as CITY MANAGER OF THE CITY OF MONTEREY PARK;
e. JESSICA SERRANO, an individual, in her capacity as INTERIM DIRECTOR OF COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT OF THE CITY OF MONTEREY PARK, and
f. DOES 1-5

Real Parties and Defendants separately demur to the first cause of action for Writ of Mandate. However, as that cause of action was dismissed on August 23, 2024, the demurrers are overruled as moot.

 

Defendants also moved to strike several portions of the TAC.  Real Parties filed a joinder on Defendants’ motion to strike.  However, as the Defendants who filed the motion to strike have been dismissed from all causes of action as of August 23, 2024, the motion to strike is denied as moot, as is Real Parties’ joinder.  (See, e.g., Barak v. Quisenberry Law Firm (2006) 135 Cal.App.4th 654, 661 [joinder in co-defendant’s special motion to strike was effective because it established requisite factual foundation and requested affirmative relief]; Commonwealth Energy Corp. v. Investor Data Exch., Inc. (2003) 110 Cal.App.4th 26, 31 n.3 [defendant’s joinder in co-defendant’s motion to dismiss was effective because it requested relief for defendant himself].)

 

Real Parties are to answer the TAC within 10 days.
