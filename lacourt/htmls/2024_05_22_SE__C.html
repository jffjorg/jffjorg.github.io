DEPARTMENT C LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22NWCV00320    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: C

PACIFIC SOUTHWEST DISTRICT OF THE LUTHERAN CHURCH-MISSOURI SYNOD v. IRENE JENSEN, ET AL.

CASE NO.:  22NWCV00320

HEARING: 5/22/24 @ 10:30 A.M.

 

#4

TENTATIVE RULING

 

I.             Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod’s motion to compel further responses to Form Interrogatories, Set Two is GRANTED. Peace Lutheran Church is to submit responses in 20 days.

 

II.            Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod’s motion to compel further responses to Special Interrogatories, Set Two is GRANTED. Peace Lutheran Church is to submit responses in 20 days.

 

III.          Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod’s motion to compel further responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set Three is MOOT. 

 

IV.         Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod’s motion to compel further responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set Four is MOOT. 

 

Moving Party to give NOTICE.

 

This is a quiet title action over land occupied by a church’s former congregation.  This hearing is a continuation. At the April 3, 2024, hearing, the Court ordered the parties to further meet and confer and then submit a joint statement of outstanding issues within 20 days. The parties untimely submitted the statement on April 25, 2024.

 

As part of the recent meet-and-confer, the parties have resolved their dispute over form interrogatories numbers 4.1 to 4.2. and 14.1. to 14.2.

 

Form Interrogatories, Set Two

 

Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod moves to compel further responses to Form Interrogatories, Set Two, request numbers 15.1 and 17.1 from defendant Peace Lutheran Church.

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300 allows a party to file a motion compelling further responses to form interrogatories if it finds that the response is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive, or an objection in the response is without merit or too general. 

 

Request number 15.1 asks Defendant to identify each denial of a material allegation and each special or affirmative defense and for each (a) state all facts upon which it bases the denial or special or affirmative defense; (b) state the names, addresses, and telephone numbers and of all persons who have knowledge of those facts; and (c) identify all documents and other tangible things that support its denial or special or affirmative defense; and state the name, address, and telephone number of the person who has each document.

 

Request number 17.1 states, “Is your response to each request for admission served with these interrogatories an unqualified admission? If not, for each response that is not an unqualified admission: (a) state the number of the request; (b) state all facts upon which you base your response; (c ) state the names, addresses, and telephone numbers of all persons who have knowledge of those facts, and (d) identify all documents and other tangible things that support your response and state the name, address, and telephone number of the person who has each document or thing.”

 

In the joint statement in response to the above requests, Peace Lutheran Church states that it stands by its objections. It also states, “Since January 10, 1945, to the Present Date, PLC has been continuously registered as a California corporation, without interruption, and; has never ceased to exist. Since September 12, 1980, to the Present Date, PLC has been the continuous and sole owner of the property located at 4513 Tweedy Blvd., 4523 Tweedy Blvd., the adjoining wall between them, and the property described in the Los Angeles County Assessor’s Office as Parcel Nos. 6217-014-056, 6217-014-057 and 6217-015-058 (“SUBJECT PROPERTY”) (Corporation. THE PACIFIC SOUTHWEST DISTRICT OF THE LUTHERAN CHURCH-MISSOURI SYNOD (“PSWD”) has not now; nor ever; been on title of the SUBJECT PROPERTY. PSWD, by the constitution and bylaws of THE LUTHERAN CHURCH MISSOURI SYNOD, 2017, Membership of a congregation in the Synod gives the Synod no equity in the property of the congregation.”

 

In response to request number 15.1, Peace Lutheran Church states, “The discovery request is objected to as premature because the request is made sooner than the ‘waiting period,’” and “notwithstanding and not waiving these objections, Defendant responds as follows: See Answer to cross complaint filed by this Defendant in this action.” But Peace Lutheran Church does not define “waiting period.” Also, if the question requires reference to some other document, it should be identified, and its contents summarized so that the answer by itself is fully responsive to the interrogatory. (Deyo v. Kilbourne (1978) 84 Cal.App.3d 771, 783-784.) Peace Lutheran Church does not do that. Thus, Peace Lutheran’s Church reference to the answer is improper.

 

Based on the above, the Court grants Plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses to request number 15.1.

 

In response to request number 17.1, Peace Lutheran Church states, “a. Objection: The Discovery request is objected to as premature because the request is made sooner than the ‘waiting period’; b. Objection: The request is otherwise improper; in that it seeks a legal conclusion; c. Objection: The discovery sought is unreasonably cumulative or duplicative, or is obtainable from some other source that is more convenient, less burdensome, or less expensive; d. Objection: Defendant objects to this interrogatory on the ground that it is vague, ambiguous, unintelligible, and overbroad, in that the question lacks specificity; e. Notwithstanding and not waiving these objections, Defendant responds as follows: See response to Request for admissions, served alongside these form interrogatories, set two.”

 

If a timely motion to compel has been filed, the burden is on the responding party to justify any objection or failure fully to answer the interrogatories. (Coy v. Super. Ct. (Wolcher) (1962) 58 Cal.2d 210, 220-221.) Peace Lutheran Church has not fully justified any objection or failure fully to answer the interrogatory, not in its opposition motion or in a separate statement. Thus, Peace Lutheran Church has not met its burden.

 

Based on the above, the Court grants Plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses to request number 17.1.

                                

Special Interrogatories, Set Two

 

Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod moves to compel further responses to Special Interrogatories, Set Two, numbers 5, 6, and 7.

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300 allows a party to file a motion compelling further responses to special interrogatories if it finds that the response is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive, or an objection in the response is without merit or too general. 

 

Special Interrogatory number 5 asks, “IDENTIFY, for each month of each year from 2017 to present, YOUR communing members.” Special Interrogatory number 6 asks, “State, for each month of each year from 2017 to present, the days upon which YOU offered communion.” Special Interrogatory number 7 states, “IDENTIFY, for each month of each year from 2017 to present, the PERSONS who offered communion at the church property at Tweedy Boulevard.”

 

In its joint statement, Peace Lutheran Church states that the term “communion” is still vague. It further states, “PSWD, has now provided three different terms or words which do not define ‘communion’”; “Those terms provided by PSWD are communion, Holy Communion, and Commune”; “Further, since this is apparently a religious definition, this terminology is subjective to each and every individual as a faith issue and how it is practiced.” Peace Lutheran Church also states, “Notwithstanding this statement, after a diligent search, we have been unable to locate any documents that are responsive to this request.”

 

But the Court does not find the term vague. According to the Merriam-Webster dictionary, it is a Christian sacrament in which consecrated bread and wine are consumed as memorials of Christ’s death or as symbols for the realization of a spiritual union between Christ and communicant or as the body and blood of Christ. <https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/communion> (as of May 20, 2024).  The Court also finds Peace Lutheran Church’s assertion that it was unable to locate any documents inadequate, given that Peace Lutheran Church contends that “communion” is vague.

 

Based on the above, the Court grants the motion to compel further responses to Special Interrogatories, Set Two, numbers 5, 6, and 7.

 

Requests for Production of Documents, Set Three and Four

 

Plaintiff Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod moves to compel further responses to Requests for Production of Documents, Set Three and Four pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.310, numbers 84 to 127 and 131 to 166.     

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.310 allows a party to file a motion compelling further responses to document requests if it finds that the response is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive, or an objection in the response is without merit or too general. 

 

According to the joint statement, the parties disagree over the interpretation of Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.280, subdivision (a), which states, “Any documents or category of documents produced in response to a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling shall be identified with the specific request number to which the documents respond.” Despite the disagreement, Peace Lutheran Church states that it will correlate bates stamp into categories as requested by Plaintiff by June 16, 2024.

 

Thus, the motion to compel further responses to Requests for Production of Documents, Set Three and Four, numbers 84 to 127 and 131 to 166 is MOOT.

 

Sanctions

 

In a motion to compel further discovery responses, the court shall impose a monetary sanction against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel further response to a demand, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300, subd. (d).)

 

In its discretion, the Court will not award any monetary sanctions.

Case Number: 22NWCV01386    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: C

FIRE INSURANCE EXCHANGE, ET AL. v. ERNESTO MARTINEZ

CASE NO.: 22NWCV01386

HEARING:¿ 5/22/24 @ 10:30 A.M. 

 

#5

TENTATIVE ORDER

 

Defendant’s motion for leave to file cross-complaint is GRANTED. Defendant is to file the cross-complaint within 10 days.  

 

Moving party to give notice.

 

 

Plaintiffs are both reciprocal insurance exchanges and seek reimbursement of settlement payments they made in a lawsuit filed against their insured, defendant Ernesto Gabriel Martinez, Jr.

 

Defendant moves for leave to file a cross-complaint.

 

Legal Standard

 

A party against whom a cause of action has been asserted in a complaint can file a cross-complaint against a codefendant or third person not yet a party to the action only if the cause of action asserted “(1) arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences [set forth in the complaint] … or (2) asserts a claim, right, or interest in the property or controversy which is the subject of the cause [of action] brought against him.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 428.10, subd. (b).) Leave may be granted in the interest of justice at any time during the action. (Code Civ. Proc., § 428.50.)

 

Leave to File Cross-Complaint

 

Here, Defendant asserts breach of contract and breach of implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing in the proposed cross-complaint arising out of the underlying action. (Decl. Yuen, ¶ 4, Ex. A.) Plaintiffs argue that Defendant cannot maintain an action for breach of contract and breach of implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing.

 

Because the causes of action asserted arise out of the same occurrence as the original action, the Court grants leave to file the cross-complaint. Plaintiffs’ argument is based on an incorrect standard to apply in a motion for leave to file cross-complaint.

 

The motion is GRANTED. Defendant is to file the cross-complaint within 10 days.

Case Number: 22NWCV01760    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: C

HAYNIE, ET AL. v. AMERICAN HONDA MOTOR COMPANY, INC.

CASE NO.:  22NWCV01760

HEARING: 5/22/24 @ 10:30 A.M.

 

#7

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiffs Savannah Haynie and Porfirio Hernandez’s motion to compel further responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set One is CONTINUED.

 

Moving Party to give NOTICE.

 

 

This is a Song-Beverly action. Plaintiffs Savannah Haynie and Porfirio Hernandez sue American Honda Motor Company, Inc. for breach of express warranty and breach of implied warranty.

 

Plaintiffs move to compel further responses to request numbers 18-20, 22-23, 45-46, and 48-49 served on defendant American Honda Motor Company, Inc. Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.310 allows a party to file a motion compelling further responses to document requests if the party finds that the response is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive, or an objection is without merit or too general. 

 

Timeliness

 

Notice of the motion to compel further must be provided within 45 days of service of the verified response or on or before any specific later date to which the parties have agreed. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.310, subd. (c).) This 45-day deadline runs from the date the verified response is served and is extended for service by mail in accordance with Code of Civil Procedure sections 1010.6, subdivision (a)(4) and 1013, subdivision (a). Failure to timely move to compel within the specified period constitutes a waiver of any right to compel a further response. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.310, subd. (c).)

 

On April 11, 2023, the defendant served unverified responses to Plaintiff’s first set of requests for production of documents. (Decl. Tran, ¶ 6, Ex. B.) Responses consisted of both factual responses and objections. (Decl. Tran, ¶ 6, Ex. B.) The 45-day clock runs only upon service of verified responses, and responses consisting of both factual responses and objections must be verified. (Golf & Tennis Pro Shop, Inc. v. Super. Ct. (2022) 84 Cal.App.5th 127, 136.) Neither party has submitted evidence that Defendant has served verifications. Thus, the clock has not run.

Meet and Confer

 

The motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.310, subd. (b)(2).) A meet and confer declaration in support of a motion shall state facts showing a reasonable and good faith attempt at an informal resolution of each issue presented by the motion. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2016.040.)

 

For purposes of determining whether a motion to compel discovery was preceded by a reasonable and good faith effort to meet and confer, such an attempt at informal resolution requires something more than bickering with opposing counsel; rather, the law requires that counsel attempt to talk the matter over, compare their views, consult, and deliberate. (Clement v. Alegre (2009) 177 Cal.App.4th 1277, 1294.)

 

Based on Plaintiffs’ counsel’s declaration, the Court finds that the parties did not sufficiently meet and confer. (Decl. Tran, ¶¶ 10-13.) Plaintiff’s counsel sent a detailed meet-and-confer correspondence, but the parties did not talk the matter over, compare their views, consult, and deliberate. (Decl. Tran, ¶ 10, Ex. D; ¶ 11, Ex. E.)

 

The parties are ordered to meet and confer again. If court intervention is still necessary, the parties are ordered to submit a joint statement in 20 days regarding the specific items of discovery still at issue.

 

The motion is CONTINUED to July 17, 2024, at 9:30 A.M. in Dept. SE-C.  


Case Number: 23NWCV02320    Hearing Date: May 22, 2024    Dept: C

PORTILLO, ET AL. v. HOMER P. COSTER, ET AL.  

CASE NO.:  23NWCV02320

HEARING: 5/22/24 @ 9:30 A.M.

 

#3

 

TENTATIVE ORDER

 

     I.        Defendant Homer P. Coster, as Trustee of the Coster Family Trust Dated July 19, 1989’s Motion to Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One as to Plaintiffs Irma Adriana Portillo, Destiny Gonzalez Portillo, and Paulina Gonzalez is GRANTED.

    II.        Defendant Homer P. Coster’s Motion to Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One as to Plaintiffs Irma Adriana Portillo, Destiny Gonzalez Portillo, and Paulina Gonzalez is GRANTED.

  III.        Defendant Brabant Realty and Management’s Motion to Compel Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One as to Plaintiffs Irma Adriana Portillo, Destiny Gonzalez Portillo, and Paulina Gonzalez is GRANTED.  

 IV.        Monetary sanctions are DENIED.   

 

Moving Party to give NOTICE.

 

The motions are unopposed as of May 20, 2024.

 

 

Defendants Homer P. Coster, trustee of the Coster Family Trust dated July 19, 1989; Homer P. Coster; and Brabant Realty and Management allegedly own and operate a single-family residential home located at 6342 Arbutus Avenue, Huntington Park, California 90255. Plaintiffs are tenants in the building. Plaintiffs allege they have experienced various problems with the building, rendering it uninhabitable.

 

Defendants move to compel initial discovery of Special Interrogatories, Set One from Plaintiffs Irma Adriana Portillo, Destiny Gonzalez Portillo, and Paulina Gonzalez.

 

Legal Standard

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.290, subdivision (b) allows the propounding party to file a motion to compel responses to interrogatories if a response has not been received.  If responses are untimely, the responding party waives objections. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.290, subd. (a).) 

 

Discussion

 

Defendants served each plaintiff with special interrogatories on November 7, 2023, by electronic mail. (Decl. Winter, ¶ 3, Ex. A.) Responses were due on or before December 11, 2023. (Id., ¶ 3.) Defendant granted an extension to January 1, 2024, January 15, 2024, January 22, 2024, and January 26, 2024. (Id., ¶¶ 4-7.) Defendant granted a further extension to March 8, 2024. (Id., ¶ 9.) Despite these extensions, Defendant has not received responses. (Id., ¶ 10.)   

 

Accordingly, the motions are GRANTED.  Plaintiffs are ordered to serve responses without objections within 30 days. 

 

Sanctions 

 

Discovery sanctions may not be imposed under Section 2023.030, even together with Section 2023.010, absent another provision of the Discovery Act that authorizes the imposition of sanctions. (City of L. A. v. PricewaterhouseCoopers, LLC (2022) 84 Cal.App.5th 466, 500.) Sanctions with respect to interrogatories are only authorized against a party who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel responses. (Code of Civ. Proc. § 2030.290, subd. (c).)

 

Here, sanctions are denied because the motions are unopposed.
