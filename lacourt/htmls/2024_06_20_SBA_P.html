DEPARTMENT P LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22TRCV01596    Hearing Date: June 20, 2024    Dept: P

Motion for Leave to File a Cross-Complaint

The court considered the moving papers.

RULING

Defendant Inglewood Unified School District’s unopposed Motion for Leave to File a Cross-Complaint is GRANTED.

BACKGROUND

I. PROCEDURAL BACKGROUND

On December 23, 2022, Plaintiff Jane Doe (D.F.) filed the complaint against Defendants Inglewood Unified School District and Does 1 through 10. The complaint alleges six causes of action: (1) sexual harassment, (2) negligent hiring, supervision, and retention, (3) breach of mandatory duty, (4) negligent failure to warn, train, or educate, (4) negligent supervision of a minor, (5) negligent supervision of a minor, and (6) negligence.

On May 29, 2024, Defendant Inglewood Unified School District filed the instant motion. As of June 17, 2024, Plaintiff has not filed an opposition.

Superior Court of California County of Los Angeles Southwest District Torrance Dept. P

JANE DOE (D.F.), Plaintiff, Case No.: 22TRCV01596

vs. [TENTATIVE] RULING

INGLEWOOD UNIFIED

SCHOOL DISTRICT, A PUBLIC

CALIFORNIA CORPORATION,

et al. Defendants.

II. FACTUAL BACKGROUND

Plaintiff filed this action on December 23, 2022, alleging that between approximately 2007 and 2010, while a student at Inglewood High School, her teacher, Garrett Lee, sexually abused her when she was 16 and 17 years old. Plaintiff alleges that in September 2010, in the final semester of her senior year, she gave birth to a child with Garrett Lee. Plaintiff sued the District, but not Garrett Lee. (See Complaint attached as Exhibit A.) The District now wishes to assert equitable indemnification and apportionment claims against Garrett Lee.

III. LEGAL STANDARD

Code of Civil Procedure section 428.10 provides: “A party against whom a cause of action has been asserted in a complaint or cross-complaint may file a cross-complaint setting forth … (b) Any cause of action he has against a person alleged to be liable thereon, whether or not such person is already a party to the action, if the cause of action asserted in his cross-complaint (1) arises out of the same transaction, occurrence, or series of transactions or occurrences as the cause brought against him or (2) asserts a claim, right, or interest in the property or controversy which is the subject of the cause brought against him.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 428.50(a)-(b); see also Newhall Land & Farming Co. v. McCarthy Constr. (2001) 88 Cal.App.4th 769, 773.)

IV. DISCUSSION

A. Positions of the Parties

a. Moving Party’s Arguments

Defendant Inglewood Unified School District (“District”), moves pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 426.50, 428.10, and 428.50 et seq., for leave to file a cross-complaint

against Garrett Lee. The District wishes to assert equitable indemnification and apportionment claims against Garrett Lee. (See Horwich v. Superior Court (1999) 21 Cal.4th 272, 285 [“principles of comparative fault and equitable indemnification support an apportionment of liability among those responsible for the loss”].) Granting leave to file the cross-complaint against Garrett Lee will promote judicial efficiency and expediency by allowing resolution of all related claims in a single judicial proceeding.

b. Responding Party’s Opposition

None

c. Moving Party’s Reply

None

B. Legal Analysis

Here, the proposed cross-complaint arises from the same transaction and occurrence from Plaintiff’s complaint. For instance, Plaintiff alleges she was groomed, sexually assaulted, and sexually molested by Garrett Lee while he was employed by the District. (Complaint at p.1.) Plaintiff alleges some of the sexual abuse of plaintiff occurred using the tasks, premises, or

instrumentalities that the District entrusted to Garrett Lee, including classrooms. (Id. at ¶ 33.) Plaintiff alleges she sustained physical and psychological distress, humiliation, fright, dissociation, anger, depression, anxiety, family turmoil, and loss of faith, a severe shock to her nervous system, physical pain and mental anguish, and emotional and psychological damage as a result of the alleged sexual assault and abuse by Garrett Lee. (Id. at ¶ 49.)

The District contends Garrett Lee is at fault for the same injuries Plaintiff claims in this action and its cross-complaint alleges arises from the same incident and therefore seeks to assert equitable indemnification and apportionment claims against Garrett Lee. The cross-complaint alleges five causes of action against Garrett Lee and Roes 1 through 10. The cross-complaint alleges “[o]n December 23, 2022, Plaintiff JANE DOE (D.F.), (“Plaintiff”), commenced an action for personal injuries as alleged in the Complaint entitled Jane Doe (D.F), and individual vs. Inglewood Unified School District, a public California Corporation; and DOES 1-10, inclusive, in the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles, Case No. 22TRCV01596.

Plaintiff alleges in her Complaint that between approximately 2007 to 2010, Plaintiff was a minor student of INGLEWOOD UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT and she was groomed, sexually assaulted, and sexually molested by Garrett Lee, a teacher of INGLEWOOD UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT.” (Cross-Complaint at ¶ 6.) Thus, the facts arise from the same incident. “[T]he practical advantage of requiring that actions for indemnity be brought by way of cross-complaint is the consolidation of related evidence and matters of proof in a single judicial proceeding.” (Valley Circle Estates v. Vtn Consol. (1983) 33 Cal.3d 604, 614.) “The cross complaint serves the purpose of permitting a complete determination of the dispute among all the parties, thus avoiding circuity of proceedings. Combining these causes in a single trial is an efficient utilization of limited judicial resources.” (Id.) A single trial allows for “more orderly and expeditious resolution of the controversy.” (E. L. White, Inc. v. Huntington Beach (1978) 21 Cal.3d 497, 506.)

As the proposed cross-complaint directly relates to the causes of action against the District, the Court finds good cause to allow Defendant to file its cross-complaint. “[T]he general rule of liberal construction of pleadings and of liberal allowance of amendments should prevail.” (Nestle v. Santa Monica (1972) 6 Cal.3d 920, 939.)

V. CONCLUSION

Thus, DEFENDANT’S UNOPPOSED MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE A CROSS-COMPLAINT is GRANTED.


Case Number: YC072760    Hearing Date: June 20, 2024    Dept: P

Motion for Attorney’s Fees

The court considered the moving papers.

RULING

Plaintiff Barrett Daffin Frappier Treder & Weiss, LLP’s Motion for Attorney’s Fees is GRANTED in the amount of $31,953.40.

BACKGROUND

On March 19, 2018, Plaintiff Barrett Daffin Frappier Treder & Weiss, LLP (“Plaintiff”) filed a Complaint in Interpleader against Defendants Carlos Garau; Olga Garau; Torrance Unified School District; Investment Retrievers, Inc.; and DOES 1 through 20 (“Defendants”). The Complaint asserted a surplus of $150,209.15 from foreclosure sale proceeds concerning real property located at 23414 Walnut Street, Torrance, CA 90501 (“Property”) was available and that the Defendants have some right, title, estate, lien or other interest in the Property.

On August 19, 2021, this Court granted Plaintiff’s Motion for Order Discharging Stakeholder and Awarding Reasonable Attorney’s Fees and Costs in the sum of $71,777.80 from the deposited surplus funds. Defendant Carlos Garau and Olga Garau appealed this order.

On January 30, 2024, the Court of Appeals affirmed the August 19 order. On April 4, 2024, the Court of Appeals issued a Remittur and awarded Plaintiff costs on appeal.

LEGAL AUTHORITY

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 386.6, “A party to an action who follows the procedure set forth in Section 386 or 386.5 may insert in his motion, petition, complaint, or cross complaint a request for allowance of his costs and reasonable attorney fees incurred in such action. In ordering the discharge of such party, the court may, in its discretion, award such party his costs and reasonable attorney fees from the amount in dispute which has been deposited with the court. At the time of final judgment in the action the court may make such further provision for assumption of such costs and attorney fees by one or more of the adverse claimants as may appear proper.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 386.6(a).)

“Section 386.6, subdivision (a), gives the trial court authority to award attorney fees to the [Plaintiff] for fees and costs incurred not only to initiate the Interpleader Case and obtain discharge, but also to defend against subsequent motions, writ petitions, and appeals attacking the validity of the interpleader complaint and discharge order.” (Southern California Gas Co. v. Flannery (2016) 5 Cal.App.5th 476, 486.)

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff moves for an award of reasonable attorney’s fees and costs in the sum of $34,703.40 to be paid from the interpleader funds deposited with the Court following the Court of Appeals’ Opinion affirming this Court’s August 19, 2021 order discharging Plaintiff and awarding reasonable attorney’s fees.

Here, Defendants Olga Garau and Carlos Garau challenged the validity of the August 19, 2021 order by filing an appeal. Subsequently, the Court of Appeals affirmed the August 19 order and awarded Plaintiff costs on appeal. (Appeal Remittur 4/4/24.) Plaintiff advances the declaration of its counsel, Cris A. Klingerman, attesting to the work performed, hours expended, and fees incurred to defend the order of discharge on appeal. (Klingerman Decl., ¶¶6-10.) Plaintiff also filed a Memorandum of Costs on Appeal. (MOC 5/8/24.) Plaintiff has sufficiently established that 62.95 hours were expended on defending the August 19 order on appeal, which was filed on May 20, 2019 and not resolved until April 4, 2024. Furthermore, Plaintiff seeks costs in the amount of $478.40 consisting of $390.00 for filing fees and $88.40 for transmitting, filing, and serving of record, briefs, and other papers, which are allowable pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section

1033.5(a)(1) and California Rules of Court, Rule 8.891(d)(1)(D). However, Plaintiff requests fees in anticipation of responding to opposition and appearance for a total of $2,750.00 consisting of 5.50 hours at $500.00 per hour. Defendants Olga Garau and Carlos Garau did not file an opposition, thus these anticipated fees are deducted from the attorney’s fees requested by Plaintiff.

Therefore, Plaintiff Barrett Daffin Frappier Treder & Weiss, LLP’s Motion for Attorney’s Fees is GRANTED in the amount of $31,953.40.
