DEPARTMENT C LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV00562    Hearing Date: October 3, 2024    Dept: C

Jimenez, et al. vs. Golden West Food Group, Inc., Case No. 21STCV00562

 

This is a wrongful death action based on premises liability theory. Attorneys for Plaintiffs move ex parte to advance the hearing date of Plaintiffs’ counsel’s motion to be relieved as counsel.

 

A party may seek ex parte relief upon an affirmative factual showing "irreparable harm, immediate danger, or any other statutory basis for granting relief ex parte." (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1202(c).)

 

Plaintiffs argue that it is currently set for July 10, 2025, but no motion to be relieved as counsel appears in the court system.

 

Thus, the court denies the application.

 

Moving party to give notice.


Case Number: 21STCV15339    Hearing Date: October 3, 2024    Dept: C

Lee vs. Payne, et al., Case No. 21STCV15339

 

This is a medical malpractice action. Defendant Brian C. Payne, M.D. moves ex parte to shorten time on the hearing on Defendant’s motion for summary judgment, currently scheduled for March 13, 2025. Trial is scheduled for February 6, 2025. Defendant requests a date between December 11, 2024 and January 7, 2025 or in the alternative, within 30 days of trial.

 

A party may seek ex parte relief upon an affirmative factual showing "irreparable harm, immediate danger, or any other statutory basis for granting relief ex parte." (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1202(c).)

 

Based on the above, the motion is GRANTED.  The Court ADVANCES the hearing from Thursday, March 13, 2025 to TODAY and CONTINUES it to Thursday, February 13, 2025 in Dept. SE-C at 10:30 A.M. The parties can move to continue trial with Department F of this court.

 

Moving party to give notice.

 


Case Number: 23NWCV00185    Hearing Date: October 3, 2024    Dept: C

RACK LEE LLC v. UNICORN, INC.

CASE NO.:  23NWCV00185

HEARING:  10/03/24

 

#4

 

Defendant/Cross-Complainant M.G. UNICORN, INC.’s Motion: (1) To Compel Cross-Defendant XIANGMEI LI’s Attendance at Deposition for Answering Questions is DENIED; (2) For a Protective Order Prohibiting Plaintiff/Cross-Defendants’ Counsel from Obstructing Deposition with Speaking Objections and Misconduct is GRANTED in part; (3) For Appointment of a Discovery Referee is DENIED; and (4) For Monetary Sanctions is DENIED.

 

Moving Party to give notice.

 

In an exercise of its discretion, this Court has reviewed and considered the untimely Reply filed on September 30, 2024 (due by September 25, 2024). (CCP §1005(b).)

 

This breach of contract action was filed by Plaintiff RACK LEE LLC (“Plaintiff”) against Defendant M.G. UNICORN, INC. (“Defendant” or “Cross-Complainant” or “M.G. Unicorn”)  on January 18, 2023. On March 29, 2023, the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) was filed. Plaintiff alleges that “[i]n or about March 2019, Plaintiff and Defendants… entered into an oral agreement for the sale and purchase of the Product….” (FAC ¶12.) “Plaintiff performed all, or substantially all, of its obligations under the contract except those things Plaintiff was prevented and/or excused from performing by the actions/inactions of Defendants, and each of them.” (FAC ¶13.) “Defendants and each of them, have breached the Contract by, at a minimum, failing to perform the terms of the contract as understood by the parties….” (FAC ¶14.)

 

The FAC asserts the following causes of action:

 

(1) Breach of Contract;

(2) Common Counts – Goods and Services Rendered; and

(3) Conversion

 

On March 28, 2024, Defendant/Cross-Complainant M.G. Unicorn, Inc. filed the operative First Amended Cross-Complaint (“FAXC”) against Cross-Defendants RACK LEE LLC; XIANGMEI LI; ZHANG CHENG GUANG; SHANDONG SHENGXIANG SMART WAREHOUSE TECHNOLOGY COMPANY LIMITED (collectively “Cross-Defendants”). The FAXC alleges that “[u]ntil April 2022, the business relationship got strained because of Cross-Defendant’s repeated deliveries of wrong sized and poor-quality products and containers Cross-Complainant did not order. Cross-Complainant requested Li to either return or lower the price of those containers she delivered. After multiple times of negotiation through phone, WeChat or in person, Li agreed to lower the price, she also agreed to return some of those wrong sized products and sell those on her own. However, Li never picked up those wrong sized products. At this time , the parties continued to do business together although their relationship was in doubt.” (FAXC ¶11.)

 

The FAXC asserts the following causes of action:

(1) Common [Count] – Goods and Services Rendered; and

(2) Conversion

 

M.G. Unicorn served a Notice of Taking the Deposition of Cross-Defendant Xiangmei Li, the principal of Rack Lee LLC for December 18, 2023. Xiangmei Li appeared on the time and date noticed. However, before the conclusion of the deposition,  Counsel for M.G. Unicorn, the deposing party, Long v. Liu, abruptly ended the deposition stating: “I am suspending this deposition right now, and I am going to file a motion against you and your client.” (Liu Decl., ¶2, Ex. A., p. 47.)

 

M.G. Unicorn now moves: (1) To Compel Cross-Defendant XIANGMEI LI’s Attendance at Deposition for Answering Questions; (2) For a Protective Order Prohibiting Xiangmei Li’s Counsel from Obstructing Deposition with Speaking Objections and Misconduct; (3) For Appointment of a Discovery Referee; and (4) For Monetary Sanctions. M.G. Unicorn argues that this Motion should be granted because Attorney Luan (counsel for Plaintiff and Cross-Defendant Xiangmei Li) engaged in inappropriate conduct which prevented the deposition from moving forward. 

 

In Opposition, Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant Rack Lee, LLC and Cross-Defendant Xiangmei Li argue that the Motion should be denied because “[t]he failure of the deposition on December 18, 2023 is solely and completely due to the deposing attorneys’ unpreparedness in that (1) they did not have a single piece of evidence or exhibits for the deposition; (2) they did not know what question to ask: [3] they did not know how to lay foundation to make their questions understandable to the deponent; and (4) they did not even know how to use ‘Share Screen’ of Zoom to do a deposition, etc.” (Opp. p. 2, ¶4.)

 

          Motion to Compel Attendance/Answers at Deposition

 

CCP §2025.460(e) states, “If a deponent fails to answer any questions or to produce any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing under the deponent’s control that is specified in the deposition notice or a deposition subpoena, the party seeking that answer or production may adjourn the deposition or complete the examination on other matters without waiving the right at a later time to move for an order compelling that answer or production under section 2025.480.” (Id.)

 

Under CCP §2025.480, “(a) If a deponent fails to answer any question or to produce any document… that is specified in the deposition notice or a deposition subpoena, the party seeking discovery may move the court for an order compelling that answer or production. (b) The motion shall be made no later than 60 days after the completion of the record of the deposition, and shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040…. If the court determines that the answer or production sought is subject to discovery, it shall order that the answer be given or the production be made on the resumption of the deposition…. The court shall impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7… against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel an answer or production, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust….” (Id.)

 

The Court has reviewed the Separate Statement and the deposition transcript. The Court finds that at no point during the deposition did Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant Li’s Counsel instruct Cross-Defendant Li not to answer certain questions. Although a number of speaking objections that appeared to coach, and which interrupted the flow of questioning were improper, the transcript does not indicate that Ms. Li failed to answer any questions or produce any documents. Moreover, it is undisputed that Ms. Li appeared for the deposition on the time and date noticed.

 

The Motion to Compel Attendance/Answer Questions at Deposition is DENIED.

 

Motion for a Protective Order

 

CCP §2025.470 provides that: “The deposition officer may not suspend the taking of testimony without the stipulation of all parties present unless any party attending the deposition, including the deponent, demands that the deposition officer suspend taking the testimony to enable that party or deponent to move for a protective order under Section 2025.420 on the ground that the examination is being conducted in bad faith or in a manner that unreasonably annoys, embarrasses, or oppresses that deponent or party.” (CCP §2025.470)

 

The Court finds that the initial session of Xiangmei Li’s deposition was not suspended. The Motion is not supported by any evidence of a stipulation to suspend Ms. Li’s deposition, or that any party demanded such a suspension so that a Motion for Protective Order could be brought. The deposition transcript shows that M.G. Unicorn’s Counsel ended the deposition by stating “I am suspending this deposition right now, and I am going to file a motion against you and your client.” (Liu Decl., ¶2, Ex. A., p. 47.) M.G. Unicorn’s Counsel did not state what Motion would be filed, and at no time was a protective order expressly mentioned.

 

Because Ms. Li’s deposition was completed, the present motion should be one to compel a second deposition. To obtain this relief, the moving party must satisfy the requirements of CCP §2025.610. “[F]or good cause shown, the court may grant leave to take a subsequent deposition….” (CCP §2025.610(b).)

 

The Court has reviewed the transcript of the deposition. Based on the Court’s review of this evidence, the Court finds that unnecessary obstructive conduct prevented the deposition from proceeding as it should. Mr. Luan (counsel for Plaintiff and Cross-Defendants) interrupted the line of questioning and insulted M.G. Unicorn’s counsel numerous times—including calling them “stupid”. Given Mr. Luan’s conduct during the initial deposition of Ms. Li, the Court finds good cause to grant M.G. Unicorn leave to take the subsequent deposition of Ms. Li, and will proceed to rule on the merits as to the request for a protective order under CCP §2025.420.

 

“Before, during, or after a deposition, any party, any deponent, or any other affected natural person or organization may promptly move for a protective order. The motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040.” (CCP §2025.420(a).) “The court, for good cause shown, may make any order that justice requires to protect any party, deponent, or other natural person or organization from unwarranted annoyance, embarrassment, or oppression, or undue burden or expense.” (CCP §2025.420(b).)

 

The Court finds that the parties have satisfied the meet and confer requirement. 

 

The Court also finds that there is good cause to issue some form of a protective order under CCP §2025.420, which vests the Court with discretion to issue any protective order “that justice requires,” to protect a party—in this case, Defendant/Cross-Complainant M.G. Unicorn, Inc. from the unwarranted annoyance and undue burden of having to ask questions over the constant interruptions, unnecessary commentary, and speaking objections of Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant’s Counsel.

 

At a discovery deposition, all objections except as to the form of the question and privilege are preserved, and the testimony is to be taken subject to those questions. A proper objection states the legal ground for the objection. If the objection is proper, it can be sustained, and the answer will not be used. If the objection is overruled, the answer will stand. Instructions not to answer are ordinarily only made for privilege, work product, and some privacy and trade secret objections.

 

Counsel are ORDERED to refrain from “speaking objections.” Objections should state the legal ground for the objection. Instructions not to answer shall only be made for valid legal grounds.

 

The parties are ORDERED to meet and confer to schedule a resumption of the deposition to be taken within 20 days from the date of the Court’s issuance of this Order. The Court notes that trial is currently scheduled to begin on January 3, 2025.

 

          Request for Appointment of Discovery Referee

 

M.G. Unicorn argues that a discovery referee is necessary as to the continued deposition of Xiangmei Li because Plaintiff/Cross-Defendants’ Counsel’s conduct was obstructive and offensive.

 

The Court has power to appoint a referee to supervise the deposition, if necessary, to hear and determine any and all discovery motions and disputes relevant to discovery in the action, and to report findings and make recommendations thereon. (CCP §639(a)(5).) Under CCP §639(d), all appointments of referees pursuant to this section shall be by written order and shall include the following: “When the referee is appointed pursuant to paragraph (a)(5), the exceptional circumstances requiring the reference, which must be specific to the circumstances of the particular case.” “Exceptional circumstances” are required to appoint a referee to hear and determine any and all discovery motions and disputes relevant to discovery in the action and to report findings and make a recommendation thereon. (CCP §639(d)(2).)

 

This request is DENIED. Exceptional circumstances do not exist. The conflict here is between counsel, and the imposition of the protective order above should eliminate the need for the appointment of a discovery referee. Counsel are reminded that name calling is unprofessional. Counsel are reminded of their oath to conduct themselves at all times with dignity, courtesy, and integrity.

 

Monetary Sanctions

 

CCP §2017.020(b) requires the Court to impose a monetary sanction “against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion for a protective order, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” (CCP §2017.020(b).)

 

Given the mixed ruling, both requests for sanctions are DENIED. Both parties acted with substantial justification.

No Reply filed as of October 1, 2024. Due by September 25, 2024. (CCP §1005(b).)

 

Defendant noticed the deposition of Plaintiff for July 12, 2024. Prior to the date of the deposition, Defendant’s counsel was told by a non-attorney at Plaintiff’s office that Plaintiff would not be appearing for the deposition. “Because Plaintiff’s counsel himself did not conform he would not appear or produce Plaintiff, Defendant appeared for the deposition. Plaintiff did not appear.” (Motion 3:23-25.)

 

Defendant now moves to compel the deposition of Plaintiff, and for monetary sanctions.

 

In Opposition, Plaintiff argues that the Motion is procedurally defective and must be denied because Defendant failed to submit a declaration stating that the Defendant contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance after Plaintiff failed to appear as required by CCP §2025.450(b)(2).

 

Defendant’s motion for an order compelling Plaintiff to appear at an oral deposition and for monetary sanctions is CONTINUED as indicated above.  

 

As argued in Opposition, Defendant has not submitted a declaration indicating either that they attempted to meet and confer after Plaintiff failed to appear or that they otherwise contacted Plaintiff/Plaintiff’s Counsel about the nonappearance. (CCP §2025.450(b)(2).) Motions under CCP §2025.450 must “be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040, or when the deponent fails to attend the deposition and produce the documents, electronically stored information, or things described in the deposition notice, by a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance.” (CCP §2025.450(b)(2).)

 

Although defense counsel has submitted a declaration indicating meet and confer efforts prior to the deposition, CCP §2025.450(b)(2) requires evidence of such efforts after the deponent fails to appear at the deposition.

 

In the interests of justice and in favor of adjudicating this matter on the merits, this matter is CONTINUED as indicated above. The Defendant/Moving Party is ORDERED to comply with CCP §2025.450(b)(2) and then FILE and SERVE a Declaration in compliance with the Code by no later than October 8, 2024.

 

The Court notes that trial is scheduled to begin on November 5, 2024, and Plaintiff’s Counsel’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel is scheduled to be heard after trial, on March 5, 2025.

 

 

 

 


Case Number: 23NWCV01018    Hearing Date: October 3, 2024    Dept: C

CARDOZA v. HERNANDEZ

CASE NO.:  23NWCV01018

HEARING:  10/03/24

 

#1

 

     I.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant ALAN GILBERT HERNANDEZ’s Responses and Verifications to Form Interrogatories (set one) is DENIED as MOOT.

    II.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant ALAN GILBERT HERNANDEZ’s Responses and Verifications to Special Interrogatories (set one) is DENIED as MOOT.

  III.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant ALAN GILBERT HERNANDEZ’s Responses and Verifications to Request for Production of Documents (set one) is DENIED as MOOT.

 IV.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem Matters Admitted Against Defendant ALAN GILBERT HERNANDEZ is DENIED as MOOT.

 

 

Moving Party to give Notice.

 

Untimely Opposition filed on October 1, 2024—2 days before the hearing. Due by September 19, 2024. (CCP §1005(b).) In the untimely Opposition, Defendant’s Counsel fails to address or explain why the Opposition could not have been timely filed or served. The Court record does not reflect that Defendant sought any type of continuance or leave from the Court to file untimely Opposition papers. Notwithstanding these deficiencies, and in the absence of prejudice to the Moving Party, the Court exercises its discretion to consider Defendant’s untimely Opposition under CCP §473(b) because defense counsel “lost contact with defendant Alan Hernandez for some time after answer and only recently re-established contact with him.” (Kwan Decl., ¶2.)

 

Motions to Compel

 

If a party to whom interrogatories and document demands are directed fails to respond at all, the propounding party’s remedy is to seek a court order compelling answers thereto. (CCP §§ 2030.290, 2031.300.) All that needs to be shown is that the discovery was properly served on the opposing party, that the time to respond has expired, and that no response of any kind has been served. The moving party is not required to show a reasonable and good faith attempt to resolve the matter informally before filing this motion. A motion to compel initial discovery responses need not show good cause, meeting and conferring, or timely filing, and need not be accompanied by a separate statement. (See Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pac. Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 404.) The failure to timely respond also waives all objections.

 

Here, Plaintiff has shown that Special Interrogatories (set one), Form Interrogatories (set one) and Request for Production of Documents (set one) were properly served onto Defendant ALAN GILBERT PEREZ on September 15, 2023.

 

In the untimely Opposition filed two days before this hearing (on October 1, 2024), Defendant’s Counsel states: “My office has worked with Mr. Hernandez to prepare responses to all of the written discovery at issue, and then served the verified responses on the same day this limited opposition is being filed, on October 1, 2024.” (Kwan Decl., ¶4.)

 

The Motions are DENIED as MOOT.

Sanctions may be awarded against a party who fails to oppose a motion to compel, or opposition to the motion was withdrawn, or the requested discovery was provided after the motion was filed. (C.R.C 3.1348(a).). Reasonable sanctions are awarded as indicated below:

Reasonable Sanctions are GRANTED in the total amount for all three Motions to Compel, of $934.95. Defendant ALAN GILBERT PEREZ and his counsel of record are jointly and severally ORDERED to pay Moving Party and their counsel of record sanctions in the total amount of $934.95, payable within 30 days from the date of the Court’s issuance of this Order.

 

Motion to Deem Admitted

 

“If a party to whom requests for admission are directed fails to serve a timely response, the following rules apply: (a) The party to whom the requests for admission are directed waives any objection to the requests…. The Court, on motion, may relieve that party from this waiver on its determination that both of the following conditions are satisfied: (1) The party has subsequently served a response that is in substantial compliance with Sections 2033.210, 2033.220, and 2033.230. (2) the party’s failure to serve a timely response was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect…. (c) The court shall make this order, unless it finds that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220. It is mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7…on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion.” (CCP §2033.280.)  Unverified responses “are tantamount to no responses at all.” (Appleton v. Superior Court (1988) 206 Cal.App.3d 632, 636.) No prior attempt to resolve the matter informally is required.

 

Here, RFAs were propounded on or about September 15, 2023.

 

In the untimely Opposition filed on October 1, 2024, Defendant contends that untimely responses were served on October 1, 2024, thus rendering this Motion moot.

 

Where untimely responses were ultimately served prior to this hearing, the Motion is DENIED as MOOT.

 

Reasonable sanctions are mandatory under CCP §2033.280(c) and are awarded as follows:

 

Defendant ALAN GILBERT HERNANDEZ and his counsel of record are jointly and severally ORDERED to pay Moving Party and their counsel of record sanctions in the total amount of $311.65, payable within 30 days from the date of the Court’s issuance of this Order.
