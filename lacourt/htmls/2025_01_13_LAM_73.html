Dept. 73

Angelica Lassiter v. R.I.C.H. Management Group, LLC, et al. (23STCV25652)

Counsel for Plaintiff/moving party: Plaintiff Angela Lassiter is in pro per

Counsel for Defendant: N/A

 

REQUEST FOR ENTRY OF DEFAULT JUDGMENT (filed 12/09/2024) 

 

TENTATIVE RULING 

 

Plaintiff’s request for default judgment is denied.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

Plaintiff Angelica Lassiter (“Plaintiff”), in pro per, filed this action against Defendants R.I.C.H. Management Group, LLC, and Kenneth Randolph (collectively “Defendants”) on October 20, 2023. Plaintiff’s complaint alleges a breach of contract cause of action against Defendants. Plaintiff alleges that Defendants approached her for monies to complete a home renovation. The parties signed a Promissory Note, and Plaintiff gave Defendants $30,000 that they were to pay back with 10% of the $30,000 accruing in interest each month for the duration that the funds were held. After the home was sold, Plaintiff alleges that Defendants owed her a total of $66,000 but never paid it out to her and ceased all communications with Plaintiff.

 

After Defendants failed to answer Plaintiff’s complaint, Plaintiff has attempted to obtain default judgment against Defendants several times. Recently, she filed a request for default judgment on November 25, 2024, that was rejected due to errors in the request. On December 9, 2024, Plaintiff filed another request for entry of default that has not been rejected. On December 11, 2024, an Order to Show Cause Re: Entry of Default was held and continued due to Plaintiff submitting a corrected request for entry of default. Upon entry of default, Plaintiff was to submit a default judgment packet prior to the continued hearing date of January 13, 2025. As of January 8, 2025, Plaintiff has not done so.

 

II.DISCUSSION

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

CCP § 585 permits entry of a judgment after a Defendant has failed to timely answer after being properly served.  A party seeking judgment on the default by the Court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) a proposed form of judgment; (6) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorneys’ fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties.  (CRC Rule 3.1800.)

TENTATIVE RULING 

 

Plaintiff’s request for default judgment is denied.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

Plaintiff Angelica Lassiter (“Plaintiff”), in pro per, filed this action against Defendants R.I.C.H. Management Group, LLC, and Kenneth Randolph (collectively “Defendants”) on October 20, 2023. Plaintiff’s complaint alleges a breach of contract cause of action against Defendants. Plaintiff alleges that Defendants approached her for monies to complete a home renovation. The parties signed a Promissory Note, and Plaintiff gave Defendants $30,000 that they were to pay back with 10% of the $30,000 accruing in interest each month for the duration that the funds were held. After the home was sold, Plaintiff alleges that Defendants owed her a total of $66,000 but never paid it out to her and ceased all communications with Plaintiff.

 

After Defendants failed to answer Plaintiff’s complaint, Plaintiff has attempted to obtain default judgment against Defendants several times. Recently, she filed a request for default judgment on November 25, 2024, that was rejected due to errors in the request. On December 9, 2024, Plaintiff filed another request for entry of default that has not been rejected. II.DISCUSSION

 

A.      Legal Standard

I. BACKGROUND

 

Plaintiff Mingzhe Zhao (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Mi Ni Fashion, Inc., Hyun S. Lee, Young Mi Lee, and Does 1 to 10 (collectively “Defendants”) on April 2, 2024. Plaintiff’s complaint alleges the following causes of action against Defendants: (1) breach of written agreement; (2) goods sold and delivered; (3) account stated; and (4) open book account; and (5) false promises. Plaintiff alleges that Defendants are in breach of a contract for purchase orders of clothing products in the amount of $535,320.35.

 

After Defendants’ answers to Plaintiff’s complaint were stricken on October 3, 2024, Plaintiff has attempted to obtain default judgment against Defendants a couple of times. Plaintiff’s most recent requests for entry of default were filed on November 25, 2024. On that date, Plaintiff filed requests for entry of default against Defendants Hyun S. Lee, Mi In Fashion, Inc., and Young Mi Lee.
