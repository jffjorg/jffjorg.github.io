Case Number: 20STCV46671    Hearing Date: June 4, 2024    Dept: 53

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles – Central District

Department 53

 

 

annette turner , et al.;

 

Plaintiffs,

 

 

vs.

 

 

aids healthcare foundation , et al.;

 

Defendants.

Case No.:

20STCV46671

 

 

Hearing Date:

June 4, 2024

 

 

Time:

10:00 a.m.

 

 

 

[tentative] Order RE:

 

(1)   defendant’s motion to bifurcate request for punitive damages

(2)   plaintiffs’ motion to bifurcate and order separate trials of the plaintiffs

 

 

MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation

 

RESPONDING PARTIES:     Plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis

(1)   Motion to Bifurcate Punitive Damages

MOVING PARTIES:              Plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation

(2)   Motion to Bifurcate and Order Separate Trials of Plaintiffs

The court considered the moving, non-opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with the motion to bifurcate punitive damages.

The court considered the amended moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with the motion to bifurcate and order separate trials of the plaintiffs.

 

DEFENDANTS’ MOTION TO BIFURCATE PUNITIVE DAMAGES

Defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation (“Defendant”) moves the court for an order bifurcating trial in this action, filed by plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis (“Plaintiffs”), to separate the issue of punitive damages from the issues of liability and compensatory damages.  Plaintiffs filed a non-opposition to this motion.

“The court shall, on application of any defendant, preclude the admission of evidence of that defendant’s profits or financial condition until after the trier of fact returns a verdict for plaintiff awarding actual damages and finds that a defendant is guilty of malice, oppression, or fraud in accordance with Section 3294.¿ Evidence of profit and financial condition shall be admissible only as to the defendant or defendants found to be liable to the plaintiff and to be guilty of malice, oppression, or fraud.¿ Evidence of profit and financial condition shall be presented to the same trier of fact that found for the plaintiff and found one or more defendants guilty of malice, oppression, or fraud.”¿¿(Civ. Code, §¿3295, subd. (d).)  “While the statute refers only to evidence of the defendant’s financial condition, in practice bifurcation under this section means that all evidence relating to the amount of punitive damages is to be offered in the second phase, while the determination whether plaintiff is entitled to punitive damages (i.e., whether the defendant is guilty of malice, fraud or oppression) is decided in the first phase along with compensatory damages.”¿ (Holdgrafer v. Unocal Corp. (2008) 160 Cal.App.4th 907, 919.)¿¿¿¿ 

The court finds that Defendant is entitled to an order bifurcating trial pursuant to Civil Code section 3295, and therefore grants Defendant’s motion and makes the orders set forth below.  (Civ. Code, § 3295, subd. (d).)

The court denies Plaintiffs’ request for monetary sanctions in the amount of $850, made in their non-opposition to Defendant’s motion.

PLAINTIFFS’ MOTION TO BIFURCATE AND ORDER SEPARATE TRIALS OF THE PLAINTIFFS

Plaintiffs move the court for an order bifurcating trial and, specifically, ordering separate jury trials for each of the Plaintiffs.  Plaintiffs request that the court order separate trials on the ground that there is now only one common claim alleged by Plaintiffs (the ninth cause of action for willful or negligent failure to exercise ordinary care of property) and that Plaintiffs would be prejudiced if they were required to present their claims at one trial.

“[T]rial courts have broad discretion to determine the order of proof in the interests of judicial economy.”¿ (Grappo v. Country Financial Corp. (1991) 235 Cal.App.3d 496, 504.)¿ Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 1048, “[t]he court, in furtherance of convenience or to avoid prejudice . . . may order a separate trial . . . of any separate issue or of any number of causes of action or issues . . . .”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 1048, subd. (b).)¿

The court finds that bifurcation of the trial of this matter in the manner suggested by Plaintiffs is not in furtherance of convenience.

First, as conceded by Plaintiffs, both Plaintiffs have alleged the ninth cause of action against Defendant.  The court acknowledges that Plaintiffs assert that they have filed a motion for leave to file a Fourth Amended Complaint, which proposes dismissing that cause of action as alleged by plaintiff Davis.  (Mot., p. 6:26-28.)  However, the court has not yet ruled on that motion and plaintiff Davis did not file a request for dismissal as to that cause of action.  Thus, that cause of action remains pending against Defendant by both Plaintiffs.

Second, Defendant has presented evidence showing that Plaintiffs’ discovery responses establish that they intend to rely on evidence presented by each other.  For example, in response to a special interrogatory requesting that plaintiff Turner describe each criminal act that she contends caused her harm, Turner stated that “Rositas sexually harassed Tammy Davis,” which “did not happen to Ms. Turner but created unsafe living conditions.”  (Stern Decl., Ex. 3, pp. 2, Ex. A to Ex. 3, p. 3.)  Similarly, in response to a special interrogatory requesting that plaintiff Davis state each action that she contends that Defendant had a legal duty to take to ensure the safety of the premises, Davis stated that (1) Defendant’s “[d]eletion of security footage reflecting a crime . . . reflects [Defendant’s] failure and/or refusal to maintain, exercise care or review, or undertake any measure to correct crime, flooding, or other significant habitability problems[,]” and (2) Defendant “destroyed footage which would have reflected [plaintiff] Annette Turner being physically assaulted, [plaintiff] Tammy Davis being sexually and physically assaulted, and someone stealing [plaintiff] Annette Turner’s belongings out of her room.”  (Stern Decl., Ex. 2, PDF p. 25:7-14, 29:3-8.)  Thus, it appears that Plaintiffs intend to rely on much of the same evidence and the claims made by each other at trial.

The court also finds that Plaintiffs have not met their burden to show that bifurcation in this manner is necessary to avoid prejudice to Plaintiffs or any other party.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1048, subd. (b).)

For the reasons set forth above, the court finds that bifurcating trial in the manner suggested by Plaintiffs is not in furtherance of convenience, judicial economy, or efficiency, and is not necessary to avoid prejudice to any of the parties involved in this action, and therefore denies Plaintiffs’ motion.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1048, subd. (b).)

ORDER

            The court grants defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation’s motion to bifurcate plaintiffs’ request for punitive damages.

            The court orders that trial of this action will be bifurcated as follows: (1) the jury will first decide the issues of whether to impose liability for compensatory damages and, if so, the amount of compensatory damages, and whether defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation is guilty of malice, oppression, or fraud, and (2) only after making findings in plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis’s favor on all those issues, the jury will then decide the issue of whether to impose punitive damages against defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation.

            The court denies plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis’s motion to bifurcate and order separate trials of plaintiffs.

            The court orders defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this motion.  No opposition or reply papers were filed.

LEGAL STANDARD

The purpose of a motion for summary judgment or summary adjudication “is to provide courts with a mechanism to cut through the parties’ pleadings in order to determine whether, despite their allegations, trial is in fact necessary to resolve their dispute.”  (Aguilar v. Atlantic Richfield Co. (2001) 25 Cal.4th 826, 843.)  “Code of Civil Procedure section 437c, subdivision (c), requires the trial judge to grant summary judgment if all the evidence submitted, and ‘all inferences reasonably deducible from the evidence’ and uncontradicted by other inferences or evidence, show that there is no triable issue as to any material fact and that the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.”  (Adler v. Manor Healthcare Corp. (1992) 7 Cal.App.4th 1110, 1119.)

“On a motion for summary judgment, the initial burden is always on the moving party to make a prima facie showing that there are no triable issues of material fact.”  (Scalf v. D.B. Log Homes, Inc. (2005) 128 Cal.App.4th 1510, 1519.)  A defendant or cross-defendant moving for summary judgment or summary adjudication “has met his or her burden of showing that a cause of action has no merit if the party has shown that one or more elements of the cause of action . . . cannot be established, or that there is a complete defense to the cause of action.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c, subd. (p)(2).)  “Once the defendant or cross-defendant has met that burden, the burden shifts to the plaintiff or cross-complainant to show that a triable issue of one or more material facts exists as to the cause of action or a defense thereto.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c, subd. (p)(2).)  “If the plaintiff cannot do so, summary judgment should be granted.”  (Avivi v. Centro Medico Urgente Medical Center (2008) 159 Cal.App.4th 463, 467.)  “When deciding whether to grant summary judgment, the court must consider all of the evidence set forth in the papers (except evidence to which the court has sustained an objection), as well as all reasonable inferences that may be drawn from that evidence, in the light most favorable to the party opposing summary judgment.”  (Id. at p. 467; Code Civ. Proc., § 437c, subd. (c).)

DISCUSSION

Defendants Cinna Toy Wohlmuth, M.D., and Sarah Simko, M.D. (“Defendants”) move the court for an order granting summary judgment in their favor and against plaintiffs Italia Arteaga (“Arteaga”) and Henry Ron (“Ron”) (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) or, alternatively, summary adjudication.

The court finds that Defendants have not shown that they served plaintiff Ron with the papers filed in connection with this motion.

As a threshold matter, the court notes that, on November 29, 2023, the court issued an order (1) granting Brenden Leib’s motion to be relieved as counsel of record for Plaintiffs, and (2) ordering that Brenden Leib will be relieved as counsel for Plaintiffs effective upon the filing of the proof of service of the signed “Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil” on the clients.  (Nov. 29, 2023 Order, p. 2:21-25.)  On December 7, 2023, Brenden Leib filed a proof of service with the court establishing that, on that date, Plaintiffs were served by mail with the “Signed . . . Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel” and the related November 29, 2023 minute order.  (Dec. 7, 2023 POS-040, ¶¶ 4-5, 6, subd. (b)(2).)  Thus, Brenden Leib was relieved as Plaintiffs’ counsel of record as of December 7, 2023, such that Defendants were required to serve the pending motion on Plaintiffs directly.

Defendants attached proofs of service to the moving papers filed in connection with this motion, all of which state that (1) Plaintiffs were served by messenger service after the declarant enclosed the subject documents in a sealed envelope and provided them to the professional messenger service Signal Attorney Service, and (2) “[a]n original proof of service by [the] messenger will be filed pursuant to California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1300(c).”  (Mot., pp. 14-15; Compendium of Evidence, pp. 107-108; Elliot Decl., pp. 9-10; Friedlich Decl., pp. 9-; Valoff Decl., pp. 4-5 [emphasis in original].)  Although the documents list Plaintiffs’ names under the title “Proof of Personal Service List[,]” those papers do not attach proofs of service signed by the messenger establishing that Plaintiffs were personally served with the moving papers.

In addition to the proofs of service attached to the moving papers, Defendants separately filed two proofs of service of this motion.  First, on March 21, 2024, Defendants filed a document entitled “Proof of Electronic Service re Motion for Summary Judgment and Documents in Support Thereof,” stating that the moving papers were served on nonmoving defendant White Memorial Medical Center d/b/a Adventist Health White Memorial by email on March 21, 2024.  (Mar. 21, 2024 Proof of Electronic Service, pp. 2-3.)  This proof of service does not state that Plaintiffs were served with this motion by electronic service on that date. Second, on April 2, 2024, Defendants filed a proof of service signed by Signal Attorney Service, Inc., in which the registered process server states that plaintiff Arteaga was personally served with the moving papers filed in connection with this motion on March 21, 2024, “by constructive service pursuant to [Code of Civil Procedure section] (b)” “by leaving [the documents] with: Ariana Cabrera[,]” who is identified as Arteaga’s “Sister/Co-Occupant[.]”  (April 2, 2024 Proof of Service, pp. 1-2.)  Plaintiff Ron is not identified as a served party in this proof of service.  (Id., at p. 2 [stating “To: Italia Arteaga”].)

Thus, the court finds that Defendants have shown that they personally served plaintiff Arteaga with the moving papers filed in connection with this motion on March 21, 2024.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1011, subd. (b) [“If, upon a party, service shall be made in the manner specifically provided in particular cases, or, if no specific provision is made, service may be made by leaving the notice or other paper at the party’s residence with some person 18 years of age or older”].)

However, Defendants did not file a proof of service as to plaintiff Ron.  As set forth above, each of the moving papers filed with the court states that both Plaintiffs were served by messenger service, and that the “proof of service by messenger will be filed” by Defendants.  (Mot., pp. 14-15; Compendium of Evidence, pp. 107-108; Elliot Decl., pp. 9-10; Friedlich Decl., pp. 9-; Valoff Decl., pp. 4-5.)  Defendants did not file the “original proof of service by messenger” of the moving papers on plaintiff Ron.

Thus, because (1) the proofs of service attached to the moving papers do not establish service on plaintiff Ron, since they merely state that the declarant provided the documents to the messenger and that subsequent proofs of service by the messenger would be filed by Defendants, and (2) Defendants did not file the “original proof of service by messenger” as to Ron, the court finds that Defendants have not established that plaintiff Ron was served with the pending motion for summary judgment or, alternatively, summary adjudication.

The court therefore finds it appropriate, and exercises its discretion, to continue the hearing on Defendants’ motion for summary judgment or, alternatively, summary adjudication to ensure that plaintiff Ron is served with the papers filed in connection with this motion.

ORDER

The court orders that the hearing on defendants Cinna Toy Wohlmuth, M.D., and Sarah Simko, M.D.’s motion for summary judgment or, alternatively, summary adjudication is continued to September 12, 2024, at 10:00 a.m., in Department 53.

The court orders defendants Cinna Toy Wohlmuth, M.D., and Sarah Simko, M.D. (1) to give notice of this ruling to plaintiffs Italia Arteaga and Henry Ron, and to all other parties who have appeared in this action, and to file a proof of service of the notice of ruling with the court no later than June 20, 2024, and (2) to serve plaintiff Henry Ron with the moving papers filed in connection with this motion, and to file a proof of service of that service with the court no later than June 20, 2024.

MOTION TO STRIKE ANSWER

Plaintiff requests that the court strike (1) Defendant’s answer in its entirety, or, in the alternative, (2) each affirmative defense alleged in Defendant’s answer.  Plaintiff moves for this relief on the ground that Defendant’s affirmative defenses are irrelevant, false, improper, and not in conformity with the laws of this state, a court rule, or an order of the court.

The court denies Plaintiff’s motion to strike Defendant’s answer, in its entirety, because Plaintiff has not shown that all of Defendant’s affirmative defenses include false, irrelevant, or improper matter, or are not drawn in conformity with the laws of this state, an order of the court, or a rule of the court.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 436.)

The court denies Plaintiff’s motion to strike the first, second, fourth, sixth, 11th, 12th, 21st-25th, 28th-30th, 32nd, and 33rd-35th affirmative defenses because Plaintiff has not shown that those defenses improperly combine several defenses therein.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 436.)

The court denies Plaintiff’s motion to strike the 23rd or 24th affirmative defense as duplicative.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 436.)  The 23rd affirmative defense alleges that Plaintiff is barred from obtaining relief because he “consented to each and every act of which he has complained[,]” and the 24th affirmative defense alleges that Plaintiff is barred from obtaining relief because he “ratified each and every act of which he has complained.” (Answer, ¶¶ 115-116 [emphasis added].)  Thus, the 23rd and 24th defenses allege two separate defenses and therefore are not duplicative of each other.

The court denies Plaintiff’s motion to strike the 10th, 20th, 21st, 32nd, and 33rd affirmative defenses as improper on the ground that Defendant “has provided responses on behalf of other Defendants” because Defendant has not answered on behalf of the other defendants involved in this action in asserting those affirmative defenses.[2]  (Code Civ. Proc., § 436; Answer, ¶¶ 102, 112, 113, 124, 125.)

The court denies Plaintiff’s motion to strike the 16th affirmative defense because Plaintiff has not shown, based on the face of the pleading or matters of which the court has taken judicial notice, that this defense is false, improper, or irrelevant.  (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 436, 437 [“The grounds for a motion to strike shall appear on the face of the challenged pleading or from any matter of which the court is required to take judicial notice”].)

The court denies Plaintiff’s motion to strike Defendant’s answer as a “sham pleading” because Plaintiff has not shown, based on the face of the pleading and matters of which the court has taken judicial notice, that its answer is a sham pleading.  (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 436, 437.)
