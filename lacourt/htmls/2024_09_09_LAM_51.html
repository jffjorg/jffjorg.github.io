DEPARTMENT 51 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling, please email the clerk at SMCdept51@lacourt.org (and “cc” all other parties in the same email) and notify all other parties in advance that you will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and the party you represent in the body of the email. If you do not have access to the internet, you may call the clerk at (213) 633-0351.

 

If you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may nevertheless appear and argue the motion, and the Court may decide not to adopt the tentative ruling. Please note that the tentative ruling is not an invitation, nor an opportunity, to file any further documents relative to the hearing in question which are not authorized by statute or Rule of Court.

 

2. For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its discretion.

3. DO NOT USE THE ABOVE EMAIL FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE RULING.  The Court will not read or respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.

 




Case Number: 24STCV02491    Hearing Date: September 9, 2024    Dept: 51

Tentative Ruling

 

Judge Upinder S. Kalra, Department 51

 

HEARING DATE:   September 9, 2024                                         

 

CASE NAME:           Dave Ventrella v. Conner Jadwin, et al.

 

CASE NO.:                24STCV02491

 

MOTION TO STAY PROCEEDINGS

 

MOVING PARTY:  Defendant Conner Jadwin

 

RESPONDING PARTY(S): Plaintiff Dave Ventrella

 

REQUESTED RELIEF:

 

1.      An Order staying this action pending Defendants’ appeals from the April 24, 2024 order denying Defendants’ motions to compel arbitration.

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

1.      Motion for Stay pending appeal is GRANTED.

STATEMENT OF MATERIAL FACTS AND/OR PROCEEDINGS:

 

On January 31, 2024, Plaintiff Dave Ventrella (Plaintiff) filed a Complaint against Defendants Let’s Jett, Inc. and Connor Jadwin (Defendants) with ten causes of action for: (1) Breach of Oral Contract, (2) Breach of Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing, (3) Breach of Fiduciary Duty, (4) Constructive Fraud, (5) Conversion, (6) Intentional Misrepresentation, (7) Unfair Competition, Business and Professions Code § 17200 et seq., (8) Accounting, (9) Unjust Enrichment, and (10) Declaratory Relief.

 

Plaintiff’s Complaint centers around an oral partnership agreement in which he alleges that he is entitled to forty-nine percent of shares in Let’s Jet stock based on his contribution to the company.

 

On March 14, 2024, Defendant Let’s Jett, Inc. (LJI) filed a motion to compel arbitration, joined by Defendant Jadwin, which the court DENIED.

 

On April 24, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Notice of Related Case with Case No. 24STCV07396.

 

On May 28, 2024, Defendant LJI filed an Appeal to the court’s denial of the motion to compel arbitration.

 

On May 31, 2024, Defendant Jadwin filed a Demurrer to the Complaint.

 

On June 3, 2024, Defendant LJI filed an Answer.

 

On August 9, 2024, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint (FAC) with the same causes of action.

 

On August 12, 2024, Defendant Jadwin filed the instant motion to stay proceedings. On August 29, 2024, Plaintiff filed an opposition. Replies were due on or before August 30, 2024. As of September 4, 2024, the court has not received a reply brief.

 

LEGAL STANDARD:

 

CCP § 1294(a) provides for an appeal from “an order dismissing or denying a petition to compel arbitration” without an automatic stay pending appeal.[1] (Code Civ. Proc. § 1293(a).) There is no authority indicating that why the court did not compel arbitration is a factor in the right to appeal. So long as the court did not compel arbitration, the party moving to compel arbitration may appeal.

 

“[A] court ordinarily has inherent power, in its discretion, to stay proceedings when such a stay will accommodate the ends of justice.” (OTO, L.L.C. v. Kho (2019) 8 Cal.5th 111, 141 [citing People v. Bell (1984) 159 Cal.App.3d 323, 329.]) “[T]he power to stay proceedings is incidental to the power inherent in every court to control the disposition of the causes on its docket with economy of time and effort for itself, for counsel, and for litigants.” (Landis v. North American Co. (1936) 299 U.S. 248, 254; OTO, L.L.C., supra, at p. 141.)

 

Request for Judicial Notice

 

The court grants Defendant’s request for judicial notice as to A, B, and C. (Evid. Code § 452(d), (h); See Kalnoki v. First American Trustee Servicing Solutions, LLC (2017) 8 Cal.App.5th 23,37.) However, the court only takes judicial notice of the foregoing documents only as to “the existence, content and authenticity of public records and other specified documents”; it does not take judicial notice of the truth of the factual matters asserted in those documents. (Dominguez v. Bonta (2022) 87 Cal. App. 5th 389, 400.)

 

ANALYSIS:

 

Aeolus Air Charter, Inc. Arbitration Agreement - Is there a choice-of-law provision at all?

 

As a threshold matter, the court asks whether there is even an applicable choice-of-law provision since there is no agreement to arbitrate Plaintiff’s claims.[2] The subject Arbitration Agreement (Agreement) advanced by Defendant Jadwin concerns an employment relationship, not a partnership relationship. Neither party addressed this issue in their briefs.

 

“There is no public policy . . . that favors the arbitration of disputes the parties did not agree to arbitrate.” (Howard v. Goldbloom (2018) 30 Cal.App.5th 659, 663 [internal citations omitted.]) “Thus, no dispute may be ordered to arbitration unless it is within the scope of the arbitration agreement.” (Ibid.) While parties may have disputes that an arbitration agreement broadly covers, the dispute “must arise out of the contract.” (Ibid.) Such broad clauses apply to “extracontractual disputes” provided that “they have their roots in the relationship between the parties which was created by the contract.” (Ibid.) To decipher these roots, courts examine the “nature of the agreement and of the claims and their relationship to one another.” (Ibid.)

 

Here, the parties have two separate relationships: one as partners (subject of this dispute) and one as employer-employee (subject of the related dispute now in arbitration). The partner relationship occurred in or about February 2020. (FAC ¶ 17.) The employment relationship with the Agreement subsequently occurred in June 2020. (Jadwin Decl., Exhibit A.) These relationships are independent even though they arise from the same underlying facts – the private charter business. Plaintiff here seeks relief pertaining to the partnership agreement only. (See generally FAC.) The Agreement covers the employment relationship. (Jadwin Decl., Exhibit A, p. 1.) Therefore, the “roots” of the instant dispute are not in the Agreement.

 

Accordingly, the parties’ adoption of the FAA for the Agreement does not apply here.[3]

 

Stay Pending Appeal

 

Defendant contends that there is good cause for the court to issue a stay pending appeal.[4] Plaintiff argues that Plaintiff has not explained how a stay will accommodate the ends of justice or otherwise established good cause for a stay.

 

As indicated above, the court has “the power to stay proceedings is incidental to the power inherent in every court to control the disposition of the causes on its docket with economy of time and effort for itself, for counsel, and for litigants.” (Landis v. North American Co. (1936) 299 U.S. 248, 254; OTO, L.L.C., supra, at p. 141.) Additionally, while the court is still not convinced that Coinbase, Inc. applies here, in the context of judicial economy and docket management, the reasoning behind implementing a stay pending appeal of an order denying a motion to compel arbitration is persuasive. (Coinbase, supra, at p. 742-744.) Here, the very matter subject to appeal is whether the partnership dispute is subject to the Agreement. It is appropriate to preserve the court’s and the parties’ resources litigating this matter pending a decision from the Court of Appeals.[5]

 

Accordingly, the court exercises its discretion to GRANT Defendant’s motion for stay pending appeal.

 

CONCLUSION:

 

            For the foregoing reasons, the Court decides the pending motion as follows:

 

1.      Motion for Stay pending appeal is GRANTED.

Moving party is to give notice.

 

IT IS SO ORDERED.
