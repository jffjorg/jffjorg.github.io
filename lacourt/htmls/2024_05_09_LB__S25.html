DEPARTMENT S25 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23LBCV00315    Hearing Date: May 9, 2024    Dept: S25

Procedural Background

On February 21, 2023, Plaintiff Victor M. Morales (“Plaintiff”) filed this lemon law action against Defendant Ford Motor Company (“Defendant”) for violations of the Song Beverly Act. On March 11, 2024, Defendant filed the instant motion to compel the deposition of Plaintiff. Plaintiff has not opposed. On May 2, 2024, Defendant filed a notice of non-opposition to the motion and a supplemental declaration.

On November 13, 2023, Defendant served a notice of deposition of Plaintiff set for December 18, 2023. (Lee Decl., ¶1, Exh. 1.) In response, on December 13, 2023, Plaintiff served an objection to the notice of deposition. (Id. at ¶2, Exh. 2.) On December 15, 2023, Defendant’s contacted Plaintiff’s counsel requesting dates when Plaintiff was available. No response was received. (Id. at ¶3, Exh. 3.) On December 21, 2023, defense counsel contacted opposing counsel again seeking Plaintiff’s availability. No response was received. (Id. at ¶4, Exh. 4.) On December 29, 2023, another email was sent by Defendant’s counsel to Plaintiff’s counsel seeking deposition dates. There was no response to this inquiry. (Id. at ¶5, Exh. 5.)

On other discovery matters, Plaintiff has provided responses. For example, on December 13, 2023, Plaintiff provided discovery responses to Defendant’s discovery requests and on January 4, 2024, Plaintiff objected to Defendant’s Notice of Vehicle Inspection. (Id. at ¶6, Exh. 6.) On January 9, 2024, and January 16, 2024, defense counsel requested alterative dates to schedule a vehicle inspection, but no response was received. (Id. at ¶8, Exh. 6.) Defendant now seeks an order to compel Plaintiff’s deposition.

Analysis

“If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for

inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.” (CCP § 2025.450(a).)

As evidenced by the Declaration of Michelle Lee, Defendant’s counsel many numerous attempts to reset the deposition to no avail. (Id. at ¶¶3-7, Exhs. 3-5.) Plaintiff has not filed an opposition to this motion and, thus, has not justified his objections to the taking of his deposition. Significantly, Defendant filed a notice of non-opposition and a supplemental declaration informing the Court that after this motion was filed, Plaintiff agreed to deposition on April 25, 2024, but failed to attend. (Lee Supp. Decl., ¶¶3-8, Exhs. B-G.)

Ruling

Plaintiff failed to justify his initial objection to the first deposition notice and failed to explain his subsequent failure to attend to April 25 noticed deposition. Based upon Plaintiff’s non-cooperation, the Court finds Defendant has shown good cause to compel Plaintiff’s deposition and the motion is, therefore, GRANTED.

The Court orders Plaintiff to appear at deposition by May 20, 2024.

Case Number: 23LBCV00563    Hearing Date: May 9, 2024    Dept: S25

Procedural Background

On March 28, 2023, Plaintiffs Keishawn Spells and Reathell (“Plaintiffs”) filed personal injury action against Defendant Fatemeh Sabetsarvestanian (“Defendant”) for motor vehicle and general negligence. On November 29, 2023, Defendant served Plaintiff Spells and Plaintiff Veal with a Request for Documents (Set One). On January 17, 2024, Plaintiffs returned unverified responses consistently of objections and no substantive or responsive documents. Plaintiffs requested and received a 30-day extension to complete these responses. No further responses were received. On February 23, 2024, Defendant provided another 7-day extension to respond. No further responses were provided. On April 15, 2024, Defendant filed these Motions to Compel Further Responses to Form Interrogatories (Set One) from Plaintiffs. While Plaintiffs expressly do not oppose the

motions, both request more time to respond due to health and family issues. Defendant requests Plaintiffs be ordered to further respond, without objection, and seeks $1,185 in sanctions be awarded against Plaintiffs and Plaintiffs’ counsel.

The Court notes that an IDC was held on May 7, 2024, however, as Plaintiffs failed to appear or provide an excuse as to the non-appearance, the matter was taken off-calendar.

Analysis

“Under Code of Civil Procedure section 2030.300, subdivision (a), a court may order a party to serve a further response to an interrogatory when the court finds that: “(1) An answer to a particular interrogatory is evasive or incomplete[;] (2) An exercise of the option to produce documents under Section 2030.230 is unwarranted or the required specification of those documents is inadequate[; or] (3) An objection to an interrogatory is without merit or too general.” The burden is on the responding party to justify any objection or failure to fully answer the interrogatories. (Fairmont Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (2000) 22 Cal.4th 245, 255.) Where the court grants a motion to compel further responses, sanctions shall be imposed against the party who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel, unless the party acted with substantial justification or the sanction would otherwise be unjust. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.300.)

Over the last three to four months, Plaintiffs have had opportunities to provide substantive responses to Plaintiffs have provided minimal responses and objections to the form interrogatories. Plaintiffs expressly do not oppose the motions but request more time to respond due to health and family issues. (See oppositions.) An independent discovery conference was held on May 7, 2024, concerning the subject discovery, and Plaintiffs did not appear.

The Court finds that Plaintiffs have not provided complete responses and have not justified their objections. Though neither expressly oppose the motions, thus conceding the arguments in the moving papers, Plaintiffs request more time to respond. Yet, the ongoing health issues do not provide sufficiently justify the inadequate responses to basic form interrogatories, which have been in their possession since November 2023. Plaintiffs may be experiencing health issues, such as high blood pressure and headaches, and family issues, but the issues do not appear to be so severe that they cannot respond to these requests in the abundant amount of time provided by Defendant. In sum, Plaintiffs voluntarily pursued this case and cannot simply abandon their discovery obligations.

Ruling

For the foregoing reasons, the Court GRANTS both of Defendant’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Form Interrogatories.
