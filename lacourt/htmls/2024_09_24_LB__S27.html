DEPARTMENT S27 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS



Case Number: 21LBCV00160    Hearing Date: September 24, 2024    Dept: S27

1.     Background Facts

Plaintiff, Heather Szafranski filed this action against Defendants, Michael Hollinghurst and his company, Network Financial Service, for quiet title, fraud, cancellation of instruments, rescission of contracts, and declaratory relief.  The crux of the complaint is that Plaintiff signed a promissory note with Defendant, but believed the transaction was canceled; Defendant, in violation of the parties’ agreement, filed the deed of trust on the property. 

 

Plaintiff filed the complaint on 3/26/21.  The Clerk, at Plaintiff’s request, entered Defendant’s default on 11/04/21.  On 11/17/21, after a default judgment prove-up hearing, the Court issued judgment in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant.  The judgment makes clear that Plaintiff owns the subject property and Defendants have no interest in the subject property.  It also makes clear that the promissory note upon which Defendants base their claim is null and void.  The judgment provides, “Defendants are to cancel the promissory note and Improper Deed of Trust (document number 8-0719664) and to deliver it to the Plaintiff or to the clerk of this Court for cancellation.”

 

2.     Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment

Defendant filed this motion on 9/03/24, setting it for hearing on 9/24/24.  9/24/24 is fifteen court days after 9/03/24, rendering the motion untimely filed per CCP §1005(b), which requires papers to be filed at least sixteen court days prior to the hearing.  Sixteen court days prior to 9/24/24 fell on Friday, 8/30/24 (because Monday, 9/02/24 was a court holiday). 

 

Defendant filed proof of service of the moving papers on Plaintiff, showing service by email on Sunday, 9/01/24.  §1010.6(a)(3)(B) extends time for email service by an additional two calendar days.  Thus, email service was required no later than 8/28/24. 

 

The motion is denied due to failure to timely file and serve the moving papers. 

 

Defendant is ordered to give notice. 

 

Parties who intend to submit on this tentative must send an email to the court at gdcdepts27@lacourt.org indicating intention to submit on the tentative as directed by the instructions provided on the court website at www.lacourt.org.  If the department does not receive an email indicating the parties are submitting on the tentative and there are no appearances at the hearing, the motion may be placed off calendar.  If a party submits on the tentative, the party’s email must include the case number and must identify the party submitting on the tentative. If any party does not submit on the tentative, the party should make arrangements to appear remotely at the hearing on this matter.  


Case Number: 23LBCV01318    Hearing Date: September 24, 2024    Dept: S27

Plaintiff filed this motion on 9/04/24, setting it for hearing on 9/24/24.  9/24/24 is fourteen court days after 9/04/24, rendering the motion untimely filed per CCP §1005(b), which requires papers to be filed at least sixteen court days prior to the hearing.  Sixteen court days prior to 9/24/24 fell on Friday, 8/30/24 (because Monday, 9/02/24 was a court holiday). 

 

Plaintiff filed proof of service of the moving papers on Defendants and Deponent, showing service by regular mail and email on Sunday, 9/01/24.  CCP §1013(a) extends time for mail service by an additional five calendar days.  §1010.6(a)(3)(B) extends time for email service by an additional two calendar days.  Thus, mail service was required no later than 8/25/24 and email service was required no later than 8/28/24. 

 

Compounding the issue, pursuant to CRC 3.1346, all moving papers filed in connection with a motion to compel deposition of non-party must be personally served on a non-party deponent.  Plaintiff filed proof of service of the moving papers on the non-party deponent via email and regular mail, but not personal service.

Case Number: 24LBCV00200    Hearing Date: September 24, 2024    Dept: S27

1.     Background Facts

Plaintiffs, Kenyeta Thompson, et al. filed this action against Defendants, Tony Huy Lim and Margaret Chung for breach of contract and related claims arising out of the parties’ landlord-tenant relationship.  The crux of the complaint is that Plaintiffs rented an illegal and unpermitted unit from Defendants.  The unit was inspected by the City, which noted various violations.  Specifically, the City noted that the unit had undergone various improvements without permits and/or inspections, and the City ordered the defendants to obtain the necessary permits and allow the required inspections.  After the inspections, Defendants terminated Plaintiffs’ tenancy with no stated reason for the termination. 

 

The original complaint is the operative complaint, and it includes causes of action for:

·         breach of contract

·         breach of the covenant of quiet enjoyment

·         wrongful eviction

·         violation of Civil Code §1940.2

·         violation of Municipal Code §8.101.030

·         violation of Civil Code §1946.2.    

 

2.     Demurrer to Complaint

a.     Legal Standard on Demurrer

A demurrer is a pleading used to test the legal sufficiency of other pleadings. It raises issues of law, not fact, regarding the form or content of the opposing party’s pleading.  It is not the function of the demurrer to challenge the truthfulness of the complaint; and for purpose of the ruling on the demurrer, all facts pleaded in the complaint are assumed to be true, however improbable they may be.

 

A demurrer can be used only to challenge defects that appear on the face of the pleading under attack; or from matters outside the pleading that are judicially noticeable. Blank v. Kirwan 39 Cal.3d 311 (1985). No other extrinsic evidence can be considered (i.e., no “speaking demurrers”). A demurrer is brought under CCP § 430.10 [grounds], § 430.30 [as to any matter on its face or from which judicial notice may be taken], and § 430.50(a) [can be taken to the entire complaint or any cause of action within].  Specifically, a demurrer may be brought per CCP § 430.10(e) if insufficient facts are stated to support the cause of action asserted.  Per CCP §430.10(a) a demurrer may be brought where the court has no jurisdiction of the subject of the cause of action alleged in the pleading.  Furthermore, demurrer for uncertainty will be sustained only where the complaint is so bad that the defendant cannot reasonably respond.  CCP § 430.10(f). 

 

However, in construing the allegations, the court is to give effect to specific factual allegations that may modify or limit inconsistent general or conclusory allegations. Financial Corporation of America v. Wilburn, 189 Cal.App.3rd 764, 769 (1987). And, if the facts pled in the complaint are inconsistent with facts which are incorporated by reference from exhibits attached to the complaint, the facts in the incorporated exhibits control. Further, irrespective of the name or label given to a cause of action by the plaintiff, a general demurrer must be overruled if the facts as pled in the body of the complaint state some valid claim for relief. Special demurrers are not allowed in limited jurisdiction courts. (CCP § 92(c).)

 

Leave to amend must be allowed where there is a reasonable possibility of successful amendment. Goodman v. Kennedy, 18 Cal.3d 335, 348 (1976). The burden is on the complainant to show the Court that a pleading can be amended successfully. (Id.)

 

Finally, CCP section 430.41 requires that “[b]efore filing a demurrer pursuant to this chapter, the demurring party shall meet and confer in person or by telephone with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to demurrer for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer.” (CCP §430.41(a).) The parties are to meet and confer at least five days before the date the responsive pleading is due. (CCP §430.41(a)(2).) Thereafter, the demurring party shall file and serve a declaration detailing their meet and confer efforts. (CCP §430.41(a)(3).)

 

  1. Meet and Confer

Defense Counsel, Jennifer Volcy, declares she and Plaintiff’s attorney met and conferred, but were unable to resolve the issues presented by way of the demurrer and motion to strike.   The meet and confer requirement is therefore satisfied, and the Court will rule on the matter on its merits.  

 

  1. Initial Note

Any opposition to the demurrer was due on or before 9/11/24.  CCP §1005(b).  Any amended complaint in lieu of opposition was also due on or before 9/11/24.  §472.  Plaintiffs have not filed an amended complaint or opposition to the demurrer.  The Court asks Plaintiffs to ensure they dismiss any uncontested claims, amend any claims amenable to amendment, and/or oppose any future pleading challenge substantively and timely. 

 

  1. Analysis

Defendants demur to the first, third, fourth, and fifth causes of action.  They do not demur to the second or sixth causes of action. 

 

As to the first cause of action, they correctly note that, to the extent the claims are premised on breach of contract, Plaintiffs have not attached a copy of the contract and have not pled which term of the contract was breached. 

 

As to the third cause of action, they also correctly note that Plaintiffs have not pled a claim for wrongful termination, as they have neither pled that their tenancy was terminated while a lease was in place nor that they were terminated for an illegal or unlawful reason; they plead only that they do not know why their lease was terminated, but they do not plead that the termination was illegal. 

 

As to the fourth cause of action, they correctly note that §1940.2 precludes a landlord to use force, threats, or menacing conduct to terminated a lease, but no such acts are pled in the complaint. 

 

Finally, as to the fifth cause of action, Long Beach Municipal Code §8.101.030 prohibits tenant harassment, but Plaintiffs have not pled tenant harassment. 

The demurrer is sustained in its entirety on the stated grounds. 

 

It is not up to the Court to figure out how the complaint can be amended to state a cause of action.  Rather, the burden is on the plaintiff to show in what manner he or she can amend the complaint, and how that amendment will change the legal effect of the pleading.  Goodman v. Kennedy (1976) 18 Cal.3d 335, 349; Hendy v. Losse (1991) 54 Cal.3d 723, 742.  In light of the lack of opposition to the demurrer, leave to amend is denied.  

 

3.     Motion to Strike

Plaintiffs seek punitive damages in connection with their second, third, and fourth causes of action.  Defendants move to strike the prayer for punitive damages, correctly noting that the complaint lacks facts supporting the claims of fraud, malice, and/or oppression.  Notably, the motion is moot as it relates to the third and fourth causes of action in light of the ruling on the demurrer, above.  The motion to strike remains ripe as to the second cause of action.  The motion to strike is granted.  Again, in light of the lack of opposition, leave to amend is denied.

 

Defendants’ notice of motion also indicates Defendants move to strike the prayer for attorneys’ fees.  Plaintiffs seek to recover attorneys’ fees in connection with their third and fifth causes of action.  The motion is therefore moot in light of the ruling on the demurrer above.  Notably, Defendants failed to brief the attorneys’ fees issue in their points and authorities.  Thus, if the motion were not moot, it would be denied. 

 

4.     Conclusion

Defendants’ demurrer is sustained without leave to amend.  Defendants’ motion to strike is granted without leave to amend to the extent it is not moot.  Defendants are ordered to file an answer to the complaint, with the subject causes of action and prayers for damages deemed stricken, within twenty days. 

 

5.     Case Management Conference

The parties are reminded that there is a CMC on calendar concurrently with the hearing on the demurrer and motion to strike.  The Court asks the parties to make arrangements to appear remotely at the CMC and hearing on the demurrer/motion to strike.
