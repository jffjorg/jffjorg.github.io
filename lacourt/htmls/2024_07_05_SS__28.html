DEPARTMENT 28 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

All parties are urged to meet and confer with all parties concerning this tentative ruling to see if they can reach an agreed-upon resolution of their matter.  If you are able to reach an agreement, please notify the courtroom staff in advance of the hearing if you wish to submit on the tentative ruling rather than argue the motion by notifying the court by e-mailing the court at: SSCDEPT28@lacourt.org.  Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the Case Number in the Subject line.  In the body of the email, please provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, and whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, intervenor, or non-party, etc.

            Please be advised that if you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may still appear at the hearing and argue the matter, and the court could change its tentative based upon the argument.  Unless you receive a submission from all other parties in the matter, you should assume that others might appear at the hearing to argue.  If you submit, but still intend to appear, include the words "SUBMITS, BUT WILL APPEAR" in the Subject line.     If you elect to argue your matter, you are urged to do so remotely, via Court-Connect.

                                       Note that once the Court has issued a tentative, the Court has the inherent authority not to allow the withdrawal of a motion and to adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the court.  This does not excuse a moving party's need to do one of the following: appear; submit; or take a matter off calendar by canceling the motion in the case reservation system before issuance of the tentative ruling if the matter moving party does not intend to proceed.    
 
            If you submitted a courtesy copy of your papers containing media (such as a DVD or thumb drive), unless you request the return of the media in your papers, the court will destroy it following the hearing of your matter.  


Case Number: 20STCV27050    Hearing Date: July 5, 2024    Dept: 28

Having considered the moving papers, the Court rules as follows. 

BACKGROUND 

On July 20, 2020, Plaintiff Bridgette Stoval (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority, City of Los Angeles (“City”), County of Los Angeles (“County”), and Does 1-50 for motor vehicle negligence and general negligence. 

On December 24, 2020, the County filed an answer. 

On January 21, 2021, Defendant Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority dba Metro (erroneously sued and served as Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority) (“Metro”) filed an answer. 

On January 29, 2021, the Court dismissed the City without prejudice at Plaintiff’s request. 

On March 17, 2021, the Court dismissed the County without prejudice at Plaintiff’s request. 

On May 31, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel Khashayar Eshraghi, Esq., filed a motion to be relieved as counsel, to be heard on July 5, 2024. 

Trial is currently scheduled for September 4, 2024. 

COUNSEL’S REQUEST 

Plaintiffs’ counsel Khashayar Eshraghi, Esq., asks to be relieved as counsel. 

LEGAL STANDARD 

California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, provides: 

“(a) Notice 

“A notice of motion and motion to be relieved as counsel under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) must be directed to the client and must be made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-051). 

“(b) Memorandum 

“Notwithstanding any other rule of court, no memorandum is required to be filed or served with a motion to be relieved as counsel. 

“(c) Declaration 

“The motion to be relieved as counsel must be accompanied by a declaration on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-052). The declaration must state in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(1). 

“(d) Service 

“The notice of motion and motion, the declaration, and the proposed order must be served on the client and on all other parties who have appeared in the case. The notice may be by personal service, electronic service, or mail. 

“(1) If the notice is served on the client by mail under Code of Civil Procedure section 1013, it must be accompanied by a declaration stating facts showing that either: 

“(A) The service address is the current residence or business address of the client; or 

“(B) The service address is the last known residence or business address of the client and the attorney has been unable to locate a more current address after making reasonable efforts to do so within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved. 

“(2) If the notice is served on the client by electronic service under Code of Civil Procedure section 1010.6 and rule 2.251, it must be accompanied by a declaration stating that the electronic service address is the client's current electronic service address. 

“As used in this rule, ‘current’ means that the address was confirmed within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved. Merely demonstrating that the notice was sent to the client's last known address and was not returned or no electronic delivery failure message was received is not, by itself, sufficient to demonstrate that the address is current. If the service is by mail, Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b) applies. 

“(e) Order 

“The proposed order relieving counsel must be prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-053) and must be lodged with the court with the moving papers. The order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. If no hearing date is presently scheduled, the court may set one and specify the date in the order. After the order is signed, a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case. The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court.” 

(Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362.) 

DISCUSSION 

Plaintiff's counsel
has completed, filed, and served the paperwork required to support the request
to be relieved as counsel.  Although the proofs of service appear to show service only on Plaintiff, Metro's counsel appeared at the hearing and confirmed receipt of the moving papers.  The Court
grants the motion.

CONCLUSION 

The Court GRANTS Khashayar Eshraghi, Esq.’s motion to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff Bridgette Stoval. Counsel
will be relieved upon filing proof of service on Plaintiff Bridgette Stoval of the Order
Granting Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel--Civil (Judicial Council
form MC-053).

Counsel is ordered to give notice of this ruling. 

Counsel is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days.

Case Number: 21STCV13117    Hearing Date: July 5, 2024    Dept: 28

Having considered the petitioning papers, the Court rules as follows. 

BACKGROUND 

On April 6, 2021, Plaintiffs Jazeer Keyes (“Keyes”), Tanya Davis (“Davis”), Jae’yon Wagner (“Jae’yon Wagner”), and Tamila Wagner (“Tamila Wagner”) filed this action against Defendants Lenita Sinna Dee Lewis (“Defendant”) and Does 1-100 for motor vehicle tort and general negligence. 

On April 27, 2021, the Court appointed Tamila Wagner to serve as Jae’yon Wagner’s guardian ad litem. 

On June 12, 2024, (1) Davis filed a petition for approval of the compromise of Keyes’s action and (2) Tamila Wagner filed a petition for approval of the compromise of Jae’yon Wagner’s action. 

PETITIONERS’ REQUESTS 

Petitioner Tamila Wagner asks the Court to approve the compromise of the pending action of minor Plaintiff Jae’yon Wagner. 

Petitioner Tanya Davis asks the Court to approve the compromise of the pending action of minor Plaintiff Jazeer Keyes. 

DISCUSSION 

A.   Davis’s petition to approve the compromise of Keyes’s action 

The Court denied Davis’s application to be appointed Keyes’s guardian ad litem, stating that a minor who (like Keyes) is 14 or older must submit the application for appointment of a guardian ad litem.  (See Code Civ. Proc., § 373, subd. (a).)  Keyes did not submit an application.  Davis has not shown that her status as Keyes’s parent authorizes her to settle Keyes’s claims.  Davis should provide the factual and legal basis (if any) for her authority to compromise the action on Keyes’s behalf.  (See, e.g., Prob. Code, § 3500, subd. (a).) 

In Section 18a of the petition, Petitioner states that “[t]here is a guardianship of the estate of the minor” in this case.  The statement appears to be incorrect.  If a guardianship of Keyes’s estate exists, Petitioner should specify the case in which a court created or oversees the guardianship of the estate. 

Petitioner has not provided a copy of the final Medi-Cal demand letter or letter agreement as Section 12b(4) of the petition requires. 

Petitioner has not provided an attachment with information about the financial institution into which the net settlement proceeds will be deposited as Section 18 of the petition requires. 

Counsel’s declaration states that the retainer agreement is attached to the declaration but it is not attached. 

B.   Petition to approve the compromise of Plaintiff Jae’yon Wagner’s action 

Section 11b(5) of the petition lists Jae’yon Wagner as an “other” plaintiff or claimant who is receiving settlement funds.  Petitioner presumably meant to list Keyes here. 

In Section 18a of the petition, Petitioner states that “[t]here is a guardianship of the estate of the minor” in this case.  The statement appears to be incorrect.  If a guardianship of Jae’yon Wagner’s estate exists, Petitioner should specify the case in which a court created or oversees the guardianship of the estate. 

Petitioner has not provided an attachment with information about the financial institution into which the net settlement proceeds will be deposited as Section 18 of the petition requires. 

Counsel’s declaration states that the retainer agreement is attached to the declaration but it is not attached. 

CONCLUSION 

The Court DENIES the petition to approve the compromise of minor Plaintiff Jazeer Keyes’s action filed by Petitioner Tanya Davis on June 12, 2024. 

The Court DENIES the petition to approve the compromise of minor Plaintiff Jae’yon Wagner’s action filed by Petitioner Tamila Wagner on June 12, 2024.

Petitioners are ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days.

Case Number: 21STCV38506    Hearing Date: July 5, 2024    Dept: 28

Having considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers, the Court rules as follows. 

BACKGROUND 

On October 19, 2021, Plaintiff Angela Park (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Erick Jarquin (“Defendant”) and Does 1-25 for motor vehicle tort. 

On April 13, 2022, Plaintiff filed a proof of service showing substituted service on Defendant of the summons, complaint, statement of damages, and other documents on December 1, 2021. 

On June 24, 2022, the clerk entered Defendant’s default. 

On April 3, 2023, Defendant filed a statement of damages dated October 19, 2021. 

On April 4, 2023, the Court dismissed the Doe defendants without prejudice at Plaintiff’s request. 

On September 13, 2023, the Court granted Plaintiff’s application for default judgment against Defendant and entered judgment for Plaintiff for $50,506.50. 

On May 21, 2024, Defendant filed a motion to set aside default judgment and default, to be heard on July 5, 2024.  On June 5, 2023, Plaintiff filed an opposition.  On July 1, 2024, Defendant filed a reply. 

No trial date is currently set. 

PARTIES’ REQUESTS 

Defendant asks the Court to set aside the default judgment and default. 

Plaintiff asks the Court to deny the motion. 

DEFENDANT’S REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE: 

 Denied

LEGAL STANDARD 

          Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (d), provides: 

“The court may, upon motion of the injured party, or its own motion, correct clerical mistakes in its judgment or orders as entered, so as to conform to the judgment or order directed, and may, on motion of either party after notice to the other party, set aside any void judgment or order.” 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (d).) 

          Code of Civil Procedure section 473.5 provides: 

“(a) When service of a summons has not resulted in actual notice to a party in time to defend the action and a default or default judgment has been entered against him or her in the action, he or she may serve and file a notice of motion to set aside the default or default judgment and for leave to defend the action. The notice of motion shall be served and filed within a reasonable time, but in no event exceeding the earlier of: (i) two years after entry of a default judgment against him or her; or (ii) 180 days after service on him or her of a written notice that the default or default judgment has been entered. 

“(b) A notice of motion to set aside a default or default judgment and for leave to defend the action shall designate as the time for making the motion a date prescribed by subdivision (b) of Section 1005, and it shall be accompanied by an affidavit showing under oath that the party’s lack of actual notice in time to defend the action was not caused by his or her avoidance of service or inexcusable neglect. The party shall serve and file with the notice a copy of the answer, motion, or other pleading proposed to be filed in the action. 

“(c) Upon a finding by the court that the motion was made within the period permitted by subdivision (a) and that his or her lack of actual notice in time to defend the action was not caused by his or her avoidance of service or inexcusable neglect, it may set aside the default or default judgment on whatever terms as may be just and allow the party to defend the action.” 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 473.5.) 

DISCUSSION 

A.   Defendant’s motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (d)   

Defendant contends the default is void and the Court must set it aside because the Court did not have proof that Plaintiff personally served a statement of damages on Defendant before entry of the default. 

On April 13, 2022, Plaintiff filed a proof of service showing substituted service on Defendant of the statement of damages (and other documents) on December 1, 2021.  The proof of service included a declaration of due diligence.  Defendant has not identified any deficiency in the proof of service.  Instead, Defendant appears to argue that a statement of damages must be served “personally.” 

“If a party has not appeared in the action, the statement [of damages] shall be served in the same manner as a summons.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 425.11, subd. (d)(1).)  A summons may be served by substituted service.  (See Code Civ. Proc., § 415.20.)  Therefore, Plaintiff properly served the statement of damages on Defendant before the clerk entered Defendant’s default. 

B.   Defendant’s motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 473.5 

Defendant has stated no facts supporting a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 473.5. 

The Court denies the motion. 

CONCLUSION 

The Court DENIES Defendant Erick Jarquin’s motion to set aside the default entered on June 24, 2022 and the default judgment entered on September 13, 2023. 

Moving party is ordered to give notice of this ruling. 

Moving party is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days.

Case Number: 21STCV40047    Hearing Date: July 5, 2024    Dept: 28

Having considered the petitioning papers, the Court rules as follows. 

BACKGROUND 

On November 1, 2021, Plaintiffs Pablo Quezada Santoyo, Maria Torres Lopez, Ashley Lopez, a minor, and Erik Quezada, a minor, by and through their guardian ad litem Maria Torres Lopez, filed this action against Defendants Yihe Qi and Does 1-50 for motor vehicle tort and general negligence. 

On February 18, 2022, the Court appointed Maria Torres Lopez to serve as Plaintiff Ashley Lopez’s guardian ad litem. 

On February 22, 2022, the Court appointed Maria Torres Lopez to serve as Plaintiff Erik Quezada’s guardian ad litem. 

On December 21, 2023, Plaintiffs filed a notice of settlement. 

On June 4, 2024, Petitioner Maria Torres Lopez (“Petitioner”) filed a petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Eric Quezada’s action. 

On June 13, 2024, Petitioner filed a petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Ashley Torres’s action. 

PETITIONER’S REQUESTS 

Petitioner asks the Court for expedited approval of the compromises of the actions of minor Plaintiffs Eric Quezada and Ashley Torres. 

DISCUSSION 

A.   Petition for expedited approval of the compromise of Plaintiff Eric Quezada’s action 

The Court finds that the petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Eric Quezada’s claims is complete and contains all necessary information.  The Court grants the petition and orders that the net settlement amount of $615.34 be paid or delivered to Maria Torres Lopez at 5678 Shull Street, Space 36, Bell, California 90201 on the terms and under the conditions specified in Probate Code sections 3401-3402, without bond. 

B.   Petition for expedited approval of the compromise of Plaintiff Ashley Torres’s action 

Section 13a(1) of the petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Ashley Torres's action states that total medical expenses were $7,197.00.  Section 13a(3) of the petition states that this amount was reduced by $3,782.66 as the result of negotiated, contractual, and statutory reductions.  This should mean that $3,414.34 (the difference between the total medical expenses and the amount of reductions) will be paid or reimbursed from the settlement proceeds.  However, Section 13a(4) of the petition states that only $3,250.00 of the medical expenses will be paid or reimbursed from the settlement proceeds.  As a result, $164.34 of the medical expenses are unaccounted for.  The Court cannot grant a petition to approve a minor’s compromise unless it shows that all medical expenses that have not been waived or reduced will be paid or reimbursed from the settlement proceeds. 

The Court denies the petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Ashley Torres’s claims without prejudice. 

CONCLUSION 

The Court GRANTS the petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Eric Quezada’s action filed by Petitioner Maria Torres Lopez on June 4, 2024. The Court orders the net settlement amount of $615.34 to be paid or delivered to Maria Torres Lopez at 5678 Shull Street, Space 36, Bell, California 90201 on the terms and under the conditions specified in Probate Code sections 3401-3402, without bond. 

The Court DENIES without prejudice the petition for expedited approval of the compromise of minor Plaintiff Ashley Torres’s action filed by Petitioner Maria Torres Lopez on June 13, 2024. 

Petitioner is ordered to give notice of this ruling. 

Petitioner is ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days. 

Case Number: 22STCV04853    Hearing Date: July 5, 2024    Dept: 28

Having considered the moving and opposition papers, the Court rules as follows. 

BACKGROUND 

On February 8, 2022, Plaintiffs Velveth Milena Ibarra Orozco (“Orozco”) and Oscar Canales Jule (“Jule”) filed this action against Defendants Samuel Fernando Baliez and Does 1-50 for motor vehicle tort and general negligence. 

On May 18, 2022, Defendant Samuel Vallez (erroneously sued as Samuel Fernandez Baliez) (“Defendant”) filed an answer. 

On May 10, 2024, Plaintiffs filed a motion to compel Defendant’s deposition and for sanctions, to be heard on June 7, 2024. On June 21, 2024, Defendant filed an opposition. The Court granted Defendant’s ex parte application to continue the hearing to July 5, 2024. 

Trial is currently scheduled for September 10, 2024. 

PARTIES’ REQUESTS 

Plaintiffs ask the Court to order Defendant to appear for a deposition. Plaintiffs also ask the Court to impose sanctions on Defendant. 

Defendant asks the Court to deny the motion. 

LEGAL STANDARD 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2025.450 provides in part: 

“(a) If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice. 

“(b) A motion under subdivision (a) shall comply with both of the following: 

“(1) The motion shall set forth specific facts showing good cause justifying the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice. 

“(2) The motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040, or, when the deponent fails to attend the deposition and produce the documents, electronically stored information, or things described in the deposition notice, by a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance. 

* * *

 “(g) (1) If a motion under subdivision (a) is granted, the court shall impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) in favor of the party who noticed the deposition and against the deponent or the party with whom the deponent is affiliated, unless the court finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust. . . .” 

(Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subds. (a), (b), (g)(1).) 

DISCUSSION 

Plaintiffs have served several notices for Defendant’s deposition.  Defendant served objections to some of the deposition notices.  On April 12, 2024, Defendant did not appear for his noticed deposition.  Plaintiffs move to compel Defendant’s deposition and for sanctions. 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2025.450, subdivision (b)(2), requires a motion to compel a party’s deposition to “be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040, or, when the deponent fails to attend the deposition and produce the documents, electronically stored information, or things described in the deposition notice, by a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (b)(2).)  Plaintiffs have not provided the required declaration. 

In addition, Defendant’s counsel report that Defendant is incarcerated.  Defendant’s counsel informed Plaintiffs’ counsel about Defendant’s incarceration on May 16, 2024 and June 5, 2024.  The Court will not compel Defendant’s deposition without additional information about Plaintiffs’ compliance with any requirements or procedures for conducting a deposition in what appears to be a Los Angeles County jail facility. 

The Court denies Plaintiffs’ motion and request for sanctions. 

CONCLUSION 

The Court DENIES the motion filed by Plaintiffs Velveth Milena Ibarra Orozco and Oscar Canales Jule to compel Defendant Samuel Vallez’s deposition and for sanctions. 

Moving parties are ordered to give notice of this ruling. 

Moving parties are ordered to file the proof of service of this ruling with the Court within five days.
