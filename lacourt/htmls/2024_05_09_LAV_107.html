DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22VECV01938    Hearing Date: May 9, 2024    Dept: 107

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

Creekside Shops, LLC,

 

         Plaintiff,

 

   v.

 

Hasan Group, LLC d/b/a Currying Flavors, et al.

 

         Defendants.

   Case Number             Department

   22VECV01938                    107

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] RULING RE:

Writ of Attachment

 

 

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

I. BACKGROUND

On January 17, 2017, the predecessor in interest to plaintiff Creekside Shops, LLC (“Plaintiff”) entered a lease agreement (the “Lease”) with defendants James O. Carter (“Carter”) and Richard Adams (“Adams”) for the lease of a certain commercial real property (the “Premises”). On December 17, 2018, Carter and Adams assigned the Lease to defendant Hasan Group, LLC (“Hasan Group”). On January 9, 2019, defendants Zia Hasan (“Zia”) and Shahana Hasan (“Shahana” and, together with Zia, the “Opposing Parties” and Opposing Parties, together with Carter and Adams, “Defendants”) executed a guaranty (the “Guaranty”) of the Lease.

 

In April 2020, Hasan Group stopped making rental payments due under the Lease. Plaintiff filed an unlawful detainer action against Hasan Group and Hasan Group surrendered possession of the Premises to Plaintiff in November 2020.

 

On November 9, 2022, Plaintiff initiated this lawsuit by filing a complaint (the “Complaint”) alleging causes of action for (1) breach of lease and (2) breach of guaranty.

 

On April 5, 2024, this court granted in part Plaintiff’s ex parte application for issuance of writs of attachment and right to attach orders against Opposing Parties or, in the alternative, for temporary protective orders by issuing temporary protective orders, which ordered Opposing Parties to not dispose of the proceeds of any sale of real property.

 

On April 9, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant application for a right to attach order and an order for the issuance of a writ of attachment (the “Application”). Hasan Group and Opposing Parties opposed the Application on April 19, 2024, Plaintiff replied to their opposition on April 29, 2019, and Opposing Parties filed a sur-reply on May 3, 2024.

 

II. DISCUSSION

 

Plaintiff seeks a right to attach order and writ of attachment in the amount of $496,666.43 against Opposing Parties on all interests in and monies received from the sale of a certain real property.

 

“Attachment is an ancillary or provisional remedy to aid the collection of a money demand by seizure of property in advance of trial or judgment as security for satisfaction of a judgment for the attaching party.” (Park v. NMSI, Inc. (2023) 96 Cal.App.5th 346, 353, quoting Burke v. Superior Court (1969) 71 Cal.2d 276, 279, fn. 3.)

 

“Upon the filing of the complaint or at any time thereafter, the plaintiff may apply pursuant to this article for a right to attach order and a writ of attachment by filing an application for the order and writ with the court in which the action is brought.” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 484.010.) 

   

A court shall consider the showing made by the parties, as well as the pleadings and other papers in the record. (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 484.090(a), (d).) A court shall issue a right to attach order if it finds all of the following:

 

(1) the claim upon which the attachment is based is one upon which an attachment may be issued,

 

(2) the plaintiff has established the probable validity of the claim upon which the attachment is based,

 

(3) the attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based, and

 

(4) the amount to be secured by the attachment is greater than zero. 

             

(Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 484.090, subds. (a)(1)–(a)(4).)  

  

“The Attachment Law statutes are subject to strict construction….” (Epstein v. Abrams (1997) 57 Cal.App.4th 1159, 1168.)  

 

  1. Procedural Requirements

 

1.     Service of Notice

 

At the times prescribed by California Code of Civil Procedure section 1005, subdivision (b), the defendant must be served with a copy of the summons and complaint, notice of application and hearing, and a copy of the application and supporting affidavits. (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 484.040.) 

 

The service of notice requirement has been met.

 

2.     California Code of Civil Procedure Section 484.020

 

The application for a right to attach order and a writ of attachment shall be executed under oath and must include (1) a statement showing that the attachment is sought to secure the recovery on a claim upon which an attachment may be issued; (2) a statement of the amount to be secured by the attachment; (3) a statement that the attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based; (4) a statement that the applicant has no information or belief that the claim is discharged or that the prosecution of the action is stayed in a proceeding under the Bankruptcy Act (11 U.S.C. section 101, et seq.); and (5) a description of the property to be attached under the writ of attachment and a statement that the plaintiff is informed and believes that such property is subject to attachment. (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 484.020.) 

 

The Application, having been filed on an approved Judicial Council form, contains the statements required by California Code of Civil Procedure section 484.020, which therefore satisfies those procedural requirements.

 

  1. Attachability of Claim

 

To issue a right to attach order, the court must find, inter alia, that “[t]he claim upon which the attachment is based is one upon which an attachment may be issued.” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 484.090, subd. (a)(1).) “Under the Attachment Law, whether or not the defendant appears in opposition, the plaintiff has the burden of proving [] that his claim is one upon which an attachment may be issued.” (Bank of America v. Salinas Nissan, Inc. (1989) 207 Cal.App.3d 260, 271.)

 

1.     California Code of Civil Procedure Section 483.010, Subdivision (a)

 

The court may only issue an attachment in an action on a claim for money, based on a contract, totaling at least $500.00 exclusive of costs, interests, and attorney fees. (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 483.010, subd. (a).)

 

Plaintiff’s breach of contract claims are based on the Lease and the Guaranty, both contracts, and total $387,899.51 exclusive of costs, interest, and attorneys’ fees. Thus, these requirements of California Code of Civil Procedure section 483.010, subdivision (a) are met by the Application.

 

2.     Security for Claim

 

“An attachment may not be issued on a claim which is secured by any interest in real property….” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 483.010, subd. (b).)

 

The Lease, which is attached to the Complaint, provides for the grant of a security interest in “all of Tenant's fixtures, machinery, equipment, furniture, furnishings, and the proceeds therefrom presently or hereafter owned by Tenant and located at the Premises and all after-acquired property of the same class and description.” The Guaranty does not provide for any security.

 

We find that the claims on which Plaintiff seeks to have the attachment issued are not secured by any interest in real property.

 

3.     “Trade, Business, or Profession”

 

When the defendant is a natural person, “an attachment may be issued only on a claim which arises out of the conduct by the defendant of a trade, business, or profession.” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 483.010, subd. (c).) “[Those terms may be found to encompass almost any activity engaged in for profit with frequency and continuity.” (Kadison, Pfaelzer, Woodard, Quinn & Rossi v. Wilson (1987) 197 Cal.App.3d 1, 4.) In determining whether a guarantor is engaged in the “business” of the primary obligor, a court must determine whether “the guarantee of the primary obligor's debt sued upon is part and parcel of an activity which occupies the time, attention and effort of the guarantor for the purpose of livelihood or profit on a continuing basis.” (Nakasone v. Randall (1982) 129 Cal.App.3d 757, 764, quoting Advance Transformer Co. v. Superior Court (1974) 44 Cal.App.3d 127, 144.)

 

Opposing Parties are both natural persons. (Application pp. 1, 4.) One of the claims upon which Plaintiff seeks to have the attachment issued is for breach of the Lease, which is a “Shopping Center Lease” and which restricts Defendants use of the Premises to only the operation of a restaurant in Section 6.01(a). However, Opposing Parties are only guarantors of the Lease or signed the assignment thereof in their capacity as Hasan Group’s manager. The other claim upon which Plaintiff seeks to have the attachment issued is for breach of the Guaranty, through which Opposing Parties guaranteed Hasan Group’s performance under the Lease. We consequently look for additional evidence that entering the Guaranty was “part and parcel” of an activity Opposing Parties engaged in “for profit with frequency and continuity.”

 

Plaintiff submits no evidence and this court does not find evidence in the record to show that Opposing Parties were frequently involved continuously in the operations of Hasan Group and in Hasan Group’s undertaking of the shopping center lease or operations as a business and restaurant. Thus, Plaintiff has failed to meet its burden of showing that its claims against Opposing Parties arises out of their conduct of a trade, business, or profession. Therefore, Plaintiff has also failed to meet its burden of showing that its claims upon which it seeks to have the attachment issued are ones for which an attachment may be issued.

 

Because this court may not issue a right to attach order without having first found that the claim upon which the attachment is based is one for which an attachment may be issued, we do not reach the probable validity of the claim, the purpose of the attachment, or the amount to be secured.

 

Accordingly, this court DENIES the Application.
