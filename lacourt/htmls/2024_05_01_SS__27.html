Case Number: 21STCV41745    Hearing Date: May 1, 2024    Dept: 27

Hon. Lee S. Arian

Department 27

Tentative Ruling

 

Hearing Date:                    5/1/2024 at 1:30 p.m.

Case No./Name.:         21STCV41745 CHRISTIAN BRAVO, AN INDIVIDUAL vs CANDY MARTINEZ

Motion:                              MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

Moving Party:                 Plaintiff’s Counsel Timothy M. Ghobrial

Responding Party:      Unopposed

Notice:                                Sufficient

 

Ruling:                               MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL IS GRANTED.

 

Background

 

Attorney Timothy M. Ghobrial represents Plaintiff. Ghobrial moves to be relieved as counsel, citing an irremediable breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. No opposition has been filed.

 

Legal Standard

 

The Court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915; People v. Prince (1968) 268 Cal.App.2d 398, 403-407.)

 

A motion to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Council Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion), MC-052 (Declaration), and MC-053 (Proposed Order). (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, subds. (a), (c), (e).) The requisite forms must be served “on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case.” (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362, subd. (d).)

 

Analysis and Conclusion

 

Ghobrial has filed Judicial Council Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion), MC-052 (Declaration), and MC-053 (Proposed Order). Ghobrial seeks to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff on the grounds that there has been a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. The Court finds this to be proper grounds for withdrawal. (See Estate of Falco (1987) 188 Cal.App.3d 1004, 1014 (a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship is grounds for allowing the attorney to withdraw).)

 

The Court notes that the next hearing is an OSC: Failure to File Default Judgment set for 6/25/24, which should be sufficient time for Plaintiff to retain new counsel. Alternatively, if Plaintiff or new counsel needs additional time to prepare for trial, sufficient time exists for Plaintiff to take action to seek a continuance of the trial. Thus, the present motion to be relieved as counsel is GRANTED effective upon service on Plaintiff.

Responding Party: PLAINTIFF

Ruling: MOTION TO COMPEL PLAINTIFF’S DEPOSITION AND FOR SANCTIONS IS GRANTED. 

Background 

On December 15, 2021, Plaintiff filed the present premises liability case. On June 22, 2023, Defendant Tarzana Treatment Centers served the initial deposition notice for Plaintiff to appear for a deposition on August 16, 2023. Defendant then re-noticed Plaintiff's deposition numerous times thereafter, with the final deposition notice served on February 12, 2024, for a deposition on February 23, 2024. No objection was filed. Plaintiff did not show up to the deposition and a certificate of non-appearance was obtained. Defendant now moves the Court to compel Plaintiff to sit for her deposition. 

Legal Standard¿and Analysis 

 

Any party may obtain any discovery of information, documents, land, property, or electronically stored information so long as the discoverable matter is not privileged, is relevant to the subject matter and can lead one to admissible evidence.¿(Code Civ. Proc. § 2017.010.) 

 

Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450¿provides in pertinent part the following: 

 

“(a) If, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection under Section 2025.410, fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent's attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice. 

 

(b) A motion under subdivision (a) shall comply with both of the following: 

 

(1) The motion shall set forth specific facts showing good cause justifying the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice. 

 

(2) The motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040¿or, when the deponent fails to attend the deposition and produce the documents, electronically stored information, or things described in the deposition notice, by a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance. 

 

Analysis 

 

The initial deposition notice was served almost a year ago. The deposition was rescheduled numerous times, with the latest notice served on February 12, 2024, for a deposition scheduled for February 23, 2024. No objection was served, and Plaintiff did not attend the deposition. After Plaintiff failed to attend her deposition, Defendant inquired and met and conferred with Plaintiff’s counsel. Defendant has met its meet and confer requirement. Good cause has been shown as the document requests in the subpoena are all related to Plaintiff’s causes of action. Defendant has met all the requirements for a motion to compel deposition. The contention that Plaintiff’s counsel could not contact Plaintiff and have her appear for her deposition is no excuse to deprive Defendant of vital evidence to form a defense. If Plaintiff does not wish to litigate the case, the proper way is to dismiss the action instead of evading the deposition. Accordingly, Defendant’s motion to compel deposition is GRANTED. Plaintiff is hereby ORDERED to sit for his deposition within 20 days of today. 

 

Defendant requests sanctions in the amount of $3,860. The Court exercises discretion and lowers the amount to $2,000. Since Plaintiff’s counsel has shown attempts to communicate and work with Defendant’s counsel to have Plaintiff’s deposition take place but was unable to secure Plaintiff's attendance, the sanction is to be imposed against Plaintiff only. Plaintiff is ORDERED to pay sanctions of $2,000 to Defendant in 4 equal payments of $500, with the first payment due within 20 days of today’s date and each other payment due within 30 days thereafter until paid in full.

Case No./Name.: 22STCV06848 JUAN GABRIEL HERRERA VENEGAS vs TGN CONSTRUCTION, INC 

Motion Name: Motion for Leave to Intervene 

Moving Party: State National Insurance Company 

Responding Party: Unopposed 

Notice: Sufficient 

 

Ruling: MOTION FOR LEAVE TO INTERVENE IS GRANTED. 

 

 

I. INTRODUCTION 

 

On February 24, 2022, Plaintiff filed this action against Defendants, TGN Construction, Inc. (“TGN”) and Juliette Dolores Herbert, individually and as Trustee under the Juliette Dolores Herbert Revocable Living Trust (sued as “Juliette D. Herbert Trust”), for general negligence and premises liability. Defendant TGN is a suspended corporation and on January 5, 2023, TGN’s insurance company, State National Insurance Company (“SNIC”), filed a prior motion to intervene on behalf of TGN against Plaintiff’s Complaint. On May 31, 2023, the Court granted the motion. On February 13, 2024, Defendant Juliette D. Herbert Trust served TGN and SNIC a cross complaint for indemnity. SNIC now moves the Court to intervene on behalf of TGN against Defendant Herbert Trust’s cross complaint. No opposition was filed. 

¿ 

II. LEGAL STANDARD 

 

“A¿nonparty shall petition the court for leave to intervene by noticed motion or ex parte application. The petition shall include a copy of the proposed complaint in intervention or answer in intervention and set forth the grounds upon which intervention rests.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 387, subd. (c).) 

¿ 

“(1)¿The court shall, upon timely application, permit a nonparty to intervene in the action or proceeding if either of the following conditions is satisfied: 

 

(A)¿A provision of law confers an unconditional right to  intervene. 

 

(B)¿The person seeking intervention claims an interest relating to the property or transaction that is the subject of the action and that person is so situated that the disposition of the action may impair or impede that person’s ability to protect that interest, unless that person’s interest is adequately represented by one or more of the existing parties. 

 

(2)¿The court may, upon timely application, permit a nonparty to intervene in the action or proceeding if the person has an interest in the matter in litigation, or in the success of either of the parties, or an interest against both.”¿(Code Civ. Proc. § 387, subd. (d).) 

 

“Pursuant to section 387 the trial court has discretion to permit a nonparty to intervene where the following factors are met: (1) the proper procedures have been followed; (2) the nonparty has a direct and immediate interest in the action; (3) the intervention will not enlarge the issues in the litigation; and (4) the reasons for the intervention outweigh any opposition by the parties presently in the action.”¿(Reliance Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (2000) 84 Cal.App.4th 383, 386.) 

¿ 

III. DISCUSSION 

 

SNIC seeks leave to intervene because TGN has been suspended as a corporation and cannot defend or otherwise participate in this action.¿(See Timberline, Inc. v. Jaisinghani (1997) 54 Cal.App.4th 1361, 1365-66 [a suspended corporation is disqualified from exercising any right, power, or privilege, including prosecuting or defending an action, or appealing a judgment].) 

¿ 

Here, SNIC meets all the Reliance Ins. Co. factors.¿First, the proper procedures have been followed.¿SNIC has petitioned the Court by noticed motion and included a copy of the proposed answer-in-intervention. 

 

Second, SNIC has a direct and immediate interest in this action.¿SNIC insured TGN when the alleged accident occurred.¿Should Cross Complainant enter a default judgment against TGN, Cross Complainant could then proceed against SNIC under Insurance Code section 11580, which permits a judgment creditor to proceed directly against any liability insurance covering the defendant. Thus, intervention is appropriate because SNIC may be required to satisfy any default judgment entered against TGN. 

 

Case law also supports SNIC’s right to intervene.¿“An insurer’s right to intervene in an action against the insured, for personal injury or property damage, arises as a result of Insurance Code section 11580.¿Section 11580 provides that a judgment creditor may proceed directly against any liability insurance covering the defendant, and obtain satisfaction of the judgment up to the amount of the policy limits.¿ Thus, where the insurer may be subject to a direct action under Insurance Code section 11580 by a judgment creditor who has or will obtain a default judgment in a third party action against the insured, intervention is appropriate.”¿ (Reliance Ins. Co., supra, 84 Cal.App.4th at pp. 386-387.)¿¿¿ 

¿ 

Third, intervention will not enlarge the scope of issues in this litigation.¿As reflected in its proposed answer-in-intervention, SNIC seeks only to litigate liability and damage issues as they relate to Defendant TGN.¿Intervention is appropriate because SNIC’s proposed answer is limited to issues raised by the pleadings.¿¿ 

¿ 

Fourth, the benefits of allowing SNIC to intervene outweigh any opposition by the parties presently in the action. Intervention will conserve judicial resources and expediency by eliminating the need for a separate action between Cross Complainant and SNIC. For the reasons stated above, denial of intervention would prejudice SNIC.¿There is no opposition to this motion.¿¿¿ 

¿ 

Additionally, SNIC requests leave to appear at trial as “TGN Construction, Inc.” rather than “State National Insurance Company, Inc.” As stated in the Court’s May 31, 2023 order, this decision is better suited for resolution by the trial court to be raised by a motion in limine. 

 

IV. CONCLUSION 

 

State National Insurance Company’s motion for leave to intervene is GRANTED. 

 

The request to appear at trial in the name of its insured, Defendant TGN Construction, Inc. is deferred for ruling by the trial court.¿ The issue may be raised pursuant to a motion in limine.

Case No./Name.:      22STCV27123 COREY BROWN vs EGL PROPERTIES

Motion:                    MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

Moving Party:           Plaintiff’s Counsel Nia Gamble

Responding Party:    Unopposed

Notice:                     Insufficient

 

Ruling:                     MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL IS DENIED.

 

Background

 

Attorney Nia Gamble represents Plaintiff. Gamble moves to be relieved as counsel, citing an irremediable breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. No opposition has been filed.

 

Legal Standard

 

The Court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915; People v. Prince (1968) 268 Cal.App.2d 398, 403-407.)

 

A motion to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Council Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion), MC-052 (Declaration), and MC-053 (Proposed Order). (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, subds. (a), (c), (e).) The requisite forms must be served “on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case.” (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362, subd. (d).)

 

Analysis and Conclusion

 

Gamble has filed Judicial Council Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion), MC-052 (Declaration), and MC-053 (Proposed Order). Gamble seeks to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff on the grounds that there has been a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. The Court finds this to be proper grounds for withdrawal. (See Estate of Falco (1987) 188 Cal.App.3d 1004, 1014 (a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship is grounds for allowing the attorney to withdraw).)

 

The Court is unclear whether Plaintiff has been properly served. Counsel states that she confirmed Plaintiff’s current address within the past 30 days through a TLO background search. However, no information has been provided about what a TLO background search entails or how counsel was able to confirm Plaintiff's address through such a search. Moreover, a copy of the search results was not submitted for the Court to assess the reliability of that search. Consequently, the present motion is denied.

Moving Party:           Defendant’s counsel Robert W. Walters

Responding Party:    Unopposed

Notice:                     Sufficient

 

Ruling:                     MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL IS DENIED.

 

Background

 

Attorney Robert W. Walters represents Defendant Ashes to Ashes Corporation. Walters moves to be relieved as counsel, citing a breach of retainer agreement. No opposition has been filed.

 

Legal Standard

 

The Court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915; People v. Prince (1968) 268 Cal.App.2d 398, 403-407.)

 

A motion to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Council Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion), MC-052 (Declaration), and MC-053 (Proposed Order). (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, subds. (a), (c), (e).) The requisite forms must be served “on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case.” (Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362, subd. (d).)

 

Analysis and Conclusion

 

Walters has filed Judicial Council Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion), MC-052 (Declaration), and MC-053 (Proposed Order). Walters seeks to be relieved as counsel for Defendant on the basis that there is a breach of the retainer agreement. The Court finds this to be proper grounds for withdrawal. Counsel was able to confirm client’s current address within the past 30 days through phone call. Counsel also properly served all Parties in the action.

 

However, the Court notes that the next hearing is an FSC set for 5/14/24, which is only 13 days away and would be insufficient time for Defendant to retain new counsel and accordingly withdrawal at this time would be prejudicial or would disrupt the orderly process of justice. Thus, the present motion is DENIED.
