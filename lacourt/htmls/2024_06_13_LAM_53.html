RESPONDING PARTY:        Plaintiff Haryati T.S. Sentoso

Motion for Summary Judgment or, in the Alternative, Summary Adjudication

Defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso (“Defendants”) filed the pending motion for summary judgment or, in the alternative, summary adjudication against plaintiff Haryati T.S. Sentoso (“Plaintiff”) on June 12, 2023.

On May 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed an opposition to Defendants’ motion, representing that “this case has been settled” and that the parties would be “submitting a Stipulated Judgment in this case consistent with the terms of the Settlement Agreement.”  (Opp., pp. 1:24, 2:1-2.)  Defendants did not (1) file a reply disputing this representation, or (2) take this motion off calendar as moot.  Moreover, the parties have not filed a Stipulated Judgment or Notice of Settlement of Entire Case (Judicial Council form CM-200) with the court.

Thus, the court finds that it is appropriate, and exercises its discretion, to continue the hearing on Defendants’ motion to allow the parties to file a stipulated judgment or notice of settlement pursuant to the terms of their settlement agreement.

            The court orders that the hearing on defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso’s motion for summary judgment or, in the alternative, summary adjudication is continued to July 25, 2024, at 10:00 a.m., in Department 53.

            The court orders defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

The court considered the moving papers and opposing declaration filed in connection with this motion.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff Angela Zoreh Farsad Nezhad (a/k/a Angela Farsad) (“Plaintiff”) filed the pending motion to compel responses to special interrogatories on February 26, 2024, moving the court for an order (1) compelling defendant Alliance Health Choice, Inc. (“Defendant”) to serve responses to Plaintiff’s Special Interrogatories, Set One, and (2) awarding monetary sanctions in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant in the amount of $2,460.

On February 28, 2024, the court issued an order (1) granting Plaintiff’s ex parte application, (2) ordering that the hearing on Plaintiff’s motion to compel Defendant’s responses to Plaintiff’s special interrogatories was advanced from November 19, 2024 to May 6, 2024, and (3) ordering Plaintiff to give notice of its February 28, 2024 order.  (Feb. 28, 2024 Order, pp. 1:25-2:1, 2:10.)  However, Plaintiff did not file a proof of service with the court establishing that Plaintiff gave Defendant and all other parties who have appeared in this action notice of the May 6, 2024 hearing date on this motion.  Moreover, on May 3, 2024, counsel for Defendant filed a declaration stating that he “missed this motion completely” and did not discover its existence until that week.  (Sabzevar Decl., ¶ 5.)  The court acknowledges that Defendant’s counsel further stated that it was not his intention to state that Plaintiff’s counsel was attempting to hide this motion.  (Ibid.)  However, this declaration does not state that Plaintiff gave Defendant notice of the May 6, 2024 hearing date on this motion.

On May 6, 2024, the court issued an order (1) noting that Plaintiff did not file a proof of service of the court’s February 28, 2024 order advancing the hearing date on the pending motion, and (2) exercising its discretion to continue the hearing on the motion to June 13, 2024, to ensure that Defendant received notice of the hearing date on this motion.  (May 6, 2024 Order, p. 2:2-8.)  The court further ordered Plaintiff to give notice of this ruling to Defendant and to all other parties who have appeared in this action, and to file a proof of service of the notice of ruling with the court no later than May 13, 2024.  (Id., p. 2:13-17; May 6, 2024 Minute Order, p. 1.)

Plaintiff has not filed a notice of ruling and proof of service of the notice of ruling with the court.  Thus, it appears that Plaintiff did not give Defendant and all other parties who have appeared in this action notice of the June 13, 2024 hearing date on this motion as ordered by the court.

The court therefore finds that it is appropriate, and exercises its discretion, to continue the hearing on Plaintiff’s motion to compel responses to special interrogatories to ensure that Defendant receives notice of the hearing date on the motion.

ORDER

            The court orders that the hearing on plaintiff Angela Zoreh Farsad Nezhad’s motion to compel responses to special interrogatories is continued to July 22, 2024, at 10:00 a.m., in Department 53.

The court orders plaintiff Angela Zoreh Farsad Nezhad (1) to give notice of this ruling to defendant Alliance Health Choice, Inc. and to all other parties who have appeared in this action, and (2) to file with the court the notice of ruling and a proof of service of the notice of ruling establishing that defendant Alliance Health Choice, Inc. and all other parties who have appeared in this action have been served with the notice of ruling no later than June 20, 2024.  

IT IS SO ORDERED.

RESPONDING PARTY:       Unopposed

(1)   Application to File Confidential Declaration Under Seal

(2)   Motion for Determination of Good Faith Settlement

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with the application to seal and the motion for determination of good faith settlement.  No opposition papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

Defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis LLC, d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis (“Defendant”) moves the court for an order (1) filing under seal the “Confidential Declaration of Rachel C. Bekerman in Support of Defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis LLC, d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis’ Motion for Determination of Good Faith Settlement,” filed on May 7, 2024, and (2) determining that the settlement entered into by and between Defendant, on the one hand, and plaintiffs Drena M. Humphries, by and through her successor-in-interest Allen Humphries, Allen Humphries, George Humphries, and John Humphries (“Plaintiffs”), on the other hand, was made in good faith pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure sections 877 and 877.6.  In the interest of efficiency, the court discusses Defendant’s two motions together.

First, the court grants Defendant’s application to seal.

Generally, court records are presumed to be open unless confidentiality is required by law.¿ (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 2.550, subd. (c).)¿ If the presumption of access applies, the court may order that a record be filed under seal “if it expressly finds facts that establish: (1) There exists an overriding interest that overcomes the right of public access to the record; (2) The overriding interest supports sealing the record; (3) A substantial probability exists that the overriding interest will be prejudiced if the record is not sealed; (4) The proposed sealing is narrowly tailored; and (5) No less restrictive means exist to achieve the overriding interest.”¿ (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 2.550, subd. (d).)¿¿ 

The court finds, as to the amount of the settlement disclosed on page 2, line 15 of the declaration of Rachel Bekerman and sections 4, 10, 20, 23, 27, 40, 43, 116, and 118 of the death certificate of plaintiff Drena Marie Humphries, attached as Exhibit C to the declaration of Rachel Bekerman, that (1) there exists an overriding interest that overcomes the right of public access to the record because (i) the declaration sets forth the amount of the parties’ settlement, which the parties have agreed to keep confidential, and (ii) the death certificate includes personal information relating to plaintiff Drena Humphries, (2) the overriding interest supports sealing the record, (3) a substantial probability exists that the overriding interest will be prejudiced if the record is not sealed, (4) the proposed sealing is narrowly tailored since Defendant requests that the court seal only the settlement amount disclosed in the declaration and certain sections of the death certificate, and (5) no less restrictive means exist to achieve the overriding interest.  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 2.550, subd. (d); Bekerman Decl., ¶ 4-5.)  The court therefore grants Defendant’s application to seal.

Second, the court finds that Defendant has shown that the parties’ settlement was entered into in good faith.  

“[Code of Civil Procedure] Section 877.6 was enacted by the Legislature in 1980 to establish a statutory procedure for determining if a settlement by an alleged joint tortfeasor has been entered into in good faith and to provide a bar to claims of other alleged joint tortfeasors for equitable contribution or partial or comparative indemnity when good faith is shown.”¿ (IRM Corp. v. Carlson (1986) 179 Cal.App.3d 94, 104.)¿ On noticed motion, “[a]ny party to an action wherein it is alleged that two or more parties are joint tortfeasors or co-obligors on a contract debt shall be entitled to a hearing on the issue of the good faith of a settlement entered into by the plaintiff . . . and one or more alleged tortfeasors or co-obligors . . . .”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 877.6, subd. (a).)  “The party asserting the lack of good faith shall have the burden of proof on that issue.”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 877.6, subd. (d).)¿¿ 

When a motion for determination of good faith settlement is uncontested, a “barebones” motion that sets forth the ground of good faith, accompanied by a declaration which sets forth a brief background of the case, is sufficient to support a good faith determination.¿ (City of Grand Terrace v. Superior Court (1987) 192 Cal.App.3d 1251, 1261.)¿¿¿¿ 

The court has not received opposition papers from any other party involved in this action.  The court finds that Defendant’s motion and the supporting declaration of Rachel C. Bekerman set forth (1) the ground of good faith of the parties’ settlement, including because the parties reached settlement after several discussions and arms-length negotiations between February and April 2024, and (2) a brief background of the case.  (Bekerman Decl., ¶¶ 3-4, 8-9, 11-17; Mot., pp. 7:23-9:7.)  The court also recognizes that counsel for Plaintiffs, defendant Niceto Lopez, M.D., and defendants Sunbridge Hallmark Health Services, LLC d/b/a Playa Del Rey Center and NewGen Operations Management, LLC entered into a stipulation in which the parties agreed that the settlement agreement between Defendant and Plaintiffs was entered into in good faith within the meaning of Code of Civil Procedure section 877.6.  (Bekerman Decl., Ex. A, pp. 2:14-19, 3-4.)  The court finds that Defendant’s motion and supporting evidence are sufficient to support a good faith determination and therefore grants Defendant’s motion.  (City of Grand Terrace, supra, 192 Cal.App.3d at p. 1261.)  

 

ORDER

            The court grants defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis, LLC d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis’s application to seal.

            The court orders that the unredacted version of the “Confidential Declaration of Rachel C. Bekerman in Support of Defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis LLC, d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis’ Motion for Determination of Good Faith Settlement,” lodged with the court on or about May 7, 2024, shall be filed under seal.

            The court orders defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis, LLC d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis to file a revised redacted version of the “Confidential Declaration of Rachel C. Bekerman in Support of Defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis LLC, d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis’ Motion for Determination of Good Faith Settlement” that identifies it as “Public-Redacts materials from conditionally sealed record.”

            Pursuant to California Rules of Court, rule 2.551, subdivision (e), the court directs the clerk to file this order, maintain the records ordered sealed in a secure manner, and clearly identify the records as sealed by this order.¿¿¿¿¿ 

The court grants defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis, LLC d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis’s motion for determination of good faith settlement.

            The court determines that the settlement entered into by and between defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis, LLC d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis, on the one hand, and plaintiffs Drena M. Humphries, by and through her successor-in-interest Allen Humphries, Allen Humphries, George Humphries, and John Humphries, on the other hand, is in good faith within the meaning of Code of Civil Procedure section 877.6, subdivision (a).

All other joint tortfeasors or co-obligors are barred from any further claims against settling defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis, LLC d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis for equitable comparative contribution, or partial or comparative indemnity, based on comparative negligence or comparative fault.¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 877.6, subd. (c).)¿¿ 

 

            The court orders defendant 300 S. Robertson Dialysis, LLC d/b/a U.S. Renal Care Westside Dialysis to give notice of this ruling.
