DEPARTMENT 53 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Tentative rulings are sometimes, but not always, posted. The purpose of posting a tentative ruling is to to help focus the argument. The posting of a tentative ruling is not an invitation for the filing of additional papers shortly before the hearing.


Case Number: 18STCV08570    Hearing Date: September 20, 2024    Dept: 53

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles – Central District

Department 53

 

 

anthony aiello , et al.;

 

Plaintiffs,

 

 

vs.

 

 

sommer ray beaty , et al.;

 

Defendants.

Case No.:

18STCV08570

 

 

Hearing Date:

September 20, 2024

 

 

Time:

10:00 a.m.

 

 

 

[tentative] Order RE:

 

defendant’s motion to compel further responses to form interrogatories and special interrogatories

 

 

MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant Sommer Ray Beaty

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Unopposed

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Form Interrogatories and Special Interrogatories

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this motion. 

The court did not consider the “Declaration of Michael P. Green re: Motion to Compel Further Responses to Interrogatories,” filed by counsel for plaintiffs Anthony Aiello and Anthony’s Worldwide Productions, LLC on September 18, 2024, because it was not filed at least nine court days before the hearing on this motion.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005, subd. (b).)

DISCUSSION

Defendant Sommer Ray Beaty (“Defendant”) moves the court for an order (1) compelling plaintiffs Anthony Aiello (“Aiello”) and Anthony’s Worldwide Productions, LLC (“Anthony’s Worldwide”) (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) to serve further responses to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories, numbers 8.2, subdivision (c), 8.3-8.5, and 12.1, subdivision (a); (2) compelling Plaintiffs to serve further responses to Defendant’s Special Interrogatories, numbers 1-3, 10-12, and 30; and (3) awarding monetary sanctions in favor of Defendant and against Plaintiffs in the amount of $3,400.

The court finds that Defendant has not shown that she served Plaintiffs with (1) the moving papers filed in connection with this motion, and (2) notice of the advanced hearing date on this motion.

First, Defendant did not file a proof of service of the moving papers on Plaintiffs “no later than five court days before the time appointed for the hearing.”[1]  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 3.1300, subd. (c).)  Instead, Defendant filed a proof of service of the motion on September 19, 2024, i.e., one court day before the hearing.

Second, the proof of service of the moving papers on Plaintiffs filed by Defendant on September 19, 2024 is defective and therefore invalid.  Specifically, the proof of service states that the moving papers were served on counsel for Plaintiffs “by internet / electronic mail[,]” but does not state the email address on which the papers were served.  (Sep. 19, 2024 Proof of Service, pp. 2:6-25, 3:1-3.)  Thus, the proof of service is incomplete and does not establish that Defendant served Plaintiffs with this motion.

Third, the proof of service of the notice of ruling providing Plaintiffs with notice that the court granted Defendant’s ex parte application to advance the hearing on this motion is similarly defective and therefore invalid.  Specifically, the proof of service of the notice of ruling states that it was served on counsel for Plaintiffs by email, but it does not state the email address on which the notice was served.  (Sep. 10, 2024 Notice of Ruling, p. 3:6-21.)  Thus, the proof of service is incomplete and does not establish that Defendant gave Plaintiffs notice of the new hearing date on this motion.

The court notes that trial is scheduled to begin in this action on October 30, 2024.  The court is unable to continue the hearing on this motion to a date before the discovery motion cutoff date passes.  The court therefore denies Defendant’s motion to compel Plaintiffs’ further responses to Defendant’s discovery.

 

ORDER

            The court denies defendant Sommer Ray Beaty’s motion to compel further responses to form interrogatories and special interrogatories.

            The court orders defendant Sommer Ray Beaty to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

 

DATED:  September 20, 2024

Motion for Leave to File First Amended Complaint

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this motion.  No opposition papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff Kristin Davidkhanian (“Plaintiff”) moves the court for an order granting her leave to file a First Amended Complaint to add a cause of action for failure to produce her personnel file in violation of Labor Code section 1198.5, the facts giving rise to which Plaintiff discovered in April 2024.  (Notice of Mot., p. 3:22-25; Banayan Decl., ¶¶ 4-5.)

The court finds that (1) it is in furtherance of justice to allow Plaintiff to amend her complaint to add a cause of action for the alleged failure to produce her personnel file in violation of Labor Code section 1198.5, and (2) defendants Advanced Skin Care Center, Inc., Elena Janev, and Cyrus Rafael Lavian, M.D., have not shown that they will be misled or unduly prejudiced by the amendment.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (a); Mac v. Minassian (2022) 76 Cal.App.5th 510, 519 [“‘ “California courts ‘have a policy of great liberality in allowing amendments at any stage of the proceeding so as to dispose of cases upon their substantial merits where the authorization does not prejudice the substantial rights of others.’  [Citation.]” ’”].)  The court therefore grants Plaintiff’s motion.

ORDER

            The court grants plaintiff Kristin Davidkhanian’s motion for leave to file first amended complaint.

            The court orders plaintiff Kristin Davidkhanian to file the First Amended Complaint, in the form attached as Exhibit A to the motion for leave to file first amended complaint, within 5 days of the date of this order.

            The court orders plaintiff Kristin Davidkhanian to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
