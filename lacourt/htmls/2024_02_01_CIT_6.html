DEPARTMENT 6 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22PSCV00248    Hearing Date: February 1, 2024    Dept: 6

Plaintiff Cindy Lo’s Request for Entry of Default Judgment 

Defendant: Treeium Inc. 

TENTATIVE RULING

            Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment is DENIED without prejudice.           

BACKGROUND

            This is a breach of contract and fraud action. On March 16, 2022, plaintiff Cindy Lo (Plaintiff) filed this action against defendant Treeium Inc. (Defendant) and Does 1 through 100, alleging causes of action for fraud, breach of contract, breach of implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing, and negligent hiring, supervision and retention. On December 18, 2023, the Court struck Defendant’s answer to the complaint and entered default against Defendant for failure to retain counsel. On January 23, 2024, Plaintiff filed a request for entry of default judgment.           

LEGAL STANDARD

Code of Civil Procedure section 585 permits entry of a default judgment after a party has failed to timely respond or appear. (Code Civ. Proc., § 585.) A party seeking judgment on the default by the court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) a proposed form of judgment; (6) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under Code of Civil Procedure section 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (7) exhibits as necessary; and (8) a request for attorneys’ fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1800.) 

ANALYSIS

Plaintiff seeks default judgment against Defendants in the total amount of $66,962.66, including $50,500.00 in damages, $15,917.50 in interest, $0.00 in attorney fees, and $545.16 in costs. The Court finds there are a few problems with Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment. First, Plaintiff’s damage calculations do not add up. Plaintiff seeks to recover $4,000.00 from the first services agreement with Defendant, which is dated June 23, 2020. (Lo Decl., Ex. A.) That agreement covered Defendant providing a floor plan and submitting it for approval. (Lo Decl., ¶ 3.) Plaintiff states in her declaration that Defendant did just that. (Lo Decl., ¶ 7.) 

Additionally, the bulk of the allegations of the complaint focus on Defendant’s failures to perform under the August 4, 2020 agreement, not the June 23, 2020 agreement. (See Compl., ¶¶ 5-14.) As such, Defendant did not breach the June 23, 2020 agreement, and the $4,000.00 paid thereunder is not recoverable. “To prevail on a cause of action for breach of contract, the plaintiff must prove (1) the contract, (2) the plaintiff's performance of the contract or excuse for nonperformance, (3) the defendant's breach, and (4) the resulting damage to the plaintiff. [Citation].” (See Richman v. Hartley (2014) 224 Cal.App.4th 1182, 1186, emphasis added.) 

As for the August 4, 2020 agreement, the Court finds that the evidence at most supports $46,050.00 in payments made to Defendant. (Lo Decl., Exs. E-L.) 

Second, Plaintiff must provide a certain date for calculating prejudgment interest, which would be the date there was both a breach and a liquidated claim. (Civ. Code § 3287, subd. (a).; Maurice L. Bein, Inc. v. Housing Authority of City of Los Angeles (1958) 157 Cal.App.2d 670, 762-763; County of Solano v. Lionsgate Corp. (2005) 126 Cal.App.4th 741, 753; see also F. Prejudgment Interest, Cal. Prac. Guide Civ. Trials & Ev. Ch. 17-F, ¶ 17:1093 [“Interest commences on the first day there exists both a breach and a liquidated claim. [Citations].”]) Plaintiff’s interest calculation appears to be based on the dates Plaintiff made payments rather than the date Defendant breached the August 4, 2020 agreement. (See Lo Decl. re Interest.) 

Third, Defendant did not dismiss any of the doe Defendants or alternatively submit an application for a several and separate judgment. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1800, subd. (a)(7); Code Civ. Proc., § 579.)

CONCLUSION

            Based on the foregoing, Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment is DENIED without prejudice.
