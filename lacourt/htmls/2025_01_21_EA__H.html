Case Number: 23PSCV01975    Hearing Date: January 21, 2025    Dept: H

Jaite v. Campa, Case No. 23PSCV01975 

ORDER ON DEFAULT JUDGMENT APPLICATION 

Plaintiff Billy Jaite’s Application for Default Judgment is DENIED without prejudice.

Background 

Plaintiff Billy Jaite (“Plaintiff”) alleges as follows:

Plaintiff and Vanessa Campa (“Defendant”) were in a relationship from approximately May 2017-April 2022. During their relationship, Plaintiff grew close to Defendant’s daughter, Victoria Isabela Fernandez (“Victoria”). Plaintiff paid Defendant’s rent from approximately October 2017 to November 2022, in large part to ensure Victoria would not be homeless. Throughout the years, Plaintiff and Defendant had numerous discussions about establishing a trust for Victoria’s exclusive benefit. After Plaintiff and Defendant ended their relationship, Plaintiff made four transfers to Defendant in November and December 2022 totaling $350,000.00, with the mutual understanding and oral agreement that these transfers would be put into the trust established for Victoria. Defendant has ceased all communication with Plaintiff. Defendant is living off the monies and has failed to establish the trust. 

On June 30, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting causes of action against Defendant for: (1) specific performance of contract, and (2) breach of contract. 

On August 17, 2023, Defendant’s default was entered. 

Pending before the Court is Plaintiff’s application for default judgment. 

 

Discussion 

Plaintiff’s application for default judgment is denied without prejudice. The following defects are noted:

1.       Plaintiff attests that “[t]hroughout the course of my relationship with Defendant, Defendant and I discussed on numerous occasions the establishment of a trust for Victoria’s benefit in order to provide her with health, education, maintenance, and support. After Defendant and I ended our relationship around August 3, 2022, I still desired to take care of Victoria. Knowing that I would not be there to raise Victoria, I wanted to make sure she was financially taken care of. I therefore made four (4) wire transfers to Defendant totaling three hundred fifty thousand dollars ($350,000.00) under the mutual understanding and oral agreement that all of these funds would be put into a trust established by Defendant for Victoria’s benefit.” (Plaintiff’s Decl., ¶ 4.) It is unclear to the Court when the oral contract was established, what the terms of the contract were, and when the terms of the contract were communicated to/by Defendant. The Court requests further information in this regard.

2.       The prayer in Plaintiff’s complaint requests that the Court order Defendant to establish a trust for Victoria’s benefit and to transfer $350,000.00 into said trust, or alternatively, to transfer $350,000.00 to Plaintiff and “[f]or such other relief as is just and equitable herein.” Plaintiff seeks $37,972.60 in interest and $1,334.23 in costs via this instant default prove-up application; however, Plaintiff’s prayer does not seek costs or interest.

3.       Plaintiff represents that he wired $350,000.00 total to Defendant between November 21, 2022 to December 7, 2022, via one transfer of $50,000.00 and three transfers of $100,000.00 each. (Id., ¶ 4, Exh. 1.) Exhibit 1, however, does not provide the Court with any information as to the recipient of any of these wire transfers or the year of these transfers.

4.       Plaintiff makes certain statements in Paragraph 6 (i.e., that “for her own personal benefit and has been living off such funds” and that “Defendant failed to establish the trust as Defendant had promised to do”) and Paragraph 7 (i.e., “Defendant deleted all my photo albums and text messages”) “upon information and belief.” (Star Motor Imports, Inc. v. Superior Court (1979) 88 Cal.App.3d 201, 204 [“An affidavit based on ‘information and belief’ is hearsay and must be disregarded, and it is ‘unavailing for any purpose’ whatsoever”].) Plaintiff must provide the Court with evidence supporting these statements.

5.       Plaintiff makes reference to hiring a phone forensics specialist, Matthew Albee from DataChasers, Inc., but fails to provide a declaration from him with regard to his unsuccessful data retrieval efforts. 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s application is denied without prejudice, and Plaintiff is directed to submit a revised and complete default judgment packet that addresses the aforementioned issues.

I.        Background 

Plaintiff Lisa Leonard (“Plaintiff”) alleges that her mother died in connection with receiving poor care at a medical facility. 

On August 20, 2024, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint against Emanate Health Medical Center dba Emanate Health Queen of the Valley Hospital’s (sued and served as Queen of the Valley) (“Defendant”) and Does 1-“1 or who company brings.” 

On October 24, 2024, the Court sustained Defendant’s demurrer to the First Amended Complaint, with 30 days’ leave to amend.

Pending before the Court is Defendant’s demurrer to the Second Amended Complaint.

 

II.      Legal Standard 

A demurrer may be made on the grounds that the pleading, inter alia, does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (e).)

“A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters. Therefore, it lies only where the defects appear on the face of the pleading or are judicially noticed.” (SKF Farms v. Superior Court (1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 902, 905 [citations omitted].) At the pleading stage, a plaintiff need only allege ultimate facts sufficient to apprise the defendant of the factual basis for the claim against him. (Semole v. Sansoucie (1972) 28 Cal. App. 3d 714, 721.) “[A] demurrer does not, however, admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law alleged in the pleading, or the construction placed on an instrument pleaded therein, or facts impossible in law, or allegations contrary to facts of which a court may take judicial knowledge.” (S. Shore Land Co. v. Petersen (1964) 226 Cal.App.2d 725, 732 [citations omitted].)

“A general demurrer based on the statute of limitations is only permissible where the dates alleged in the complaint show that the action is barred by the statute of limitations. The running of the statute must appear ‘clearly and affirmatively’ from the dates alleged. It is not sufficient that the complaint might be barred.” (Roman v. County of Los Angeles (2000) 85 Cal.App.4th 316, 324 [citation omitted].)

On October 24, 2024, the Court sustained Defendant’s demurrer to Plaintiff’s First Amended Complaint, with 30 days’ leave to amend and ordered Defendant’s counsel to give notice. On October 25, 2024, Defendant filed (and served via mail and email) a “Notice of Ruling.”

Defendant is treating the declaration as plaintiff’s second amended complaint. During meet and confer efforts, plaintiff has not disputed this.”
