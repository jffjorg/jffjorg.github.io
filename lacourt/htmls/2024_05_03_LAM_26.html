DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.




 






Case Number: 23STCV05719    Hearing Date: May 3, 2024    Dept: 26

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Department 26

 

SETTLERS BAY GOLF COURSE,

                        Plaintiff,

            v.

 

HIGH TIMES PRODUCTION, INC., et al

 

                        Defendants.

 

  Case No.:  23STCVO5719

 

  Hearing Date:  May 3, 2024

 

 [TENTATIVE] order RE:

motion to be relieved as counsel

 

 

On March 15, 2024, Michael J. Kapin, Esq. (“Counsel”), filed the instant motion to be relieved as counsel for defendant High Times Production, Inc. (“Client”).  On March 25, 2024, Counsel filed an updated proposed order.

Counsel has filed a form MC051 and MC-052 and has lodged with the Court a copy of the proposed order on form MC-053 pursuant to CRC Rule 3.1362. 

The MC-052 form states that Counsel served Client via mail at Client’s last known mailing address which Counsel states he has confirmed as current within 30 days of the motion by mail, return receipt requested. 

Counsel states “the specific facts which give rise to this motion are confidential pursuant to Business and Professions Code § 6068(e), the California Rules of Professional Conduct and by the attorney client privilege (Evid.C. §§950 et seq).  In the event that this Court desires further information to ascertain the good faith basis for this motion and for withdrawal, it is respectfully requested that the Court have an in camera hearing outside of the presence of all other parties so that the specific facts demonstrating good cause for this withdrawal may be demonstrated to the court. (E.g., Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1136, 1137).  (MC-051 ¶ 3.)

Legal Standard

California Rules of Court Rule 3.350(a) states in relevant part:

(1) A notice of motion to consolidate must:

(A) List all named parties in each case, the names of those who have appeared, and the names of their respective attorneys of record;

(B) Contain the captions of all the cases sought to be consolidated, with the lowest numbered case shown first; and

(C) Be filed in each case sought to be consolidated.

 

(2) The motion to consolidate:

(A) Is deemed a single motion for the purpose of determining the appropriate filing fee, but memorandums, declarations, and other supporting papers must be filed only in the lowest numbered case;

(B) Must be served on all attorneys of record and all non-represented parties in all of the cases sought to be consolidated; and

(C) Must have a proof of service filed as part of the motion.

(Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.350(a).) Also, the consolidation statute, Code of Civil Procedure § 1048, states in relevant part:

 

(a) When actions involving a common question of law or fact are pending before the court, it may order a joint hearing or trial of any or all the matters in issue in the actions; it may order all the actions consolidated and it may make such orders concerning proceedings therein as may tend to avoid unnecessary costs or delay.

(b) The court, in furtherance of convenience or to avoid prejudice, or when separate trials will be conducive to expedition and economy, may order a separate trial of any cause of action, including a cause of action asserted in a cross-complaint, or of any separate issue or of any number of causes of action or issues, preserving the right of trial by jury required by the Constitution or a statute of this state or of the United States.

(CCP § 1048(a).)

The granting or denial of the motion to consolidate rests in the sound discretion of the trial court and will not be reversed except upon a clear showing of abuse of discretion. (See Fellner v. Steinbaum (1955) 132 Cal.App.2d 509, 511.)

Discussion

            Plaintiff Walters seeks to consolidate the Walters Action with the American Heating Action, Vargas Action, White Cap Action and Gavin Plumbing Action for all purposes.

            “Code of Civil Procedure section 1048, subdivision (a), authorizes the trial court, when appropriate, to ‘order a joint hearing or trial’ or to ‘order all the actions consolidated.’ Under the statute and the case law, there are thus two types of consolidation: a consolidation for purposes of trial only, where the two actions remain otherwise separate; and a complete consolidation or consolidation for all purposes, where the two actions are merged into a single proceeding under one case number and result in only one verdict or set of findings and one judgment.”  (Hamilton v. Asbestos Corp., Ltd. (2000) 22 Cal.4th 1127, 1147.)  “Consolidation under Code of Civil Procedure section 1048 is permissive, and it is for the trial court to determine whether the consolidation is for all purposes or for trial only.”  (Id. at p.1149.)  Moreover, there is no requirement that the parties be identical for a complete consolidation. (See e.g., Jefferson Street Ventures, LLC v. City of Indio (2015) 236 Cal.App.4th 1175, 1206 [noting in action involving multiple but not completely overlapping parties that “consolidation appears particularly appropriate in this case—the cases are in the same court, involve the same property, and many (if not all) of the same damages.”] [italics added].)

            Here, numerous factors support a complete consolidation.  First, the five actions do involve the same parties or subsidiaries of the same parties and subcontractors.  Further, each of the five actions involve the foreclosure of mechanics liens and breach of contract regarding the money due for the electrical equipment and/or further supplies related to the construction. The time frames also overlap, being that the suits are filed between March of 2021 and July of 2023, concerning events beginning mainly commencing between 2018-2019 (with the agreement in Walters dating back to 1998.)   (Walters Compl. ¶ 8.)  Finally and perhaps more important, the actions involve the same property located at 510 S. Oxford Ave., Los Angeles, California 90020.

In sum, “consolidation appears particularly appropriate in this case—the cases are in the same court, involve the same property, and many (if not all) of the same damages.” (Jefferson Street Ventures, LLC, supra, 236 Cal.App.4th at p.1206.)

As explained above, the Actions share many common issues of law or fact as they involve the same defendants, the same property, the same harm, the same damages, and cover the same timeframe.  The fact that the actions do not share exactly the same causes of action is immaterial as the underlying factual basis for the claims concerns overlapping facts.  Moreover, the fact that the plaintiffs and defendants are represented by different firms is also immaterial, as litigation can regularly involve numerous firms representing numerous parties without issue. 

Further, on January 12, 2024, Plaintiff Walters served a Proposed Stipulation and Order to Consolidate Actions (“Joint Stipulation”) and a First Amended Motion to Consolidate Actions (“Motion to Consolidate”) on all parties named in the Motion to Consolidate. Plaintiff Walters also served a Proposed Order Consolidating Cases for All Purposes.  (Decl. of McCabe, Ex. A) As of the date of February 13, 2024, 13 of the 20 parties named in the motion, including Walters, had executed signature pages for the Joint Stipulation.[1]  (McCabe Decl., Ex 1. )

Accordingly, the Court finds that consolidation for all purposes is warranted under the lead action Case No. 23STCV06189.

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Based on the foregoing, Walters Motion for complete consolidation of Case Nos. 23STCV06189 and 23STCV08994; 23STCV09227; 23STCV11102; 23STCV16220 is GRANTED.  Consolidation is ordered for all purposes with Case No. 23STCV06189 as the lead case.  All future pleadings are to be filed in the Lead Action only under Case No. 23STCV06189, with annotation that it is the lead case.

  [TENTATIVE] order RE:

PLAINTIFF KASRA SHAYAN’S motion to compel THE DEPOSITIONs of ahmadreza rastegarrazi, mehre iran today’s pmk, and optima financial venture’s pmk, and request for sanctions

 

 

 

Background   

            On April 20, 2023, Plaintiff Kasra Shayan aka Sina Masoumi (“Shayan”) filed the  Complaint alleging multiple causes of actions relating to a breach of contract involving a proposed investment venture against Defendants Ahmadreza Rastegarrazi (a/k/a Arash) Rastegarrazi (“Arash”), Mehre Iran Today (“Mehre Iran”), and Dani Gonzalez (“Dani”), and Optima Financial Ventures (‘Optima”).  

            On November 29, 2023, Plaintiff Shayan filed the instant motion to compel the deposition of Defendants Ahmadreza Rasetgarrazi, Mehre Iran’s Person Most Knowledgable, and Optima Financial Ventures’ Person Most Knowledgeable (“PMK”).  The Motion also requests sanctions in the amount of $4,500. 

Legal Standard

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2025.450, if after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action, without having served a valid objection, fails to appear for examination, or proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, the party giving notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document.  (CCP § 2025.450(a).)  “The motion shall set forth specific facts showing good cause justifying the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.”  (CCP § 2025.450(b)(1).)  Additionally, a motion to compel the deposition of a party to the action must be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration, or, when the deponent failed to attend the deposition, a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance.  (CCP § 2025.450(b)(2).) 

Discussion

Motion to Compel Deposition

On August 9, 2023, Plaintiff Shayan’s counsel, Fahim Farivar, served Defendant Ahmadreza Rastegarrazi with a Notice of Deposition.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 8, Ex. A.)  On August 9, 2023, Farivar’s office met and conferred with Defendant’s former Counsel to request that if the noticed deposition date of September 28, 2023, was not available, then Defendant’s Counsel was to immediately provide alternate dates for Defendant Rastegarrazi’s deposition.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 9, Ex. B.) 

On September 6, 2023, Defendant’s Counsel sent Farivar’s office Substitution of Attorney Forms for all Defendants.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 10, Ex. C.)

On September 12, 2023, Farivar’s office served Defendants Mehre Iran and Optima Financial Ventures with Notices of Deposition.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 11, Exs. D-E.)

On September 12, 2023, Farviar’s office emailed Defendant Rastegarrazi to request that if the deposition dates for Optima Financial Ventures and Mehre Iran’s PMKs were not acceptable, they should provide dates of availability in October.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 12, Ex. F.)  Farivar’s office did not receive any dates for availability.  Id.

On September 12, 2023, Defendant Ahmadreza Rastegarrazi emailed Farivar’s office stating that he was not permitted to participate in any deposition on behalf of himself, noting that he could not appear for a deposition as he has a pending criminal case.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 14, Ex. H.)  Defendant Ahmadreza Rastegarrazi further elaborated that he would not participate in any deposition until the criminal case was concluded.  Id

On September 19, 2023, Defendants Optima Financial Ventures and Mehre Iran objected on the grounds that Defendant Ahmadreza Rastegarrazi was scheduled to commence a long cause criminal trial on September 27, 2023, so would not be attending his deposition on September 28, 2023.  (Farivar Decl, Ex. K.)

On or about September 19, 2023, Defendant Ahmadreza Rastegarrazi objected to the depositions of Mehre Iran and Optima Financial Ventures’ PMK. (Farivar Decl. ¶ 17, Ex. K.)

On or about November 2, 2023, and November 7, 2023, Farivar’s office received Substitution of Attorney forms for all Defendants.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 18, Ex. L.)

On November 17, 2023, Farivar sent another email to Mr. Shafie,  requesting Deposition dates for each of the Defendants, in a good faith attempt to avoid filing this Motion.  (Farivar Decl. ¶ 19, Ex. M.)  As of the date November 29, 2023, Farivar’s Office had yet to receive a response.  Id.  

Opposition

On April 25, 2024, Defendants attorney of record for all Defendants, Behrouz Shaftie opposed the Motion to Compel Defendants’ Depositions and filed a declaration.  (Shafie Decl.) Shafie declared that he has been suffering with health issues and provided a declaration of by his treating physician, Dr. Kamran Broukhim, MD.  (Shafie Decl. ¶ 2-3.)  Shafie stated he is almost eighty years old and a solo practitioner who is currently suffering from undiagnosed intermediate chest pain and high stress.  (Shafie Decl. ¶ 2.)

Dr. Broukim stated Shafie has been his patient for the past forty years and that on August 3, 2023, Mr. Shafie came to his office to see him on an emergency basis.  (Broukhim Decl. ¶ 2.)  Dr. Broukim stated that he has recommended Shafie reduce his work, and specifically, not attend trials, depositions, or any contested hearings, pending the outcome of his medical tests within the next two months in order to prevent any health complications.  (Broukim Decl. ¶ 2.) 

Shafie further declared that his only Paralegal of twenty years recently left him.  (Shafie Decl. ¶ 4.) Shafie stated that the absence of his long-term paralegal is the reason why Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Discovery was not calendared, as well as why no responsive pleadings were filed.  Id.  Shafie stated he was the new counsel on the case.  Id.  Lastly, Shafie stated Defendant believed that Plaintiff was seeking to depose Defendant regarding the other matter because Plaintiff’s counsel in the instant matter also represents the complaining party, Dr. Simon, in the civil case against Defendant.  Id.

            Additionally, Noah Peter, Defendant Rastegarrazi’s Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner at Mentally Healthy Care provided a report explaining why Rastegarrazi could not attend his scheduled Deposition as the PMK with respect to his Deposition.   (Shafie Decl ¶ 5, Ex. B.)  Nurse Practitioner Peter’s report states that Rastegarrazi has been under his care since the date of April 23, 2024, due to experiencing challenges related to insomnia, depression, PTSD, panic episodes, severe anxiety, ADHD, and other mood related problems.  Id.  As such, Peter recommends that Rastegarrazi should be “abstained from activities to enable his cognitive processing enhance his mental wellness” and that he will continue to receive regular medication management and therapy to address his mental health concerns for a period of 3-4 months.  Id.

Finally, Shafie requested that the court continue Plaintiff’s Motions to Compel Defendants to respond to further discovery to a future date so that Defendants have ample time to retain new Counsel.  (Shafie Decl. ¶ 6.)

While it is true that Shafie was substituted as Defendants’ counsel as of the date November 7, 2023 and the Court accepts Mr. Shafie’s medical documentation recommending he abstain from depositions and trial, the Court also finds that Defendants have had ample time to retain counsel and appropriately engage in meet and confer efforts since Plaintiff first served Defendant Rastegarrazi and Defendants Optima Financial Ventures and Mehre Iran’s with Notices of Deposition on August 9, 2023 and September 12, 2023, respectively.  (Farivar Decl. ¶¶ 8-11, Exs. A-C.).

Defendant Rastegarrazi’s doctor’s report states Nurse Practitioner Peter has been treating Rastegarri since April 23, 2024, and does not specify Rastegarrazi need abstain from a deposition or that a deposition will interfere with Rastegarrazi’s treatment plan.  On April 15, 2024, the Court continued the Hearing on Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendants’ Depositions, on the understanding that no further continuances would be granted.  (4/18/24 Notice of Ruling.) 

Plaintiff Shayan has adequately met and conferred with Defendants over these issues.  (Farivar Decl. ¶¶ 9, 12-19, Exs. A-M.)  Plaintiff has been attempting to take the referenced depositions for months and Defendants have not even provided alternate dates. This case was initiated over a year ago, and trial is less than 7 months away. Discovery, especially depositions of key players in the alleged events must be deposed.

For the reasons stated, Plaintiff’s motion to compel the deposition of Defendants Rastegarrazi, Optima Financial Ventures, and Mehre Iran’s is GRANTED. The depositions are ordered to occur within 30 days.  

 

 

Sanctions

Plaintiff Shayan requests sanctions under Code of Civil Procedure sections 2025.480 and 2023.010 for misuse of discovery against both Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s Counsel.               

            Under Code of Civil Procedure section 2025.480, “[t]he court shall impose a monetary sanction . . . against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel an answer or production, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.”  (CCP § 2025.480(j).)  Similarly, under Code of Civil Procedure 2023.010, it is a misuse of the discovery process to fail to respond or to submit to an authorized method of discovery.  (CCP § 2023.010(d).)

            Here, Defendants’ Optima Financial Ventures and Mehre’s failure to provide dates to schedule their PMK’s depositions is a misuse of the discovery process.  Further, Defendant Rastegarrazi’s failure to provide such dates and to meet and confer is also an abuse of the discovery process that is not excused by an unrelated criminal proceeding. The failure to provide deposition dates predates Counsel Shafie’s representation of Defendants.  The Court finds that the amount requested -- $4,5000 – is unreasonable in light of the simple nature of the motion and the fact that Plaintiff did not file a reply.  The Court will grant sanctions in a lesser amount of $1000.00 as this sum more accurately reflects the filing fees and attorney’s fees reasonably incurred for preparation and filing of the instant motion and appearance at the hearing.

CONCLUSIONS AND ORDER

Plaintiff Shayan’s motion to compel the deposition of Defendant Optima Financial Ventures’ PMK, Defendant Mehre Iran’s PMK, and Defendant Rastegarrazi is GRANTED and the Defendants are ordered to cooperate with the setting of their depositions within 30 days.    

Defendants Optima and Iran are jointly and severally liable and ordered to pay sanctions in the amount of $1000.00 to Plaintiff Shayan, by and through counsel, within thirty (30) days of notice of this order.  As Defendants’ failure to comply with deposition notices and to appropriately meet and confer predates Counsel Shafie’s representation of Defendants, Counsel Shafie is not subject to sanctions.

Moving Party is ordered to provide notice of this order and file proof of service of such.
