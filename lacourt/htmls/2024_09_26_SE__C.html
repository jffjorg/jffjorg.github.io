DEPARTMENT C LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22NWCV00258    Hearing Date: September 26, 2024    Dept: C

This is a breach of rental contract action. Defendant Penn-Star Insurance Company move ex parte for an order to put its Anti-SLAPP Motion (and related request for attorneys’ fees) back on calendar and to vacate the entered dismissal of plaintiff’s complaint without prejudice and to enter judgment of dismissal with prejudice or, in the alternative, to advance the hearing on the motion for the same relief.

A party may seek ex parte relief upon an affirmative factual showing "irreparable harm, immediate danger, or any other statutory basis for granting relief ex parte." (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1202(c).)

Defendant contends that after the stay in this action was lifted Plaintiff dismissed this action, without prejudice as a way to avoid the Anti-SLAPP motion. Defendant contends it is prejudiced as the Anti-SLAPP motion was originally scheduled for October 17, 2024 and now it is scheduled for May 7, 2025, which is 33 months after the anti-SLAPP was originally filed.

Although, the Court understands the inconvenience of the rescheduling of the Anti-SLAPP motion, the Court is not persuaded that Defendant faces irreparable harm or immediate danger.

Accordingly, Defendant Penn-Star’s ex parte application is DENIED.

Case Number: 23NWCV01037    Hearing Date: September 26, 2024    Dept: C

Plaintiff EVERYTABLE, PBC’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Production of Documents (set one) is DENIED as MOOT. Sanctions are DENIED.

 

 

Opposing Party to give notice.

 

This action for breach of contract was filed by Plaintiff EVERYTABLE, PBC (“Plaintiff”) against Defendant MJ GLOBAL ENTERPRISE, INC. (“MJ Global”) and Defendant JUNI ESPINOSA on April 3, 2023.

 

On February 20, 2024, the operative Second Amended Complaint (“SAC”) was filed. The SAC asserts the following causes of action:

 

(1) Breach of Contract – Failure to Properly Assess Common Area Charges;

(2) Fraud and Deceit;

(3) Accounting;

(4) Declaratory Relief; and

(5) Breach of Contract – Failure to Service, Maintain, and Repair

 

Plaintiff moves to compel Defendant MJ Global’s further responses to Request for Production of Documents (set one) Numbers 1-52. The discovery at issue was served on June 29, 2023. MJ Global’s initial responses were served on August 7, 2023.  The Subject Motion was filed on January 18, 2024.

 

In Opposition, MJ Global indicates that, in an effort to meet and confer in good faith, supplemental documents and responses were served on September 21, 2023 and January 29, 2024. MJ Global argues that “[e]very bill, email, ledger, etc. was served by January 2024, yet Everytable has refused to take this Motion of calendar.” (Opp. 2:13-14.)

 

Where supplemental responses were served after a motion to compel further is filed, the Court has discretion to deny a motion to compel further as moot, and just impose sanctions. (Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 409.)

 

It is undisputed that supplemental responses were served after initial responses were served, and then again after the subject Motion was filed. The sufficiency of the supplemental responses are not at issue here. Where supplemental responses were served after the date the Motion was filed, there is nothing further to compel and the Motion to Compel Further Responses is DENIED as MOOT.

 

The issue remaining, therefore, is whether monetary sanctions should be granted in favor of Plaintiff because responses were served after the Motion was filed.

 

“Except as provided in subdivision (j), the court shall impose a monetary sanction… against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel further response to a demand, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” (CCP §2031.310(h).)

 

The Court declines to award monetary sanctions in favor of the Moving Party. The Court has reviewed RPD Nos. 1-52. Most, if not all, of the 52 Requests at issue are impermissibly overbroad. For example, Plaintiff seeks “any and all communications” relating to any of the allegations in the FAC and all internal communications related to the allegations of the FAC. (See SS RPD Nos. 2-3.) Notwithstanding, MJ Global served almost 3,000 pages of responsive documents in an effort to informally resolve the discovery dispute.  The Court finds that MJ Global was substantially justified in objecting to the RPDs as drafted.

 

The Court also declines to award monetary sanctions favor of MJ Global. Although the Requests, as drafted, are overbroad—it is undisputed that MJ Global is/was in possession of responsive documents and information that could have been produced without the need for Court intervention. MJ Global cold have responded or produced more than just boilerplate objections to all 52 Requests.


Case Number: 23NWCV01758    Hearing Date: September 26, 2024    Dept: C

Plaintiff’s unopposed Motion for Issuance of Letters Rogatory is GRANTED.

 

Moving Party to give notice.

 

No Opposition filed as of September 24, 2024.

 

 

This childhood sexual abuse action was filed by Plaintiff EDITH LORENA ENCALADA CETINA (“Plaintiff”) on June 7, 2023. On August 13, 2024, the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) was filed against Defendants THE ESTATE OF DEFENDANT JEAN SUCCAR KURI; GLORIA PITA RODRIGUEZ; and Does 1 through 15.

 

The FAC alleges the following facts: “Beginning when Plaintiff was a 13-year-old child, Defendants Kuri and Rodriguez used their role, status, and power to gain access to, groom, manipulate, and exploit plaintiff, as well as to coerce sexual contact with her and produce and disseminate child pornography over a course of years, much of it occurring in the State of California. As a result of the sexual harassment, abuse and assault, Plaintiff has suffered severe emotional, physical and psychological distress, including humiliation, shame, and guilt. Plaintiff brings this action to hold Defendants accountable for the serious harm they have caused her.” (FAC ¶2.)

 

Plaintiff now moves for this Court to issue letters rogatory to serve Defendants Kuri and Rodriguez who reside in Mexico under the Hague Service Convention.  

 

When a defendant is a resident of another country, the plaintiff must serve process in compliance with the Hague Service Convention. (In re R.L. (2016) 4 Cal.App.5th 125, 146.) Both Mexico and the United States are signatories to the Hague Service Convention, and thus service must be conducted in accordance with eh Hague Service Convention’s requirements. (In re Vanessa Q. (2010 187 Cal.App.4th 128, 134.)

 

CCP §430.10(c) governs service of a summons on a resident of a foreign country and provides that, except as otherwise provided by statute, service is to be performed “as provided in this chapter or as directed by the court in which the action is pending, or, if the court before or after service finds that the service is reasonably calculated to give actual notice, as prescribed by the law of the place where the person is served or as directed by the foreign authority in response to a letter rogatory.” (Id.) “These rules are subject to the provisions of the Convention on the ‘Service Abroad of Judicial and Extrajudicial Documents’ in Civil or Commercial Matters (Hague Service Convention).” (Id.)

 

Here, Defendant Kuri was sentenced to 112 years in prison in Cancun, Quintana Roo. He is currently incarcerated in Cancun, Mexico. (Motion 3:16-19.) Defendant Rodriguez’s legal residence is in Downey, California. However, Plaintiff received a Non-Service Report from the process server identifying multiple unsuccessful service attempts on Defendant Rodriguez. (Menzies Decl., ¶7, Ex. 3.) Plaintiff argues that “[b]ased on information and belief, Defendant Rodriguez is currently located at Defendants residence at a resort in Cancun, Mexico.” (Motion 3:25-26.)

 

The Court finds that the Letters Rogatory are necessary and convenient to effectuate service upon the defendants in compliance with the Hague Convention.

 

The unopposed Motion is GRANTED.

Case Number: 24NWCV01399    Hearing Date: September 26, 2024    Dept: C

This is a civil rights action. Defendant Lakewood Regional Medical Center, Inc. moves ex parte for an order advancing the hearing date on its’ Demurrer or in the alternative a temporary stay on discovery.

A party may seek ex parte relief upon an affirmative factual showing "irreparable harm, immediate danger, or any other statutory basis for granting relief ex parte." (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1202(c).)

“The Court may stay or phase discovery in the interest of justice.” (CCP § 2017.020 and § 2019.020(b).) However, the party seeking the protective order to stay discovery has the burden of showing good cause for the order sought. (Fairmonrt Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (2000) 22 Cal.4th 245, 255.)

Here, Defendant contends that the if the Court does not advance the Demurrer, it will be harmed by having to produce discovery. Plaintiff opposes the stay in discovery, but agrees to advancing the Demurrer.

Accordingly, Defendant Lakewood Regional Medical Center, Inc’s ex parte is GRANTED. Defendant Lakewood is ADVANCED from Thursday, March 6, 2025 to this date and CONTINUED to February 20, 2025 in Department SE-C at 10:30 a.m.
