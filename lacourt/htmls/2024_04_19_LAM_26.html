DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.




 






Case Number: 22STCV37929    Hearing Date: April 19, 2024    Dept: 26

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Department 26

 

 

Esther Tendo Atam,

 

                        Plaintiff,

            Vs.

 

Southern California Permanente Medical Group (SCPMG), Cheryl Surma, Michael Zackos, Marla Hunt, Wilber Jones, And Xavier Edwards,

 

                        Defendants.

 

 Case No.: 22STCV37929

 

 Hearing Date: April 19, 2024

 

[TENTATIVE] order RE:

Plaintiff’s Motion to Vacate Judgment of Dismissal

 

 

 

Background

            On December 5, 2022, Esther Tendo Atam (Plaintiff) filed a Complaint alleging three causes of action against the following defendants: Southern California Permanente Medical Group (SCPMG), Cheryl Surma, Michael Zackos, Marla Hunt, Wilber Jones, and Xavier Edwards (collectively, Defendants). On May 9, 2023, a motion to dismiss filed by Defendants was granted in part and Plaintiff was ordered to post a security of $10,000.00 pursuant to Civ. Code § 391.3 in light of the determination by the Court that Plaintiff is a vexatious litigant. Due to a typographical error, the Court corrected a prior minute order that mistakenly directed Defendants to pay, instead of Plaintiff and allowed Plaintiff 30 days to pay the $10,000.00 security, which meant that Plaintiff was to post the $10,000 bond by July 13, 2023. That hearing was eventually continued to December 13, 2023, by which time Plaintiff had failed to post the bond. The case was dismissed with prejudice.

The motion now before the Court is Plaintiff’s Motion to Vacate the Dismissal (the Motion). Defendants file an opposition, and Plaintiff files a reply.  

Legal Standard         

            Plaintiff specifically points to Code Civ. Proc. § 472(d) which provides that: “The court may, upon motion of the injured party, or its own motion, correct clerical mistakes in its judgment or orders as entered, so as to conform to the judgment or order directed, and may, on motion of either party after notice to the other party, set aside any void judgment or order.” Additionally, “Although a trial court has discretion to vacate the entry of a default or subsequent judgment, this discretion may be exercised only after the party seeking relief has shown that there is a proper ground for relief, and that the party has raised that ground in a procedurally proper manner, within any applicable time limits.” (Cruz v. Fagor America, Inc. (2007) 146 Cal. App. 4th 488, 495.)

Discussion

            Plaintiff alleges that the ground for seeking to set aside the dismissal is fraud. The Court cannot discern any fraudulent conduct by anyone from the arguments presented, nor the attached exhibits to the Motion.

            Plaintiff first argues that another judge, Judge Michael Small, relied on misrepresented facts by Kaiser Foundation Hospitals (Kaiser) in a motion for reconsideration on Plaintiff’s status as a vexatious litigant. (Motion, 5:23-26.) However, Kaiser is not a party in this case, and it is unclear in the Motion how Kaiser caused or perpetuated fraud that would be pertinent in this matter. The Motion goes on to state that Plaintiff’s prior attorney failed to disclose issues naming the proper defendants in the matter. However, there is no nexus between the alleged misrepresentation of facts (whatever those might be) by Kaiser or mistakes by Plaintiff’s former attorney to any fraud committed on the Court or otherwise.  

            Plaintiff maintains that the vexatious litigant designation was an improper application of the law by Judge Small, however, Plaintiff notes only that Judge Small did not specify what subsection of the statute applied and gave no explanation of how the law was applied on his case.  (Motion, 11:7-16.)

            Plaintiff’s final arguments consist of criticisms of and inaccuracies in Judge Small’s case management such as:

·       “Judge Small relied on the wrong citation of the law to issue his order for security deposit” (Motion 12:18-20)

·       “Judge Small failed to check the fraud committed by Judge Steven Kleifield” in a separate case. (Motion, 13:22-23)

·       “Judge Small failed to prevent the BRN [Board of Registration of Nursing] from revoking my license” (Motion, 17:17-18)

·       “Judge Small is already disqualified from the case but still continues to preside over the matter”

To the extent that Judge Small may have committed any error, Plaintiff’s recourse was to make a motion for reconsideration before him or seek an appeal from his orders. Plaintiff apparently has done neither.

Plaintiff fails to establish proper grounds for vacating the dismissal, which was in turn based on Plaintiff’s failure to post the $10,000.00 security. Therefore, the Motion is DENIED.

Conclusion and ORDER

            For the reasons stated, Plaintiff’s Motion to Vacate the Dismissal is DENIED.

Defendant Southern California Permanente Medical Group is to give notice.

Background

            GD Services (Plaintiff) is an Alaskan company that was hired by High Times Productions, Inc. (Defendant) to provide services at a conference in 2020. After Plaintiff provided the requested services, Defendant refused to pay. Plaintiff then filed suit for breach of contract and fraud. The motion now before the Court is Defense Counsel’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel (Motion). 

 

Legal Standard

            An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (Cal. Rules of Court, rule

3.1362(e)). The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or

proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).)

            Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).) A motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994), 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) 

Discussion

            Defense counsel has met all the necessary requirements to grant the Motion. Counsel has filed both forms MC-051 and MC-052. Additionally, form MC-052 denotes all upcoming hearings, including the June 5, 2024 Continued Trial Setting Conference. Trial has not been set, and the client has been notified (see Proof of Service filed on March 25, 2024). Finally, Defense counsel notes in the Amended MC-052, that counsel and client have irreconcilable differences as the reason for the Motion.

Conclusion and ORDER

            Accordingly, Defense Counsel’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel is GRANTED.  Counsel is not relieved as counsel of record until Counsel serves the Order on his client and all parties and files proof of same with the Court.
