Case Number: 20STCV42653    Hearing Date: January 16, 2025    Dept: 1

Background of 20STCV37963 Doe S.V. v. Doe

 

On October 2, 2020, Plaintiff John Doe S.V. filed this action against John Does 1 through 8 asserting causes of action for: (1) childhood sexual assault as defined by C.C.P. section 340.1; (2) battery; (3) assault; (4) negligence; (5) negligent retention/supervision/failure to warn; (6) breach of fiduciary duty; (7) fiduciary fraud and conspiracy; and (8) gross negligence.

 

The complaint alleged the defendants “worked cooperatively to conduct a DARE program for the students at St. John the Baptist Catholic Elementary School in Baldwin Park, California.” (Compl. ¶ 12.) Doe 1 allegedly began grooming Plaintiff in 1989, which allegedly led to more than a dozen sexual assaults over a five-year period beginning in 1991. The complaint further alleges the Defendants failed to take steps to protect Plaintiff, concealed Doe 1’s assaults, and continued to assign Doe 1 to positions where he would engage with minors, including Plaintiff.

 

On August 23, 2021, the court entered the parties’ stipulation and order to add-on and coordinate this action to JCCP 5101 In re: Southern California Clergy Cases.

 

On April 29, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed “DEFENDANT JOHN DOE 4 (Roman Catholic Archbishop of Los Angeles); DEFENDANT JOHN DOE 5 (Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Los Angeles); DEFENDANT JOHN DOE 6 (The Archdiocese of Los Angeles Risk Management Corporation); DEFENDANT JOHN DOE 7 (The Archdiocese of Los Angeles Education & Welfare Corporation); DEFENDANT JOHN DOE 8 (St. John’s Seminary in California)” from the action.

 

On June 21, 2024, as amended nunc pro tunc on July 3, 2024, the court severed 20STCV37963 from JCCP 5101 and deemed the matter noncomplex. The order further provided “Counsel to file a Notice of Related Case pursuant to CRC 3.300.”

 

On July 11, 2024, Judge Christian R. Gullon issued an order finding “20STCV37963 and 22PSCV03108, are not related within the meaning of California Rules of Court, rule 3.300(a).

 

On September 17, 2024, the court granted Plaintiff’s application to file an amended complaint naming the defendants. The court’s ordered “to file the Amended Complaint by end of business on Friday, September 20, 2024.” Plaintiff has not filed an amended complaint.

 

This case is pending in Department O of the Pomona Courthouse with the Case Management Conference Set for March 4, 2025.

 

Background of 22PSCV03108 Doe J. R. v. Doe

 

On December 29, 2022, plaintiff Doe J.R. filed this action against John Does 1 through 3. On March 25, 2024, Plaintiff filed the Second Amended Complaint naming the Defendants. The SAC alleges the Defendants “worked cooperatively to conduct a DARE program for the students at local schools in Baldwin Park, California.” (SAC ¶ 8.) Plaintiff allegedly interacted with Doe 1 through the DARE and explorer programs and was later sexually assaulted by Doe 1 in 1993. The SAC alleges Defendants failed to take steps to protect Plaintiff, concealed Doe 1’s assaults, and continued to assign Doe 1 to positions where he would engage with minors, including Plaintiff.

 

This case is pending in Department 6 of the West Covina Courthouse.

 

Motion

 

On September 18, 2024, Plaintiffs John Doe S.V. and John Doe J.R. filed the instant motion seeking to relate 20STCV37963 and 22PSCV03108.

 

Opposition

 

In opposition, Doe 1 contends the cases are not related because they do not involve the same parties or the same claims and the Plaintiffs allegedly met Doe 1 under different circumstances.

 

In a separate opposition, Doe 2 and 3 contend the motion is untimely and the cases are not related because they involve different plaintiffs, different claims, and the Plaintiffs allegedly met Doe 1 under different circumstances.

 

Reply

 

In reply, Plaintiffs argue the motion is timely and the cases will require a substantial duplication of judicial resources if heard by different judges.

 

Motion to Relate Cases

 

Standard

 

Pursuant to Local Rule 3.3(f)(3), “[i]n the event that the judge designated under California Rules of Court, rule 3.300(h)(1)(A)(B)(C) to make the decision, does not order related any of the cases set forth in the Notice of Related Cases, any party may file a motion to have the cases related. Department 1 shall hear the motion, if the cases are all pending in the Central District or are pending in two or more different districts.”

 

Department 1 Finds the Two Cases are Related

 

On July 11, 2024, Judge Gullon issued an order declining to relate 20STCV37963 and 22PSCV03108. Accordingly, the motion is properly brought in Department 1. (LASC Local Rules, rule 3.3(f)(3).) In their opposition, Does 2 and 3 contend the motion is untimely. (Doe 2 Opp. at 3:14-3:26.) However, Does 2 and 3 cites the non-jurisdictional deadline for filing a Notice of Related Case. (Ibid.) Neither California Rules of Court 3.300(h)(1)(D) nor LASC Local Rules, rule 3.3(f)(3) impose a time constraint upon a motion to relate cases.

 

Cases are related when they (1) involve the same parties and are based on the same or similar claims, (2) arise from the same or substantially identical transactions, incidents, or events requiring the determination of the same or substantially identical questions of law or fact, (3) involve claims against, title to, possession of, or damages to the same property, or (4) are likely for other reasons to require substantial duplication of judicial resources if heard by different judges. (Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.300(a).)

                                                                                      

Plaintiffs contend the cases are related based upon the first, second, and fourth grounds enumerated in Rule 3.300(a).

 

Plaintiffs note the cases involve “childhood sexual assault perpetrated by the same Perpetrator Defendant who was employed by the same City Defendant and Police Department Defendant.” (Mot. at 4:21-25.) As argued in the oppositions, the case involve different plaintiffs are therefore do not involve the same parties. (Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.300(a)(1).)

 

Plaintiffs also contend the cases arise from the same or substantially identical transactions, incidents, or events requiring the determination of the same or substantially identical questions of law or fact. (Mot. at 5:14-22; Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.300(a)(2).) In opposition, Doe 1 contends the Plaintiff Doe S.V. alleges they met Doe 1 in 1989 at a Catholic school whereas Plaintiff Doe J.R. testified they met Doe 1 at the Police Department in 1993. (Doe 1. Opp. at 4:11-26.) Doe 1 further argues Plaintiff Doe S.V. asserted fiduciary claims that are not present in 22PSCV03108 and therefore there is not “a complete identity of legal issues between the two cases.” (Doe 1 Opp. at 4:27-5:7.) In their opposition, Does 2 and 3 similarly cite the manner in which the Plaintiffs allegedly met Doe 1. (Does 2 Opp. at 4:26-5:17.) Does 2 and 3’s opposition also appears to partially respond to different cases as Does 2 and 3 contends “[t]he alleged events also occurred almost ten years apart.” (Doe 2 Opp. at 5:7.) However, Plaintiff Doe S.V. alleges the first assault occurred in 1991 and continued “over a five year period,” (Doe S.V. FAC ¶¶ 25-29), whereas Plaintiff Doe J.R. alleges the assault occurred in 1993. (Doe J.R. SAC ¶ 3.)

 

The cases arise from the same or substantially identical transactions, incidents, or events requiring the determination of the same or substantially identical questions of law or fact. (Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.300(a)(2).) Both cases arise out of an alleged sexual assault of a minor by Doe 1, for which Does 2 and 3 are allegedly also liable. Does 2 and 3’s conduct, knowledge regarding Doe 1, and potential liability are issues that will require the determination of substantially identical questions of law and fact in both cases. Moreover, because the cases involve the same defendants and alleged childhood sexual assaults during the same time period, the cases are likely to require a substantial duplication of judicial resources if heard by different judges. (Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.300(a)(4).) Having these two cases heard by the same judicial officer is likely to promote judicial efficiency and reduce the risk of inconsistent rulings on similar issues.

 

The Court finds the cases are related within the meaning of California Rules of Court, rule 3.300.

 

Conclusion

 

Plaintiffs’ Motion to Relate Cases is GRANTED.

 

Department 1 relates 20STCV37963 and 22PSCV03108 and orders 22PSCV03108 reassigned to Department O of the Pomona Courthouse South for all purposes. All hearings currently set in 22PSCV03108 are hereby advanced and vacated.

Motion

 

On October 28, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to transfer this action to the Santa Monica Courthouse.

 

The motion is unopposed. (Code Civ. Proc. § 1005.)

 

Motion to Transfer Between Districts

 

Standard

 

LASC Local Rules, rule 2.3(b)(2) authorizes Department 1 to transfer civil cases from one judicial district to another via a noticed motion on three enumerated grounds: (1) when the case was filed in an improper district; (2) for the convenience of witnesses; or (3) to promote the ends of justice. (LASC Local Rule 2.3(b)(2).) A transfer under the Local Rules is discretionary.

 

Because the same language appears in both LASC Local Rule 2.3(b)(2) and Code of Civil Procedure section 397(c), Department 1 applies the case authority related to Section 397. Accordingly, a party acting under the authority of Los Angeles Superior Court Local Rule 2.3(b)(2) bears the same burden of proof if it seeks a district transfer. (See Lieberman v. Superior Court (1987) 194 Cal.App.3d 396, 401.) That burden of proof calls for affidavits that contain more than generalities and conclusions. (See Hamilton v Superior Court (1974) 37 Cal. App.3d 418, 424.) Convenience to non-party witnesses alone is the key to the success of the motion, and not the convenience of parties or employees of parties. (See Stute v. Burinda (1981) 123 Cal. App. 3d Supp. 11, 17.)

 

The Case was Initially Filed in Accordance with the Local Rules

 

Plaintiff contends the case is in an improper venue and relies upon Code of Civil Procedure sections 395(a), which governs venue in the proper county, not the assignment of cases within a superior court. (Mot. at 4:3-22.) The Local Rules of the Los Angeles Superior Court govern the assignment of cases between districts and courthouses. (Code Civ. Proc. § 402; LASC Local Rule 2.3; Glade v. Glade (1995) 38 Cal.App.4th 1441, 1449 (“Even though a superior court is divided into branches or departments, pursuant to California Constitution, article VI, section 4, there is only one superior court in a county and jurisdiction is therefore vested in that court, not in any particular judge or department. Whether sitting separately or together, the judges hold but one and the same court.”).)

 

When this case was filed, it was properly assigned pursuant to the then-governing Local Rules and standing orders regarding personal injury actions. Plaintiff has not demonstrated the case was filed in an improper venue. Rather, the case was transferred for administrative purposes and as a result of the acceptance of peremptory challenge.

 

The Convenience of Witnesses Warrant the Requested Transfer

 

Plaintiff also contends the case should be transferred for the convenience of witnesses. (Mot. at 4:23-6:4.) Plaintiff’s counsel avers that Plaintiff resides in Venice, California, the incident occurred in Venice, California, and defense counsel is agreeable to the transfer. (Malek Decl. ¶¶ 3-9.) The Court finds the declaration from Plaintiff’s counsel sufficient under the circumstances. (Rycz v. Superior Court of San Francisco County (2022) 81 Cal.App.5th 824, 846 n.22 (“Although that may be advisable where possible, witness declarations are not required by section 397, subdivision (c); counsel declarations have been employed in past published cases . . . and the inconvenience to witnesses may be inferred where the witnesses are located far from the trial venue.”).)

 

The Court notes the current version of the Local Rules would assign this action to the West District, which includes the Santa Monica Courthouse. (LASC Local Rules, rule 2.3(A)(1)(A) (“Every unlimited civil tort action for bodily injury, wrongful death, or damage to personal property (hereinafter referred to as ‘Personal Injury Action’) must be filed in the judicial district where the incident arose.”).)

 

Accordingly, the Court finds the convenience of witnesses warrants a transfer to the district identified by Plaintiff.

 

Conclusion

 

Plaintiff’s Motion to Transfer Venue is GRANTED and 21STCV26474 is ordered reassigned to the Santa Monica Courthouse in the West District of the Los Angeles Superior Court.

 

Notice of case reassignment shall issue shortly.

Background

 

On July 8, 2024, Plaintiffs John Doe MT, John Doe ID, John Doe NO, John Doe BL, John Doe RE, John Doe TV, John Doe JD, John Doe DB, and John Doe PS filed this action against the County of Los Angeles arising out of alleged childhood sexual assaults that occurred at Barry J. Motion to be Relieved as Counsel

 

On November 21, 2024, J. Kyle Gaines of Slater, Slater, Schulman, LLP filed the instant motion seeking to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff John Doe NO.

 

Standard

 

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Code Civ. Proc., § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.) Further, under such discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, the motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.)

 

“A notice of motion and motion to be relieved as counsel under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) must be directed to the client and must be made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (form MC-051).” (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1362(a).) “Notwithstanding any other rule of court, no memorandum is required to be filed or served with a motion to be relieved as counsel.” (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1362(b).) “The motion to be relieved as counsel must be accompanied by a declaration on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (form MC-052). The declaration must state in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(1).” (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1362(c).)

 

“The notice of motion and motion, the declaration, and the proposed order must be served on the client and on all other parties who have appeared in the case. The notice may be by personal service or mail. If the notice is served on the client by mail under Code of Civil Procedure section 1013, it must be accompanied by a declaration stating facts showing that either: (1) The service address is the current residence or business address of the client; or (2) The service address is the last known residence or business address of the client and the attorney has been unable to locate a more current address after making reasonable efforts to do so within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved. As used in this rule, ‘current’ means that the address was confirmed within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved. Merely demonstrating that the notice was sent to the client's last known address and was not returned is not, by itself, sufficient to demonstrate that the address is current. If the service is by mail, Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b) applies.” (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1362(d).)

 

“The proposed order relieving counsel must be prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (form MC-053) and must be lodged with the court with the moving papers. The order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. If no hearing date is presently scheduled, the court may set one and specify the date in the order. After the order is signed, a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case. The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court.” (Cal. R. Ct. 3.1362(e).)

 

Discussion

 

Counsel seeks to be relieved because “[i]t has become unreasonably difficult for this office to carry out Plaintiff’s representation effectively under CRPC 1.16(b)(4).” (Gaines Decl. ¶ 2.) Accordingly, counsel stated a proper basis for withdrawal. (Cal. R. Prof. Cond., rule 1.16(b)(4).)

 

However, counsel failed to demonstrate proper service of the motion. The motion is not accompanied by a separate proof of service and counsel failed to timely file a proof of service. (Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.1300(c) (“Proof of service of the moving papers must be filed no later than five court days before the time appointed for the hearing.”).)

 

The accompanying declaration states “[a]ttorney has . . . served the client by mail at the client's last known address with copies of the motion papers served with this declaration.” (Gaines Decl. ¶ 3(a)(2).) This is not a sufficient proof of service by mail as it does not contain any of the information or language required by statute. (See Code Civ. Proc. § 1013a.) Moreover, when service is attempted by mail in connection with a motion to be relieved, “Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b) applies.” (Cal. R. Ct., rule 3.1362(d).) If, as here, the client’s address is known, Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b) requires an attempt at personal service before service by mail is authorized. Counsel failed to provide a declaration demonstrating an attempt at personal service and therefore has not established compliance with Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b).

 

The motion is therefore taken OFF-CALENDAR.

 

Conclusion

 

J. Kyle Gaines of Slater, Slater, Schulman, LLP’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiff John Doe NO is taken OFF-CALENDAR.

 

Clerk to give notice.


Case Number: 24NWCV00099    Hearing Date: January 16, 2025    Dept: 1

Background

 

On January 11, 2024, Plaintiff Alex Yu, Jr. filed this action against Defendant Maya Elizabeth Burrell Marrero asserting a single cause of action for negligence arising out of an automobile collision on Interstate 105.

 

On August 21, 2024, the parties submitted a stipulation to transfer this action to the Torrance Courthouse. However, the clerk issued a Notice of Rejection of Electronic Filing noting, as the reason for rejection, “Other - cannot transfer by stipulation and order; file motion in Department 1.”

 

Motion

 

On September 16, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to transfer this action to the Torrance Courthouse.

 

The motion is unopposed, (Code Civ. Proc. § 1005), and the parties stipulated to the transfer.

 

Motion to Transfer Between Districts

 

Standard

 

LASC Local Rules, rule 2.3(b)(2) authorizes Department 1 to transfer civil cases from one judicial district to another via a noticed motion on three enumerated grounds: (1) when the case was filed in an improper district; (2) for the convenience of witnesses; or (3) to promote the ends of justice. (LASC Local Rule 2.3(b)(2).) A transfer under the Local Rules is discretionary.

 

Because the same language appears in both LASC Local Rule 2.3(b)(2) and Code of Civil Procedure section 397(c), Department 1 applies the case authority related to Section 397. Accordingly, a party acting under the authority of Los Angeles Superior Court Local Rule 2.3(b)(2) bears the same burden of proof if it seeks a district transfer. (See Lieberman v. Superior Court (1987) 194 Cal.App.3d 396, 401.) That burden of proof calls for affidavits that contain more than generalities and conclusions. (See Hamilton v Superior Court (1974) 37 Cal. App.3d 418, 424.) Convenience to non-party witnesses alone is the key to the success of the motion, and not the convenience of parties or employees of parties. (See Stute v. Burinda (1981) 123 Cal. App. 3d Supp. 11, 17.)

 

The Case was Filed in an Incorrect District

 

Defendant seeks to transfer this action from the Norwalk Courthouse in the Southeast District to the Torrance Courthouse in the Southwest District. Defendant contends the case was filed in an incorrect district under the applicable local rules.

 

In “Step 4” of the civil case cover sheet addendum where the filing party is asked to “[c]heck the appropriate boxes for the numbers shown under Column C for the type of action that you have selected. Enter the address, which is the basis for the filing location including zip code,” Plaintiffs selected Reason 4, which corresponds to “Location where bodily injury, death or damage occurred” and listed the relevant address as “on or around the 105 freeway on or around the city of Downey, Downey, CA 90242.”

 

However, the parties stipulated that the collision occurred “just after the Sofi/Forum exit sign on the 105 freeway placing the accident within the Torrance District.” (Cox Decl. Ex. D.) The parties made this determination based upon dash cam footage, (Id. ¶ 3, Ex. C), and the map of the Southwest Judicial District in Appendix 2.A of the Los Angeles County Rules of Court. Pursuant to Local Rule 2.3(A)(1)(A), “[e]very unlimited civil tort action for bodily injury, wrongful death, or damage to personal property (hereinafter referred to as ‘Personal Injury Action’) must be filed in the judicial district where the incident arose.”

 

Defendant demonstrated the action was filed in an improper district and the motion is GRANTED.

 

Conclusion

 

Defendant’s Motion for Order Transferring Case to Proper District of the Los Angeles County Superior Court per Stipulation of the Parties is GRANTED and 24NWCV00099 is ordered reassigned to the Torrance Courthouse in the Southwest District of the Los Angeles Superior Court.

 

Notice of case reassignment shall issue shortly. 

 

Clerk to give notice.
