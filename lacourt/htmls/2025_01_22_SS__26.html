Case Number: 21TRCV00820    Hearing Date: January 22, 2025    Dept: 26

Shayler v. Prairie Hospitality Group, Inc. et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendants William B. Serns and Eydie Serns’ Motion for Attorney’s Fees is DENIED.  

ANALYSIS:

On November 5, 2021, Plaintiff James Shayler (“Plaintiff”) filed the Complaint in this action against Defendants Prairie Hospitality Group Inc. (“Defendant PHG”), William B. Serns and Eydie Serns (“the Serns Defendants”). The action was assigned to an unlimited civil courtroom, Department 8 in the Torrance Courthouse. Default was entered against the Serns Defendants on April 25, 2022.

Plaintiff stipulated to vacate the entry of default with the Serns Defendants on August 2, 2022, who filed their answer on October 3, 2022. The case was then reclassified to the limited jurisdiction court by stipulation on May 3, 2024. (Stip and Order, 05/03/24.) On October 23, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed the Serns Defendants from the action, who filed a memorandum of costs on the same date.

The Serns Defendants filed the instant Motion for Attorney’s Fees on November 20, 2024. Plaintiff filed an opposition on January 8, 2025 and the Serns Defendants replied on January 15, 2025.

Discussion

The Motion is brought pursuant to Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 870.2, Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 1021.5, and 42 USC section 12205. (Notice of Motion, p. 2:9-11.) The first two statutes, however, are not cited in the memorandum of points and authorities. The Serns Defendants rely on 42 USC section 12205 for the argument that this federal statute that allows a prevailing defendant to obtain attorney’s fees in a state law case brought under the Unruh Civil Rights Act (“the UCRA”).

This action alleges a single cause of action for violation of the UCRA, as set forth at section 51 of the Civil Code. (Compl., ¶¶34-37.) The UCRA makes violations of the Americans with Disabilities Act (“the ADA”) a violation of California law. (Civ. Code, § 51, subd. (f).) However, the Serns Defendants cite no valid authority for the proposition that the fee shifting provision under section 12205 of the Americans with Disabilities Act (“the ADA”) applies to a state law action brought under the UCRA. Instead, the Motion cites only an unpublished appellate court case, and even fails to provide a page citation. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 8.1115(a) [“Except as provided in (b), an opinion of a California Court of Appeal or superior court appellate division that is not certified for publication or ordered published must not be cited or relied on by a court or a party in any other action.”].) Despite the non-citable nature of the case being pointed out in Plaintiff’s opposition, Defendant doubles-down on its improper citation by relying on the same case in reply.

The reply also fails to address the case law cited by Plaintiff. Specifically, that in Molski v. Arciero Wine Group (2014) 164 Cal. App. 4th 786, the Court of Appeals explains that a litigant who choses to brings an action under section 51 of the UCRA does so “without being exposed to any risk of an adverse judgment for fees.” (Molski v. Arciero Wine Group (2014) 164 Cal. App. 4th 786, 792.) The Serns Defendants’ position would effectively eliminate this statutory protection for plaintiffs who seek to hold defendants liable for ADA violations under section 51 of the UCRA. Accordingly, the Motion has not shown any legal basis for an award of attorney’s fees.

Conclusion

Defendants William B. Serns and Eydie Serns’ Motion for Attorney’s Fees is DENIED.

Robles v. Mochkin et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING: 

Plaintiff Jose Robles’ Motion to Vacate Dismissal is GRANTED. THE DISMISSAL ENTERED ON NOVEMBER 18, 2024 IS HEREBY VACATED.

ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE REGARIDNG FAILURE TO FILE PROOF OF SERVICE OF SUMMONS AND COMPLAINT IS SET FOR MARCH 26, 2025 AT 8:30 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. 

ANALYSIS:

Plaintiff Jose Robles (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against Defendant Peretz Mochkin (“Defendants”) on May 22, 2023. The case was set for trial on November 18, 2024. When Plaintiff failed to appear, the Court dismissed the action without prejudice. (Minute Order, 11/18/24.)

Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Vacate Dismissal on December 3, 2024. No opposition to the Motion has been filed to date.

Discussion

The motion is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure, section 473, subdivision (b). Under this statute, an application for relief must be made no more than six months after entry of the order from which relief is sought and must be accompanied by an affidavit of fault attesting to the moving party’s mistake, inadvertence, surprise or neglect. (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b); English v. IKON Business Solutions (2001) 94 Cal.App.4th 130, 143.) When based on attorney fault with respect to entry of default, default judgment, or involuntary dismissal, a timely request for relief must be granted. (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b).) When brought pursuant to the provision for discretionary relief based on party fault, the request must have been filed within a reasonable amount of time. 

The Motion was timely filed two weeks after dismissal of the action and is supported by an attorney affidavit of fault. Plaintiff’s counsel declares that they failed to calendar the correct trial date. (Motion, Iwuchuku Decl., ¶¶3-5.) Therefore, the case must be reinstated under Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b).

Conclusion

Plaintiff Jose Robles’ Motion to Vacate Dismissal is GRANTED. THE DISMISSAL ENTERED ON NOVEMBER 18, 2024 IS HEREBY VACATED.

Case Number: 23STLC05961    Hearing Date: January 22, 2025    Dept: 26

Cal. Automobile Ins. Co. v. Benson Crane, Inc. et al.,

1. MOTION TO VACATE ORDER DEEMING MATTERS ADMITTED

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendant David Allen Benson dba Benson Crane’s Motion to Vacate Order Deeming Matters Admitted is DENIED.

ANALYSIS:

On September 15, 2023, Plaintiff California Automobile Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) filed this action for automobile subrogation against Defendant David Allen Benson dba Benson Crane (erroneously sued as Benson Crane, Inc.) (“Defendant”). Defendant filed an answer on January 24, 2024. On August 14, 2024, the Court granted Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted and Request for Monetary Sanctions against Defendant. (Minute Order, 08/14/24.) 

Plaintiff filed a Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings against Defendant on September 9, 2024. The Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings initially came for hearing on December 12, 2024, at which time Defendant appeared and argued. (Minute Order, 12/12/24.) The Court continued the hearing to January 22, 2025 and Defendant filed the instant Motion to Vacate Order Deeming Matters Admitted on December 16, 2024, set for hearing concurrently. Plaintiff filed an opposition on January 15, 2025. 

Discussion

Defendant moves for relief from the order deeming Requests for Admission admitted against him pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.300, which states in relevant part: “[t]he court may permit withdrawal or amendment of an admission only if it determines that the admission was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect, and that the party who obtained the admission will not be substantially prejudiced in maintaining that party’s action or defense on the merits.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.300, subd, (b).) 

The Motion does not include facts that demonstrate how the admissions were the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect. Defendant submits a declaration attaching a contract that purports to absolve him of liability in this action but does not address failing to timely respond to the discovery propounded and the resulting order deeming the requests for admission admitted. (Motion, Benson Decl., ¶2 and Exhs. A-B.) The merits of this action are not part of the Court’s consideration under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.300. Defendant contends that he was denied due process and equal protection rights to challenge the order deeming the requests for admission admitted, but does not explain how. (Id. at ¶4.) Proofs of service of all relevant documents, including the Motion to Deem Requests for Admission, are in the record. 

Therefore, Defendant has not demonstrated a basis for relief under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.300.

Conclusion

Defendant David Allen Benson dba Benson Crane’s Motion to Vacate Order Deeming Matters Admitted is DENIED.

2. MOTION FOR JUDGMENT ON THE PLEADINGS

TENTATIVE RULING:

Plaintiff California Automobile Insurance Company’s Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings against Defendant David Allen Benson dba Benson Crane is GRANTED. PLAINTIFF IS TO FILE AND SERVE A PROPOSED JUDGMENT WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER.

ANALYSIS:

On September 15, 2023, Plaintiff California Automobile Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) filed this action for automobile subrogation against Defendant David Allen Benson dba Benson Crane (erroneously sued as Benson Crane, Inc.) (“Defendant”). Defendant filed an answer on January 24, 2024. On August 14, 2024, the Court granted Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted and Request for Monetary Sanctions against Defendant. (Minute Order, 08/14/24.) 

Plaintiff filed the instant Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings against Defendant on September 9, 2024. The Motion initially came for hearing on December 12, 2024 and was continued following Defendant’s appearance at the hearing. (Minute Order, 12/12/24.) At the hearing, Defendant reported to the court that he had an opposition brief for the court to review. The court ordered that Defendant had permission to file his late opposition brief and also ordered Defendant to serve the opposition on Plaintiff. No opposition to the Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings has been filed to date. The court notes that Defendant filed a separate Motion to Vacate the Court's Order Deeming Matters Admitted, but not an opposition to the instant Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings.

Discussion

Plaintiff moves for judgment on the pleadings, the standard for which is essentially the same as that applicable to a general demurrer. That is, under the state of the pleadings, together with matters that may be judicially noticed, it appears that a party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.  (Bezirdjian v. O’Reilly (2010) 183 Cal.App.4th 316, 321-322, citing Schabarum v. California Legislature (1998) 60 Cal.App.4th 1205, 1216.) Matters which are subject to mandatory judicial notice may be treated as part of the complaint and may be considered without notice to the parties. Matters which are subject to permissive judicial notice must be specified in the notice of motion, the supporting points and authorities, or as the court otherwise permits. (Id.)  The motion may not be supported by extrinsic evidence. (Barker v. Hull (1987) 191 Cal.App.3d 221, 236.)

While a statutory motion for judgment on the pleadings brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 438, et seq. must be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration, there is no such requirement for a motion for judgment on the pleadings brought pursuant to the common law. (See Code Civ. Proc., § 439 [moving party must file declaration demonstrating an attempt to meet and confer in person or by telephone, at least five days before the date a motion for judgment on the pleadings is filed].) 

Plaintiff’s Motion is accompanied by a request for judicial notice of the matters deemed admitted in Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem Requests for Admission, Set One, Admitted, and this Court’s August 14, 2024 order deeming the Requests for Admission admitted against Defendant. The Court takes judicial notice of these admissions pursuant to Cal. Evidence Code section 452, subdivision (d). (Cal. Evid. Code, § 452, subd. (d); Cloud v. Northrop Grumman Corp. (1998) 67 Cal.App.4th 995, 999; Evans v. California Trailer Court, Inc. (1994) 28 Cal.App.4th 540, 549) [holding that the court may take judicial notice of matters that cannot be reasonably controverted, including “admissions and concessions.”].) 

The admissions in the Request for Admissions directly contradict the general denial and affirmative defenses asserted in Defendant’s Answer. The admissions admit that Defendant, during the operation of a crane, caused an electrical surge on the property of Plaintiff’s insured that caused damage to the property. (RJN, Exh. 2 at Exh. A, Request for Admission Nos. 1-4.) They also admit that Defendant failed to act with reasonable care in the operation of the crane and as a result, Defendant caused damage to a power line on the insured’s property, and to the property. (Id. at Request for Admission Nos. 5-7.) The admissions admit that Defendant’s negligence caused the insured, and subsequently, Plaintiff to incur damages of $11,888.23. (Id. at Request for Admission Nos. 8-10.) Finally, Defendant admits that the affirmative defenses asserted in the Answer lack merit and evidentiary support. (Id. at Request for Admission No. 11.)

By this Motion, Plaintiff has demonstrated that it served Defendant with Requests for Admissions that effectively establish the truth of the allegations in the Complaint, as detailed above. The admissions establish the facts upon which Plaintiff based its Complaint and that Defendant has not alleged a defense to Plaintiff’s Complaint in the Answer. Therefore, judgment on the pleadings is appropriate against Defendant. 

Conclusion

Plaintiff California Automobile Insurance Company’s Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings against Defendant David Allen Benson dba Benson Crane is GRANTED. PLAINTIFF IS TO FILE AND SERVE A PROPOSED JUDGMENT WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER.

Case Number: 24STCP02878    Hearing Date: January 22, 2025    Dept: 26

TENTATIVE RULING:

Petitioner Weiss Family Properties, LLC’s Petition for Permanent Injunction is CONTINUED TO FEBRUARY 20, 2025 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY FEBRUARY 6, 2025, PETITIONER IS TO FILE AN AMENDED PETITION CORRECTING THE DEFECTS NOTED HEREIN AND PROOF OF SERVICE OF THE AMENDED PETITION AND NOTICE OF THE NEW HEARING DATE.

ANALYSIS:

On September 6, 2024, Petitioner Weiss Family Properties, LLC (“Petitioner”) filed the instant Petition for Permanent Injunction Pursuant to Civil Code section 798.88 against Respondent George Waterman (“Respondent”). No response to the Petition has been filed to date.

Discussion

Petitioner moves pursuant to Civil Code section 786.88 for an order enjoining Respondent from continuing violations of the rules of Petitioner’s Mobile Home Park, Royal Oaks Mobile Home Park (“the Park”), located at 18145 Soledad Canyon Road, Canyon Country, California. (Pet., ¶¶2-3.) Respondents’ Mobilehome is located on Space 33 of the Park. (Id. at ¶4.) Respondent’s violations include failing to adjust the pole awnings that encroach on neighboring spaces, failing to comply with Title 25 § 1428(2)(A)(B) regarding proper placement of awning poles in relation to closeness of lot lines, failing to remove improperly stored items beyond the lot line, failing to comply with federal, state, and/or local law, including but not limited to California Code of Regulations Title 25 §§ 1018, 1130, 1428, and 1606, failing to cease all erratic and harassing behavior when addressing Park Management, and failing to comply with the Rental Agreement, Park Rules and Regulations, and Title 25. (Id. at ¶7 and Exhs. 1-2.)

Petitioner requests an injunction against Respondent prohibiting further violations and an award of reasonable attorney fees and costs. (Id. at p. 6:4-22.)

Legal Standard

The Mobilehome Residency Law (“MRL”) gives management an injunctive relief remedy for violations of reasonable park rules and regulations. (Civ. Code, § 798.88.) Specifically, management may obtain a restraining order against a continuing or recurring violation of any “reasonable rule or regulation.” (Civ. Code, § 798.88, subd. (b); Rancho Santa Paula Mobilehome Park, Ltd. v. Evans (1994) 26 Cal.App.4th 1139, 1142.) At the time of the hearing, the court shall issue the injunction if it finds by clear and convincing evidence the existence of a continuing or recurring violation of a “reasonable rule or regulation.” (Civ. Code, § 798.88, subd. (d).) The duration of the injunction may not exceed three years. (Civ. Code, § 798.88, subd. (d).)

Discussion

Although the moving statute does not provide for a specific notice period, Respondent is entitled to reasonable notice prior to issuance of an order on the Petition. (See Menefee & Son v. Dent. of Food &Agriculture (1988) 199 Cal.App.3d 774, 781.) Failure to give notice is a violation of due process. (Jones v. Otero (1984) 156 Cal.App.3d 754, 757.) Petitioner filed proof of substitute service on September 20, 2024, demonstrating that Respondent was served by substitute service and posting on September 15, 2024. (Proofs of Personal Service, ¶3.) Therefore, Petitioner has provided Respondent with reasonable notice.

A verified petition, as required under the moving statute must be made pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 446, which states: “When a corporation is a party, the verification may be made by any officer thereof.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 446, subd. (a).) The verification of this Petition, however, does not indicate it was made by an officer of the corporate Plaintiff. Rather it is signed by “Authorized Agent for Petitioner,” who does not provide their name or title. Also, “when the verification is made on behalf of a corporation or public agency by any officer thereof, the attorney’s or officer’s affidavit shall state that he or she has read the pleading and that he or she is informed and believes the matters therein to be true and on that ground alleges that the matters stated therein are true. However, in those cases the pleadings shall not otherwise be considered as an affidavit or declaration establishing the facts therein alleged.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 446, subd. (a).) Therefore, Petitioner cannot rely solely on the facts set forth in, or the exhibits attached to, the Petition as evidence in support of the injunction sought against Respondent.

Based on the foregoing, the hearing on the Petition is continued to give Petitioner an opportunity to correct these defects.

Conclusion

Petitioner Weiss Family Properties, LLC’s Petition for Permanent Injunction is CONTINUED TO FEBRUARY 20, 2025 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY FEBRUARY 6, 2025, PETITIONER IS TO FILE AN AMENDED PETITION CORRECTING THE DEFECTS NOTED HEREIN AND PROOF OF SERVICE OF THE AMENDED PETITION AND NOTICE OF THE NEW HEARING DATE.

Case Number: 24STLC03828    Hearing Date: January 22, 2025    Dept: 26

Chen v. IDD (Int'l) Investment, Inc., et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Plaintiff Zhiwei Chen’s Motion for Order Authorizing Service on Secretary of State is GRANTED.  

ANALYSIS:

On May 29, 2024, Plaintiff Zhiwei Chen (“Plaintiff”), in propria persona, filed this action for property damage, fraud, and money laundering against Defendant IDD (Int’l) Investment, Inc. (“Defendant”). Plaintiff filed the instant Motion for Order Authorizing Service on Secretary of State on July 8, 2024. The Motion initially came for hearing on October 11, 2024 and was continued for further briefing. (Minute Order, 10/11/24.) Plaintiff filed a supplemental brief on November 19, 2024. To date, no opposition has been filed.

Discussion

Plaintiff moves for an order authorizing service on Defendant through the Secretary of State pursuant to Corporations Code sections 1702. Code of Civil Procedure section 416.10 provides that a summons may be served on a corporation, if authorized, by any provision in Corporations Code sections 1701, 1702, 2110 or 2111. Under Corporations Code section 1702, this includes by hand delivery “to the Secretary of State, or to any person employed in the Secretary of State’s office in the capacity of assistant or deputy.” (Cal. Corp. Code, § 1702, subd. (a).) Service on the Secretary of State’s Office is permitted if the corporation’s agent “cannot with reasonable diligence be found at the address designated for personally delivering the process . . . and it is shown by affidavit to the satisfaction of the court that process against a domestic corporation cannot be served with reasonable diligence upon the designated agent.” (Cal. Corp. Code, § 1702, subd. (a).)

Plaintiff has demonstrated by affidavit their inability to serve Defendant despite reasonable diligence. Plaintiff’s requests to the Sheriff’s Office and to process servers to effectuate service have been unsuccessful. (Motion, Chen Decl., Exh. A.) Service by mail was also unsuccessful because no authorized recipient was available. (Id. at Exhs. B-C.) The service attempts were at the address provided to the California Secretary of State as that of Defendant’s agent: 3940 N Charlotte Ave., San Gabriel, California. (Id. at Exh. D.) Therefore, Plaintiff has demonstrated the corporation’s agent “cannot with reasonable diligence be found at the address designated for personally delivering the process . . . and it is shown by affidavit to the satisfaction of the court that process against a domestic corporation cannot be served with reasonable diligence upon the designated agent.”

Conclusion

Plaintiff Zhiwei Chen’s Motion for Order Authorizing Service on Secretary of State is GRANTED.
