DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21VECV01111    Hearing Date: January 18, 2024    Dept: 107

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

ROIC CALIFORNIA, LLC, A DELAWARE LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANY

 

Plaintiff.

 

   v.

CHUNIL PARK, et al

Defendants.

 

 

 

   Case Number             Department

       21VECV01111           107

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

(1) DEEM RFAS ADMITTED RE DEFENDANT CHUNIL PARK D/B/A KYOTO SUSHI AND REQUESTS FOR MONETARY SANCTIONS IN THE AMOUNT OF $2,825.00

 

 

(2) DEEM RFAS ADMITTED RE DEFENDANT SOO SUNG NAM D/B/A KYOTO SUSHI AND REQUESTS FOR MONETARY SANCTIONS IN THE AMOUNT OF $2,825.00

 

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

[Tentative] Ruling:  

·       Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem the Truth of Matters in Requests for Admission Set Two Admitted as to Defendant Chunil Park is DENIED.

·       Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem the Truth of Matters in Requests for Admission Set Two Admitted as to Defendant Soo Sung Nam is GRANTED.

·       Plaintiff’s request for sanctions as to Defendant Chunil Park is also GRANTED in the amount of $847.50 to be paid by Defendant Chunil Park to Plaintiff’s counsel within thirty (30) days of notice of this order.¿

·       Plaintiff’s request for sanctions as to Defendant Soo Sung Nam is also GRANTED in the amount of $585.00 to be paid by Defendant Soo Sung Nam to Plaintiff’s counsel within thirty (30) days of notice of this order.¿

Background

            This is a commercial unlawful detainer case. On October 14, 2022, Plaintiff ROIC California LLC filed this the First Amended Complaint against Defendants Chunil Park d/b/a Kyoto Sushi, Soo Sung Nam d/b/a Kyoto Sushi and Does 1 through 20 (collectively “Defendants”) for injuries arising from (1) breach of written lease, (2) for account stated, and (3) money had and received. (Complaint p.3-4.)

 

On May 17, 2023, Plaintiff served Requests for Admission, Set Two on both Defendants by email and U.S. mail. (Yebri Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. A.) Defendants’ responses were due on June 18, 2023. (Id. at ¶ 3.) However, Plaintiff did not receive any responses or objections by the due date. (Id.) On June 22, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel sent a meet and confer letter to Defendants, by email and U.S. mail, requesting responses no later than June 29, 2023. (Id. at ¶ 4.)

 

Defendants’ counsel failed to respond to the meet and confer letter. (Id.) To date, no responses to the Requests for Admission have been provided by Defendant Soo Sung Nam. (Id. at ¶ 5.) However, on December 29, 2023, Defendant Chunil Park served his responses with his opposition. (Smith Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. 1.) Plaintiff filed the instant motion on June 29, 2023, compelling Defendant to have the requests for admission deemed admitted.  Plaintiff also requests monetary sanctions from each Defendant.

 

Defendant Chunil Park filed his opposition on December 29, 2023. Plaintiff filed its reply on January 10, 2024. However, Defendant Sung Nam d/b/a Kyoto Sushi did not file an opposition. Plaintiff filed a notice of non-opposition on January 10, 2024.

Analysis

 

Deem Admitted 

Where a party fails to timely respond to a request for admission, the propounding party may move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (b).)  The party who failed to respond waives any objections to the demand, unless the court grants them relief from the waiver, upon a showing that the party (1) has subsequently served a substantially compliant response, and (2) that the party’s failure to respond was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subds. (a)(1)-(2).)  The court shall grant a motion to deem admitted requests for admissions, “unless it finds that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).) 

 

Defendant Chunil Park d/b/a Kyoto Sushi

 

Here, on May 17, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel served Requests for Admission, Set Two, on Defendant Chunil Park. (Yebri Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. A.) On December 29, 2023, Defendant Chunil Park served his responses with his opposition to this motion. (Id. at ¶6.) 

            In his opposition, Defendant argues that Plaintiff’s motion must be denied as Mr. Park provided full verified responses. (Opp. p.3.) Moreover, Defendant Chunil Park argues that the failure to provide timely responses to the Requests for Admissions was the product of mistake, inadvertence, and excusable neglect. (Opp. p.5.)

            However, Plaintiff argues that the responses were not substantially compliant with  Section 2033.220 because they did not provide complete, substantive responses to each Request for Admission. (Reply p.1.) Plaintiff argues that the responses failed to comply with each element of Form Interrogatory 17.1 for each Request for Admission. (Id.)

Significantly, Defendant filed verified responses, not unverified responses, that satisfied C.C.P §2033.220 because they are complete and straightforward. (St. Mary v. Superior Ct., 223 Cal. App. 4th 762, 780, 167 Cal. Rptr. 3d 517, 531–32 (2014).) For example, the response to Form Interrogatory 17.1 states “Responding Party executed the lease agreement dated May 2005 attached as Exhibit A to Plaintiff’s Complaint. Responding Party did not execute any further leases or extensions. Parties with knowledge of these facts are Responding Party, Soo Sung Nam, and Propounding Party.”  

Thus, Plaintiff is not entitled to an order deeming Requests for Admissions, Set Two, admitted in regard to Defendant Chunil Park.

 

Defendant Soo Sung Nam d/b/a Kyoto Sushi

 

Here, on May 17, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel served Requests for Admission, Set Two, on Defendant Soo Sung Nam. (Yebri Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. A.) To date, Defendant Soo Sung Nam or his counsel have responded to the discovery requests. Therefore, Plaintiff is entitled to an order deeming Requests for Admissions, Set Two, admitted in regard to Defendant Soo Sung Nam.

 

Monetary Sanctions 

 

Where a party fails to provide a timely response to requests for admission, “[i]t is mandatory that the court impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) on the party or attorney, or both, whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated this motion.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).) 

 

Defendant Chunil Park d/b/a Kyoto Sushi

 

Here, Plaintiff’s attorney seeks $2,685.00 in monetary sanctions for five hours in preparing this motion and also hearing time, billed at $525.00 per hour plus a $60.00 court filing fee. (Yebri Decl.  ¶ 9.) Defendant argues that awarding the sanctions would not be in the interest of justice because the untimely response was due to his former counsel’s inadvertence, mistake, and excusable neglect. (Opp. p.6.) Moreover, he argues that he was not involved during the term of the Second Amendment to the original Lease which was when there was a potential breach. (Id.) Nevertheless, Defendant’s untimely response led to the filing of this motion and therefore, sanctions are mandatory.

 

¿However, the amount sought is greatly excessive given the simplicity of this Motion, even with the opposition and reply. Moreover, this motion is nearly identical to that of Defendant Soo Sung Nam’s motion. Thus, the Court finds $847.50, based on one and half hours of attorney time and the filing fee, to be reasonable.¿ 

 

Defendant Soo Sung Nam d/b/a Kyoto Sushi

 

Here, Plaintiff’s attorney seeks $2,685.00 in monetary sanctions for five hours in preparing this motion and also hearing time, billed at $525.00 per hour plus a $60.00 court filing fee. (Yebri Decl.  ¶ 9.) 

The Court finds Defendant’s failure to respond to Plaintiff’s discovery requests a misuse of the discovery process. The Court is also required to impose a monetary sanction on Defendant for his failure to respond to the Requests for Admission under Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280, subdivision (c).¿However, the amount sought is greatly excessive given the simplicity of this Motion and the lack of opposition and reply. Moreover, this motion is nearly identical to that of Defendant Chunil Park’s motion. Thus, the Court finds $585.00, based on one hour of attorney time and the filing fee, to be reasonable.¿ 

 

Conclusion

 

For the foregoing reasons, Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem the Truth of Matters in Requests for Admission Set Two Admitted as to Defendant Chunil Park is DENIED.

However, Plaintiff’s Motion to Deem the Truth of Matters in Requests for Admission Set Two Admitted as to Defendant Soo Sung Nam is GRANTED.

Plaintiff’s request for sanctions as to Defendant Chunil Park is also GRANTED in the amount of $847.50 to be paid by Defendant Chunil Park to Plaintiff’s counsel within thirty (30) days of notice of this order.¿

Plaintiff’s request for sanctions as to Defendant Soo Sung Nam is also GRANTED in the amount of $585.00 to be paid by Defendant Soo Sung Nam to Plaintiff’s counsel within thirty (30) days of notice of this order.

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: ORDER GRANTING THE RIGHT TO ORDER AND APPLICATION FOR WRIT OF ATTACHMENT

 

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

[Tentative] Ruling:   The court GRANTS the right to attach order and a writ of attachment.

 

Plaintiff, A & E Construction & Painting, Inc., seeks right to attach order and a writ of attachment against Defendant Santos Vasquez in the amount of $88,484.70.

 

Allegations in the Complaint

 

Plaintiff A&E Construction & Painting, Inc. (“Plaintiff”) filed the First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) on December 8, 2023, against Santos Vasquez (“Vasquez”), SMC Pools & General Construction, Inc. (“SMC”), and Does 1-10 (collectively, “Defendants”). Plaintiff claims that it entered into an oral agreement with Defendants for the sale of a 2010 Bobcat skip loader, model # S570, serial number ALM429632 (“Tractor 1”) which Defendants have failed to pay for. (FAC ¶¶ 13-19.) Moreover, Plaintiff claims that Defendants failed to return a 2021 Bobcat skip loader, Model S7 40, serial number B3BT16446 (“Tractor 2”) which they were storing. (FAC ¶¶ 24-25.) Lastly, Plaintiff alleges that by withholding Tractor 1 and Tractor 2, Defendants received a benefit from both items and were unjustly enriched. (FAC ¶¶ 31-32.)

 

The First Amended Complaint alleges causes of action for (1) breach of an oral contract, (2) conversion, and (3) common count- restitution for unjust enrichment. (FAC p.1.)

 

On December 21, 2023, Plaintiff applied for a right to attach order and a writ of attachment in the total amount of $88,484.70. 

 

STANDARD OF REVIEW

 

“Upon the filing of the complaint or at any time thereafter, the plaintiff may apply pursuant to this article for a right to attach order and a writ of attachment by filing an application for the order and writ with the court in which the action is brought.”  (CCP § 484.010.)

 

The application shall be executed under oath and must include:

 

(1)  a statement showing that the attachment is sought to secure the recovery on a claim upon which an attachment may be issued;

(2)  a statement of the amount to be secured by the attachment;

(3)  a statement that the attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based;

(4)  a statement that the applicant has no information or belief that the claim is discharged or that the prosecution of the action is stayed in a proceeding under the Bankruptcy Act (11 U.S.C. section 101 et seq.); and

(5)  a description of the property to be attached under the writ of attachment and a statement that the plaintiff is informed and believes that such property is subject to attachment.

 

(CCP § 484.020.)

 

“The application [for a writ of attachment] shall be supported by an affidavit showing that the plaintiff on the facts presented would be entitled to a judgment on the claim upon which the attachment is based.”  (CCP § 484.030.)  Statutory attachment procedures are purely creations of the legislature and as such “are subject to ‘strict construction.’”  (Hobbs v. Weiss (1999) 73 Cal.App.4th 76, 79 [citing Vershbow v. Reiner (1991) 231 Cal.App.3d 879, 882]; see also Nakasone v. Randall (1982) 129 Cal.App.3d 757, 761.)  A judge does not have authority to order any attachment that is not provided for by the attachment statutes.  (Jordan-Lyon Productions, Ltd. v. Cineplex Odeon Corp. (1994) 29 Cal.App.4th 1459, 1466.) “The declarations in the moving papers must contain evidentiary facts, stated ‘with particularity,’ and based on actual personal knowledge with all documentary evidence properly identified and authenticated.” (Hobbs, supra, 73 Cal.App.4th at p. 79–80 [citing CCP § 482.040].)  “In contested applications, the court must consider the relative merits of the positions of the respective parties and make a determination of the probable outcome of the litigation.”  (Hobbs, supra, 73 Cal.App.4th at p. 80 [ellipses and quotation marks omitted].)

 

The Court shall issue a right to attach order if the Court finds all of the following:

(1)   The claim upon which the attachment is based is one upon which an attachment may be issued.

(2)   The plaintiff has established the probable validity of the claim upon which the attachment is based.

(3)   The attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based.

(4)   The amount to be secured by the attachment is greater than zero.

 

(Code Civ. Proc. § 484.090.)

 

ANALYSIS

 

Probable Validity of Plaintiff’s Claims:

 

“A claim has ‘probable validity’ where it is more likely than not that the plaintiff will obtain a judgment against the defendant on that claim.” (CCP § 481.190.) 

 

Breach of Contract 

Plaintiff claims it is entitled to a writ of attachment and right to attach order against Defendant in connection with its cause of action for breach of the contract. (Dec. Alas ¶ 3.) “The elements of a breach of oral contract claim are the same as those for a breach of written contract: a contract; its performance or excuse for nonperformance; breach; and damages.” (Stockton Mortg., Inc. v. Tope (2014) 233 Cal.App.4th 437, 453.)

 

Here, Plaintiff claims it has sufficiently alleged a claim for breach of contract. For instance, Plaintiff states that (1) an oral contract existed between the parties for the sale of Tractor 1 because the invoice provided shows that Plaintiff owned Tractor 1 which is now in Defendant’s possession. (FAC p.4-5, Dec. Alas ¶ 3; Ex. A.) Further, Plaintiff provides that (2) it performed its part of the contract because it gave Defendant possession of Tractor 1. (Id. at ¶ 4.) Plaintiff argues that Defendant (3) breached the agreement because Mr. Vazquez failed to make the payments and failed to return Tractor 1. (Id. at ¶¶ 4-7.) Therefore, Plaintiff suffered damages (4) because Defendant still owes $44,484.70. (Id. at ¶ 9.) Defendant did not file an opposition.

Thus, Plaintiff satisfies the requirements for issuance of an attachment order by proving probable validity in its breach of contract claim. 

Common Count-Conversion

Conversion is “the wrongful exercise of dominion over the property of another.” Lee v. Hanley, (2015) 61 Cal. 4th 1225 (quotations omitted). To establish a claim for conversion, a plaintiff must plead: “(1) the plaintiff's ownership or right to possession of the property; (2) the defendant's conversion by a wrongful act or disposition of property rights; and (3) damages.” Id. (quotations omitted).

Here, the Plaintiff shows that he meets the conversion claim because he (1) provides an invoice for Tractor 2 showing that he owns it; (2) that Defendant kept Tractor 2 and used it without permission; and (3) Plaintiff suffered damages in the amount of at least $39,000. (FAC ¶¶ 23-27.) Defendant did not file an opposition.

Thus, Plaintiff satisfies the requirements for issuance of an attachment order by proving probable validity in its conversion claim. 

Unjust Enrichment

“The elements of an unjust enrichment claim are the receipt of a benefit and the unjust retention of the benefit at the expense of another.” (Peterson v. Cellco P’ship (2008) 164 Cal.App.4th 1583, 1593 [internal brackets and quotations omitted].)

 

Here, Plaintiff states that Defendant is unlawfully withholding Tractor 1 and Tractor 2 and is therefore benefitting from the property. (FAC ¶¶ 31-32.)

Thus, Plaintiff satisfies the requirements for issuance of an attachment order by proving probable validity in its unjust enrichment claim. 

Basis of Attachment:

 

The Court shall issue a right to attach order if the claim upon which the attachment is based is one upon which an attachment may be issued. (CCP § 484.090.) “[A]n attachment may be issued only in an action on a claim or claims for money, each of which is based upon a contract, express or implied, where the total amount of the claim or claims is a fixed or readily ascertainable amount not less than five hundred dollars ($500) exclusive of costs, interest, and attorney’s fees.” (CCP § 483.010, subd. (a).) “If the action is against a defendant who is a natural person, an attachment may be issued only on a claim which arises out of the conduct by the defendant of a trade, business, or profession.” (CCP § 483.010, subd. (c).)

 

Here, there is a claim for money based on an express oral contract because the parties entered into an oral contract on March 18, 2021. (Dec. Alas ¶ 3.) Moreover, Plaintiff provides evidence that Tractor 1 and Tractor 2 have a readily ascertainable amount above $500 exclusive of costs, interest, and attorney’s fees. (Id. at ¶¶ 17-18; Ex. A-Bobcat Invoice- Tractor 1; Ex.B- Bobcat Invoice Tractor 2.) Although the action is against Santos Vasquez who is a natural person, the claim arises out of his conduct in the construction trade. (Id. at ¶ 2.)

 

Additionally, based on the unopposed application, Plaintiff has established its entitlement to the requested right to attach order and writ of attachment.  (See CCP 484.090(a) & (b).)

 

Therefore, there is a basis for attachment.

 

Purpose and Amount of Attachment:

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 484.090 states that the Court shall issue a right to attach order if “the attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on the claim upon which the attachment is based . . . [and] the amount to be secured by the attachment is greater than zero.”

 

Here, Plaintiff attests on Form AT-105 that its application for attachment is not sought for a purpose other than the recovery on a claim upon which the attachments are based. (AT-105 ¶ 4.)  There is no indication that the application is sought for any other purpose. Accordingly, the Court finds that Plaintiff has complied with Code of Civil Procedure sections 484.020 and 484.090.

 

Subject Property:

 

Code Civil Procedure section 487.010(a) provides that “[w]here the defendant is a corporation, all corporate property for which a method of levy is provided” is subject to attachment. “Where the defendant is a natural person, the description of the property shall be reasonably adequate to permit the defendant to identify the specific property sought to be attached.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 484.020, subd. (e).) 

 

Here, Plaintiff seeks attachment of “One-2010 Bobcat skip loader, model # S570, serial number ALM429632” ("Tractor 1”) and “One-2021 Bobcat skip loader, Model S7 40, serial number B3BT16446” (“Tractor 2”). (AT-105 p.2.) Thus, Plaintiff has properly identified the property to attach against this Defendant.

 

CONCLUSION

 

For the foregoing reasons, the Court GRANTS the right to attach order and application for writ of attachment. 

 

Code Civil Procedure Section 489.210 requires the plaintiff to file an undertaking before issuance of a writ of attachment. Code Civil Procedure Section 489.220 provides that, unless an objection has been made, “the amount of an undertaking filed pursuant to this article shall be ten thousand dollars ($10,000).” Here, no undertaking has been submitted by the Plaintiff. Therefore, the granting of the applications is conditioned upon plaintiff posting an undertaking in the amount of $10,000.00 and submission of a proposed right to attach order and order for issuance of writ of attachment after hearing (Form AT-120).
