DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Department 26

 

 

ce-gh urbanite fund 2, llc,

                        Plaintiff,

            v.

 

DIGITALBRIDGE GROUP, INC.; DIGITALBRIDGE OPERATING COMPANY, LLC; CNI CENTURY PLAZA MM, LLC; CDCF IV CENTURY MEZZ, LLC, et al.

                        Defendants

 

  Case No.:  23STCV20554

 

  Hearing Date:  November 15, 2023

 

     [TENTATIVE] order RE:

application for an order permitting andrew R. Kurland and sheron korpus to appear as counsel pro hac vice

 

 

 

Andrew R. Kurland (“Kurland”) and Sheron Korpus (“Korpus”) each apply to appear as counsel pro hac vice for Defendants DigitalBridge Group, Inc., DigitalBridge Operating Company, LLC, CNI Century Plaza MM, LLC, and CDCF IV Century Mezz, LLC.  The applications are unopposed.

Pursuant to California Rules of Court, Rule 9.40, attorneys licensed and in good standing in other states may, upon the California court’s approval, appear as counsel pro hac vice in a pending case if an active member of the state bar is associated as attorney of record.  (CRC 9.40(a).)  

Kurland is an active member, in good standing, of the State Bar of New York.  (Kurland Decl. ¶¶ 5, 7.)  Kurland is also admitted to practice in and is in good standing with the Supreme Court of the State of New York, Appellate Division, First Judicial Department since 2010, the U.S. Court of Appeals, Second Circuit since 2016, the U.S. District Court, Southern District of New York since 2010, the U.S. District Court, Eastern District of New York since 2010, and the U.S. District Court, Western District of New York since 2010.  (Kurland Decl. ¶¶ 6-7.)  In addition, Kurland states that he resides in Brooklyn, New York and has an office in New York, New York.  (Kurland Decl. ¶¶ 2-4.)  Kurland states that, within the last two years, he has only applied to appear pro hac vice in the lead action CE-GH Urbanite Fund 2, LLC v. David Belford et al., No. 22STCV20842, which this department granted between the filing of the instant application and the hearing date.  (Kurland Decl. ¶ 8.)  Thus, Kurland satisfies the eligibility requirements of CRC 9.40(a).

Korpus is an active member, in good standing, of the State of New York.  (Korpus Decl. ¶ 5.)  Korpus is also admitted to practice in and is in good standing with the Supreme Court of the State of New York, Appellate Division, First Judicial Department since 1997, the U.S. District Court, Southern District of New York since 1997, the U.S. District Court, Eastern District of New York since 1997, the U.S. Court of Appeals, Second Circuit since 2004, the U.S. Court of Appeals, Third Circuit since 2010, the U.S. Court of Appeals, Federal Circuit since 1997, the U.S. Court of Appeals, District of Columbia Circuit since 2011, and the United States Supreme Court since 2006.  (Korpus Decl. ¶¶ 6-7.)  In addition, Korpus resides in New York, New York and has an office in New York, New York.  (Korpus Decl. ¶¶ 3-4.)  Within the last two years, Korpus has only applied to appear pro hac vice in the lead action CE-GH Urbanite Fund 2, LLC v. David Belford et al., No. 22STCV20842, which this department granted. (Korpus Decl. ¶ 8.)  Thus, Korpus satisfies the eligibility requirements of CRC 9.40(a).

Kurland’s and Korpus’s applications successfully address all six content requirements outlined in CRC 9.40(d).  Specifically, Kurland and Korpus provide in their respective applications: (1) their residence and office address (Kurland Decl. ¶¶ 3-4; Korpus Decl. ¶¶ 3-4); (2) the courts to which Kurland and Korpus have been admitted to practice and the dates of admission (Kurland Decl. ¶ 6; Korpus Decl. ¶ 6); (3) that Kurland and Korpus are members in good standing in those courts (Kurland Decl. ¶ 7; Korpus Decl. ¶ 7); (4) that they are not currently suspended or disbarred in any court (Kurland Decl. ¶ 7; Korpus Decl. ¶ 7); (5) the title of each court and cause in which the applicants have filed an application to appear as counsel pro hac vice in this state in the preceding two years, and whether or not it was granted if any (Kurland Decl. ¶ 8; Korpus Decl. ¶ 8); and (6) the name, address, and telephone number of an active member, Daniel A. Saunders of the State Bar of California who is the current counsel of record for Defendants DigitalBridge Group, Inc., DigitalBridge Operating Company, LLC, CNI Century Plaza MM, LLC, and CDCF IV Century Mezz, LLC.  (Kurland Decl. ¶ 9; Korpus Decl. ¶ 9).

Furthermore, Kurland and Korpus each demonstrate that they meet the requirement of CRC 9.40(c)(1). That rule provides: “a person desiring to appear as counsel pro hac vice in a superior court must file with the court a verified application together with proof of service . . . of a copy of the application and of the notice on all parties who have appeared in the cause and on the State Bar of California at its San Francisco office.”  (Kurland Decl. ¶ 12; Korpus Decl. ¶ 12.)

 

 

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

The applications of Andrew R. Kurland and Sheron Korpus to appear as counsel pro hac vice for Defendants DigitalBridge Group, Inc., DigitalBridge Operating Company, LLC, CNI Century Plaza MM, LLC, and CDCF IV Century Mezz, LLC are GRANTED.

Moving Parties are to give notice and file proof of service of such.
