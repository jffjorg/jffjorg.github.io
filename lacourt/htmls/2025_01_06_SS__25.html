On December 18, 2020, Plaintiff Chuck Peters (“Plaintiff”), proceeding pro se, filed a Complaint against Defendant Equinox Fitness Century City, Incorporated (“Defendant”) alleging 32 causes of action: Theft; Deceit; Breach of Contract (Oral); Conduct Unbecoming of a Government Licensed Health and Fitness Club; Breach of Contract (Written); Failure to Accommodate; Failure to Engage in the Interactive Process; Discrimination on the Basis of Race, Religion, Disability; Failure to Prevent Discrimination and Retaliation; Intent; Inducing Breach of Contract; Intentional Interference with Contractual Relations; Conspiracy; Physical Pain, Mental Suffering, and Emotional Distress; Punitive Damages; Fraud; Malice; Oppression; Despicable Conduct; Abuse of Process; Breach of Implied covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing; Retaliation; Wrongful Termination of Written Agreement; Specific Performance; Injunctive Relief; Negligence/Liability from Dangerous Conditions on Private Property; Unlawful Conversion; Violation of Civil Rights Under 42 U.S.C. Section 1983 and Violation of Fifth Amendment; Violation of Civil Rights Under 42 U.S.C. Section 1983 and Violation of Fifth Amendment “Private Taking of Property”; Denial of Equal Protection as Applied to All Causes of Action; Declaratory Relief; and Unlawful Invasion of Privacy. (Compl., pp. 1-2.)

 

The case was initially assigned in Northwest District, Van Nuys Courthouse East, Department. 

 

On July 26, 2023, Default Judgment was entered for Plaintiff and against defendant Equinox Fitness Century City, Incorporated in the amount of $3,150.

 

On July 17, 2024, Defendant filed a Motion to Set Aside Default and Default Judgment. On October 4, 2024, the Court granted Defendant’s Motion to Set Aside Default and Default judgment. Defendant was granted 60-days to file a responsive pleading. (Minute Order, 10.4.2024.)

 

On October 4, 2024, pursuant to the stipulation of the parties, the case was ordered reclassified as civil limited jurisdiction case. The case was referred to Department 1 for assignment. (Minute Order, 10.4.2024.)

The Court set an Order to Show Cause Re: Why Sanctions of up to $260 should not be imposed based on Plaintiff’s failure to appear on 11/15/2024, which is scheduled for 12/16/2024 at 9:30 a.m. in Department 25 at Spring Street Courthouse.

 

On December 4, 2024, Defendant filed the instant Demurrer to Plaintiff’s Complaint (“Demurrer”) as to every cause of action. Defendant also filed the instant Motion to Strike (the “Motion”) as to portions of Plaintiff’s Complaint.

 

No Opposition was filed.

 

II.                 Legal Standard

 

“As a general matter, in a demurrer proceeding, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or via proper judicial notice. (Donabedian v. Mercury Ins. Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994.) “A demurrer tests the pleading alone, and not the evidence or facts alleged.” (E-Fab, Inc. v. Accountants, Inc. Servs. (2007) 153 Cal.App.4th 1308, 1315.) As such, the court assumes the truth of the complaint’s properly pleaded or implied factual allegations. (Ibid.) The only issue a demurrer is concerned with is whether the complaint, as it stands, states a cause of action. (Hahn v. Mirda (2007) 147 Cal.App.4th 740, 747.)

A general demurrer may be brought under Code of Civil Procedure section 430.10, subdivision (e) if insufficient facts are stated to support the cause of action asserted or under section 430.10, subdivision (a), where the court has no jurisdiction of the subject of the cause of action alleged in the pleading. All other grounds listed in Section 430.10, including uncertainty under subdivision (f), are special demurrers. Special demurrers are not allowed in limited jurisdiction courts. (Code Civ. Proc., § 92, subd. (c).) 

Where a demurrer is sustained, leave to amend must be allowed where there is a reasonable possibility of successful amendment. (Goodman v. Kennedy (1976) 18 Cal.3d 335, 348.) The burden is on the plaintiff to show the court that a pleading can be amended successfully. (Id.; Lewis v. YouTube, LLC (2015) 244 Cal.App.4th 118, 226.) However, “[i]f there is any reasonable possibility that the plaintiff can state a good cause of action, it is error to sustain a demurrer without leave to amend.” (Youngman v. Nevada Irrigation Dist. (1969) 70 Cal.2d 240, 245).

III.              Discussion

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 430.41 requires that “[b]efore filing a demurrer pursuant to this chapter, the demurring party shall meet and confer in person, by telephone, or by video conference with the party who filed the pleading that is subject to demurrer for the purpose of determining whether an agreement can be reached that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41, subd. (a).) The parties are to meet and confer at least five days before the date the responsive pleading is due. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41, subd. (a)(2).) Thereafter, the demurring party shall file and serve a declaration detailing their meet and confer efforts. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41, subd. (a)(3).) 

The Demurrer is accompanied by a meet and confer declaration as required by Code of Civil Procedure section 430.41, subdivision (a). (Buddingh, Jr. Decl.) According to the declaration, Defendant’s counsel sent a letter to Plaintiff, which invited Plaintiff to meet and confer with Defendant regarding the instant matter. (Buddingh, Jr. Decl., ¶ 2, Exh. A.) Additionally, the correspondence also included a copy of a letter from 2017 regarding a similar claim filed by Plaintiff against Defendant, and the written correspondence was served by FedEx to the address listed for Plaintiff in the Complaint. (Buddingh, Jr. Decl., ¶ 2.) Defendant states Plaintiff has not responded to the written correspondence, and Defendant has been unable to telephone Plaintiff “as the caption of the Complaint fails to list a contact telephone number for Plaintiff in violation of California Rule of Court 2.111(1).” (Buddingh, Jr. Decl., ¶ 2.)

 

While the Court may not overrule a demurrer for failure to adequately meet and confer (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41(a)(4), the Court may not let the requirement go wholly ignored. Accordingly, the parties are ordered to exchange telephone numbers and commence meet and confer via telephonic conference in the next five (5) days. The demurring party will initiate the call, and the call must discuss each cause of action. Ten (10) days later, a second meet and confer will take place between the parties. Thereafter, the demurring party is ordered to file and serve a declaration detailing the meet and confer efforts made, the discussions that transpired during meet and confer, and the responses given for each argument discussed. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41.)

II.                 Legal Standard & Discussion

 

Code of Civil Procedure, section 581, subdivision (f)(2) provides that upon motion of any party, the Court may dismiss the Complaint when a defendant’s demurrer is sustained with leave to amend and the plaintiff fails to amend the pleading within the time allowed by the court. Here, more than 20 days has passed since the ruling sustaining the Demurrer to the Complaint with leave to amend, and Plaintiff has not filed an Amended Complaint. As Plaintiff has neither filed an Amended Complaint nor an Opposition to the instant Motion to date, Defendant’s request to dismiss the Complaint is granted with prejudice.  “The failure to amend … is an admission that plaintiff has state the case as strongly as he can and there are no facts that could be alleged to cure the defect.” (Cano v. Accordingly, Defendant’s Motion is GRANTED.

ANALYSIS:

 

I.                    Background

 

On August 29, 2024, Rita Rodriguez (“Plaintiff”), proceeding pro se, initiated this action against Defendants Charming Bunny (“Charming Bunny”) and Mengtong Zhang (“Zhang”) (“Defendants”), alleging causes of action for “Stalking, Harassment, Forced Abortion, and Wrongful Termination.” (Compl., p. 1.)

 

On September 3, 2024, Plaintiff filed proof of service by mail of the Summons and Complaint and accompanying documents on Charming Bunny. (9/3/24 Proof of Service, ¶¶ 4, 5, 6, Attachment to Proof of Service.)

 

On October 16, 2024, Specially Appearing Defendants filed the instant Motion to Quash Service of Summons and Complaint (the “Motion”) and Demurrer to the Complaint (“Demurrer”).

 

On November 6, 2024, Plaintiff filed an Opposition (titled “Response”) to the Motion and Demurrer.

 

On December 26, 2024, Defendants filed a Reply.

 

II.                 Procedural Issues

 

As an initial matter, the Court notes the Motion is not timely. A motion to quash must be made by the last day for a party to file a responsive pleading, or within any further time the court allows for good cause. (Code Civ. Proc., § 418.10, subd. (a).) Service being effectuated by September 3, 2024, the last day for Defendants to respond to the Complaint was October 3, 2024. However, given the defects in service of the Summons and Complaint, the Court finds good cause exists and exercises its discretion to address the untimely Motion on the merits. (Code Civ. Proc., § 418.10, subd. (a).)

 

Additionally, Defendants state that a separate Request for Judicial Notice and “certified copies of the files” in another action are filed with the Demurrer. (Motion, p. 4); however, no such documents are attached to the Demurrer or the Motion. The Court cannot rule on a request and documents that are not before it.

 

III.              Legal Standard & Discussion

 

A.    Motion to Quash Service of Summons and Complaint

 

Defendants move to quash service of process on the grounds that “[w]hen multiple actions concerning the same controversy are pending between the same parties in more than one case in California Superior Court, the first court to assume jurisdiction has ‘exclusive and continuing jurisdiction over the subject matter and all parties involved until such time as all necessarily related matters have been resolved.’ ” (Motion, p. 9, no citation included in Motion.) Defendants contend that since there is another case between the parties (Case No. 24STCV21003, Rodriguez v. Romero, et al.) regarding the same subject matter and where improper service was first effected, the instant action should be abated and the Complaint dismissed. (Motion, pp. 9-10, citing People ex rel. Garamendi v. American Autoplan, Inc. (1993) 20 Cal.App.4th 760, 769-772; Consumer Advocacy Group, Inc. v. ExxonMobil Corp. (2008) 168 Cal.App.4th 675, 682.)  

 

In Opposition, Plaintiff states the following: “Plaintiff hereby requests the dismissal and complete opposition of this Motion to Quash of the Summons and the complaint on the grounds: The Court lacks jurisdiction over the Plaintiff. Code Civ. Proc. Section 418.10(a)(1).”[1] (Motion, p. 2.) While stated in relation to the Demurrer, Plaintiff further contends that she “properly served the actual lawsuit. Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure 684.210-684220,” and “[Plaintiff] herself was not properly served the motion to quash.”[2]

 

In Reply, Defendants reiterate many of the same arguments in the Motion as well as further dispute the veracity of Plaintiff’s factual allegations. (Reply, pp. 1, 3-4.) Defendants also state that, contrary to Plaintiff’s claim otherwise, the Notice of Motion was properly served on Plaintiff. (Reply, p. 2.)

 

“A defendant, on or before the last day of his or her time to plead or within any further time that the court may for good cause allow, may serve and file a notice of motion for one or more of the following purposes: To quash service of summons on the ground of lack of jurisdiction of the court over him or her.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 418.10, subd. (a)(1), emphasis added.) 

 

 “When a defendant challenges the court’s personal jurisdiction on the ground of improper service of process ‘the burden is on the plaintiff to prove the existence of jurisdiction by proving, inter alia, the facts requisite to an effective service.’ ” (Summers v. McClanahan (2006) 140 Cal.App.4th 403, 413; see also Lebel v. Mai (2012) 210 Cal.App.4th 1154, 1160.) However, a proof of service containing a declaration from a registered process server invokes a rebuttable presumption affecting the burden of producing evidence of the facts stated in the return. (Cal. Evid. Code, § 647; see¿American Express Centurion Bank v. Zara¿(2011) 199 Cal.App.4th 383, 390.) 

 

Here, Defendants move to quash service of the Summons and Complaint on the grounds that the instant court lacks subject matter jurisdiction and personal jurisdiction over the Defendants. As Plaintiff’s proof of service was not attested to by a registered process server, the burden of proving service in conformity with the statutory requirements falls to Plaintiff. (Summers, supra, 140 Cal.App.4th at p. 413.)

 

While Plaintiff opposes the Motion in its entirety, Plaintiff does not provide any evidence or argument to prove service was done in compliance with statutory requirements. As to Plaintiff’s contention that Defendants’ improper service of the Motion renders Plaintiffs’ service of the Summons and Complaint proper, service of Defendants’ Motion does not impact the determination of whether Plaintiff properly served the Summons and Complaint on Defendants.

 

Additionally, the Court notes Plaintiff’s proof of service was filed on form POS-040, which states: “Do not use this form to show service of a summons and complaint or for electronic service” (emphasis added), and Plaintiff served the Summons and Complaint by mail only.

 

Accordingly, the Court finds Plaintiff has failed to meet her burden of demonstrating service of the Summons and Complaint on Defendants was in conformance with statutory requirements.

 

Thus, Defendants’ Motion is GRANTED.

 

IV.              Conclusion & Order

 

Specially Appearing Defendants’ Mengtong Zhang and Charming Bunny’s Motion to Quash Service of Summons and Complaint is GRANTED.

 

Based on the Court’s Ruling on the Motion to Quash the Demurrer is MOOT.
