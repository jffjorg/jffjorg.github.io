Hearing Date: June 20, 2024

Department 54, Judge Maurice A. Leiter

Motion to Expunge Lis Pendens

Moving Party: Defendant Adnan Mohamed Aljojo

Responding Party: Plaintiff Nadia Kamel Salem

 

T/R:     DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO EXPUNGE LIS PENDENS IS GRANTED.

 

THE REQUEST FOR ATTORNEY’S FEES IS GRANTED IN THE AMOUNT OF $10,500.00, PAYABLE WITHIN 30 DAYS.

 

DEFENDANTS TO NOTICE.

 

If the parties wish to submit on the tentative, please email the courtroom at SMCdept54@lacourt.org with notice to opposing counsel (or self-represented party) before 8:00 am on the day of the hearing.

 

The Court considers the moving papers, opposition, and reply.

 

BACKGROUND

               

On April 21, 2022, Plaintiff sued Defendants for cancellation of instruments. Plaintiff is Defendant Aljojo’s mother. Plaintiff alleges Defendant recorded a fraudulent deed transferring Plaintiff’s real property to Defendant.

 

ANALYSIS

 

When a motion to expunge a lis pendens is filed, the burden is on the opposing party to show that the complaint contains allegations of a real-property claim, and to show the probable validity of the claim based upon a preponderance of evidence.  (CCP §§ 405.32, 405.31, 405.30; Howard S. Wright Construction Co. v. Superior Court (2003) 106 Cal. App. 4th 314, 319.)  “[T]he court shall order that the notice be expunged if the court finds that the claimant has not established by a preponderance of the evidence the probable validity of the real property claim. The court shall not order an undertaking to be given as a condition of expunging the notice if the court finds the claimant has not established the probable validity of the real property claim.”  (CCP § 405.32.)

 

Defendant Adnan Aljojo moves to expunge the lis pendens recorded by Plaintiff against the property subject to this action on the grounds that the lis pendens was not properly served or filed, and that Plaintiff cannot establish the probable validity of the real property claim.

 

CCP § 405.22 requires that a lis pendens be served on any adverse parties by certified mail before recordation. CCP § 405.23 states that a lis pendens is void if it fails to comply with the notice requirements of CCP § 405.22. Defendant represents that Plaintiff did not serve the lis pendens before recordation. In opposition, Plaintiff concedes this. As the lis pendens is void for lack of service, the Court need not address the arguments relating to the validity of Plaintiff’s claims.

 

Defendant’s motion to expunge lis pendens is GRANTED.

 

CCP § 405.38 provides for an award of attorney’s fees to the prevailing party in a motion to expunge lis pendens. Defendant requests $10,500.00 in attorney’s fees for 20 hours of work at $525.00 per hour. The Court finds this reasonable. The request for attorney’s fees is GRANTED in the amount of $10,500.00.

Motion to Quash Service of Summons

Responding Party: Plaintiff Monarch, LLC

 

T/R:     SPECIALLY APPEARING DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO QUASH SERVICE OF SUMMONS IS DENIED.

 

TOTAL TO NOTICE. 

If the parties wish to submit on the tentative, please email the courtroom at SMCdept54@lacourt.org with notice to opposing counsel (or self-represented party) before 8:00 am on the day of the hearing.

 

The Court considers the moving papers, opposition and reply.

 

“A defendant, on or before the last day of his or her time to plead or within any further time that the court may for good cause allow, may serve and file a notice of motion for one or more of the following purposes: (1) To quash service of summons on the ground of lack of jurisdiction of the court over him or her.”  (CCP § 418.10(a)(1).)  “When a motion to quash is properly brought, the burden of proof is placed upon the plaintiff to establish the facts of jurisdiction by a preponderance of the evidence.” (Aquila, Inc. v. Sup. Ct. (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 556, 568.)

Specially Appearing Defendant Total Resources International, Inc. moves to quash service of the summons and complaint on the grounds that they were not properly served. The proof of service shows that Total’s agent for service, George del Carmen Rivera, was served by substitute service on Lito Capinia, a shipping manager for Total. Total asserts that this was improper because Capinia is not in charge of the office where Rivera works. Total represents that Capinia works in a different building from Rivera and is not in charge of Rivera’s office.

 

In opposition, Plaintiff asserts that Total’s secretary of state documents do not distinguish between two different buildings and argues that substitute service need only be made on someone “apparently” in charge. Capinia declares that he told the process server that he was the shipping manager. This is sufficient to show service was affected on someone “apparently” in charge.

 

Defendant’s motion to quash is DENIED.
