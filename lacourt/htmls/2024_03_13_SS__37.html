DEPARTMENT 37 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23STUD10315    Hearing Date: March 13, 2024    Dept: 37

HEARING DATE:                 Wednesday, March 13, 2024

CASE NUMBER:                   23STUD10315

CASE NAME:                        Wilshire Crescent Heights, LLC v. Michael Cherbakov

MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant Michael Cherbakov

OPPOSING PARTY:             Plaintiff Wilshire Crescent Heights,

TRIAL DATE:                       

PROOF OF SERVICE:           OK

                                                                                                                                                           

PROCEEDING:                      Demurrer to Second Amended Complaint with Leave to Amend

OPPOSITION:                        3 March 2024

REPLY:                                  None Filed.

 

TENTATIVE:                         Defendant’ demurrer is overruled. Defendant’s motion to strike is denied. Defendant to file an Answer within 10 days and to give notice. The court sets an OSC Re: Answer for March 28, 2024, at 8:30 a.m. and continues the Case Management Conference to that date and time.  Defendant to give notice.

                                                                                                                                                           

 

Background

 

On August 10, 2023, Wilshire Crescent Heights, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed a Complaint against Michael Cherbakov (“Defendant”) for unlawful detainer. On October 13, 2023, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint (“FAC”). On December 26, 2023, Plaintiff filed a Second Amended Complaint (“SAC”).

On January 4, 2024, Defendant filed a demurrer with a motion to strike.  Plaintiff opposes the motion. The matter is now before the court.

Discussion

 

I.         Legal Standard

 

A.        Demurrer 

 A demurrer is an objection to a pleading, the grounds for which are apparent from either the face of the complaint or a matter of which the court may take judicial notice. (CCP § 430.30(a); see also Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318.)¿“To survive a demurrer, the complaint need only allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action; each evidentiary fact that might eventually form part of the plaintiff’s proof need not be alleged.”¿(C.A. v. William S. Hart Union High School Dist. (2012) 53 Cal.4th 861, 872.)¿For the purpose of testing the sufficiency of the cause of action, the demurrer admits the truth of all material facts properly pleaded.¿ (Aubry v. Tri-City Hospital Dist. (1992) 2 Cal.4th 962, 966-967.)¿A demurrer “does not admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law.”¿(Daar v. Yellow Cab Co. (1967) 67 Cal.2d 695, 713.)¿¿ 

 

B.        Motion to Strike 

 

¿Any party, within the time allowed to respond to a pleading may serve and file a notice of motion to strike the whole or any part thereof. (CCP, § 435(b)(1); CRC, rule 3.1322(b).) The court may, upon a motion or at any time in its discretion and upon terms it deems proper: (1) strike out any irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading; or (2) strike out all or any part of any pleading not drawn or filed in conformity with the laws of California, a court rule, or an order of the court. (CCP, § 436(a)-(b); Stafford v. Shultz (1954) 42 Cal.2d 767, 782 [“Matter in a pleading which is not essential to the claim is surplusage; probative facts are surplusage and may be stricken out or disregarded”].)¿¿¿¿ 

 

C.        Leave to Amend 

 

“Where the defect raised by a motion to strike or by demurrer is reasonably capable of cure, leave to amend is routinely and liberally granted to give the plaintiff a chance to cure the defect in question.” (CLD Construction, Inc. v. City of San Ramon (2004) 120 Cal.App.4th 1141, 1146.) The burden is on the complainant to show the Court that a pleading can be amended successfully. (Goodman v. Kennedy (1976) 18 Cal.3d 335, 348.)¿¿¿ 

 

II.        Demurrer

 

            A.        Defendant Failed to Comply with the Meet and Confer Requirement

 

The court notes that Defendant has failed to file a declaration showing compliance with the mandatory requirements to meet and confer as required CCP §§ 430.41 and 435.5(a). The court has the discretion to continue the hearing and order the parties to meaningfully meet and confer prior to this hearing. (See Dumas v. Los Angeles County Bd. of Supervisors (2020) 45 Cal. App. 5th 348, 355.) “Any determination by the court that the meet and confer process was insufficient shall not be grounds to overrule or sustain a demurrer.” (CCP § 430.41(a)(4).) As the failure to meet and confer does not constitute grounds to overrule a demurrer, the court continues to the merits.  

 

B.        Defendant’s Demurrer Fails to Dispose of an Entire Cause of Action

 

The SAC alleges that Plaintiff is the owner of 6245 Wilshire Blvd. #PH205 Los Angeles, CA (the “Subject Property”). (SAC ¶¶ 1-4.) Plaintiff rented the Subject Property to Defendant for $14,402.00 per month. (SAC ¶ 1, 6, Ex. 1.) The SAC asserts that the tenancy terminated for at-fault cause under Civ. Code § 1946.2(b)(1) and a Notice to Pay Rent in 3 days or Quit in 30 Days was served. (SAC ¶¶ 8, 9.) The 3 Day Notice to Quit was served on Defendant by posting a copy on the Subject Property on June 21, 2023, giving a copy to a person residing at the premises, and mailing a copy to the defendant on June 21, 2023. (SAC ¶ 10(a)(3).)

 

Defendant demurs to the SAC on the basis that Plaintiff is not entitled to holdover damages until after the 30-day notice period has expired and the tenant has not vacated the premises. Therefore, Defendant argues that holdover damages began to accrue on July 22, 2023, the day of the expiration of the notice period, and not on July 1, 2023.

 

The court need not decide what damages are due, as the issue of damages owed presents a triable issue of material fact not subject to demurrer. (Fuhrman v. California Satellite Systems (1986) 179 Cal.App.3d 408, 422.) Moreover, a demurrer does not lie in a portion of a cause of action. (PH II, Inc. v. Superior Court (1995) 33 Cal.App.4th 1680, 1682.) The fact that Plaintiff may improperly requested holdover damages that accrue on wrong date, does not dispose of Plaintiff’s unlawful detainer cause of action. Accordingly, the demurrer is overruled.

 

C.        Defendant’s Motion to Strike Improperly Seeks to Strike the Entire SAC

 

Defendant moves to strike the entire SAC on the basis that it does not have proper verification pursuant to CCP §§ 446(a) and 1166. A “complaint shall [b]e verified and include the typed or printed name of the person verifying the complaint.” (CCP § 1166(a)(1).) 

 

Plaintiff does not dispute that the verification is not signed by the Plaintiff. Instead, Plaintiff maintains that Defendant is precluded from filing a motion to strike because the demurrer and the motion to strike must be raised at the same time. However, Plaintiff fails to show that the demurrer and motion to strike were not filed simultaneously. Therefore, Plaintiff’s argument is without merit.

 

Nevertheless, the motion to strike is denied. It is improper to strike a whole cause of action pursuant to a motion to strike. (Quiroz v. Seventh Ave. Center (2006) 140 Cal.App.4th 1256, 1281.) Such challenges must be made by demurrer. (Id.) Defendant should have raised the issue of the SAC’s lack of verification by way of demurrer, not via a motion to strike.

Conclusion

 

Defendant’ demurrer is overruled. Defendant’s motion to strike is denied. Defendant to file an

Answer within 10 days and to give notice. The court sets an OSC Re: Answer for March 28,

2024, at 8:30 a.m. and continues the Case Management Conference to that date and time.

Defendant to give notice.
