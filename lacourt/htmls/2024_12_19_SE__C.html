Case Number: 22NWCV00691    Hearing Date: December 19, 2024    Dept: C

Melendez-Rodriguez v. Citistaff Solutions, Inc. 22NWCV00691

This wrongful termination action was filed on August 10, 2022. On January 10, 2023, this Court granted Defendants Motion to Compel Arbitration and stayed proceedings in this case pending conclusion of arbitration. On December 18, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Motion for Reconsideration of this Court's January 10, 2023 Order, citing a change in law with the recent decision from the Second District Court of Appeal of Kader v. Southern California Medical Center, Inc. (2024) 99 Cal.App.5th 214. Plaintiff's Motion for Reconsideration of this Court's January 10, 2023 Order is scheduled to be heard on Tuesday, September 30, 2025. Arbitration is set for January 27-31, 2025. 

Plaintiff now moves ex parte to advance the hearing date on Plaintiff's Motion for Reconsideration and/or a Stay of Arbitration Proceedings. 

The Court notes that this Court retains jurisdiction over this case for reconsideration of it's order compelling arbitration, and can reconsider it's Order upon the emergence of a new law. (See Malek v. Blue Cross (2004) 121 Cal.App.4th 44, 60.) 

Plaintiff's Ex Parte Application to Advance the Hearing on her Motion for Reconsideration of this Court's January 10, 2023 Order Granting Defendants' Motion to Compel Arbitration is GRANTED. Plaintiff's Motion for Reconsideration is ADVANCED from Tuesday, September 30, 2025 to today's date and CONTINUED to Tuesday, February 4, 2025 at 10:30 a.m. in Dept. SE-C. Opposition and Reply to be filed and served per Code in accordance with the new hearing date. Arbitration is STAYED pending a ruling on Plaintiff's Motion for Reconsideration. Moving Party to give notice. 

Case Number: 22NWCV01760    Hearing Date: December 19, 2024    Dept: C

Haynie v. American Honda Motor Co., Inc. 22NWCV01760

This lemon law action was filed on December 29, 2022. Trial is scheduled to begin on March 12, 2025. Plaintiff's Motion to Compel Compliance with the Court's October 2, 2024 Order is set to be heard after trial on September 16, 2025. 

Plaintiff moves ex parte to advance the hearing on Plaintiff's Motion to Compel Compliance to be heard before the discovery cutoff and trial dates. 

Plaintiffs' Ex Parte Application to Advance the Hearing Date on Plaintiffs' Motion to Compel Compliance is GRANTED in part. The Court's congested calendar cannot accomodate Plaintiffs' request to have the matter heard before the current trial date. However, Plaintiffs' Motion to Compel Compliance is ADVANCED from Tuesday, September 16, 2025 to today's date and CONTINUED to Tuesday, April 15, 2025 at 10:30 a.m. in Dept. SE-C. Oppostion and Reply to be filed and served per Code in accordance with the new hearing date. Absent stipulation from all parties, this Court cannot continue the current trial date. If the parties are unable to informally resolve their discovery dispute or if discovery is ongoing, the parties are not foreclosed from seeking a trial continuance in Dept. SE-F. Moving Party to give notice. 

Case Number: 23NWCP00337    Hearing Date: December 19, 2024    Dept: C

City of South Gate v. Delacruz 23NWCP00337

This public nuisance action was filed on August 14, 2023. Respondent Benjamin De La Cruz ("De La Cruz") defaulted on October 20, 2023. A Receiver was appointed on December 15, 2023 to remediate the Subject Property. 

On December 13, 2024. The Receiver's Application for Issuance of Order Authorizing and Confirming Sale of Receivership Real Property Located at 10325 California Avenue, South Gate, CA 90280 was granted. Despite having received notice of the Receiver's Ex Parte Application to Sell the Property, De La Cruz did not appear. 

De La Cruz's first noticed Motion to Set Aside Default was denied on the merits on April 17, 2024. 

On October 16, 2024, De La Cruz filed a second noticed Motion to Set Aside Default, which is scheduled to be heard on August 6, 2025. Since this is De La Cruz's second attempt at moving to set aside his default, the Motion is actually an untimely and disguised Motion for Reconsideration of this Court's April 17, 2024 Order. 

As of December 13, 2024 (when the Receiver's Ex Parte Application to Authorize and Confirm the Sale of the Property was heard and granted), De La Cruz had not moved ex parte to advance the hearing date on his Motion to Set Aside Default. 

De La Cruz filed a Notice of Appeal on December 13, 2024. The Court notes that the filing of a Notice of Appeal in this action does not warrant an automatic stay. A stay may be sought by noticed motion, and will only be granted upon the payment of an approved undertaking. 

On December 16, 2024, this Court denied without prejudice De La Cruz's ex parte application to stay enforcement of this Court's December 13, 2024 Order approving the sale of the real property located at 10325 California Ave., South Gate, CA 90280 pending appeal. "Given that Respondent De La Cruz's is currently in default, the only motion he may property make is to set aside the default. As De La Cruz's default has been entered and not set aside, this Court lacks jurisdiction to address any action by the defaulted party other than a motion to vacate entry of default. (See, e.g. W.A. Rose Co. v. Municipal Court for Oakland-Piedmont Judicial Dist. (1959) 176 Cal.App.2d 67, 72.) “[T]he entry of the default terminates [the defaulting defendant’s] rights to take any further affirmative steps in the litigation until either the default is set aside or a default judgment is entered.” (City of Riverside v. Horspool (2014) 223 Cal.App.4th 670, 681.)" (12/16/24 M.O.) 

Respondent DE LA CRUZ's Ex Parte Application to Advance the Hearing Date on the Second Noticed Motion to Set Aside the Default is GRANTED. The Motion for Reconsideration/Motion to Set Aside Default is ADVANCED from Wednesday, August 6, 2025 to today's date and CONTINUED to Wednesday, January 29, 2025 at 10:30 a.m. in Dept. SE-C. Opposition and Reply to be filed and served per Code in accordance with the new hearing date. The request to stay enforcement of the December 13, 2024 Order is DENIED. "[T]he entry of default terminates [Respondent's] rights to take any further affirmative steps in the litigation until either the default is set aside or a default is entered." (City of Riverside v. Horspool (2014) 223 Cal.App.4th 670, 681.)   Moving Party to give notice.
