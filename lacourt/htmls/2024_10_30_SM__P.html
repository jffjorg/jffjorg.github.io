DEPARTMENT P LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 20SMCV01214    Hearing Date: October 30, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Cohen v. Kaplan, Case no. 20SMCV01214

Hearing date October 30, 2024

Cross-Defendants’ Motion to Strike Punitive Damages & Prejudgment Interest from FACC;

Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant Cohens’ Demurrer to FACC

Plaintiffs Paul and Elaine Cohen sued defendants Michael Kaplan and Lisa Johnson for quiet title, trespass and declaratory relief arising from a wall and stairs allegedly constructed on plaintiffs’ property. Parties engaged in settlement discussions which resulted in an agreement being circulated and signed. Plaintiff Elaine Cohen subsequently asserted she did not sign the agreement. Defendants Kaplan, Johnson and the Michael Kaplan Living Trust dated 7/9/2002 (“the Kaplan trust”) cross-complained against plaintiffs/cross-defendants Cohen as well as Sotheby’s International Realty Affiliates, LLC, Sotheby’s International Realty, Inc. (collectively “Sotheby’s”), Lisa Willis, the Fedorowycz Family Trust dated 6/8/2004 (“the Fedorowycz trust”) Markian Fedorowycz, Donna T. F. Fedorowycz and Lea Johnson.

Cross-complainants’ fourth amended cross-complaint (“FACC”) seeks adverse possession and proscriptive easement against Elaine Cohen and alternatively Paul Cohen, for breach of the settlement agreement against Elaine Cohen, alternatively for fraudulent and negligent misrepresentation, false advertising and unfair competition against the Fedorowycz trust, Markian and Donna Fedorowycz, Sotheby’s, Willis and Johnson, alternatively for negligence against Sotheby's, Willis and Johnson, alternatively for breach of fiduciary duty against Sotheby’s and Johnson and for declaratory relief against Paul and Elaine Cohen.

Sotheby’s, Willis and Johnson move to strike exemplary and punitive damages and prejudgment interest from the FACC. Elaine Cohen demurs to the FACC’s third COA for breach of a settlement agreement.

Cross-Defendants’ Motion to Strike

Punitive damages are allowable only when plaintiff proves defendant is guilty of oppression, fraud or malice. Civ. Code §3294(a). An employer shall not be liable for [punitive] damages based upon acts of an employee of the employer, unless the employer had advance knowledge of the unfitness of the employee and employed him or her with a conscious disregard of the rights or safety of others or authorized or ratified the wrongful conduct for which the damages are awarded or was personally guilty of oppression, fraud or malice. With respect to a corporate employer, the advance knowledge and conscious disregard, authorization, ratification or act of oppression, fraud or malice must be on the part of an officer, director or managing agent of the corporation. Civ. Code §3294(b). Every person who is entitled to recover damages certain, or capable of being made certain by calculation, and the right to recover which is vested in him upon a particular day, is entitled also to recover interest thereon from that day. Civ. Code §3287.

Defendants/cross-complainants previously sought punitive damages against Sotheby’s, Willis and Johnson in the second amended cross-complaint. The court granted a motion to strike punitive damages against Sotheby’s under Cal. Civ. Code §3294(b) but denied as to Willis and Johnson. Min. Order 3/1/2022.

Defendants/cross-complainants again seek punitive damages from Sotheby’s. Cross-defendants argue the court already ruled on the issue, and no new facts are alleged against Sotheby’s in the FACC that allow for punitive damages. The court did not grant leave for cross-complainants to amend the request for punitive damages against Sotheby’s. Min. Order 3/1/2022.

Cross-complainants argue Sotheby’s committed fraud via its qualified broker, so is liable for punitive damages but offer no new facts to change the court’s previous analysis. The new arguments are insufficient; the court reaffirms its 3/1/2022 ruling. Punitive damages as to Sotheby’s are not allowable.

Cross-complainant’s argument that striking the entirety of FACC paras. 77 and 109 is inappropriate, as it would also strike punitive damages against Willis and Johnson, which the court previously upheld. Min. Order 3/1/2022. Cross-complainants adequately alleged fraud as to Willis and Johnson. The court will strike only references to cross-defendants Sotheby’s International Realty Affiliates, LLC and Sotheby’s International Realty, Inc.

Cross-defendants also move to strike the prayer for prejudgment interest. The court previously struck the prayer for prejudgment interest in the SAC. Min. Order 3/1/2022. Cross-defendants argue cross-complainants again fail to allege a basis for prejudgment interest per Cassinos v. Union Oil Co. (1993) 14 Cal. App. 4th 1770, 1789 (holding the test for recovery of prejudgment interest under [Civil Code] §3287(a) is whether defendant actually know[s] the amount owed or from reasonably available information could the defendant have computed that amount).

Cross-complainants argue under Cal. Civ. Code §3288 discretionary prejudgment interest for unliquidated tort claims is allowed. Cross-complainants assert they suffered a loss of use and ownership of land, which can be evaluated using expert testimony, so is ascertainable. The court previously struck the prayer for prejudgment interest, and cross-complainants did not seek leave to allege such a second time, which would essentially be a motion to reconsider. See Min. Order 3/1/2022. The prayer for prejudgment interest is STRICKEN.

Cross-defendants’ motion to strike references to punitive damages as to Sotheby’s and to strike the prayer for prejudgment interest from the FACC is GRANTED.

Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant Elaine Cohen’s Demurrer to the FACC

“The function of a demurrer is to test the sufficiency of the complaint as a matter of law.” Holiday Matinee, Inc. v. Rambus, Inc. (2004) 118 Cal.App.4th 1413, 1420. A complaint “is sufficient if it alleges ultimate rather than evidentiary facts” Doe v. City of Los Angeles (2007) 42 Cal.4th 531, 550, but plaintiff must allege essential facts “with reasonable precision and with particularity sufficient to acquaint [the] defendant with the nature, source and extent” of the plaintiff’s claim. Doheny Park Terrace Homeowners Ass’n., Inc. v. Truck Ins. Exchange (2005) 132 Cal.App.4th 1076, 1099.

Plaintiff/cross-defendant Elaine Cohen demurs to the FACC’s third COA for breach of settlement agreement, arguing it fails to state facts sufficient to constitute a claim under Code Civ. Proc. §430.10. She argues the FACC fails to allege a basis to support the theory that Paul Cohen acted as her authorized agent. Cross-complainants allege Paul Cohen signed the draft agreement as Elaine Cohen’s authorized agent. FACC paras. 56, 61-62. Elaine Cohen asserts the claim does not allege a written instrument signed by both spouses as required to support a transfer of any interest in community real property.

Cross-complainants allege the Cohens represented through their attorney William Bloch that both signed the agreement. FACC paras. 6, 33-36, 38-41, 56-65, 112. Cross-complainants alleged a written agreement signed by both Cohens existed or was represented to exist as such.

Elaine Cohen asserts the third COA is barred by the express provisions of the agreement. Cross-complainants alleged Paul Cohen executed the agreement as Elaine Cohen’s agent, forming the basis for a breach of contract COA. The court previously concurred with this logic. See Min. Order 3/12/2024. Cross-complainants alleged every party signed the agreement, and as such was effective. This is sufficient to survive demurrer. Elaine Cohen’s demurrer to the third COA of the FACC is OVERRULED.


Case Number: 23SMCV04741    Hearing Date: October 30, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Rodgers v. Eddie’s Liquor and JR Market, Inc., Case no. 23SMCV04741

Hearing date October 30, 2024

Defendant’s Motion To Have Attorney Brendan Johns Admitted Pro Hac Vice

Plaintiff alleges he was assaulted by an employee of defendant and sues for premises liability. Defendant seeks to have Colorado and Washington attorney Brendan Johns as counsel pro hac vice. The motion is unopposed.

A person who is not a licensee of the State Bar of California but is an attorney in good standing of and eligible to practice before the bar of state and who has been retained to appear in a case pending in California may be permitted to appear as counsel pro hac vice, provided an active licensee of the State Bar of California is associated as attorney of record. No person is eligible to appear as counsel pro hac vice under this rule if the person is: (1) a resident of California; (2) regularly employed in California; or (3) regularly engaged in substantial business, professional or other activities in California. Cal. Rules of Court 9.40(a). Absent special circumstances, repeated appearances by any person under this rule is a cause for denial of an application. Cal. Rules of Court 9.40(b).

The application must state: (1) the applicant’s residence and office address; (2) the courts to which the applicant has been admitted to practice and the dates of admission; (3) that the applicant is a licensee in good standing in those courts; (4) that the applicant is not currently suspended or disbarred in any court; (5) the title of each court and cause in which the applicant has filed an application to appear as counsel pro hac vice in this state in the preceding two years, the date of each application to appear as counsel pro hac vice in this state in the preceding two years, the date of each application, and whether or not it was granted; and (6) the name, address, and telephone number of the active licensee of the State Bar of California who is attorney of record. Cal. Rules of Court 9.40(d).

Defendant offers the declarations of Johns and counsel of record Arron Nesbitt. Johns’ petition is in compliance with Cal. Rules of Court 9.40. Nesbitt will associate with Johns. Decl. Nesbitt para. 5. The uncontested petition is GRANTED.


Case Number: 24SMCP00418    Hearing Date: October 30, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Gursey Schneider, LLP v. Aslin, Case no. 24SMCP00418

Hearing date October 30, 2024

Plaintiff Gursey Schneider’s Petition to Confirm Arbitration Award

Defendant retained plaintiff to perform accounting services regarding defendant's dissolution of marriage proceedings. Plaintiff and defendant executed a litigation consulting services engagement agreement which contained a binding arbitration provision. Plaintiff subsequently initiated arbitration proceedings for unpaid accounting services. The arbitrator awarded plaintiff $21,004.86 plus interest on 9/20/2023. Defendant subsequently failed to make payments as agreed. Plaintiff now petitions for confirmation of the arbitration award and seeks costs. Defendant is in pro per and does not oppose the petition.

“Any party to an arbitration in which an award has been made may petition the court to confirm, correct or vacate the award.” Cal. Code Civ. Proc. §1285. “If a petition or response under this chapter is duly served and filed, the court shall confirm the award as made.” Cal. Code Civ. Proc. §1286.

Plaintiff seeks to have the court confirm an award of $21,004.86 plus interest pursuant to AAA Consumer Rules 44(a), accruing since 9/21/2023 at 10% per annum. Decl. Kramer para. 5. Defendant has made no payments on the arbitration award. Decl. Kramer para. 6. Plaintiff served and filed the petition 8/2/2024. Confirmation of the award is appropriate.

Plaintiff seeks 5 hours of attorney’s fees at $395/hour for preparing the petition and declarations, meetings on such, and the proposed order. Plaintiff anticipates an additional 3 hours attending hearings on the petition. Plaintiff seeks $370 in costs. The court awards 5 hours for preparation of the moving papers. Three hours to attend an uncontested petition is excessive. The court awards one hour for attending the hearing, a total of 6 hours at $395/hour, plus $370 in costs. GRANTED, attorney’s fees and costs awarded as modified. Plaintiff to prepare a proposed order.
