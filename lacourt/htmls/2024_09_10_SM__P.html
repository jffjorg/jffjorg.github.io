DEPARTMENT P LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22SMCV01932    Hearing Date: September 10, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Chun v. Justin Smith, Case No. 22SMCV01932

Hearing Date: September 10, 2024

Defendant Smith’s Demurrer to Amended Complaint

Cross-Defendant Chun’s Demurrer to Cross-Complainant

 

Chun brought a motor vehicle and intentional tort claim against defendant Smith alleging Smith swung at Chun’s face, attempting to strike him. Smith cross-complained. The court sustained Chun’s demurrer to the cross-complaint with leave to amend. Chun now demurs to the amended cross-complaint.

 

Demurrer to the Complaint

Smith demurrers to the complaint, arguing that it fails to state the claim and is uncertain. On August 30, 2024 Smith was dismissed from the complaint with prejudice. Based on this, Smith’s demurrer is MOOT.

 

Demurrer to the Cross-Complaint

A. Intentional Tort

Chun demurs to the cause of action for intentional tort for failure to state facts that constitute a cause of action. Chun argues Smith had two prior opportunities to allege sufficient facts. The amended cross-complaint does not make it clear whether Smith’s claims are based on Chun’s civil or criminal complaints or how these complaints were alleged to have been made with malicious intent. Smith refers to video evidence but does not present facts about what this video allegedly shows. He refers to damages to personal relationships and reputation, but the pleading lacks detail regarding these alleged damages. Chun is protected by Code of Civil Procedure §47(2), which provides immunity for citizens reporting wrongdoing to law enforcement.

 

Smith seeks to have the court strike the demurrer and lawsuit “as frivolous and corrupt without evidence of allegations.” Such is not proper on demurrer. Smith states charges against him have been dismissed. This neither states a claim nor establishes why the amended pleading sufficiently alleges intentional tort. To the extent Smith’s claim is based on Chun’s communications to law enforcement, these are protected. Smith had two prior opportunities to amend the pleadings, without success. SUSTAINED without leave to amend.

 

B. Fraud

Chun demurs to the cause of action for fraud for failure to state facts a cause of action. Smith again refers to video evidence without alleging specific facts regarding the alleged contents of this evidence. Smith inappropriately argues fraud and conclusory allegations, without providing facts to support a cause of action for fraud.

 

A claim for fraud must plead: (1) misrepresentation; (2) knowledge of falsity; (3) intent to induce reliance; (4) justifiable reliance; and (5) damage. Odorizzi v. Bloomfield School Dist. (1966) 246 Cal.App.2d 123, 128; Wilhelm v. Pray, Price, Williams & Russell (1986) 186 Cal.App.3d 1324, 1332. Fraud actions are subject to strict requirements of particularity in pleading. Committee on Children’s Television, Inc. v. General Foods Corp. (1983) 35 Cal.3d 197, 216.

 

Smith alleges Chun and his legal representatives filed a civil lawsuit against Smith, claiming damages due to assault and motor vehicle tort, to collect damages from Smith’s auto insurance company. These claims were alleged to have been made knowingly and intentionally after Smith provided “irrefutable video evidence of the event in question.” Id.

 

Chun allegedly concealed facts regarding his initiation of the altercation and contact with Smith or his vehicle. Id. at FR-3. As a result, Smith alleges “emotional distress, mental anguish, loss of times, loss of income, harm to personal relationships, and loss of future prosperity.” Id. at FR-6. Smith’s claims appear to be premised on Chun’s filing of the complaint, which is protected activity. Civil Code §47. Moreover, the allegations are conclusory and do not meet the requirement of pleading facts with particularly. Given that Smith has already had two prior opportunities to amend the pleadings, the demurrer to the fraud cause of action is SUSTAINED without leave to amend.

 

C. Punitive Damages

Chun demurs to the allegations forming the basis for punitive damages allegations. Throughout the amended cross-complaint, Smith makes conclusory allegations, defames Chun’s counsel by alleging complicity in Chun’s allegedly fraudulent acts and refers to video evidence without description of the alleged evidence. No facts are presented that rise to the level of maliciousness required for requesting punitive damages. As the causes of action are both sustained without leave to amend, this issue is MOOT.


Case Number: 23SMCV01332    Hearing Date: September 10, 2024    Dept: P

Tentative Ruling

Poulton v. Network Capital Funding, Case No. 23SMCV01332

Hearing Date: September 10, 2024

Plaintiff Poulton’s Motion to Lift Stay and Restore Case to Civil Active List, Request for Fees and Sanctions

 

Plaintiff Poulton claims defendant employer Network Capital Funding constructively terminated him in retaliation for complaining about working conditions, illegal practices and deceptive recruitment techniques. The court stayed and ordered the parties to binding arbitration per the employment agreement. Poulton moves to lift the stay and restore the case to the civil active list.

 

Poulton moves to lift the stay, arguing Network Capital failed to make timely payments for the arbitration costs, per Labor Code §§1281.97 and 1281.98. In employment or consumer arbitration, if arbitration agreement requires drafting party to pay certain fees and costs before arb. can proceed, and such fees/costs are not paid within 30 days of due date, drafting party “is in material breach . . . and waives right to compel arbitration.” Calif. Arb Act (and C.C.P, including §1281.97), will be implied into agreement, if otherwise silent on these issues. Espinoza v. Superior Court (2022) 83 Cal.App.5th761. 

 

In opposition, Network Capital contends §1281.97 is preempted by the FAA as the terms of the agreement provide the FAA governs, rather than state law, citing Hernandez v. Sohnen Enterprises, Inc. (2024) 102 Cal.App.5th 222, 231, reh'g denied (June 3, 2024).

 

The parties’ agreement provides “Any arbitration proceeding under this agreement shall proceed under and be governed by the Federal Arbitration Act (“FAA”) because both I and the Company are engaged in interstate commerce. To the extent they are not contrary to the FAA, the procedures of the California Arbitration Act (“Act”) shall also apply.” Gafa Decl. Ex. B. pp. 1-2.

 

Based on this, the court could conclude the parties intended the FAA to govern, and where it would not conflict with the FAA, the CAA would also apply. Noting both the FAA and CAA support arbitration of disputes, the court finds no reason to lift the stay.

 

Network Capital argues the delay in payment is de minimis. Poulton argues Labor Code §§1282.97 and 1281.98 provide no exceptions for late payments. Payment were due February 13 and April 23, 2024. Peter Decl., Ex. A & D. Payments were received March 4 and May 6, 2024. Id. Ex. C.

 

Payments made two-three weeks past their initial due dates does not equate to a material breach. As there is a strong public policy in favor of upholding arbitration agreements, a brief delay in payment does not require the court to find a waiver of the right to arbitrate. As the motion is DENIED, no sanctions are appropriate.

Tentative Ruling

Craig Blair v. Lauren Mathers White, et al., Case No. SC128193

Hearing Date: September 10, 2024

Defendants Hannaford and Margolin’s Petition to Confirm Arbitration Award (UNOPPOSED)

 

Blair sued, alleging defendants’ home was not as advertised. The parties were ordered to binding arbitration on July 29, 2019. Defendants Hannaford and Margolin move to confirm the award by arbitrator Judy Lam.

 

Defendants submitted a copy of the arbitration award entered April 29, 2024. Unless a petition or response seeking to correct or vacate an arbitration award was filed properly based on valid grounds or the proceeding is dismissed, courts must enter judgment in conformity with the award. Code Civ. Proc. sec. 1286; Valsan Partners Ltd. Partnership v. Calcor Space Facility (1994) 25 Cal.App.4th 809, 818. 

 

Blair did not file a petition to correct or vacate or file a response/opposition to the petition. GRANTED.
