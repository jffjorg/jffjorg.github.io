[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: MOTION TO STRIKE DEFENDANT’S ANSWER

 

Dept. 3

On October 31, 2022, plaintiff Perla Mageno (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against defendant Pei Wei Asian Diner, LLC (“Defendant”) alleging violations of the Unruh Civil Rights Act. On August 7, 2024, Plaintiff filed this motion to strike Defendant’s answer.

“[A[ corporation, unlike a natural person, cannot represent itself before courts of record in propria persona, nor can it represent itself through a corporate officer, director or other employee who is not an attorney. It must be represented by licensed counsel in proceedings before courts of record.” (CLD Construction, Inc. v. City of San Ramon (2004) 120 Cal.App.4th 1141, 1145.)

On September 12, 2024, the Court signed an order granting defense counsel’s motion to be relieved, to be effective upon the filing of the proof of service of the signed order upon Defendant.

On September 13, 2024, the Court continued the hearing on Plaintiff’s motion to strike and ordered defense counsel to serve the signed order on Defendant.

There is no indication that the signed order on Form MC-053 was served on Defendant as ordered by the Court. Accordingly, the Court cannot find that Defendant is not represented by counsel. Plaintiff’s motion to strike is therefore denied without prejudice.

The Court sets an OSC re: Sanctions for Failure to Serve Defendant with Order Relieving Counsel for October 28, 2024, at 8:30 a.m. in Department 3 of the Alhambra Courthouse.

Dated this 10th day of October, 2024

 

 

 

 

       William A. Crowfoot

Judge of the Superior Court

On July 21, 2023, plaintiff Financial Credit Network, Inc. (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against defendant Gildarda B. Martinez dba JL Screens & Doors (“Defendant”). Defendant, who is self-represented, filed an answer on August 28, 2023. On May 24, 2024, Plaintiff served Form Interrogatories (Set One) on Defendant. No responses were received.

On June 28, 2024, Plaintiff sent Defendant a letter attempting to meet and confer regarding the status of Defendant’s responses. No response to the letter was received and no discovery responses have been served. Plaintiff filed this motion on July 10, 2024.

Where a party fails to serve timely responses to discovery requests, the court may make an order compelling responses. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290.) A party that fails to serve timely responses waives any objections to the request, including ones based on privilege or the protection of attorney work product. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (a).)

Defendant did not oppose the Motion and it is undisputed responses were not served. Plaintiff’s Motion is GRANTED and Defendant is ordered to serve verified responses without objections to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories (Set One) within 20 days of the date of this Order.

Plaintiff’s request for monetary sanctions is GRANTED and imposed against Defendant in the reduced amount of $290 for 1 hour at Plaintiff’s counsel’s hourly rate of $230 and $60 in filing fees, to be paid within 20 days of the date of this Order. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (c).)

Moving party to give notice.
