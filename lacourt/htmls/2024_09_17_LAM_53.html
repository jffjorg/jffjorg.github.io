Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles – Central District

Department 53

 

 

nicolitsa pitsos , et al.;

 

Plaintiffs,

 

 

vs.

 

 

rosa y. jou , et al.;

 

Defendants.

Case No.:

20STCV23621

 

 

Hearing Date:

September 17, 2024

 

 

Time:

10:00 a.m.

 

 

 

[tentative] Order RE:

 

defendant’s motion to compel further responses to discovery

 

 

MOVING PARTY:                 Defendant Rosa Y. Jou          

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Plaintiffs Nicolitsa Pitsos, Georgios Pitsos, Stavroula Pitsos, a minor by and through her guardian ad litem, Nicolitsa Pitsos, and Demetrios Pitsos, a minor by and through his guardian ad litem Nicolitsa Pitsos

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Discovery

The court considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with this motion.

DISCUSSION

Defendant Rosa Y. Jou (“Defendant”) moves the court for an order (1) compelling plaintiff Nicolitsa Pitsos (“N. Pitsos”) to serve further responses to Defendant’s Requests for Production of Documents, numbers 1-111, (2) compelling plaintiff Georgios Pitsos (“G. Pitsos”) to serve further responses to Defendant’s Requests for Production of Documents, numbers 1-97, (3) compelling N. Pitsos to serve further responses to Defendant’s Special Interrogatories, numbers 1-47, (4) compelling G. Pitsos to serve further responses to Defendant’s Special Interrogatories, numbers 1-45, and (5) awarding monetary sanctions in favor of Defendant and against plaintiffs N. Pitsos and G. Pitsos (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) in the amount of $8,385.

            First, the court finds that Plaintiffs timely served their responses to Defendant’s discovery requests.

Defendant served Plaintiffs with the subject sets of discovery by email on May 9, 2024.[1]  (Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 1, Request for Production as to N. Pitsos, p. 24 [proof of service by mail and electronic service on May 9, 2024]; Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 2, Request for Production as to G. Pitsos, p. 22 [proof of service by mail and email on May 9, 2024]; Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 3, Special Interrogatories as to N. Pitsos, p. 13 [proof of service by mail and electronic service on May 9, 2024]; Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 4, Special Interrogatories to G. Pitsos, p. 12 [proof of service by mail and electronic service on May 9, 2024].)  Plaintiffs had 30 days from May 10, 2024, to serve responses.  (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 12 [“The time in which any act provided by law is to be done is computed by excluding the first day, and including the last, unless the last day is a holiday, and then it is also excluded”], 2031.260, subd. (a) [responding party shall serve responses “[w]ithin 30 days after service of a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling”], 2030.260, subd. (a) [responding party shall serve responses “[w]ithin 30 days after service of interrogatories”].)  The court has calculated 30 days from May 10, 2024 to be June 10, 2024.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 12.)  Because Defendant served this discovery on Plaintiffs by email, Plaintiffs’ responses were due two court days thereafter, i.e., on June 12, 2024.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1010.6, subd. (a)(3)(B) [any duty to make any response “shall be extended after service by electronic means by two court days”].) 

Thus, the court finds that Plaintiffs’ responses, served on June 12, 2024, were timely served, such that Plaintiffs have not waived their rights to assert objections.  (Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 1, Pl. Responses to Requests for Production, p. 5 [proof of service of responses on June 12, 2024]; Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 2, Pl. Responses to Requests for Production, p. 5 [proof of service of responses on June 12, 2024]; Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 3, Pl. Responses to Special Interrogatories, p. 15 [proof of service of responses on June 12, 2024]; Sargsyan Decl., Ex. 4, Pl. Responses to Special Interrogatories, p. 15 [proof of service of responses on June 12, 2024]; Rand-Lewis Decl., ¶ 11 [Plaintiffs served responses on June 12, 2024].)

Second, the court has reviewed the meet and confer correspondence and the declaration of Gurgen Sargsyan, in which counsel detailed efforts to schedule an informal discovery conference with Plaintiffs’ counsel, and finds that Defendant made a reasonable and good faith attempt at an informal resolution of each issue presented by this motion.  (Sargsyan Decl., ¶¶ 5-8; Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2016.040, 2031.310, subd. (b)(2).)

Motion to Continue Trial

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this motion.  No opposition papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff Pacific Apex Partnership, LLC (“Plaintiff”) moves the court for an order continuing trial in this action, set for October 9, 2024, for at least 90 days, on the ground that counsel for Plaintiff is moving to be relieved as counsel.

As a threshold matter, the court notes that, on September 6, 2023, the court issued an order granting the motion to be relieved as counsel for defendants CNN Logistics, LLC, Rebiga Co, and Julian Rebiga (“Defendants”), filed by Scott C. Lee (“Defendants’ Counsel”).  The court ordered that Defendants’ Counsel would be relieved as counsel for Defendants “effective upon the filing of the proof of service of the signed ‘Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil’” on Defendants.  (Id. at p. 2:22-24; Sep. 6, 2023 Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil, ¶ 5, subd. (a).)  While Defendants’ Counsel filed a proof of service of the signed Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil on September 18, 2023, the proof of service states that the order was served on Defendants at the address 2110 Artesia Boulevard, Suite B, PO Box 451, Redondo Beach, California, 90278.  (Sep. 18, 2023 POS-040, p. 3 [Attachment to Proof of Service – Civil (Persons Served)].)  However, Defendants’ address of record is 1044 E. Del Amo Blvd., Carson, California, 90746.  (Sep. 6, 2023 Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil, ¶ 6.)  Moreover, while Defendants’ Counsel also filed a proof of service showing that the Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil was served on Defendants by email, the court cannot confirm that those email addresses are current and valid.  (Sep. 18, 2023 Notice of Ruling, p. 3.)

Thus, it appears to the court that Defendants’ Counsel has not filed a valid proof of service of the Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil on Defendants, and therefore has not been relieved as counsel for Defendants.  (Sep. 6, 2023 Order, p. 2:22-24; Sep. 6, 2023 Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil, ¶ 5, subd. (a).)  The court will therefore require Plaintiff to serve a notice of this ruling on both   (1) Defendants, directly, at their address of record, as set forth in the signed “Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil,” filed by the court on September 6, 2023, and (2) Defendants’ Counsel.

The court finds that Plaintiff has not shown good cause justifying the continuance of trial and therefore denies Plaintiff’s motion.  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 3.1332, subd. (c) [“The court may grant a continuance only on an affirmative showing of good cause requiring the continuance”].) 

The court recognizes that counsel for Plaintiff has filed a motion to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff, which, if granted, would require Plaintiff to retain new counsel.  However, Plaintiff has not submitted a declaration (1) to establish what efforts, if any, it has made to retain new counsel, or (2) to justify its delay in finding and retaining new counsel.  Thus, the court finds that Plaintiff has not shown good cause to continue the trial based on Plaintiff’s counsel’s motion to be relieved as counsel.  (Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 3.1332, subd. (c)(4).)

ORDER

            The court denies plaintiff Pacific Apex Partnership, LLC’s motion to continue trial.

            The court orders plaintiff Pacific Apex Partnership, LLC to give notice of this order (1) to defendants CNN Logistics, LLC, Rebiga Co, and Julian Rebiga at their address of record, as set forth in the signed “Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel – Civil,” filed by the court on September 6, 2023, and (2) to attorney Scott C. Lee.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
