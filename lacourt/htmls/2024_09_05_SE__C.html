DEPARTMENT C LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV41940    Hearing Date: September 5, 2024    Dept: C

KIM v. PARK

CASE NO.:  21STCV41940

HEARING:  09/05/24

 

#1

 

     I.        Defendant HANNAH SEUING EUN AHN’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff ERICA KIM’s Further Responses to Deposition Questions is placed OFF-CALENDAR.

 

    II.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant HANNAH SEUNG EUN AHN’s Further Responses to Request for Production of Documents and tangible Things (set two) is placed OFF-CALENDAR.

 

Court Clerk to give notice.

 

This action for sexual assault/battery of a minor was filed on November 15, 2021. On July 5, 2022 the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) was filed.

 

Plaintiff asserts the following causes of action:

 

(1) Sexual Assault and Battery;

(2) IIED;

(3) Negligence Per Se;

(4) Gross Negligence;

(5) Parental Negligence;

(6) Negligent Supervision;

(7) Premises Liability – Unsafe Condition;

(8) Breach of Contract;

(9) Breach of Implied Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing

 

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Deposition Questions

 

Defendant HANNAH SEUING EUN AHN moves to compel further responses from Plaintiff Erica Kim pursuant to CCP § 2025.480. There are 10 deposition questions at issue. Plaintiff Kim seemingly refused to answer many of the subject questions citing privacy objections/concerns.

 

This matter was initially set for hearing on April 11, 2024, but continued to April 25, 2024 to enable Defendant the opportunity to respond to, and this Court to adequately review, Plaintiff’s untimely filed Opposition papers.

On April 25, 2024, this Court continued the Motion for a second time and ordered the parties to meet and confer, and file a Joint Statement outlining any disputed issues by no later than Monday, June 3, 2024.

 

An untimely Joint Statement was filed on June 7, 2024. The untimely Joint Statement indicates “the parties have not had an opportunity to further meet and confer.” (JS 2:14.)

 

On June 13, 2024, this matter was CONTINUED again. Counsel were, once again, instructed to further meet and confer on the issues outlined in the subject Motions. The following Order was issued: “Counsel are ORDERED to make further efforts to resolve the issues presented. If, after exhausting those efforts, court intervention is needed, counsel may appear and argue the merits on the continued hearing date. If counsel are unable to informally resolve their discovery disputes, then counsel are instructed to submit a JOINT STATEMENT outlining the remaining disputed issues for which a ruling is required. The joint statement must be FILED on or before Monday, August 26, 2024.” (06/13/24, M.O.)

 

As of September 3, 2024, no Joint Statement has been filed/lodged with this Court.

 

The matter is placed OFF-CALENDAR.

 

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Production of Documents

 

The Opposition to this Motion was originally due by May 31, 2024. Without leave of Court, Defendant filed an untimely Opposition on June 3, 2024. As of June 11, 2024, no Reply had been filed.

 

On June 13, 2024, this matter was CONTINUED to September 5, 2024 to ensure that Plaintiff had the ability to review and respond to Defendant’s untimely Opposition. Plaintiff was given the opportunity to file and serve a Reply per Code in accordance with this hearing date.

 

Additionally, the parties were ORDERED to submit a Joint Statement. The following Order was issued: “Counsel are ORDERED to make further efforts to resolve the issues presented. If, after exhausting those efforts, court intervention is needed, counsel may appear and argue the merits on the continued hearing date. If counsel are unable to informally resolve their discovery disputes, then counsel are instructed to submit a JOINT STATEMENT outlining the remaining disputed issues for which a ruling is required. The joint statement must be FILED on or before Monday, August 26, 2024.” (06/13/24, M.O.)

THE PACIFIC SOUTHWEST DISTRICT OF THE LUTHERAN CHURCH – MISSOURI SYNOD v. JENSEN

#3

    II.        Defendant/Cross-Complainant PEACE LUTHERAN CHURCH, INCORPORATED, OF SOUTH GATE, CALIFORNIA’s Ex Parte Application to Continue Hearing for Motion for Summary Judgment, or in the alternative, Summary Adjudication is OFF-CALENDAR. 

  III.        Defendant/Cross-Complainant PEACE LUTHERAN CHURCH, INCORPORATED, OF SOUTH GATE, CALIFORNIA’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant THE PACIFIC SOUTHWEST DISTRICT OF THE LUTHERAN CHURCH – MISSOURI SYNOD to produce Michael E. Gibson for Deposition is GRANTED.

 IV.        Defendant/Cross-Complainant PEACE LUTHERAN CHURCH, INCORPORATED, OF SOUTH GATE, CALIFORNIA’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant THE PACIFIC SOUTHWEST DISTRICT OF THE LUTHERAN CHURCH – MISSOURI SYNOD to produce Jon Niederbrach for Deposition is GRANTED.

 

Moving Party to give notice.


Motion for Summary Judgment/Ex Parte Application to Continue Motion

 

This action concerning real property was filed by Plaintiff THE PACIFIC SOUTHWEST DISTRICT OF THE LUTHERAN CHURCH – MISSOURI SYNOD (“Plaintiff”) on April 26, 2022.  

 

The subject Motion for Summary Judgment/Adjudication was filed on July 5, 2024. A First Amended Memorandum of Points and Authorities in Support of Motion for Summary Judgment or in the alternative Summary Adjudication was filed on July 17, 2024. Defendant/Cross-Complainant PEACE LUTHERAN CHURCH INCORPORATED, OF SOUTH GATE, CALIFORNIA (“PLC”) moves for summary judgment/adjudication against Plaintiff’s Complaint and all causes of action contained therein.

 

On July 31, 2024, this Court GRANTED Plaintiff’s Motion for Leave to File a First Amended Complaint. Consequently, the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) was FILED on August 5, 2024.

 

The FAC filed on August 5, 2024 is the operative pleading, and moots the subject Motion directed toward the original Complaint. “It is well established that an amendatory pleading supersedes the original one, which ceases to perform any function as a pleading.” (Foreman & Clark Corp. v. Fallon (1971) 3 Cal.3d 875, 884.) Thus, an amended complaint supersedes all prior complaints. (Grell v. Laci Le Beau Corp. (1999) 73 Cal.App.4th 1300, 1307.) “The amended complaint furnishes the sole basis for the cause of action and the original complaint ceases to have any effect either as a pleading or as a basis for judgment. [Cite]” (State Compensation Ins. Fund. v. Superior Court (2010) 184 Cal.App.4th 1124, 1130-1131.) “Because there is but one complaint in a civil action, the filing of an amended complaint moots a motion directed to a prior complaint. Thus, once an amended complaint is filed, it is error to grant summary adjudication on a cause of action contained in a previous complaint. [Internal Citations Omitted.]” (Id.)

 

The Motion is placed OFF-CALENDAR as MOOT.

 

For the reasons stated above, Defendant/Cross-Complainant PLC’s ex parte application to continue the motion for summary judgment/summary adjudication is placed OFF-CALENDAR as MOOT. 

 

Motions to Compel Depositions

 

Defendant/Cross-Complainant PLC moves to compel the depositions of (1) Party Employee MICHAEL E. GIBSON as the President of The Pacific Southwest District of the Lutheran Church – Missouri Synod (“PSWD”); and (2) Party Employee JON NIEDERBRACH as the Chief Operating Officer of PSWD. PLC argues that the depositions of both Jon Niederbrach and Michael Gibson were properly noticed. PSWD failed to produce Jon Niederbrach for his properly noticed deposition date of June 13, 2024, and failed to produce Michael Gibson for his properly noticed deposition date of June 14, 2024.

 

In Opposition(s), PSWD argues: (1) the Notice of Taking Deposition of Jon Niederbrach was defective and that PSWD was within its rights to end the deposition because the court reporter provided by PLC was not certified; and (2) a valid objection to the Notice of Taking Deposition of Michael Gibson was issued because counsel was unavailable due to a medical emergency.

 

A party may obtain discovery by taking oral depositions. (CCP §2025.010.) CCP §2025.450, which governs motions to compel deposition, provides that “[i]f, after service of a deposition notice, a party to the action or an officer, director, managing agent, or employee of a party, or a person designated by an organization that is a party…without having served a valid objection under §2025.410, fails to appear for examination…the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony.” (CCP §2025.450(a).) “Unless the parties agree or the court orders otherwise, the testimony, as well as any stated objections, shall be taken stenographically. If taken stenographically, it shall be by a person certified….” (CCP §2025.330(b).)

 

In both Oppositions, PSWD argues that both Motions are unnecessary and could have been informally resolved if PLC had engaged in good faith meet and confer efforts to reschedule the depositions. PSWD indicates that they are willing to reschedule and produce both Mr. Gibson and Mr. Niederbrach.

 

The Motions are GRANTED. PLC is entitled to take PSWD’s employee’s depositions, and PSWD does not substantively oppose producing either Mr. Niederbrach or Mr. Gibson for depositions.

 

If [the] motion is granted, the court shall impose a monetary sanction…in favor of the party who noticed the deposition and against the deponent or the party with whom the deponent is affiliated, unless the court finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” (CCP §2025.450(g)(2).)

 

It is undisputed that the court reporter hired for PLC for the deposition of Jon Niederbrach was not certified. Moreover, PSWD served an Objection to the Deposition Notice of Michael Gibson because Counsel was unavailable on the dates noticed due to a scheduled medical procedure.

 

Sanctions are DENIED.

 

Trial is currently scheduled for October 15, 2024. The Court notes that there is a Motion to Continue Trial scheduled for September 20, 2024, and that there are a number of Motions scheduled to occur after the currently scheduled trial date. PSWD is ORDERED to produce Jon Niederbrach and Michael Gibson to appear for depositions by no later than 15 calendar days from the date of the issuance of this Order. The date may be extended by agreement of the parties.

 

 


Case Number: 22NWCV00532    Hearing Date: September 5, 2024    Dept: C

T.M. v. WHITTIER CITY SCHOOL DISTRICT

CASE NO.:  22NWCV00532

HEARING:  09/05/24

 

#4

 

     I.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Requests for Production of Documents is GRANTED.

 

    II.        Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories is GRANTED.

 

Moving Party to give notice.

 

This sexual assault action was filed by Plaintiff T.M. on June 28, 2022, and stems from conduct that allegedly occurred more than 18 years ago. Plaintiff alleges that she was sexually abused by her 8th grade math teacher, Jason Kreuger, in 2005, while she was a student at Walter Dexter Middle School, in the Whittier City School District (“District”). (Complaint ¶¶3, 5, 31). Plaintiff’s Complaint alleges two causes of action against the District: (1) Negligence; (2) Negligent Hiring, Retention, and Supervision.

 

In separately filed Motions, Plaintiff moves to compel the District’s further responses to the following discovery:

 

·        RPD (set two) No. 37. ALL DOCUMENTS RELATING TO a class list for PLAINTIFF’S class during the RELEVANT TIME PERIOD.

·        RPD (set two) No. 38. A class list for PLAINTIFF’S class during the RELEVANT TIME PERIOD.

·        SI (set two) No. 65. IDENTIFY all of PLAINTIFF’S classmates during the RELEVANT TIME PERIOD. (For the purpose of these Interrogatories, the term “IDENITFY” when referring to an individual, corporation, or other entity shall mean to set forth the name and telephone number and if a corporation or other entity, its principal place of business, or if an individual, present or last known home address, his or her job title or titles, by whom employed and address of the place of employment.

 

For purposes of the discovery at issue, the “relevant time period” is defined as January 1, 2004 – December 31, 2008.

 

“On receipt of a response to a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling, the demanding party may move for an order compelling further response to the demand if the demanding party deems that any of the following apply: (1) A state of compliance with the demand is incomplete. (2) A representation of inability to comply is inadequate, incomplete, or evasive. (3) An objection in the response is without merit or too general.” (CCP §2031.310(a).)

A motion to compel further responses to a request for production “shall set forth specific facts showing good cause justifying the discovery sought by the inspection demand.” (CCP §2031.310(b).)

 

“If the propounding party, on receipt of a response to interrogatories, deems that (1) an answer to a particular interrogatory is…incomplete…or (3) an objection to an interrogatory is without merit or too general, that party may move for an order compelling further response.” (CCP §2030.300(a).)

 

The District opposes both Motions in order to ensure privacy protections of its students, as well as to ensure compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (“FERPA”) and the California Educational Code. Moreover the District argues that Plaintiff improperly seeks the identities and contact information of all students over a four-year period that attended any of Plaintiff’s classes at the subject school, when Plaintiff only attended the school for a single year.

 

Plaintiff maintains that the need to discover the identities of her classmates is essential because “potential witnesses are essential for Plaintiff’s case in chief because they witnessed the perpetrator—Defendant’s employee – repeatedly kiss and fondle Plaintiff. At her deposition taken on June 1, 2023, Plaintiff testified that her math teacher kissed her on the lips in the class in the presence of approximately 33 students around ten times. (See Supp. Niman Decl., Exhibit 4 (Plaintiff’s Transcript, pp 135-138.) Plaintiff further testified that her math teacher also touched her breasts and vaginal area in the classroom in the presence of other students. (Id.).” (Reply RPD 2:7-13.)

 

“[P]arties may conduct discovery ‘regarding any matter, not privileged, that is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action… if the matter either is itself admissible in evidence or appears reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence.’”  (Perlan Therapeutics, Inc. v. Sup. Ct. (2009) 178 Cal.App.4th 1333, 1351 (fn. 12.)

 

“[T]here is a general public interest in ‘facilitating the ascertainment of truth in connection with legal proceedings’ and in obtaining just results in litigation….” (Tien v. Sup. Ct. (2006) 139 Cal.App.4th 528, 539.) “If these public interests in disclosure of private information are found to be ‘compelling,’ the individual’s right of privacy must give way and disclosure will be required.” (Id.) In each case, the Court must balance the right of privacy against the need for disclosure. (Britt v. Sup. Ct. (1978) 20 Cal.3d 844, 855.)

 

Education Code §49077 provides that “[i]information concerning a student shall be furnished in compliance with a court order or a lawfully issued subpoena. The school district shall make a reasonable effort to notify the parent or legal guardian and the pupil in advance of compliance with a lawfully issued subpoena and, in the case of compliance with a court order, if lawfully possible within the requirements of the order.”

 

Here, Plaintiff alleges that she was sexually molested by her math teacher in the classroom and in the presence of other students.  Given the seriousness of Plaintiff’s allegations, the Court finds that the public interest in disclosure outweighs the privacy interests of third parties.

 

However, the Court also finds that the Requests and SI, as phrased, are impermissibly overbroad. Plaintiff only attended the school for one year. Plaintiff has not established the relevance of documents or information that go beyond the scope of the one year that Plaintiff attended the school.

 

Therefore, the Motions are GRANTED in part.

 

The District is ORDERED to produce the documents and further responses responsive to the Requests for Production and Special Interrogatories at issue, limited to the one year that Plaintiff was a student at the school. Production may be delayed to allow for compliance with Education Code §49077 requiring the District to notify the third parties and any applicable parents (where minors are concerned), and compliance with CCP §1985.3. Production of documents and further responses need not occur prior to thirty days of the Court’s issuance of this Order, but in no event shall production occur any later than 90 days from the date of the Court’s issuance of this Order.

 

On its own motion, the Court ORDERS that a protective order should be entered to govern the disclosure of implicated documents produced or that are to be produced throughout the course of discovery. (CCP §2031.060(b).) Therefore, it is ORDERED that documents to be produced by the District in response to RPD (set two) nos. 37 and 38; and SI (set two) No. 65, which contain confidential information are “Protected Documents.” The Protected Documents and the information contained therein shall be treated by all parties as confidential. Except upon the prior written consent of the District, or upon further order of this Court, the Protected Documents or information contained therein may be shown, disseminated, or disclosed only to: the parties of this litigation and/or their counsel of record in this case; or employees of counsel or of associated counsel who assist in the preparation of this case; or experts and consultants retained by the parties to this litigation.
