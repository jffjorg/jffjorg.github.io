DEPARTMENT 73 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV26615    Hearing Date: November 27, 2024    Dept: 73

11/27/2024 

Dept. 73 

Hon. Rolf Treu, Judge presiding 

 

YUN v. PICO BUILDING SUPPLY CO., et al. (21STCV26615) 

 

Counsel for Plaintiff:  Jung Won Yun (Pro Per) 

Counsel for Defendants: Thomas Gelini (Bennett, Gelini & Gelini)

 

Defendants’ MOTION TO enforce settlement (filed 10/22/2024) 

 

TENTATIVE RULING 

 

The Court GRANTS Defendants’ Motion to Enforce Settlement. Plaintiff’s counsel is ordered to provide his W9 to Defendants’ counsel within 10 days for check issuance.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On July 19, 2021, Plaintiff Jung Won Yun (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants Pico Building Supply Corporation (erroneously sued as Pico Building Supply Co.) and Saviss Pico, LLC (“Defendants”).

 

Plaintiff brought this action with causes of action for negligence and premises liability for injuries resulting from a slip and fall that occurred on Defendant’s property. Plaintiff alleges that as he was exiting from the back yard of the property of Defendant Pico, he slipped and fell because “sand and dirt had been spilled from a big truck of Pico.” (Complaint p. 4, 5.)

 

On October 22, 2024, Defendants filed the instant Motion to Enforce Settlement, arguing:  

·       The parties agreed to mediate this dispute with Hon. Joe Hilberman (ret.) on January 24, 2024. Plaintiff Jung Won Yun was present with his counsel, Andrew Kim. Defendants appeared through their counsel and Defendants’ insurer participated throughout the mediation.

·       The parties did reach settlement. The parties agreed:

o   1. Defendants will pay Plaintiff a total sum of $45,000.00.

o   2. No funding to take place until the Medi-Cl and Medicare lien issues are resolved.

o   3. In exchange of payments described, Plaintiff will dismiss this action with prejudice.

o   4. This settlement is governed and binding by the terms of CCP section 664.4.

o   5. A more formal and robust settlement agreement and mutual release will be executed. 6. Each side to bear their own fees and costs.

o   7. Waiver of Civil Code section 1542.

·       Plaintiff and his attorney executed the short form settlement agreement on January 24, 2024, binding themselves to this agreement. A formal settlement agreement was submitted to Plaintiff, but it was never returned executed.

·       Due to the fact that there is an enforceable executed agreement, that Defendants have already discharged the lien portion of their obligation, and are ready to fulfill the remainder of their obligation but for the fact that Plaintiff has been uncooperative, this motion is necessary.

·       All requirements have been met in order to obtain an order enforcing settlement under CCP section 664.6. The first requirement is met: pending litigation. Plaintiff filed his lawsuit on July 19, 2021, and it is still active. The second and third requirements are also met: the party’s stipulation to settlement. The written agreement, Exhibit A, contains all the materials terms of the settlement.

·       Pursuant to CCP section 664.6, Defendants respectfully request the following:

o   1. An order for Plaintiff’s attorney Andrew Kim to provide his W9 for check issuance so that Defendants can tender the balance of the settlement funds in the amount of $29,375 to Plaintiff. Defendants respectfully request that the W9 be tendered no later than end of business December 2, 2024; and

o   2. An order dismissing this action once payment of the balance of the settlement funds is made. The settlement funds will be issued and tendered to Plaintiff within 10 business days. This matter should be dismissed by December 20, 2024.

 

Plaintiff filed a declaration in opposition:

·       Plaintiff’s declaration states:

o   “On Jan.24, 2024, I was in my attorney's office, engaging in the mediation through zoom with opposing counsel and defendants. Hon. Joe Hilberman mediated the session.”

o   “During the mediation, I was surprised that defendants' counsel started the negotiation with very low amount.”

o   “Ultimately, my attorney recommended me to accept their offer to settle in the amount of $45,000.00. I reluctantly indicated that I will settle at $45,000.00.”

o   “I even signed the Short Form Settlement Agreement. However, now, I have to say that I simply do not agree with the settlement amount. I have much more medical cost than the settled amount of $45,000.00.”            

·       Plaintiff states he would like to cancel the settlement.

 

Defendants filed a reply, arguing:

·       Plaintiff admitted to executing the short form settlement agreement which very specifically stated the settlement would be governed by CCP section 664.6 unequivocally. The very purpose of CCP section 664.6 is to create a binding legal agreement. If the requirements for an agreement to be enforceable under section 664.6 are met, the statute creates a mechanism to enforce the agreement in the event an opposing party disputes the settlement terms. Viejo Bancorp, Inc. v. Wood (1989) 217 Cal.App.3d 200, 206.

·       Plaintiff’s opposition fails to raise any fact, evidence or argument that could undo this settlement under CCP 664.6.

·       Not only has Plaintiff and his attorney executed the short form settlement agreement, but Defendants have already materially relied upon the settlement agreement and paid off the Medicare Demand as part of the terms of the settlement agreement.

 

II.ANALYSIS

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

CCP section 664.6 provides that “[i]f parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement.  If requested by the parties, the court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 664.6.)

 

CCP section 664.6 further provides “a writing is signed by a party if it is signed by any of the following: (1) The party. (2) An attorney who represents the party. (3) If the party is an insurer, an agent who is authorized in writing by the insurer to sign on the insurer's behalf.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 664.6.)

 

B.      Discussion

 

Defendants move the Court for an order enforcing the settlement reached between the parties. Defendants request: (1) An order for Plaintiff’s attorney Andrew Kim to provide his W9 for check issuance so that Defendants can tender the balance of the settlement funds in the amount of $29,375 to Plaintiff; and (2) An order dismissing this action once payment of the balance of the settlement funds is made.

 

On January 24, 2024, the parties participated in a mediation with Hon. Joe Hilberman (ret.), Plaintiff, Plaintiff’s counsel, Defendants’ counsel and Defendants’ insurer. (Kung Decl., ¶ 2.) The parties reached a settlement. (Ibid.)

 

In support of its motion, Defendants attached a copy of the executed Mediated Settlement Agreement (Short Form) (“Short Form Settlement Agreement”). The Short Form Settlement Agreement sets forth the terms of the settlement, stating in relevant part:

 

“Defendants Pico Building Supply Corporation, and Saviss Pico, LLC, through Great Lakes Insurance have agreed to pay Plaintiff the total amount of $45,000 as follows:

 

• $45,000 payable to Jung Won Yun and his attorney Law Offices of Andrew Kim, subject to liens asserted to Medicare and Medi-Cal.

 • No funding to take place until the final Medi-Cal/Medicare lien is provided and then three separate settlement drafts will be issued, one to Medicare for the final lien, one to Medi-Cal/DHCS for the final lien amount, and one for the remaining balance of the $45,000 settlement amount, payable to Plaintiff and his Attorneys, Law Offices of Andrew Kim.  

 

In exchange for such payment Plaintiff agree to dismiss the above-referenced action as to the settling Defendants with prejudice…”

 

“The parties agree that this agreement is binding and enforceable on all parties and is

intended to be enforceable under California Code of Civil Procedure Section 664.6.

 

The parties, by signing below, hereby express their intent to be bound by the terms of this mediated agreement. The parties understand that they are giving up their right to further litigation between plaintiff(s) and defendant(s) referable to this dispute and that the release of all claims applies equally to the plaintiff and the defendant.”

 

(Kung Decl., Ex. A, pp. 1-2.) The Short Form Settlement Agreement is signed by Plaintiff, Jung Won Yun, and his attorney, Andrew Kim. (Kung Decl., Ex. A, p. 2.)

 

In Plaintiff’s declaration that he filed in opposition, Plaintiff does not dispute he accepted Defendants’ settlement offer and that he signed the Short Form Settlement Agreement. (Yun Decl., ¶¶ 4-5.) Plaintiff states in his declaration: “However, now, I have to say that I simply do not agree with the settlement amount.” (Yun Decl., ¶ 5.)

 

In Defendants’ reply they assert that all the requirements of CCP section 664.6 have been met and Plaintiff’s opposition fails to raise any fact, evidence or argument that could undo this settlement under CCP 664.6. Defendants contend they have also already materially relied upon the settlement agreement and paid off the Medicare Demand as part of the terms of the settlement agreement. (Kung Decl., ¶¶ 7-9, Exs. B-D.)

 

The Court finds Defendants are entitled to an order to enforce the settlement. The Short Form Settlement Agreement was signed by Plaintiff. The agreement contained the material terms of the settlement and indicates the parties intended for it to be enforceable under California Code of Civil Procedure Section 664.6. (Kung Decl., Ex. A, pp. 1-2.)

 

Based on the foregoing, the Court grants Defendants’ Motion to Enforce Settlement and orders Plaintiff’s counsel to provide his W9 to Defendants’ counsel within 10 days for check issuance.

 

III. CONCLUSION 

 

The Court GRANTS Defendants’ Motion to Enforce Settlement. Plaintiff’s counsel is ordered to provide his W9 to Defendants’ counsel within 10 days for check issuance.

MIGLIORE v. FCA US LLC, et al. (24STCV13115)

 

Counsel for Plaintiff/moving party: Sam Azimtash (Azimtash Law Firm PC)

Counsel for Defendant/opposing party:  Erin Hanson (Clark Hill LLP)

 

PLAINTIFF’S MOTION TO COMPEL DEFENDANT PREMIER AUTOMOTIVE OF PLACENTIA, LLC DBA PREMIER CHRYSLER DODGE JEEP RAM OF BUENA PARK’S OBJECTION-FREE RESPONSES TO FORM INTERROGATORIES, SET ONE (filed 09/04/2024)

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant Premier Automotive of Placentia, LLC dba Premier Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram of Buena Park’s responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One is GRANTED. Defendant F Defendant Premier Automotive of Placentia, LLC dba Premier Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram of Buena Park is ordered to provide verified, objection-free responses to Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, Set One, within 20 days of this order.  

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On May 24, 2024, Plaintiff Merik Migliore filed the instant action against Defendants FC US LLC, Premier Automotive of Placentia, LLC dba Premier Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram of Buena Park  (“PA”) and Does 1-100, alleging the following causes of action:

1.      Violation of Subdivision (d) of Civil Code section 1793.2

2.      Violation of Subdivision (b) of Civil Code section 1793.2

3.      Violation of Subdivision (a)(3) of Civil Code section 1793.2

4.      Breach of Express Warranty

5.      Breach of Implied Warranty

6.      Negligent Repair

Plaintiff alleges this action arises out of the warranty obligations of Defendants in connection with a vehicle purchased by Plaintiff and for which Defendants issued a written warranty.

On September 4, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Motion to Compel Defendant PA’s objection free responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One. No opposition was filed.

 

II. ANALYSIS

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

A responding party has 30 days after service of interrogatories to serve their responses on the propounding party. (Code of Civ. Proc., § 2030.260, subd. (a).) However, if interrogatories are served by electronic service, a responding party has an additional two (2) court days to respond. (Code of Civ. Proc., § 1010.6, subd. (a)(3)(B).)  

 

If the responding party fails to timely respond to the interrogatories, “The party to whom the interrogatories are directed waives any right to exercise the option to produce writings under Section 2030.230, as well as any objection to the interrogatories, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product under Chapter 4 (commencing with Section 2018.010).” (Code of Civ. Proc., § 2030.290, subd. (a).)  

  

Furthermore, the party propounding the interrogatories may move for an order compelling response to the interrogatories. (Code of Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.290, subd. (b), 2031.300, subd. (b).) And “Unlike a motion to compel further responses, a motion to compel responses is not subject to a 45-day time limit, and the propounding party does not have to demonstrate either good cause or that it satisfied a ‘meet and confer’ requirement.” (Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 404.)   

 

B.      Discussion

On July 5, 2024, Plaintiff served Form Interrogatories (Set One) on Defendant PA. (Azimtash Decl., ¶ 3, Ex. A.) The responses were due on or about August 7, 2024. As of the date of this motion, no requests for an extension of time or responses have been served. (Id. at ¶ 8.) No opposition to Plaintiff’s motion has been filed.

As such, the Court GRANTS Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant Premier Automotive of Placentia, LLC dba Premier Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram of Buena Park’s responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One.

III. DISPOSITION

 

Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Defendant Premier Automotive of Placentia, LLC dba Premier Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram of Buena Park’s responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One is GRANTED. Defendant F Defendant Premier Automotive of Placentia, LLC dba Premier Chrysler Dodge Jeep Ram of Buena Park is ordered to provide verified, objection-free responses to Plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, Set One, within 20 days of this order.
