DEPARTMENT 78 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 19STCV02947    Hearing Date: November 19, 2024    Dept: 78

 

Superior Court of California 

County of Los Angeles 

Department 78 

¿ 

FRANCES ATKINS, 

Plaintiff(s), 

vs. 

ST. CECILIAS SCHOOL, et al., 

Defendant(s). 

Case No.: 

19STCV02947 

Hearing Date: 

November 19, 2024 

 

 

[TENTATIVE] ORDER DENYING WITHOUT PREJUDICE MOTION TO CONTINUE TRIAL 

 

I. BACKGROUND 

January 28, 2019, plaintiff Frances Atkins (“Plaintiff”) filed this FEHA and Labor Code violation action against defendants St. Cecilias School, et al. alleging that, after having been employed by defendants between 1978 and 2018 and her good performance, defendants terminated her in 2018 to replace her with a significantly younger person. On March 29, 2019, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint against the same parties.  

Trial is set for December 30, 2024. 

On October 2, 2024, defendant St. Cecilia Catholic School, erroneously sued as St. Cecilias School, (“Defendant”) filed the instant motion to continue trial by at least four months. 

On October 15, 2024, Plaintiff filed an opposition to the motion. 

On October 25, 2024, Defendant filed a reply. 

 

II. LEGAL STANDARD 

Although continuances of trials are disfavored, each request for a continuance must be considered on its own merits.¿ (CRC Rule 3.1332(c).)¿The Court may grant a continuance only on an affirmative showing of good cause requiring the continuance. (CRC Rule 3.1332(c).) The Court may look to the following factors in determining whether a trial continuance is warranted: (1) proximity of the trial date; (2) whether there was any previous continuance of trial due to any party; (3) the length of the continuance requested; (4) the availability of alternative means to address the problem that gave rise to the motion; (5) the prejudice that parties or witnesses will suffer as a result of the continuance; and (6) whether trial counsel is engaged in another trial.¿ (See generally, CRC Rule 3.1332(d)(1)-(11).) 

Additional factors for the Court to consider include: a party’s excused inability to obtain essential testimony, documents, or other material evidence despite diligent efforts; whether all parties have stipulated to a continuance; and any other fact or circumstance relevant to the fair determination of the motion or application. (CRC Rule 3.1332(c), (d).) 

 

III. DISCUSSION 

Defendant moves to continue trial on the grounds that its trial counsel is unavailable for the current trial date. Defendant argues Anthony Bellone (“Mr. Bellone”) was the lead trial counsel in the matter, and lead trial counsel in Depto v. St. Paul the Apostle Church (“Depto”), LASC case number BC691437, pending in Department 53. Because of Mr. Bellone’s serious illness, Will S. Skinner (“Mr. Skinner”) was appointed as lead trial counsel in Depto in late February 2024 prior and that the case is scheduled for trial on August 7, 2024 and then continued to January 8, 2024. Defendant avers Mr. Skinner was appointed as lead trial counsel in the instant matter in August 2024. Defendant argues that it is impossible for Mr. Skinner to attend and try two trials simultaneously. Defendant therefore seeks a four-month trial continuance in the instant matter. 

In opposition, Plaintiff argues the case matter is almost six years old, and that this is the fourth trial continuance. Plaintiff contends that current counsel Sara Bagheri and Mr. Skinner are also counsel in the Depto matter, and that Mr. Skinner took over the case in August 2024 knowing he had a trial conflict with Depto. Plaintiff asserts that there are two future hearing dates in the Depto Case in April 2025, and that therefore it is unlikely the Depto case will go to trial on January 8, 2025. 

In reply, Defendant argues Plaintiff has not demonstrated any prejudice as a result of a continuance, and that not any lawyer can substitute as lead trial counsel. 

Here, trial is set for December 30, 2024, which is approximately one month and eleven days away from the hearing date. There were three prior stipulations on March 18, 2020, July 22, 2020, and February 24, 2021 by the parties to continue trial, and the case matter is now nearly five years and ten months old to date. While the Court understands Defendant’s contention that its current lead trial counsel Mr. Skinner will be engaged in another trial that is scheduled to proceed on January 8, 2025, the Court would like more certainty that trial will proceed as expected in the Depto case before making a finding that there exists an actual trial calendaring conflict between the two matters. 

 

IV. CONCLUSION 

Accordingly, defendant St. Cecilia Catholic School’s motion to continue trial is DENIED without prejudice. 

 

Moving Party is ordered to give notice.

[TENTATIVE] ORDER GRANTING UNOPPOSED MOTION TO STRIKE DEFENDANT’S ANSWER TO FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT 

 

I. BACKGROUND 

On December 2, 2021, plaintiff 608 Mateo, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed a First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) against defendants Arts District Patients Collective, Inc., James Shaw, Ryan Jennemann (“Jennemann”), THC Design, THC Design, LLC, and Does 1 through 50 for breach of written contract and breach of written guaranty arising from the leasing of the premises commonly known as 608 S. Mateo Street and 609 Imperial Street, Los Angeles, California.¿The FAC sets forth two causes of action for (1) breach of written contract, and (2) breach of written guaranty. 

On January 4, 2022, defendants Jennemann and THC Design filed a collective Answer to Plaintiff’s FAC. 

On August 6, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion seeking to strike Jennemann’s Answer to the FAC and enter default against him pursuant to CCP §128, CCP §575.2, and Local Rules 3.10 and 3.25 on the grounds that Jennemann has failed to participate in the action and comply with the Court’s Orders and Rules. 

As of November 5, 2024, no opposition has been filed to the motion. 

 

II. LEGAL STANDARD 

Every court has statutory power to preserve and enforce order in its immediate presence (Code Civ. Proc. § 128(a)(1)), to enforce order in the proceedings before it or before a person or persons empowered to conduct a judicial investigation under its authority (Id.., § 128(a)(2)), to provide for the orderly conduct of proceedings before it or its officers (Id., § 128(a)(3)), and to compel obedience to its judgments, orders, and process, and to the orders of a judge out of court, in an action or proceeding pending therein (Id., § 128(a)(4)). A court's right to control its own proceedings must be exercised in the light of the fundamental interest of the public in maintaining an independent bar.¿(Mowrer v. Superior Court¿(1969) 3 Cal.App.3d 223, 230; Del Junco v. Hufnagel (2007) 150 Cal.App.4th 789, 799 [“Trial courts should only exercise this authority in extreme situations. . .”].)¿ 

“Although authorized to impose sanctions for violation of local rules [], courts ordinarily should avoid treating a curable violation of local procedural rules as the basis for crippling a litigant's ability to present his or her case.”¿(Elkins v. Superior Court (2007) 41 Cal.4th 1337, 1364; see also Kalivas v. Barry Controls Corp. (1996) 49 Cal.App.4th 1152, 1161 [“An order based upon a curable procedural defect (such as the failure to file a separate statement), which effectively results in a judgment against a party, is an abuse of discretion.”].) 

 

III. DISCUSSION 

Plaintiff argues striking Jennemann’s Answer is warranted under Code Civ. Proc. § 575.2(a) because Jennemann failed to appear at the Status Conferences on July 20, 2023, August 1, 2023, December 13, 2023, March 15, 2024, and at the FSCs on July 8, 2024, July 22, 2024 and on July 29, 2024 pursuant to Local Rules 3.10 and 3.25(f) and (f)(1). 

Local Rule 3.10 states, “The court may impose appropriate sanctions for the failure or refusal to comply with the rules in this chapter, including the time standards and/or deadlines, and any court order made pursuant to the rules. Counsel are directed to Code of Civil Procedure sections 128, 128.7, 177.5, 575.2, 583.150, 583.430, 2016.010-2036.050, Government Code section 68608, and California Rules of Court, rule 2.30. The sanctions may be imposed on a party and, if appropriate, on counsel for that party.” 

Local Rule 3.25 provides in relevant part that “Counsel must attend a final status conference, which the court will set not more than ten days prior to the trial date” (Local Rule 3.25(f)) and that “At least five days prior to the final status conference, counsel must serve and file lists of pre-marked exhibits to be used at trial (Local Rules 3.151, 3.53, and 3.149), jury instruction requests, trial witness lists, and a proposed short statement of the case to be read to the jury panel explaining the case. Failure to exchange and file these items may result in not being able to call witnesses, present exhibits at trial, or have a jury trial” (Local Rule 3.25(f)(1)). 

Code Civ. Proc. § 575.2(a) states in relevant part that “Local rules promulgated pursuant to Section 575.1 may provide that if… a party if in pro se, fails to comply with any of the requirements thereof, the court on motion of a party or on its own motion may strike out all or any part of any pleading of that party, or, dismiss the action or proceeding or any part thereof, or enter a judgment by default against that party…. No penalty may be imposed under this section without prior notice to, and an opportunity to be heard by, the party against whom the penalty is sought to be imposed.” On October 28, 2022, Jennemann’s counsel’s motion to be relieved as counsel was granted. As of November 7, 2022, Jennemann is unrepresented. (Notice of Order, Nov. 7, 2022.) Since then, there has been no appearance by Jennemann at any of the hearings to date, nor is there any indication that any party is in communication with him. No opposition has been filed to date. Considering Jennemann’s continuing absence in this matter over the course of two years, it is not apparent that any lesser penalties or sanctions would be effective. The motion will therefore be granted. 

   

IV. CONCLUSION 

Plaintiff 608 Mateo, LLC’s motion to strike defendant Ryan Jennemann’s Answer to the FAC is GRANTED.
