DEPARTMENT S25 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Imed Hsini v. Mohsen Ghariani, et al.
LASC Case No. 24LBCV00194
Hearing Date: September 11, 2024
Request for Default Judgment by Court
 
 
In preparation for the September 11, 2024 default judgment hearing, the court has identified several concerns with Plaintiff’s request: 
 

  1. Request for Entry of Default was entered on May 9, 2024 which is less than 30 days after service of the summons and complaint to Defendant Danielle Canissa and CDW Logistics, LLC. (C.C.P. §587);
 
  1. Plaintiff checked the wrong box and should have checked “Court Judgment” in the “Request For” section on the 1st page. (See 7/29/2024 Request for Entry of Default/Judgment);
 
  1. No DOEs dismissal filed;
 
  1. No attached/adequate and/or authenticated exhibits provided with Plaintiff’s Declaration. Exhibits should demonstrate, e.g. costs for title and insurance for two vehicles, power of attorney, agreement entered into with Defendants to purchase trucks and provide logistic and shipping services to clients;
 
  1. No contractual or statutory basis provided by Plaintiff as to recovery of attorney fees. Also, attorney fees requested is more than what is allowed in a contract for recovery of reasonable attorney fees under Local Rule of the Court, rule 3.214(a);  
 
  1. Prayer for complaint does not specify damages claimed;
 
  1. No notice of punitive damages as required under Code Civ. Proc., § 425.11, subd. (c).  Appears Plaintiff may be waiving punitive damages based upon proposed judgment;
 
  1. Proposed Judgment (UD-100) incorrect calculations as to attorney fees and prejudgment interest calculations.


Case Number: 23LBCV01066    Hearing Date: January 21, 2025    Dept: S25

Defendant American Honda Motor Company, Inc.’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff Annette Hamilton’s Deposition

Ruling

Defendant’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff’s Hamilton Deposition is GRANTED. Plaintiff is ordered to: (1) provide deposition dates within 10 days of the date of this hearing, (2) appear for deposition within 30 days of the date of this hearing and (3) provide code-compliant responses to the requests for production contained in the deposition notice.

The Court will not award monetary sanctions at this time.


Plaintiff Annette Hamilton’s Motion to Compel Defendant’s Compliance with Defendant American Honda Motor Company, Inc.’s Own Response to Plaintiff’s Request for Production of Documents, Set One, and for Monetary Sanctions

Tentative Ruling

It appears the instant motion is moot. Defendant states it provided responsive documents on December 20, 2024 – the day after the motion was filed. See Adjei Declaration Dated January 12, 2025. The issue of whether Defendant’s document production is incomplete should be resolved in a motion compel further responses, if necessary. As guidance, the Court refers the parties to the recent discovery guidelines set forth in CCP Section 871.6(h) which requires a defendant or manufacturer to provide the following:

1. Copy of or access to a version of the owner’s manual for a motor vehicle of the same make, model, and year.

2. Any warranties issued in conjunction with the sale of the motor vehicle.

3. Sample brochures published for the motor vehicle.

4. The motor vehicles original invoice, if any, to the selling dealer.

5. Sales or lease agreement, if the manufacturer is in possession.

6. Motor vehicle information reports, including build documentation, component information, and delivery details.

7. Entire warranty transaction history for the motor vehicle.

8. Listing of required field actions applicable to the motor vehicle.

9. Published technical service bulletins (TSBs) for the same make, model, and year reasonably related to the nonconformities pertaining to the motor vehicle.

10. Published information service bulletins (ISBs) for the same make, model, and year reasonably related to the nonconformities pertaining to the motor vehicle.

11. Records relating to communications between the manufacturer or dealership and the owner or lessee of the motor vehicle, including those related to repair orders or claims involving the motor vehicle.

12. Warranty policies and procedure manuals.

13. Service manuals reasonably related to the nonconformities pertaining to the motor vehicle.

14. If a pre-suit restitution or replacement request is made, all call recordings of pre-suit communications with the consumer available at the time of service of the complaint.

15. If a pre-suit restitution or replacement request is made, the manufacturers written statement of policies and procedures used to evaluate customer requests for restitution or replacement pursuant to Lemon Law claims.

16. If a pre-suit restitution or replacement request is made, any nonprivileged, prelitigation evaluation.

17. Any warranty extensions or modifications issued by the manufacturer on the motor vehicle.”

The Court declines to award monetary sanctions.

Case Number: 24LBCV01843    Hearing Date: January 21, 2025    Dept: S25

Procedural Background

On August 30, 2024, Plaintiffs filed a complaint against Defendants Palazzo Realty, Inc., Todd Family Trust and Does 1 to 10, alleging the following causes of action:

1) Breach of Implied Warranty of Habitability;

2) Breach of Contract;

3) Negligence;

4) Constructive Eviction;

5) Unjust Enrichment;

6) Violation of California Civil Code § 1942.5;

7) Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress;

8) Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress.

Plaintiffs allege that on they have resided as tenant of the residential property at 2459 Elm Avenue, Long Beach, California and that the tenancy was initiated based on a verbal rental agreement between Plaintiffs and the now-deceased Richard Todd. (Compl., ¶¶ 1.1, 3.1.) Plaintiffs further allege that they were met with severely substandard living conditions. (Compl., ¶ 4.1.)

Meet and Confer

Defense counsel states that she attempted to meet and confer with Plaintiffs but to no avail; the requirements are satisfied. (Seyler Decl., ¶¶ 2-8; Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41, subd. (a)(2), and § 435.5, subd. (a)(2) & (3).)

Tentative Ruling

Defendant Demurrer to the Complaint is sustained with leave leaved to amended as to all causes of action.

Statute of Limitations

The statute of limitations for a breach of an oral contract is two years. (Code Civ. Proc., § 339.) The statute of limitations for “an action for assault, battery, or injury to, or for the death of, an individual caused by the wrongful act or neglect of another” is two years. (Code Civ. Proc., § 335.1; see also Wassman v. South Orange County Community College

District (2018) 24 Cal.App.5th 825, 853.) Plaintiffs’ allegation that they have been aware of the breach of the oral contract or damages since 2005 means that the statute of limitations have run on the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 7th and 8th causes of action. The Court SUSTAINS the instant demurrer with leave to amend as to the 1st, 2nd, 3rd 7th and 8th causes of action on this ground.

The Statute of Frauds

Under the statute of frauds, codified in section 1624 of the Civil Code, certain transactions, including, as relevant here, an agreement regarding an interest in real property, must be reduced to writing and subscribed by the party to be charged to be enforceable. (Civ. Code § 1624(a)(3) [“An agreement for the leasing for a longer period than one year, or for the sale of real property, or of an interest therein; such an agreement, if made by an agent of the party sought to be charged, is invalid, unless the authority of the agent is in writing, subscribed by the party sought to be charged”.]) However, equitable estoppel permits enforcement of an oral agreement to prevent fraud when one party has detrimentally relied on an oral promise or another party has been unjustly enriched. (Juran v. Epstien (1994) 23 Cal.App.4th 882, 892). Equitable estoppel may preclude a statute of frauds defense. (Byrne v. Laura (1997) 51 Cal.App.4th 1054, 1068). Whether the doctrine of equitable estoppel should be applied in a given case is a question of fact. (Ibid.) Here, no written agreement has been attached to the complaint that overcomes the statute of frauds defense. Moreover, Plaintiffs have not alleged anything regarding equitable estoppel. The instant demurrer is SUSTAINED with leave to amend on the statute of frauds ground as well.

Todd Family Trust

“[A] trust itself can neither sue nor be sued in its own name. Instead, the real party in interest in litigation involving a trust is always the trustee.” (Powers v. Ashton (1975) 45 Cal.App.3d 783, 787; see also Presta v. Tepper (2009) 179 Cal.App.4th 909, 914.) The Court agrees with Defendant that Plaintiffs may not sue the Todd Family Trust in its own name; rather, Plaintiffs must sue the trustee of the Todd Family Trust. The instant demurrer is SUSTAINED with leave to amend as to this ground as to Defendant Todd Family Trust.

2nd Cause of Action – Breach of Contract

Plaintiffs allege that they agreed to pay rent in exchange for the right to occupy the property; however, there is no direct allegation that Plaintiffs did perform their obligation of paying rent. Plaintiffs’ request for emotional distress damages does not apply to the 2nd cause of action. (See Ehrlich v. Menezes (1999) 21 Cal.4th 543, 558 [“damages for mental suffering and emotional distress are generally not recoverable in an action for breach of an ordinary commercial contract in California.”]) The instant demurrer is also SUSTAINED WITH LEAVE TO AMEND as to the 2nd cause of action on this ground.

4th Cause of Action – Constructive Eviction

The Court finds that Plaintiffs have failed to allege that they surrendered or vacated the property, an essential element for a constructive eviction claim. The instant demurrer is SUSTAINED with leave to amend as to the 4th cause of action on this ground.

5th Cause of Action – Unjust Enrichment

"California does not recognize a cause of action for unjust enrichment.” (Hooked Media Group, Inc.. v. Apple. Inc. (2020) 55 Cal.App.5th 323, 336.) As the Court has sustained the demurrer as to all causes of action, the 5th cause of action cannot not survive standalone cause of action. The instant demurrer is SUSTAINED with leave to amend on this ground.
