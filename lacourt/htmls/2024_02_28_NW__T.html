23VECV03739 Ally Bank v Hovhannisyan

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Plaintiff Ally Bank’s Application for Writ of Possession is GRANTED.  The Court waives an undertaking from Plaintiff Ally Bank. 

Introduction

Plaintiff Ally Bank (Plaintiff) moved for a writ of possession against Defendant Meline Hovhannisyan (Defendant).      An opposition was due on February 14, 2024 and none was filed.

Discussion

Plaintiff’s proof of service showed that Defendant was properly served with the summons and complaint and properly notified of the hearing on the application.  (Code Civ. Proc. sec. 512.010.) 

Plaintiff provided a declaration to show the probable validity of their claim and right to repossess the vehicle from Defendant because Plaintiff provided evidence showing: Defendant’s entry into the Retail Installment Sales Contract (RISC) for the vehicle; Plaintiff assignor’s performance of delivery of the vehicle; Defendant’s breach in failing to pay; and Plaintiff’s damages.  (Code Civ. Proc sec. 512.040.)  (James Singleton Decl. pars. 5-9.)  Plaintiff further provided that the vehicle is in the custody and control of Defendant and located at Defendant’s residence.  (Singleton Decl. par. 10.)  (Code Civ. Proc. sec. 512.060.)  With Defendant’s breach, Defendant no longer has rights to possess and further refused to surrender the vehicle.  Plaintiff has shown their rights to possession.   

Plaintiff submitted the amount owed on the account and the value of the vehicle.  (Singleton Decl. pars. 6-7.)  The evidence showed that the account balance exceeds the value of the vehicle and no equity exists for the Defendant.  The Court finds that the requirement to post an undertaking is waived.  (Code Civ. Proc. sec. 515.010.) 

Plaintiff’s application for writ of possession is GRANTED.  The writ will issue forthwith.

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.

COBIO V MULHOLLAND SECURITY 23VECV05109

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Plaintiff Jennifer Cobio’s Unopposed Motion to Compel Arbitration and Stay the Action is GRANTED. 

Arbitration Status Conference is set 2/26/2025 at 8:30 a.m. in dept. T.

Plaintiff Jennifer Cobio’s Request for Monetary Sanctions is DENIED. 

Introduction

Plaintiff Jennifer Cobio (Plaintiff) moved to compel arbitration and stay the action she filed against Defendants Mulholland Security Centers, LLC, Abraham Ben David, and Avi ‘Coby’ Jacoby (collectively, Defendants).

Defendants’ opposition was due February 14, 2024 and none was filed.           

Discussion

A court must order an action to arbitration when the moving party establishes by a preponderance of evidence the existence of an arbitration agreement and that the dispute is within the scope of the arbitration agreement.  The burden then shifts to the opposing party to show a defense to enforcement.  (Hotels Nevada v. L.A. Pacific Center, Inc. (2006) 144 Cal.App.4th 754, 758)  Plaintiff submitted the arbitration agreement which showed that the employment claims alleged in the Complaint are within the scope of the arbitration provision.  (Pingle Decl. par. 3.)  Despite Plaintiff’s and Defendants’ Counsel’s efforts to arbitrate the dispute, Defendants failed to respond to the requests to arbitrate.  (Pingle Decl. pars. 5-11.)  Plaintiff met her burden of proof on the motion and the burden shifted to Defendants.  Because Defendants did not file an opposition, Defendants failed to meet their burden.

The motion to compel and stay the action is GRANTED. 

Within the points and authorities only, Plaintiff requested monetary sanctions to be levied against Defendants and Counsels.  (Code Civ. Proc., secs. 177.5 and 128.5.)  However, the Notice to the Motion failed to reference the request for sanctions, the amount requested, the authority for the request, and against whom the sanctions are to be levied.  Without any notice, Plaintiff failed to give proper notice and opportunity to be heard on the sanctions request.  Without due process, any award of sanctions would be void.

Plaintiff’s request for sanctions is DENIED. 

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.
