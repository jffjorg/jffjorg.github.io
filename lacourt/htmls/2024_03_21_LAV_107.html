DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23VECV01606    Hearing Date: March 21, 2024    Dept: 107

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

 

Michael Yoakum,

       

Plaintiff,

 

   v.

 

Arrowhead Financial Group, et al.

 

          Defendants.

 

 

   Case Number             Department

23VECV01606           107

 

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] RULING RE:

Leave to Amend the First Amended Complaint

 

 

 

 

 

I. BACKGROUND

On December 31, 2012, defendants Michael Henschel (“Henschel”), J. Velasquez a/k/a Carlos Velasquez (“Velasquez”), Todd J. Schellbach (“Schellbach”), and Camerina Islas (“Islas” and, together with Henschel, Velasquez, and Schellbach, “Defendants”) created an irrevocable trust, defendant Arrowhead Financial Group Trust (“Arrowhead”). (FAC p. 4.) On January 1, 2015, Defendants created a trust called the Orion Trust. (Ibid.) Defendants persuaded plaintiff Michael Yoakum (“Plaintiff”), a senior, to execute and record a deed of trust with assignment of rents, which is recorded as instrument no. 20122035812. (Ibid.) On December 31, 2012, Defendants created a second deed of trust with assignment of rents, which is recorded as instrument no. 2012035811. (Id. at p. 5.) On February 28, 2012, Defendants foreclosed on a certain real property and, although a trustee sale never took place, recorded a trustee’s deed upon sale as instrument no. 20120314433. (Ibid.) Defendants executed a grant deed for a certain real property at which Plaintiff resides and recorded it as instrument no. 20150060515. (Ibid.) All of these deeds and instruments were recorded without the authorization, consent, or ratification of Plaintiff. (Id. at p. 6.) Defendants have been convicted of committing similar real estate frauds in California and other states. (Ibid.)

 

On April 13, 2023, Plaintiff initiated this lawsuit by filing a complaint (the “Complaint”) alleging causes of action for (1) cancellation of instrument pursuant to Civil Code Sec. 3412, (2) elder financial abuse, and (3) revoke and terminate an irrevocable trust procured through fraud in factum pursuant to Probate Code Sec. 15403.

 

On August 29, 2023, Plaintiff amended the Complaint by filing an amended complaint (the “FAC”) alleging the same causes of action.

 

On January 11, 2024, defendants Arrowhead, Schellbach, and Henschel demurred to the FAC on the grounds that the claims therein are barred by the statute of limitations, that the allegations therein are vague and ambiguous, and that the FAC does not plead fraud with the requisite specificity. (Demurrer p. 1.)

 

On January 29, 2024, a case management conference was held, at which Plaintiff determined he wanted to amend the FAC and the court therefore scheduled this hearing on the Motion. (Minute Order 01/29/2024 p. 1; Motion p. 6.)

 

On February 16, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion (the “Motion”) for leave to amend the FAC and to file a second amended complaint (the proposed “SAC”). Defendants have not filed any opposition thereto.

 

II. THE MOTION

 

Plaintiff explains that the purpose of the SAC is primarily to plead new theories of liability based on facts previously pled and to join additional parties with interests in the real properties at issue. (Motion pp. 6–7.) Plaintiff categorizes the proposed amendments as either (1) non-substantive clerical corrections, (2) the addition of Highland Associates, L.P. as an additional plaintiff, (3) the addition of new title documents and clarification of the same along with clarification of existing causes of action, (4) the addition of new causes of action based on facts previously pled, or (5) the addition of new prayers for relief based on the new causes of action. (Id. at pp. 7–8.)

 

Plaintiff argues that leave to amend pleadings should be liberally granted except where an unreasonable delay in such amendment would cause prejudice to the other party. (Id. at pp. 8–9.) Plaintiff contends that leave to amend is timely sought here and that Defendants will not be prejudiced by such leave because no trial date has been set. (Id. at pp. 9–10.) Plaintiff argues that it is permissible for an amendment to a pleading to expand the scope of litigation so long as discovery is ongoing. (Id. at pp. 10–11.) Finally, Plaintiff seeks leave to add Highland Associates, L.P. as an additional plaintiff because it has an interest in one of the real properties involved in this case. (Id. at pp. 7, 11.)

 

III. ANALYSIS

 

“The court may…, in its discretion, after notice to the adverse party, allow, upon any terms as may be just, an amendment to any pleading or proceeding….” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 473, subd. (a)(1).) “A trial court has wide discretion to allow the amendment of pleadings, and generally courts will liberally allow amendments at any stage of the proceeding.” (Falcon v. Long Beach Genetics, Inc. (2014) 224 Cal.App.4th 1263, 1280.) “The policy favoring amendment is so strong that it is a rare case in which denial of leave to amend can be justified.” (Howard v. County of San Diego (2010) 184 Cal.App.4th 1422, 1428.) “Time and knowledge are important factors to be considered when granting or denying a motion to amend.” (Stockton v. Ortiz (1975) 47 Cal.App.3d 183, 194.) “If the motion to amend is timely made and the granting of the motion will not prejudice the opposing party, it is error to refuse permission to amend and where the refusal also results in a party being deprived of the right to assert a meritorious cause of action or a meritorious defense, it is not only error but an abuse of discretion.” (Morgan v. Superior Court of Cal. In and For Los Angeles County (1959) 172 Cal.App.2d 527, 530.) A court may grant leave to amend a pleading to add new parties. (See People By and Through Dept. of Public Works v. Clausen (1967) 248 Cal.App.2d 770, 785.) A court may also grant leave to amend to add new causes of action, except an amendment may not be permitted where “an attempt is made to state facts which give rise to a wholly distinct and different legal obligation against the defendant” and “a change is made in the liability sought to be enforced against the defendant.” (Klopstock v. Superior Court in and for the City and County of San Francisco (1941) 17 Cal.2d 13, 20.)

 

Here, a trial date has not yet been set and Plaintiff brings the Motion after the filing of a demurrer. This case remains in an early stage of litigation, and we do not believe that granting the Motion will prejudice the Defendants because of any delay.

 

Plaintiff seeks to add a new party as a plaintiff because that party has an interest in a real property of which the FAC already challenges proper ownership. Thus, adding this party will not substantially change the claims against Defendants and Defendants have already been made aware of the general nature of the dispute regarding that real property. We do not believe, therefore, that adding this party will prejudice the Defendants.

 

Plaintiff seeks to add new title documents related to the real properties described in the FAC. Like the above scenario, this will not substantially change the claims against Defendants and Defendants have already been made aware of the general nature of the dispute regarding those real properties. We do not believe, therefore, that adding these title documents will prejudice Defendants.

 

Plaintiff also seeks to add new theories of liability, quiet title and constructive trust, based on the same set of facts already pled in the FAC. This, similarly, is permissible because these claims, which arise from the general facts already pled will not “give rise to a wholly distinct and different legal obligation” against Defendants.

 

This court has broad discretion to liberally grant leave to amend so long as such amendment does not prejudice the other party. We find that no prejudice to the other party will result from the proposed amendments in this case.

 

Accordingly, this court GRANTS the Motion. [TENTATIVE]
