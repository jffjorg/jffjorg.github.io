DEPARTMENT 30 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

 PLEASE NOTE:    

The parties are encouraged to meet and confer concerning this tentative ruling to determine if there is an agreement to submit.  

Regardless of whether there is any such agreement, each party who wishes to submit must send an email to the Court at SSCdept30@LACourt.org indicating the party's intention to submit. 

Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the case number in the subject line of the email and in the body provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, or non-party.  

If a party submits but still intends to appear at the hearing, include the words "SUBMITS BUT WILL APPEAR" in the subject line of the email. 

If the Court does not receive emails from the parties indicating submission on this tentative ruling and there are no appearances at the hearing, the Court may, at its discretion, adopt the tentative as the final order or place the motion off calendar. 

Unless all the parties have submitted, the Court will hear argument from any party that appears at the hearing and wishes to argue. The Court may change its tentative as a result of the argument and adopt the changed tentative as the final order at the end of that hearing, even if all the parties are not present. 

Be advised that after the Court has posted/issued a tentative ruling, the Court has the inherent authority to prohibit the withdrawal of said motion and may adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the Court.     


Case Number: 20STCV33142    Hearing Date: September 20, 2023    Dept: 30

LORNA HOYLE vs MAX FOLLMER, et al.

Motion to Compel Independent Medical Examinations

Ruling:  Defendant’s IME motion is DENIED. Moving party to give notice.

Discussion:  The Court finds good cause exists to conduct the examinations.  The Court finds, however, that the notices lack the specificity required.  Namely, the Court finds that "deemed necessary" by the respective physician as a limitation on the scope of examination is too uncertain. For this reason, the motion is denied.

Case Number: 21STCV13016    Hearing Date: September 20, 2023    Dept: 30

ANN GELFAND, et al. vs LOS ANGELES DEPARTMENT OF WATER AND POWER, A PUBLIC ENTITY

Motion for Trial Preference

Ruling:  Court has signed the stipulation advancing the trial date, so the motion is off-calendar.  Parties chose a court holiday, so Court scheduled the trial for next best date for the Court.

Discussion:  Court initially rejected the stipulation because it had not read the motion and opposing pleadings.  This appears to be a reasonable resolution of the issues, affecting the Court's calendar.

Case Number: 21STCV13286    Hearing Date: September 20, 2023    Dept: 30

Narda Tercero v. Bailey Rae Brietzke, et al.

Motion to Consolidate

Ruling:  Defendant’s Motion to Consolidate is GRANTED.  Los Angeles case numbers 21STCV13286 and 21STCV13288 are consolidated for all purposes, including trial, under case number 21STCV13286.  All dates in 21STCV13288 are vacated and will be rescheduled by JA.  Moving party is ordered to give notice.

Discussion:  Factors weigh in favor of granting the motion, and no showing that consolidation would adversely affect the rights of any party was proffered.

Case Number: 21STCV44935    Hearing Date: September 20, 2023    Dept: 30

BLANCA C. ALVARADO vs ABM INDUSTRIES, INC., A CORPORATION, et al.

Motion to Continue the Trial Date

Ruling:  Defendant’s motion is GRANTED.  Trial is continued to March 5, 2024, at 8:30 am, in Department 30.  Final Status Conference is continued to February 20, 2024, at 10:00 am, in Department 30.  All trial related deadlines are continued in correspondence with the new trial date.  Moving party to give notice.

Discussion:  Good cause appearing and no prejudice shown, motion is granted.  This is a noticed motion with no opposition.

Case Number: 22STCV22226    Hearing Date: September 20, 2023    Dept: 30

CHARLES E. SMITH vs RICHARD POE SUAAVA, et al.

Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiff

Ruling:  Counsel’s Motion to be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiff Charles Smith is GRANTED.  Pursuant to CRC Rule 3.1362(e), a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case.  The effective date of the order relieving counsel is delayed until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the Court. Moving party to give notice.

Discussion:  Counsel filed a Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel (MC-051), an Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel (MC-053), and a Declaration in Support of Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel (MC-052). (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, subd. (a), (c), (e).)

Plaintiff will not be prejudiced if Counsel’s motion is granted. At the time this motion was initially filed, the next hearing in this matter was a final conference on Final Status Conference on December 22, 2023. This is still the next hearing. Trial is set to begin January 5, 2024.

The Court is satisfied that Counsel has a compelling reason to withdraw.
