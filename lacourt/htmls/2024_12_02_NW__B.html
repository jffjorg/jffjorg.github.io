Case Number: 21STCV05058    Hearing Date: December 2, 2024    Dept: B

 

Hearing:             12/02/2024

Re:                         21STCV05058 David Medina vs Rives Grogan, et al.

 

 

Plaintiff David Medina’s Request for Default Judgment

 

Defendant: Rives Grogan

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiff David Medina’s Request for Default Judgment against Defendant Rives Grogan is DENIED.

           

BACKGROUND

 

This is a personal injury action. On February 9, 2021, Plaintiff David Medina (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint against Defendants Rives Grogan (“Grogan”), New Beginnings Christian Discipleship (“New Beginnings”), GM Service (“GM”), and Does 1 through 25 (collectively “Defendants”), alleging two causes of action: (1) general negligence; and (2) premises liability.

 

On June 5, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

 

On 3/22/23 default was entered as to Grogan.

 

On August 1, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant request for default judgment.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

Cal. Code of Civil Procedure § 585 permits entry of a default judgment after a party has failed to timely respond or appear. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 585.) A party seeking judgment on the default by the court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) declaration of nonmilitary status; (6) a proposed form of judgment; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorney’s fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1800.) 

 

ANALYSIS

 

Plaintiff seeks damages in the total amount of $490,718.81, representing $188,325.48 in special damages, $300,000.00 in general damages, and $2,393.33 in attorney’s fees.

 

Plaintiffs’ request for default judgment against Defendants is denied for the following deficiencies:

 

1.       Plaintiff failed to file a CIV-100 requesting default judgment. (CRC 3.1800(a).)

2.       Plaintiff failed to file a JUD-100 or other proposed order. (CRC 3.1800(a)(6).)

 

3/ Plaintiff requests attorney’s fees but fails to provide a retainer agreement or other statutory basis for attorney’s fees. (Cal. Civ. Code § 1717.5(a); CRC 3.1800(a)(9).)

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s request for default judgment against Defendant Rives Grogan is DENIED. Plaintiff is put on notice that further deficient filings risk sanctions or dismissal pursuant to Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 583.410 and CRC 3.110 (h).

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from the September 2019 stabbing and subsequent death of Defendant Cyndy’s Cleaning Services (“Cyndy’s”)’s employee Kamryn Stone. (Complaint ¶¶ 4-5.) On June 30, 2021, Barry F. Stone, individually and as successor-in-interest to the Estate of Kamryn Stone and Kristie Campbell-Stone, sued ABM Industry Groups, LLC (“ABM”), and other defendants, alleging premises liability, negligence, negligent hiring, and assault and battery. (Ibid. at ¶ 4.)

 

On June 7, 2024, this action was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

 

On October 29, 2024, Samuel C. Gazzo, Esq. (“Counsel”) filed and served the instant unopposed Motion to be Relieved as Counsel for Cyndy’s.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

The Court GRANTS the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel filed by Samuel C. Gazzo, Esq.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

The court may order that an attorney be changed or substituted at any time before or after judgment or final determination upon request by either client or attorney and after notice from one to the other. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 284(2).) “The determination whether to grant or deny a motion to withdraw as counsel lies within the sound discretion of the trial court.” (Manfredi & Levine v. Superior Court (1998) 66 Cal.App.4th 1128, 1133.)   

 

An application to be relieved as counsel must be made on Judicial Counsel Form MC-051 (Notice of Motion and Motion) (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1362(a)), MC-052 (Declaration) (CRC Rule 3.1362(c)), and MC-053 (Proposed Order) (CRC Rule 3.1362(e)).  The proposed order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. (CRC Rule 3.1362(e).)

 

Further, the requisite forms must be served on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case. (CRC Rule 3.1362(d).) The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (CRC Rule 3.1362(e).) The Court has discretion on whether to allow an attorney to withdraw, and a motion to withdraw will not be granted where withdrawal would prejudice the client.  (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.) 

 

DISCUSSION

Counsel declares that Developers Surety & Indemnity Company (“DSIC”) retained Counsel to defend Cyndy’s in this matter. (MC-052 Item 2.) DSIC obtained judgment against Cyndy’s which provides in pertinent part that DSIC has no duty to defend or indemnify Cyndy’s in this matter. (Ibid.) Accordingly, DSIC has terminated Counsel’s representation and directed Counsel to withdraw from this case. (Ibid.)

Counsel has filed and served Cyndy’s with the required documents under CRC Rule 3.1362. Cyndy’s will not be prejudiced by Counsel’s relief as they have adequate time to find alternative representation. Therefore, Counsel’s motion to be relieved is granted, effective upon service and filing of the proof of service of the Order with the Court.

CONCLUSION

 

The Court GRANTS the Motion to be Relieved as Counsel filed by Samuel C. Gazzo, Esq. Court to sign proposed Order.

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

 

 

 


Case Number: 22STCV04240    Hearing Date: December 2, 2024    Dept: B

 

Hearing:             12/02/2024

Re:                         22STCV04240 Arturo Sandoval vs Michael Scott Hurtado Gomez, et al.

 

 

Plaintiff Arturo Sandoval’s Request for Default Judgment

 

Defendant: Michael Scott Hurtado Gomez

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiff Arturo Sandoval’s Request for Default Judgment against Defendant Michael Scott Hurtado Gomez is DENIED.

           

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises from a motor vehicle accident. On February 3, 2022, Plaintiff Arturo Sandoval (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint against Defendants Michael Scott Hurtado Gomez (“Gomez”), Jesus Edgar Salazar (“Salazar”), and Does 1 to 15 (collectively “Defendants”), alleging two causes of action: (1) motor vehicle negligence; and (2) general negligence.

 

On June 6, 2024, the case was reassigned to the Honorable Karen Moskowitz in Department B at Van Nuys Courthouse East, effective June 24, 2024.

 

On November 20, 2024, Plaintiffs filed the instant request for default judgment.

 

Gomez default was entered on 5/1/24.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

Cal. Code of Civil Procedure § 585 permits entry of a default judgment after a party has failed to timely respond or appear. (Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 585.) A party seeking judgment on the default by the court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) declaration of nonmilitary status; (6) a proposed form of judgment; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorney’s fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties. (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1800.) 

 

ANALYSIS

 

Plaintiff seeks damages in the total amount of $680,560.82, representing $180,060.82 in special damages, $500,000.00 in general damages, and $500.00 in costs.

 

Plaintiff’s request for default judgment against Defendant Michael Scott Hurtado Gomez is denied for the following deficiencies:

 

1.      Plaintiffs failed to file a copy of the Statements of Damages served upon Defendants. (CRC Rule 3.250(a)(20).)

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s request for default judgment against Defendant Michael Scott Hurtado Gomez is DENIED. Plaintiffs are put on notice that further deficient filings risk sanctions or dismissal pursuant to Cal. Code of Civ. Proc. § 583.410 and CRC 3.100(h).

 

Moving party is ordered to give notice.
