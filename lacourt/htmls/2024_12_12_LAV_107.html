DEPARTMENT 107 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23VECV02397    Hearing Date: December 12, 2024    Dept: 107

 

 

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

NORTHWEST DISTRICT

 

 

Zeferino Juarez Vazquez,

Plaintiff.

 

   v.

 

Uber Technologies, Inc., et al.

 

Defendants.

 

 

 

   Case Number             Department

    23VECV02397                             107

 

 

COURT’S [TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:       Motion to be Relieved as Counsel

 

 

 

[THE FOLLOWING IS A TENTATIVE RULING IN THE ABOVE CASE]:

 

Grant

 

 

I.                BACKGROUND

 

On or about June 2, 2021, Plaintiff Zeferino Juarez Vazquez (“Plaintiff”) was riding a bicycle when Plaintiff was hit by a vehicle driven by Defendants Caeros O. Durias and Does 1 through 10. (Complaint ¶¶ 19-21, 24.) Defendants PV Holding Corp. and Does 61 through 70 were the owners, lessors, and/or lessees of the vehicle. (Complaint ¶ 13.) At the time of the incident, Defendants Caeros O. Durias and Does 1 through 10 were providing services for Defendants Uber Technologies, Inc., Raiser, LLC, Raiser-CA, LLC, Raiser-DC, LLC, Raiser-PA, LLC, and Does 11 through 60. (Complaint ¶ 14.)

 

On June 1, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint against Defendants Uber Technologies, Inc., Raiser, LLC, Raiser-CA, LLC, Raiser-DC, LLC, Raiser-PA, LLC, Caeros O. Durias, PV Holding Corp., and Does 1 to 70, alleging causes of action for negligence/negligence per se, negligence (hiring, training, supervision and retention) and negligent entrustment.

 

On December 11, 2023, Plaintiff filed a request to dismiss Defendants Raiser, LLC, Raiser-CA, LLC, Raiser-DC, LLC, and Raiser-PA, LLC. (Request for Dismissal filed 12/11/23.) On December 18, 2023, Plaintiff filed a request to dismiss Defendant PV Holding Corp. (Request for Dismissal filed 12/18/23.)  

 

On October 23, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel (“Counsel”) filed a motion to be relieved as counsel. This court denied that motion. (11/18/24 Minute Order p. 5.)

 

On December 4, 2024, Counsel filed another motion to be relieved as counsel (the “Motion”). No opposition has been filed.

 

II.             LEGAL STANDARD

 

“The attorney in an action or special proceeding may be changed at any time before or after judgment or final determination . . . [u]pon the order of the court, upon the application of either client or attorney, after notice from one to the other.” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 284(2).)

 

California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362 requires:

 

1.     Notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client (made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-051));

 

2.     A declaration stating in general terms, and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney client relationship, why a motion under California Code of Civil Procedure section 284, subdivision (2) is brought instead of filing a consent under California Code of Civil Procedure section 284, subdivision (1) (made on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-052));

 

3.     Service of the notice of motion and motion, declaration, and proposed order on the client and all other parties who have appeared in the case, and

 

4.     The proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-053)).

 

The court may deny the motion to withdraw if it will cause undue delay in the proceeding or cause injustice. (Mandell v. Superior Court (1977) 67 Cal.App.3d 1, 4.) “The determination whether to grant or deny an attorney’s motion to withdraw as counsel of record lies within the sound discretion of the trial court, having in mind whether such withdrawal might work an injustice in the handling of the case.” (Lempert v. Superior Court (2003) 112 Cal.App.4th 1161, 1173.) An attorney is not limited to withdrawing from a case for cause and may withdraw when withdrawal can be accomplished without undue prejudice to the client. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 26 Cal.Rptr.2d 554, 559.)

 

III.           DISCUSSION

 

(A) Notice—Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(a)

 

“A notice of motion and motion to be relieved as counsel under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) must be directed to the client and must be made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-051).” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(a).)

 

The Motion complies with this requirement.

 

(B) Declaration—Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(c)

 

“The motion to be relieved as counsel must be accompanied by a declaration on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-052). The declaration must state in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284(1).” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(c).)

 

The Motion complies with this requirement. Counsel submits a declaration on Form MC-052 stating:

 

There has been a complete breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. The client has repeatedly failed to comply with essential litigation requirements, including appearing for scheduled depositions and meeting court deadlines, despite numerous attempts to communicate and coordinate compliance. The client has also been unresponsive to communications and requests for necessary information and cooperation. Furthermore, the client has made explicit threats to report counsel to the State Bar, creating a situation where continued representation would be unethical and unworkable, impairing counsel's ability to effectively advocate on the client's behalf. Therefore, withdrawal is necessary to preserve counsel’s ethical obligations and avoid prejudice to the client.

 

(MC-052 p. 1.)

 

(C) Service—Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(d); Super. Ct. L.A. County, Local Rules, Rule 4.35

 

“The notice of motion and motion, the declaration, and the proposed order must be served on the client and on all other parties who have appeared in the case. The notice may be by personal service, electronic service, or mail.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d).)

 

“If the notice is served on the client by mail under Code of Civil Procedure section 1013, it must be accompanied by a declaration stating facts showing that either:

 

1.     The service address is the current residence or business address of the client; or

 

2.     The service address is the last known residence or business address of the client and the attorney has been unable to locate a more current address after making reasonable efforts to do so within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved.”

 

(Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d)(1).)

 

“As used in this rule, ‘current’ means that the address was confirmed within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved. Merely demonstrating that the notice was sent to the client's last known address and was not returned or no electronic delivery failure message was received is not, by itself, sufficient to demonstrate that the address is current. If the service is by mail, Code of Civil Procedure section 1011(b) applies.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d).)

 

“When an attorney files a motion to withdraw as attorney of record for a fiduciary, service must be made by citation. The citation must be served in the manner provided in Code of Civil Procedure sections 415.10 or 415.30. If the fiduciary resides outside of California, service may also be made in the manner provided in Code of Civil Procedure section 415.40.” (Super. Ct. L.A. County, Local Rules, rule 4.35.)

 

The Motion complies with these requirements.

 

Counsel submits a proof of service showing the Notice of Motion and Motion, Declaration, and Proposed Order were served on Plaintiff by mail. (Proof of Service filed 12/04/24.) Counsel’s declaration states that Counsel confirmed by telephone and conversation, within the past thirty days, that Plaintiff’s address is current. (MC-052 p. 1.)

 

Counsel also submits a proof of service showing the Notice of Motion and Motion, Declaration, and Proposed Order were served on Defendants Caeros O. Durias and Uber Technologies, Inc. (Counsel’s Declaration filed 11/15/24 Ex. B.)

 

(D) Proposed Order—Cal. Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362(e)

 

“The proposed order relieving counsel must be prepared on the Order Granting Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel-Civil (form MC-053) and must be lodged with the court with the moving papers. The order must specify all hearing dates scheduled in the action or proceeding, including the date of trial, if known. If no hearing date is presently scheduled, the court may set one and specify the date in the order. After the order is signed, a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case. The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).)

 

The Motion complies with this requirement.

 

IV.           CONCLUSION

 

For the foregoing reasons, this court grants the Motion.

I.                BACKGROUND 

 

On or about June 2, 2021, Plaintiff Zeferino Juarez Vazquez (“Plaintiff”) was riding a bicycle when Plaintiff was hit by a vehicle driven by Defendants Caeros O. Durias and Does 1 through 10. (Complaint ¶¶ 19-21, 24.) Defendants PV Holding Corp. and Does 61 through 70 were the owners, lessors, and/or lessees of the vehicle. (Complaint ¶ 13.) At the time of the incident, Defendants Caeros O. Durias and Does 1 through 10 were providing services for Defendants Uber Technologies, Inc., Raiser, LLC, Raiser-CA, LLC, Raiser-DC, LLC, Raiser-PA, LLC, and Does 11 through 60. (Complaint ¶ 14.)  

 

On June 1, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint against Defendants Uber Technologies, Inc., Raiser, LLC, Raiser-CA, LLC, Raiser-DC, LLC, Raiser-PA, LLC, Caeros O. Durias, PV Holding Corp., and Does 1 to 70, alleging causes of action for negligence/negligence per se, negligence (hiring, training, supervision and retention) and negligent entrustment.

 

On December 11, 2023, Plaintiff filed a request to dismiss Defendants Raiser, LLC, Raiser-CA, LLC, Raiser-DC, LLC, and Raiser-PA, LLC. (Request for Dismissal filed 12/11/23.) On December 18, 2023, Plaintiff filed a request to dismiss Defendant PV Holding Corp. (Request for Dismissal filed 12/18/23.)

 

On October 25, 2024, Defendant Caeros O. Durias (“Defendant”) filed a motion to continue trial (the “Motion”). No opposition has been filed.

 

II.             LEGAL STANDARD 

 

Trial dates are firm to ensure prompt disposition of civil cases and continuances are generally disfavored. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1332(a), (c).)¿ Nevertheless, the trial court has discretion to continue trial dates.¿ (Hernandez v. Superior Court (2004) 115 Cal.App.4th 1242, 1246.) 

 

Circumstances that may indicate good cause include:  

 

1.               The unavailability of an essential lay or expert witness because of death, illness, or other excusable circumstances;  

 

2.               The unavailability of a party because of death, illness, or other excusable circumstances;  

 

3.               The unavailability of trial counsel because of death, illness, or other excusable circumstances;  

 

4.               The substitution of trial counsel, but only where there is an affirmative showing that the substitution is required in the interests of justice;  

 

5.               The addition of a new party if (A) the new party has not had a reasonable opportunity to conduct discovery and prepare for trial, or (B) the other parties have not had a reasonable opportunity to conduct discovery and prepare for trial in regard to the new party’s involvement in the case;  

 

6.               A party’s excused inability to obtain essential testimony, documents, or other material evidence despite diligent efforts; or 

 

7.               A significant, unanticipated change in the status of the case as a result of which the case is not ready for trial.¿  

 

(Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1332(c).)

 

The court must also consider relevant factors, that may include:  

 

1.               The proximity of the trial date;  

 

2.               Whether there was any previous continuance, extension of time, or delay of trial due to any party;  

 

3.               The length of the continuance requested;  

 

4.               The availability of alternative means to address the problem that gave rise to the motion or application for a continuance;

 

5.               The prejudice that parties or witnesses will suffer as a result of the continuance; 

 

6.               If the case is entitled to a preferential trial setting, the reasons for that status and whether the need for a continuance outweighs the need to avoid delay; 

 

7.               The court’s calendar and the impact of granting a continuance on other pending trials;  

 

8.               Whether trial counsel is engaged in another trial; 

 

9.               Whether all parties have stipulated to a continuance;  

 

10.            Whether the interests of justice are best served by a continuance, by the trial of the matter, or by imposing conditions on the continuance; and 

 

11.            Any other fact or circumstance relevant to the fair determination of the motion or application.¿  

 

(Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1332(d).)¿¿ 

 

III.           DISCUSSION 

 

The trial is currently set for February 18, 2025. Defendant seeks a 180-day continuance. (Motion p. 7.) Defendant states good cause exists to continue the trial because: (1) Plaintiff’s counsel filed a motion to be relieved as counsel, and will, therefore, be unavailable for trial in February; (2) Defendant has been unable to prepare for trial because Plaintiff has not participated in a deposition; and (3) Defendant’s counsel is unavailable because of a planned family vacation. (Motion pp. 5-6.)

 

Defendant’s counsel submits a declaration stating that: “[o]n or around October 1, 2024, after several unsuccessful attempts to set Plaintiff for deposition, our office was informed by Plaintiff’s counsel that they have lost contact with their client and are actively working on locating him” and “Plaintiff’s counsel has filed a motion to be relieved as counsel due to a complete breakdown in the attorney-client relationship.” (Riddles Declaration ¶ 5.)

 

Defendant’s counsel’s declaration also states that counsel has “a personal conflict with the current set trial dates due to a planned family vacation from February 15, 2025 through February 22, 2025, which was unknown to me at the time of the Case Management Conference when this case was set for trial. The trip was booked by a family member of mine without my knowledge and accommodations have been paid for. Further, my son’s school schedule for the 2024 – 2025 school year was unknown at the time of the Case Management Conference, and I since learned he does not have school the week of February 17, 2024. I will face . . .  undue hardship between trial dates and childcare obligations given these circumstances.” (Riddles Declaration ¶ 7.)

 

Finally, Defendant’s counsel’s declaration states “Plaintiff’s absence of participation in the discovery process is prejudicial to Defendants’ ability to narrow the liability issue in controversy, evaluate Plaintiff’s claims, conduct percipient witness and expert witness discovery, develop defenses, engage in resolution efforts, and prepare for trial. Given this absence, it is not practical to realistically settle this matter before the current trial dates set in February 2025 or to fully prepare for the trial considering the anticipated cost and difficulty to deal with a Plaintiff that may not appear.” (Riddles Declaration ¶ 8.)

  

California Rules of Court, rule 3.1332, subdivision (c)(6) provides that circumstances that may indicate good cause to continue a trial include “a party’s excused inability to obtain essential testimony, documents, or other material evidence despite diligent efforts[.]”  

 

Defendant made “several unsuccessful attempts” to schedule Plaintiff’s deposition. (Riddles Declaration ¶ 5.) Defendant filed a motion to compel Plaintiff’s deposition, which is set for hearing on January 22, 2024. (Motion to Compel Deposition pp. 1-2; Riddles Declaration ¶ 6.) Further, Defendant submits emails showing that on October 1, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel informed Defendant that Plaintiff’s counsel lost contact with Plaintiff. (Ex. A.) Plaintiff’s counsel subsequently filed a motion to withdraw as counsel. (Riddles Declaration ¶ 5.)

 

This court finds that Defendant made diligent efforts to obtain Plaintiff’s deposition testimony, and Defendant’s inability to complete the deposition is excused. Thus, good cause exists to continue the trial.

 

Defendant’s inability to obtain Plaintiff’s deposition testimony constitutes good cause to continue the trial, so this court does not consider whether Defendant’s planned vacation constitutes good cause. However, the court does note that Defendant’s counsel’s planned vacation is approximately one-week long. (Riddles Declaration ¶ 7.) Thus, even if considered, the vacation alone does not justify continuing the trial for six months.

 

The current trial date is set for approximately two months after the hearing on this Motion. There have been no previous continuances requested by any party in this case. (Riddles Declaration ¶ 9.) Defendant is seeking a relatively lengthy 180-day continuance. (Motion p. 7.) Defendant has sought to use alternative means to obtain the necessary discovery – including filing a motion to compel Plaintiff’s deposition. (Riddles Declaration ¶¶ 5-6; Motion to Compel Deposition.) With the trial date approaching, no other alternative means exist to address the problem giving rise to the requested continuance. There is no evidence any party or witness will be prejudiced by this continuance. (Motion p. 7.) Finally, the interests of justice are best served by a continuance because Plaintiff’s failure to appear at Defendant’s depositions has prevented Defendant from preparing for trial. (Riddles Declaration ¶ 8.)

 

Overall, this court finds that these factors weigh in favor of granting the Motion.

 

IV.           EXTENDING DISCOVERY

 

Defendant also requests this court extend “all Trial-Related Deadlines for a period of 180 days, or the earliest date thereafter that is convenient for this Court.” (Motion p. 8.)

 

“Except as provided in Section 2024.050, a continuance or postponement of the trial date does not operate to reopen discovery proceedings.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2024.020(b).)

 

Under Civil Code of Procedure section 2024.050, subdivision (a), “On motion of any party, the court may grant leave to complete discovery proceedings, or to have a motion concerning discovery heard, closer to the initial trial date, or to reopen discovery after a new trial date has been set. This motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under Section 2016.040.”

 

Defendant’s counsel’s declaration does not contain the required meet and confer declaration. (Riddles Declaration.)

 

V.              CONCLUSION 

 

For the foregoing reasons, this court grants the Motion. This court denies, without prejudice, Defendant’s request to extend discovery deadlines.
