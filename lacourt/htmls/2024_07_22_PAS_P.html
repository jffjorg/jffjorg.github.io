Case Number: 19GDCV00813    Hearing Date: July 22, 2024    Dept: P

[TENTATIVE] ORDER GRANTING THE FIRM OF FREEDMAN, TAITELMAN AND COOLEY, LLP’S MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS ATTORNEY OF RECORD FOR CROSS-DEFENDANT JONGHYEOK CHOI

The Court may issue an order allowing an attorney to withdraw from representation after notice to the client. (CCP § 284.) The attorney may withdraw from representation as long as the withdrawal would not result in undue prejudice to the client's interest—i.e., counsel cannot withdraw at a critical point in the litigation. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal. App. 4th 904, 915; see California Rule of Professional Conduct 3-700.)

Attorney Steven E. Formaker, on behalf of Freedman, Taitelman and Cooley, LLP, seeks to be relieved as counsel for Cross-Defendant Jonghyeok Choi.  Mr. Formaker asserts that he is seeking to withdraw because “there is an amount owing, unpaid, and past due for the representation of Mr. Choi. Mr. Choi has not responded to repeated requests that he pay the past due amount and provide a deposit to secure payment of future fees.”  The motion is unopposed. Mr. Formaker has complied with all procedural requirements, including giving notice of this hearing to his client.

There are no future court hearings scheduled.  Mr. Choi’s court trial occurred in February 2024.  Therefore, this is not a critical point in the litigation.

 THE MOTION IS GRANTED. COUNSEL IS TO FILE PROOF OF SERVICE OF ORDER ON MR. CHOI WITHIN 5 DAYS OF NOTICE OF RULING. COUNSEL WILL BE RELIEVED UPON FILING OF PROOF OF SERVICE OF ORDER.

 The clerk to give notice.

Dated July 22, 2024

JARED D. MOSES
JUDGE OF THE SUPERIOR COURT

[TENTATIVE] ORDER GRANTING ANNA GREENSTIN AND THE FIRM OF HART, KIENLE & PENTECOST LLP’S MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS ATTORNEYS OF RECORD FOR PLAINTIFF ELECTRACORP, INC.

The Court may issue an order allowing an attorney to withdraw from representation after notice to the client. (CCP § 284.) The attorney may withdraw from representation as long as the withdrawal would not result in undue prejudice to the client's interest—i.e., counsel cannot withdraw at a critical point in the litigation. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal. App. 4th 904, 915; see California Rule of Professional Conduct 3-700.)

Attorney Anna Greenstin and the firm of Hart, Kienle and Pentecost seek to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff Electracorp, Inc. Ms. Greenstin asserts that she is seeking to withdraw because “(1) there has been a breakdown in attorney client communications, which has rendered it unreasonably difficult for counsel to carry out representation effectively (See Rule 1.16(b)(4)); and (2) a material breach of client's obligations to attorney (see Rule 1.16(b)(5)). Client has declined to secure new counsel rendering this Motion necessary as a Substitution cannot be filed.”

The motion is unopposed. Ms. Greenstin has complied with all procedural requirements, including giving notice of this hearing to her client.

The next court hearing in this matter is a status conference re mediation completion set for June 20, 2025.  Therefore, this is not a critical point in the litigation.

             THE MOTION IS GRANTED. COUNSEL IS TO FILE PROOF OF SERVICE OF ORDER ON ELECTRACORP, INC. WITHIN 5 DAYS OF NOTICE OF RULING. COUNSEL WILL BE RELIEVED UPON FILING OF PROOF OF SERVICE OF ORDER.

The clerk to give notice.
