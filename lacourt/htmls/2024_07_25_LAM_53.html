[tentative] Order RE:

 

defendants’ motion for summary judgment or, in the alternative, summary adjudication

 

 

MOVING PARTIES:              Defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso

 

RESPONDING PARTY:        Plaintiff Haryati T.S. Sentoso

Motion for Summary Judgment or, in the Alternative, Summary Adjudication

Defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso (“Defendants”) filed the pending motion for summary judgment or, in the alternative, summary adjudication against plaintiff Haryati T.S. Sentoso (“Plaintiff”) on June 12, 2023.

On May 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed an opposition to Defendants’ motion, representing that “this case has been settled” and that the parties would be “submitting a Stipulated Judgment in this case consistent with the terms of the Settlement Agreement.”  (May 30, 2024 Opp., pp. 1:24, 2:1-2.)  Defendants did not (1) file a reply disputing this representation, or (2) take this motion off calendar as moot before the June 13, 2024 hearing on this motion. 

            Pursuant to Plaintiff’s representations, the court issued an order on June 13, 2024 exercising its discretion to continue the hearing on Defendants’ motion to July 25, 2024, at 10:00 a.m., in Department 53.

The parties have not filed a Stipulated Judgment with the court, nor has Plaintiff filed a Notice of Settlement of Entire Case.  However, on July 11, 2024, Plaintiff filed a second opposition to Defendants’ motion, in which Plaintiff (1) opposes the motion on the ground that “this case has been settled[,]” (2) states that, on May 9, 2024, the Probate Court in the case Estate of Mulyadi Tirto Susielo Sentoso (Case No. 20STPB02178) granted Plaintiff’s motion to enforce the settlement agreement pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6, and (3) states that Defendants’ counsel advised Plaintiff’s counsel that Defendants appealed the Probate Court’s order granting Plaintiff’s motion to enforce settlement.  (May 30, 2024 Opp., p. 1:20-24; July 11, 2024 Opp., pp. 1:23-2:6.)  On July 12, 2024, Defendants filed a “Notice of Filing of Appeal in Related Case,”[1] advising the court that Defendants filed an appeal of the court’s order granting Plaintiff’s motion to enforce the settlement agreement in the probate action.  (Def. Notice of Appeal, pp. 1:25-2:4 and Ex. A [Notice of Appeal and Designation of Record].)

Plaintiff has attached a copy of the order granting the motion to enforce settlement agreement, issued by the court in the probate action on May 9, 2024, in which the Probate Court (1) set forth the terms of the settlement agreement, including that it (i) was executed by Plaintiff, Defendants, and their respective counsel, (ii) provides for the payment of $450,000 to Plaintiff from the Estate of Mulyadi Sentoso, (iii) requires the entry of a stipulated judgment “in a related civil action” in Plaintiff’s favor for $2,550,000, and (iv) requires the dismissal of individual claims asserted against defendant Keiko Hasegawa in the civil action, and (2) granted Plaintiff’s motion to enforce the settlement agreement over the objection of Defendants.  (May 9, 2024 Order, pp. 1, 5.)  Although the Probate Court’s order did not identify the related civil action to be this case and the parties did not submit a copy of the settlement agreement to the court in this case, both Plaintiff and Defendants appear to agree that the settlement agreement that was the subject of the court’s order provided for the settlement of this action.  (July 11, 2024 Opp., pp. 1:23 [opposing motion on the grounds that this action was settled], 2:7-9 [requesting the court deny the motion and set an Order to Show Cause re status of appeal based on the parties’ settlement and possible appeal of the order issued in the probate action]; Def. Notice of Appeal, p. 2:4-6 [stating that Defendants appealed the order “granting a motion to enforce a settlement agreement between the parties in this action”].) 

The parties also appear to contend that this action is stayed by Defendants’ filing of their appeal of the Probate Court’s order granting Plaintiff’s motion to enforce settlement in the probate action.  (Pl. July 11, 2024 Opp., p. 2:4-5 [“the appeal could result in a stay of the order enforcing the settlement”], 2:7-8; Def. Notice of Appeal, p. 2:7-8 [“the appeal stays the proceedings in this matter”]; Code Civ. Proc., § 916, subd. (a) [Except as otherwise provided, “the perfecting of an appeal stays proceedings in the trial court upon the judgment or order appealed from or upon the matters embraced therein or affected thereby, including enforcement of the judgment or order, but the trial court may proceed upon any other matter embraced in the action and not affected by the judgment or order”].)  Although this issue has not been fully briefed, and it is not clear whether Defendants’ filing of the appeal from the May 9, 2024 order in the probate action stays this action, the court finds that the interests of justice and judicial efficiency support staying this action and therefore orders that this action is stayed pending completion of Defendants’ appeal of the Probate Court’s order granting Plaintiff’s motion to enforce the parties’ settlement agreement.  (Freiberg v. City of Mission Viejo (1995) 33 Cal.App.4th 1484, 1489 [“Trial courts generally have the inherent power to stay proceedings in the interests of justice and to promote judicial efficiency”].)[2]

 

 

ORDER

            The court orders that this action is stayed pending completion of defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso’s appeal of the May 9, 2024 order granting the motion to enforce settlement in the probate case Estate of Mulyadi Tirto Susielo Sentoso (Case No. 20STPB02178).

            The court orders that the hearing on defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S.             The court orders defendants Keiko Hasegawa, as an individual and as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso, and Philip Sentoso, as personal representative of the Estate of Mulyadi T.S. Sentoso to give notice of this ruling.

Motion to Vacate Default and Default Judgment and Order

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this motion.  No opposition papers were filed.

DISCUSSION

On July 25, 2023, plaintiff Equitable Plaza, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed its Complaint for unlawful detainer in this action against defendant Last Management, LLC (“Defendant”).

The clerk entered Defendant’s default on November 21, 2023.  Thereafter, on December 1, 2023, the clerk entered default judgment for possession in favor of Plaintiff and against Defendant as to the premises located at 3435 Wilshire Boulevard, Suite 2170, Los Angeles, California, 90010.  (UD-110, ¶¶ 1, 4.)  On February 26, 2024, Plaintiff filed a request for dismissal of this entire action without prejudice.  (CIV-110, ¶ 1.)  The clerk entered dismissal of this action as requested by Plaintiff on February 27, 2024.  (CIV-100, ¶ 4.)

Plaintiff now moves the court for an order vacating Defendant’s default and the resulting default judgment entered in Plaintiff’s favor and against Defendant.  Plaintiff moves for this relief on the ground that “as of January 16, 2024, Plaintiff and Defendant have entered into a new lease amendment that addresses Defendant’s outstanding balance.”  (Mot., p. 2:1-3; Cha Decl., ¶¶ 3-4.)  

“When a court has jurisdiction over the parties and subject matter of a suit, its jurisdiction continues until a final judgment is entered.  [Citation.]  When there is a voluntary dismissal of an entire action, the court’s jurisdiction over the parties and the subject matter terminates.”  (Wackeen v. Malis (2002) 97 Cal.App.4th 249, 437 [internal citation omitted].)  Plaintiff has not cited authority establishing that (1) the court has jurisdiction over this action following Plaintiff’s dismissal of the action without prejudice, or (2) the court may vacate the default and default judgment entered in its favor and against Defendant. 

The court therefore denies Plaintiff’s motion.

ORDER

            The court denies plaintiff Equitable Plaza, LLC’s motion to vacate default and default judgment and order.

            The court directs the clerk to give notice of this order.   

IT IS SO ORDERED.
