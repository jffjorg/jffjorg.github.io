DEPARTMENT 56 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

DEPARTMENT 56 JUDGE HOLLY J. FUJIE, LAW AND MOTION RULINGS. The court makes every effort to post tentative rulings by 5.00 pm of the court day before the hearing. The tentative ruling will not become the final ruling until the hearing [see CRC 3.1308(a)(2)], and are also available in the courtroom on the day of the hearing [see CRC 3.1308(b)]. If the parties wish to submit on the tentative ruling and avoid a court appearance, all counsel must agree and choose which counsel will give notice. That counsel must 1) call Dept 56 by 8:30 a.m. on the day of the hearing (213/633-0656) and state that all parties will submit on the tentative ruling, and 2) serve notice of the ruling on all parties. If any party declines to submit on the tentative ruling, then no telephone call is necessary and all parties should appear at the hearing in person or by Court Call. Court reporters are not provided, and parties who want a record of motions and other proceedings must hire a privately retained certified court reporter.

Case Number: 21STCV27498    Hearing Date: October 11, 2023    Dept: 56

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

FOR THE COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES - CENTRAL DISTRICT

 

LAVI BEN HAMO,

 

                        Plaintiff,

            vs.

 

91 ENTERPRISE, LLC, et al.,

 

                        Defendants.

 

      CASE NO.:  21STCV27498

 

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE: MOTION TO COMPEL RESPONSES

 

Date: October 11, 2023

Time: 8:30 a.m.

Dept. 56

Judge: Holly J. Fujie

 

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendant 91 Enterprise, LLC (“Moving Defendant”)

 

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff

 

The Court has considered the moving, opposition and reply papers.

 

BACKGROUND

            This action arises out of an employment relationship.  The currently operative first amended complaint (the “FAC”) alleges: (1) failure to pay minimum wage; (2) failure to pay overtime compensation; (3) failure to pay wages for missed rest periods; (4) failure to pay wages when due; (5) failure to provide accurate and itemized wage statements; and (6) violation of the Unfair Competition Law. 

 

 

            On September 14, 2023, Moving Defendant filed a motion to compel Plaintiff’s responses to Requests for Production, Set One (“RFPs”) (the “Motion”).  The Motion also requests monetary sanctions against Plaintiff and counsel for Plaintiff’s failure to submit timely responses to the RFPs. 

             

DISCUSSION

Under California Code of Civil Procedure (“CCP”) section 2031.300, where there has been no timely response to a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling, the demanding party may seek an order compelling a response.  (CCP § 2031.300, subd. (b).)  Failure to timely respond waives all objections, including privilege and work product.  (CCP § 2031.300, subd. (a).)  The moving party is not required to meet and confer before filing a motion to compel responses to requests for production, a motion to compel responses to interrogatories, or a motion to deem requests for admission admitted.  (Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 411.)

 

In support of the Motion, Moving Defendant provides evidence that it propounded the RFPs on July 31, 2023.  (Declaration of Benjamin Davidson (“Davidson Decl.”) ¶ 3, Exhibit 1.)  On the day the responses were due, September 1, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel requested that the deadline be extended through September 6, 2023, which Moving Defendant confirmed.  (See Davidson Decl. ¶¶ 5-8, Exhibit 2.)  On September 6, 2023, Moving Defendant confirmed Plaintiff’s counsel’s request to further extend the deadline to submit responses until September 8, 2023 due to illness.  (See Davidson Decl. ¶ 9, Exhibit 2.)  At the end of the business day on September 8, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel sent an email stating that she tested positive for the COVID-19 virus and requested an extension through September 12, 2023.  (See Davidson Decl., Exhibit 3.)  Upon learning of the third extension request on September 11, 2023, Moving Defendant’s counsel responded that a third extension would not be granted.  (See Davidson Decl. ¶¶ 11-13, Exhibit 4.)  Although on September 11, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel served Moving Defendant with Plaintiff’s objections to his notice of deposition, Moving Defendant’s counsel did not receive a response to the September 11, 2023 email or receive RFP responses by the requested September 12, 2023 deadline.  (See Davidson Decl. ¶ 14.) 

 

In support of the opposition (the “Opposition”), Plaintiff provides evidence that responses to the RFPs were provided on September 21, 2023.  (Declaration of Charmaine L. Huntting ("Huntting Decl.") ¶ 9, Exhibit 3.)  Plaintiff’s counsel visited an emergency room to seek medical treatment for her illness on September 10, 2023,and was too sick to perform work on September 12 through September 13, 2023, during which time the law firm’s other attorney was appearing in an arbitration.  (Huntting Decl. ¶¶ 4, 6.)  The deposition objections that Plaintiff’s counsel served on September 11, 2023, were prepared the previous week.  (Huntting Decl. ¶ 5.) 

 

As Defendants have provided responses, the Motions are MOOT insofar as they seek to compel responses.

 

As it is undisputed that Plaintiff served RFA responses by the time of the hearing, the RFA Motion is MOOT.  As the responses were submitted past the deadline, Plaintiff has waived any objections.  The proper procedure for Plaintiff to seek relief from this waiver should she seek such relief is by motion.  (See CCP § 2033.280.) 

 

Monetary Sanctions

Moving Defendant requests $1,960 in monetary sanctions in connection with the Motion.  This amount represents: (1) two hours preparing the moving papers; (2) an anticipated one hour reviewing and responding to the Opposition; (3) an anticipated one hour appearing at the hearing at an hourly rate of $475 per hour; and (4) a $60 filing fee.  (Davidson Decl. ¶¶ 7-8.)

 

The Court exercises its discretion and does not award sanctions.  In light of the illness of counsel, it would not be equitable to assess sanctions under these circumstances.
