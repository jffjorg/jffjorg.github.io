DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

If you desire to submit on the tentative ruling, you may do so by e-mailing Dept. 26 at the Spring Street Courthouse until the morning of the motion hearing.

The e-mail address is SSCdept26@lacourt.org

The heading on your e-mail should contain the case name, number, hearing date, and that you submit. The message should indicate your name, contact information, and the party you represent. Please note, the above e-mail address is to inform the court of your submission on the tentative ruling. All other inquiries will not receive a response.

If there are no appearances by either side and no submission on the Court's tentative ruling, the matter will be placed OFF CALENDAR. 

Due to overcrowding concerns of COVID-19, all parties shall make every effort to schedule a remote appearance via LACourtConnect (https://my.lacourt.org/laccwelcome) for their next hearing. The parties shall register with LACourtConnect at least 2 hours prior to their scheduled hearing time.

Case Number: 23STCV24569    Hearing Date: January 15, 2025    Dept: 26

TENTATIVE RULING

Motion of David L. Prince, Esq. to be Relieved as Counsel for Reunion Apparel, Inc. and Babak Akhavan is GRANTED. A PROPOSED ORDER HAS BEEN FILED. RULING TO TAKE EFFECT UPON FILING OF PROOF OF SERVICE OF SIGNED ORDER ON ALL PARTIES.

ORDER TO SHOW CAUSE REGARDING LEGAL REPRESENTATION OF DEFENDANT REUNION APPAREL, INC. IS SET FOR FEBRUARY 18, 2025 AT 9:30 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE.

Case Number: 23STLC00815    Hearing Date: January 15, 2025    Dept: 26

USAA Casualty Ins. Co. v. Woods, et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Based on the foregoing, Plaintiff USAA Casualty Insurance Company’s Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories and Request for Production of Documents Sets One; and Request for Order Awarding Monetary Sanctions is CONTINUED TO FEBRUARY 4, 2025 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY JANUARY 21, 2025, PLAINTIFF IS TO MAKE AN ADDITIONAL MOTION RESERVATION AND PAY THE REQUIRED FILING FEE.

ANALYSIS:

On December 13, 2024, Plaintiff USAA Casualty Insurance Company (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories and Request for Production of Documents Sets One; and Request for Order Awarding Monetary Sanctions. Specifically, Plaintiff requests an order compelling Defendant Jesus Mora (“Defendant Mora”) to provide initial responses to Form Interrogatories, Set No. One, and Request for Production of Documents, Set No. One. Plaintiff also requests monetary sanctions in the amount of $1,060.00. No opposition has been filed to date.

Discussion

Plaintiff’s motion is two motions improperly combined into one: (1) a Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One; and (2) a Motion to Compel Responses to Request for Production, Set One. Combining multiple motions under the guise of one motion with one hearing reservation manipulates the Court Reservation System and unfairly jumps ahead of other litigants. Moreover, combining motions to avoid payment of filing fees deprives the Court of filing fees it is otherwise entitled to collect. Statutorily required filing fees are jurisdictional and “it is mandatory for the court clerks to demand and receive statutorily required filing fees.” (See Duran v. St. Luke’s Hospital (2003) 114 Cal.App.4th 457, 460.) Plaintiff’s motion, therefore, cannot be heard in full until an additional motion reservation is made and the corresponding filing fee is paid.

Conclusion

Based on the foregoing, Plaintiff USAA Casualty Insurance Company’s Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories and Request for Production of Documents Sets One; and Request for Order Awarding Monetary Sanctions is CONTINUED TO FEBRUARY 4, 2025 AT 10:00 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE. BY JANUARY 21, 2025, PLAINTIFF IS TO MAKE AN ADDITIONAL MOTION RESERVATION AND PAY THE REQUIRED FILING FEE.

Dennis Block & Associates, APC v. Lee et al., 

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendant Charles Lee’s Motion to Vacate Default Judgment is GRANTED. THE ORDER ENTERED ON OCTOBER 17, 2024 IS VACATED. TRIAL IS RE-SET FOR APRIL 16, 2025 AT 8:30 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE.

ANALYSIS:

On April 20, 2023, Plaintiff Dennis Block & Associates, APC (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against Defendant Charles Lee (“Defendant”). Defendant filed an answer in propria persona on November 30, 2023. The case came for trial on October 17, 2024, at which time Defendant did not appear. (Minute Order, 10/17/24.) Plaintiff proved up its case and the Court found in its favor in the amount of $5,470.00, plus interest, and costs. (Ibid.) Plaintiff filed a proposed judgment, as ordered, on October 18, 2024.

On November 19, 2024, Defendant filed the instant Motion to Vacate Default Judgment. No opposition has been filed to date.

Discussion

The Motion is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b). An application for discretionary relief pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 473, subdivision (b) must be made within a reasonable time, no more than six months after entry of the order from which relief is sought, and must be accompanied by an affidavit of fault attesting to the party’s excusable mistake, inadvertence, surprise or neglect. (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b); English v. IKON Business Solutions (2001) 94 Cal.App.4th 130, 143.) The motion must also be accompanied by a copy of the moving defendant’s proposed pleading. (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b).) This can be corrected if the defendant submits a proposed responsive pleading by the hearing date. (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (b); Carmel, Ltd. v. Tavoussi (2009) 175 Cal.App.4th 393, 403.)

The Motion was timely brought one month after the trial date and is accompanied by a declaration of fault from Defendant. Defendant declares that they did not know trial was set for October 17, 2024. (Motion, Lee Decl., ¶5.) Although the trial date was provided with the packet served with the Summons and Complaint, Defendant did not realize it was included in the thick pile of papers. (Ibid.) Also, at the time Defendant filed their answer, the clerk at the filing window said a notice of trial or notice of next procedure would be sent out. (Id. at ¶7.) No further notice was sent to Defendant alerting them of the upcoming trial date. (Ibid.) Accordingly, Defendant has demonstrated that their failure to appear for trial was the result of excusable mistake, inadvertence, surprise or neglect and relief from the judgment is warranted under the moving statute. The Court notes that although the motion is for relief from “default judgment,” there is no default in this case. This has no effect on the legal authority for relief.

Conclusion

Defendant Charles Lee’s Motion to Vacate Default Judgment is GRANTED. THE ORDER ENTERED ON OCTOBER 17, 2024 IS VACATED. TRIAL IS RE-SET FOR APRIL 16, 2025 AT 8:30 AM IN DEPARTMENT 26 IN THE SPRING STREET COURTHOUSE.

Carrillo v. Becerra, et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Petitioner Fabian Carrillo’s Petition to Confirm Arbitration Award is DISMISSED.

ANALYSIS:

On March 17, 2024, Petitioner Fabian Carrillo (“Petitioner”) filed the instant Petition to Confirm Arbitration Award (the “Petition”) against Laura Becerra (“Respondent”). The Petition initially came for hearing on July 31, 2024, at which time the Court ruled that it did not meet the necessary procedural or substantive statutory requirements. (Minute Order, 07/31/24.) These requirements were detailed in the Court’s order and the hearing was continued to give Petitioner an opportunity to correct the deficiencies. (Ibid.) On September 26, 2024, Petitioner filed a document entitled “Evidence.”

At the second hearing on October 30, 2024, the Court noted that still no proof of service of the Petition and Notice of Hearing has been filed as required by Code of Civil Procedure section 1290.4. (Minute Order, 10/30/24, p. 1.) It also pointed out that the arbitration award resolves a dispute between Laura Becerra and “Fabian Carrillo Pools.” (Id. at p. 3) As it is unclear if “Fabian Carrillo Pools” is a dba of Petitioner, and it is unclear whether Petitioner is a named party to the arbitration, the Petition could not be granted solely on behalf of “Fabian Carrillo.” (Ibid.) Following oral argument by Petitioner, the Court continued the hearing again. (Ibid.)

To date, no proof of service to the Petition, nor amended Petition, has been filed.

Legal Standard

“Any party to an arbitration in which an award has been made may petition the court to confirm, correct or vacate the award. The petition shall name as respondent all parties to the arbitration and may name as respondents any other persons bound by the arbitration award.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1285.) “If a petition or response under this chapter is duly served and filed, the court shall confirm the award as made, whether rendered in this state or another state, unless in accordance with this chapter it corrects the award and confirms it as corrected, vacates the award or dismisses the proceeding.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1286.)

A response to a Petition to Confirm Arbitration Award that seeks to vacate or correct the award must be served and filed no later than 100 days after the date of the service of a signed copy of the award on the respondent. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1288.2.)

Discussion

Code of Civil Procedure, section 1290.4 requires that the Petition and Notice of Hearing be served on Respondents “in the manner provided in the arbitration agreement for the service of such petition and notice” or “[i]f the arbitration agreement does not provide the manner in which such service shall be made . . . [s]ervice within this State shall be made in the manner provided by law for the service of summons in an action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1290.4, subds. (a), (b).)

It remains that no proof of service of the Petition and Notice of Hearing has been filed as required by Code of Civil Procedure section 1290.4. The arbitration agreement is submitted with the “Evidence,” which demonstrates that it does not provide for the manner of service of the Petition and Notice of Hearing. The Petition must therefore be served in the manner provided for service of a summons. Without a proof of service, the Court cannot find that the requirements of Code of Civil Procedure section 1290.4 are satisfied.

Service of the Arbitration Award and Timing of Service of Petition (CCP §§ 1283.6, 1288, 1288.4)

Code of Civil Procedure section 1283.6 requires that “[t]he neutral arbitrator shall serve a signed copy of the award on each party to the arbitration personally or by registered or certified mail or as provided in the agreement.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1283.6.) This requirement may be satisfied by service by the arbitrator, or upon proper service of the Award with the Petition. (See Murry v. Civil Service Employees Ins. Co. (1967) 254 Cal.App.2d 796, 799-800.) The arbitration award was served on the parties by certified mail on February 7, 2023, meeting the requirements of Code of Civil Procedure section 1283.6. (Evidence, p. 3.)

Also, a party seeking a court judgment confirming an arbitration award must file and serve the petition no more than four years, but not less than 10 days, after the award is served. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 1288, 1288.4.) As there is no proof of service of the Petition, Petitioner also has not demonstrated compliance with Code of Civil Procedure sections 1288 and 1288.4.

Confirmation of the Arbitration Award

An arbitration award is not directly enforceable until it is confirmed by a court and judgment is entered. (Code Civ. Proc. § 1287.6; Jones v. Kvistad (1971) 19 Cal.App.3d 836, 840.) The court must confirm the award as made, unless it corrects or vacates the award, or dismisses the proceeding. (CCP § 1286; Valsan Partners Limited Partnership v. Calcor Space Facility, Inc. (1994) 25 Cal.App.4th 809, 818.) Code of Civil Procedure, section 1285.4 states a petition under this chapter shall:

a) Set forth the substance of or have attached a copy of the agreement to arbitrate unless the petitioner denies the existence of such an agreement.

b) Set forth the names of the arbitrators.

c) Set forth or have attached a copy of the award and the written opinion of the arbitrators, if any.

(Code Civ. Proc., § 1285.4.) Also, as previously noted, the Petition demonstrates that the arbitration award resolves a dispute between Laura Becerra and “Fabian Carrillo Pools.” (Id. at p. 3.) The Petition cannot be granted solely on behalf of “Fabian Carrillo.”

Conclusion

Therefore, Petitioner Fabian Carrillo’s Petition to Confirm Arbitration Award is DISMISSED.

Case Number: 24STLC00642    Hearing Date: January 15, 2025    Dept: 26

Davidson v. Albertsons Companies, Inc. et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendant Albertsons Companies, Inc. dba Pavilions’ Demurrer to the Complaint is OVERRULED. DEFENDANT IS TO FILE AND SERVE AN ANSWER TO THE COMPLAINT WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER.

ANALYSIS:

On January 29, 2024, Plaintiff Lisa Davidson (“Plaintiff”) filed the Complaint in this action against Defendant Albertsons Companies, Inc. dba Pavilions (“Defendant”). On December 13, 2024, Defendant filed the instant Demurrer to the Complaint. Plaintiff filed an opposition on January 2, 2025 and Defendant replied on January 7, 2025.

Discussion

Defendant’s purported demurrer is an improper combination of the demurrer and venue statutes. The Motion first cites Code of Civil Procedure section 430.30 for the proposition that an objection to a complaint may be taken by demurrer when the grounds for the objection appear on the face of the complaint or on any matter of which the court may take judicial notice. (Citing Code Civ. Proc., § 430.30, subd. (a).) Defendant goes on to argue that the Court lacks jurisdiction over this action, which is a basis for demurrer under Code of Civil Procedure section 430.10, subdivision (a). However, only general demurrers are permitted in the limited jurisdiction court. (Code Civ. Proc., § 92, subd. (c).) A demurrer for lack of subject matter jurisdiction is a special demurrer, which the Court cannot consider. (See Workmen's Comp. Appeals Bd. v. Small Claims Court (1973) 35 Cal.App.3d 643, 645 n. 3; Middlecoff v. Superior Court of Los Angeles County (1934) 220 Cal. 410, 414.)

The “demurrer” is alternatively brought as a motion to dismiss under Code of Civil Procedure section 392 on the grounds that it concerns property located in Ventura County, and therefore, must be heard in the Ventura County Superior Court. The address of the property that is the subject of this action is 1135 Lindero Canyon Road, Thousand Oaks, California 91362. (Compl., ¶2.) Defendant contends that the subject property is in Ventura County but offers no admissible evidence of this fact. Also, Defendant does not demonstrate either by reference to the Complaint, nor to matters of which the Court may take judicial notice, that the property in this action is located in Ventura County. Plaintiff disputes that the address is in Ventura County and contends it is in Los Angeles County by providing a copy of the Court’s online filing locator. (Opp., Exh. 1.) The Court’s filing locator indicates that the relevant zip code is within both Los Angeles and Ventura Counties and directs parties to file limited civil cases in the Stanley Mosk Courthouse. (Ibid.)

Even if the Court were to find that the subject property is in Ventura County, Plaintiff has not shown that venue is proper under Code of Civil Procedure section 392, which states in relevant part:

(a) Subject to the power of the court to transfer actions and proceedings as provided in this title, the superior court in the county where the real property that is the subject of the action, or some part thereof, is situated, is the proper court for the trial of the following actions:

  (1) For the recovery of real property, or of an estate or interest therein, or for the determination in any form, of that right    or interest, and for injuries to real property.
  (2) For the foreclosure of all liens and mortgages on real property.

(Code Civ. Proc., § 392, subd. (a).) Defendant overreaches by arguing that the statute expressly states any action that involves real property must be filed in the county where the real property is located. This action, however, does not concern the recovery of real property, or of an estate or interest in said property, for injuries to the real property, or for the foreclosure of all liens and mortgages on the property. Instead, it is for an action pertaining injuries that occurred on the subject property. Defendant offers no authority that an action for injuries sustained on real property is subject to this venue provision. The motion to dismiss, erroneously brought as a demurrer, therefore, is without merit.

Conclusion

Defendant Albertsons Companies, Inc. dba Pavilions’ Demurrer to the Complaint is OVERRULED. DEFENDANT IS TO FILE AND SERVE AN ANSWER TO THE COMPLAINT WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER.

Chapman et al., v. IWorld of Travel, Ltd., et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendant IWorld of Travel Limited’s Motion to Dismiss or Stay Action is DENIED. DEFENDANT IS TO FILE AND SERVE ITS RESPONSE PLEADING WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER.

ANALYSIS:

On June 24, 2024, Plaintiffs Howard Chapman and Kathryn Chapman (“Plaintiffs”) filed the Complaint in this action against Defendants IWorld of Travel Ltd. (“Defendant”) and American Express National Bank, which was subsequently dismissed.

On November 15, 2024, Defendant filed the instant Motion to Dismiss or Stay Action. Plaintiffs filed an opposition on December 23, 2024 and Defendant replied on January 8, 2025.

Discussion

Defendant’s Motion to Dismiss or Stay Action is brought pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 410.30, which states in relevant part: “When a court upon motion of a party or its own motion finds that in the interest of substantial justice an action should be heard in a forum outside this state, the court shall stay or dismiss the action in whole or in part on any conditions that may be just.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 410.30, subd. (a).) This statute codifies the doctrine of forum non conveniens, which “authorizes a court to decline to exercise its jurisdiction in appropriate instances on the ground that the plaintiff has unfairly or unreasonably invoked the jurisdiction of an inconvenient forum.” (Furda v. Superior Court (1984) 161 Cal.App.3d 418, 425, n. 3.) The doctrine of forum non conveniens is an appropriate means of enforcing a forum selection clause. (Ibid.)

Defendant presents the following evidence that the parties entered in a forum selection clause in which New York is the exclusive jurisdiction concerning disputes regarding the parties’ agreement. Plaintiff purchased a luxury vacation package online through Defendant’s website. (Motion, Gelber Decl., ¶6 and Exh. B.) When a customer pays online, they are taken to a webpage to fill in the necessary information to process the payment. (Id. at ¶7.) During this process, the customer is presented with additional acknowledgments, as well as the Terms and Conditions that govern Defendant’s relationship with its customers. (Id. at ¶8.) Plaintiffs acknowledged the Terms and Conditions, which provide that the exclusive venue for disputes over the trip is the Superior Court of New York, New York County (Manhattan). (Id. at Exh. C.)

Plaintiffs’ opposition, however, disputes that they acknowledged the Terms and Conditions attached to the Gelber declaration. Plaintiffs contend they booked the trip through their travel agent, Paula Fortner, who made the arrangements over telephone and email in November 2022. (Motion, Fortner Decl., ¶2; Chapman Decl., ¶¶2-3.) Fortner did not book Plaintiff’s trip through Defendant’s website and was not provided with any terms and conditions at the time of booking. (Fortner Decl., ¶2.) On November 18, 2022, Defendant emailed Fortner Plaintiffs’ itinerary, which Fortner then forwarded to Plaintiffs. (Id. at ¶3 and Exh. 1; Chapman Decl., ¶2 and Exh. 1.) Plaintiffs revised the itinerary in May 2023 with Fortner’s assistance and were provided with a revised itinerary that included the same terms and conditions as previously provided. (Motion, Chapman Decl., ¶3 and Exh. 2.) The terms and conditions provided to Fortner and Plaintiffs in November 2022 and May 2023 did not include a forum selection clause. (Motion, Chapman Decl., Exhs. 1, 3; Fortner Decl., Exh. 1.) Also, when Plaintiffs first initiated this disputed in October 2023 and made a charge back request of American Express, Defendant challenged the charge back request with a copy of the same terms and conditions, which did not include a forum selection clause. (Id. at ¶4 and Exh. 3.) Upon receipt of the declaration in support of Defendant’s Motion, Plaintiff Howard Chapman went to Defendant’s website and reviewed the terms and conditions posted therein. (Id. at ¶8.) Those terms and conditions included the forum selection clause but indicated a “time of publishing” as “12/6/2023.” (Id. at ¶8 and Exh. 5.)

In reply, Defendant now offers a different explanation of the terms and conditions to which it contends Plaintiffs agreed. Defendant’s officer now declares that Plaintiffs agreed to two different terms and conditions. (Reply, Gelber Decl., ¶¶5-8.) The first was upon payment of the initial deposit and the second was upon full payment. (Ibid.) The second terms and conditions included the forum selection and attorney’s fees clauses. (Id. at ¶¶7-8 and Exh. B.) The second terms and conditions document is not publicly available and is only accessible by password by customers who have paid for the full package. (Ibid.)

The Court does not find Defendant’s evidence demonstrates Plaintiffs agreed to the terms and conditions that contained the forum selection clause. Defendant’s first supporting declaration lacks credibility in light of the new and different facts raised in the second Gelber declaration. Gelber initially incorrectly declared that Plaintiffs booked their trip through the website and made no mention of two different terms and conditions documents. Furthermore, Defendant’s evidence speaks in general terms of what Plaintiffs would have agreed to upon making the initial deposit and full payment but does not provide evidence of any specific communications with Plaintiffs or their travel agent, nor screen shots of the password protected landing page that purportedly gave Plaintiffs access to the second terms and conditions.

Therefore, Defendant has not demonstrated that this action should be dismissed or stayed pursuant to a contractually agreed upon forum selection clause.

Conclusion

Defendant IWorld of Travel Limited’s Motion to Dismiss or Stay Action is DENIED. DEFENDANT IS TO FILE AND SERVE ITS RESPONSE PLEADING WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER.

Case Number: 24STLC06641    Hearing Date: January 15, 2025    Dept: 26

Eber v. Saban Clinic, et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendant The Los Angeles Free Clinic dba Saban Community Clinic’s Demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.

ANALYSIS:

On September 18, 2024, Plaintiff Tara Eber (“Plaintiff”), in propria persona, filed the Complaint in this action against Defendant Los Angeles Free Clinic dba Saban Community Clinic (“Defendant”) (erroneously sued as Saban Clinic).

On December 11, 2024, Defendant filed the instant Demurrer to the Complaint. No opposition has been filed to date.

Discussion

The Demurrer is accompanied by a meet and confer declaration as required by Code of Civil Procedure section 430.41. (Demurrer, Lowell Decl., ¶2 and Exh. A.) Defendant demurs on the grounds that the Complaint fails to allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action. (Citing Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (e).) The Complaint indicates that it raises a cause of action for premises liability but attaches no facts in support of such a claim, as required. (Compl., ¶10(e).) Some words are handwritten on the second page of the Complaint but none form a coherent sentence. (Id. at p. 2.) Nor has Plaintiff filed any opposition to the instant Demurrer demonstrating that facts can be alleged to articulate a cause of action against Defendant. Therefore, the demurrer is sustained without leave to amend.

Conclusion

Defendant The Los Angeles Free Clinic dba Saban Community Clinic’s Demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITHOUT LEAVE TO AMEND.
