DEPARTMENT 34 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

The Court often posts its tentative several days in advance of the hearing. Please re-check the tentative rulings the day before the hearing to be sure that the Court has not revised the ruling since the time it was posted.

Please call the clerk at (213) 633-0154 by 4:00 pm. the court day before the hearing if you wish to submit on the tentative.

Case Number: 19STCV41520    Hearing Date: January 5, 2024    Dept: 34

SUBJECT:        Motion for Reconsideration of Court’s Order of November 28, 2023

 

Moving Party: Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC

Resp. Party:    Judgment Creditor Westlake Village Property, L.P.

 

SUBJECT:        Motion to Quash Service of Order for Appearance of Non-Party, Dr. Leevil SPE LLC

 

Moving Party: Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC

Resp. Party:    Judgment Creditor Westlake Village Property, L.P.

 

SUBJECT:        Objection to Proposed Amended Judgment

 

Moving Party: Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC

Resp. Party:    None

 

SUBJECT:        Objection to Proposed Judgment, Request for a Hearing if the Objection is not Sustained

 

Moving Party: Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC

Resp. Party:    None

 

 

The Motion for Reconsideration is DENIED.

 

The Motion to Quash is GRANTED. The Order for Appearance and Examination on Michael Schlesinger is QUASHED.

 

Dr. Leevil SPE LLC’s Objection and Dr. Leevil, LLC’s Objection are OVERRULED.

 

The Order for Appearance and Examination on Ronald Richards remains, and Mr. Richards shall appear on January 5, 2024 for examination.

 

BACKGROUND:

 

        On February 15, 2019, Plaintiff filed its Complaint against Defendants Dr. Leevil, LLC and D-Day Capital, LLC on causes of action for breach of written contract, accounting, and money had and received.

 

        On November 19, 2019, this case was transferred from the Superior Court of California, County of Ventura to the Superior Court of California, County of Los Angeles.

 

        On September 29, 2021, pursuant to an oral request made by Plaintiff, the Court dismissed with prejudice Plaintiff’s Complaint. The Court retained jurisdiction to make orders to enforce any and all terms of the settlement, including judgment, pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6.

 

        On January 2, 2023, the Court entered Judgment in this matter in favor of Plaintiff (now “Judgment Creditor”) and against Defendants (now “Judgment Debtors”).

 

        On October 16, 2023, the Court denied without prejudice Judgment Creditor’s Motion to Amend Judgment to Add Alter Ego Defendant as Judgment Debtor, and Identify Alter Ego (“First Motion to Amend”).

 

        On October 25, 2023, by separate applications, the Court ordered Ronald Richards and Michael Schlesinger to appear on November 28, 2023 for examination.

 

        On November 20, 2023, then-Non-Party Dr. Leevil SPE LLC filed its Motion to Quash Service of Order for Appearance of Non-Party, Dr. Leevil SPE LLC (“Motion to Quash”).

 

        On November 27, 2023, the Court continued the examination of Michael Schlesinger from November 28, 2023 to January 5, 2024.

 

        On November 27, 2023, then-Non-Party Dr. Leevil SPE LLC filed its Notice of Errata regarding the Motion to Quash.

 

        On November 28, 2023, the Court granted in part Judgment Creditor’s Motion to Amend Judgment to Add Alter Ego Defendant as Judgment Debtor, and Identify Alter Ego (“Second Motion to Amend”). The Court granted Judgment Creditor’s request that the Court amend the Judgment to add Dr. Leevil SPE LLC to the Judgment as an alter ego of Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC.

 

        On November 28, 2023, the Court continued the examination of Ronald Richards from November 28, 2023 to January 5, 2024.

 

        On November 29, 2023, Judgment Creditor filed its proposed Amended Judgment.

 

        On November 30, 2023, Judgment Creditor filed two copies of Judicial Council Form AT-138/EJ-125, Application for Order for Appearance and Examination for Enforcement of Judgment Against Third Person, one for Ronald Richards and one for Michael Schlesinger.

 

        On December 1, 2023, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC filed its Objection to Proposed Amended Judgment (“Dr. Leevil SPE LLC’s Objection”).

 

        On December 4, 2023, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC filed its Objection to Proposed Judgment, Request for a Hearing if the Objection is not Sustained (“Dr. Leevil, LLC’s Objection”).

 

        On December 6, 2023, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC filed its Motion for Reconsideration of Court’s Order of November 28, 2023 (“Motion for Reconsideration”).

 

        On December 11, 2023, the Court issued a minute order that stated, among other things, that the Court will consider objections prior to the Amended Judgment being signed and set a hearing if necessary.

 

        On December 21, 2023, Judgment Creditor filed: (1) Opposition to Motion to Quash; and (2) Opposition to Motion for Reconsideration. Judgment Creditor concurrently filed: (1) Declaration of James Passmore in support of Opposition to Motion to Quash; and (2) Declaration of Matthew W. Rosene in support of Opposition to Motion for Reconsideration.

 

        On December 26, 2023, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC filed: (1) Reply regarding Motion to Quash; and (2) Reply regarding Motion for Reconsideration. 

 

ANALYSIS:

 

I.          Motion for Reconsideration

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

“When an application for an order has been made to a judge, or to a court, and refused in whole or in part, or granted, or granted conditionally, or on terms, any party affected by the order may, within 10 days after service upon the party of written notice of entry of the order and based upon new or different facts, circumstances, or law, make application to the same judge or court that made the order, to reconsider the matter and modify, amend, or revoke the prior order. The party making the application shall state by affidavit what application was made before, when and to what judge, what order or decisions were made, and what new or different facts, circumstances, or law are claimed to be shown.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1008, subd. (a).)

 

“Courts have construed section 1008 to require a party filing an application for reconsideration or a renewed application to show diligence with a satisfactory explanation for not having presented the new or different information earlier.” (Even Zohar Constr. & Remodeling, Inc. v. Bellaire Townhouses, LLC (2015) 61 Cal.4th 830, 839, citations omitted; see also Cal. Corr. Peace Officers Ass’n v. Virga (2010) 181 Cal.App.4th 30, 46, fn. 15.)

 

B.      The Parties’ Arguments

 

On November 28, 2023, the Court determined:

 

“that Non-Party Dr. Leevil SPE LLC and Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC have such a unity of interest and ownership that the separate personalities of the entities and their owners no longer exist. Accordingly, as Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC had control of the underlying litigation and was represented in the proceeding, Non-Party Dr. Leevil SPE LLC also had such control and representation. An inequitable result will follow if Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC’s acts are treated as those of that entity alone.”

 

(Minute Order dated November 28, 2023, p. 13.)

 

Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC moves the Court to reconsider this determination. (Motion for Reconsideration, pp. 6:21–28, 7:1–2.)

 

Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC argues: (1) that any party affected by an order may, within ten days, make an application to the same judge to reconsider the matter; (2) that the payment to Judgment Creditor of the full amount of the Judgment defeats any claim of an inequitable result if Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC is not found to be the “alter ego” of Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC; and (3) since the Judgment has been paid in full, it would be an idle act for the Court to grant the Second Motion to Amend. (Motion for Reconsideration, pp. 3:20–21, 4:17–19, 6:2–4.)

 

Judgment Creditor disagrees, arguing: (1) that the Judgment has not been satisfied; (2) that Judgment Debtors cannot deprive Judgment Creditor of its opportunity to recover attorneys’ fees and costs; (3) that the alter ego factors are still present, and it would be inequitable to consider amending the Judgment as an “idle act”; and (4) that the Motion for Reconsideration was premature because it was filed before the notice of ruling was served or filed. (Opposition to Motion for Reconsideration, pp. 2:1–3, 4:24–25, 5:5–6.)

 

In its Reply, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC argues: (1) that the payment of the outstanding Judgment is a new fact that warrants reconsideration of the matter; (2) that it will not be inequitable if Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC is not the alter ego of Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC, regardless of whether Judgment Creditor is entitled to recover attorney’s fees and costs; and (3) that the Motion for Reconsideration should not be denied just because it was filed on December 6, 2023. (Reply regarding Motion for Reconsideration, pp. 4:6–7, 4:16–18, 5:18–19.)

 

C.      Discussion

 

Judgment Creditor has the better arguments.

 

First, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC claims that there is a “new fact” that warrants reconsideration — that the existing Judgment was satisfied on November 30, 2023. (Motion for Reconsideration, p. 3:10–11.) This alleged satisfaction of the Judgment purportedly occurred two days after the Court made a determination regarding whether Dr. Leevil SPE LLC is an alter ego of Dr. Leevil, LLC. (Minute Order dated November 28, 2023, p. 13.)

 

But Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC has not shown diligence with a satisfactory explanation for not having presented the new information earlier. Nowhere in its Memorandum of Points and Authorities, Declaration of Dale E. Motley, or Declaration of Ronald Richards is there any explanation for why the Judgment was not purportedly satisfied prior to the November 28, 2023 ruling on Judgment Creditor’s Second Motion to Amend. Without such an explanation, to the extent that the Judgment was actually satisfied two days after the Court’s ruling, it appears to the Court that the Judgment was only satisfied because of the Court’s ruling, which would make reconsideration entirely inappropriate. It would be inequitable to allow reconsideration to benefit Judgment Debtors when it appears they only satisfied the Judgment after the Court ruled against them.

 

Because Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC has not shown diligence or presented the Court with a satisfactory explanation for why the Judgment was not satisfied prior to its determination on November 28, 2023, the Motion for Reconsideration must be denied for this reason alone. 

 

Second, even if the Court were to reconsider its November 28, 2023 determination, the Court has not been presented with a sufficient reason why a later satisfaction of the Judgment should impact a prior determination that a non-party should be added to the existing Judgment as a judgment debtor. The Court’s determination on the merits of this issue should stand, even if for some reason the Judgment was not subsequently amended.

 

Finally, Judgment Creditor is the prevailing party on the application to enforce settlement agreement. (See Minute Order dated December 5, 2022; see also Judgment dated January 2, 2023.) Thus, Judgment Creditor is entitled to its costs. (Code Civ. Proc., § 1032, subd. (b) [“Except as otherwise expressly provided by statute, a prevailing party is entitled as a matter of right to recover costs in any action or proceeding.”].) Further, although the Court is not deciding at this time the Motion for Award of Attorney’s Fees and Costs (filed on December 18, 2023), the Court notes that the purported settlement agreement submitted in connection with that motion contains a provision for a prevailing party “to recover its attorneys’ fees and costs” in any action to enforce the purported settlement agreement. Because the Judgment may still be amended to reflect Judgment Creditor’s costs and fees, it is not a moot issue or an idle act for the Court to also amend the Judgment to reflect the fact that Dr. Leevil SPE LLC is now a judgment debtor because it is an alter ego of Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil, LLC.

 

D.      Conclusion

 

The Motion for Reconsideration is DENIED.

 

II.       Motion to Quash

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

“The judgment creditor may apply to the proper court for an order requiring the judgment debtor to appear before the court, or before a referee appointed by the court, at a time and place specified in the order, to furnish information to aid in enforcement of the money judgment.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 708.11, subd. (a).)

 

“The judgment creditor shall personally serve a copy of the order on the judgment debtor not less than 30 days before the date set for the examination. Service shall be made in the manner specified in Section 415.10. Service of the order creates a lien on the personal property of the judgment debtor for a period of one year from the date of the order unless extended or sooner terminated by the court.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 708.110, subd. (d).)

 

“Upon ex parte application by a judgment creditor who has a money judgment and proof by the judgment creditor by affidavit or otherwise to the satisfaction of the proper court that a third person has possession or control of property in which the judgment debtor has an interest or is indebted to the judgment debtor in an amount exceeding two hundred fifty dollars ($250), the court shall make an order directing the third person to appear before the court, or before a referee appointed by the court, at a time and place specified in the order, to answer concerning such property or debt. The affidavit in support of the judgment creditor’s application may be based on the affiant’s information and belief.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 708.120, subd. (a).)

 

“Not less than 10 days prior to the date set for the examination, a copy of the order shall be: (1) Served personally on the third person. (2) Served personally or by mail on the judgment debtor.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 708.120, subd. (b).)

 

“An order made pursuant to subdivision (a) is not effective unless, at the time it is served on the third person, the person serving the order tenders to the third person fees for the mileage necessary to be traveled from the third person’s residence to the place of examination. The mileage fees shall be in the same amount generally provided for witnesses when legally required to attend civil proceedings in the court where the examination proceeding is to be conducted.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 708.120, subd. (f).)

 

“If the person ordered to appear denies service of the order for appearance, the court shall conduct a hearing solely to determine if proper service was made. The court may continue the examination to permit any person to present evidence in support or in opposition to the claim of non-service. If the court determines that service was not made, it may dismiss the proceeding without costs awarded to the applicant.” (Super. Ct. of Cal., Cnty. of Los Angeles, Civ. Div. Rules, rule 3.221(f).)

 

B.      The Parties’ Arguments

 

Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC moves the Court to quash the Order for Appearance and Examination of Michael Schlesinger, who was ordered on October 25, 2023 to appear for examination on November 28, 2023. (Motion to Quash, p. 6:9–11.) (The examination of Mr. Schlesinger was continued on November 27, 2023 to January 5, 2024.)

 

        Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC argues that this would be appropriate because: (1) Judgment Creditor did not serve the Order for Appearance and Examination on Mr. Schlesinger; and (2) any service would be ineffective because Judgment Creditor did not pay or offer to pay witness fees. (Motion to Quash, pp. 4:6–7, 5:5–6.)

 

        Judgment Creditor disagrees, arguing: (1) that Mr. Schlesinger was served with the Order for Appearance and Examination; and (2) that the Order for Appearance and Examination should be continued to February 28, 2024 “to provide [Judgment Creditor] with the opportunity to serve Mr. Schlesinger.” (Opposition to Motion to Quash, pp. 2:5, 3:3.)

 

        In its Reply, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC argues: (1) that Judgment Creditor admitted there was no payment or offer to pay witness fees; and (2) that the video evidence establishes that Mr. Schlesinger was never served with the Order for Appearance and Examination. (Reply, p. 3:5–6, 3:16–17.)

 

C.      Discussion

 

        For this motion, Judgment Debtor Dr. Leevil SPE LLC has the better arguments.

 

        First, it appears that Judgment Creditor has admitted that Mr. Schlesinger has not yet been served, even though the examination is scheduled to occur in three days. Even without this admission, the evidence submitted does not demonstrate that Mr. Schlesinger has in fact been served.

 

        Second, the Court has not been presented with any evidence that would indicate Mr. Schlesinger was paid or offered to be paid the fees for third party witnesses, much less at the time of service. (Code Civ. Proc., § 708.120, subd. (f).) Failure to do so makes the Order for Appearance and Examination ineffective, as that is a requirement for such an order on third parties. (Ibid.)

 

        Finally, the Court notes that Dr. Leevil SPE LLC has been determined to be a judgment debtor (and thus not a third party) subsequent to the time the Order for Appearance and Examination was signed. Therefore, the Order for Appearance and Examination is ineffective for the further and independent reason that the Application and Order for Appearance and Examination was requested for a third person, not for a judgment debtor.

 

D.      Conclusion

 

The Motion to Quash is GRANTED. The Order for Appearance and Examination on Michael Schlesinger is QUASHED.

 

III.     The Objections and the Remaining Application for Order for Appearance and Examination on Ronald Richards

 

Two remaining items are noteworthy: first, consideration of Dr. Leevil SPE LLC’s Objection and Dr. Leevil, LLC’s Objection; and second, the Order for Appearance and Examination on Ronald Richards.

 

The objections made by Judgment Debtors are identical to the issues considered in the Motion for Reconsideration. For the same reason, Dr. Leevil SPE LLC’s Objection and Dr. Leevil, LLC’s Objection are OVERRULED.

 

As to the second item, the existing Order for Appearance and Examination on Ronald Richards has not been objected to. Thus, Mr. Richards shall appear on January 5, 2024 for examination. Notably, the Application and Order for Appearance and Examination filed on November 30, 2023 regarding Ronald Richards is superfluous since Mr. Richards has already been ordered to appear for examination.
