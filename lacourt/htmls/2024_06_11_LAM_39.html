DEPARTMENT 39 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23STCV19761    Hearing Date: June 11, 2024    Dept: 39

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPT:

39

HEARING DATE:

June 11, 2024

CASE NUMBER:

23STCV19761

MOTION: 

Motion to Compel Discovery Responses

MOVING PARTY:

Plaintiff Ariel Alfaro

OPPOSING PARTY:

None

 

            Plaintiff Ariel Alfaro (“Plaintiff”) moves to compel responses from Defendant Man Staffing, LLC (“Defendant”) to Form Interrogatories—Employment Law, set one.   Defendant has not filed an opposition to the motion.

In a proof of service attached to Plaintiff’s motion, an individual states she had the notice of motion and motion served personally on Defendant’s attorney “through a courier.”  That is hearsay and insufficient proof of service.  Any such proof of personal service requires a declaration from the courier who actually effected the service, not someone who gave the document to the courier for service.  (Weil & Brown, Cal. Practice Guide: Civil Procedure Before Trial (The Rutter Group 2023) ¶ 9.85 et seq., p. 9(1)-55.)  Sufficient proof of service is particularly relevant here when there is no indication Defendant opposes the motion, and the circumstances call into question whether Defendant was served such notice. 

Accordingly, the motion is denied without prejudice.  Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of this order and to file proof of service of same.

 


Case Number: 23STCV24494A    Hearing Date: June 11, 2024    Dept: 39

TENTATIVE RULING

 

DEPT:

39

HEARING DATE:

June 11, 2024

CASE NUMBER:

23STCV24494A

MOTION: 

Motion to Compel Further Discovery Responses

MOVING PARTY:

Plaintiff Saul Gonzalez

OPPOSING PARTY:

Defendant General Motors LLC

 

MOTION

            Plaintiff Saul Gonzalez (“Plaintiff”) moves to compel further responses from Defendant General Motors LLC (“Defendant”) to Requests for Production of Documents, set one (“RPD”), Nos. 1, 3, 9, 17, 37-51, 54, 59-63, and 65.  Defendant opposes the motion.  But in their Joint Statement in keeping with the court’s April 30, 2024 order, the parties inform the court only responses to RPD Nos. 9, 17, and 37-51 remain in dispute.

 

ANALYSIS

If the propounding party deems responses to requests for production of documents unsatisfactory, the propounding party may move to compel further responses.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.310.) 

Here, Plaintiff alleges his 2019 Chevrolet Silverado 1500 vehicle had defects Defendant was unable to repair within a reasonable number of attempts.  Plaintiff, therefore, contends Defendant is liable under the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act.  

Defendant objects to the RPD to the extent Plaintiff seeks documents about vehicles other than Plaintiff’s vehicle.  As Plaintiff timely filed this motion to compel further responses, Defendant has the burden to justify its objections.  (Fairmont Ins. Co. v. Superior Court (2000) 22 Cal.4th 245, 255.)  Pursuant to Santana v. FCA US, LLC (2020) 56 Cal.App.5th 334, Plaintiff is entitled to discovery regarding the alleged defects in other vehicles of the same year, make, and model as Plaintiff’s vehicle.  Likewise, Plaintiff is entitled to evidence of Defendant’s conduct towards other purchasers of defective vehicles, which is pertinent to a finding that the manufacturer willfully violated the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act in failing to adequately repair a vehicle.  (Johnson v. Ford Motor Co. (2005) 35 Cal. 4th 1191, 1204.)  Furthermore, “whether [Defendant] had a written policy on the requirement to repair or replace” under the Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act is relevant to a finding of willfulness.  (Jensen v. BMW of North America, Inc. (1995) 35 Cal.App.4th 112, 136.)  Thus, Plaintiff is entitled to discovery concerning Defendant’s policies and procedures.  

Defendant contends some of the information Plaintiff seeks is irrelevant. “In the context of discovery, evidence is ‘relevant’ if it might reasonably assist a party in evaluating its case, preparing for trial, or facilitating a settlement. Admissibility is not the test, and it is sufficient if the information sought might reasonably lead to other, admissible evidence.”  (Glenfed Development Corp. v. Superior Court (1997) 53 Cal.App.4th 1113, 1117; see also Code Civ. Proc., § 2017.010.)  As set forth above, information about the same year, make, and model as Plaintiff’s vehicle is pertinent to this case.

Defendant claims, given the breadth of the documents Plaintiff seeks, some of the responsive information would constitute trade secrets.  Defendant has not substantiated that objection.  “‘Trade secret’ means information, including a formula, pattern, compilation, program, device, method, technique, or process, that: (1) Derives independent economic value, actual or potential, from not being generally known to the public or to other persons who can obtain economic value from its disclosure or use; and (2) Is the subject of efforts that are reasonable under the circumstances to maintain its secrecy.”  (Civ. Code, § 3426.1.)  First, the court notes the parties in this action entered into a stipulation and protective order which addresses confidential materials.  Second, in support of its contention in this regard, Defendant provides a declaration dated October 25, 2018 that is unrelated to this case.  (See Declaration of Huizhen Lu, attached as Exhibit A to Declaration of Ryan Kay.)  There is no evidence before the Court the averments in that more than five-year-old declaration are accurate. The court, therefore, disregards it.

Therefore, Defendant is ordered to produce responsive documents regarding “service bulletins and/or technical service bulletins” (RPD, No. 9) and “the Warranty Administration Manual” governing “the warranty diagnosis and repairs to the SUBJECT VEHILCE,” given Plaintiff’s statement in the Joint Statement it is willing to compromise concerning this request (RPD, No. 17).  Additionally, as Plaintiff requests, Defendant is ordered to provide code-compliant further responses to requests concerning the production of documents relating to California owners of the same year, make, model as the subject vehicle regarding issues with “an abnormal noise coming from the front end as identified on Repair Order No. 248764 on August 3, 2020, at Delano Chevrolet Buick GMC”; “a loud knocking noise coming from under the hood as identified on Repair Order No. 154813 on June 5, 2023, at Delano Chevrolet Buick GMC”; “the front center seatbelt as identified on Repair Order No. 154813 on June 5, 2023, at Delano Chevrolet Buick GMC”; “hard transmission shifting as identified on repair Order No. 162100 on August 21, 2023, at Delano Chevrolet Buick GMC”; and “the vehicle juddering when accelerating as identified on Repair Order No. 162100 on August 21, 2023, at Delano Chevrolet Buick GMC” (RPD, Nos. 37-51).

The court declines to impose sanctions because, for the most part, Defendant has agreed to produce many of the documents Plaintiff seeks.  (See Opposition to Motion to Compel Further Responses, pp. 4-5.)  Accordingly, an award of sanctions in this instance would be unjust.

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses to the subject RPDs which remain in dispute is granted.  Defendant is ordered to produce responsive documents to, and further responses regarding, those RPDs noted above in keeping with Section D of the Courtroom Information for Department 39 .

Plaintiff is ordered to give notice of this order and file proof of service of same.

Motion to Compel Deposition

MOVING PARTY:

Plaintiff Saul Gonzalez

OPPOSING PARTY:

Defendant General Motors LLC

 

MOTIONS

            Plaintiff Saul Gonzalez (“Plaintiff”) moves to compel Defendant General Motors LLC (“Defendant”) to produce its person most qualified to testify at deposition and to produce documents set forth in its deposition notice.  Defendant opposes the motion.

 

ANALYSIS

If a party to the action fails to appear for deposition after service of a deposition notice, and the party has not served a valid objection to that deposition notice, the party who noticed the deposition may move for an order to compel the deponent to attend and testify at deposition.  (Code Civ. Proc., §2025.450, subd. (a).)  In this action, Plaintiff served Defendant with a notice of deposition.  Defendant has not produced its person most knowledgeable for deposition.  Accordingly, the Court grants the motion to compel Defendant to provide for deposition its person[s] most knowledgeable in response to Plaintiff’s deposition notice. 

In opposition to Plaintiff’s motion, Defendant argues Plaintiff’s deposition notice is overbroad and seeks trade secret material.  If Defendant sought to avoid producing a person most qualified to testify or documents on specified topics, Defendant was required to seek a protective order so “[t]hat certain matters [would] not be inquired into.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.420, subd. (b)(9).)  Defendant did not do so.  Nor is the scope of the requested information an “error or irregularity” in the deposition notice, to which Defendant could object. (See Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.410.)

Defendant also argues Plaintiff failed to adequately attempt to informally resolve this matter.  Plaintiff was not required to do so, as Defendant did not appear for deposition.  Instead, Plaintiff was required to “contact[] [Defendant] to inquire about the nonappearance.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (b)(2).)  Plaintiff did so. 

 

 

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Plaintiff’s motion to compel Defendant to produce its person(s) most qualified to testify on specified topics and to produce documents is granted.  Defendant is to produce its person(s) most qualified for deposition within 30 days of the notice of this order unless Plaintiff stipulates to a different time frame. The scope of the deposition is as set forth in Plaintiff’s November 30, 2023 Notice of Deposition and the Joint Statement submitted by the parties on May 17, 2024, in which they noted the production of requested documents upon which they had agreed, and the court hereby orders Defendant to produce those which remained in dispute.

Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of this order and file proof of service of same.
