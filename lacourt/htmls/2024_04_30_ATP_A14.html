Case Number: 23AVCV00180    Hearing Date: April 30, 2024    Dept: A14

Background

 

This is a breach of contract action. Plaintiff Lorraine M. Schaum (“Plaintiff”) alleges a breach of contract against Defendants Antelope Valley Hospital, the California Business Bureau, Inc. (“CBB”), and Administrative Concepts, Inc. (“ACI”). Plaintiff presents that she had surgery on August 04, 2021 on her right arm for the replacement of a prosthetic radius head and, a few months ago, when running a credit report, she saw that she had a debt of $45,653.85 to CBB for Antelope Valley Hospital. Plaintiff states she then spoke to Antelope Valley Hospital’s billing department and was told that the debt was forwarded to CBB and Antelope Valley Hospital would not discuss the matter with her. Plaintiff alleges she then went to medical records; obtained her medical record; saw that the medical record did not contain notes regarding approval from the insurance to Antelope Valley Hospital representative, Justin; so, she contacted CBB and spoke to Sean who told her that her insurance company refused to cover the bill leaving her in debt in the amount of $57,238.18. Plaintiff contends that Sean informed her that there was a note in the hospital file that Antelope Valley Hospital received verbal approval from ACI for the surgery, but that he could not give her a copy of this note. Plaintiff further alleges that because there is a notation that there was verbal authorization, the bill should have been covered by ACI.

 

On February 13, 2024, Plaintiff filed her Complaint alleging one cause of action for Breach of Contract.

 

On March 20, 2024, Antelope Valley Healthcare District (“AVHD”) filed this Demurrer. The Court believes that AVHD is responding for Antelope Valley Hospital; however, there is no notation on the Demurrer that AVHD was erroneously sued as Antelope Valley Hospital. The Court notes that the Cross-Complaint, discussed infra, states that AVHD does business as Antelope Valley Hospital.

On March 20, 2024, AVHD filed a Cross Complaint against Plaintiff, CBB, and ACI alleging two causes of action for (1) Breach of Contract, and (2) Declarator Relief.

 

On March 26, 2024, Plaintiff filed two documents: (1) her Opposition to AVHD’s Demurrer; and (2) her Answer to AVHD’s Cross-Complaint.

 

On April 15, 2024, AVHD filed its Reply.

 

The Court notes that, at this time, ACI has not been served with Plaintiff’s Complaint.

Legal Standard

 

Standard for Demurrer – A demurrer for sufficiency tests whether the complaint states a cause of action.¿ (Hahn v.¿Mirda¿(2007) 147 Cal.App.4th 740, 747.) ¿When considering demurrers, courts read the allegations liberally and in context.¿ (Taylor v. City of Los Angeles Dept. of Water and Power¿(2006) 144 Cal.App.4th 1216, 1228.)¿ In a demurrer proceeding, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or by proper judicial notice.¿ (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 430.30(a).)¿A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters.¿ (SKF Farms v. Superior Court¿(1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 902, 905.)¿Therefore, it lies only where the defects appear on the face of the pleading or are judicially noticed.¿¿(Ibid.)¿¿The only issue involved in a demurrer hearing is¿whether the complaint, as it stands, unconnected with extraneous matters, states a cause of action.¿ (Hahn,¿supra,¿147 Cal.App.4th at 747.)¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿ 

 

A general demurrer admits the truth of all factual, material allegations properly pled in the challenged pleading, regardless of possible difficulties of proof.¿¿(Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318.)¿ Thus, no matter how unlikely or improbable, plaintiff’s allegations must be accepted as true for the purpose of ruling on the demurrer.¿¿(Del E. Webb Corp. v. Structural Materials Co.¿(1981) 123 Cal.App.3d 593, 604.)¿ Nevertheless, this rule does not apply to allegations expressing mere conclusions of law, or allegations contradicted by the exhibits to the complaint or by matters of which judicial notice may be taken.¿¿(Vance v. Villa Park¿Mobilehome¿Estates¿(1995) 36 Cal.App.4th 698, 709.)¿A general demurrer does not admit contentions, deductions, or conclusions of fact or law alleged in the complaint; facts impossible in law; or allegations contrary to facts of which a court may take judicial notice.¿¿(Blank,¿supra, 39 Cal.3d at p. 318.)¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿¿ 

 

Pursuant to¿Code Civ. Proc.¿§430.10(e), the party against whom a complaint has been filed may object by demurrer to the pleading on the grounds that the pleading does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.¿ It is an abuse of discretion to sustain a demurrer without leave to amend if there is a reasonable probability that the defect can be cured by amendment.¿¿(Schifando¿v. City of Los Angeles¿(2003) 31 Cal.4th 1074, 1082,¿as modified Dec. 23, 2003.)¿¿ 

 

----- 

 

Meet and Confer Requirement – Before filing a demurrer or a motion to strike, the demurring or moving party is required to meet and confer with the party who filed the pleading demurred to or the pleading that is subject to the motion to strike for the purposes of determining whether an agreement can be reached through a filing of an amended pleading that would resolve the objections to be raised in the demurrer. (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. §§ 430.41 and 435.5.) The Court notes that this requirement was attempted, but no response was received from Plaintiff. (See Decl. Lauren E. Raya ¶ 3.) “A determination by the court that the meet and confer process was insufficient shall not be grounds to overrule or sustain a demurrer.” (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 430.41(a)(4).) Accordingly, the Court addresses the Demurrer on its merits.

 

-----

 

Discussion

 

Application – AVHD presents that, as alleged, there is no contract between Plaintiff and AVHD as Plaintiff’s Complaint makes conclusory statements that there is a contract between AVHD and Plaintiff without detailing any of the terms or providing a copy of the contract because no such contract exists. AVHD also presents that the Complaint is uncertain, ambiguous and unintelligible because more than one defendant is named and the Complaint fails to distinguish between the defendants.

 

Plaintiff argues that she does allege a contract and directs the Court and the parties to two exhibits that she has attached to her Opposition. Plaintiff presents arguments related to these documents.

 

In Reply, Defendant argues that Plaintiff’s Opposition has not addressed the defects in her pleading as it regurgitates the allegations within the Complaint and Plaintiff’s declaration is procedurally improper as Plaintiff fails to declare anything and attempts to offer legal research as support without analysis or commentary.

 

Such exhibits are improper for the purpose of this Demurrer. In a demurrer proceeding, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or by proper judicial notice.¿ (Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 430.30(a).)

 

Plaintiff has filed a declaration on March 27, 2024, a day after her Opposition was filed. It is unclear if this declaration is related to the Demurrer as it is simply titled “Declaration.” Assuming it is a declaration filed in support of the Opposition to the Demurrer, the declaration includes an exhibit Plaintiff states she has obtained from CBB. As discussed, ante, such an exhibit is improper for the purpose of this Demurrer.

 

The Court notes that the sole cause of action is directed at all three defendants. As such, it is unclear whether Plaintiff is alleging that all defendants breached one contract or whether each defendant breached their respective contracts with Plaintiff

 

“To prevail on a cause of action for breach of contract, the plaintiff must prove (1) the contract, (2) the plaintiff’s performance of the contract or excuse for nonperformance, (3) the defendant’s breach, and (4) the resulting damage to the plaintiff.” (Richman v. Hartley (2014) 224 Cal.App.4th 1182, 1186.)

 

As pled, Plaintiff has not alleged a contract between AVHD and herself. The Complaint focuses on ACI’s agreement to cover Plaintiff’s surgery. (See Attachment PLD-C-011 #BC-1 at 1:3-10, 1:20-24, 2:1-6, 2:14-19, 2:23-24.) That is, Plaintiff has not pled either an oral or written contract with AVHD; if the contract is a written contract, the contract is not attached or the terms of the contract are not recited; and, as such, Plaintiff has not sufficiently pled a Breach of Contract Claim. (See Otworth v. S. Oac. Transp. Co. (1985) 166 Cal.App.3d 452, 459 [“[I]f the action is based on an alleged breach of a written contract, the terms must be set out verbatim in the body of the complaint or a copy of the written instrument must be attached and incorporated by reference.”]).

 

The Court need not discuss the remaining elements as, on its face, a defect exists in the pleadings as to the contract and, thus, the Complaint fails to state a cause of action for Breach of Contract against AVHD.

 

Accordingly, the Demurrer is SUSTAINED.

 

-----

 

Leave to Amend

 

Leave to amend must be allowed where there is a reasonable possibility of successful amendment. (Goodman v. Kennedy¿(1976), 18 Cal.3d 335, 348.) While under California law leave to amend is liberally granted, “leave to amend should not be granted where, in all probability, amendment would be futile.” (Vaillette v. Fireman's Fund Ins. Co. (1993), 18 Cal. App. 4th 680, 685).¿ “A trial court does not abuse its discretion when it sustains a demurrer without¿leave to amend¿if either (a) the facts and the nature of the claims are clear and no liability exists, or (b) it is probable from the nature of the defects and previous unsuccessful attempts to plead that the plaintiff cannot state a claim.” (Cantu v. Resolution Trust Corp.¿(1992)¿4 Cal.App.4th 857, 889.)¿¿

 

Here, because the Complaint unclear as to what contract exists between Plaintiff and AVHD, there is a reasonable possibility of an amendment rectifying the deficiencies within the pleadings.

 

Accordingly, the Court allows leave to amend.

 

-----

 

Conclusion

 

Defendant Antelope Valley Healthcare District’s Demurrer is SUSTAINED with leave to amend.

Case Number: 23AVCV01232    Hearing Date: April 30, 2024    Dept: A14

Background

 

This is a civil rights/discrimination action. Plaintiff David Greenstein (“Plaintiff”) alleges that on August 28, 2023, he was at the Arco AM/PM located at 41923 Sierra Highway in Palmdale, California, (“the Premises”), owned by Guar Enterprise, Inc. (“Guar”), when he sought to use the restroom and was told by an agent named Maggie that there was no public restroom available. Plaintiff further alleges he returned twice and (1) was told from an unknown source that the restroom was not available, but was being worked on; and (2) was told by some bikers that someone had overdosed on drugs in the restroom and the restroom had been welded shut. Plaintiff presents that, on the third time, after talking to the bikers, he returned inside the Arco AM/PM and confirmed the information with Maggie who informed him that the restroom door was welded shut several months ago. Plaintiff contends that lack of access to a restroom violates California law. Plaintiff alleges that Defendants Sanjay Guar, Seema Guar, and Sunny Guar are stockholders for Guar.

 

On October 27, 2023, Plaintiff filed his Complaint alleging one cause of action for Violation of Unruh Civil Rights Act and the Americans with Disabilities (“ADA”) Act, Title III against B.P. America, Inc.; Guar; Sanjay Guar; Seema Guar; and Sunny Guar.

 

On November 29, 2023, Defendants Guar; Sanjay “Sunny” Guar, erroneously sued as Sanjay Guar and Sunny Guar (“Sanjay”)[1]; and Seema Guar (“Seema” and collectively “Defendants”) filed their Demurrer to the Complaint.

 

On December 06, 2023 and December 11, 2023, Plaintiff filed Oppositions. It appears that the first filing included a proposed First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) that had an unfinished header. Plaintiff’s Opposition requested leave to amend to file the FAC.

 

On December 20, 2023, the Court held a Non-Appearance case review. Because a party may amend its pleading once without leave of court any time before an answer, demurrer, or motion to strike is filed, or after a demurrer or motion to strike is filed but before the demurrer or motion to strike is heard if the amended pleading is filed and served no later than the date for filing an opposition to the demurrer or motion to strike, and the Opposition with the proposed FAC was filed on the date the Opposition was due, the Court advanced and vacated the Demurrer. (See Cal. Code Civ. Proc. § 472(a).) The Court ordered Plaintiff to separately file the Amended Complaint by January 01, 2024.

 

On December 22, 2023, Plaintiff filed his FAC alleging one cause of action for Violation of Civil Rights against Guar, Sanjay[2], and Seema[3].

 

On January 25, 2023, Defendants filed their Demurrer, subsequently sustained with leave to amend.

 

On February 29, 2024, Plaintiff filed his Second Amended Complaint (“SAC”). The SAC does not state what cause of action Plaintiff is suing under. It alleges that in August 2023, Plaintiff went to the Premises; Plaintiff asked for the restroom and was told there was no public restroom; California requires service stations that started operating on or after January 01, 1990 and are located within 660 feet of major highways to have clean and sanitary public restrooms; the Premises was located within 660 feet of a major highway; Plaintiff left as he was concerned he might have an accident; Plaintiff returned on further visits and was told that the bathroom was being worked on, third parties informed him that the bathroom had been welded shut several months previously because it was being used by homeless people and one person had died in the bathroom from a drug overdose, and a clerk at the Premises confirmed that the bathroom was welded shut. Plaintiff further alleges that Defendants knew of the aforementioned law and chose to ignore it to the detriment of himself and other customers and, as of the date of filing the SAC, the Premises still does not have a public restroom as required by California law.

 

On March 25, 2024, Defendants filed their Demurrer to the SAC.

 

On April 02, 2024, Plaintiff filed his Opposition.

 

On April 22, 2024, Defendants filed their Reply.

Discussion

 

Application – Defendant presents that Plaintiff’s allegation of the California law requiring service stations that started operating on or after January 1, 1990 and are located within 660 feet of major highways to have clean and sanitary public restrooms found in the SAC at paragraph seven is Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 13651 and Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 13653 provides that the chapter may be enforced by the city attorney, district attorney, or Attorney General. Thus, Defendants argue that Plaintiff has failed to state a cause of action as there is no private right of action for the alleged violation.

 

Plaintiff, in Opposition, concedes that Defendants are correct in their argument. Plaintiff argues that the SAC is not seeking to enforce any law or regulation, but instead seeks damages for personal injuries and damages for violation of a California law.

 

In Reply, Defendants reiterate that there is no private cause of action for this law (i.e., lack of standing).

 

The SAC is unclear as to what duty is breached, the injury, and damages. As such, the Court cannot provide an analysis of the elements to determine whether a cause of action is stated. Defendants do not provide argument addressing Plaintiff’s right to seek damages based on personal injury.

 

Accordingly, the Demurrer is SUSTAINED.

Leave to amend must be allowed where there is a reasonable possibility of successful amendment. (Goodman v. Resolution Trust Corp.¿(1992)¿4 Cal.App.4th 857, 889.)¿

 

As the SAC is unclear, the Court cannot state with certainty that an amendment would not have the possibility of rectifying the deficiencies within the SAC.

 

Accordingly, the Court allows leave to amend. The Court informs Plaintiff that this will be his final opportunity to amend the pleadings.
