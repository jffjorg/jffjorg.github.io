DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.

Dept. 26

Hon. Rolf Treu, Judge presiding 

 

KITCHENS v. THE HERTZ CORPORATION, et al. (23STCV20669)

 

Counsel for Plaintiff:  Heather Conniff (Shegerian Conniff LLP)

Counsel for Defendants/moving party: Allison Scott, Sarah George (Husch Blackwell LLP)

 

DEFENDANTS’ PRO HAC VICE APPLICATION (filed 07/09/2024)

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Defendants’ Pro Hac Vice Application is GRANTED.

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On August 29, 2023, Plaintiff Alexus Kitchens (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendants The Hertz Corporation and Wayne (Last Name Unknown) alleging the following causes of action:

1.      Discrimination on the basis of sex/gender in violation of FEHA;

2.      Sexual Harassment in violation of FEHA; 

3.      Retaliation for complaining of discrimination and/or harassment on the basis of sex/gender in violation of FEHA;

4.      Failure to Prevent discrimination, harassment, and retaliation in violation of FEHA;

5.      Negligent hiring, supervision and retention;

6.      Violation of Labor Code Section 1102.5;

7.      Wrongful Termination in violation of public policy; and

8.      Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress.

 

The Complaint alleges the following. On April 20, 2022, Plaintiff began her employment with Defendant Hertz. Plaintiff was hired as an Instant Return Clerk/Flex staff member. Plaintiff alleges her supervisor, Defendant Wayne, sexually harassed during her employment. Plaintiff further alleges that after complaining about the harassment she was retaliated against and wrongfully terminated.

 

On July 9, 2024, Defendants filed the instant application for Hillary Klein to appear as counsel pro hac vice on behalf of Defendants. On July 8, 2024, Defendants filed a notice of non-opposition stating that Plaintiff had indicated she does not oppose the application.

 

II. DISCUSSION

 

CRC Rule 9.40(d) provides that an application to be admitted pro hac vice must state the following: (1) applicant attorney’s residence and office addresses; (2) the courts to which the applicant attorney has been admitted and dates of admission; (3) a representation that the attorney applicant is a member in good standing in the courts of admission and is not currently suspended or disbarred in any court; (4) the title of each court and action in which the applicant attorney has appeared pro hac vice in this State in the preceding two years, if any; and (5) the name, address, and telephone number of the active California State Bar member who is counsel of record in the local action. (CRC Rule 9.40(d).) 

 

C.R.C., Rule 9.40(e) provides as follows, “[a]n applicant for permission to appear as counsel pro hac vice under this rule must pay a reasonable fee not exceeding $50 to the State Bar of California with the copy of the application and the notice of hearing that is served on the State Bar.” 

 

Defendants’ application on behalf of Hillary Klein provides the information required by C.R.C., Rule 9.40(d).  The declarations supplied sufficient information to satisfy the requirements. (Klein Decl., ¶¶ 1-12; Scott Decl., ¶¶ 1-6.)  Defendants submitted proof that the $50.00 fee was paid to the State Bar of California.  (C.R.C., Rule 9.40(e); Scott Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. B.) Defendants submitted proof of service on the State Bar of California via its online portal.  (C.R.C., Rule 9.40(c)(1); Scott Decl., ¶ 6, Exh. A.) Defendants’ application satisfies rule 9.40.

 

Accordingly, Defendants’ unopposed motion for an order admitting Hillary Klein to appear pro hac vice is GRANTED. 

 

III. DISPOSITION

 

Defendants’ Pro Hac Vice Application is GRANTED.

The Court GRANTS Plaintiff’s request for entry of default judgment.  

 

I. BACKGROUND

 

On December 28, 2023, Plaintiff LVNV Funding LLC filed the instant action against Defendant Carlos Andrade alleging causes of action for breach of contract and open book account.

 

The Complaint alleges the following. Plaintiff is the successor in interest to original creditor, SoFi Lending Corp. Plaintiff is a debt buyer, and is the sole owner of the debt at issue. Plaintiff alleges that within the last four years, Defendant has failed to make payments owed under contract. Defendant owes Plaintiff in the amount of $40,886.01.

 

On March 21, 2024, Plaintiff filed a Request for Entry of Default Judgment. Plaintiff seeks judgment against Defendant in the total amount of $45,880.89. 

 

II.ANALYSIS

 

A.      Legal Standard

 

CCP § 585 permits entry of a judgment after a Defendant has failed to timely answer after being properly served.  A party seeking judgment on the default by the Court must file a Request for Court Judgment, and: (1) a brief summary of the case; (2) declarations or other admissible evidence in support of the judgment requested; (3) interest computations as necessary; (4) a memorandum of costs and disbursements; (5) a proposed form of judgment; (6) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought; (7) a dismissal of all parties against whom judgment is not sought or an application for separate judgment under CCP § 579, supported by a showing of grounds for each judgment; (8) exhibits as necessary; and (9) a request for attorneys’ fees if allowed by statute or by the agreement of the parties.  (CRC Rule 3.1800.)
