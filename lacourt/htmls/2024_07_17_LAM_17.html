DEPARTMENT 17 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV22100    Hearing Date: July 17, 2024    Dept: 17

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

 

DEPARTMENT 17

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

JOHNNIE MCGEE

 

         vs.

 

COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

 

 Case No.:  21STCV22100

 

 

 

 Hearing Date:  July 17, 2024

 

Plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses is MOOT. The Court declines to award sanctions at this time.

 

On 12/15/2022, Plaintiff Johnnie McGee (Plaintiff) filed suit against Defendant County of Los Angeles (County), alleging: (1) discrimination; (2) retaliation; and (3) failure to prevent discrimination, harassment, and retaliation. 

 

            On 4/29/2024, Plaintiff moved to compel further responses to his Special Interrogatories (Set Five).

 

Discussion

 

            Plaintiff seeks to compel further responses to his Special Interrogatories Nos. 43-48, arguing that Defendants’ objections to these requests are meritless.

 

            After review, the Court agrees Plaintiff’s requests are relevant. These Interrogatories ask Defendant the race and ethnicity of Department personnel who have/had the title of FPEA I or FPEA II, and have had their performance evaluation prepared by either Jackie Switzer, James Bailey, and/or Albert Yanagisawa.

 

However, in opposition, Defendant indicated that it agreed to supplement its responses and will provide: “Two years of race and ethnicity prior to the lawsuit and two years after the lawsuit for Fire Prevention Engineering Assistants I and II (“FPEA”) that were evaluated by three (3) supervisors for the County - Jackie Switzler, James Baily and Albert Yanagisawa.”

 

Given Defendant’s willingness to informally resolve this motion through meet-and-confer, the Court finds substantial justification for Defendant’s conduct, and declines to award sanctions.  

Based on the foregoing, Plaintiff’s motion to compel further responses is moot.

 

It is so ordered.

 

Dated:  July    , 2024

                                                                                                                                                          

   Hon. Jon R. Takasugi
   Judge of the Superior Court

            Plaintiffs’ motion to compel discovery and issue sanctions is GRANTED IN PART, MOOT IN PART consistent with the ruling set forth below.  Defendants are sanctioned, jointly and severally with counsel, $1,750 in connection with this motion.

 

Plaintiffs’ motion to compel further responses to their RFPs (Set Three) Nos. 49-54 (to Quantgene), and Nos. 41-47 (to Bhakdi) was withdrawn on 7/9/2024 and thus is MOOT.  

 

On 1/31/2023, Plaintiffs Sebastian Donovan and Arconex (collectively, Plaintiffs) filed suit against Quantgene, Inc. and Johannes Bhakdi (collectively, Defendants), alleging: (1) breach of contract; (2) breach of implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing; (3) unjust enrichment; (4) intentional misrepresentation; (5) negligent misrepresentation; and (6) violation of Business and Professions Code section 17200.

 

            On 3/3/2023, Cross-Complainants Quantgene, Inc. and Johannes Bhakdi filed a cross-complaint (XC) against Cross-Defendants Sebastian Donovan and Arconex, alleging: (1) breach of contract; (2) breach of implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing; (3) false promise; (4) intentional misrepresentation; (5) concealment; (6) negligent misrepresentation; (7) conversion; (8) unjust enrichment; (9) intentional interference with contractual relations; and (10) unfair business practices.

 

            On 5/1/2024, Plaintiffs moved to compel Defendants to produce discovery and seek the imposition of issue sanctions.

 

            On 5/14/2024, Plaintiffs moved to compel further responses from Defendants to their Requests for Production (Set Three).

 

Discussion

 

I.                   RFPs (Set One) and Issue Sanctions

 

            Plaintiffs argue that an order compelling Defendants to respond to their Requests for Production (Set One) is necessary because “a year later—and less than four months before trial, Defendants’ have still not produced significant, important discovery that they agreed to produce and that is necessary for Plaintiffs to conduct meaningful depositions and to prepare to address key disputed issues at trial.” (Motion, ii: 11-14.)

 

            In opposition, Defendants argue that they have produced approximately 150,000 pages of documents, financials, communications, emails, text messages and Slack messages. They also contend that they have, during the pendency of this motion, provided a privilege log and thus has resolved that issue.

 

            However, Defendants contend that there remains an issue over whether or not Plaintiffs are entitled to financial data which allows them to calculate “contribution margin”.

 

            Defendants characterized contribution margins as follows:

 

Essentially, contribution margin has two elements – the first is focused on the word “contribution,” which Defendants assert means the margins on sales generated only by sales and marketing personnel that Plaintiff Donovan recruited (See December 2021 Agreement attached as Exhibit A to the First Amended Complaint). The second part is focused on “margin” which is somewhat debated by the Parties but is essentially the gross revenues of a sale minus the costs associated with that sale, including costs of goods sold (“COGS”), labor costs, and overhead expenses (which is the part of this calculation that the parties mostly dispute, both as to the elements and what values should be used).

 

            (Opp., 4: 18-24.)

 

            Defendants also indicated that on 6/26/2024, Defendants counsel contacted Plaintiffs’ counsel and referencing this motion, offered to provide Plaintiffs with read-only access to bill.com, the online database and service that Quantgene uses to process incoming invoices and pay them, and to provide complete extracts from Quantgene’s QuickBooks system showing its general ledger, payroll, sales and marketing staff, vendor payments and customer receipts for the entirety of January 1, 2021 to December 31, 2023.

 

Defendants’ counsel explained that other than pulling the underlying invoices (many of which are already in bill.com), these data sources, along with unredacted bank statements already produced would give Plaintiffs all of the data necessary to calculate margins. Defendants also offered Quantgene’s newly placed CFO, Bernar Berkirov to explain this data and how to use it as a PMQ witness for Quantgene (Mr. Berkirov’s deposition is scheduled for July 18, 2024). (Woo Decl., ¶ 19, Ex. B).

 

            After review, the Court concludes that this offer resolves Plaintiffs’ concerns that Defendants have only provided summary-level calculations of contribution margin, without allowing Plaintiffs the opportunity to analyze how the alleged margins were calculated.

 

            For this reason, the Court declines to impose issue or evidentiary sanctions on Defendants at this time. While Plaintiffs argue they have been unduly prejudiced by Defendants delay, given that trial is set for 8/26/2024, Plaintiffs offer no convincing explanation as to why they have only just moved to compel this discovery. Moreover, Defendants offered to share this additional data back on 6/26/2024, before their opposition was due.

 

            However, the Court does find monetary sanctions to be appropriate, given Defendants failure to provide a privilege log until this motion was pending. Moreover, Defendants are ordered to produce all relevant discovery here within 10 days of entry of this order.

 

            Based on the foregoing, Plaintiffs’ motion is granted in part, moot in part, consistent with the ruling set forth above. Defendants are sanctioned, jointly and severally with counsel, $1,750. ($350/hr x 3.)

 

II.               RFPs (Set Three)

 

Plaintiffs argue that good cause exits to compel further responses to Plaintiffs’ RFP Nos. Nos. 49-54 (to Quantgene), and Nos. 41-47 (to Bhakdi), respectively. Plaintiffs argue that Defendants’ objections, which they raise to each of the at-issue requests, are unfounded.

 

On 7/9/2024, Plaintiff filed a notice of withdrawal for this motion. Accordingly, this motion is moot.

 

It is so ordered.
