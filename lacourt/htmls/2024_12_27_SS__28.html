DEPARTMENT 28 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

All parties are urged to meet and confer with all parties concerning this tentative ruling to see if they can reach an agreed-upon resolution of their matter.  If you are able to reach an agreement, please notify the courtroom staff in advance of the hearing if you wish to submit on the tentative ruling rather than argue the motion by notifying the court by e-mailing the court at: SSCDEPT28@lacourt.org.  Include the word "SUBMITS" in all caps and the Case Number in the Subject line.  In the body of the email, please provide the date and time of the hearing, your name, your contact information, the party you represent, and whether that party is a plaintiff, defendant, cross-complainant, cross-defendant, claimant, intervenor, or non-party, etc.

            Please be advised that if you submit on the tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may still appear at the hearing and argue the matter, and the court could change its tentative based upon the argument.  Unless you receive a submission from all other parties in the matter, you should assume that others might appear at the hearing to argue.  If you submit, but still intend to appear, include the words "SUBMITS, BUT WILL APPEAR" in the Subject line.     If you elect to argue your matter, you are urged to do so remotely, via Court-Connect.

                                       Note that once the Court has issued a tentative, the Court has the inherent authority not to allow the withdrawal of a motion and to adopt the tentative ruling as the order of the court.  This does not excuse a moving party's need to do one of the following: appear; submit; or take a matter off calendar by canceling the motion in the case reservation system before issuance of the tentative ruling if the matter moving party does not intend to proceed.    
 
            If you submitted a courtesy copy of your papers containing media (such as a DVD or thumb drive), unless you request the return of the media in your papers, the court will destroy it following the hearing of your matter.

Case Number: 22STCV39136    Hearing Date: December 27, 2024    Dept: 28

On December 16, 2022, Plaintiff Joanne Williams filed this action against Defendants Henry Franklin as Trustee of Robert S. Anderson and Earlean M. Anderson, as Trustees of the Anderson Revocable Trust dated February 12, 1994, Penny Holt as Trustee of Robert S. Anderson and Earlean M. Anderson, as Trustees of the Anderson Revocable Trust dated February 12, 1994, and Does 1-50 for general negligence and premises liability. 

On May 3, 2023, Penny Holt (“Holt”), on her own behalf and purportedly on behalf of Henry Franklin, filed a motion to dismiss the case.  On June 28, 2023, the Court denied the motion.  The Court explained that the motion lacked a proof of service, did not comply with the requirements for motions contained in California Rules of Court, rule 3.1112, and did not establish grounds for dismissal. 

On April 22, 2024, Plaintiff amended the complaint to include Defendant Robert Anderson (“Anderson”) as Doe 1.  On July 24, 2024, Anderson filed an answer. 

On October 30, 2024, Holt, on her own behalf and purportedly on behalf of Henry Franklin, filed another motion to dismiss.  The motion was set for hearing on December 27, 2024.  Like Holt’s May 3, 2023 motion, the October 30, 2024 motion does not include a proof of service. 

The Court denies the motion without prejudice to the filing of a motion that includes a valid proof of service showing service of the motion and any supporting papers on all parties who have appeared in the case.

Moving party is ordered to give notice.

Case Number: 23STCV00997    Hearing Date: December 27, 2024    Dept: 28

On January 17, 2023, Plaintiff Julie Jaleh Sabaierad (“Plaintiff’) filed this action against Defendants County of Los Angeles (“County”), Esayas Alemu (“Alemu”), and Does 1-100 for negligence. 

On February 21, 2023, the County and Alemu ("Defendants") filed answers. 

At the September 25, 2024 final status conference, the Court continued the trial from October 2, 2024 to November 13, 2024 based on counsel’s oral stipulation.  The Court directed that all discovery deadlines would remain closed except for expert discovery deadlines, which would be based on the new trial date. 

On November 13, 2024, Defendants filed a motion to quash Plaintiff’s subpoena served on third-party witness Corina Mary Weaver.  The motion was set for hearing on December 27, 2024.  Plaintiff has not filed an opposition. 

Also on November 13, 2024, based on counsel’s oral stipulation, the Court continued the trial from November 13, 2024 to December 9, 2024. 

On November 18, 2024, the Court granted Defendants’ ex parte application and continued the trial from December 9, 2024 to February 13, 2025.  The Court did not continue or reopen discovery. 

On December 9, 2024, the parties filed a stipulation stating in part: 

·       “WHEREAS, Plaintiff agrees to conditionally withdraw the trial subpoena for Corina Mary Weaver that forms the basis of the Motion to Quash, thereby rendering it moot;”

·       “WHEREAS, Defendants have agreed to conditionally withdraw the pending Motion to Quash and agree to have the hearing on December 27, 2024, vacated;”

·       “WHEREFORE, the parties conditionally stipulate to advance the trial date and FSC to a date convenient for this Court within the second half of January 2025.”

  The parties did not, however, provide a proposed order for the Court’s signature or set the matter for hearing.  The Court has not considered the parties’ stipulated request.  In addition, the parties have not taken the hearing on Defendants’ motion to quash the subpoena served on third-party witness Corina Mary Weaver, set for December 27, 2024, off calendar. 

The Court continues the hearing on Defendants’ motion to quash Plaintiff’s subpoena to a date to be provided at the December 27, 2024 hearing.  At least ten court days before the continued hearing, Defendants shall file a supplemental brief or declaration addressing the status of the motion.  Plaintiff may file a responsive supplemental brief or declaration no later than five court days before the continued hearing. 

Moving parties are ordered to give notice.
