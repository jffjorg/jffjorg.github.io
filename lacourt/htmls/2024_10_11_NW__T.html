22VECV00081 RIF V – ODESSA, LLC vs LETS RESTYLE CA ET AL

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Plaintiff RIF V – Odessa, LLC’s Unopposed Petition to Confirm the Arbitration Award is GRANTED.

Introduction

Plaintiff RIF V – Odessa, LLC (Plaintiff) moved to confirm the arbitration award decided against Defendants Lets Restyle CA, Inc. and I Beauty Brands Inc. (collectively, Defendants).  The arbitrator awarded Plaintiff a total of $418,482.58, which is inclusive of the principal amount of $225,645.37, attorneys’ fees in the amount of $55,191.50, costs of suit in the amount of $14,317.05, JAMS Arbitration fees and costs in the amount of $45,250.00, and 10% daily interest of $61.82 per day from January 18, 2021 to July 3, 2024 (1,263 days) in the amount of $78,078.66.  The Arbitration Award further awarded Plaintiff 10% daily interest of $61.82 from July 4, 2024 through to the final entry of Judgment herein.   A signed copy of the award is attached as Attachment 8(c).   There are 100 days between July 4, 2024 and this hearing date, October 11, 2024.  The amount of days is inclusive of both July 4, 2024 and October 11, 2024.

Discussion                             

Unless a petition or response seeking to correct or vacate an arbitration award was filed properly based upon valid grounds, or the proceeding is dismissed, courts are required to enter judgment in conformity with the award.  (Code of Civ. Proc. sec. 1286; Valsan Partners Ltd. Partnership v. Calcor Space Facility (1994) 25 Cal.App.4th 809, 818.)  Defendants argued that the Petition is defective because it requests an award in excess of the amount awarded in the arbitration award.  The Petition is not seen to be requesting an amount above the award.  The Petition’s paragraphs 8(b)(f) and 10(e) and (f) are not in conflict.  The Arbitrator’s Award, attached as Attachment 8(c), makes it clear that the $418,482.58 includes the costs and fees identified in Petition paragraph 10(e) and (f).  Defendants’ first argument to vacate the award is unpersuasive.

An arbitration award can be vacated only on specific statutory grounds.  (Code Civ. Proc., sec. 1286.2.)  Defendants asserted that the Arbitration Award should be vacated because their rights were substantially prejudiced by the arbitrator’s misconduct.  (Code Civ. Proc., sec. 1286.2(a)(3).)  The burden of proof is upon the party challenging the Award to establish facts to support their contention.  (Betz v. Pankow (1993) 16 Cal.App.4th 919, 926.)  Defendants argued that there was ex parte communications between the Arbitrator and Plaintiff’s counsel regarding Defendants’ exhibits and the communications biased the Arbitrator.  (Lyric Enav (Enav) Decl. pars. 2-4.)  Allegedly the ex parte communications led to the arbitrator’s refusal to consider all of Defendants’ exhibits.  (Enav Decl. pars. 5-9.)  However, Plaintiff’s Counsel categorically denies any ex parte communications with the arbitrator at any time during the arbitration.  (Lane Nussbaum (Nussbaum) Decl. pars. 3-5.)  The evidence shows two competing declarations.  The conflicting declarations by both Counsels present a “50-50” balancing of the scale.  Because it is Defendants’ burden to show substantial prejudice and/or bias, Defendants Counsel’s declaration is insufficient to tip the balance of the scale in their favor.  Defendants failed to meet their burden.  Defendants failed to sufficiently show a statutory ground to vacate the Arbitration Award.

The Petition to Confirm the Award is GRANTED.

MOVING PARTY SHALL SUBMIT A JUDGMENT IN ACCORDANCE WITH THIS ORDER, ATTACHING A COPY OF THE ARBITRATION AWARD.

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.

23VECV05505 Sepulveda Village v Nguyen

Tentative ruling on motion for terminating and monetary sanctions: Grant.

Code of Civil Procedure Section 2023.030 authorizes the Court to issue terminating sanctions (2023.030(d)), issue sanctions (2023.030(b)) and evidence sanctions (2023.030(c))  Against a party who misuses the discovery process. In order to award such sanctions, it is necessary only for the Court to find that the party's disobedience is willful - no finding of bad faith is required. Creed21 v. City of Wildomar (2017) 18 CA5th 690, 703. "Misuses" of the discovery process include "Failing to respond or submit to an authorized method of discovery" (CCP 2023.010(d)), "Making an evasive response to discovery" (CCP 2023.010(f), and "Disobeying a court order to provide discovery" (CCP 2023.010(g)).

Defendant has clearly misused the discovery process in this case, not only by willfully refusing to properly respond, but also by willfully refusing to comply with this Court's order compelling him to do so, despite the several occasions when Plaintiff's counsel reminded Defendant of his duty to respond and to comply with this Court's order - overtures which have been ignored.

This appears to be a part of Defendant's pattern of refusing to comply with the procedures and rules of this Court.

To date, Defendant has refused to:

(a) file the required Case Management Statement,

(b) appear at the March 13, 2024 Case Management Conference,

(c) respond to Plaintiff's DPD1 and FI1,

(d) respond to Plaintiff's motions to compel responses to the DPD1 and FI1,

(e) appear at the hearing on Plaintiff's motions to compel responses to the DPD1 and FI1,

(f) appear at the July 9, 2024 Trial Setting Conference,

(g) comply with this Court's order of July 9, 2024 regarding discovery responses, despite Plaintiff's counsel's communications to him requesting such compliance and

warning of the consequences.

No opposition was filed to this motion.

The court finds that Defendant has willfully failed to comply with the court’s order of 7/9/2024 and as a sanctions, the court strikes Defendant’s General Denial of 2/23/2024 and enters default against Defendant.

The court also finds that Defendant has engaged in discovery abuse without substantial justification which warrants the imposition of monetary sanctions against Defendant Kevin Nguyen in the sum of $796.50, payable by 10/21/2024 to the Client Trust Account of BOSCHÉ & BOSCHÉ,  300 Spectrum Center Drive, Suite 400, Irvine, California 92618.

Plaintiff to submit a Default Judgment package.
