DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

APPLICATION FOR AN ORDER PERMITTING TIMOTHY B. FITZGERALD AS COUNSEL PRO HAC VICE

 

 

Timothy B. Fitzgerald (“Fitzgerald”) applies as counsel pro hac vice for Defendants Keelson Strategic Corporation and Nicholas J. Molina.  The application is unopposed.

Under CRC Rule 9.40, attorneys licensed and in good standing in other states may, upon the California court’s approval, appear as counsel pro hac vice in a pending case if an active member of the state bar is associated as attorney of record.  (CRC 9.40(a).)  Fitzgerald is an active member, in good standing, of the Supreme Court of the State of New York Appellate Division: Second Judicial Department, the District of Columbia, South Carolina, Washington State, the United States District Court of the Western District of Washington, the United States District Court for Southern District of New York, and the U.S. Court of Appeals, Ninth Circuit.  (Firzgerald Decl. ¶ 2(c)-(d).)  In addition, Fitzgerald states that he is not a resident of California or regularly engaged in business in California.  Fitzgerald is a resident of Mercer Island Washington with an office in Seattle, Washington.  (Fitzgerald Decl. ¶¶ 2(a)-(b).)  Fitzgerald states that states that he has applied to be admitted pro hac vice in a California proceeding once in the Los Angeles Superior Court Case entitled action entitled TGC Restaurant Limited, L.P. v. Las Vegas Restaurant, LLC and Forum Restaurant Venture, Case No. BC714026 which was granted on October 23, 2018.  (Fitzgerald Decl. ¶ 2(e).)  Thus, counsel satisfies the eligibility requirements of CRC 9.40(a).

In the interests of judicial efficiency, the Court herby orders the parties to attend an informal discovery conference (IDC) to seek a resolution of their discovery disputes.  Within 3 court days of notice of this order, the parties must jointly contact Department 26 to schedule a mutually convenient date and time for the IDC, to occur within the next 3 weeks.

 

The parties are ordered to meet and confer by way of a realtime conversation (by phone, in person, or by videoconference) to attempt resolution of their disputes prior to appearing for the informal discovery conference.  An exchange of emails or correspondence will not suffice for purposes of this meet and confer.

 

In addition, in preparation for the IDC, each party is to file and serve a short IDC statement that sets forth: (1) any recurring issues, objections, or assertions of privilege that are common to all or most of the disputed items, and (2) for each form of written discovery, a representative sample of at least three requests and the universe of responses received to date for each of the requests.  For example, if the parties cannot resolve their disputes regarding a motion to compel further responses to requests for production, each party should file an IDC statement that sets forth recurring issues, objections, and assertions of privilege common to all the disputed requests, and each party should also provide the text of at least three disputed requests for production and under each, all the responses served for that request for production. The IDC statement should not exceed more than two pages for each disputed motion. Each party must file its IDC statement no later than three court days before the IDC.  For purposes of this IDC statement, it is unnecessary to lay out the procedural history of the parties’ meet and confer efforts.

 

The parties may appear remotely through LACourtConnect for the informal discovery conference, but if they appear remotely, they must at least appear by a video connection on LACourtConnect  (as opposed a telephonic connection on LACourtConnect).

 

The moving party should be prepared to file all moving papers for these CRS reservations within 10 days of the IDC.  Failure to do so will result in cancellation of any CRS reservations for which no moving papers are filed so that these unused CRS reservations will become available for litigants in other cases to schedule hearings for their discovery motions.

 

If the moving party that reserved a CRS reservation for a motion to compel further hearing is satisfied that the dispute has been resolved, and that a motion to compel further responses is no longer necessary, the moving party is ordered to cancel the CRS reservation immediately – in advance of the IDC – so that these unused CRS reservations will become available for litigants in other cases to schedule hearings for their discovery motions.

 

If the moving party fails to contact the Court within three court days to schedule an IDC, the CRS reservations will all be cancelled so that these unused CRS reservations will become available for litigants in other cases to schedule hearings for their discovery motions.

 

In addition, the parties should note that a court-wide change in the CRS system is imminent such that parties will no longer be able to make a CRS reservation without filing moving papers within three days of making the reservation; unused CRS reservations will be automatically cancelled if moving papers are not filed within three days of making the CRS reservation.  It may well be that the court-wide change to the CRS system may occur before the parties attend the IDC in this matter.  The Court thus urges the parties that made the multiple CRS reservations in this case to file their moving papers as soon as possible if they wish to avoid automatic cancellation by the CRS system.
