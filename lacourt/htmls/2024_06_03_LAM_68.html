Case Number: 23STCV03450    Hearing Date: June 3, 2024    Dept: 68

Dept. 68

Date: 6-3-24 (a/f 8-6-24)

Case Number: 23STCV03450

Trial Date: 11-25-24

 

DOCUMENTS

 

MOVING PARTY:                Plaintiff, APB Properties, Inc.

RESPONDING PARTY:       Defendant, Jeff Kern

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

Motion to Compel Responses to Request for Production of Documents

 

SUMMARY OF ACTION

On March 11, 2022,, Defendant Jeff Kern, et al. entered into an agreement with Plaintiff APB Properties, Inc. for the purchase of certain commercial property located at 6342 Pacific Bl., Huntington Park. The purchase price was $2,050,000, with a 90-day escrow period. Kern deposited $60,000. Subsequent negotiations based on requests for repair led to a reduction in price, with escrow closing date remaining May 31, 2022. Plaintiff buyer failed to follow through on the purchase. APB Properties, Inc. seeks the $60,000 deposit as liquidated damages.

 

On February 16, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint for Breach of Written Contract and Declaratory Relief. Kern filed a cross-complaint for Breach of Contract of Contract and Declaratory Relief on April 13, 2024, with the answer filed on April 14, 2023. Kern alleges APB Properties, Inc. withheld material information on the condition of the property, thereby breaching the purchase contract.

 

On May 19, 2023, the court granted the motion to compel arbitration. On December 27, 2023, the court terminated the arbitration order and lifted the stay. APB Properties, Inc. answered the cross-complaint on December 28, 2023.

 

RULING: Granted.

Plaintiff/Cross- Defendant, APB Properties, Inc. (APB) move to compel response to Request for Production of Documents (set one), served on Defendant/Cross-Complainant, Jeff Kern. The court electronic filing system shows no opposition or reply at the time of the tentative ruling publication cutoff.

 

APB represents service of Request for Production of Documents (set one) on January 4, 2023. The discovery was served on former counsel. New counsel substituted into the case on January 26, 2024. Moving counsel contacted new counsel, but received no update on the outstanding discovery. [Declaration of Harris Cohen.]

 

The motion is granted. Defendant/Cross-Complainant, Jeff Kern is ordered to serve verified responses to request for production of documents (set one) without objections within ten days. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.300, subd. (a-b).)

 

Sanctions in the amount of $872.50. The mandatory statutory minimum required for request for production of documents is now $1,000, but since counsel only requested $872.50, the court declines to raise the minimal level beyond the noticed amount. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2023.040,  2023.050, subd. (a), 2031.300, subd. (c).) Sanctions payable in 30 days and imposed jointly against responding party and counsel.

 

Trial set for November 25, 2024.

 

APB to give notice.

Dept. 68

Date: 6-3-24 (a/f 12-4-24 via 4-3-24 stipulation)

Case 23STCV11602

Trial Date: Not Set

 

QUASH SERVICE

 

MOVING PARTY: Specially Appearing Defendant, Cosentino Group, S.A.

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff, Wendy Viridinia Solano-Castro

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

Motion to Quash Service of the Summons and Complaint

 

SUMMARY OF ACTION

Wendy Viridinia Solano-Castro, et al. alleges the wrongful death of Raul Garcia-Leon caused from as a stone fabricator and installer. Plaintiffs contend working with “toxic” stone products led to the development of silicosis and death of Garcia-Leon on February 16, 2023.

 

On May 22, 2023, Plaintiff filed a 582 paragraph complaint for negligence, strict liability – warning defect, strict liability – design defect, fraudulent concealment, and breach of implied warranties. The complaint names a multitude of parties comprised of suppliers, sellers, etc. The case was deemed non-complex on June 5, 2023, and assigned to Department 68 on June 7, 2023.

 

On October 11, 2023, the court sustained the demurrer of MS International, Inc. to the fourth cause of action with 20 days leave to amend. On November 2, 2023, Caesarstone USA, Inc. filed a cross-complaint for indemnity, declaratory relief and apportionment. On December 29, 2023, Lowes Home Centers, LLC filed a cross-complaint indemnity, declaratory relief and apportionment.

 

RULING: Continued.

Request for Judicial Notice: Granted in Part/Denied in Part

·         The court takes judicial notice of the denied writs of mandate from the Second Appellate District to the extent it reflects and impacts the same parties in the subject action.

·         The court declines to take judicial notice of the email regarding the rejection of the writ of certiorari from the California Supreme Court.

 

Specially appearing defendant Cosentino Group, S.A. (Cosentino Group) moves to quash service of the summons and complaint on grounds of improper service, or alternatively lack of general or specific jurisdiction. Plaintiff both opposes the motion on grounds of invalid service, and requests a continuance of the hearing on the jurisdictional issues. Cosentino Group in reply represents that the further discovery confirms a lack of jurisdiction. Cosentino Group submits substantial discussion on the holding company relationship between Cosentino Group and C & C North America.

 

On December 8, 2023, the court issued a 12-page order denying the motion of Cosentino Group to quash service of the summons and complaint based on improper service of Eduardo Cosentino in British Columbia Canada. The court also deferred consideration of the minimum contacts challenge to a future hearing date in order to allow Consentino Group to take up a writ of mandate on the proof of service issue. Upon resolution of the appeal, the parties also agreed to allow Plaintiff to conduct discovery into jurisdictional support, if applicable.

 

The Court of Appeal denied the writ on March 20, 2024. The parties subsequently agreed to a deposition of a person most knowledgeable regarding jurisdictional issues for April 30, 2024, and the subject June 3, 2024, hearing date.

 

The original motion was filed on October 12, 2023. Following the December 8, 2023, and denial of the appeal on March 20, 2024, Plaintiff represents both the denial of certiorari from the California Supreme Court, and the deposition of the person most knowledgeable for Cosentino Group taking place on May 6, 2024. Notwithstanding the completed deposition on May 6, 2024, however, Plaintiff requests a continuance in order to allow for time to review the deposition transcript and draft an opposition. Plaintiff filed the request on May 20, 2024, the opposition due date.

 

The court acknowledges the reply arguments challenging any finding of general or specific jurisdiction, but declines to consider the substantive merits of the challenge to jurisdiction regarding Cosentino Group at this hearing. The court adheres to a policy of robust adjudication of legal argument on the merits, and therefore elects to allow Plaintiff an opportunity to review the deposition transcript and present a substantive opposition on the remaining jurisdiction challenge.

 

While the court advanced the instant hearing pursuant to the parties’ April 3, 2024, stipulation, the court finds no prejudice in continuing the hearing in order to allow full briefing on the general and specific jurisdiction arguments. The court therefore continues the hearing to July 2, 2024, in order to be concurrently heard with the Case Management Conference and OSC re: Sanctions. The opposition on the remaining jurisdictional issue and any new, revised reply are due in conformance with statutory deadlines. Wednesday June 19, 2024, is a court holiday, and therefore not counted as a court day for purposes of determining the opposition filing deadline. (See Code Civ. Proc., § 12b.)

Cosentino Group, S.A. to give notice to all parties.
