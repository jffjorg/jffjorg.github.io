DEPARTMENT O LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

The Court may change tentative rulings at any time. Therefore, attorneys are advised to check this website to determine if any changes or updates have been made to the tentative ruling.

Counsel may submit on the tentative rulings by calling the clerk in Dept. O at 909-802-1126 before 8:30 the morning of the hearing. Submission on the tentative does not bind the court to adopt the tentative ruling at the hearing should the opposing party appear and convince the court of further modification during oral argument.

The Tentative Ruling is not an invitation, nor an opportunity, to file any further documents relative to the hearing in question. No such filing will be considered by the Court in the absence of permission first obtained following ex-parte application therefore.



Case Number: 22PSCV00069    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: O

Tentative Ruling

PLAINTIFF’S NOTICE OF MOTION AND MOTION TO ENFORCE SETTLEMENT is TBD because defendant’s default precludes the ability to enter into a stipulation; should the default be set aside; the motion is granted. (A proposed order has been filed.)

Background

This is an ADA case.

On January 21, 2022, Plaintiff REBECCA CASTILLO filed suit against Defendant Uptown Koop, LLC.

On March 30, 2022, default was entered against Defendant.

On September 22, 2022, the court held its OSC re: Dismissal (Settlement). The minute order provides in relevant part the following: “There is no appearance by or for the defense. Court and Counsel confer regarding the case status, indicating the parties reached a settlement, however the defendant is no longer in contact with the plaintiff. Plaintiff's counsel intends to move forward with the default Judgment.”

On November 18, 2022, the court held another OSC re: Dismissal. The minute order provides in relevant part the following: “There is no appearance by or for the defense. Court and Counsel confer regarding the status of settlement, indicating plaintiff is attempting to retrieve payments from defense. Further counsel indicates he may elect to proceed by way of Default Judgment if he is unable to collect on the settlement agreement.”

On June 13, 2023, the court held another OSC re: Dismissal wherein Defendant again did not appear and Plaintiff’s counsel again informed the court it has been unable to secure settlement funds.

On August 26, 2023, Plaintiff filed a NOTICE OF WITHDRAWAL OF NOTICE OF SETTLEMENT.

On October 6, 2023, Plaintiff filed the instant application for entry of default judgment.

On October 30, 2023, the court denied with prejudice Plaintiff’s application for entry of default judgment for the failure to plead sufficient facts to establish an UCRA violation. The court allowed for 30 days leave to amend the complaint, which was to be filed by 11/29/23.

On February 27, 2024, the court again held hearings re: ‘Order to Show Cause Re: Default; Case Management Conference; Order to Show Cause Re: Failure to File Proof of Service as to Amended Complaint.’ Plaintiff’s counsel again informed the court about the settlement.

On May 28, 2024, the court again held an OSC re: dismissal and stated that “In light of the enforceable settlement agreement between the parties, the Court admonishes Plaintiff's Counsel that the case will be dismissed if not done so prior to the next court date.”

On September 17, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion.

Legal Standard

California Code of Civil Procedure §664.6(a) provides that, "If parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside of the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, . . . the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement."

Discussion

In November 2023, the Parties reached a settlement. This settlement was memorialized in writing and signed by both Parties; Defendant signed the settlement agreement on December 18, 2023, and Plaintiff signed the settlement agreement on December 26, 2023. The settlement amount was for $11,000. Per the terms of the agreement, the amount was due within 10 business days of Defendant receiving a copy of the agreement. (Fitzgerald Decl., p. 5 of 43 of PDF.) In the event of a dispute or issues with enforcement, the agreement provides that the agreement shall be enforceable pursuant to CCP section 664.6. (p. 9 of 43 of PDF, P11.)

However, as reflected in the minute orders and Plaintiff’s Counsel’s numerous emails to Defense Counsel, there has been no successful communication between the parties. As the court can enforce the judgment pursuant to the terms of the agreement, the court does so. That said, the defendant is in default.  Entry of a defendant's default terminates that defendant's rights to participate in the litigation (Devlin v. Kearny Mesa AMC/Jeep/Renault, Inc. (1984) 155 Cal.App.3d 381, 385, 202 Cal.Rptr. 204) Entering into a settlement agreement is tantamount to participating in litigation. Should Plaintiff set aside the default entered against Defendant, the settlement may be enforceable.

Conclusion

Based on the foregoing, the motion is TBD, but likely granted upon setting aside Defendant’s default.


Case Number: 22PSCV02847    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: O

Tentative Ruling

 

PLAINTIFF’S NOTICE OF MOTION AND MOTION FOR APPROVAL OF PRIVATE ATTORNEYS GENERAL ACT (CAL. LABOR CODE § 2698,  ET SEQ.) SETTLEMENT AGREEMENT AND AWARD OF ATTORNEYS’ FEES AND COSTS, GENERAL RELEASE FEE, AND SETTLEMENT ADMINISTRATION COSTS is GRANTED.

 

Background

 

This is a wage and hour action.

 

On December 12, 2022, Plaintiff TIERRA FRANKLIN, individually, and on behalf of other aggrieved employees pursuant to the California Private Attorneys General Act, filed suit against Defendant POMONA HEALTHCARE & WELLNESS CENTER, LLC.  

 

On September 14, 2023, the court granted Defendant’s motion to compel arbitration of the individual PAGA claim and stayed the non-individual/representative PAGA claims.

 

On June 12, 2024, a notice of settlement was filed.

 

On September 17, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion.

 

Legal Standard

 

Labor Code section 2699, subdivision (l)(2) states:¿“The superior court shall review and approve any settlement of any civil action filed pursuant to this part. The proposed settlement shall be submitted to the agency at the same time that it is submitted to the court.”  “A copy of the superior court’s judgment in any civil action filed pursuant to this part and any other order in that action that either provides for or denies an award of civil penalties under this code shall be submitted to the agency within 10 days after entry of the judgment or order.”  (Lab. Code, §2699, subd. (l)(3).) 

 

While PAGA requires court review and approval of settlements, it does not specify the standard or criteria for such review. (Moniz v. Adecco USA, Inc. (2021) 72 Cal.App.5th 56, 75 (Moniz).) Thus, courts have generally applied the same review standards used for class actions, which inquire “independently whether a PAGA settlement is fair and reasonable.” (Id., at p. 77.) To determine if a PAGA settlement is fair and reasonable, courts consider factors “including the strength of the plaintiff's case, the risk, the stage of the proceeding, the complexity and likely duration of further litigation, and the settlement amount.” (Ibid.) Lastly, courts will also consider if the settlement is “adequate in view of PAGA's purposes to remediate present labor law violations, deter future ones, and to maximize enforcement of state labor laws.” (Ibid.)

 

Discussion

 

Plaintiff moves for court approval of PAGA settlement pursuant to Labor Code section 2699, subdivision (l)(2). The total Settlement amount is for $351,862.00. The Net Settlement Amount will be the Total Settlement Amount less the following amounts: (1) Attorneys’ Fees and Costs consisting of attorneys’ fees of $123,151.70 and reimbursement of litigation costs and expenses of $2,929.15 to Plaintiff’s Counsel; (2) General Release Fee of $15,000.00 to Plaintiff; and (3) Settlement Administration Costs of up to $7,500.00 to Phoenix Settlement Administrators. Seventy-five percent (75%) of the Net Settlement Amount (or approximately $152,460.86) will be distributed to the LWDA (i.e., LWDA Payment) and twenty-five percent (25%) of the Net Settlement Amount (or approximately $50,820.29) (i.e., Employees’ Portion) will be distributed to the Aggrieved Employees on a pro rata basis, based on their Compensable Pay Periods.

 

Here, the court finds no defects with the application.  

 

Conclusion

 

Based on the foregoing, the motion is granted. (A proposed order has been filed.)

 

 

 


Case Number: 24PSCV00349    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: O

Tentative Ruling

 

(1)   DEFENDANTS NAVIGATOR REAL ESTATE INC. AND MINGJU ZHANG'S MOTION TO RECLASSIFY SECOND AMENDED COMPLAINT TO LIMITED JURISDICTION is GRANTED.

 

(2)   DEFENDANTS NAVIGATOR REAL ESTATE INC. AND MINGJU ZHANG'S DEMURRER TO SECOND AMENDED COMPLAINT is MOOT.

 

 

Background[1]

 

This is a contracts case that arises from a lease agreement. Plaintiffs allege that they wanted to move for a better school for their children. Jing Pan (“Pan”) told Plaintiffs about Mingju, who has a house for rent. Plaintiffs wrote a check to Pan, who was to “hand it” to Mingju. “Mingju [] signed a contract with Pan [].” However, Pan has moved into the home and will not turn the keys over to Plaintiffs. Plaintiffs went to Pinnacle’s office wherein ‘manager Michael’ told Plaintiffs that Mingju “bypassed the company” and signed the contract himself.

 

On February 2, 2024, Plaintiff Zhou Chen filed suit against Defendants NAVIGATORS REAL ESTATE INC., (“Navigators”), MINGJU ZHANG, JING PAN (“Pan”), QINGFENG CHEN.

 

On March 15, 2024, Navigators filed a demurrer and MTS, which the court on 5/2/24 sustained with leave to amend due to uncertainty, rendering the MTS moot.

 

On May 8, 2024, Plaintiff Chen and Dan Chen filed their first amended complaint (FAC) against the same defendants.

 

On June 7, 2024, Jing Pan filed a General Denial.

 

On June 14, 2024, Navigators and Zhang (“moving Defendants”) filed another demurrer x MTS, which the court on July 25, 2024 sustained with leave to amend, but struck the mental damages for $40,000 without leave to amend. (The court did not post a tentative ruling.)

 

On August 26, 2024, Plaintiffs filed their second amended complaint (SAC); Qingfen Chen was removed as a named defendant.

 

On September 24, 2024, moving Defendants filed the instant demurrer to the SAC and motion to reclassify.

 

Discussion

To reclassify a case from unlimited to limited, a trial court must find that the matter will necessarily result in a verdict below the jurisdictional amount required by statute for classification as an unlimited civil case. (Hiona v. Superior Court (2020) 48 Cal.App.5th 866, 872.) “Necessarily” means that there is “a high level of certainty that a damage award will not exceed $25,000.” (Walker v. Superior Court (1991) 53 Cal.3d 257, 268-270.) Put simply, in making the determination to reclassify, legal certainty is required. (Chahal v. Superior Court (1999) 73 Cal.App.4th 399, 402-403.) Per California Code of Civil Procedure section 85, the amount in controversy for purposes of distinguishing between limited and unlimited means the amount of the demand exclusive of attorney fees, interest and costs.

Here, Plaintiffs seek either $16,680 or $18,690 in damages, though it is unclear. Nevertheless, as either of those amounts is less than $35,000, the case is a limited case. Effectively, the demurrer is MOOT.

 Conclusion

Based on the foregoing, the case is reclassified to a limited case, which renders the demurrer moot.
