DEPARTMENT H LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22PSCV00391    Hearing Date: October 24, 2024    Dept: H

Chalmers-Walnut, LLC v. Stone, et al., Case No. 22PSCV00391

For the reasons stated below, the Court intends to continue the hearing on Plaintiff Chalmers-Walnut, LLC’s Motion for Attorneys’ Fees. During the hearing on October 24, 2024, the Court will hear from counsel regarding an appropriate continued date.

 

Background 

Case No. 22PSCV00391 

This is an unlawful detainer action involving the premises located at 19224 East Walnut Drive North, Suite C, Rowland Heights, CA 91748 (“Premises”). 

On April 19, 2022, Plaintiff Chalmers-Walnut, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed a complaint, asserting two unlawful detainer causes of action against Snow Stone, Meng Wang (“Wang”), All Unnamed Occupants in Possession of or in Use of the Premises, and Does 1-50. 

On October 10, 2022, the case proceeded to trial; that day, the matter was taken under submission. On October 25, 2022, a Court Order Re: Judgment was entered. On November 8, 2022, judgment was entered. 

On November 21, 2022, Plaintiff filed (and electronically served) a “Notice of Entry of Judgment.” On December 6, 2022, Plaintiff filed a “Memorandum of Costs.” 

On April 20, 2023, Case Nos. 22PSCV00391 and 23PSCV01012 were deemed related; Case No. 22PSCV00391 was designated as the lead case. 

On June 20, 2023, Snow Stone filed a “Notice of Stay of Proceedings,” advising therein of its Chapter 7 bankruptcy filing. 

On September 26, 2023, Wang filed a “Notice of Stay of Proceedings,” advising therein of her Chapter 7 bankruptcy filing. 

A Status Conference Re: Bankruptcy is set for October 24, 2024. Pending before the Court is Plaintiff’s motion for attorneys’ fees.

 

Case No. 23PSCV01012 

In the related case, Plaintiff alleges that on November 29, 2022, Plaintiff, Snow Stone, and Wang entered into a Settlement and Release Agreement, wherein Snow Stone and Wang were required to, inter alia, leave the Premises in the condition required under the terms of the Standard Industrial/Commercial Multi-Tenant Lease—Gross (the “Lease”). Snow Stone and Wang failed to comply with this requirement. 

On April 6, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting a breach of contract cause of action against Snow Stone, Wang, and Does 1-50. 

Again, on April 20, 2023, Case Nos. 22PSCV00391 and 23PSCV01012 were deemed related; Case No. 22PSCV00391 was designated as the lead case. 

On September 26, 2023, Wang filed a “Notice of Stay of Proceedings,” advising therein of her Chapter 7 bankruptcy filing. 

A Status Conference Re: Bankruptcy is set for October 24, 2024.

Discussion 

Plaintiff moves the court for an award of attorney fees in the amount of $20,756.00 against Snow Stone and Wang.

The Court intends to continue the hearing on the motion as there is no indication that Snow Stone and Wang have been made aware of the October 24, 2024 hearing date.[FN]

Further, while Plaintiff’s counsel Michael A. Taitelman during the July 23, 2024 Status Conference Re: Bankruptcy that the bankruptcy as to Snow Stone has “closed,” he further represented that the bankruptcy as to Wang “remains ongoing.”

There is no indication on the docket that Wang’s bankruptcy proceeding has concluded; as such, the stay remains in effect. 


[FN] The motion was filed (and electronically served) on January 20, 2023 and originally set for hearing on July 10, 2023. On June 28, 2023, the court filed a “Notice Re: Continuance of Hearing and Order,” wherein the July 10, 2023 hearing was continued to December 18, 2023; notice was given to counsel. On August 4, 2023, Plaintiff filed (and mail-served) a “Notice of Continuance of Hearing on Plaintiff’s Motion for Attorney Fees,” advising therein of the new December 18, 2023 hearing date. On October 3, 2023, the Court filed a “Notice Re: Continuance of Hearing and Order,” wherein the December 18, 2023 hearing was continued to March 25, 2024; notice was given to counsel. On October 11, 2023, Plaintiff filed (and electronically served) a “Notice of Continuance of Hearing on Plaintiff’s Motion for Attorney Fees,” advising therein of the new March 25, 2024 hearing date.

Plaintiff Rebecca Castillo’s Application for Default Judgment is DENIED without prejudice.


Background 

In this case brought under the Unruh Civil Rights Act, Plaintiff Rebecca Castillo (“Plaintiff”), a visually impaired and legally blind person who requires screen-reading software to read website content using a computer, alleges that D.S.A. Sports, Inc. (“Defendant”) has failed to design, construct, maintain, and operate its website to be fully accessible to and independently usable by Plaintiff and other blind or visually impaired people.

On November 15, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting a cause of action against Defendant and Does 1-10 for violations of the Unruh Civil Rights Act, California Civil Code § 51 et seq. 

On February 13, 2024, the Clerk entered default against Defendant. An Order to Show Cause Re: Default Judgment is set for October 24, 2024. 

Discussion 

Plaintiff’s Application for Default Judgment is DENIED without prejudice. The following defects are noted: 

1.       Plaintiff has not provided the Court with any evidence reflecting that the website in question, www.sportsfevercal.com, is operated and/or controlled by Defendant.

2.       Plaintiff has failed to attach any documentary evidence in support of paragraphs 6 through 13 of her declaration. For instance, Plaintiff represents that she encountered “multiple unlabeled or mislabeled buttons and links” and “multiple pages containing insufficient navigational headings” (Plaintiff’s Decl., ¶¶ 7 and 8), but has not provided the Court with any screen shots demonstrating the website’s deficiencies.

Accordingly, the Court denies Plaintiff’s application without prejudice and directs Plaintiff to submit a new default judgment packet that addresses the aforementioned defects.

Galvan v. Allied Professional Nursing Care, Inc., et al., Case No. 24PSCV00062

ORDER ON MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

Counsel for Defendant Karen Powell Gutierrez’s (i.e., Brown & Charbonneau LLP) Motion to be Relieved as Counsel is GRANTED, effective upon the filing of a proof of service showing service of the signed order upon Client at Client’s last known address.

 

Background

Plaintiff Crystal Galvan (“Plaintiff”) alleges as follows:

In February 2023, Plaintiff learned from her sister, Claire Powell, that she owns a 1/3 interest in Allied Professional Nursing Care, Inc. (“Allied”), which she inherited through a divorce settlement between her mother, Karen Powell Gutierrez, and her former stepfather when she was a minor in 2004. On or about December 31, 2004, Gutierrez had asked Plaintiff to sign a document which Gutierrez said stated that Plaintiff was a “shareholder [in Allied] to which there is no value.” Plaintiff, from December 31, 2004 to the present, has never been asked to sign any documents or make any decisions related to Allied. Gutierrez and Powell have received yearly bonuses from Allied while Plaintiff has not received anything. Plaintiff had a forensic accountant conduct an audit of Allied which has revealed serious financial wrongdoings.

On January 5, 2024, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting causes of action against Allied, Karen Powell Gutierrez, Claire Powell, Darren Orvananos, Annie Gutierrez, and Does 1-10 for:

1.       Concealment,

2.       Fraudulent Misrepresentation,

3.       Negligent Misrepresentation,

4.       Money Had and Received,

5.       Conversion,

6.       Breach of Fiduciary Duty,

7.       Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress,

8.       Violation of California Bus. & Prof. Code §§ 17200 et seq. & 17500 et seq.,

9.       Unjust Enrichment, and

10.     Constructive Trust.

Pending before the Court is Brown & Charbonneau LLP’s motion to be relieved as Karen Powell Gutierrez’s counsel.


Discussion

Brown & Charbonneau LLP seeks to be relieved as counsel of record for Karen Powell Gutierrez (the “Client”).

The Court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915; People v. Prince (1968) 268 Cal.App.2d 398.)

The Court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (Cal. Rules Court, rule 3.1362(e).) 

Attorney Mark Higuchi attests that there has been a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship, such that irreconcilable differences have developed between them. Higuchi states that he has personally served Client with copies of the motion papers filed with his declaration.

The motion is not opposed. The Court determines that the requirements of Rules of Court Rule 3.1362 enumerated above have been sufficiently met.

Accordingly, the motion is granted, effective upon the filing of a proof of service showing service of the signed order upon Client at Client’s last known address.  

Case Number: 24PSCV01947    Hearing Date: October 24, 2024    Dept: H

Tatlonghari v. Rushmore Loan Management Services, LLC, et al.,
Case No. 24PSCV01947

 

ORDER ON DEMURRER AND MOTION TO STRIKE

1.       Defendants Rushmore Servicing and U.S. Bank Trust National Association’s (not in its individual capacity, but solely as Trustee of the Truman 2021 SC9 Title Trust) Demurrer to Plaintiffs’ Complaint is SUSTAINED, with 20 days’ leave to amend.

2.       Defendants Rushmore Servicing and U.S. Bank Trust National Association’s (not in its individual capacity, but solely as Trustee of the Truman 2021 SC9 Title Trust) Motion to Strike Portions of Plaintiffs’ Complaint is DENIED as MOOT.


I.        Background

Plaintiffs Virginia Tatlonghari and Ruelito Tatlonghari (together, “Plaintiffs”) allege as follows:

Plaintiffs are the owners of the real property located at 16320 E. Cherry Blossom Street in La Puente (“subject property”). Plaintiffs obtained a mortgage loan on the subject property from J.P. Morgan Chase Bank, N.A. (“Chase”), which was memorialized by a deed of trust recorded April 8, 2008 (the “Deed”). On April 8, 2015, an “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, wherein the Deed was assigned from Chase to Nationstar Mortgage, LLC (“Nationstar”). On April 29, 2019, a second “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, wherein the Deed was assigned from Nationstar to Selene Finance LP (“Selene”). On February 22, 2023, a third “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, wherein the Deed was assigned from Specialized Loan Servicing, LLC (“SLS”) to U.S. Bank Trust National Association, not in its individual capacity but solely as trustee of the Truman 2021 SC9 Title Trust (“U.S. Bank”). On September 12, 2023, a fourth “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, wherein the Deed was assigned from Selene to SLS. On October 3, 2023, a “Notice of Default and Election to Sell” (“Notice of Default”) was recorded. Plaintiffs were never notified of any foreclosure alternatives prior to or after the Notice of Default was recorded. Plaintiffs submitted three loan modification applications and never received any response. On February 13, 2024, a “Notice of Trustee’s Sale” was recorded. Rushmore Loan Management Services, LLC (“Rushmore”) was the purported loan servicer of the mortgage loan. The subject property was sold on May 28, 2024.

On June 17, 2024, Plaintiffs filed a complaint, asserting causes of action against Rushmore, U.S. Bank and Does 1-10 for:

1.       Violation of Civil Code § 2923.5,

2.       Violation of Civil Code § 2924.9,

3.       Wrongful Foreclosure,

4.       Unfair Business Practices, Violation of Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200, et seq., and

5.       Violation of Civ. Code § 2923.6.

Pending before the Court is Rushmore and U.S. Bank’s demurrer and motion to strike portions of the complaint. For the reasons stated herein, the Court sustains the demurrer, with 20 days’ leave to amend, and denies the motion to strike as moot.

 

II.      Legal Standard

A demurrer may be made on the grounds that the pleading, inter alia, does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (e).)

“A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters. Therefore, it lies only where the defects appear on the face of the pleading or are judicially noticed.” (SKF Farms v. Superior Court (1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 902, 905 [citations omitted].) At the pleading stage, a plaintiff need only allege ultimate facts sufficient to apprise the defendant of the factual basis for the claim against him. (Semole v. Sansoucie (1972) 28 Cal. App. 3d 714, 721.) “[A] demurrer does not, however, admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law alleged in the pleading, or the construction placed on an instrument pleaded therein, or facts impossible in law, or allegations contrary to facts of which a court may take judicial knowledge.” (S. Shore Land Co. v. Petersen (1964) 226 Cal.App.2d 725, 732 [citations omitted].)

 

III.     Request for Judicial Notice

A court may take judicial notice of, among other things, “official acts of the legislative, executive, and judicial departments of a state and of facts and propositions that are not reasonably subject to dispute and are capable of immediate and accurate determination by resort to sources of reasonably indisputable accuracy.” People v. Terry, 38 Cal. App. 3d 432, 439, 113 Cal. Rptr. 233, 237 (Ct. App. 1974) [citing Evid. Code § 452]; See also Intengan v. BAC Home Loans Servicing LP (2013) 214 Cal.App.4th 1047, 1054 [court “may take judicial notice of the fact of the existence and legal effect of legally operative documents, such as the identity of the beneficiary designated in the deed of trust, where not subject to reasonable dispute”].) With this standard in mind, the Court grants Defendants’ Request for Judicial Notice (i.e., of the Trustee’s Deed Upon Sale dated June 13, 2024 and recorded June 20, 2024).

 

IV.     Discussion

Rushmore and U.S. Bank (together, “Defendants”) demur, per Code of Civil Procedure section 430.10(e), to the first through fifth causes of action in Plaintiffs’ complaint, on the basis that they each fail to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.

 

First Cause of Action (i.e., Violation of Civil Code § 2923.5)

Civil Code section 2923.5 requires a mortgage servicer to attempt to contact a borrower in person or telephonically in order to assess the borrower’s financial situation and explore options to avoid foreclosure at least 30 days before a notice of default is recorded. (Civ. Code § 2923.5, subd. (a)(2).) If the servicer is unable to make the above contact, it may satisfy the outreach requirements by first sending a letter and, after the letter has been sent, attempting to contact the borrower telephonically at least three times. (Civ. Code § 2923.5, subd. (e)(1) and (2).)

Plaintiffs allege that Defendants “failed to satisfy the requirements of Civ. Code § 2923.5(a)(2) before recording a Notice of Default and violated this statute” (Complaint, ¶ 34); however, they earlier alleged only that they “did not receive any letters from RLMS DEFENDANT” (Id., ¶ 20). As Defendants state, there is no provision in section 2923.5 requiring the servicer to confirm that the letter is received; it only requires that the letter be sent. Because Plaintiffs fail to allege that Defendants made no attempts to contact them telephonically, any argument to that effect made in the opposition is disregarded as improper. Defendants’ demurrer to the first cause of action is sustained on this basis.

 

Second Cause of Action (i.e., Violation of Civil Code § 2924.9)

Plaintiffs allege that Defendants failed to notify them as required by Civil Code section 2924.9. (Complaint, ¶ 38.)

Civil Code section 2924.9 provides that “[u]nless a borrower has previously exhausted the first lien loan modification process offered by, or through, his or her mortgage servicer . . . within five business days after recording a notice of default . . . , a mortgage servicer that offers one or more foreclosure prevention alternatives shall send a written communication to the borrower that includes” the following information: (1) that “the borrower may be evaluated for a foreclosure prevention alternative or, if applicable, foreclosure prevention alternatives”, (2) “[w]hether an application is required to be submitted by the borrower in order to be considered for a foreclosure prevention alternative”, and (3) the “means and process by which a borrower may obtain an application for a foreclosure prevention alternative.” (Civ. Code § 2924.9, subd. (a).) This requirement “shall not apply to entities described in subdivision of Section 2924.18” and “shall apply only to mortgages or deeds of trust described in Section 2924.15.” (Id., subds. (b) & (c).) Mortgage servicers subject to this requirements are liable for economic damages resulting from “material violations” of section 2924.9. (Civ. Code § 2924.12, subd. (b).)

First, Plaintiffs have not alleged that the lien is a first trust deed loan or that the servicer is not an entity under section 2924.18. Next, Plaintiffs have not pled facts reflecting that the purported statutory violation was “material” pursuant to section 2924.12, subdivision (b). “Courts have interpreted the term ‘material’ to refer to whether the alleged violation affected a plaintiff’s loan obligations or the modification process.” (Cornejo v. Ocwen Loan Servicing, LLC (E.D. Cal. 2015) 151 F.Supp.3d 1102, 1113.) Plaintiffs have failed to allege how any such violation affected their loan obligations or the modification process. Defendants’ demurrer to the second cause of action is sustained on this basis.

 

Third Cause of Action (i.e., Wrongful Foreclosure)

“To . . . maintain a wrongful foreclosure claim, a plaintiff must allege that (1) the defendants caused an illegal, fraudulent, or willfully oppressive sale of the property pursuant to a power of sale in a mortgage or deed of trust; (2) the plaintiff suffered prejudice or harm; and (3) the plaintiff tendered the amount of the secured indebtedness or was excused from tendering.” (Chavez v. IndyMac Mortgage Services (2013) 219 Cal.App.4th 1052, 1062.)

Plaintiffs allege that Defendants wrongfully foreclosed on the subject property based upon violations of sections 2923.5 and 2924.9. (Complaint, ¶ 44.) Plaintiffs’ cause of action fails, inasmuch as it is based on the first two causes of action. Defendants’ demurrer to the third cause of action is sustained on this basis.

 

Fourth Cause of Action (i.e., Unfair Business Practices, Violation of Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200, et seq.)

To state a cause of action for unfair business practices, a plaintiff must establish defendant engaged in “unlawful, unfair or fraudulent business act or practice and unfair, deceptive, untrue or misleading advertising.” (Bus. & Prof. Code, § 17200.) This section establishes three types of unfair competition, prohibiting “practices that are either ‘unfair,’ or ‘unlawful,’ or ‘fraudulent.’” (Pastoria v. Nationwide Ins. (2003) 112 Cal.App.4th 1490, 1496.)

 

Plaintiffs have alleged that Defendants violated section 17200 “when they purposely violated Civ. Code §§ 2923.5 and 2924.9.” (Complaint, ¶ 56.) Plaintiffs’ cause of action fails, inasmuch as it is based on the first two causes of action and because they have failed to allege standing.

Defendants’ demurrer to the fourth cause of action is sustained on this basis.

 

Fifth Cause of Action (i.e., Violation of Civ. Code § 2923.6)

Civil Code section 2923.6 prohibits “dual tracking,” that is, a permitting a foreclosure sale to occur while a loan modification application is pending. More  specifically, subdivision (c) provides that “[i]f a borrower submits a complete application for a first lien loan modification offered by, or through, the borrower’s mortgage servicer at least five business days before a scheduled foreclosure sale, a mortgage servicer, mortgagee, trustee, beneficiary, or authorized agent shall not record a notice of default or notice of sale, or conduct a trustee’s sale, while the complete first loan modification application is pending.” A mortgage servicer “shall not record a notice of default or notice of sale or conduct a trustee’s sale until any of the following occurs: (1) The mortgage servicer makes a written determination that the borrower is not eligible for a first lien loan modification, and any appeal period . . . has expired[,] (2) The borrower does not accept an offered first lien loan modification within 14 days of the offer[,] [or] (3) The borrower accepts a written first lien loan modification, but defaults on, or otherwise breaches the borrower’s obligations under, the first lien loan modification.” (Civ. Code § 2923.6.)

Here, Plaintiffs do not allege that any of their loan modification applications were deemed complete or that a complete application was submitted prior to the recording of the Notice of Default or Notice of Sale or prior to the date of the trustee’s sale. Defendants’ demurrer is sustained on this basis.

 

V.       Conclusion

The Court sustains the demurrer as to all five causes of action. Plaintiff is granted 20 days’ leave to amend. (City of Stockton v. Superior Ct., 42 Cal. 4th 730, 747, 171 P.3d 20, 32 (2007) [“[L]eave to amend is liberally allowed as a matter of fairness, unless the complaint shows on its face that it is incapable of amendment.”].)

In light of the ruling on the demurrer, the Court denies the motion to strike as moot.

Case Number: 24PSCV02666    Hearing Date: October 24, 2024    Dept: H

Leonard v. Queen of the Valley, Case No. 24PSCV02666

ORDER ON DEFENDANT’S DEMURRER

Defendant Emanate Health Medical Center dba Emanate Health Queen of the Valley Hospital’s (sued and served as Queen of the Valley) Demurrer to Plaintiff’s First Amended Complaint is SUSTAINED, with 30 days’ leave to amend.


Background

Plaintiff Lisa Leonard (“Plaintiff”) alleges that her mother died in connection with receiving poor care at a medical facility.

On August 20, 2024, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint (the “Operative Complaint” or “FAC”) against Emanate Health Medical Center dba Emanate Health Queen of the Valley Hospital’s (sued and served as Queen of the Valley) (“Defendant”) and “Does 1 to 1 or who company brings.”

Pending before the Court is Defendant’s demurrer to the Operative Complaint. For the reasons stated herein, the demurrer is sustained, with 30 days’ leave to amend.


Legal Standard

A demurrer may be made on the grounds that the pleading, inter alia, does not state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10, subd. (e).)

“A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters. Therefore, it lies only where the defects appear on the face of the pleading or are judicially noticed.” (SKF Farms v. Superior Court (1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 902, 905 [citations omitted].) At the pleading stage, a plaintiff need only allege ultimate facts sufficient to apprise the defendant of the factual basis for the claim against him. (Semole v. Sansoucie (1972) 28 Cal.App.3d 714, 721.) “[A] demurrer does not, however, admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law alleged in the pleading, or the construction placed on an instrument pleaded therein, or facts impossible in law, or allegations contrary to facts of which a court may take judicial knowledge.” (S. Shore Land Co. v. Petersen (1964) 226 Cal.App.2d 725, 732 [citations omitted].)

“A general demurrer based on the statute of limitations is only permissible where the dates alleged in the complaint show that the action is barred by the statute of limitations. The running of the statute must appear ‘clearly and affirmatively’ from the dates alleged. It is not sufficient that the complaint might be barred.” (Roman v. County of Los Angeles (2000) 85 Cal.App.4th 316, 324 [citation omitted].)

 

Discussion

Defendant demurs, per Code of Civil Procedure section 430.10, to the Operative Complaint, on the basis that it fails to state facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action. Specifically, Defendant contends the action is time-barred. The Court agrees, based on the allegations pled.

On August 20, 2024, Plaintiff filed as the Operative Complaint a 2-page Judicial Council Form PLD-C-001, wherein she purported to state causes of action for “Breach of Contract” and “Other.” Plaintiff failed to attach any causes of action, as stated in Paragraph 8 of the Operative Complaint, and has failed to identify or attach any contract. A review of the Operative Complaint reflects that Plaintiff has brought this lawsuit against Defendant, described in Paragraph 7 as a “medical provider,” for her mother’s passing “from connection of poor care.” (Id., ¶ 5; see also, ¶ 8 [“Mother passed away from wrongdoers. . .There are things contributed that led to her death. I also noticed different diagnosis than what she went in for on the death certificate of 7/3/23. • Negligence. Breached duty of care. • Waited long hours after notifying Q.O.T.V. that my mother needed care/treatment right away, and that she had 2 strokes previously. Asked for her to have IV fluids and oxygen right away. • Had third stroke after admitted. • Fell out of bed in their care, body was swollen, then not able to walk causing to go to rehab for care with cost. • Incorrect records, didn’t see notes of what happened”].) Based on these allegations, Plaintiff’s complaint sounds in wrongful death stemming from medical negligence.

Code of Civil Procedure section 340.5 states, in relevant part, that “[i]n an action for injury or death against a health care provider based upon such person’s alleged professional negligence, the time for the commencement of action shall be three years after the date of injury or one year after the plaintiff discovers, or through the use of reasonable diligence should have discovered, the injury, whichever occurs first.”  

Here, Plaintiff makes reference to her mother’s “death certificate of 7/3/23,” yet Plaintiff did not file her original complaint until over one year later on August 16, 2024. Although Plaintiff alleges that she “gave [an] intent to sue letter” (FAC, ¶ 5), presumably pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 364, there is no indication when the intent to sue letter was served and thus no way for the Court to determine if the purported notice tolled the statute of limitations. The Court sustains Defendant’s demurrer on this basis, but will allow Plaintiff to provide the foregoing information.

Defendant further asserts that the Operative Complaint fails to plead a cause of action for medical negligence. The Court agrees. “The elements of [a] medical negligence cause of action allege (1) the duty of the professional to use such skill, prudence, and diligence as other members of his profession commonly possess and exercise; (2) a breach of that duty; (3) a proximate causal connection between the negligent conduct and the resulting injury; and (4) actual loss or damage resulting from the professional's negligence.” (Belfiore-Braman v. Rotenberg (2018) 25 Cal.App.5th 234, 238, fn. 3 [quotations and citation omitted].) Plaintiff has not alleged an act or omission by Defendant that breached any duty of care owed to Plaintiff and/or her mother. Plaintiff has not pled causation. The Court sustains Defendant’s demurrer on this basis. Plaintiff is granted 30 days’ leave to amend the Operative Complaint.
