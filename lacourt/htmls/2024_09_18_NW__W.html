DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23VECV04206    Hearing Date: September 18, 2024    Dept: W

NATHANIEL JOSUE RODRIGEZ, et al. vs MOHAMMAD HOSSEIN SHAHBIKI

 

defendant’s motions to compel initial discovery

 

Date of Hearing:        September 18, 2024                         Trial Date:       None set.

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        23VECV04206

 

Moving Party:             Defendant Mohammad Hossein Shahbiki

Responding Party:     No opposition  

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action arises out of a motor vehicle collision. On September 25, 2023, Plaintiff Nathaniel Josue Rodrigez and Amanda Marie Arce filed a complaint against Mohammad Hossein Shahbiki for motor vehicle negligence.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant Mohammad Hossein Shahbiki’s Motion to Compel Plaintiffs’ Responses and Production of Documents in Response to Request for Production of Documents (Set No. One) is GRANTED

 

Defendant Mohammad Hossein Shahbiki’s Motion to Compel Plaintiffs’ Responses to Form Interrogatories (Set One) and Special Interrogatories (Set One) is GRANTED

 

DISCUSSION

 

Defendant Mohammad Hossein Shahbiki move to compel Plaintiffs Nathaniel Josue Rodrigez and Amanda Marie Arce to provide responses and documents, without objection, to Defendant’s Request for Production of Documents (Set One) as well as provide responses, without objection, to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories (Set One) and Special Interrogatories (Set One). Defendant also seeks monetary sanctions against Plaintiffs and Plaintiffs’ counsel, Onyinye N. Anyama, Esq. of Anyama Law Firm, APC, jointly and severally.

 

Where a party fails to serve timely responses to discovery requests, the court may make an order compelling responses. (CCP §§ 2030.290, 2031.300, 2033.280; Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 403.) A party that fails to serve a timely response to the discovery request waives any objection to the request, including one based on privilege or the protection of attorney work product. (CCP §§2030.290(a), 2031.300(a), 2033.280(a); Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc., supra, 148 Cal.App.4th at 404.) Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.010, the propounding party may also move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted, as well as for a monetary sanction. (CCP § 2033.280(b).)

 

On February 21, 2024, Defendant propounded discovery including Request for Production of Documents, Set One, Form Interrogatories, Set One, and Special Interrogatories, Set One. (Moreno Decl. ¶1, Exh. A.) Plaintiffs' deadline to respond was March 25, 2024. (Moreno Decl. ¶2.) Plaintiffs did not ask for an extension of time to respond and no extension was granted. (Moreno Decl. ¶3.) No responses were served by the deadline. (Moreno Decl. ¶4.) Defendant did eventually receive responses to the Request for Production on April 22, 2024; however, they were unverified and boilerplate objections. (Moreno Decl. ¶5, Exh. B.) Plaintiffs stated they would produce the documents by May 29, 2024, but never did so. (Moreno Decl. ¶5, Exhs. C, D.) As for the Special Interrogatories, Plaintiffs again served unverified responses with boilerplate objections. (Moreno Decl. ¶6, Exhs. C, D.) Plaintiffs never served any responses to the Form Interrogatories. (Moreno Decl. ¶5.) Defense counsel attempted to meet and confer with Plaintiffs’ counsel regarding the deficient responses. (Moreno Decl. ¶8.) However, no additional responses have been received. (Moreno Decl. ¶11.)

 

No opposition has been filed. The court agrees the unverified responses are tantamount to no responses at all and as to the objections served, those had been waived by Plaintiffs’ failure to timely serve responses.

 

Based on the foregoing, Defendant Shahbiki’s Motion to Compel Plaintiffs’ Responses and Production of Documents in Response to Request for Production of Documents (Set No. One) and Motion to Compel Plaintiffs’ Responses to Form Interrogatories (Set One) and Special Interrogatories (Set One) are GRANTED.

 

Plaintiffs are ordered to produce responses, without objection, within 30 days.

 

Sanctions

 

Defendant seeks $1,380.00 for their motion to compel production of documents and $2,179.00 for their motion to compel interrogatories against Plaintiffs and their counsel, jointly and severally. If a propounding party moves for and obtains a court order compelling a response, the court shall impose monetary sanctions against the party failing to timely respond to interrogatories and demands for inspection unless that party acted with substantial justification or the sanction would otherwise be unjust. (CCP §§ 2030.290(c), 2031.300(c), 2033.280(c); Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc., supra, 148 Cal.App.4th at 404.)

 

The court GRANTS the request for attorney fees in the reduced amount of $1,909.00, total ($598 for the first motion, $1,311 for the second motion). No opposition was filed and the court finds only one appearance was necessary for the motions. Moreover, the declarations and memoranda were nearly identical.  Sanctions are awarded against plaintiff and counsel, jointly and severally, to be paid within 30 days.


Case Number: 24VECV03787    Hearing Date: September 18, 2024    Dept: W

6701 MATILIJA AVE LLC V. TODD EDWARDS, et al.

 

demurrer to the complaint

 

Date of Hearing:        September 18, 2024                         Trial Date:       None Set  

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        24VECV03787

 

Moving Party:            Defendants Todd Edwards and Rae Edwards

Responding Party:     No opposition

 

BACKGROUND

 

This is an unlawful detainer action. On August 8, 2024, Plaintiff 6701 Matilija Ave LLC filed a complaint against Defendants Todd Edwards and Rae Edwards. Plaintiff allegedly served a three-day notice to pay rent or quit.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendants Todd Edwards and Rae Edwards’s Demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITH LEAVE TO AMEND.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Defendants Todd Edwards and Rae Edwards demur to Plaintiff’s complaint on the grounds Plaintiff fails to allege proper service of the notice to quit and either compliance or exemption from the Los Angeles Just Cause Ordinance.

 

Defendants first argue the complaint does not allege proper service. Specifically, Defendants contend if the tenant is not available to be personally served with the 3-day notice, substituted service may be made if “he or she is absent from the commercial rental property” or “at the time of attempted service, a person of suitable age or discretion is not found at the rental property through the exercise of reasonable diligence”. (CCP § 1162(b)(2), (3).) Here, however, the allegation of service is via posting only. Accordingly, because the only allegation of service is deficient, there is no cause of action for unlawful detainer.

 

The court agrees, as alleged, it is unclear whether Plaintiffs first properly attempted personal service or leaving the Notice with someone else who is of appropriate age before posting the copy. (See CCP §1162(b).) Section 1162 requires to attempted personal and substitute service prior to posting and mailing the Notice.

 

Next, Defendants argue Plaintiff has failed to allege that Defendants’ tenancy is subject to the applicable City and State Eviction Control Protections. “To plead a valid unlawful detainer claim, the complaint must allege that the tenancy was terminated for ‘just cause.’” (Attenello v. Basilious (2022) 88 Cal.App.5th Supp. 1.) Defendants contend the property at issue in this case is located in the City and County of Los Angeles and thus, Plaintiff must allege compliance with City of Los Angeles Just Cause Ordinance or allege a specific exemption. Plaintiff fails to do so.

 

The court disagrees. Section 7 of the complaint alleges the tenancy is not subject to Civil Code section 1946.2, the statewide rent control and just cause eviction statute that went into effect in 2020, and states it is exempt Section 1946.2(e)(8)(A).

 

Because the complaint fails to allege proper service, the court SUSTAINS the demurrer with leave to amend.
