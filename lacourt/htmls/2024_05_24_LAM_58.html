DEPARTMENT 58 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 19STCV24892    Hearing Date: May 24, 2024    Dept: 58

Judge Bruce Iwasaki

Department 58

Hearing Date:             May 24, 2024

Case Name:                 Rebecca West, et al. v. Eduardo Thackeray, et al.

Case No.:                    19STCV24892

Motion:                       Demurrer to First Amended Complaint

Moving Party:             Defendant Eduardo Thackeray

Responding Party:      Plaintiffs Rebecca West and Luca Giorgetti

 

Tentative Ruling:      Defendant Thackeray’s Demurrer to the First Amended Complaint is overruled. 

 

 

Background

 

            Plaintiffs Rebecca West and Luca Giorgetti purchased property located at 1217 Daniels Street in Los Angeles, CA from Defendant Eduardo Thackeray, the seller.  Plaintiffs purchased the home based on the misrepresentation of Defendant Belen Palacios, the real estate agent showing the house.  Plaintiffs allege Defendant Palacios misrepresented to them that the home had fully approved plans for a 1,050 square foot addition to the property.  After purchasing the home, Plaintiffs allege they discovered that Defendant Thackeray had revoked those plans before he put the property on the market and the plans could not be reinstated. 

 

            Plaintiffs filed this action on July 16, 2019.  The operative complaint is the First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) filed on January 17, 2020.  The FAC alleges (1) intentional misrepresentation; (2) negligent misrepresentation; (3) breach of fiduciary duty; and (4) breach of implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing. 

 

            On August 20, 2020, the Court granted Plaintiff West’s motion to compel arbitration of the claims against Defendant Thackeray.  On December 5, 2023, the Court granted Plaintiffs motion to confirm the arbitration award they obtained against Defendant Thackeray.  On January 19, 2024, the Court entered judgment on the arbitration award in favor of Plaintiff West and against Defendant Thackeray in the amount of $466,539.86. 

 

            On February 27, 2024, Plaintiffs filed a dismissal without prejudice as to Defendant Alex Griswold.  As such, the only claims in the FAC that remain pending are (1) West and Giorgetti’s claims Defendants Belen Palacios and Rodeo Realty and (2) Giorgetti’s claims against Thackeray. 

 

Demurrer

 

            A demurrer is an objection to a pleading, the grounds for which are apparent from either the face of the complaint or a matter of which the court may take judicial notice. (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.30, subd. (a); see also Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318.) The purpose of a demurrer is to challenge the sufficiency of a pleading “by raising questions of law.” (Postley v. Harvey (1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 280, 286.) “In the construction of a pleading, for the purpose of determining its effect, its allegations must be liberally construed, with a view to substantial justice between the parties.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 452.) The court “ ‘ “treat[s] the demurrer as admitting all material facts properly pleaded, but not contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law . . ..” ’ ” (Berkley v. Dowds (2007) 152 Cal.App.4th 518, 525.) 

 

Request for Judicial Notice

 

            Evidence Code §451 provides:

 

“Judicial notice shall be taken of the following:

 

(a) The decisional, constitutional, and public statutory law of this state and of the United States and the provisions of any charter described in Section 3, 4, or 5 of Article XI of the California Constitution.

 

(b) Any matter made a subject of judicial notice by Section 11343.6, 11344.6, or 18576 of the Government Code or by Section 1507 of Title 44 of the United States Code.

 

(c) Rules of professional conduct for members of the bar adopted pursuant to Section 6076 of the Business and Professions Code and rules of practice and procedure for the courts of this state adopted by the Judicial Council.

 

(d) Rules of pleading, practice, and procedure prescribed by the United States Supreme Court, such as the Rules of the United States Supreme Court, the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, the Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure, the Admiralty Rules, the Rules of the Court of Claims, the Rules of the Customs Court, and the General Orders and Forms in Bankruptcy.

 

(e) The true signification of all English words and phrases and of all legal expressions.

 

(f) Facts and propositions of generalized knowledge that are so universally known that they cannot reasonably be the subject of dispute.”

 

            Defendant’s Request for Judicial Notice pursuant to Evidence Code §451(a) is DENIED. Defendant asks that the Court judicially notice the ADR arbitrator’s order granting in part Defendant’s Motion for Summary Adjudication and the Final Arbitration Award.  Neither of these documents falls within any of the categories of judicially noticeable documents that must be judicially noticed under Evidence Code §451(a). 

 

Analysis

 

            Defendant Thackeray demurs to the FAC on grounds of res judicata (claim preclusion) and collateral estoppel (issue preclusion).  Defendant argues the arbitration between himself and Plaintiff West resolved all claims alleged against him in the FAC.  Defendant argues his demurrer should therefore be sustained without leave to amend and all claims against him should be dismissed.

 

            On January 19, 2024, the arbitration award in favor of West and against Thackeray was confirmed and entered as a judgment against Thackeray.  (Judgment filed on January 19, 2024.)  No judgment has been entered on Giorgetti’s claims, nor is Giorgetti a claimant in the arbitration award.  Counsel for both West and Giorgetti contends that the entry of Judgment against Thackeray resolved all claims against him by those parties.

 

            Thackeray’s demurrer is overruled on both procedural and substantive grounds.  Procedurally, the demurrer is extremely untimely.  Thackeray had 30 days after service of the FAC to file a demurrer. (Code Civ. Proc., §430.40(a).)    The FAC was filed on January 17, 2020 but the docket does not reflect when the FAC was served. However, Thackeray filed an opposition to Plaintiffs’ Motion to Compel Arbitration on August 7, 2020.  The time to demur to the FAC has long passed. 

 

            Substantively, the Judgment was entered in West’s favor against Thackeray on the claims alleged in the FAC.  The judgment therefore establishes that the FAC states a cause of action against him. 

 

            In addition, Thackeray’s demurrer rests entirely on his request for judicial notice.  Thackeray’s request for judicial is denied.  The arbitrator’s Final Award and Order granting in part and denying in part Thackeray’s Motion for Summary Adjudication therefore cannot be considered on demurrer.  A demurrer tests the pleadings alone and not the evidence or other extrinsic matters.  Therefore, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or via proper judicial notice.  (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 430.30, 430.70; Donabedian v. Mercury Ins. Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994.)

 

            Defendant Thackeray’s demurrer is overruled.
