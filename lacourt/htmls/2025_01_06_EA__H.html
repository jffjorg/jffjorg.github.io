DEPARTMENT H LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23PSCV02395    Hearing Date: January 6, 2025    Dept: H

Rizo v. 555 Vinton Avenue Land, LLC, et al., Case No. 23PSCV02395

TENTATIVE RULING ON MOTION TO COMPEL DEPOSITION OF DEFENDANTS’ PERSON MOST KNOWLEDGEABLE

 

While the parties have now come to agreement as to a deposition date, it is unclear to the Court whether the parties have resolved their differences with respect to the categories of knowledge and document production requests set forth in Plaintiff’s various notices of deposition, to which Defendants interposed objections. The Court will hear further from counsel at the time of the hearing as to the status of the document production requests and objections and as to whether a continuance of the hearing date is necessary. As to the issue of sanctions, the Court is inclined to order Defendants to reimburse Plaintiff in the total amount of $1,391.94 ($731.94 for court reporter fees, 1.5 hours in attorney fees spent preparing the instant motion at $400.00/hour, and the $60.00 filing fee).

 

 

Background

Plaintiff Ernesto Rizo (“Plaintiff”) alleges that he sustained injuries in an August 30, 2021 slip and fall near one of the gas pumps at 555 Vinton Avenue in Pomona (the “premises”).

On August 7, 2023, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting causes of action against 555 Vinton Avenue Land, LLC, 555 Vinton, LLC, Chevron U.S.A., Inc. (Chevron”), and Does 1-20 for (1) general negligence and (2) premises liability.

On November 29, 2023, Plaintiff dismissed Chevron, without prejudice.

A Post/Mediation Status Conference/Case Management Conference is set for March 19, 2025.

Pending before the Court is Plaintiff’s motion to compel 555 Vinton Avenue Land, LLC and 555 Vinton, LLC dba Chevron U.S.A., Inc. (together, “Defendants”) to produce a Person Most Knowledgeable (“PMK”) for deposition.


 

Legal Standard

“If the deponent named is not a natural person, the deposition notice shall describe with reasonable particularity the matters on which examination is requested. In that event, the deponent shall designate and produce at the deposition those of its officers, directors, managing agents, employees, or agents who are most qualified to testify on its behalf as to those matters to the extent of any information known or reasonably available to the deponent.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.230).

“If, after service of a deposition notice, . . . a person designated by an organization that is a party under Section 2025.230, without having served a valid objection. . ., fails to appear for examination, or to proceed with it, or to produce for inspection any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice, the party giving the notice may move for an order compelling the deponent’s attendance and testimony, and the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (a).)

“A motion under subdivision (a) shall comply with both of the following:

(1) The motion shall set forth specific facts showing good cause justifying the production for inspection of any document, electronically stored information, or tangible thing described in the deposition notice.

(2) The motion shall be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration. . ., or, when the deponent fails to attend the deposition and produce the documents, electronically stored information, or things described in the deposition notice, by a declaration stating that the petitioner has contacted the deponent to inquire about the nonappearance.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (b).)

“If a motion under subdivision (a) is granted, the court shall impose a monetary sanction. . ., unless the court finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2025.450, subd. (g)(1).)

Any motion involving the content of a discovery request or the responses to such a request must be accompanied by a separate statement providing all information necessary to understand each discovery request and all the responses at issue. (California Rules of Court, rule 3.1345(a), (c).)

 

Discussion

Plaintiff moves the Court for an order compelling Defendants’ PMK to appear and provide testimony pursuant to the “Amended Notice of Deposition of Defendants’ Person Most Knowledgeable” served September 24, 2024. Plaintiff also seeks an award of $2,791.94 against Defendants and/or their counsel of record.


Procedural Deficiencies

At the outset, the Court notes that Plaintiff’s motion is not accompanied by a California Rules of Court rule 3.1345 separate statement.

 

Merits

Plaintiff’s counsel Victor Cruz (“Cruz”) represents, and/or the accompanying exhibits reflect, as follows:

          On June 26, 2024, Plaintiff served Defendants with a “Notice of Taking Deposition of Defendants’ Person(s) Most Qualified and Request for Production of Documents” (“Notice”), setting the deposition for July 10, 2024. (Cruz Decl., ¶ 2, Exh. A.) On July 2, 2024, Defendants served an objection to the Notice. (Id., ¶ 3, Exh. B.)

          On July 3, 2024, Cruz emailed Defendants’ counsel, requesting available dates for deposition in mid/late August. (Id., ¶ 4, Exh. C.) On August 2, 2024, having not heard back, Cruz sent a follow-up email to Defendants’ counsel, requesting available dates for deposition in September. (Id.) On September 14, 2024, Cruz emailed Defendants’ counsel again with the following proposed dates for the deposition: October 2, 3, 7, or 9. (Id.) Cruz asked Defendants’ counsel for alternate dates if the above dates did not work. (Id.) On September 19, 2024, Cruz emailed Defendants’ counsel and advised that if he did not receive a response to his prior email by September 23, he would schedule the deposition for October 9. (Id.)

          Cruz did not receive a response from Defendants’ counsel; as such, on September 24, 2024, he served an “Amended Notice of Taking Deposition of Defendants’ Person(s) Most Qualified and Request for Production of Documents” (“Amended Notice”) for October 9, 2024. (Id., ¶ 5, Exh. D.) On October 2, 2024, Defendants served an objection to the Amended Notice. (Id., ¶ 6, Exh. E.) On October 4, 2024, Cruz emailed Defendants’ counsel and advised that he was leaving the October 9, 2024 deposition on calendar and would take a “Certificate of Non-Appearance,” if needed; he did not receive a response. (Id., ¶¶ 7 and 8, Exhs. F and G.) On October 9, 2024, Cruz took a “Certificate of Non-Appearance.” (Id., ¶ 8, Exh. G.)

Defendants’ counsel Erika Stecker (“Stecker”), in response, concedes that Plaintiff has been diligent in attempting to take Defendants’ PMK deposition, but represents that Defendants had difficulties in identifying and ultimately contacting the appropriate PMK to produce for deposition. (Stecker Decl., ¶¶ 3 and 4.)  Stecker represents that Defendants’ difficulties were compounded by the PMK’s international travel. (Id., ¶ 5.) Stecker advises that Plaintiff’s counsel has since been provided with available dates (namely, January 14, 16, 17, 21, and 22). (Id., ¶ 5, Exh. 1.)

Cruz, in reply, advises that Plaintiff has now noticed the deposition for January 16, one of the dates proposed by Stecker, but requests that the Court order that the deposition occur on this date, given the history of this matter. (Cruz Reply Decl., ¶¶ 2 and 3, Exhs. A and B.) Cruz reasserts Plaintiff’s request for sanctions of $2,791.94.

It appears to the Court that the parties have resolved the date of the PMK deposition. However, it is unclear to the Court whether or not the parties have resolved their differences with respect to the categories of knowledge and document production requests set forth in Plaintiff’s various notices of deposition, to which Defendants interposed objections. Plaintiff did not file a separate statement with the instant motion, nor did he identify any meet-and-confer efforts made with respect to these objections. The parties’ briefing, moreover, is devoid of any discussion regarding the document production requests and objections. The Court will hear further from counsel at the time of the hearing in this regard.

As to the issue of sanctions, the Court is inclined to order Defendants to reimburse Plaintiff in the amount of $731.94 for court reporter fees, plus 1.5 hours in attorney fees at $400.00/hour preparing the instant motion and plus the $60.00 filing fee, for a total of $1,391.94. 

Case Number: 24PSCV00578    Hearing Date: January 6, 2025    Dept: H

Fess v. PHH Mortgage Corporation, et al., Case No. 24PSCV00578

ORDER ON MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

Counsel for Plaintiff Gregory Fess as Administrator of the Estate of Barbara Fess’ (i.e., CDLG, PC) Motion to be Relieved as Counsel is GRANTED, effective upon the filing of a proof of service showing service of the final order upon the Client at the Client’s last known address.  The Court will set an Order to Show Cause Re: Representation of Administrator for February 18, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. and will continue the Case Management Conference to this same date.

 

Background

Plaintiff Gregory Fess as Administrator of the Estate of Barbara Fess (“Plaintiff”) alleges as follows:

          Plaintiff is the owner of the property located at 226 Shay Avenue in La Puente (the “subject property”). The subject property is Plaintiff’s personal residence. Plaintiff obtained a mortgage loan on the subject property from Financial Freedom Senior Funding Corporation (“Financial Freedom”), which was memorialized by a deed of trust recorded on September 5, 2006. On October 1, 2009, a “Corporate Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, in which Financial Freedom transferred its rights to Mortgage Electronic Registration Systems, Inc. (“MERS”), as nominee for Financial Freedom. On September 22, 2015, an “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, in which MERS transferred its rights to CIT Bank, N.A. (“CIT”).

          On September 26, 2019, an “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, in which CIT transferred its rights to Bank of New York Mellon Trust Company, N.A. as Trustee for Mortgage Assets Management Series I Trust (“Bank of New York”). On November 15, 2021, a “Notice of Default and Election to Sell Under Deed of Trust” was recorded. On October 24, 2023, a “Notice of Trustee’s Sale” was recorded. On December 2, 2023, another “Notice of Trustee’s Sale” was recorded, which showed the beneficiary as U.S. Bank, Successor to Lasalle Bank National Association As Indenture Trustee Under The Transfer and Administration Agreement Dated February 7, 2007 for the Mortgage Equity Conversion Asset Trust 2007-FF3 (“U.S. Bank”). On December 28, 2023, an “Assignment of Deed of Trust” was recorded, in which Bank of New York transferred its rights to U.S. Bank. The subject property was sold on January 4, 2024. The Notice of Default and Notice of Trustee’s Sale are void as the beneficiary was not the entity that did the foreclosure. 

On February 26, 2024, Plaintiff filed a complaint, asserting causes of action against PHH Mortgage Corporation (“PHH”), Bank of New York, and Does 1-10 for: (1) violation of Civ. Code § 2923.5, (2) violation of Civ. Code § 2924.9; (3) wrongful Foreclosure; (4) unfair business practices, in violation of Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200, et seq. and (5) cancellation of written instruments, Civ. Code § 3412. 

Pending before the Court is CDLG, PC’s motion to be relieved as counsel of record for Plaintiff.

 

Discussion

CDLG, PC (the “Firm”) seeks to be relieved as counsel of record for Plaintiff (the “Client”). 

The Court has discretion to allow an attorney to withdraw, and such a motion should be granted provided that there is no prejudice to the client and it does not disrupt the orderly process of justice. (See Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915; People v. Prince (1968) 268 Cal.App.2d 398.) 

California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362 requires (1) a notice of motion and motion directed to the client (made on the Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-051)); (2) a declaration stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure § 284(2) is brought instead of filing a consent under section 284(1) (made on the Declaration in Support of Attorney's Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-052)); (3) service of the notice of motion and motion, the declaration, and the proposed order on the client and on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and (4) a proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on the Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil form (MC-053)). The court may delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client has been filed with the court. (Cal. Rules Court, rule 3.1362(e).) 

Attorney Tony Cara (“Cara”) attests that the “[a]ttorney client relationship has been compromised and a withdrawal of representation is necessary. . .” Cara states that he served the Client by mail at the Client’s last known address with copies of the motion papers served with his declaration and that he has confirmed within the past 30 days that the address is current, by mail, return receipt requested. 

The
motion is not opposed. The Court determines that the requirements of Rules of Court
Rule 3.1362 enumerated above have been sufficiently met. 

Accordingly, the motion is granted, effective upon the filing of a proof of service showing serving of the final order upon the Client at the Client’s last known address. 

The Court notes that Plaintiff has sued in his capacity as administrator of the Estate of Barbara Fess[FN]; accordingly, the Court will set an Order to Show Cause Re: Representation of Administrator for February 18, 2025, at 8:30 a.m. The Court continues the Case Management Conference to this same date and time (February 18, 2025, at 8:30 a.m.)

    

[FN] “A person who is unlicensed to practice law and who represents a decedent’s estate cannot appear in propria persona on behalf of the estate in matters outside the probate proceedings.” (Hansen v. Hansen (2003) 114 Cal.App.4th 618, 621). “’Personal representative’ means executor, administrator, administrator with will annexed, special administrator, successor personal representative, public administrator acting pursuant to Section 7660, or a person who performs substantially the same function under the law of another jurisdiction governing the person’s status.” (Prob. Code § 58(a).)
