Case Number: 24TRCV00625    Hearing Date: December 4, 2024    Dept: B

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Southwest District

Torrance Dept. B

 

JOSELYN L. MARQUEZ, an individual,

 

 

 

Plaintiff,

 

Case No.:

 

 

24TRCV00625

 

vs.

 

 

[Tentative] RULING

 

 

ROLLINS, INC, a corporation; WHEELS LT, an out of State Association; ADONNIS ALEXANDER ANDRADE, an individual; and DOES 1 to 30, inclusive

 

 

 

Defendants.

 

 

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Sanctions

 

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Sanctions

 

Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Admissions, Set One and Request for Sanctions

 

 

 

 

Hearing Date:                          December 4, 2024

 

Moving Parties:                      Plaintiff Joselyn L. Marquez

Responding Party:                  None

(1) Motion to Compel Defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade’s Further Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Sanctions

(2) Motion to Compel Defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade’s Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One and Request for Sanctions

(3) Motion to Compel Defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade’s Further Responses to Request for Admissions, Set One and Request for Sanctions

            The court considered the moving papers.  No opposition or reply papers were filed.

RULING

            The motions are GRANTED.  Sanctions are GRANTED against defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade, and his counsel of record, in the total amount of $1,780.00.  Sanctions are payable within 30 days to counsel for moving party.

BACKGROUND

            On February 22, 2024, plaintiff Joselyn L. Marquez filed a complaint against defendants Rollins, Inc., Wheels LT, and Adonnis Alexander Andrade for (1) negligence, (2) negligent hiring, supervision, retention, and (3) negligent entrustment.  Plaintiff alleges that defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade, operating a vehicle owned by defendant Orkin Services of California (erroneously sued as Rollins, Inc.), collided into plaintiff’s vehicle causing plaintiff to sustain severe injuries.

            On April 19, 2024, defendants Orkin Services of California (erroneously sued as Rollins, Inc.) and Adonis Alexander Andrade filed an answer to the complaint.

            These Motions all involve a significant failure of counsel for defendant to attempt in good faith to comply with defendant’s discovery obligations.  The uniform response provided by counsel demonstrates a meaningful misunderstanding of the obligation to comply with the discovery statutes. To all discovery the same response was given.

 

Defendants Response to Form Interrogatories Nos. 1.1 - 2.10; 3.1 - 4.2; 7.1 - 7.3; 12.2 - 12.7; 17.1; and 20.1 - 20.11

Defendants Response to Special Interrogatories, Set One, Nos. 1 through 32

Defendants Response to Request for Admissions Nos. 1-14:

“Objection.  Defense counsel submits these objections in good faith and in the spirit of complying with the Professional Rules of Conduct and Guidelines for Civility in Litigation.  Professional courtesies have been extended previously and the parties have been able to resolve issues in the past.  Defense counsel remains willing to cooperate so that substantive responses can be provided.  However, due to unforeseen circumstances additional time is necessary to complete the meet and confer process to allow Responding Party to provide substantive responses.  At this time, out of an abundance of caution to preserve appropriate objections, this responding party objects as follows:  The request is vague, ambiguous, overbroad, burdensome, harassing, unintelligible, and shotgun in nature.  Responding party further objects to the request in that it is not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence, irrelevant to the subject matter of the action, and therefore a misuse of discovery.  The request lacks foundation and calls for a legal conclusion.  This request seeks information gathered in anticipation of litigation and information protected by the attorney-client and attorney-work product privileges, including violating the authorities of Nacht & Lewis Architects, Inc. v. Super Ct. (McCormick) (1996) 47 Cal.App.4th 215 and Soltani-Rastegar v. Super. Ct. (Brinzo) (1989) 208 Cal.App.3d 424, 429.  This request is also objected to in that it seeks the premature disclosure of expert opinions or witness information and seeks information that violates the privacy rights of responding party and/or third-parties.”

            The Court does not find that this boilerplate response to all the discovery is an acceptable response nor is it given “in good faith.”

 

LEGAL AUTHORITY

            Timeliness

A motion to compel further discovery responses must be filed and served within 45 days after the initial verified response or supplemental verified response was personally served or by any specific later date that the discovering party agreed to in writing.  CCP §§ 2030.300(c), 2031.310(c), 2033.290(c); see Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 403.  Extra time is added to the 45 days if the response was served by a method other than personal delivery.  See CCP §§ 1010.6(a)(3)(B), 1013(c), (e), 2016.050.

            Meet and Confer

A motion to compel further interrogatory responses or further admission responses must be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration under CCP § 2016.040.  CCP §§ 2030.300(b)(1), 2033.290(b)(1).  A meet and confer declaration in support of a motion shall state facts showing a reasonable and good faith attempt at an informal resolution of each issue presented by the motion.  CCP § 2016.040.

Separate Statement

A motion to compel further responses to interrogatories, demands to produce, or requests for admissions must be accompanied by a separate statement unless (1) no response has been provided to the discovery request or (2) the court has allowed the moving party to submit a concise outline of the discovery request and each response in dispute in place of the separate statement.  Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345(a)(1); see CCP §§ 2030.300(b)(2), 2031.310(b)(3), 2033.290(b)(2).

Interrogatories

A motion to compel a further response is used when a party gives unsatisfactory answers or makes untenable objections to interrogatories, demands to produce, or requests for admission.  See CCP §§ 2030.300(a), 2031.310(a), 2033.290(a); Sinaiko, supra, 148 Cal.App.4th at 403 [interrogatories and demands to produce].

To compel a further response to interrogatories, the movant can show that: (1) the responding party’s answer to a particular interrogatory is evasive or incomplete; (2) the responding party’s exercise of the option to produce documents in response to an interrogatory was unwarranted or the required specification of those documents was inadequate; and (3) the responding party’s objection to an interrogatory is without merit or too general.  CCP § 2030.300(a)(1-3).

Request for Admissions

To obtain further responses to requests for admission, the movant must establish that (1) the response to a request for admission is evasive or incomplete, CCP § 2033.290(a)(1), or (2) the objection to a request for admission is without merit or too general.  CCP § 2033.290(a)(2).

DISCUSSION

            Procedural Requirements

Plaintiff served Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Admissions, Set One, on defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade (Andrade) via electronic service on June 21, 2024.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶ 4; Ex.  1.  Andrade served objection only responses on September 3, 2024.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶ 5; Ex. 2.  Plaintiff filed the instant motions to compel further on October 18, 2024.  Thus, the motions are timely.  CCP §§ 2030.300(c), 2033.290(c).

On September 28, 2024, plaintiff’s counsel sent a letter to Andrade’s counsel outlining the deficiencies of the discovery responses and requesting supplemental responses.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶ 6; Ex. 3.  The parties were unable to resolve the issues presented in these motions.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶¶ 8-9; Ex. 4.  Thus, the parties have satisfied the meet and confer requirement.  CCP §§ 2030.300(b)(1), 2033.290(b)(1).

Plaintiff also filed separate statements accompanying each motion.  All Mots., Separate Statement; Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345(a)(1); CCP §§ 2030.300(b)(2), 2033.290(b)(2).

Form Interrogatories

            Plaintiff moves to compel further responses from Andrade to Form Interrogatories Nos. 1.1- 2.10, 4.1-4.2, 7.1-7.3, 12.1-12.7, 17.1, and 20.1-20.11.

            Andrade responded to each interrogatory is not a good faith response.  The Court has quoted it above.  Contrary to the statements contained in it, the objections are not provided “in good faith.”  They are not provided “in the spirit of complying with the Professional Rules of Conduct and Guidelines for Civility in Litigation.”  They are the antithesis of what counsel professes.

            Andrade’s responses are evasive and incomplete.  Further the Court finds that the objections to this discovery are not meritorious.  For example, Andrade objects to Form Interrogatory No. 2.2 which requests his date and place of birth as vague, burdensome, violating attorney-client privilege, and seeking premature disclosure of expert opinions, among other objections.  Mot. FROGs, Klein Decl., Ex. 2, pp 4:24-5:18.  Andrade is required to answer each interrogatory as completely and straightforwardly as possible.  CCP §2030.220(a).  Additionally, Andrade has not filed an opposition substantiating his objections.  The responding party has the burden of justifying the objections thereto.  Coy v. Superior Ct. (1962) 58 Cal.2d 210, 220-221.)  

Thus, Andrade has not provided code complaint response to plaintiff’s Form Interrogatories, Set One.

            Accordingly, plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, without objection, from defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade is GRANTED.

Special Interrogatories

Plaintiff moves to compel further responses from Andrade to Special Interrogatories Nos. 1 through 32.  Andrade responded to each interrogatory with the same objections as stated above.  Mot. SROGs, Klein Decl., Ex. 2.  The Court adopts its reasoning from the preceding section that Andrade’s responses are evasive and incomplete, and his objections are without merit.  Thus, Andrade has not provided code complaint response to plaintiff’s Special Interrogatories, Set One.

            Accordingly, plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, without objection, from defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade is GRANTED.

Request for Admissions

Plaintiff moves to compel further responses from Andrade to Request for Admissions Nos. 1 through 14.  Again, Andrade responded to each request for admission with the objection only response quoted above.  Mot. RFAs, Klein Decl., Ex. 2.  The Court adopts its reasoning from the Form Interrogatories section above to find that Andrade’s responses are evasive and incomplete, and his objections are without merit.  Thus, Andrade has not provided code complaint response to plaintiff’s Request for Admissions, Set One.

            Accordingly, plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Admissions, Set One, without objection , from defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade is GRANTED.

Sanctions

The court must impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel a further response to interrogatories, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.  CCP §§ 2030.300(d), 2033.290(d).

The court may award sanctions under the Discovery Act in favor of a party who files a motion to compel discovery, even though no opposition to the motion was filed, or opposition to the motion was withdrawn, or the requested discovery was provided to the moving party after the motion was filed.  Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1348(a).

Plaintiff requests sanctions for the Motion to Compel Further Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, against defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade, and Andrade’s counsel, in the total amount of $860.00 based upon counsel’s rate of $400/hour for: (1) 0.5 hours attempting to resolve this matter informally; (2) 1.0 hour preparing the motion and accompanying documents; (3) 0.5 to prepare for and attend the hearing; (4) $60 in filing fees.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶ 11.

The request for sanctions is GRANTED in the total amount of $860.00.

 

Plaintiff requests sanctions for the Motion to Compel Further Responses to Special Interrogatories, Set One, against defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade, and Andrade’s counsel, in the total amount of $860.00 based upon counsel’s rate of $400/hour for: (1) 0.5 hours attempting to resolve this matter informally; (2) 1.0 hour preparing the motion and accompanying documents; (3) 0.5 to prepare for and attend the hearing; (4) $60 in filing fees.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶ 11.  Because the 0.5 hours spent attempting to resolve the matter informally and the 0.5 hours spent to attend the hearing are duplicative of the time for the other motions, a 1.0 hour reduction is appropriate.

The request for sanctions is GRANTED in the reduced amount of $460.00.

 

Plaintiff requests sanctions for the Motion to Compel Further Responses to Request for Admissions, Set One, against defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade, and Andrade’s counsel, in the total amount of $860.00 based upon counsel’s rate of $400/hour for: (1) 0.5 hours attempting to resolve this matter informally; (2) 1.0 hour preparing the motion and accompanying documents; (3) 0.5 to prepare for and attend the hearing; (4) $60 in filing fees.  All Mots., Klein Decl., ¶ 11.  Because the 0.5 hours spent attempting to resolve the matter informally and the 0.5 hours spent to attend the hearing are duplicative of the time for the other motions, a 1.0 hour reduction is appropriate.

The request for sanctions is GRANTED in the reduced amount of $460.00.

            Plaintiff’s motions are thus GRANTED.  Sanctions are GRANTED against defendant Adonnis Alexander Andrade and his counsel of record in the total amount of $1,780.00.  Sanctions are payable within 30 days to counsel for moving party.
