DEPARTMENT P LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23AHCV02008    Hearing Date: November 4, 2024    Dept: P

[TENTATIVE] ORDER GRANTING ATTORNEYS' MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL FOR PLAINTIFFS LIGHTKING AMERICA TECHNOLOGY AND YUAN SUN AND PLAINTIFFS' REQUEST FOR CONTINUANCE. 

The Court may issue an order allowing an attorney to withdraw from representation after notice to the client. (CCP § 284.) The attorney may withdraw from representation if the withdrawal would not result in undue prejudice to the client's interest—i.e., counsel cannot withdraw at a critical point in the litigation. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal. App. 4th 904, 915; see California Rule of Professional Conduct 3-700.)

The Court has discretion to grant or deny a motion to be relieved as counsel, particularly when granting such a motion would cause undue prejudice to the client's interests or result in “an unreasonable disruption of the orderly processes of justice.” (People v. Ortiz (1990) 51 Cal. 3d 975, 979.) Furthermore, the Court may deny an attorney’s request to withdraw “where such withdrawal would work an injustice or cause undue delay in the proceeding.” (Mandell v. Superior Court (1977) 67 Cal. App. 3d 1, 4.) However, this discretion must be exercised reasonably, balancing the client’s right to choose representation with the court’s duty to maintain the orderly administration of justice. (Ibid.)

In making a motion to be relieved as counsel, the attorney must comply with procedures set forth in Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362.  The motion must be made using mandatory forms:

1.     Notice of Motion and Motion to be Relieved as Counsel directed to the client – (MC-051);


2.     Declaration “stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship” the reasons that the motion was brought (MC-052);

3.     Proposed Order (MC-053).

The forms must be timely filed and served on all parties who have appeared in the case.  If these documents are served on the client by mail, there must be a declaration stating either that the address where client was served is “the current residence or business address of the client” or “the last known residence or business address of the client and the attorney has been unable to locate a more current address after making reasonable efforts to do so within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved.”  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d)(1).)

 This matter involves two separately filed motions to be relieved as counsel. One motion was filed by Supreme Law Group, APC, Ian Li, and Avery Tam (“Attorneys”) to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff Yuan Sun. The second motion was filed by the same Attorneys to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff Lightking America Technology (L.A.) LTD. Both motions, filed by the Attorneys representing Plaintiffs Yuan Sun and Lightking America Technology, (collectively “Plaintiffs”) seek to withdraw as counsel in this matter.

Attorneys request to be relieved as counsel, citing that Plaintiff did not provide a signed substitution of attorney form and asserting that a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship has made effective representation unreasonably difficult.

Under California Rule of Court 2.251 and Code of Civil Procedure section 1010.6, electronic service must be accompanied by a declaration affirming that the client’s current electronic service address was confirmed within 30 days before filing the motion. Merely sending notice to the last known address, without confirmation or return, does not satisfy the "current" address requirement.  Here, Counsel filed the motion on August 23, 2024, with proof of electronic service, but did not comply with Rule 2.251’s requirement to confirm the Plaintiffs current electronic address within 30 days prior to filing. Despite this issue, Plaintiffs filing of an opposition indicates they have received actual notice of the Attorneys intent to withdraw.  

Plaintiffs oppose the motion, contending that Attorneys have not provided sufficient detail about the breakdown of the attorney-client relationship. Although the explanation lacks specificity, counsel seeking to withdraw may not disclose privileged attorney-client communications.  Therefore, of necessity, the details of the breakdown in communication may not be disclosed (See CRC 3.1362, cited above).

Plaintiffs declare that they have “lost trust in Ian Li” and are “actively looking for new counsel to represent them in this matter” (Sun Decl. ¶ 13). Given these statements, the Court finds that a sufficient breakdown in the attorney-client relationship exists to justify the withdrawal. Furthermore, given that no trial date has yet been set, this matter is not at a critical stage of the proceedings.

The Court GRANTS Attorneys motion to be relieved as counsel due to the breakdown in the attorney-client relationship.  

“After the order is signed, a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case.”  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).)  The Order on this Motion will not be effective “until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on Plaintiff has been filed with the court.” (Ibid.)

GRANTS Plaintiffs request for continuance to allow time to retain new counsel or prepare to proceed without representation. A hearing on demurrer with motion to strike is currently set for January 23, 2025.  For the present, that hearing will remain on calendar and will also be set for status re retention of new counsel. 

Counsel is ordered to give notice.

Case Number: 24NNCV00742    Hearing Date: November 4, 2024    Dept: P

[TENTATIVE] ORDER GRANTING MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL FOR PLAINTIFF CAROLINA CRUZ-SALAZAR.

On April 3, 2024, Plaintiff Carolina Cruz-Salazar (“Plaintiff”) filed the present action against Defendant Sierra’s Beauty Salon (“Defendant”) and DOES 1 through 20, asserting causes of action for (1) negligence and (2) strict product liability. Plaintiff alleges that on or about April 27, 2023, she visited Sierra’s Beauty Salon for hair treatment and suffered severe burns to her scalp as a result of the salon's negligence in administering, inspecting, and maintaining the hair dryer chair used during the treatment. 

On August 5, 2024, Defendant filed an answer.

On October 2, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel filed a motion to be relieved as counsel.

I.                Legal Standard

The Court may issue an order allowing an attorney to withdraw from representation after notice to the client. (CCP § 284.) The attorney may withdraw from representation if the withdrawal would not result in undue prejudice to the client's interest—i.e., counsel cannot withdraw at a critical point in the litigation. (Ramirez v. Sturdevant (1994) 21 Cal. App. 4th 904, 915; see California Rule of Professional Conduct 3-700.)

The Court has discretion to grant or deny a motion to be relieved as counsel, particularly when granting such a motion would cause undue prejudice to the client's interests or result in “an unreasonable disruption of the orderly processes of justice.” (People v. Ortiz (1990) 51 Cal. 3d 975, 979.) Furthermore, the Court may deny an attorney’s request to withdraw “where such withdrawal would work an injustice or cause undue delay in the proceeding.” (Mandell v. Superior Court (1977) 67 Cal. App. 3d 1, 4.) However, this discretion must be exercised reasonably, balancing the client’s right to choose representation with the court’s duty to maintain the orderly administration of justice. (Ibid.)

In making a motion to be relieved as counsel, the attorney must comply with procedures set forth in Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362. The motion must be made using mandatory forms:

1. Notice of Motion and Motion to be Relieved as Counsel directed to the client – (MC-051);

2. Declaration “stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship” the reasons that the motion was brought (MC-052);


3. Proposed Order (MC-053).The forms must be timely filed and served on all parties who have appeared in the case. If these documents are served on the client by mail, there must be a declaration stating either that the address where client was served is “the current residence or business address of the client” or “the last known residence or business address of the client and the attorney has been unable to locate a more current address after making reasonable efforts to do so within 30 days before the filing of the motion to be relieved.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(d)(1).)

 II.             Discussion

Attorneys Aidin Ghavimi and Ilana Fine, on behalf of Starpoint, LC, (collectively “Counsel”) seek to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff Carolina Cruz-Salazar (“Plaintiff”), citing a lack of communication and a breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. Counsel indicates that Plaintiff initially agreed to sign a substitution of counsel, and the necessary forms were sent to Plaintiff via mail and electronic mail. However, Plaintiff has not responded. Counsel further asserts that Plaintiff’s refusal or inability to communicate has made effective representation unreasonably difficult, as Plaintiff has continually breached a material term of the retainer agreement, specifically, the obligation to maintain contact with counsel.

The Court notes that the motion is unopposed. Counsel has complied with all procedural requirements, including notice to the client and submission of a proposed order and declaration as required by MC-051, MC-052, and MC-053.

III.           Conclusion and Order

The Court GRANTS Attorneys motion to be relieved as counsel due to the breakdown in the attorney-client relationship and lack of communication from Plaintiff.

“After the order is signed, a copy of the signed order must be served on the client and on all parties that have appeared in the case.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362(e).) The Order on this Motion will not be effective “until proof of service of a copy of the signed order on Plaintiff has been filed with the court.” (Ibid.)

Counsel is ordered to give notice.
