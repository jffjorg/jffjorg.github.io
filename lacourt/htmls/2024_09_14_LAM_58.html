DEPARTMENT 58 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 24STCV04415    Hearing Date: September 14, 2024    Dept: 58

Judge Bruce G. Iwasaki

Department 58

Hearing Date:             September 16, 2024

Case Name:                Guy T. Reid v. California Highway Patrol Officer Cindy Nevarez, 022707

Case No.:                    24STCV04415

Motion:                       Demurrer and Motion to Strike

Moving Party:             Defendant, California Highway Patrol Officer Cindy Nevarez, 022707

Opposing Party:          Plaintiff, Guy T. Reid

 

Tentative Ruling:      Defendant’s demurrer is sustained in its entirety, without leave to amend.

 

                                    Defendant’s motion to strike is denied as moot.

 

             

            Plaintiff Guy T. Reid filed his initial complaint against defendant California Highway Patrol Officer Cindy Nevarez, 022707 on February 22, 2024, alleging a single cause of action for negligence.

 

On July 15, 2024, the Court sustained Defendant’s demurrer to the initial complaint.

 

On July 25, 2024, Plaintiff filed his operative first amended complaint (“FAC”), still asserting a cause of action for negligence.

 

On August 16, 2024, Defendant demurred to and moved to strike portions of Plaintiff’s FAC.

 

On September 3, 2024, Plaintiff filed his opposition. Defendant contends the opposition was not served. (See 09-09-2024 Notice of Non-Opposition.)

 

On September 9, 2024, Defendant filed a Reply and Notice of Non-Opposition.

 

Demurrer

 

            A demurrer is an objection to a pleading, the grounds for which are apparent from either the face of the complaint or a matter of which the court may take judicial notice.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.30, subd. (a); see also Blank v. Kirwan, supra, 39 Cal.3d at p. 318.)  The purpose of a demurrer is to challenge the sufficiency of a pleading “by raising questions of law.”  (Postley v. Harvey (1984) 153 Cal.App.3d 280, 286.)  “In the construction of a pleading, for the purpose of determining its effect, its allegations must be liberally construed, with a view to substantial justice between the parties.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 452.)  The court “ ‘ “treat[s] the demurrer as admitting all material facts properly pleaded, but not contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law . . ..” ’ ” (Berkley v. Dowds (2007) 152 Cal.App.4th 518, 525.)

 

Timeliness

 

            Defendant contends Plaintiff did not timely serve his opposition. (See 09-09-2024 Notice of Non-Opposition.)

 

            Papers opposing a demurrer or motion to strike must be filed and served within nine (9) court days of the hearing. The hearing is September 16, 2024; Plaintiff was required to file and serve no later than September 5, 2024. He did not. He filed his papers on September 9, 2024, and per his proof of service, he served Defendant by mail on that date. Neither was timely; service of opposition papers must be “reasonably calculated to ensure delivery to the other party or parties not later than the close of the next business day after the time the opposing papers ... are filed.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(c); cf. Blake v. Ecker (2001) 93 Cal.App.4th 728, 736, fn. 6 [service of opposition by regular mail on filing date “arguably sufficient, particularly [where] both plaintiff’s attorney and defendant’s attorney were in the same general area (Woodland Hills and downtown Los Angeles)”].))

 

            Notwithstanding Plaintiff’s failure to timely serve or file his opposition, the Court opts to consider his papers in the interests of efficiency and disposition of this matter on the merits. (See Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b) [“a judge ... may prescribe a shorter time” for service in a law and motion matter].)

 

Discussion

 

            The demurrer is sustained without leave to amend. Plaintiff has not cured the deficiencies identified in his initial complaint. He has not shown he timely exhausted his government claims requirements or he can overcome relevant government immunities. His initial and amended pleadings demonstrate he cannot cure these deficiencies by amendment, and he has not carried his burden to show otherwise.

 

            Plaintiff failed to timely exhaust his administrative remedies.

 

            “[A] cause of action against a public employee or former public employee for injury resulting from an act or omission in the scope of his employment as a public employee is barred if an action against the employing public entity is barred . . . .” (Gov. Code § 950.2.) “A cause of action for such injury may not be maintained against the public employee or former public employee whose act or omission caused such injury until the claim has been rejected, or has been deemed to have been rejected, in whole or in part by the public entity.” (Gov. Code, § 950.6, subd. (a).) “Timely claim presentation is not merely a procedural requirement, but rather, a condition precedent to plaintiff’s maintaining an action against defendant, and thus, an element of the plaintiff’s cause of action.” (K.J. v. Arcadia Unified School Dist. (2009) 172 Cal.App.4th 1229, 1238) “Complaints that do not allege facts demonstrating either that a claim was timely presented or that compliance with the claims statute is excused are subject to a general demurrer for not stating facts sufficient to constitute a cause of action.” (Rubenstein v. Doe No. 1 (2017) 3 Cal.5th 903, 906.) And the claim must be presented to the government within six months of a plaintiff’s injury (with limited exceptions that do not apply here). (See Gov. Code, §§ 950.6, 911.2, 901, 945.4, 950.2.)

 

            Along with his FAC Plaintiff submits a citizen complaint form he filed with the California Highway Patrol, not a claim for money damages. (See FAC, Exh. A.) The citizen complaint was filed in July 22, 2024, which is nearly two years after the incident underlying this suit took place. (See ibid.; compare FAC, Exhs. C-D [incident dates 10/23/22, 10/24/22, and 12/23/22].) Plaintiff’s opposition to Defendant’s prior demurrer suggested he filed a claim for money damages in May 2024 (07-01-24 Opp., Exh. A) – again, long after the claims period lapsed.

 

            Plaintiff failed to exhaust his administrative remedies within the requisite claims period. The defect is apparent from the fact of his complaint, previously noticed materials, and exhibits he has submitted to the Court along with past filings. The demurrer must be sustained without leave to amend.

 

Plaintiff’s allegations do not defeat the several immunities that apply to a government official exercising her duties.

 

            In its July 15, 2024 ruling, the Court observed that Defendant is immunized from liability for most of the conduct Plaintiff alleges based on Government Code section 820.2 [exercise of vested discretion], section 821 [failure to enforce an enactment], or to a lesser extent section 812 [failure to make an arrest]. The Court found those sections did not apply to Plaintiff’s allegations that Defendant filed a false incident report, but those allegations are barred by the immunity in Government Code section 822.2 [misrepresentation in course of employment]. (See 07-15-24 Minute Order, at pp. 5-7 [sections 820.2, 812, and 821 do not bar entire negligence claim, but “crux of the Complaint is Defendant writing a false investigation report”, immunized under section 822.2].)

 

            Defendant is immune from suit based on the facts Plaintiff has alleged, when those facts are accepted as true. Plaintiff has done nothing to demonstrate he can plead around immunity upon amendment. The demurrer is sustained without leave to amend on this separate, sufficient ground.

 

Motion to Strike

 

         The motion to strike is denied as moot.
