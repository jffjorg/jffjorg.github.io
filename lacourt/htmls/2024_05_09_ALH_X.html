Case Number: 22BBCV00741    Hearing Date: May 9, 2024    Dept: X

   Tentative Ruling

 

Judge Joel L. Lofton, Department X

 

 

HEARING DATE:    May 9, 2024                                         TRIAL DATE: No date set.

                                                          

CASE:                         Brian Whitaker v. Jasjit K.Bisla et al.

 

CASE NO.:                 22BBCV00741

 

 

MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

 

MOVING PARTY:               Manuel Eli Gonzalez

 

RESPONDING PARTY:      None

 

SERVICE:                              Filed

 

OPPOSITION:                       None

 

REPLY:                                   None

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

To be relieved as Defendant Pinkberry Inc.’s counsel.

 

             

BACKGROUND

 

On October 11, 2022, Plaintiff Brian Whitaker (“Plaintiff”) filed the Complaint against Jasjit K. Bisla, Satinder S. Bisla, and Pinkberry Inc. (collectively, “Defendants”) seeking damages and injunctive relief of Unruh Civil Rights Act and California Disabled Persons Act.

 

On April 15, 2024, Defendant’s counsel, Manuel Eli Gonzalez (“Counsel”) filed the instant action.

 

As of May 6, 2024, no oppositions have been filed. 

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

The Court conditionally grants the motion pursuant to Counsel filing the Proposed Order.

             

LEGAL STANDARD

For a Motion to Withdraw, California Rule of Court, rule 3.1362 requires (1) a notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client; (2) a declaration stating in general terms the basis for the withdrawal, without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship; (3) service of the notice of motion and motion and declaration on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and (4) the proposed order relieving counsel.  

 

DISCUSSION

 

Plaintiff’s Counsel’s Motion fails to comply with California Rules of Court, Rule 3.1362. For instance, the Court finds that Counsel failed to file the Proposed Order (MC-053).¿However, Counsel did file a notice of motion and motion to be directed to the client and a declaration. The declaration states that he seeks to withdraw because “it has been very challenging for his office to maintain regular communication with [the client].” (Gonzalez Decl. ¶¶ 3-6.) Moreover, Counsel states there has been a serious breakdown in the attorney-client relationship. (Id. at ¶ 9.) For instance, Counsel states “[o]n April 13, 2024, I called defendant who for some reason was very hostile towards me. During the telephone call Ms. Kaur informed me that she no longer desired my services. Also, during that phone call, Ms. Kaur put her boyfriend on the phone who then threatened to “kick my ass,” and he then demanded a refund. (Id. at ¶¶ 7- 8.) There does not seem to be obvious prejudice because trial has not been set and oppositions have not been filed. Although the Case Management Conference is set for June 13, 2024, it can be continued if prejudice arises.

 

CONCLUSION

 

            Therefore, the Court conditionally grants the motion pursuant to Counsel filing the Proposed Order.

 

            The court will delay the effective date of the order relieving counsel until (1) proof of service of a copy of the signed order on the client and (2) proof that the client has been properly served with notice of the next trial date have been filed with the court.

 

 

 

           

Dated: May 9, 2024                                        ___________________________________

                                                                                    Joel L. Lofton

                                                                                    Judge of the Superior Court

HEARING DATE:     May 9 , 2024                                       TRIAL DATE: October 29, 2024

                                                          

CASE:                         Chia Huey Lin v. Chung Huei Ling et al

 

CASE NO.:                 23AHCV00524

 

 

MOTION TO COMPEL INITIAL FROGS AND RFAS; REQUEST FOR MONETARY SANCTIONS

 

MOVING PARTY:               Plaintiff Chia Huey Lin

 

RESPONDING PARTY:      None

 

SERVICE:                              Filed

 

OPPOSITION:                       None  

 

REPLY:                                   None

 

RELIEF REQUESTED

To compel Defendants Chung Huei Lin and Willy Ling’s (collectively, “Defendants”) responses to Requests for Admissions, Set One and Form Interrogatories, Set One. Plaintiff also seeks monetary sanctions in the amount of $3,261.65 against Defendants.

             

 

BACKGROUND

On March 9, 2023, Plaintiff Chia Huey Lin (“Plaintiff”) filed the Complaint against Defendants for quiet title. On May 26, 2024, Defendant Willy Ling filed a Cross-Complaint against Plaintiff.

 

On January 30, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant improperly combined discovery motions. As of May 6, 2024, Defendants have not filed an opposition.

 

TENTATIVE RULING

 

Plaintiff’s motion to compel initial responses from Defendants to Form Interrogatories, Set One is granted. Defendants are ordered to provide responses without objection within 30 days of the Court’s order. Also, the Requests for Admissions, Set One are deemed admitted against Defendants.

 

The Court grants monetary sanctions for a total of 3 hours at $250.00 per hour for a total of $750.00. Defendants are ordered to pay monetary sanctions in the amount to Plaintiff, by and through counsel, within thirty (30) days of notice of this order. Plaintiff is ordered to pay an additional filing fee of $60.00 to the Court within (20) days of this Order.

 

Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of this ruling.  

 

LEGAL STANDARD

 

If a party to whom interrogatories are directed fails to serve a timely response, the propounding party may move for an order compelling responses and for a monetary sanction.¿(CCP § 2030.290(b).)¿The statute contains no time limit for a motion to compel where no responses or no verified responses have been served.¿¿All that need be shown in the moving papers is that a set of interrogatories was properly served on the opposing party, that the time to respond has expired, and that no response of any kind has been served.¿(See¿Leach v. Superior Court (1980) 111 Cal.App.3d 902, 905-906.) 

 

Where a party fails to timely respond to a request for admission, the propounding party may move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (b).)¿ The party who failed to respond waives any objections to the demand, unless the court grants them relief from the waiver, upon a showing that the party (1) has subsequently served a substantially compliant response, and (2) that the party’s failure to respond was the result of mistake, inadvertence, or excusable neglect.¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subds. (a)(1)-(2).)¿ The court shall grant a motion to deem admitted requests for admissions, “unless it finds that the party to whom the requests for admission have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with Section 2033.220.”¿ (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).)¿ 

 

DISCUSSION

 

Form Interrogatories, Set One

Here, on August 8, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel’s office served Form Interrogatories, Set One on each of the Defendants in this action. (Lin Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. 1.) Plaintiff provided multiple extensions per Defendants’ counsel’s requests. (Id. at ¶ 3.) On January 3, 2024, Defendant’s counsel served a motion to withdraw which was granted on February 5, 2024.  To date, Defendants have not responded to the discovery request. (Id. at ¶ 4.) Defendants did not file an opposition. Therefore, the Court grants the Motion.

 

Requests for Admissions, Set One

 

Here, on August 8, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel’s office served Form Interrogatories, Set One on each of the Defendants in this action. (Lin Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. 1.) Plaintiff provided multiple extensions per Defendants’ counsel’s requests. (Id. at ¶ 3.) On January 3, 2024, Defendant’s counsel served a motion to withdraw which was granted on February 5, 2024.  To date, Defendants have not responded to the discovery request. (Id. at ¶ 4.) Defendants did not file an opposition. Thus, Plaintiff is entitled to an order deeming the Requests for Admission, Set One, admitted against Defendants. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.)¿ 

 

Monetary Sanctions

 

Plaintiff requests monetary sanctions against Defendants, jointly or severally in the amount of $3,261.65.  

 

Although Defendant did not oppose the motions, the Court can impose monetary sanctions for misuse of discovery, which includes failing to respond to an authorized method of discovery. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2023.010, subd. (d); see also Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1348(a) [“The court may award sanctions under the Discovery Act in favor of a party who files a motion to compel discovery, even though no opposition to the motion was filed, or opposition to the motion was withdrawn, or the requested discovery was provided to the moving party after the motion was filed.”].)  

 

The Court finds Defendants’ failure to respond was a misuse of the discovery process. Sanctions have been sufficiently noticed. However, the Court reduces the requested fees because: (1) Plaintiff does not justify the $400 hourly rate with an explanation of Plaintiff’s counsel’s experience (Lin Decl. ¶ 7.); (2) two hours spent trying to resolve the discovery dispute informally (via emails) is excessive given this is a regular, simple task for counsel (Id.); (3) the one hour and half preparing discovery is excessive due to its simple nature; and (4) three and a half hours spent preparing this simple discovery motion is excessive.

 

In addition, Plaintiff filed a single motion for what should have been two separate motions. Combining discovery motions allows the moving party to avoid paying the requisite filing fees. “Every direction of a court or judge, made or entered in writing, and not included in a judgment, is denominated an order. An application for an order is a motion.” (Code of Civ. Proc., § 1003.) Government Code section 70617 requires payment of a $60 filing fee for each motion filed with the Court. Plaintiff may not circumvent the Government Code with an omnibus filing. An additional filing fee is required before the Court may hear the motion. Filing fees are jurisdictional and it is mandatory for court clerks to demand and receive them. (See Duran v. St. Luke’s Hospital (2003) 114 Cal.App.4th 457, 460.) Thus, Plaintiff is ordered to pay an additional filing fee.

 

Accordingly, the Court grants monetary sanctions for a total of 3 hours at $250.00 per hour for a total of $750.00. Defendants are ordered to pay monetary sanctions in the amount to Plaintiff, by and through counsel, within thirty (30) days of notice of this order. Plaintiff is ordered to pay an additional filing fee of $60.00 to the Court within (20) days of this Order.

 

CONCLUSION

 

Plaintiff’s motion to compel initial responses from Defendants to Form Interrogatories, Set One is granted. Defendants are ordered to provide responses without objection within 30 days of the Court’s order. Also, the Requests for Admissions, Set One are deemed admitted against Defendants.

 

The Court grants monetary sanctions for a total of 3 hours at $250.00 per hour for a total of $750.00. Defendants are ordered to pay monetary sanctions in the amount to Plaintiff, by and through counsel, within thirty (30) days of notice of this order. Plaintiff is ordered to pay an additional filing fee of $60.00 to the Court within (20) days of this Order.

 

Plaintiff is ordered to provide notice of this ruling.
