DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 21STCV03942    Hearing Date: September 20, 2024    Dept: W

MARIA DE LA CRUZ BRAVO FERNANDEZ V. WEST HILLS HOSPITAL AND MEDICAL CENTER, ET AL.

 

DEFENDANT WEST HILLS HOSPITAL AND MEDICAL CENTER'S MOTIONS TO COMPEL DISCOVERY

 

Date of Hearing:        September 20, 2024                         Trial Date:       June 2, 2025

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        21STCV03942

 

Moving Party:            Defendant West Hills Hospital and Medical Center

Responding Party:     No opposition

 

BACKGROUND

 

This is a medical malpractice action. On February 1, 2021, Plaintiff Marai De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez filed a complaint against Defendants West Hills Hospital and Medical Center, Jonathan S. Matthew, M.D., Jamshid Nazarian, M.D., and Laurance J. Friedman, M.D. for medical malpractice. Plaintiff alleges on December 10, 2016, Plaintiff underwent an esophagogastroduodenoscopy performed by Dr. Jonathan S. Matthew, MD with the assistance of Dr. Laurence Friedman. However, the procedure fell below the standard of care. Plaintiff further alleges on January 23, 2020, she was seen by Dr. Li for what appeared to be multiple adhesions. Plaintiff alleges and believes that the foreign bodies were left there from the esophagogastroduodenoscopy procedure. Plaintiff named John J. Li, M.D. as Doe 1 on January 30, 2024.

 

Plaintiff dismissed Defendant Jamshid Nazarian, M.D. on November 29, 2023. The court dismissed Defendant Laurence J. Friedman, M.D. On August 8, 2024, Plaintiff dismissed John J. Li, M.D. Summary Judgment was entered in Defendant Jonathan S. Matthew on February 6, 2024.

 

[TENTATIVE] RULING:

 

Defendant West Hills Hospital and Medical Center’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez’s Responses to Defendant’s Request for Production, Set One is GRANTED.

 

Defendant West Hills Hospital and Medical Center’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez’s Responses to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories, Set One is GRANTED.

 

 

Defendant West Hills Hospital and Medical Center’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez’s Responses to Defendant’s Special Interrogatories, Set One is GRANTED.

 

DISCUSSION

 

Defendant West Hills Hospital and Medical Center moves the court for an order compelling Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez to provide full and complete, verified responses to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories, Set One; Request for Production, Set One; and Special Interrogatories, Set One, all without objections. Defendant also seeks Plaintiff and/or her counsel of record to pay monetary sanctions for reasonable expenses and attorney fees incurred by Defendant having to bring these motions.

 

Where a party fails to serve timely responses to discovery requests, the court may make an order compelling responses. (CCP §§ 2030.290, 2031.300, 2033.280; Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pacific Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 403.) A party that fails to serve a timely response to the discovery request waives any objection to the request, including one based on privilege or the protection of attorney work product. (CCP §§2030.290(a), 2031.300(a), 2033.280(a); Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc., supra, 148 Cal.App.4th at 404.) Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.010, the propounding party may also move for an order that the genuineness of any documents and the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted, as well as for a monetary sanction. (CCP § 2033.280(b).)

 

On February 27, 2024, Defendant served Plaintiff with Defendant’s Form Interrogatories, Set One, Special Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Production, Set One. (Paggabao Decl. ¶4, Exh. A.) Plaintiff’s responses were due by April 2, 2024. (Paggabao Decl. ¶5.) Counsel met and conferred with Plaintiff’s counsel regarding the overdue discovery responses. (Paggabao Decl. ¶6, Exh. B.) Plaintiff’s counsel confirmed they had ten days to provide responses. (Paggabao Decl. ¶7, Exh. C.) By June 14, 2024, Defendant still had not received responses to any discovery. (Paggabao Decl. ¶8.) Defendant notified Plaintiff of the overdue responses on June 24, 2024 and July 2, 2024 (Paggabao Decl. ¶¶8, 9, Exh. D, E.) On July 8, 2024, Plaintiffs counsel contacted Defendant's counsel and asked to extend the due date to July 11, 2024, to which Defendant's counsel agreed. (Paggabao Decl. ¶10, Exh. E.) To date, Plaintiff has failed to serve any objections or responses to the discovery requests. (Paggabao Decl. ¶11.)

 

Based on the foregoing, Defendant West Hills Hospital and Medical Center’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez’s Responses to Defendant’s Request for Production, Set One; Motion to Compel Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez’s Responses to Defendant’s Form Interrogatories, Set One; and Motion to Compel Plaintiff Maria De La Cruz Bravo Fernandez’s Responses to Defendant’s Special Interrogatories, Set One are GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff is ordered to produce responses, without objection, within 20 days.

 

Sanctions

 

Defendant seeks $1,297.50 in monetary sanctions per motion. If a propounding party moves for and obtains a court order compelling a response, the court shall impose monetary sanctions against the party failing to timely respond to interrogatories and demands for inspection unless that party acted with substantial justification or the sanction would otherwise be unjust. (CCP §§ 2030.290(c), 2031.300(c), 2033.280(c); Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc., supra, 148 Cal.App.4th at 404.)

 

The court GRANTS the request for monetary sanctions in the reduced amount of $847.50 for the first motion and $285.00 each for the other two motions. The court finds the motions were largely identical and only one appearance fee necessary.


Case Number: 22VECV01096    Hearing Date: September 20, 2024    Dept: W

TANNETTE GATES v. warner center condominiums, et al.

 

defendant afsaneh safaei’s motion to set aside/vacate entry of default

 

Date of Hearing:        September 20, 2024                         Trial Date:       None Set

Department:              W                                                        Case No.:        22VECV01096

 

Moving Party:            Defendant Afsaneh Safaei  

Responding Party:     Plaintiff Tannette Gates

 

BACKGROUND

 

On August 3, 2022, Plaintiff Tannette Gates filed a complaint against Warner Center Condominiums, Ross Morgan & Company, Inc., Amber Hill, Valerie Vincent, Joanne Catran, Mimi Himes, Afsaneh Safaei, Arash Safaei, and Hippo Analytics, Inc. for damages arising out of a kitchen pipe exploding in the unit upstairs and water from the upstairs unit damaging Plaintiffs. On January 27, 2023, Plaintiff filed an amended complaint asserting causses of action for: (1) Breach of CCRs; (2) Enforcement of Equitable Servitudes; (3) Breach of Fiduciary Duty; (4) Nuisance; (5) Negligence; (6) Intentional Infliction of Emotional Distress; (7) Breach of Contract and Implied Covenant of Duty of Good Faith and Fair Dealing; (8) Third-Party Beneficiary Breach of Contract; (9) Professional Negligence and (Negligent Misrepresentation (10) Violation of Business and Professional Code § 17200; (11) Declaratory Relief; and (12) Unjust Enrichment against. Plaintiff also named Topa Insurance Company as a defendant. On November 30, 2023, Plaintiff named Chetan Chandrakant Rane as Doe 1.

 

On January 30, 2023, Plaintiff dismissed Amber Hill, Valerie Vincent, Joanne Catran, and Mimi Himes.

 

Plaintiff filed a Second Amended Complaint on May 11, 2023. On September 7, 2023, Plaintiff dismissed Hippo Analytics, Inc. Plaintiff has now filed a Third Amended Complaint as of January 4, 2024. The Third Amended Complaint did not include Chetan Chandrakant Rane. On January 9, 2024, Plaintiff named Abidya Bahl as Doe 2.

 

Request for entry of default was entered against Afsaneh Safaei and Arash Safaei on September 28, 2022.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Defendant Afsaneh Safaei’s Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Entry of Default is GRANTED.

 

ANALYSIS

 

Defendant Afsaneh Safaei moves the court or an order setting aside/vacating the default entered against Defendant on September 28, 2022 on the grounds: (1) the default was improperly entered as Plaintiff failed to serve any statement of damages on Defendant (CCP §473(d); (2) California Rules of Court, Rule 3.110(h); (3) when a complaint is substantively amended after default is entered against a defendant, the amendment “opens” the default (Ford v. Superior Court (1973) 34 Cal.App.3d 338, 342-43); (4) that the default was taken by reason of mistake, inadvertence, surprise or excusable neglect (CCP §473(b); and (5) the inherent equitable power of the Court.

 

I.                    Improper Service

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 473(d) provides that “[t]he court may . . . on motion of either party after notice to the other party, set aside any void judgment or order.” (CCP §473(d).) A default judgment is void against a defendant who was not served with a summons in the manner prescribed by statute. (See Sakaguchi v. Sakaguchi (2009) 173 Cal.App.4th 852, 858.) Relief pursuant to section 473(d) may be made at any time. (See Manson, Iver & York v. Black (2009) 176 Cal.App.4th 36, 42.) 

 

Defendant argues Code of Civil Procedure section 425.11 provides in part that, before a default may be taken in an action to recover damages for personal injury against a non-appearing defendant, the plaintiff must serve the non-appearing defendant with a statement of damages in the same manner as a summons. (CCP § 425.11, (b), (c) & (d).) This requirement was “designed to give defendants one last clear chance to respond to allegations of complaints by providing them with actual notice of their exact potential liability.” (Lopez v. Fancelli (1990) 221 Cal.App.3d 1305, 1310.) Defendant contends Section 425.11 is applicable to this action because Plaintiff’s causes of action for Nuisance, Negligence, and Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress alleged against Defendant in the original Complaint apparently seek to recover damages for Plaintiff’s alleged personal injuries. As a result, Plaintiff was to serve Defendant with a Statement of Damages.

 

In opposition, Plaintiff argues this is not an action that involves personal injury or death. Moreover, Section 425.11(b) states that the defendant may request a statement of damages from Plaintiff. However, Defendant Afsaneh’s counsel did not and refused to contact Plaintiff by phone or otherwise, until instructed by this court to do so.

 

Regardless of whether Section 425.11 is applicable to the facts at hand, the court agrees that the complaint failed to allege the amount of damages and Defendant did not receive the Statement of Damages. Therefore, Defendant did not receive adequate notice of the specific relief sought against Defendant before default was entered. Since relief pursuant to Section 473(d) can be made at any time, the court GRANTS Defendant’s motion to set aside the request for default.

 

The court will briefly discuss Defendant’s other contentions below.

 

II.                  California Rules of Court, Rule 3.110

 

Defendant argues that they must be relieved from default because Plaintiff failed to obtain a default judgment within 45 days of the entry of default pursuant to California Rules of Court, Rule 3.110(h). Specifically, Plaintiff never sought to obtain a default judgment against Defendant, let alone within 45 days after default was entered.

 

In opposition, Plaintiff argues this argument fails because Defendant’s own motion to set aside the default is untimely. The court finds Rule 3.110(h) is not grounds to set aside the request for entry of default. Rather, the failure to obtain a default judgment within 45 days should have resulted in the issuance of an OSC re dismissal.

 

III.                Amended Pleadings

 

Defendant next argues because Plaintiff made substantial additions and changes to the nature of their complaint, the filing of the FAC “opened” the default entered against Defendant and waived Plaintiff’s right, if any, to obtain judgment on the default. (See Ford v. Superior Court (1973) 34 Cal.App.3d 338, 342.)

 

In opposition, Plaintiff argues none of the amendments to Plaintiff’s Second Amended Complaint relate to Afsaneh or any causes of action against Afsaneh.

 

Default was entered against Defendant on the original complaint. Against Afsaneh, Plaintiff alleged breach of CC&Rs, Enforcement of Equitable Servitude, Nuisance, Negligence, Negligent Infliction of Emotional Distress, and Declaratory Relief. In the Second Amended Complaint, which was filed after default was obtained, Plaintiff added a cause of action for Unjust Enrichment against Defendant Afsaneh. Moreover, the SAC contained more allegations against Defendant Afsaneh including that she engaged in conduct that created loud obnoxious noises. (SAC ¶21.)

 

Accordingly, such an amended did “open” the default and give Defendant Afsaneh another opportunity to respond. (See Sass v. Cohen (2020) 10 Cal.5th 861, 880.) This is not like the distinction in Cole since no judgment has been obtained.

 

IV.               Code of Civil Procedure 473(b)

 

Defendant argues the default should be set aside due to mistake. Specifically, Defendant’s new counsel was never notified from prior counsel of the default and instead told that the FAC was erroneously filed and that further amendments were forthcoming. After being made aware of the default on January 22, 2024, Defendant’s counsel promptly, yet unsuccessfully, sought to meet and confer with Plaintiff to set aside the default.

 

The motion is untimely under Section 473(b). Under Code of Civil Procedure § 473(b), defendants are required to bring any motion to vacate a default judgment within six months of its entry.

 

Based on the foregoing, Defendant Afsaneh Safaei’s Motion to Set Aside/Vacate Entry of Default is GRANTED.
