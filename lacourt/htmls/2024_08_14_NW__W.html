DEPARTMENT W LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 22VECV02109    Hearing Date: August 14, 2024    Dept: W

YVONNE JOHNSON, AKA YVONNE MANESH v. SAFEBUILT INSURANCE SERVICES, LLC, et al.

 

motion for leave to file third amended complaint

 

Date of Hearing:        August 14, 2024                    Trial Date:       N/A

Department:              W                                            Case No.:        22VECV02109

 

Moving Party:            Plaintiff Yvonne Johnson aka Yvonne Manesh

Responding Party:     Defendant United Specialty Insurance Company   

 

BACKGROUND

 

Plaintiff Yvonne Johnson brought a prior action (via a cross-complaint) against defendant United Specialty Insurance Company’s insured, Arina Builders, who she claimed had damaged her property. That action resulted in a judgment in plaintiff’s favor for $261,120. Plaintiff subsequently sought attorney’s fees against the insured, which were granted. The judgment and the fees were not paid. Plaintiff then brought the instant suit against the insurance company (United), and Golden State Claims Adjusters (GSCA), a third party claims administrator, seeking the full amount of the judgment and attorneys fees. The first six causes of action in the First Amended Complaint are against United only, for 1) Judgment Creditor’s action against the Insurer; 2) Failure to Pay a Covered Claim; 3) Unreasonable Failure to Defend; 4) Bad Faith Failure to Investigate a Claim; 5) Breach of the Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing; and 6) Violation of Bus. & Prof. Code Section 17200, et seq. Plaintiff also stated claims against GSCA and United for Intentional and Negligent Misrepresentation (the Seventh and Eighth Causes of Action).

 

On November 14, 2023, Plaintiff dismissed United from the 7th and 8th causes of action and dissed GSCA from the 7th and 8th causes of action. Upon sustaining of a demurrer to the FAC, Plaintiff filed a Second Amended Complaint for 1) Judgment Creditor’s action against the Insurer; 2) Breach for Failure to Pay a Covered Claim; 3) Breach of the Covenant of Good Faith and Fair Dealing; and 4) Violation of Bus. & Prof. Code Section 17200, et seq.

 

[Tentative] Ruling

 

Plaintiff’s Motion for Leave to File Third Amended Complaint is GRANTED.

 

ANALYSIS

 

Plaintiff Yvonne Johnson aka Yvonne Manesh moves the Court for leave to file a Third Amended Complaint in the instant action on the grounds that the proposed causes of action contained in the Third Amended Complaint contain a series of acts of occurrence logically interrelated and thus, are related causes of action subject to forfeiture if not pleaded in this action.

 

The courts have a strong policy of allowing motions for leave to amend. “If the motion to amend is timely made and the granting of the motion will not prejudice the opposing party, it is error to refuse permission to amend….” (Morgan v. Sup. Ct. (1959) 172 Cal.App.2d 527.)  A court can deny leave to amend after long, inexcusable delay, where there is cognizable prejudice, such as discovery needed, trial delay, critical evidence lost, or added preparation expense. (Solit v. Tokai Bank (1999) 68 Cal.App.4th 1435, 1448; Atkinson v. Elk Corp. (2003) 109 Cal.App.4th 739, 761; Green v. Rancho Santa Margarita Mortgage Co. (1994) 28 Cal.App.4th 686, 692; Magpali v. Farmers Group, Inc. (1996) 48 Cal.App.4th 471, 487.) Notably, “it is not required to accept an amended complaint that is not filed in good faith, is frivolous, or sham.” (American Advertising & Sales Co. v. Mid-Western (1984) 152 Cal.App.3d 875, 878.)

 

Under California Rules of Court, rule 3.1324, a motion to amend a pleading before trial must (1) include a copy of the proposed amendment or amended pleading, which must be serially numbered to differentiate it from previous pleadings or amendments; (2) state what allegations in the previous pleading are proposed to be deleted, if any, and where, by page, paragraph and line number, the deleted allegations are located; and (3) state what allegations are proposed to be added to the previous pleading, if any, and where, by page, paragraph, and line number, the additional allegations are located. (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1324(a).) A separate supporting declaration specifying (1) the effect of the amendment; (2) why the amendment is necessary and proper; (3) when the facts giving rise to the amended allegations were discovered; and (4) the reason why the request for amendment was not made earlier must accompany the motion. (Id., rule 3.1324(b).) 

 

Plaintiff asserts upon the sustaining of Defendants’ demurrer with motion to strike Plaintiff’s complaint, Plaintiff timely filed a Second Amended Complaint. After Defendant United Specialty Insurance Company filed a Declaration of Demurring Party in Support of Automatic Extension, the parties met and conferred regarding Plaintiff’s SAC and Plaintiff agreed to file a Third Amended Complaint. However, upon filing the TAC, Plaintiff received a Notice of Rejection from the filing clerk indicating that, “a court order is required in order to file this amended complaint.” However, USIC has refused to stipulate to Plaintiff filing a TAC. Plaintiff argues the TAC simply alleges a new cause of action for Bad Faith-Unreasonable Failure to Defend and Breach of Implied Obligation of Good Faith and Fair Dealing and that the amendment is related to the facts originally pleaded in this action so no prejudice will result against Defendant USIC. Moreover, no trial date has been set.

 

In opposition, USIC first argues this court has already sustained USIC’s demurrer to the third cause of action for bad faith – unreasonable failure to defend. (Exh. A.) USIC is correct. However, Plaintiff now alleges Plaintiff is an additional insured under the USIC policy. For the purposes of the motion, the court finds these allegations sufficiently pled. While the arguments may have been briefly touched upon in their opposition to the demurrer, it was not the basis of the court’s consideration and Plaintiff may take the position in their TAC. 

 

USIC next argues the proposed third and fourth causes of action are barred by the statute of limitations on the grounds the statute of limitations for any tort-based bad faith claim ran by April of 2020 at the latest – over four years ago. Even if Plaintiff were asserting a contractual bad faith claim based on her “additional insured” theory, the statute of limitations on that claim ran in April of 2022, over two years ago. USIC contends Plaintiff cannot use the relation back doctrine because these claims are premised on an entirely new set of facts, namely Plaintiff’s purported status as an additional insured. “Whether a plaintiff may amend the complaint to change a party's description or characterization ‘after the statute of limitations has run depends on whether the misdescription or mischaracterization is merely a misnomer or defect in the description or characterization, or whether it is a substitution or entire change of parties. In the former case an amendment will be allowed; in the latter, it will not be allowed.’” (Hawkins v. Pacific Coast Bldg. Products, Inc. (2004) 124 Cal.App.4th 1497, 1504.)  On the face of the complaint, the court finds the relation back doctrine applies. The same general facts are alleged – USIC denied the claim under the policy and not alleging them as the additional insured of the policy was a defect in their allegations.

 

Defendant is free to make merit-based arguments on a demurrer or the like.

 

The court briefly notes USIC’s argument on Plaintiff’s fifth cause of action for violation of Business and Professions Code 17200. USIC contends it is barred by the statute of limitations and even if it did relate back to the original filing of the complaint, the claim would still be time barred because Plaintiff didn’t file the original Complaint until November of 2022, eight months after the statute of limitations expired. USIC did not address this contention in their demurrer to the complaint.  This argument is better raised at the demurrer stage once the new complaint has been filed.  

 

Accordingly, Plaintiff’s Motion for Leave to File Third Amended Complaint is GRANTED.
