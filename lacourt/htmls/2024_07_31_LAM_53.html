DEPARTMENT 53 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Tentative rulings are sometimes, but not always, posted. The purpose of posting a tentative ruling is to to help focus the argument. The posting of a tentative ruling is not an invitation for the filing of additional papers shortly before the hearing.


Case Number: 20STCV05807    Hearing Date: July 31, 2024    Dept: 53

Jung v. Turn Around Communications, Inc.  20STCV05807  

Ex Parte Application   7/31/24

The court notes that, as stated in plaintiffs’ ex parte application, “[o]n February 21, 2024, Plaintiff’s Counsel came to this court on an ex parte basis seeking the same relief requested herein; the ex parte was denied, and the Court ordered the parties to meet and confer.”  (Plaintiffs’ ex parte application, memorandum of points and authorities, filed 7/30/24, p. 1:19-22.) 

The court rules on plaintiffs’ ex parte application, filed July 30, 2024, and presented to the court on July 31, 2024, as follows.

The court finds that plaintiffs have not satisfied the requirement of California Rules of Court, rule 3.1202, subdivision (c), that an applicant for an ex parte order must make an affirmative factual showing in a declaration containing competent testimony based on personal knowledge of irreparable harm, immediate danger, or other statutory basis for granting relief ex parte. 

The court does not find good cause to grant plaintiffs’ request for an order shortening time on the hearings on their motions to compel depositions, filed February 20, 2024, or to prescribe shorter times for the filing and service of papers than the times specified in Code of Civil Procedure section 1005, subdivision (b).  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1300, subd. (b).)

The court therefore denies the ex parte application without prejudice to plaintiffs’ seeking other appropriate relief, including but not limited to, filing an ex parte application to continue the trial and to advance the hearings on plaintiffs’ motions to compel depositions. 

The court orders the parties’ counsel to meet and confer no later than August 2, 2024, to make a reasonable and good faith effort to informally resolve the issues presented by plaintiffs’ motions to compel depositions.

The court orders plaintiffs to give notice of this order.

[tentative] Order RE:

 

plaintiffs’ motion for leave to file fourth amended complaint

 

 

MOVING PARTIES:              Plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis            

 

RESPONDING PARTY:       Defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation

Motion for Leave to File Fourth Amended Complaint

The court considered the amended moving papers filed on April 23, 2024, and the opposition and reply papers filed in connection with this motion.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiffs Annette Turner (“Turner”) and Tammy Davis (“Davis”) (collectively, “Plaintiffs”) move the court for an order granting them leave to file their proposed Fourth Amended Complaint in this action (1) to delete references to the causes of action and supporting allegations to which the court sustained the demurrer filed by defendant AIDS Healthcare Foundation (“Defendant”) in its June 29, 2023 order (i.e., the first cause of action alleged by plaintiff Turner, the second cause of action alleged by Plaintiffs, and the sixth cause of action alleged by plaintiff Davis); (2) to delete references to Doe defendants; (3) to separate the cause of action for negligence into two causes of action, to be alleged separately by plaintiffs Turner and Davis; (4) to change the title of the fourth cause of action from “Violation of Civil Code §1941.1—Uninhabitable Conditions” to “Violation of Breach of Implied Warranty of Habitability as Listed in Civil Code §1941.1—Uninhabitable Conditions;” (5) to delete references to allegations of other tenants’ behavior to clarify the negligence claims asserted by Plaintiffs; and (6) to add a negligence cause of action against defendant Victor’s Services, LLC by plaintiff Davis.  (Notice of Mot., pp. 2:11-3:5; June 29, 2023 Order, pp. 4:25-5:6.)

“The court may, in furtherance of justice, and on any terms as may be proper, allow a party to amend any pleading or proceeding by adding or striking out the name of any party, or by correcting a mistake in the name of a party, or a mistake in any other respect . . . .  The court may likewise, in its discretion, after notice to the adverse party, allow, upon any terms as may be just, an amendment to any pleading or proceeding in other particulars . . . .”  (Code Civ. Proc., §¿473, subd. (a)(1).)  “Statutes like 473 are ‘construed liberally so that cases might be tried upon their merits in one trial where no prejudice to the opposing party . . . is demonstrated.’  [Citation.]  Further, this liberal policy applies to amendments ‘ “at any stage of the proceedings, up to and including trial,” ’ absent prejudice to the adverse party.  [Citation.]”  (Tung v. Chicago Title Company (2021) 63 Cal.App.5th 734, 747 [internal citations omitted].)

The court finds that (1) it is in furtherance of justice to allow Plaintiffs to amend their complaint in the manner proposed, and (2) Defendant, and the other named defendants, have not demonstrated that they will be unduly prejudiced by the amendment.  The court therefore grants Plaintiffs’ motion.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 473, subd. (a)(1); Tung, supra, 63 Cal.App.5th at p. 747.)

First, the court finds that Plaintiffs have shown that amendment is necessary so that Plaintiffs may clarify their negligence claims, delete irrelevant allegations, and add a negligence cause of action to be asserted by plaintiff Davis against defendant Victor’s Services, LLC.  Moreover, Plaintiffs have stated, and Defendant does not appear to dispute, that many of the proposed alterations are based on the same set of facts that Plaintiffs have previously alleged, such that Plaintiffs are not changing the theory of this action.  (Notice of Mot., pp. 2:25-26, 3:3-4; Harings Decl., ¶ 9 [the factual allegations will remain the same against defendant Victor’s Services, LLC, and the new negligence cause of action will join the sexual assault cause of action against it]; Opp., p. 9:3-10 [contending that Plaintiffs should not be permitted to stylistically alter their pleading “as to their otherwise same allegations”].)  Thus, the court finds that it is in furtherance of justice to permit Plaintiffs to amend their complaint.

Second, the court finds that Defendant has not shown that it will be unduly prejudiced by the proposed amendment.  

To the extent that Defendant is concerned that Plaintiffs will raise claims that will be deleted in the Fourth Amended Complaint at trial or in opposition to its dispositive motions, Defendant may address that concern at a more appropriate time (i.e., at trial or in reply to its motion).  (Opp, p. 9:13-16.)  Further, the court recognizes that various of Defendant’s motions directed to the Third Amended Complaint have not yet been ruled on (i.e., its motion for judgment on the pleadings, motion for summary adjudication, and motion to file amended answer to the Third Amended Complaint) and will be rendered moot by Plaintiffs’ filing of their Fourth Amended Complaint.  However, trial has been continued in this action to January 22, 2025.  Thus, Defendant will have approximately six months to refile its motions directed to the operative pleading, and may file an ex parte application or other appropriate motion requesting that the court advance any hearing that is scheduled after trial. 

ORDER

            The court grants plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis’s motion for leave to file fourth amended complaint.

            The court orders plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis to file with the court their Fourth Amended Complaint, in the form attached as Exhibit 5 to the declaration of Annette Harings, no later than August 2, 2024.

            The court orders plaintiffs Annette Turner and Tammy Davis to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.

Petition for Approval of Compromise of Claim for Minor Claimant Nathan Jonathan Oliveros

The court considered the moving papers filed in connection with this petition.  No opposition papers were filed.  

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff and petitioner Jeanette Oliveros (“Petitioner”) seeks court approval of the settlement made on behalf of minor claimant Nathan Jonathan Oliveros (“Minor Claimant”) in this action.

The compromise of a minor’s disputed claim for damages is valid only after it has been approved, upon the filing of a petition, by the court.¿ (Prob. Code, § 3500.)¿ The petition must be verified by the petitioner, must contain a full disclosure of all information that has any bearing upon the reasonableness of the compromise, and must be prepared on Judicial Council form MC-350.¿ (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 7.950.)¿

Defendants Joseph Heffesse, trustee of the Coldwater Canyon Trust, Sandra B. Sternberg Heffesse, and LA Properties Heffesse LLC have agreed to pay a total of $175,000 to settle this action, of which $5,000 will be separately allocated to Minor Claimant.  (MC-350, ¶¶ 10-11.)  Of the $5,000 allocated to Minor Claimant, $1,250 will be paid to counsel for attorney’s fees and $134.35 will be paid to counsel for costs.  (MC-350, ¶¶ 13, 16.)  The remaining $3,615.65 will be paid or delivered to Petitioner, as the parent of Minor Claimant, without bond, on the terms and under the conditions specified in Probate Code sections 3401-3402.  (MC-350, ¶ 18, subd. (b)(5); MC-350, Attachment 18b(5), Oliveros Decl., ¶¶ 1, 4, 6; Prob. Code, §§ 3401, 3402.)

The court has reviewed the petition and finds the settlement to be fair and reasonable, and in the best interest of Minor Claimant.  The court further finds that the declaration of Rachel Fishenfeld is sufficient to support the request for attorney’s fees in the amount of $1,250 (representing 25 percent of the $5,000 settlement).  (MC-350, Attachment 13(a), Fishenfeld Decl., ¶¶ 2-3, 6-11; Cal. Rules of Ct., rule 7.955.)  The court therefore grants Petitioner’s petition.

ORDER

            The court grants petitioner Jeanette Oliveros’s petition for approval of compromise of claim on behalf of minor claimant Nathan Jonathan Oliveros.

            The court orders that the $3,615.65 settlement on behalf of minor claimant Nathan Jonathan Oliveros may be paid to plaintiff and petitioner Jeanette Oliveros pursuant to Probate Code sections 3401 and 3402.

            The court orders petitioner Jeanette Oliveros to give notice of this ruling.

IT IS SO ORDERED.
