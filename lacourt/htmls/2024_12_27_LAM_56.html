DEPARTMENT 56 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

DEPARTMENT 56 JUDGE HOLLY J. FUJIE, LAW AND MOTION RULINGS. The court makes every effort to post tentative rulings by 5.00 pm of the court day before the hearing. The tentative ruling will not become the final ruling until the hearing [see CRC 3.1308(a)(2)], and are also available in the courtroom on the day of the hearing [see CRC 3.1308(b)]. If the parties wish to submit on the tentative ruling and avoid a court appearance, all counsel must agree and choose which counsel will give notice. That counsel must 1) call Dept 56 by 8:30 a.m. on the day of the hearing (213/633-0656) and state that all parties will submit on the tentative ruling, and 2) serve notice of the ruling on all parties. If any party declines to submit on the tentative ruling, then no telephone call is necessary and all parties should appear at the hearing in person or by Court Call. Court reporters are not provided, and parties who want a record of motions and other proceedings must hire a privately retained certified court reporter.

Case Number: 23STCV30311    Hearing Date: December 27, 2024    Dept: 56

 

 

 

SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA

FOR THE COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES - CENTRAL DISTRICT

 

 MARIA GABRIELA VIEYRA, an individual,

                        Plaintiff,

            vs.

 

 CALIFORNIA MEDICAL PHARMACY, INC., a business entity, exact form unknown; DAVID LIAUTAUD, an individual; and Does 1 through 100, inclusive,

                                                                             

                        Defendants.                              

 

      CASE NO.:  23STCV30311

 

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

MOTION TO QUASH DEPOSITION SUBPOENA OF EMPLOYMENT RECORDS

 

Date: December 27, 2024

Time: 8:30 a.m.

Dept. 56

 

 

 

MOVING PARTY:  Plaintiff Maria Gabriela Vieyra (“Plaintiff”)

RESPONDING PARTY: Defendants California Medical Pharmacy, Inc. and David Liautaud (“Defendants”)

 

            The Court has considered the moving, opposition and reply papers.

 

BACKGROUND

            This is a harassment and constructive termination action arising out of an employment relationship. On December 12, 2023 Plaintiff filed a complaint (the “Complaint”) against Defendants and Does 1 through 100 alleging cause of action for: (1) harassment based on sex/gender/race and national origin in violation of the Fair Employment and Housing Act (“FEHA”); (2) discrimination based on sex/gender/race and national origin in violation of FEHA; (3) retaliation in violation of FEHA; (4) failure to prevent harassment, discrimination and retaliation in violation of FEHA; (5) sexual battery; (6) intentional infliction of emotional distress; and (7) constructive tortious termination in violation of public policy.  

 

             On November 26, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to quash Defendants’ deposition subpoena of employment records and request for sanctions (the “Motion”). Defendants filed an opposition (the “Opposition”) to the Motion on December 13, 2024, and Plaintiff filed a reply (the “Reply”) on December 18, 2024.

 

JUDICIAL NOTICE

            Although it is unnecessary to request judicial notice of a matter in the same file, pursuant to Defendants’ request, the Court takes judicial notice of: (1) The Complaint in this matter; and (2) this Court’s December 4, 2024 Order re: Motion to Quash, or in the Alternative, Modify, Defendants’ Deposition Subpoena of Records from a Medical Provider. (Evidence Code § 452, subd. (d).)

 

DISCUSSION

            Code of Civil Procedure (“CCP”) section 1987.1 states, “[w]hen a subpoena requires the attendance of a witness or the production of books, documents or other things before a court, or at the trial of an issue therein, …, upon motion reasonably made by the party, the witness, or any consumer described in Section 1985.3, or upon the court's own motion after giving counsel notice and an opportunity to be heard, may make an order quashing the subpoena entirely, modifying it, or directing compliance with it upon such terms or conditions as the court shall declare, including protective orders.” There is no requirement that the motion contain a meet-and-confer declaration demonstrating a good-faith attempt at informal resolution. (See Id.) A motion to quash does require a separate statement, unless the party being subpoenaed did not provide any response to the subpoena. (Rules of Court, rule 3.1345(a)(5), (b)(6).) 

 

“Unless otherwise limited by order of the court in accordance with this title, any party may obtain discovery regarding any matter, not privileged, that is relevant to the subject matter involved in the pending action or to the determination of any motion made in that action, if the matter either is itself admissible in evidence or appears reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence. Discovery may relate to the claim or defense of the party seeking discovery or of any other party to the action. Discovery may be obtained of the identity and location of persons having knowledge of any discoverable matter, as well as of the existence, description, nature, custody, condition, and location of any document, electronically stored information, tangible thing, or land or other property.” (CCP § 2017.010.) 

 

Plaintiff moves to quash the deposition subpoena issued to St. John’s Hospital, Plaintiff’s current employer, on the grounds that the subpoena “violates Plaintiff’s right to privacy, seeks

inadmissible character evidence, seeks irrelevant evidence, and Plaintiff has not waived her right

to Privacy.” (Mot. p. 4:20-22.) The subpoena seeks the following information: “Any and all writings, documents, records and electronically stored information, including, but not limited to, the personnel file, employment records, earnings records, applications for employment, payroll records, work absence records, incident reports, correspondence, employee progress reports, evaluations, records of workers' compensation claims, records of disability claims, records of wage claims, termination records, records of discrimination and/or harassment complaints, records of any lawsuits and records of disciplinary actions/reports pertaining to the employment of plaintiff Maria Gabriela Vieyra for the period from December 1, 2023 to the present.” (Mot. p. 6:21-28; Nevarez Decl., Ex 1.)

 

            Defendants argue that that information sought “will (1) assist the defendants in evaluating and refuting Vieyra’s extensive allegations of physical, emotional, and economic injuries and damages, (2) identify potential trial witnesses at least on the issues surrounding damages, (3) and will identify witnesses relevant to Vieyra’s credibility.” (Opp. p. 5:7-11.)

 

In the Complaint, Plaintiff seeks “damages for lost employment income and benefits, past and future, according to proof.” (Compl., p. 15.) Plaintiff also asserts that she continues to suffer from “depression, anxiety, stress, fear, embarrassment, humiliation, anger, lack of sleep, PTSD, lack of self-esteem, nightmares, weight gain, high blood pressure, and chest pains.” (Compendium of Exs., Ex B [Response to Form Interrogatories, No. 6.3].) Thus, the employment records sought are relevant to assessing Plaintiff’s damages claim for future lost employment income and benefits and to assessing her claim of ongoing emotional injuries.

 

Request for Sanctions

            The court shall impose monetary sanctions against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to quash a deposition notice, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust (CCP, § 2025.410 subd. (d).)

           

            Defendants request sanctions against Plaintiff and her counsel, jointly and severally, in the total amount of $5,940.00 based upon counsel’s rate of $495/hour for: (1) 10 hours researching and preparing the Opposition; and (2) 2.0 hours preparing for and traveling to the hearing on this matter. (Opp., Vásquez Decl., ¶ 2.) Although the Court has ruled in Defendants’ favor on this motion, the sanctions requested are excessive. Thus, a 9.0 hour reduction is appropriate here. Accordingly, Defendants’ request for sanctions is GRANTED in the reduced amount of $1,485.00.

 

            Plaintiff’s Motion to Quash Defendants’ Deposition Subpoena of Employment is DENIED.

            Plaintiff is ordered to pay sanctions to Defendants in the total amount of $1,485.00 within 20 days of this order.
