Case Number: 24STCV16648    Hearing Date: November 8, 2024    Dept: 55

NATURE OF PROCEEDINGS: Hearing on Defendant's Demurrer – without Motion to Strike

                                                                                                                                       

BACKGROUND

Plaintiff Richard J. Rosiak brought this action against his ex-wife Margarita Rosiak and defendants 1 to 10 seeking to quiet title to four Nevada properties.

The Court previously sustained a demurrer to Plaintiff’s original complaint with leave to amend.

Defendant demurs to the First Amended Complaint (FAC). Plaintiff opposes.

Defendant’s Demurrer – without Motion to Strike is sustained without leave to amend.

 

LEGAL STANDARD

When considering demurrers, courts read the allegations liberally and in context. Wilson v. Transit Authority of City of Sacramento (1962) 199 Cal.App.2d 716, 720-21. In a demurrer proceeding, the defects must be apparent on the face of the pleading or via proper judicial notice. Donabedian v. Mercury Ins. Co. (2004) 116 Cal.App.4th 968, 994. “A demurrer tests the pleading alone, and not on the evidence or facts alleged.” E-Fab, Inc. v. Accountants, Inc. Servs. (2007) 153 Cal.App.4th 1308, 1315. As such, courts assume the truth of the complaint’s properly pleaded or implied factual allegations. Ibid. However, it does not accept as true deductions, contentions, or conclusions of law or fact. Stonehouse Homes LLC v. City of Sierra Madre (2008) 167 Cal.App.4th 531, 538.

REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE

The Court grants Defendant’s Request for Judicial Notice (RJN) of the September 11, 2022, Nevada District Court’s Findings of Fact, Conclusions of Law, and Decision.

 

ANALYSIS

Defendant renews her argument that Plaintiff’s quiet title action is precluded by the Nevada divorce decree awarding Defendant the subject properties.

“Collateral estoppel, or issue preclusion, ‘precludes relitigation of issues argued and decided in prior proceedings.’” Gabriel v. Wells Fargo Bank, N.A. (2010) 188 Cal.App.4th 547, 556 (quoting, Mycogen Corp. v. Monsanto Co. (2002) 28 Cal.4th 888, 896). “Collateral estoppel precludes the relitigation of an issue only if (1) the issue is identical to an issue decided in a prior proceeding; (2) the issue was actually litigated; (3) the issue was necessarily decided; (4) the decision in the prior proceeding is final and on the merits; and (5) the party against whom collateral estoppel is asserted was a party to the prior proceeding or in privity with a party to the prior proceeding.” Zevnik v. Superior Court (2008) 159 Cal.App.4th 76, 82, 70 Cal.Rptr.3d 817.

“[A] decree of divorce which does not adjudicate or dispose of property interests does not bar a subsequent suit. On the other hand, a decree which does adjudicate property rights and makes a division is final as to the property which is actually divided.” Lewis v. Superior Court (1978) 77 Cal.App.3d 844, 847. As the Court previously concluded, all requirements for collateral estoppel are present here. The four properties Plaintiff seeks to quiet title to where previously the subject of divorce proceedings between Plaintiff and Defendant in Nevada. RJN at p. 124. The ownership of the properties, as between Plaintiff, both individually and as trustee, and Defendant was actually litigated and necessarily decided. RJN p. 96-99 ¶¶ 44-54, p. 124. As determined by the Nevada court, the 2012 transfer of the three properties on Villa De Conde Way to the trust was void. RJN p. 99, ¶ 54. In dividing the marital assets, the Nevada court awarded Defendant a 100% interest in each of the four properties at issue here. RJN p.124. Because the Nevada divorce proceeding is final, on the merits, and Plaintiff was a party to that proceeding he is precluded from relitigating the issue here.

Plaintiff fails to allege new facts showing that he has an interest in the subject properties, and instead alleges that the Nevada court lacked jurisdiction over the RJRP Family Trust  (the Trust) because it was never joined as a party. FAC ¶ 17. Moreover, as explained in the Court’s previous ruling, a trust is not a legal person who can be a party to an action, a trust can only sue and be sued through its trustee. Galdjie v. Darwish (2003) 113 Cal.App.4th 1331, 1343. It is undisputed that Plaintiff was a party to the divorce proceedings and that he was the trustee of the RJRP Family Trust.

In his opposition, Plaintiff fails to address the preclusive effect of the Nevada divorce decree, and instead argues about the status of the RJRP Trust. In ruling on the original demurrer, the Court noted that that the Trust had been revoked, based on the judicially noticeable “Notice of Revocation of the RJRP Family Trust Dated 5/8/1995” filed by Plaintiff in another case in May 2020, and the Nevada divorce decree revoking the trust. Plaintiff argues that neither revocation were valid, and that the trust was never revoked. Whether or not the Trust was revoked is irrelevant. The Nevada court was within its power to void the re-titling and transfer of the properties to the Trust, and to award those properties to Defendant in the divorce.

The Court again finds that collateral estoppel precludes Plaintiff’s causes of action and sustains the demurrer without leave to amend.

 

CONCLUSION

Defendant's demurrer is sustained without leave to amend.
