Counsel wishing to submit on a tentative ruling may inform the clerk or courtroom assisant in North Valley Department F47, 9425 Penfield Ave., Chatsworth, CA 91311, at (818) 407-2247.  Please be aware that unless all parties submit, the matter will still be called for hearing and may be argued by any appearing/non-submitting parties. If the matter is submitted on the court's tentative ruling by all parties, counsel for moving party shall give notice of ruling. This may be done by incorporating verbatim the court's tentative ruling. The tentative ruling may be extracted verbatim by copying and specially pasting, as unformatted text, from the Los Angeles Superior Court’s website, http://www.lasuperiorcourt.org. All hearings on law and motion and other calendar matters are generally NOT transcribed by a court reporter unless one is provided by the party(ies).

Case Number: 19CHCV00892    Hearing Date: June 5, 2024    Dept: F47

Dept. F47

Date: 6/5/24                                                                      TRIAL DATE: 9/9/24

Case #19CHCV00892

 

MOTION TO COMPEL

(Requests for Production of Documents, Set 1)

 

Motion filed on 11/7/23.

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendant Padita Ayana

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff Jamal Salim Nasrallah

NOTICE: ok

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order compelling Plaintiff to serve verified answers, without objections, to Form Interrogatories, Set 1, served on Plaintiff on 8/15/23, within 20 days of the hearing.  Additionally, Defendant Padita Ayana seeks an order imposing sanctions against Plaintiff and/or his attorney of record in the amount of $460.00.

 

RULING: The motion is granted as set forth below.

 

SUMMARY OF FACTS & PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

This action arises out of a landlord tenant dispute regarding property located at 13547 Osborne Street in Arleta, California. On 11/4/19, Plaintiff Jamal Salim Nasrallah (Plaintiff) filed this action alleging causes of action for: (1) forcible entry and (2) forcible detainer against Padita Ayana and Parawan Ayana.

 

On 5/10/22, default was entered against Padita Ayana.  Also on 5/10/22, Plaintiff filed a Doe Amendment naming Padita Ayana as Trustee of the Parawan Ayana Living Trust.  On 5/11/22, pursuant to Plaintiff’s request, the Court entered a dismissal without prejudice of Parawan Ayana. (See 5/11/22 Minute Order).  On 6/6/22, the Court entered an order pursuant to the stipulation of the parties setting aside the default entered against Padita Ayana.

 

On 12/7/22, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint which again asserted causes of action for: (1) forcible entry and (2) forcible detainer.  On 2/28/23, the Court sustained, in part, Padita Ayana’s demurrer to the First Amended Complaint and granted Padita Ayana’s motion to strike portions of the First Amended Complaint, both with leave to amend. (See 2/28/23 Minute Order). On 4/4/23, Plaintiff filed a Second Amended Complaint for forcible entry against Padita Ayana, individually (Padita), and as Trustee of the Parawan Ayana Living Trust (Trustee) (collectively, Defendant).  On 7/25/23, Defendant’s motion to strike portions of the Second Amended Complaint was denied. 

 

On 8/15/23, Padita served Plaintiff with Requests for Production of Documents, Set 1, making answers due 9/18/23.  (Petrosyan Decl. ¶¶4-5, Ex.A).  Despite being granted multiple extensions of time to respond, Plaintiff has failed to serve responses to the subject discovery.  (Petrosyan Decl. ¶¶6-11, Ex.B-F).    

Therefore, on 11/7/23, Padita filed and served the instant motion seeking an order compelling Plaintiff to serve verified answers, without objections, to Requests for Production of Documents, Set 1, served on Plaintiff on 8/15/23, within 20 days of the hearing.  Additionally, Defendant Padita Ayana seeks an order imposing sanctions against Plaintiff and/or his attorney of record in the amount of $460.00.  Plaintiff has not opposed or otherwise responded to the motion. 

 

ANALYSIS

 

Due to Plaintiff’s failure to serve timely responses to the subject Requests for Production of Documents, Padita is entitled to an order compelling Plaintiff to provide responses without objections.  CCP 2031.300(a), (b).  Additionally, due to Plaintiff’s and Plaintiff’s counsel’s failure to comply with their discovery obligations, sanctions in the amount of $460.00 are warranted against Plaintiff and Plaintiff’s counsel, Bryan Castorina.  CCP 2023.010(d); CCP 2031.300(c); (Petrosyan Decl. ¶12). 

 

CONCLUSION

 

The motion is granted.  Verified responses, without objections, to Requests for Production of Documents, Set 1, are due and sanctions against Plaintiff and/or Plaintiff’s counsel, Bryan Castorina, in the amount of $460.00 are payable within 30 days. 

 

 


Case Number: 21STCV18579    Hearing Date: June 5, 2024    Dept: F47

Dept. F47

Date: 6/5/24                                                                TRIAL DATE: 9/9/24

Case #21STCV18579

 

MOTION TO ALLOW A CLAIM FOR PUNITIVE DAMAGES

 

Original motion filed on 4/11/24.  Second Amended Notice of Motion filed on 5/8/24.

 

MOVING PARTY: Plaintiff Valeria Segoviano Hernandez

RESPONDING PARTY # 1: Defendant Walmart Inc.

RESPONDING PARTY # 2: Defendant The Clorox Company

NOTICE: ok

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order deeming various paragraphs regarding punitive damages against Defendant Walmart Inc. (¶¶32-53 and 61) and a 4th cause of action for Gross Negligence (¶¶79-86) against Defendant Walmart Inc. and The Clorox Company to be incorporated into Plaintiff’s Third Amended Complaint.  Additionally, Plaintiff seeks to add a prayer for punitive damages against all Defendants in relation to the 1st cause of action and a prayer for relief regarding the 4th cause of action which seeks compensatory and punitive damages against all Defendants.

 

Alternatively, Plaintiff requests an order granting her leave to amend to file a Fourth Amended Complaint which incorporates the proposed additions. 

 

RULING: The motion is denied without prejudice.   

 

SUMMARY OF FACTS & PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

This action arises out of an incident that occurred on 5/25/20 at a Walmart located in Stevenson Ranch, California.  On that date at the Walmart, Plaintiff Valeria Segoviano Hernandez  (Plaintiff) grabbed a bottle of Clorox bleach from the top shelf.  Plaintiff claims that the cap on the bottle was not secured causing it to spill all over her right arm causing her injury and permanent scarring.

 

On 5/18/21, Plaintiff filed this action against Defendants Walmart Inc. (Walmart) and The Clorox Company (Clorox) for general negligence, premises liability and products liability.  On 6/21/21, as a matter of right, Plaintiff filed a First Amended Complaint alleging the same three causes of action.  On 8/10/23, pursuant to stipulation and order, Plaintiff filed a Second Amended Complaint alleging causes of action for: (1) Strict Liability – Design Defect, (2) Negligence – Failure to Recall and (3) Negligence.  The Second Amended Complaint included a claim for punitive damages against Clorox.  Clorox filed a motion to strike the claim and in response, on 1/10/24, Plaintiff, again pursuant to a stipulation and order, filed the operative Third Amended Complaint alleging causes of action for: (1) Strict Liability – Design Defect, (2) Negligence – Failure to Recall and (3) Negligence.  The Third Amended Complaint also includes a claim for punitive damages against Clorox in relation Plaintiff’s 1st cause of action.  (Third Amended Complaint (TAC) ¶39). 

On 3/20/24, the Court denied Clorox’s motion to strike various portions of the Third Amended Complaint, including allegations regarding punitive damages against Clorox.  (See 3/20/24 Minute Order). 

 

On 4/11/24, Plaintiff filed and served the instant motion seeking an order deeming various paragraphs regarding punitive damages against Walmart (¶¶32-53 and 61) and a 4th cause of action for Gross Negligence (¶¶79-86) against Walmart and Clorox to be incorporated into Plaintiff’s Third Amended Complaint.  Additionally, Plaintiff seeks to add a prayer for punitive damages against all Defendants in relation to the 1st cause of action and to add a prayer for relief regarding the 4th cause of action which seeks compensatory and punitive damages against all Defendants.  Alternatively, Plaintiff requests an order granting her leave to amend to file a Fourth Amended Complaint which incorporates the proposed additions.  Both Walmart and Clorox have opposed the motion.  Plaintiff has filed a combined reply to the oppositions. 

 

ANALYSIS

 

Plaintiff has failed to provide proper notice of the relief sought.  See CRC 3.1110(a).  Plaintiff has titled this motion as a “Motion to Allow a Claim for Punitive Damages Against Defendant Walmart, Inc.” and then lists numerous paragraphs Plaintiff seeks to have “incorporated within her Third Amended Complaint.”  (See Notice of Motion, p.1:16-18, p.2:5-6).  Confusingly, Plaintiff seeks to incorporate “new” paragraphs numbered 32-53 into her Third Amended Complaint when paragraphs with the same numbers already exist in the pleading and without explaining how that will affect the paragraphs which already exist.  (See Notice of Motion, p.2:9-p.7:28; Third Amended Complaint).  Plaintiff also seeks to add a paragraph 61 regarding punitive damages; however, it is not clear where/in what cause of action that allegation would be included.  (See Notice of Motion, p.8:3-5).  Additionally, despite the title of the motion indicating that it only seeks to add a claim for punitive damages against Walmart, Plaintiff also seeks to add the 4th cause of action for Gross Negligence against both Walmart and Clorox.  (See Notice of Motion, p.8:8-p.9:20; Third Amended Complaint).

 

Plaintiff supports this unconventional request to deem various paragraphs as part of her current Third Amended Complaint by citing CCP 187, regarding this Court’s jurisdiction, and CCP 128(a)(3) and (8), regarding this Court’s inherent power to control its proceedings.  Since  specific statutes (i.e., CCP 473(a), CCP 576) and court rules (CRC 3.1324) exist regarding amending pleadings, there is no need for this Court to exercise its inherent equitable powers to address a gap in the law with regard to the relief requested.  (See Motion, p.12:3-20).

 

As an alternative to the foregoing, Plaintiff requests that the Court treat the instant motion as a motion for leave to amend.  However, Plaintiff has failed to comply with all of the procedural requirements for such relief. 

 

CRC 3.1324 provides, in relevant part:

 

“a) Contents of motion

A motion to amend a pleading before trial must:

(1) Include a copy of the proposed amendment or amended pleading, which must be serially numbered to differentiate it from previous pleadings or amendments;

(2) State what allegations in the previous pleading are proposed to be deleted, if any, and where, by page, paragraph, and line number, the deleted allegations are located; and

(3) State what allegations are proposed to be added to the previous pleading, if any, and where, by page, paragraph, and line number, the additional allegations are located.

(b) Supporting declaration

A separate declaration must accompany the motion and must specify:

(1) The effect of the amendment;

(2) Why the amendment is necessary and proper;

(3) When the facts giving rise to the amended allegations were discovered; and

(4) The reasons why the request for amendment was not made earlier.”

 

Plaintiff has failed to clearly state what allegations are proposed to be added and/or deleted and where by page, paragraph and line number.  CRC 3.1324(a)(2), (3).  As noted above, the motion does not explain the renumbering of various paragraphs in the proposed Fourth Amended Complaint from the Third Amended Complaint, etc. 

 

Additionally, while the declaration filed in support of the motion seems sufficient to satisfy the requirements of CRC 3.1324(b) as to the allegations regarding punitive damages against Walmart, it fails as to the addition of the new 4th cause of action against either Walmart or Clorox, especially since case law holds that California does not recognize a distinct common law cause of action for gross negligence apart from negligence and Plaintiff has already alleged a negligence claim against both Walmart and Clorox in her 3rd cause of action.  See Joshi (2022) 80 CA5th 814, 825; Jimenez (2015) 237 CA4th 546, 552 fn.3.  Further, the declaration admits that the facts recently learned through discovery support Plaintiff’s claim for gross negligence and claim for punitive damages against Walmart, not Clorox.  (See Eagan Decl. ¶5). 

 

CONCLUSION

 

The motion is denied without prejudice.

Eagan in violation of CRC 3.1110(f)(4).  The parties are warned that failure to comply with this rule in the future may result in matters being continued so that papers can be re-filed in compliance with the rule, papers not being considered and/or the imposition of sanctions. 


Case Number: 22CHCV00016    Hearing Date: June 5, 2024    Dept: F47

Dept. F47

Date: 6/5/24

Case #22CHCV00016

 

SUMMARY JUDGMENT

 

Motion filed 5/30/24.

 

MOVING PARTY: Plaintiff Chris LaCroix

RESPONDING PARTY: Defendant JW Brands, LLC

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order granting Plaintiff Chris LaCroix’s motion for summary judgment in favor of Plaintiff.

 

RULING: The motion is placed off calendar.

 

SUMMARY OF FACTS & PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

This action arises out of an agreement between Defendant 024 Management Services, Inc. (024 Management), Defendant JW Brands LLC (JW Brands) (collectively, Defendants) and Plaintiffs Chris Lacroix and Martin Willerford (collectively, Plaintiffs).  (Complaint, Ex.A).  Plaintiffs allege that in or about 2020, Plaintiffs and Defendants entered a written agreement whereby Defendants hired Plaintiffs to operate a division of its business, Plaintiffs would provide services, pricing and terms, and Defendants would pay for said products. (Complaint ¶6, p.3).  Plaintiffs further allege that they provided a loan of startup capital to Defendants.  (Id. and Ex.A thereto). Plaintiffs allege that Defendants breached the agreement by preventing Plaintiffs from providing services and refusing to return the startup loan funds. (Complaint ¶12, p.4).

 

On 1/10/22, Plaintiffs filed this action against 024 Management Services, Inc., JW Brands LLC, Michael Wayman, Tim McCandless and Derek Markstein for: (1) Fraud, (2) Breach of Written Contract, (3) Quantum Meruit, (4) Account Stated and (5) Open Book Account.  On 10/4/22, JW Brands and Derek Markstein filed separate answers to the complaint.  On 10/10/22, default was entered against 024 Management and on 10/11/22, default was entered against Tim McCandless and Michael Wayman.

 

On 12/12/23, Markstein and JW Brands filed a cross-complaint against 024 Management, Wayman and McCandless for: (1) Intentional or Negligence Misrepresentation, (2) Concealment, (3) Equitable Indemnity, (4) Implied and Comparative Indemnity, (5) Contribution and Equitable Apportionment and (6) Declaratory Relief. 

 

On 11/20/23, Plaintiffs dismissed Markstein from the complaint.  On 1/10/24, the Court granted JW Brands motion for judgment on the pleadings as to the 1st cause of action for Fraud and the 3rd cause of action for Quantum Meruit in Plaintiffs’ complaint.

 

On 4/29/24, JW Brands attorney filed a substitution of attorney form indicating that JW Brands was representing itself. 

On 5/30/24, Plaintiff Chris LaCroix (LaCroix) filed the instant motion seeking an order granting summary judgment in his favor.  No proof of service for the motion has been filed.  No opposition or other response to the motion has been filed.

 

ANALYSIS

 

CCP 437c(a)(2) requires that the notice of a motion for summary judgment be served at least 75 days before the hearing date, if personally served.  As noted above, no proof of service for the motion has been filed.  LaCroix was required to file a proof of service for the motion at least five court days before the hearing date.  CRC 3.1300(c). 

 

The Court further notes that while the separate statement and memorandum of points and authorities filed in support of the motion are dated March 7, 2024, the notice of motion is dated  May 30, 2024.  (See Separate Statement, p.2:19, Memorandum of Points and Authorities, p.12:17; Notice of Motion, p.2:6).  As such, the notice of motion could not have been served at least 75 days before the 6/5/24 hearing date as required by CCP 437c(a)(2).    

 

As noted above, no opposition or other response to the motion has been filed to cure the defects in notice. 

 

CONCLUSION

 

Based on the foregoing, the hearing on the motion is placed off calendar.
