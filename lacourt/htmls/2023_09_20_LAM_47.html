DEPARTMENT 47 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 19STCV26512    Hearing Date: September 20, 2023    Dept: 47

Tentative Ruling

 

Judge Theresa M. Traber, Department 47

 

 

HEARING DATE:     September 20, 2023               TRIAL DATE [Phase 1]: July 14, 2023

 

CASE:                         Frontline Medical Associates, Inc. v. Bird, Marella, Boxer, Wolpert, Nessim, Drooks, Lincenberg & Rhow, P.C., et al.

 

CASE NO.:                 19STCV26512

 

     

 

MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE OVERLENGTH MOTION

 

MOVING PARTY:               Defendants Bird, Marella, Boxer, Wolpert, Nessim, Drooks, Lincenberg & Rhow, P.C. and Benjamin Gluck;

 

RESPONDING PARTY(S): No opposition on eCourt as of 9/15/23

 

CASE HISTORY:

  • 07/30/19: Complaint filed.
  • 12/16/21: First Amended Complaint filed.

 

STATEMENT OF MATERIAL FACTS AND/OR PROCEEDINGS:

           

            Plaintiff alleges that the lawyer Defendants made misrepresentations to it so that it would pay them $600,000 to provide services to another of their clients, Paul Turley.

 

            Defendants move for leave to file an overlength motion for terminating sanctions.

 

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

            Defendants move for leave to file an overlength motion for terminating sanctions.

 

            Ordinarily, the memorandum of points and authorities for a motion may not exceed 20 pages for a motion for summary judgment or summary adjudication, or 15 pages for any other type of motion. (Cal. Rules of Court Rule 3.1113(d).) However, “[a] party may apply to the court ex parte but with written notice of the application to the other parties, at least 24 hours before the memorandum is due, for permission to file a longer memorandum.” (Rule 3.1113(e).) The application must state reasons why the argument cannot be made within the limit provided by the rules. (Id.)

 

            Defendants seek leave to file a motion for terminating sanctions that is 27 pages in length. According to Defendants, the nature of the motion and the arguments advanced therein rely on a significant portion of the factual record in this case, and the additional length is necessary for a full recitation of the facts. The Court finds, given the lack of any opposition by Plaintiff and the complex procedural and factual history of this case, that there is good cause to permit Defendants to file an overlength motion for terminating sanctions so that they may fully and accurately state the factual basis for their motion.

 

            Accordingly, Defendants’ Motion for Leave to File Overlength Motion is GRANTED.

 

            Defendants’ Motion for Terminating Sanctions is deemed filed this date and set for hearing pursuant to the Court’s September 15, 2023 Minute Order.

 

            The parties are ordered to adhere to the briefing schedule on this motion ordered by the Court on September 15, 2023.

 

            Moving parties to give notice.

 

IT IS SO ORDERED.

 

Dated:   September 20, 2023                          ___________________________________

                                                                                    Theresa M. Traber

                                                                                    Judge of the Superior Court

By submitting on the tentative you have, in essence, waived your right to be present at the hearing, and you should be aware that the court may not adopt the tentative, and may issue an order which modifies the tentative ruling in whole or in part.

Tentative Ruling

 

Judge Theresa M. Traber, Department 47

CASE:                         Nabil Mihoubi v. General Motors, LLC

 

CASE NO.:                 22STCV33724           

 

MOTION FOR ORDER DEEMING TRUTH OF MATTERS IN REQUESTS FOR ADMISSIONS

 

MOVING PARTY:               Plaintiff Nabil Mihoubi

 

RESPONDING PARTY(S): Defendant General Motors, LLC

 

STATEMENT OF MATERIAL FACTS AND/OR PROCEEDINGS:

           

            This is a lemon law action filed on October 17, 2022. Plaintiff purchased a 2022 Cadillac Escalade which is alleged to be defective.

 

Plaintiff moves for an order deeming the truth of matters in requests for admissions.

           

TENTATIVE RULING:

 

Plaintiff’s Motion for Order Deeming the Truth of Matters in Requests for Admissions is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s request for sanctions is GRANTED in the amount of $411.65 against Defendant and its counsel, jointly and severally. Payment is to be made within 10 days of this order.

 

DISCUSSION:

 

Plaintiff moves for an order deeming the truth of matters in requests for admissions.

 

//

 

 

Legal Standard

 

When a party to whom requests for admission are directed fails to respond, the party propounding the requests may move for an order that the truth of any matters specified in the requests be deemed admitted. (Code Civ. Proc. § 2033.280(b).) “The court shall make this order [deem the requests admitted], unless it finds that the party to whom the request for admissions have been directed has served, before the hearing on the motion, a proposed response to the requests for admission that is in substantial compliance with section 2033.220.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 2033.280(c).)

 

Analysis

 

            Plaintiff requests that the Court issue an order deeming the truth of the matters in Plaintiff’s requests for admissions propounded to Defendant. Plaintiff served Requests for Admissions, Set One, on January 31, 2023 by mail. (Declaration of Nadia Yashar ISO Mot. ¶ 2, Exh. 1.) Defendant’s responses were therefore due on March 6, 2023. (Id. ¶ 3.) To date, no responses have been received. (Id. ¶ 5.)

 

            Plaintiff also requests that the Court order Defendant to provide complete responses to the requests for admission without objections. Plaintiff’s request is not authorized by any provision in the Code of Civil Procedure, and the Court therefore refuses to consider it further.

 

            Defendant opposes this motion on the basis that Plaintiff did not adequately meet and confer with Defendant and that the failure to respond was due to a calendaring error and responses “will be served” by the date of the hearing on this motion. These contentions are irrelevant. Plaintiff is under no obligation to meet and confer with Defendant before filing a motion to deem the truth of matters in requests for admissions. All that Plaintiff must do is establish that requests for admission were properly served, and no response was provided within the time authorized by statute or agreed upon by the parties. Plaintiff has done so, and Defendant concedes that, as of now, no responses have been served.

 

            Plaintiff is therefore entitled to an order deeming the truth of the matters in requests for admissions.

 

Sanctions

 

            Plaintiff requests sanctions for Defendant’s failure to provide timely responses to requests for admissions.

 

Failure to respond or submit to an authorized method of discovery is a misuse of the discovery process for which sanctions may be imposed. (Code Civ. Proc. §§ 2023.010(d); 2023.030(a).) Sanctions are mandatory on the party or attorney or both whose failure to serve a timely response to requests for admission necessitated a motion to deem requests for admissions as admitted. (Code Civ. Proc. § 2033.280(c).)

 

Here, Plaintiff requests sanctions against Defendant and its counsel, jointly and severally, in the amount of $1,681.65. This request is based on one hour of attorney time incurred at a rate of $350 per hour, plus $61.65 in filing fees, plus an additional 3.5 hours anticipated attorney time and $45 in anticipated costs. (Yashar Decl.  ¶¶ 8-9.) The Court declines to award fees and costs not actually incurred and will therefore reduce the award of sanctions to $411.65, representing the fees and costs actually incurred.

 

CONCLUSION:

 

            Accordingly, Plaintiff’s Motion for Order Deeming the Truth of Matters in Requests for Admissions is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff’s request for sanctions is GRANTED in the amount of $411.65 against Defendant and its counsel, jointly and severally. Payment is to be made within 10 days of this order.

 

Moving Party to give notice.

 

IT IS SO ORDERED.
