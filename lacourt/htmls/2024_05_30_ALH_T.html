DEPARTMENT T LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 19STCV28377    Hearing Date: May 30, 2024    Dept: T

MOTION TO QUASH SERVICE OF SUMMONS

Moving Party:                         Defendant Estate of Lillian Horwitz

Responding Party:                   Plaintiff Clive Duvall

Tentative Ruling:                    Denied

I.          INTRODUCTION

This is an action for negligence arising from a vehicle collision which took place in August 2017. Plaintiff Clive Duvall filed the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) against the Estate of Lillian Horwitz, the Estate of Nathan Horwitz, and Does 1 through 50 on December 19, 2023.

On February 21, 2024, specially appearing defendant Estate of Lillian Horwitz (“The Estate” or “Defendant”) filed the instant motion to quash service of summons due to untimely service. Plaintiff filed his opposition on April 16, 2024, and Defendant replied on May 22, 2024.

II.        LEGAL STANDARD

Pursuant to CCP section 418.10(a)(1), a defendant may move to quash service of summons on or before the last day to plead in response to the complaint or within such further time as the Court may allow for good cause. 

“When a motion to quash is properly brought, the burden of proof is placed upon the plaintiff to establish the facts of jurisdiction by a preponderance of the evidence.” (Aquila, Inc. v. Superior Court (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 556, 568.) A summons is the means by which a court acquires personal jurisdiction over a civil defendant, and compels them to appear in court. (Mannesmann DeMag, Ltd. v. Superior Court (1985) 172 Cal.App.3d 1118, 1122.) A defendant is under no duty to respond in any way to a defectively served summons. (Kappel v. Barlett (1988) 200 Cal.App.3d 1457, 1466; Ruttenberg v. Ruttenberg (1997) 53 Cal.App.4th 801, 808.) It makes no difference that defendant had actual knowledge of the action, as such knowledge does not dispense with the statutory requirements for service of summons. (Ibid.) 

III.       REQUEST FOR JUDICIAL NOTICE

            Defendant requests judicial notice pursuant to Evidence Code §§ 450, 451, 452 and 454, of the following documents:

1.      Lillian Horwitz’s certificate of death issued by the Registrar-recorder/county clerk of the County of Los Angeles on March 8, 2023 (Slack Decl., Exhibit A);

2.      This Court’s docket for this matter reflecting that no proof of service has been filed showing service on Lillian Horwitz or her Estate before August 12, 2022;

3.      This Court’s March 28, 2023 minute order as amended nunc pro tunc on April 14, 2023 dismissing Specially Appearing Defendant Estate of Lillian Horwitz, Deceased (Slack Decl., Exhibit B);

4.      This Court’s December 15, 2023 minute order (Slack Decl., Exhibit C);

The court may take judicial notice of “official acts of the legislative, executive, and judicial departments of the United States and of any state of the United States,” “[r]ecords of (1) any court of this state or (2) any court of record of the United States or of any state of the United States,” and “[f]acts and propositions that are not reasonably subject to dispute and are capable of immediate and accurate determination by resort to sources of reasonably indisputable accuracy.” (Evid. Code § 452, subds. (c), (d), and (h); see also People v. Terry (1974) 38 Cal.App.3d 432, 439, holding that judicial notice may be taken of a death certificate.) 

The Court may take judicial notice of the existence of the records, but not the truth of matters asserted in such records. (Sosinsky v. Grant (1992) 6 Cal.App.4th 1548, 1565).  Defendant’s request for judicial notice is GRANTED as to the existence of the records, but not the truth of the matters contained in the records.

IV.       DISCUSSION

Deadline to Serve

Defendant argues that Plaintiff is not entitled to maintain an action against The Estate under Probate Code §§ 550 et seq. as Plaintiff failed to serve the summons on The Estate through Decedent Lillian Horwitz’s (“Decedent”) insurer under Probate Code § 552(a) on or before August 14, 2022, which is three years from the start of this action as required by Code of Civil Procedure § 583.210(a).

Plaintiff contends that this Court already heard and rejected this argument on December 15, 2023, when it granted Plaintiff’s motion to substitute and ruled that the three-year deadline to serve Plaintiff’s Complaint on the Estate was tolled due to Decedent’s death on September 10, 2018. Indeed, the Court’s December 15, 2023 Minute Order found:

“Under CCP § 583.240(a), “In computing the time within which service must be made” pursuant to CCP § 583.210(a), the time when the “defendant was not amenable to the process of the court” is excluded. When a defendant has died and there is no personal representative, the defendant is not amenable to the service of process. (Polony v. White (1974) 43 Cal.App.3d 44, 48 [“If a defendant is dead and no estate has been probated, no one is in existence on whom process can be served”].)

As such, under Polony, supra, 43 Cal.App.3d at 48, the time when Defendant was not amenable to service of process is excluded. . . .

Further, the heirs or executor of the estate could have probated the estate of the decedent, thereby giving notice of the ability to file claims against the estate. See Wills v. Williams, 47 Cal.App.3d 941, 945 (1975).”

(12/15/23 Minute Order, pp. 5-6.) Decedent’s estate was not probated and to date no personal representative has been appointed. (Khoshnou Decl. ISO Plaintiff’s Opposition, ¶ 2.)

Statute of limitations

Defendant argues that the statute of limitations for an action under Probate Code §§ 550 et seq. expired on February 8, 2021. Therefore, even if the three-year deadline to serve the summons on Decedent was tolled, Plaintiff’s First Amended Complaint is time-barred by the statute of limitations. The Court disagrees. On December 15, 2023, this Court granted Plaintiff’s motion to substitute the Estate in place of decedent. As a matter of law, such substitution related back to the date of original filing of a Complaint – i.e. August 12, 2019. (See 12/15/23 Minute Order, at p. 4.)

IV.       CONCLUSION

            Defendant’s motion to quash service of summons is DENIED.   Moving party to give notice. 


Case Number: 22STCV07645    Hearing Date: May 30, 2024    Dept: T

Motion to Enforce Settlement

Moving Party:                         Plaintiff Debra Hoyte

Responding Party:                  None

Tentative Ruling:                    Denied.

The court considered the moving papers. No opposition has been filed as of May 28, 2024. 

BACKGROUND

On March 2, 2022, Plaintiff Debra Hoyte commenced this premises liability action against Defendants Douglas Emmett Realty Fund 2002, LLC, Douglas Emmett Management, LLC, Douglas Emmett Builders, and JS Glass & Mirror, Inc.

On February 6, 2024, a Notice of Settlement of Entire Case was filed.

On March 5, 2024, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to enforce settlement pursuant to CCP § 664.6. The motion is unopposed.

LEGAL STANDARD

            Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6 provides: “If parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement. If requested by the parties, the court may retain jurisdiction over the parties to enforce the settlement until performance in full of the terms of the settlement.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 664.6.)

            Strict compliance with the statutory requirements is necessary before a court can enforce a settlement agreement under this statute. (Sully-Miller Contracting Co. v. Gledson/Cashman Construction, Inc. (2002) 103 Cal.App.4th 30, 37.) The settlement must include the signatures of the parties seeking to enforce the agreement, and against whom enforcement is sought. (J.B.B. Investment Partners, Ltd. v. Fair (2014) 232 Cal.App.4th 974, 985.)

DISCUSSION

            No agreement is attached to the motion and Plaintiff even states that one Defendant has not executed the agreement. Plaintiff only attaches emails between counsels but does not represent that an agreement is contained therein. Strict compliance is required to enforce a settlement under CCP § 664.6. Without proof of a fully executed settlement agreement between the parties seeking enforcement, and the parties in which enforcement is sought against, the Court cannot grant the motion.

            Based on the foregoing, the motion is DENIED.  Moving Party is ordered to give notice of this ruling.
