DEPARTMENT F49 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

Counsel wishing to submit on a tentative ruling may inform the clerk or courtroom assistant in North Valley Department F49, 9425 Penfield Ave., Chatsworth, CA 91311, at (818) 407-2249.  Please be aware that unless all parties submit, the matter will still be called for hearing and may be argued by any appearing/non-submitting parties. If the matter is submitted on the court's tentative ruling by all parties, counsel for moving party shall give notice of ruling. This may be done by incorporating verbatim the court's tentative ruling. The tentative ruling may be extracted verbatim by copying and pasting, as unformatted text, from the Los Angeles Superior Court’s website, http://www.lasuperiorcourt.org.
All hearings on law and motion and other calendar matters are generally NOT transcribed by a court reporter unless one is provided by the party(ies). 


Case Number: 23CHCV00689    Hearing Date: October 21, 2024    Dept: F49

Dept. F49

Date: 10/21/24

Case Name: James Hoseini v. Reza Abad; Aiden Bohlouli; Teza Products, Inc.; and Does 1-50

Case No. 23CHCV00689

 

LOS ANGELES SUPERIOR COURT

NORTH VALLEY DISTRICT

DEPARTMENT F49

 

JUNE 17, 2024

 

MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES; ISSUE AND/OR EVIDENTIARY SANCTIONS AGAINST PLAINTIFF FOR FAILURE TO COMPLY WITH THE COURT’S JUNE 17, 2024, ORDER; OR ALTERNATIVELY FOR AN ORDER FOR SANCTIONS

Los Angeles Superior Court Case No. 23CHCV00689

 

Motion filed: 8/2/24

 

MOVING PARTY: Defendant/Cross-Complainant Aiden Bohlouli

RESPONDING PARTY: Plaintiff James Hoseini

NOTICE: OK.

 

RELIEF REQUESTED: An order from this Court compelling compliance with the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order, which granted Defendant/Cross-Complainant Aiden Bohlouli’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff’s Responses to Request for Production of Documents, Set Two, and imposing applicable sanctions under Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.300, subdivision (c), and awarding Defendant/Cross-Complainant Aiden Bohlouli monetary sanctions against Plaintiff James Hoseini in the amount of $3,950.00.

 

TENTATIVE RULING: The motion is GRANTED. The request for monetary sanction is GRANTED IN PART.

 

BACKGROUND

 

This action stems from alleged fraud by joint venture partners.  

 

On March 8, 2023, Plaintiff/Cross-Defendant James Hoseini (“Hoseini”) initiated this action against Defendants Reza Abad, Aiden Bohlouli (“Bohlouli”), Teza Products, Inc. (collectively, “Defendants”), and Does 1-50. Subsequently, on March 20, 2023, Hoseini filed the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) against all Defendants, alleging the following causes of action: (1) fraud, (2) breach of written contract, (3) breach of fiduciary duty, (4) elder abuse, (5) accounting, and (6) constructive trust. Subsequently, on April 24, 2023, Defendants filed their Answer to the FAC.

 

On the same day, April 24, 2023, Defendants filed their Cross-Complaint against Hoseini and Roes 1 to 20, which was subsequently amended on September 1, 2023, to join Teza Doors, Inc., as an additional Cross-Complainant. This operative First Amended Cross-Complaint (“FACC”) alleges the following causes of action: (1) fraud, (2) conversion, (3) constructive trust, and (4) permanent injunction.

 

On June 17, 2024, the Court granted Bohlouli’s Motion to Compel Plaintiff Hoseini’s Responses to Requests for Production of Documents (“RFP”), Set Two. (2024/6/17 Minute Order.)

 

Subsequently, on August 2, 2024, Bohlouli filed the instant Motion to Compel Plaintiff Hoseini’s Further Responses to the RFP, Set Two, and to Compel Compliance with the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order (the “Motion”). On October 9, 2024, Hoseini filed his Opposition to the Motion, and Bohlouli filed his Reply on October 14, 2024.

 

ANALYSIS

 

“If a party then fails to obey the order compelling a response, the court may make those orders that are just, including the imposition of an issue sanction, an evidence sanction, or a terminating sanction.... In lieu of or in addition to this sanction, the court may impose a monetary sanction.” (Code Civ. Procs., § 2031.300, subd. (c).)

 

A.    Failure to Comply with a Court Order Compelling Responses

 

The Court’s June 17, 2024, order granted Bohlouli’s motion to compel Plaintiff Hoseini’s responses to RFP, Set Two. Hoseini’s responses were “due within 30 days without objection.” (2024/6/17 Minute Order.)

 

The Code of Civil Procedure provides separate statutes for failing to obey an order compelling response to a demand for production (see Code Civ. Procs., § 2031.300, subd. (c)) and for failing to obey an order compelling further responses (see Code Civ. Procs., § 2031.310, subd. (i)).

As a preliminary matter, Bohlouli cites both code sections as authorities for the instant Motion. However, the Court finds that only the Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.300 subdivision (c) applies to the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order.

 

Here, Bohlouli argues that Hoseini’s responses, which were served on July 14 and subsequently supplemented on July 15, 2024, are improper and non-compliant with the code requirements, failing to obey the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order.

Bohlouli asserts that Hoseini’s responses contain no caption, do not identify the propounding party and the responding party, nor the set number, and were unverified. Furthermore, Bohlouli contends that Hoseini’s responses to RFP, Set Two, Nos. 5-13 do not comply with the requirements of the Code of Civil Procedure. The Court agrees.

 

Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.210 provides, in pertinent part:

 

“(a) The party to whom a demand ... has been directed shall respond separately to each item or category of item by any of the following:

(1) A statement that the party will comply with the particular demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling by the date set for the inspection, copying, testing, or sampling pursuant to paragraph (2) of subdivision (c) of Section 2031.030 and any related activities.

(2) A representation that the party lacks the ability to comply with the demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling of a particular item or category of item.

.....

(b) In the first paragraph of the response immediately below the title of the case, there shall appear the identity of the responding party, the set number, and the identity of the demanding party.

(c) Each statement of compliance, each representation, and each objection in the response shall bear the same number and be in the same sequence as the corresponding item or category in the demand, but the text of that item or category need not be repeated.”

           

Additionally, “A representation of inability to comply with the particular demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling shall affirm that a diligent search and a reasonable inquiry has been made in an effort to comply with that demand. This statement shall also specify whether the inability to comply is because the particular item or category has never existed, has been destroyed, has been lost, misplaced, or stolen, or has never been, or is no longer, in the possession, custody, or control of the responding party. The statement shall set forth the name and address of any natural person or organization known or believed by that party to have possession, custody, or control of that item or category of item.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.230.)

 

Here, Hoseini’s responses merely state:

 

“Request# 5 please see the attached document

Request# 6 same documents as# 5

Request# 7 same document as# 5

RRequest # 8 dont have any

Request# 9 do not have any

Request # 10 do not have any

Request# 11 do not have any

Request# 12 do not have any

Request# 13 do not have any”

 

(Cohen Decl. Ex. “3.”)

 

The Court finds that the responses to RFP, Set Two, Nos. 8-13 constitute “a representation of inability to comply with the particular demand.” However, they fail to specify “whether the inability to comply is because the particular item or category has never existed, has been destroyed, has been lost, misplaced, or stolen, or has never been, or is no longer, in the possession, custody, or control of the responding party” as mandated by the applicable code section. Nor do the responses “set forth the name and address of any natural person or organization known or believed by that party to have possession, custody, or control of that item or category of item,” failing to meet the requirements outlined under Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.230.

 

Bohlouli also argues that responses to RFP, Set Two, were non-responsive to the demands, which require Hoseini to produce all communications between himself and Moses Abonal, as well as between himself and Brett Lewis. (Mot. at p. 3.) Furthermore, Bohlouli contends that the responses were incomplete because these communications “are clearly in his possession, since they were previously produced redacted, but were not produced now un-redacted[.]” (Ibid.)

 

The Court finds that Hoseini’s responses to RFP, Set Two, Nos.5-7, contain two documents, both titled “Joint Venture and Operating Agreement.” (Cohen Decl. Ex. “5.”) However, merely attaching the documents, without further explanation or context, does not appear to be fully responsive to the demands for documents regarding “communications”.

 

            The Court does not need to resolve the factual dispute regarding whether Hoseini has additional responsive documents concerning communications in his possession. Even if Hoseini posits that he is unable to comply with the production, he is required to specify the reasons for this inability, in accordance with Code of Civil Procedure section 2031.230, which Hoseini has failed to do. It is clear that this non-compliance stems from substantive deficiencies rather than mere technical deviations.

 

            The Court also finds Plaintiff’s argument that the responses “are more than adequate” to be unpersuasive and insufficient to override the Court’s findings outlined above.

 

            Based on the foregoing, the Court finds Hoseini has failed to produce code-compliant responses to RFP, Set Two, as required by the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order.

 

Accordingly, the Court GRANTS the Motion to Compel Plaintiff Hoseini to produce verified, code-compliant responses to RFP, Set Two.

B.     Sanctions

Pursuant to the authority of Code of Civil Procedure sections 2031,300, subdivision (c), the Court finds it just to impose monetary sanctions against Plaintiff Hoseini for his failure to comply with the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order and to produce code-compliant responses.

 

Utilizing the lodestar approach, and in view of the totality of the circumstances, the Court finds that the total and reasonable amount of attorney’s fees and costs incurred for the work performed in connection with the Motion is $1,264.50, calculated based on a reasonable hourly rate of $400.00 for three hours reasonably spent, plus $64.50 for filing fee.

           

CONCLUSION

 

Defendant/Cross-Complainant Aiden Bohlouli’s Motion to Compel Further Responses to Requests for Production of Documents, Set Two, due to Plaintiff’s failure to comply with the Court’s June 17, 2024, Order, is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff James Hoseini is ordered to provide verified, code-compliant responses to Aiden Bohlouli’s Requests for Production of Documents, Set Two, within 20 days.

 

Defendant/Cross-Complainant Aiden Bohlouli’s request for monetary sanctions is GRANTED IN PART.

 

Plaintiff James Hoseini is ordered to pay $1,264.50 in sanctions to Defendant/Cross-Complainant Aiden Bohlouli’s counsel.

 

Moving party to give notice.
