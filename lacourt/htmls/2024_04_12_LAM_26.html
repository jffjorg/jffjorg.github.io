DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS





1. If you wish to submit on the tentative ruling,
please email the clerk at SMCdept26@lacourt.org (and “cc” all
other parties in the same email) no later than 7:30 am on
the day of the hearing, and please notify all other parties in advance that you
will not be appearing at the hearing.  Include the word "SUBMISSION" in all caps in the
subject line and include your name, contact information, the case number, and
the party you represent in the body of the email. If you submit on the
tentative and elect not to appear at the hearing, the opposing party may
nevertheless appear at the hearing and argue the motion, and the Court may
decide not to adopt the tentative ruling.




2. 
For any motion where no parties submit to the tentative ruling in
advance, and no parties appear at the motion hearing, the Court may elect to
either adopt the tentative ruling or take the motion off calendar, in its
discretion.




3. PLEASE DO NOT USE THIS
EMAIL (SMCdept26@lacourt.org) FOR ANY PURPOSE OTHER THAN TO SUBMIT TO A TENTATIVE
RULING.  The Court will not read or
respond to emails sent to this address for any other purpose.




4. IN ORDER TO IMPLEMENT
PHYSICAL DISTANCING GOING FORWARD AND UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, THE COURT STRONGLY
ENCOURAGES ALL COUNSEL AND ALL PARTIES TO APPEAR TELEPHONICALLY FOR NON-TRIAL
AND NON-EVIDENTIARY MATTERS.  Thus, until further
notice, Department 26 strongly encourages telephonic appearances for motion
hearings that do not require the presentation of live testimony.

Saleh, an individual; Mahmoud Saleh a/k/a Mahmoud K. Saleh, an individual; and Does 1-10 (collectively, “Defendants”) asserting the same five causes of action in the original complaint.

Saleh ("Mahmoud Saleh") executed a "Confidential General Release and Settlement Agreement" ("the Settlement Agreement"), where these Defendants settled the claims alleged against them in this case for the principal sum of $52,000.00, payable over 43 months. The Settlement Agreement includes the following provision “The Stipulation will contain an Order for Conditional Stipulation for Entry of Judgment pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure § 664.6 pursuant to which the Court retaining jurisdiction to enforce the terms of the Stipulation.” (Besnyl Decl. ¶ 3; Ex. 1.)  On May 31, 2023, Plaintiff dismissed Defendants Vierri and Mahmoud Saleh subject to the Court’s retention of jurisdiction under Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6.

On January 29, 2024, Plaintiff filed a motion to enforce the Settlement Agreement with the Court retaining jurisdiction pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6 to enforce the settlement which was entered on May 25, 2023. On the same day, Plaintiff filed a proposed judgment.

On March 8, 2024, the Court advanced the hearing date of the motion to April 12, 2024. (Minute Order March 8, 2024.) As of April 10, 2024, Defendants have not filed an opposition.

Legal Standard

Code of Civil Procedure section 664.6 provides that “[i]f parties to pending litigation stipulate, in a writing signed by the parties outside the presence of the court or orally before the court, for settlement of the case, or part thereof, the court, upon motion, may enter judgment pursuant to the terms of the settlement.” In ruling on a motion to enter judgment the trial court acts as a trier of fact. It must determine whether the parties entered into a valid and binding settlement. To do so it may receive oral testimony in addition to declarations. (Kohn v. Jaymar-Ruby, Inc. (1994) 23 Cal.App.4th 1530, 1533.)  However, “the power of the trial court under section 664.6 ‘is extremely limited.’”  (Howeth v. Coffelt (2017) 18 Cal.App.5th 126, 134.)

Discussion

            Plaintiff moves this court for entry of judgment against defendants, Vierri and  Mahmoud K. Saleh, jointly and severally, in the total amount of $59,138.60, pursuant to the terms of the Settlement Agreement between the parties. Pursuant to the parties’ Settlement Agreement, Defendants agreed to pay $52,000.00 to Plaintiff.  (Besnyl Decl. ¶ 2; Ex.1.)  Under the settlement agreement, the parties further agreed to the following monthly payments: (a) On or before June 15, 2023, Defendants shall pay Plaintiff the sum of One Thousand US Dollars ($1,000.00); (b) On or before July 15, 2023, Defendants shall pay Plaintiff the sum of One Thousand US Dollars ($1,000.00); (c) On or before August 25, 2023, Defendants shall pay Plaintiff the sum of Ten Thousand US Dollars ($10,000.00). The Parties acknowledge that the additional 10 days for this payment is because Defendants will be receiving the $10,000 loan on or about August 20,2023; (d) Beginning September 15, 2023 and through December 15, 2026, Defendants shall make monthly payments to Plaintiff the sum of One Thousand US Dollars ($1,000.00) per month; and (e) No interest shall accrue on the Settlement Monies while the Defendants are not in default. If a default occurs and is not cured, interest shall begin to accrue on the defaulted balance at the rate of 10% per annum beginning on the date of the filing of the instant action, i.e., January 14, 2021. (Id.; Settlement Agreement 1.2.)

Section 1.4 of the Settlement Agreement provides that Defendant will have five (5) business days to cure a default after email notice to Defendants' counsel of a default. (Besnyl Decl. ¶ 7; Ex.1.) The Defendants failed to make the $10,000.00 settlement payment that was due on August 25, 2023, but plaintiff agreed to allow the Defendants additional time to cure their default. (Id.at  ¶ 8; Ex.2.) However, as part of that agreement, the Defendants were supposed to make a payment of $3,000.00 by the end of December, but instead only paid $1,000.00. (Id.) Accordingly, on January 9, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel emailed a "Default in Payment Schedule" letter to defense counsel putting Defendants on notice as to their overdue payment and giving them 5-7 business days to cure their default. (Id.)

To date, Plaintiff has not received the overdue payment, or any portion thereof. Plaintiff has received a total of $9,000.00 in settlement payments from the Defendants, leaving an unpaid balance of $43,000.00 still due and owing under the terms of the Settlement Agreement (plus 10 % per annum pre-judgment interest from January 14, 2021). (Id. at ¶ 9.)  A proposed Judgment has been lodged with the Court. (Id. at ¶ 10.)  

            Accordingly, pursuant to the Settlement Agreement, judgment is to be entered in the sum of unpaid payments of $43,000.00 plus a prejudgment interest rate of 10% for a total of $59,138.00.

 
 CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Based on the foregoing, Plaintiff’s motion to enforce the settlement agreement is GRANTED in the total amount of $59,138.00 consisting of a principal amount of $43,000.00 and prejudgment interest of $16,138.60.  The Court will sign Plaintiff’s proposed judgment submitted on January 29, 2024.

Plaintiff is to give notice to all parties and file proof of service of such.

Plaintiff’s MOTION TO COMPEL DEFENDANT RYAN TORIGOE’S RESPONSES TO PLAINTIFF’S REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION, SET ONE and request for monetary sanctions IN THE AMOUNT OF $1,035.00

 

 

Background

On October 2, 2023, Plaintiff 1333 Las Palmas Avenue, LLC (“Plaintiff”) filed the instant action against defendant Ryan Torigoe (“Defendant”). This is an unlawful detainer action for failure to pay rent.

            On October 31, 2023, Plaintiff filed the instant motion to compel defendant Ryan Torigoe to provide responses to Plaintiff’s requests for production, set one; and request for monetary sanctions. As of April 10, 2024, Defendant has not filed an opposition. 

Requests for Production

If “a party to whom a demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling is directed fails to serve a timely response to it” (Code Civ. Proc. §2031.300), the party propounding the demand or the interrogatories may “move for an order compelling response to the demand” (Code Civ. Proc. §2031.300(b)), unless the non-responding party satisfies his or her burden of proof than an exception applies. Additionally, “party to whom the demand for inspection, copying, testing, or sampling is directed waives any objection to the demand, including one based on privilege or on the protection for work product under Chapter 4” when failing to provide a timely response. (Code Civ. Proc. §2031.300(a).)

            Plaintiff served Requests for Production, Set One on Defendant by US Standard Mail on October 20, 2023, making responses due on or before October 30, 2023. (Godinez Decl. ¶3, Ex. 1 [Request for Production, Set One].) No responses have ever been received. (Godinez Decl. ¶ 5.)

As Defendant has not responded to the Request for Production, Set One Plaintiff’s motion is GRANTED.  

Sanctions

Plaintiff requests sanctions against Defendant Ryan Torigue in the sum of $1,035.00.

“The court shall impose a monetary sanction under Chapter 7 (commencing with Section 2023.010) against any party, person, or attorney who unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel a response to interrogatories, unless it finds that the one subject to the sanction acted with substantial justification or that other circumstances make the imposition of the sanction unjust.” See Code Civ. Proc., § 2030.290(c). 

Here, Defendant did not file an opposition therefore the Court is unaware of any substantial justification for the delay or reasons for it being unjust. However, the Court finds the request for $1,035.00 for 3.0 hours of one attorney’s time and the filing/appearance fee at a rate of $325 and filing fee of $60 to be excessive due to the simplicity of the motion. Therefore, the Court reduces the amount to $385.00 based on one hour of attorney time at a rate of $325 and $60.00 in filing fees.  

CONCLUSION AND ORDER

Plaintiff’s motion to compel Defendant Ryan Torigoe’s responses to Plaintiff’s request for production, set one granted pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 2033.280.

Plaintiff’s request for sanctions is granted in the modified amount of $385.00. 

The moving party is ordered to provide notice of this order and file proof of service of such.
