Case Number: 23AHCV01908    Hearing Date: October 14, 2024    Dept: X


MOTION TO BE RELIEVED AS COUNSEL

 

Plaintiff’s counsel moves to be relieved as counsel.

 

This case involves claims for legal malpractice against an attorney and his law firm.  Attorney Michael J. Fairchild moves to be relieved as counsel for Plaintiff, asserting attorney-client privilege regarding the specific facts and states that without waiving these privileges, “the firm can no longer effectively and ethically represent the client.”

 

Tentative Ruling

 

The Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel for Plaintiff filed by Michael J. Fairchild, Esq. is GRANTED.

 

The Court sets a Trial Setting Conference for January 22, 2025 at 8:30 a.m. in Department X of the Alhambra Courthouse.


Attorney Fairchild is to give notice.

 

Legal Analysis

 

“The question of granting or denying an application of an attorney to withdraw as counsel (Code Civ. Proc., § 284, subd. (2)) is one which lies within the sound discretion of the trial court ‘having in mind whether such withdrawal might work an injustice in the handling of the case.’ [Citation.]”  (People v. Prince (1968) 268 Cal.App.2d 398, 406 [internal quotations omitted].)  The Court should also consider whether the attorney’s “withdrawal can be accomplished without undue prejudice to the client’s interests.”  (Ramirez v. Sturdivant (1994) 21 Cal.App.4th 904, 915.)

 

California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362 requires that the following be submitted in support of an attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel pursuant Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2): (1) a notice of motion and motion directed to the client (made on Notice of Motion and Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (Judicial Council Form, MC-051)); (2) a declaration stating in general terms and without compromising the confidentiality of the attorney-client relationship why a motion under Code of Civil Procedure section 284, subdivision (2) is brought instead of filing a consent under Code of Civil Procedure section 284, subdivision (1) (made on Declaration in Support of Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (Judicial Council Form, MC-052)); (3) a proof of service evidencing service of the notice of motion and motion, declaration, and proposed order on the client and on all other parties who have appeared in the case; and (4) a proposed order relieving counsel (prepared on Order Granting Attorney’s Motion to Be Relieved as Counsel—Civil (Judicial Council Form, MC-053)).  (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, subds. (a), (c), (d), (e).)

 

Counsel for Plaintiff, Michael J. Fairchild, filed a motion to be relieved as counsel pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 284(2) and California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362.  A declaration on Judicial Council Form MC-052 accompanies the motion, as required by California Rules of Court, rule 3.1362, stating that “the firm can no longer effectively and ethically represent the client.”  

 

Attorney Fairchild has also provided a Proof of Service of the motion on Plaintiff and defense counsel on September 10, 2024.

 

No oppositions have been filed.

 

The Court finds good cause to grant the request to be relieved as counsel.  Attorney Fairchild has stated he cannot state the specific reasons for the withdrawal but has offered that he can no longer effectively and ethically represent the Plaintiff.  Plaintiff will not be prejudiced if counsel’s motion is granted.  There is currently no trial date and the Court is setting a Trial Setting Conference in January 2025, which should give Plaintiff sufficient time to engage new counsel if such is desired.


Case Number: 24NNCV02416    Hearing Date: October 14, 2024    Dept: X


DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO TRANSFER VENUE

 

Defendant moves to transfer the case from Los Angeles Superior Court to San Bernardino Superior Court.

 

The lawsuit arises out of a May 12, 2023 car accident between Plaintiff and Defendant, which occurred on the 210 Freeway.  Plaintiff sued for negligence and negligent entrustment.  Plaintiff is a resident of Arizona and Defendant resides in Redlands, California.

 

Tentative Ruling

 

The case is ordered transferred to Superior Court of California, County of San Bernardino. Plaintiff shall pay transfer fees in accordance with Code of Civil Procedure section 399, subdivision (a) within 30 days of notice of the Court’s ruling.

 

Legal Analysis

 

“Except as otherwise provided in Section 396a, if an action or proceeding is commenced in a court having jurisdiction of the subject matter thereof, other than the court designated as the proper court for the trial thereof, under this title, the action may, notwithstanding, be tried in the court where commenced, unless the defendant, at the time he or she answers, demurs, or moves to strike, or, at his or her option, without answering, demurring, or moving to strike and within the time otherwise allowed to respond to the complaint, files with the clerk, a notice of motion for an order transferring the action or proceeding to the proper court, together with proof of service, upon the adverse party, of a copy of those papers.  Upon the hearing of the motion the court shall, if it appears that the action or proceeding was not commenced in the proper court, order the action or proceeding transferred to the proper court.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 396b(a).)

 

“The court may, on motion, change the place of trial in the following cases:

 

(a) When the court designated in the complaint is not the proper court.

(b) When there is reason to believe that an impartial trial cannot be had therein.

(c) When the convenience of witnesses and the ends of justice would be promoted by the change…”

 

(Code Civ. Proc. § 397.)

 

Defendant brings this motion to transfer on the grounds that the accident and Plaintiff’s injuries occurred in San Bernardino County, so Los Angeles Superior Court is not the proper venue. Specifically, the accident occurred on the 210 Freeway in Redlands, California, where Defendant is also a resident.  There are no other defendants in this lawsuit.  

 

Plaintiff filed a notice of non-opposition, stating she does not oppose a transfer of this case to San Bernardino Superior Court.

 

Accordingly, the motion is granted.  The case is ordered transferred to Superior Court of California, County of San Bernardino.  Plaintiff shall pay transfer fees in accordance with Code of Civil Procedure section 399, subdivision (a).
