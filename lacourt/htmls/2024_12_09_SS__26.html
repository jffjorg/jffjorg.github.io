DEPARTMENT 26 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

If you desire to submit on the tentative ruling, you may do so by e-mailing Dept. 26 at the Spring Street Courthouse until the morning of the motion hearing.

The e-mail address is SSCdept26@lacourt.org

The heading on your e-mail should contain the case name, number, hearing date, and that you submit. The message should indicate your name, contact information, and the party you represent. Please note, the above e-mail address is to inform the court of your submission on the tentative ruling. All other inquiries will not receive a response.

If there are no appearances by either side and no submission on the Court's tentative ruling, the matter will be placed OFF CALENDAR. 

Due to overcrowding concerns of COVID-19, all parties shall make every effort to schedule a remote appearance via LACourtConnect (https://my.lacourt.org/laccwelcome) for their next hearing. The parties shall register with LACourtConnect at least 2 hours prior to their scheduled hearing time.

Case Number: 23STLC03954    Hearing Date: December 9, 2024    Dept: 26


TENTATIVE RULING:

Plaintiff Aspire General Insurance Company’s (1) Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Sanctions; and (2) Motion to Deem Requests for Admission, Set One, Admitted, and Request for Sanctions, are GRANTED. DEFENDANT MICHICO MARIE ALEXANDER IS TO SERVE VERIFIED RESPONSES WITHOUT OBJECTION TO THE INTERROGATORIES WITHIN 20 DAYS’ NOTICE OF THIS ORDER. ALSO, DEFENDANT MICHICO MARIE ALEXANDER IS TO PAY SANCTIONS OF $530.00 TO PLAINTIFF’S COUNSEL. THE ORDER TO PAY SANCTIONS IS STAYED, PENDING COMPLIANCE WITH THE ORDER TO SERVE VERIFIED RESPONSES WITHOUT OBJECTION TO THE INTERROGATORIES WITHIN 20 DAYS' NOTICE OF THIS ORDER.

ANALYSIS:

Plaintiff Aspire General Insurance Company (“Defendant”) propounded Form Interrogatories, Set One, and Requests for Admission, Set One, on Defendant Michico Marie Alexander (“Defendant”) on February 27, 2024. (Motions, Tapper Decl., Exh. A.) Despite a meet and confer effort, Defendant had not served responses as of the filing of the instant (1) Motion to Compel Responses to Form Interrogatories, Set One, and Request for Sanctions; (2) Motion to Deem Requests for Admission, Set One, Admitted, and Request for Sanctions, on September 30, 2024. (Id. at ¶3 and Exh. B.)

The Motions initially came for hearing on November 4, 2024. (Minute Order, 11/04/24.) Both parties appeared and Defendant represented to the Court that she had responses to the Motions. (Ibid.) The Court ordered the parties to meet and confer and continued the hearing date to December 9, 2024. (Ibid.) To date, no supplemental papers have been filed regarding the responses or the parties’ meet and confer efforts.

Discussion

Based on Defendant’s failure to serve initial responses to the discovery, the Motions to compel responses to interrogatories, and to deem admissions admitted, are granted. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.290, subd. (b); 2033.280.) There is no requirement for a prior meet and confer effort before a motion to compel initial responses or deem admissions admitted can be filed. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.290; 2033.280.) Further, the motion can be brought any time after the responding party fails to provide the responses. (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.290; 2033.280.) Therefore, Plaintiff is entitled to an order compelling Defendant to serve verified responses to the interrogatories without objections. The Court also deems the requests for admission admitted against Defendant.

Defendant’s failure to timely respond constitutes a misuse of the discovery process. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2023.010, subd. (d).) Sanctions are appropriate under Code of Civil Procedure sections 2023.010 and 2023.030 and have been properly noticed. However, the amount sought is excessive for these straightforward and unopposed motions. Sanctions are awarded against Defendant in the amount of $530.00 based on two hours of attorney time billed at $225.00 per hour, plus $40.00 in filing fees per motion. (Motions, Tapper Decl., ¶4.)

Conclusion

ANALYSIS:
Plaintiff Midway Rent a Car, Inc. dba Midway Car Rental (“Plaintiff”) filed this action against Defendant Carlos A. Salazar (“Defendant”) on October 19, 2023. Defendant answered in propria persona on December 1, 2023. On December 7, 2023, Plaintiff served Requests for Admissions on Defendant. (Motion, Moss Decl., Exh. 1.) To date, despite a meet and confer effort, Defendant has not served verified responses to the Requests for Admission. (Id. at ¶¶6-7 and Exh. 2.) Plaintiff filed the instant Motion to Deem Requests for Admission Admitted (“the Motion”) on July 11, 2024.The Motion initially came for hearing on September 10, 2024, at which time Defendant appeared through an attorney, Evan Selik (“Selik”). (Minute Order, 09/10/24.) The Court, on its own motion, continued the hearing to September 24, 2024. (Ibid.) On September 24, 2024, the matter was again continued to allow the parties to engage in settlement discussions. (Minute Order, 09/24/24, p. 2.) No substitution of attorney, nor additional papers regarding the Motion, have been filed to date. 

Discussion

There is no requirement for a prior meet and confer effort before a motion to deem requests for admission can be filed. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280.) The Court is required to award sanctions under the moving statute. (Code Civ. Proc., § 2033.280, subd. (c).) The sanctions sought are properly noticed and are granted against Defendant in the amount of $250.00 to include the $60.00 filing fee. (Motion, Moss Decl., ¶8.)

Case Number: 24STLC04931    Hearing Date: December 9, 2024    Dept: 26

Starnet Ins. Co. et al., v. Fred Loya Casualty, et al.,

TENTATIVE RULING:

Defendant Fred Loya Casualty’s Motion for Order Discharging Stakeholder and for Related Orders is GRANTED. DEFENDANT FRED LOYA CASUALTY IS TO DEPOSIT FUNDS OF $14,565.5 WITH THE COURT WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER. NO EARLIER THAN 20 DAYS AFTER DEPOSIT OF THE FUNDS, DEFENDANT FRED LOYA CASUALTY IS TO FILE A PROPOSED JUDGMENT OF DISMISSAL.

ANALYSIS:

On July 10, 2024, Plaintiffs Starnet Insurance Company, Texas Utility Solutions, and Newcomer & Associates, Inc. (“Plaintiffs”) filed the instant action for “recovery on unpaid workers’ compensation lien from personal injury settlement” against Defendants Fred Loya Casualty, Edward Love, Robert Pearman, and Law Offices Of Robert Pearman (“Defendants”). Defendant Love filed an answer on July 26, 2024; Defendant Fred Loya filed an answer on August 23, 2024.

Defendant Fred Loya filed the instant Motion for Order Discharging Stakeholder and for Related Orders on October 22, 2024. Plaintiffs filed an opposition on November 25, 2024 and Defendant Fred Loya replied on November 27, 2024.

Discussion

Defendant Fred Loya moves for an order (1) that Defendant Fred Loya deposit with the Court the property that is the subject matter of this action ($15,000.00), less the costs incurred in this action ($434.50.); (2) that Defendant Fred Loya be disharged from liability to any party in relation to the money deposited; and (3) entering a judgment of dismissal in favor or Defendant Fred Loya. The Motion is made on the grounds that Defendant Fred Loya is facing conflicting demands to the money, in which it has no interest. In support, the Motion cites Code of Civil Procedure section 386.5:

Where the only relief sought against one of the defendants is the payment of a stated amount of money alleged to be wrongfully withheld, such defendant may, upon affidavit that he is a mere stakeholder with no interest in the amount or any portion thereof and that conflicting demands have been made upon him for the amount by parties to the action, upon notice to such parties, apply to the court for an order discharging him from liability and dismissing him from the action on his depositing with the clerk of the court the amount in dispute and the court may, in its discretion, make such order.

(Code Civ. Proc., § 386.5.) The Motion also points to relevant case law regarding the interpleader procedure:

  “Interpleader is an equitable proceeding by which an obligor who is a mere stakeholder may compel conflicting claimants to money or property to interplead and litigate the claims among themselves instead of separately against the obligor.... After admitting liability and depositing the money or property with the court, the obligor is discharged from liability and freed from the necessity of participating in the litigation between the claimants.” (4 Witkin, Cal. Procedure (5th ed. 2008) Pleading, § 237, p. 317; see Code Civ. Proc., § 386.) Under section 386, subdivision (b), any person or entity “against whom double or multiple claims are made, or may be made, by two or more persons which are such that they may give rise to double or multiple liability, may bring an action against the claimants to compel them to interplead and litigate their several claims.” “The true test of suitability for interpleader is the stakeholder's disavowal of interest in the property sought to be interpleaded, coupled with the perceived ability of the court to resolve the entire controversy as to entitlement to that property without need for the stakeholder to be a party to the suit. ‘ “[I]f the relations of the parties are such that the court's decision would determine the responsibility of the [interpleader plaintiff], he is for the purposes and within the scope of the code section authorizing interpleader a mere stake-holder.” ’ [Citations.]” (Pacific Loan Management Corp. v. Superior Court (1987) 196 Cal.App.3d 1485, 1489–1490 [242 Cal.Rptr. 547].)

(Southern California Gas Co. v. Flannery (2014) 232 Cal.App.4th 477, 486-487.) Defendant Fred Loya contends it is subject to multiple liability regarding the $15,000.00 from Defendant Love and Plaintiffs Starnet and Texas. (Notice of Motion, p. 5:1-4; Motion, p. 2:18-21.) This is evidenced by the Complaint filed by Plaintiff in this action against Defendant Fred Loya, and two different settlement agreements purportedly between Defendant Love and Defendant Fred Loya, and Plaintiff Texas Utility and Defendant Fred Loya. (Compl., ¶10; Motion, Exhs. A-B.) Also, Defendant Fred Loya has shown that a party moving for discharge under the interpleader statute may include “a request for allowance of his costs and reasonable attorney fees incurred in such action.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 386.6, subd. (a).) Defendant Fred Loya incurred filing costs of $434.50 in this action. (Motion, Konczul Decl., ¶7.)

In opposition, Plaintiffs argue that they are entitled to costs and attorney’s fees pursuant to their settlement with Defendant Fred Loya, which was achieved through arbitration. The opposition cites the arbitration statute at Code of Civil Procedure section 1281, but points to no language therein regarding an award of attorney’s fees and costs. That statute only provides the following: “A written agreement to submit to arbitration an existing controversy or a controversy thereafter arising is valid, enforceable and irrevocable, save upon such grounds as exist for the revocation of any contract.” (Code Civ. Proc., § 1281.)

Therefore, the Court finds that Defendant Fred Loya may deposit the interpleader funds of $15,000.00, less its costs of $434.50, and be discharged from liability with respect to those funds.

Conclusion

Defendant Fred Loya Casualty’s Motion for Order Discharging Stakeholder and for Related Orders is GRANTED. DEFENDANT FRE LOYA CASUALTY IS TO DEPOSIT FUNDS OF $14,565.5 WITH THE COURT WITHIN 20 DAYS OF THIS ORDER. NO EARLIER THAN 20 DAYS AFTER DEPOSIT OF THE FUNDS, DEFENDANT FRE LOYA CASUALTY IS TO FILE A PROPOSED JUDGMENT OF DISMISSAL.

Moving party to give notice.
