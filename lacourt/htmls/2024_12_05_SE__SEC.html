DEPARTMENT SEC LAW AND MOTION RULINGS

DEPARTMENT SE-C LAW & MOTION PROCEDURES ARE AS FOLLOWS: APPEARANCES: The Court will hear oral arguments on all matters at the scheduled time of hearing. If all counsel intend to submit on the Tentative Order and do not want oral argument, please advise the clerk, in Department “C”, by calling (562-345-3702). If all sides submit on the Tentative Order and the clerk is so advised, the Tentative Order will become the final order of the court and the prevailing party shall give written Notice of Ruling per CRC 3.1312. If the Moving and Responding parties do not agree to submit on the Tentative Order, the motion will be called as calendared for hearing. There is no need to contact Department “C”, as the matter will remain on calendar for hearing. If the Moving party does not call Department “C” to submit on the Tentative Order and there is no appearance by any party, then the motion(s), at the Court’s discretion, may be taken off calendar without ruling on the motion(s). ORDERS: The minute order reflecting the Court’s Order will constitute the final Order. No additional orders should be submitted to the Court for signature unless required by law or by the Court. Prevailing party shall give written Notice of Ruling per CRC 3.1312. Minute orders, which constitute the final Order of the Court, will only be sent to the parties via U.S. mail  for the following: OSC re: sanctions, OSC re: contempt or matters taken under submission after oral arguments or briefing. Counsel or parties may request copies of all other minute orders/final orders either at the clerk’s office or in writing. If a request is in writing, a self-addressed stamped envelope and the appropriate fee for copies shall be submitted.

Case Number: 20STCV21816    Hearing Date: December 5, 2024    Dept: SEC

Skinner v. Downey Unified School District, et al. 20STCV21816

 

This personal injury action was filed on June 9, 2020. Trial is currently scheduled to begin on January 21, 2025. Plaintiff moves ex parte to Reopen Discovery After Trial Date Has Been Set due to a prior counsel’s failure to depose Defendants’ person most knowledgeable.

 

Plaintiff’s ex parte application to Reopen Discovery After Trial Date Has Been Set is DENIED without prejudice. “[A]ny party shall be entitled as a matter of right to complete discovery proceedings on or before the 30th day, and to have motions concerning discovery heard on or before the 15th day, before the date initially set for the trial of the action.” (Code Civ. Proc. § 2024.020 subd. (a).) Any request to reopen discovery must be filed and served as a separately noticed motion and must be accompanied by a meet and confer declaration in compliance with Code of Civil Procedure section 2016.040. The contents of a motion to reopen discovery must comply with Code of Civil Procedure section 2024.050.

 

In opposition, Defendants request monetary sanctions under Code of Civil Procedure section 2024.050 subd. (c) for opposing this application. The Court DENIES Defendants’ request for monetary sanctions because this ex parte application is not a noticed motion. Moving Party to give notice. 

 


Case Number: 21STCV00562    Hearing Date: December 5, 2024    Dept: SEC

JIMENEZ v. GOLDEN WEST FOOD GROUP, INC.

CASE NO.:  21STCV00562

HEARING:  12/05/24

 

#1

 

P. Ryan Banafshe and the Banafshe Law Firm, PC’s unopposed motion to withdraw as counsel of record for Plaintiff Pablo Albarran is GRANTED.  

 

 

Moving Party to give notice.

 

No Oppositions filed as of December 2, 2024.   

 

The Court finds that Counsel has met the procedural requirements, including proper notice to the client at the last known residence or business address of the client.  C.R.C. 3.1362.

 

Here, Counsel’s declaration indicates that there has been a breakdown of the attorney-client relationship. Where the issue (or conflict) is between the lawyer and client(s), without implicating other parties, the court can rely on counsel’s sworn declaration absent any reason to doubt the representations.

 

There is an undisputed breakdown in attorney-client communications. It does not appear that the Attorney Banafshe’s continued representation would serve the interests of justice. The motion to withdraw is GRANTED. 

 

The Court notes that there is a hearing for Defendants’ Motion for Summary Judgment set for January 23, 2025, and trial is scheduled to begin on March 19, 2025.

 

 

 

 

 


Case Number: 22STCV04425    Hearing Date: December 5, 2024    Dept: SEC

BRADY v. DOE 1

CASE NO.:  22STCV04425

HEARING:  12/05/24

 

#3

 

Plaintiff’s Unopposed Motion for an Order to Seal the Unredacted Declaration of Thomas B. Burtland and Attached Exhibits and the Unredacted Version of Earle Merchant’s Declaration in Connection with Plaintiff’s Joint Motion to Consolidate is GRANTED.

 

Moving Party to give notice.

 

No Opposition filed as of December 2, 2024.

 

This action (Constance Brady v. Doe 1 22STCV04425) was filed on February 4, 2022.  The following three cases have been deemed related to the subject action: Houk v. Doe 1 22NWCV00550; Doe v. Defendant Doe City 22NWCV01519; and Doe v. City of Whittier 23NWCV04037. There is a Motion to Consolidate scheduled to be heard on December 19, 2024.

 

On August 29, 2024, Plaintiff moved ex parte for an order requesting the in camera review and order to keep under seal the Declarations of Thomas Murtland and Earle Merchant filed in support of the Joint Motion to Consolidate. This Court denied the ex parte application without prejudice, and set the matter to be heard as a regularly noticed motion on December 5, 2024. The ex parte application was deemed the moving papers, and opposition and reply briefs were to be filed and served per Code in accordance with the new hearing date. As of December 2, 2024, no Opposition(s) have been filed.

 

Plaintiff requests for the following documents filed in connection with Plaintiffs’ Joint Motion to Consolidate, to be filed under seal:

 

(1) Exhibit B.1 referenced in the Declaration of Thomas Murtland, which contains the discovery verification of Plaintiff Jane D.B. Doe, signed under her true name;

(2) Exhibit C referenced in the Declaration of Thomas Murtland, which contains copies of Plaintiff Jane D.B. Doe’s confidential medical records; and

(3) The Declaration of Earle Merchant, which contains the true names and identities of Plaintiff Jane D.I. Doe, Jane D.B. Doe, and Doe Police Corporal.

 

“The court may order that a record be filed under seal only if it expressly finds facts that establish: [¶] (1) There exists an overriding interest that overcomes the right of public access to the record; [¶] (2) The overriding interest supports sealing the record; [¶] (3) A substantial probability exists that the overriding interest will be prejudiced if the record is not sealed; [¶] (4) The proposed sealing is narrowly tailored; and [¶] (5) No less restrictive means exist to achieve the overriding interest.” (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 2.550(d).)

 

Identities of Doe Plaintiffs

 

“The judicial use of ‘Doe plaintiffs’ to protect legitimate privacy rights has gained wide currency, particularly given the rapidity and ubiquity of disclosures over the World Wide Web.” (Starbucks Corp. v. Sup. Ct. (2008) 168 Cal.App.4th 1436, 1452, fn. 7.) “[F]ederal courts have permitted plaintiffs to use pseudonyms in three situations: (1) when identification creates a risk of retaliatory physical or mental harm; (2) when anonymity is necessary to preserve privacy in a matter of sensitive and highly personal nature; and (3) when the anonymous party is compelled to admit his or her intention to engage in illegal conduct, thereby risking criminal prosecution,” and “a party may preserve his or her anonymity in judicial proceedings in special circumstances when the party’s need for anonymity outweighs prejudice to the opposing party and the public’s interest in knowing the party’s identity.” (Doe v. Lincoln Unified School Dist. (2010) 188 Cal.App.4th 758, 767.)

 

Here, the Court finds that there are sufficient reasons to permit Plaintiffs to litigate this action as “Does”. Plaintiffs allege that they were victims of sexual assault. Plaintiffs’ rights to privacy include their identities as victims of sexual assault—a matter of sensitive and personal nature.

 

Moreover, the Court finds that Defendant Doe Police Corporal may not yet be named in this matter. The procedures set out in CCP §340.1 must be followed before publicly naming/identifying Defendant Doe Police Corporal.

 

The Motion to Seal Exhibit B.1 referenced in the Declaration of Thomas Murtland, which contains the discovery verification of Plaintiff Jane D.B. Doe, signed under her true name; and The Declaration of Earle Merchant, which contains the true names and identities of Plaintiff Jane D.I. Doe, Jane D.B. Doe, and Doe Police Corporal is GRANTED.

 

Plaintiff Jane D.B. Doe’s Confidential Medical Records

 

“There is no question that medical records are highly sensitive materials that fall within the scope of the right to privacy.” (Manela v. Sup. Ct. (20009) 177 Cal.App.4th 1139, 1150.)

 

The Motion to Seal Exhibit C referenced in the Declaration of Thomas Murtland, which contains copies of Plaintiff Jane D.B. Doe’s confidential medical records is GRANTED. Plaintiff’s medical information is entitled to privacy.

 

The motion to seal is GRANTED. The Court finds that the parties’ interest in the confidentiality overcomes the right of public access to court records. This overriding interest supports sealing the records identified. This order is narrowly tailored, and no less restrictive means exist to protect the parties’ confidential information.  

 


Case Number: 23NWCV00851    Hearing Date: December 5, 2024    Dept: SEC

SANDOVAL v. ADVANCED BUILDING SYSTEMS, INC.

CASE NO.:  23NWCV00851

HEARING:  12/05/24

 

#4

 

Plaintiff’s Motion to Compel Responses by Defendant ADVANCED BUILDING SYSTEMS, INC. to Plaintiff’s Request for Production of Documents (set two) is GRANTED.

 

Moving Party to give Notice.

 

 

If a party to whom interrogatories and document demands are directed fails to respond at all, the propounding party’s remedy is to seek a court order compelling answers thereto. (CCP §§ 2030.290, 2031.300.) All that needs to be shown is that the discovery was properly served on the opposing party, that the time to respond has expired, and that no response of any kind has been served. The moving party is not required to show a reasonable and good faith attempt to resolve the matter informally before filing this motion. A motion to compel initial discovery responses need not show good cause, meeting and conferring, or timely filing, and need not be accompanied by a separate statement. (See Sinaiko Healthcare Consulting, Inc. v. Pac. Healthcare Consultants (2007) 148 Cal.App.4th 390, 404.) The failure to timely respond also waives all objections.

 

Here, Plaintiff has shown that Request for Production of Documents (set two) was properly served on June 28, 2024. The deadlines to respond have expired, and no responses of any kind have been provided.  This Motion was filed on September 12, 2024—almost three months after service of the discovery.

 

In Opposition, Defendant/Defendant’s Counsel does not argue that timely or untimely responses have been served. Rather, Defense Counsel explains they are seeking to be relieved as counsel because “Defendants are in breach of their retainer agreement with counsel, have not paid all sums owed and due to counsel, and are no longer responsive to counsel’s emails….” (Opp. 1:3-6.) The Opposition argues that the Motion should be denied without prejudice “because Plaintiff prematurely brought this motion in an effort… to ‘set case up for sanctions and striking of [Defendant’s’] Answer. [Citation.]” (Opp. 1:24-25.)

 

The substantively unopposed Motion is GRANTED. It is undisputed that the discovery at issue was served, and to date, no responses have been provided. Defendant ADVANCED BUILDING SYSTEMS, INC. is ORDERED to provide verified responses and documents, without objection by no later than 30 days from date of the Court’s issuance of this Order. This date may be extended by stipulation of the parties. If any objections are asserted, it will be tantamount to no response at all and will be deemed a violation of this Court’s order.

 

Reasonable sanctions are GRANTED in the total amount of $1,160.00. ($550/hr. x 2 hrs.) + ($60 filing fee). Defendant ADVANCED BUILDING SYSTEMS, INC. is ORDERED to pay Plaintiff and their counsel of record sanctions in the total amount of $1,160.00, payable within 30 days from the date of the Court’s issuance of this Order.
