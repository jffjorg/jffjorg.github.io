DEPARTMENT 50 LAW AND MOTION RULINGS


Case Number: 23STCV22953    Hearing Date: May 23, 2024    Dept: 50

 

 

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles

Department 50

 

DENISE WHEELER,

 

                        Plaintiff,

            vs.

 

CENTER STREET LENDING CORPORATION, et al.,

 

                        Defendants.

Case No.:

  23STCV22953

Hearing Date:

May 23, 2024

Hearing Time:    2:00 p.m.

 

[TENTATIVE] ORDER RE:

 

DEFENDANTS CENTER STREET LENDING CORPORATION AND CENTER STREET LENDING FUND IV SPE, LLC’S DEMURRER TO PLAINTIFF’S FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT;

 

DEFENDANTS CENTER STREET LENDING CORPORATION AND CENTER STREET LENDING FUND IV SPE, LLC’S MOTION TO STRIKE PORTIONS OF PLAINTIFF’S FIRST AMENDED COMPLAINT

 

 

Background

Plaintiff Denise Wheeler (“Plaintiff”) filed this action on September 21, 2023 against Defendant Center Street Lending Corporation.

Plaintiff filed the operative First Amended Complaint (“FAC”) on January 8, 2024 against Defendants Center Street Lending Corporation and Center Street Lending Fund IV SPE, LLC (jointly, “Defendants”). The FAC alleges causes of action for (1) violation of Civil Code section 2923.55 and (2) violation of Business and Professions Code section 17200, et seq.

Defendants now demur to both of the causes of action of the FAC. Defendants also move to strike portions of the FAC. Plaintiff filed oppositions to the demurrer and motion to strike.  

Requests for Judicial Notice

The Court grants Defendants’ request for judicial notice. The Court also grants Plaintiff’s request for judicial notice.

Demurrer

A.    Legal Standard  

A demurrer can be used only to challenge defects that appear on the face of the pleading under attack or from matters outside the pleading that are judicially noticeable. ((Blank v. Kirwan (1985) 39 Cal.3d 311, 318.) “To survive a demurrer, the complaint need only allege facts sufficient to state a cause of action; each evidentiary fact that might eventually form part of the plaintiff’s proof need not be alleged.” ((C.A. v. William S. Hart Union High School Dist. (2012) 53 Cal.4th 861, 872.) For the purpose of testing the sufficiency of the cause of action, the demurrer admits the truth of all material facts properly pleaded. ((Aubry v. Tri-City Hospital Dist. (1992) 2 Cal.4th 962, 966-967.) A demurrer “does not admit contentions, deductions or conclusions of fact or law.” ((Daar v. Yellow Cab Co. (1967) 67 Cal.2d 695, 713.)

B.    Allegations of the FAC 

In the FAC, Plaintiff alleges that at all relevant times, she owned the property located at 637 E 29th Street, Los Angeles California 90011 (the “Property”). (FAC, ¶ 8.) Plaintiff alleges that “[t]he Property has been in Plaintiff’s family since 1993, but she was not on title until August 2015. The [P]roperty has served as her personal and principal residence for all times referenced herein.” (FAC, ¶ 9.) “In mid-2015, a fire virtually destroyed most of the Property and left it in severe disrepair. As a result, Plaintiff received insurance proceeds to fix the Property. This is also when Plaintiff was put on title to the Property.” (FAC, ¶ 10.)

            “On August 20, 2015, Plaintiff received a ‘refinance’ from Defendant CENTER STREET in the amount of $350,000.00.” (FAC, ¶ 11.) “In the loan origination process, Plaintiff was told by CENTER STREET to use a different address for her personal residence.” (FAC, ¶ 12, emphasis omitted.) Plaintiff alleges that “CENTER STREET knew Plaintiff’s only residence was the property but was told to use a different address to fund the loan.” (FAC, ¶ 12.) “In February 2016, the loan came due, and Plaintiff was unable to pay the balloon amount.” (FAC, ¶ 14.)

            “In September 2017, CENTER STREET filed a Notice of Default. On December 26, 2017, CENTER STREET filed a Notice of Trustee’s Sale.” (FAC, ¶ 15.) “On January 18, 2018, Plaintiff filed for Chapter 11 Bankruptcy. Plaintiff made payments throughout the bankruptcy until early 2023 when CENTER STREET filed a Motion for Relief from stay.” (FAC, ¶ 16.) “The Bankruptcy court granted the Motion for Relief from stay and CENTER STREET recorded a new Notice of Default on May 31, 2023.” (FAC, ¶ 17.)

            Plaintiff alleges that “[o]n September 5, 2023, Defendant recorded a Notice of Trustee’s Sale and set the property for auction on October 3, 2023. Despite the fact that Defendant failed to contact Plaintiff to ‘assess her financial situation and explore options to avoid foreclosure’ as required by Civil Code 2923.55, Defendant intends to proceed the sale [sic]…” (FAC, ¶ 19.)

C.    First and Second Causes of Action

In the FAC, Plaintiff alleges that “CENTER STREET LENDING FUND IV SPE, LLC…is a business entity primarily in residential mortgage banking and/or related business and was the beneficiary or servicer of Plaintiff’s loan during the operative time period herein.” (FAC, ¶ 5.) Plaintiff also alleges that “CENTER STREET LENDING CORPORATION…is a California stock corporation engaged primarily in residential mortgage banking and/or related business and was the beneficiary or servicer of Plaintiff’s loan during the operative time period herein.” (FAC, ¶ 6.)

In the demurrer, Defendants first assert that “Plaintiff’s allegations fail as alleged against Defendants because Defendants are not the current beneficiary of the loan.” (Demurrer at p. 5:2-3.)[1] Defendants cite to Exhibit “B” to their request for judicial notice, which is an “Assignment of Beneficial Interest Under Deed of Trust” recorded on recorded on August 31, 2015. (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 2, Ex. B.) This Assignment provides, inter alia, that “BY THIS ASSIGNMENT OF BENEFICIAL INTEREST UNDER DEED OF TRUST…dated to be effective as of August 11, 2015, CENTER STREET LENDING CORPORATION, a California Corporation (‘Assignor’)…does by these presents grant, bargain, sell, assign, transfer and set over unto CENTER STREET LENDING FUND IV SPE, LLC…all of Assignor’s right, title and interest as Beneficiary under that certain Deed of Trust dated August 10, 2015 executed by Denise L. Wheeler…” (Ibid.)

Defendants also cite to Exhibit “G” to their request for judicial notice, which is another “Assignment of Beneficial Interest Under Deed of Trust” recorded on recorded on September 4, 2019. (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 7, Ex. G.) This Assignment provides, inter alia, that “BY THIS ASSIGNMENT OF BENEFICIAL INTEREST UNDER DEED OF TRUST…dated to be effective as of August 1, Center Street Lending Fund IV SPE, LLC, a Delaware Limited Liability Company (‘Assignor’)…does by these presents grant, bargain, sell, assign, transfer and set over unto Center Street Lending VIII SPE, LLC…all of Assignor’s right, title and interest as Beneficiary under that certain Deed of Trust dated August 10, 2015 executed by Denise L. Wheeler…” (Ibid.)

In the opposition, Plaintiff counters that “Defendants do not dispute that either is the servicer of the loan. Since liability under Civil Code § 292.55 [sic] also applies to mortgage servicers, Plaintiff’s allegations do not fail despite the beneficial interest in the Deed of Trust having been transferred in September 2019 to Center Street VIII.” (Opp’n at p. 4:6-9.) Indeed, Civil Code section 2923.55, subdivision (a) provides that “[a] mortgage servicer, mortgagee, trustee, beneficiary, or authorized agent shall not record a notice of default pursuant to Section 2924 until all of the following: (1) The mortgage servicer has satisfied the requirements of paragraph (1) of subdivision (b). (2) Either 30 days after initial contact is made as required by paragraph (2) of subdivision (b) or 30 days after satisfying the due diligence requirements as described in subdivision (f). (3) The mortgage servicer complies with subdivision (c) of Section 2923.6, if the borrower has provided a complete application as defined in subdivision (h) of Section 2923.6.” (Emphasis added.)

Based on the foregoing, the Court does not find that Defendants have demonstrated that Plaintiff’s allegations fail “because Defendants are not the current beneficiary of the loan.” (Demurrer at p. 5:3-4.)

D.    First Cause of Action for Violation of Civil Code Section 2923.55

In the first cause of action, Plaintiff alleges, inter alia, that “Defendant recorded the NOD without sending the NOD to Plaintiff or contacting Plaintiff ‘in person or by telephone’ to assess her financial situations and explore options to avoid foreclosure. Additionally, the Notice of Trustee’s Sale…also fails to include the above required declaration and is not appropriately executed, thus Defendants violated Cal. Civ. Code§ 2923.55.” (FAC, ¶ 23.) Plaintiff alleges that “[s]ince Defendant also failed to notify or correspond with Plaintiff in writing concerning her options regarding the NOD and alleged default, Defendants recorded the NOD in violation of Cal. Civ. Code § 2923.55.” (FAC, ¶ 24.)

In the demurrer, Defendants argue that “Plaintiff does not allege facts showing that Defendants are subject to Section 2923.55.” (Demurrer at p. 5:24-25.) Defendants note that Civil Code section 2923.55, subdivision (g) provides that “[t]his section shall not apply to entities described in subdivision (b) of Section 2924.18.”

Civil Code section 2924.18, subdivision (b) provides that “[t]his section shall apply only to a depository institution chartered under state or federal law, a person licensed pursuant to Division 9 (commencing with Section 22000) or Division 20 (commencing with Section 50000) of the Financial Code, or a person licensed pursuant to Part 1 (commencing with Section 10000) of Division 4 of the Business and Professions Code, that, during its immediately preceding annual reporting period, as established with its primary regulator, foreclosed on 175 or fewer residential real properties, containing no more than four dwelling units, that are located in California.” In Billesbach v. Specialized Loan Servicing LLC (2021) 63 Cal.App.5th 830, 844, footnote 7, the Court of Appeal noted that “Section 2923.55 generally applies only to larger mortgage servicers. (See §§ 2923.55, subd. (g) [‘This section shall not apply to entities described in subdivision (b) of Section 2924.18’], 2924.18, subd. (b) [describing certain entities who foreclosed on 175 or fewer residential properties in preceding year].).”

 Defendants assert that “Plaintiff does not allege any facts that indicate that Defendants are ‘larger mortgage servicers’ subject to section 2923.55 or even make a reference to publicly available information via the California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation’s annually published report of entities that have foreclosed on more than 175 homes in California during the prior years. It follows that Plaintiff cannot pursue a claim against the Defendants for an alleged violation of section 2923.55 and this claim fails.” (Demurrer at p. 6:2-6.)

In the opposition, Plaintiff asserts that “Defendant suggests that each of Plaintiff’s claims fail because she has not alleged that Civil Code § 2924.18(b) does not apply. This is not Plaintiff’s burden.” (Opp’n at p. 3, fn. 1.) Plaintiff cites to Casterson v. Superior Court (2002) 101 Cal.App.4th 177, 183, where the Court of Appeal noted that “[a] general demurrer will lie where the complaint has included allegations that clearly disclose some defense or bar to recovery. Thus, a demurrer based on an affirmative defense will be sustained only where the face of the complaint discloses that the action is necessarily barred by the defense.” (Internal quotations, citation, and emphasis omitted.) Here, Defendants do not point to allegations of the FAC demonstrating that Plaintiff’s first cause of action is barred by Civil Code section 2923.55, subdivision (g).

Next, Defendants assert that “the subject business purpose loan is not covered by HBOR.” (Demurrer at p. 6:8.) Defendants cite to Civil Code section 2923.55, subdivision (h), which provides that “[t]his section shall apply only to mortgages or deeds of trust described in Section 2924.15.”  Pursuant to Civil Code section 2924.15, subdivision (a)(1), “[u]nless otherwise provided, paragraph (5) of subdivision (a) of Section 2924 and Sections 2923.5, 2923.55, 2923.6, 2923.7, 2924.9, 2924.10, 2924.11, and 2924.18 shall apply only to a first lien mortgage or deed of trust that meets either of the following conditions: (1) (A) The first lien mortgage or deed of trust is secured by owner-occupied residential real property containing no more than four dwelling units. (B) For purposes of this paragraph, ‘owner-occupied’ means that the property is the principal residence of the borrower and is security for a loan made for personal, family, or household purposes.” (Emphasis added.)

Defendants assert that “on its face, the DOT secures a business purpose loan.” (Demurrer at p. 6:25.) Defendants cite to Exhibit “A” to Defendants’ request for judicial notice, which is a “Deed of Trust, Security Agreement and Fixture Filing (With Assignment of Rents and Leases)” recorded on August 20, 2015. (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 1, Ex. A.) This Deed of Trust provides, inter alia, that “This Deed of Trust, Security Agreement and Fixture Filing (with Assignment of Rents and Leases) (‘Deed of Trust’ or ‘Deed’) is made August 10, 2015, between Denise L. Wheeler, an unmarried woman, herein called Trustor, whose address is 5750 Centinela Avenue #424, Los Angeles, CA 90045, and Center Street Lending Corporation, a California Corporation, herein called Beneficiary…” (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 1, Ex. A, p. 1 [emphasis omitted].) Defendants note that the Deed of Trust states that “[e]ach of the following shall be considered an event of default of this Deed: a. Any Borrower or Guarantor occupying 637 East 29th Street, Los Angeles, CA 90011 as their residence…” (Defendants’ RJN, Ex. A, p. 3.)

Defendants also assert that “in Plaintiff’s Bankruptcy filed in 2018, Plaintiff certifies under penalty of perjury that she rents out the Subject Property for profit.” (Demurrer at p. 6:27-28.) Defendants cite to, inter alia, Exhibit I to their Request for Judicial Notice, which is a “Debtor’s Post Confirmation Status Conference Report No. 1; Declaration of Denise Wheeler in Support Thereof” filed in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Central District of California in the matter In re: Denise Latrice Wheeler, Case No. 2:18-bk-10597, on April 30, 2020. (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 9, Ex. I.) Exhibit I provides, inter alia, that “Debtor also generates rental income from rental property in the amount of $4,500 per month. The major assets of the estate are: Debtor’s rental property located at 637 E. 29th Street, Los Angeles, CA 90011 (the ‘Rental Property/Real Property’), two vehicles and other personal assets.” (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 9, Ex. I, p. 2.)

In the opposition, Plaintiff counters that “the loan at issue was clearly a loan for personal, family, or household purposes.” (Opp’n at p. 5:15-16.) Plaintiff argues that “the purpose of the debt was straightforward. Plaintiff’s family has owned the home 1993, and Plaintiff has lived in the home for decades. In mid-2015, a fire virtually destroyed most of the Property and left it in severe disrepair. Thereafter, title to the Property was put in Plaintiff’s name, and Plaintiff received insurance proceeds to fix the Property that she has lived in so that she could continuing [sic] living there.” (Opp’n at p. 5:16-21, emphasis omitted.) As discussed above, the FAC alleges that “[i]n mid-2015, a fire virtually destroyed most of the Property and left it in severe disrepair. As a result, Plaintiff received insurance proceeds to fix the Property.” (FAC, ¶ 10.) Plaintiff points to paragraph 9 of the FAC, which alleges that “[t]he Property has been in Plaintiff’s family since 1993, but she was not on title until August 2015. The property has served as her personal and principal residence for all times referenced herein.” (FAC, ¶ 9.) However, the FAC does not appear to expressly allege that the subject property “is security for a loan made for personal, family, or household purposes…” (Civ. Code § 2924.15, subd. (a)(1)(B).)

Defendants also assert that “the subject property was not owner-occupied at the time of the alleged violation.” (Demurrer at p. 7:10-11.) As set forth above, Civil Code section 2924.15, subdivision (a)(1) provides that “[u]nless otherwise provided, paragraph (5) of subdivision (a) of Section 2924 and Sections 2923.5, 2923.55, 2923.6, 2923.7, 2924.9, 2924.10, 2924.11, and 2924.18 shall apply only to a first lien mortgage or deed of trust that meets either of the following conditions: (1)(A) The first lien mortgage or deed of trust is secured by owner-occupied residential real property containing no more than four dwelling units. (B) For purposes of this paragraph, ‘owner-occupied’ means that the property is the principal residence of the borrower and is security for a loan made for personal, family, or household purposes.” (Emphasis added.)

Defendants assert that “Plaintiff cannot substantiate that the Property is her ‘principal residence’ as required by Civil Code section 2924.15 (a)(1) and as she now claims in her FAC.” (Demurrer at p. 8:5-6.) Defendants cite to Exhibit E to their request for judicial notice, which is a “Voluntary Petition for Individuals Filing for Bankruptcy” filed in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Central District of California in the matter In re: Denise Latrice Wheeler, Case No. 2:18-bk-10597 on January 18, 2018. (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 5, Ex. E.) Defendants note that page 2 of the Petition states, inter alia, “1191 1/2 West 38th Street Los Angeles, CA 90037” after “Where you live.” (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 5, Ex. E, p. 2.) Defendants also cite to Exhibit U to their request for judicial notice, which is a “Declaration of Lionel E. Giron in Support of Application for Compensation for Law Offices of Lionel E. Giron Period January 2, 2019 through April 18, 2023” filed in the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Central District of California, in the matter In re: Denise Latrice Wheeler, Case No. 2:18-bk-10597 on May 23, 2023. (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 21, Ex. U.) Defendants note that Exhibit U states, inter alia, “[o]n April 19, 2023 my office served a copy of the Application for Compensation to Mrs. Wheeler at 1191 ½ West 38th Street, Los Angeles, CA 90037.” (Defendants’ RJN, ¶ 21, Ex. U, ¶ 5.)

Defendants cite to Cantu v. Resolution Trust Corp. (1992) 4 Cal.App.4th 857, 877-878, where the Court of Appeal noted that “[b]oth trial and appellate courts may properly take judicial notice of a party’s earlier pleadings and positions as well as established facts from both the same case and other cases. The complaint should be read as containing the judicially noticeable facts, even when the pleading contains an express allegation to the contrary. A plaintiff may not avoid a demurrer by pleading facts or positions in an amended complaint that contradict the facts pleaded in the original complaint or by suppressing facts which prove the pleaded facts false. Likewise, the plaintiff may not plead facts that contradict the facts or positions that the plaintiff pleaded in earlier actions or suppress facts that prove the pleaded facts false. The principle is that of truthful pleading. When the plaintiff pleads inconsistently in separate actions, the plaintiff’s complaint is nothing more than a sham that seeks to avoid the effect of a demurrer. Under such circumstances, the court will disregard the falsely pleaded facts and affirm the demurrer.” (Internal quotations, citations, and emphasis omitted.)

In the opposition, Plaintiff asserts that “the property was owner occupied when the notice of default was recorded.” (Opp’n at p. 6:1-2.) But in support of this assertion, Plaintiff appears to solely cite to exhibits attached to her request for judicial notice. Plaintiff does not appear to cite any allegations of the FAC stating that the subject property was “owner occupied” when the notice of default was allegedly recorded. (See Opp’n at pp. 6:1-7:7.) As set forth above, “[u]nless otherwise provided, paragraph (5) of subdivision (a) of Section 2924 and Sections 2923.5, 2923.55, 2923.6, 2923.7, 2924.9, 2924.10, 2924.11, and 2924.18 shall apply only to a first lien mortgage or deed of trust that meets either of the following conditions: (1) (A) The first lien mortgage or deed of trust is secured by owner-occupied residential real property containing no more than four dwelling units.” (Civ. Code § 2924.15, subd. (a)(1)(A).)

Based on the foregoing, the Court sustains Defendants’ demurrer to the first cause of action of the FAC, with leave to amend.[2]

 

E.     Second Cause of Action for Violation of Business and Professions Code Section 17200 et seq.  

“The UCL does not proscribe specific activities, but broadly prohibits any unlawful, unfair or fraudulent business act or practice and unfair, deceptive, untrue or misleading advertising. The UCL governs anti-competitive business practices as well as injuries to consumers, and has as a major purpose the preservation of fair business competition. By proscribing any unlawful business practice, section 17200 borrows violations of other laws and treats them as unlawful practices that the unfair competition law makes independently actionable. Because…section 17200 is written in the disjunctive, it establishes three varieties of unfair competition—acts or practices which are unlawful, or unfair, or fraudulent.” ((Puentes v. Wells Fargo Home Mortgage, Inc. (2008) 160 Cal.App.4th 638, 643-644 [internal quotations and citations omitted].)

Defendants assert that “Plaintiff’s UCL cause of action relies on the allegations in her first cause of action for violation of Civil Code section 2923.55. As shown above, this cause of action fails.” (Demurrer at p. 10:5-7.) Indeed, in the second cause of action, Plaintiff alleges that “Defendants’ conduct, as alleged above, constitutes unlawful, unfair, and/or fraudulent business practices, as defined in the California Business and Professions Code § 17200 et seq. California Business and Professions Code § 17200 et seq. incorporates and provides a basis for enforcement of violations of other statutes and laws and those violations as a business practice…Specifically, Defendant’s violation of California Civil Code § 2923.55 constitute unfair business practices in violation of California Business and Professions Code § 17200 et seq.” (FAC, ¶¶ 30-31.)

In the opposition, Plaintiff asserts that “[s]ince Plaintiff has sufficiently alleged their underlying claims, his [sic] claim for violation of Civil Code § 17200 [sic] is also sufficiently alleged.” (Opp’n at p. 10:9-10.) As set forth above, the Court sustains Defendants’ demurrer to the first cause of action of the FAC.

Based on the foregoing, the Court sustains Defendants’ demurrer to the second cause of action of the FAC, with leave to amend.[3]

Motion to Strike

A court may strike any “irrelevant, false, or improper matter inserted in any pleading” or any part of a pleading “not drawn or filed in conformity with the laws of this state, a court rule, or an order of the court.” ((Code Civ. Proc., § 436.) “The grounds for a motion to strike shall appear on the face of the challenged pleading or from any matter of which the court is required to take judicial notice.” ((Id., § 437.)

Defendants move to strike paragraph 5 of Plaintiff’s “Prayer for Damages,” which alleges, “[f]or exemplary damages in an amount sufficient to punish Defendant’s wrongful conduct and deter future misconduct.” Defendants assert that “nothing in the FAC warrants an award of exemplary damages.” (Mot. at p. 4:2-3.)

As an initial matter, as set forth above, the Court sustains Defendants’ demurrer to the first and second causes of action of the FAC. In addition, Plaintiff’s opposition to the motion to strike provides, inter alia, that “Plaintiff submits this response to get into the record the true facts of the case at hand and dispute Defendant’s incorrect recitation of facts. However, Plaintiff does not oppose the request to strike exemplary damages, found at Paragraph 5 of the Prayer for Damages on page 7, line 5.” (Opp’n at p. 2:7-9.)

Based on the foregoing, the Court grants Defendants’ motion to strike, without leave to amend.

Conclusion

Based on the foregoing, the Court sustains Defendants’ demurrer to the first and second causes of action of the FAC, with leave to amend.

The Court grants Defendants’ motion to strike, without leave to amend.

The Court orders Plaintiff to file and serve an amended complaint, if any, within 20 days of the date of this order. If no amended complaint is filed within 20 days, the Court orders Defendants to file and serve a proposed judgment of dismissal within 30 days of the date of this Order.¿¿¿ 

Defendants are ordered to give notice of this Order.
