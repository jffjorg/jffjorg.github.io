Case Number: 21VECV00418    Hearing Date: October 15, 2024    Dept: T

CYPERS V. GRANATELLI 21VECV00418            

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Defendants Fox Rothschild LLP and Conrad Wilton’s Motion to Consolidate and Stay the Action is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE.

Introduction

Defendants Fox Rothschild LLP and Conrad Wilton (collectively, Fox Defendants) moved to consolidate Reuven Justin Cypers aka Rory Cypers; Residual Income Opportunities, Inc. v. Joseph R. Granatelli; David Gurnick; Stephen Holzer; Lewitt, Hackman, Shapiro, Marshall & Harlan, a Law Corporation; Lincoln D. Bandlow; Rom Bar-Nissim; Conrad Wilton; Fox Rothschild LLP; Law Offices of Lincoln Bandlow, P.C. (Super. Ct. Los Angeles County, pending, No.  21VECV00418)  (Instant Action) with the related action, Reuven Justin Cypers aka Rory Cypers; Residual Income Opportunities, Inc. v. David Gurnick; Stephen Holzer; Lewitt, Hackman, Shapiro, Marshall & Harlan, a Law Corporation; Lincoln D. Bandlow; Rom Bar-Nissim; Law Offices of Lincoln Bandlow, P.C. (Super. Ct. Los Angeles County, pending, No.  23VECV00811) (Related Action).   The Fox Defendants further requested that, upon consolidation, the entire consolidated action be stayed based upon the two appeals as to the denials of the Anti-SLAPP motions filed by Co-Defendants Stephen Holzer and Lewitt, Hackman, Shapiro, Marshall & Harlan, a Law Corporation (collectively, Lewitt Defendants) and Co-Defendants The Law Offices of Lincoln Bandlow PC and Lincoln Bandlow (collectively, Bandlow Defendants). 

In the Instant Action, the only viable party defendants are the Fox Defendants.  All other named defendants in the Instant Action were voluntarily dismissed by Plaintiff on June 15, 2021, June 21, 2021 and July 28, 2021.  Further, the Fox Defendants are not named defendants in the Related Action.

Discussion

When actions involving a common question of law or fact and are pending before the same court, the Court may order complete or partial consolidation.  (Code Civ. Proc., sec. 1048.)  There is no dispute between the parties that the two actions involve common questions of law or fact.  However, the Court notes that the two actions are not pending before the same court.  The two cases are technically assigned to this Court.  However, as admitted by both parties, the Related Action is currently on appeal from the denial of two Anti-SLAPP motions filed by the Lewitt Defendants and the Bandlow Defendants.  Due to the two Appeals in the Related Action, this Court does not have jurisdiction over a portion of the Related Action and more specifically does not have jurisdiction over proceedings that could affect the merits of the appealed denial of the Anti-SLAPP.  Because the Fox Defendants failed to show that the entirety of the Related Action is pending before this Court, the Fox Defendants failed to meet their initial burden on the motion to consolidate.

The Court exercises its discretion to maintain the independence of the two actions, at least until the two appeals in the Related Action are resolved and the Court of Appeal issues its remittitur. 

The motion to consolidate the two actions and to stay the consolidated action is DENIED WITHOUT PREJUDICE. 

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.


Case Number: 23VECV00647    Hearing Date: October 15, 2024    Dept: T


Tarzana v Work 23VECV00647

Motion to stay proceedings:

Ronald Reinsdorf moves to stay this case pending an appeal of his motion to intervene.  This court denied his motion to intervene.  He has another action which is currently pending in another lawsuit.  The lawsuit in the other action and the proposed complaint in intervention in this action are the same.  The Court (Judge Kenny) found that this case (23VECV00647) and the other case (24VECV03344), are not related within the meaning of California Rules of Court, rule 3.300(a). 

There is no automatic stay required in the appeal of a motion to intervene.  In light of Judge Kenny's ruling that is case is unrelated to the (in essence) proposed complaint in intervention, and the court's analysis denying the motion to intervene, the court denies the motion to stay this action.

Case Number: 23VECV05528    Hearing Date: October 15, 2024    Dept: T

MCCALL V GM 23VECV05528

[TENTATIVE] ORDER:  Defendant General Motors LLC’s Demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITH 10 DAYS LEAVE TO AMEND.

Defendant General Motors LLC’s Motion to Strike Portions of the Complaint is GRANTED WITH 10 DAYS LEAVE TO AMEND.

Plaintiff Zachary McCall is GRANTED LEAVE to re-file his First Amended Complaint that was previously rejected by the Court within 20 days.

Introduction

Defendant General Motors LLC (Defendant) demurred to Plaintiff Zachary Thomas McCall’s (Plaintiff) Complaint.  The demurrer placed into issue the first cause of action (COA) for breach of contract through breach of express warranty; the second COA for breach of the implied warranty of merchantability; the third COA for strict liability for manufacturing defect; the fourth COA for strict liability for design defect – consumer expectations; the fifth COA for strict liability for design defect – risk benefit; the sixth COA for strict liability for failure to warn; the seventh COA for general negligence; the eighth COA for negligent failure to recall/retrofit; the ninth COA for breach of contract by way of breach of implied warranty of fitness for purpose; tenth COA for breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing; the eleventh COA for concealment; the twelfth COA for false promise; the thirteenth COA for negligent misrepresentation; and the fourteenth COA for intentional misrepresentation.  Plaintiff filed an opposition to the demurrer and Defendant filed their reply.

Defendant’s motion to strike sought to strike improper allegations supporting the requested relief under the Song-Beverly Claims, breach of warranty and breach of contract; injunctive relief; rescission or restitution.  Plaintiff did not file an opposition to the motion to strike.

Discussion

The Court finds Defendant’s arguments in the demurrer and motion to strike to be persuasive.  Plaintiff’s opposition to the demurrer was merely a rendition of the general rules governing demurrers and failed to present argument against the substance of the demurrer.  Plaintiff also relied on their attempt to file a First Amended Complaint (FAC).    The attempted filing of the FAC was properly rejected by the Court on July 30, 2024 because Co-Defendant Keyes Motors Inc. (Keyes) filed an Answer on March 6, 2024.  Keyes’ Answer foreclosed Plaintiff’s right to file an amendment without leave of Court.  (Code Civ. Proc., sec. 472;  Woo v. Superior Court (1999) 75 Cal.App.4th 169, 175.)  However, Plaintiff’s attempt to amend the Complaint is sufficient to show that there is a possibility of curing the pleading defects identified by Defendant’s demurrer and motion to strike.  In that this is the Court’s first review of the pleading, the Court exercises its discretion in following its rule of liberally permitting amendments.  (Tarrar Enterprises, Inc. v. Associated Indemnity Corp. (2022) 83 Cal.App.5th 685, 688-689; Stevens v. Superior Court (1999) 75 Cal.App.4th 594, 601.) 

The demurrer to the Complaint is SUSTAINED WITH LEAVE TO AMEND.

The motion to strike is GRANTED WITH LEAVE TO AMEND. 

IT IS SO ORDERED, CLERK TO GIVE NOTICE.


Case Number: 24VECV02353    Hearing Date: October 15, 2024    Dept: T

24VECV02353 Broumandi v Lavian

The motion for trial setting priority is denied without prejudice.

The Declaration of Mr. Madnick is insufficient to support the need for priority.  Plaintiff’s age of 90, alone, is not enough to obtain trial setting priority.  The motion is made pursuant to CCP section 36(a) which requires that  (1) the party has a substantial interest in the action as a whole, and (2) the party’s health is such that preference is necessary “to prevent prejudicing the party’s interest in the litigation.” (Emphasis added.)

To that end, there must be some evidence of the plaintiff’s health.  In Mr. Madnick’s declaration, the references to his client’s health are as follows: He suffers from various medical conditions.  He has a difficult time hearing.  Ha has general body pain in his knees and joints.  He has stress from the case.  His health continues to decline.

There is no description of the various medical conditions. No mention is made of how failure to grant priority causes him prejudice because of his hearing loss.  No mention is made of how failure to grant priority causes him prejudice because of aches and pains in his knees and joints. There is no mention of how his health continues to decline. At a minimum, the declaration should disclose counsel’s understanding of the plaintiff’s medical diagnosis and prognosis.  Again, age alone is insufficient under the law. Under Life Expectancy Tables in CACI, a man of plaintiff’s age has an estimated life expectancy of 4.5+ years. This is not a personal injury case.  The Elder Abuse claim is for financial abuse.

In the absence of any additional evidence, on information and belief or otherwise, of the plaintiff’s health conditions which would make preference necessary to prevent prejudice, the court denies the motion without prejudice.
