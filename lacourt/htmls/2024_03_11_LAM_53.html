Tentative rulings are sometimes, but not always, posted. The purpose of posting a tentative ruling is to to help focus the argument. The posting of a tentative ruling is not an invitation for the filing of additional papers shortly before the hearing.


Case Number: 19STCV28515    Hearing Date: March 11, 2024    Dept: 53

Superior Court of California

County of Los Angeles – Central District

Department 53

 

 

michael laporta , et al.;

 

Plaintiffs,

 

 

vs.

 

 

david w. affeld , et al.;

 

Defendants.

Case No.:

19STCV28515

 

 

Hearing Date:

March 11, 2024

 

 

Time:

10:00 a.m.

 

 

 

[Tentative] Order RE:

 

plaintiffs’ motion to allow additional expert testimony

 

 

MOVING PARTIES:             Plaintiffs Michael LaPorta and Beverlee LaPorta

 

RESPONDING PARTIES:     Defendants Affeld Grivakes LLP and David W. Affeld

Motion to Allow Additional Expert Testimony

The court considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with this motion.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiffs Michael LaPorta and Beverlee LaPorta (“Plaintiffs”) move the court for an order allowing their expert witness Robert Moss (“Moss) to testify at trial in this action against defendants Affeld Grivakes LLP and David W. Affeld (“Defendants”).  Plaintiffs request this relief on the ground that (1) Plaintiffs designated Paul Hittelman as their expert and intended to rely on his testimony for issues of liability, and (2) on December 15, 2023, Plaintiffs’ counsel was advised that Mr. Hittelman passed away.  (Tillipman Decl., p. 1:8-12.)  Plaintiffs therefore seek to designate Moss as an expert in lieu of Mr. Hittelman.  (Id., p. 1:21-22.)

The court (1) has taken into account the extent to which Defendants have relied on Plaintiffs’ designation of their experts, (2) has determined that Defendants will not be unduly prejudiced in maintaining their defense on the merits if Plaintiffs augment their expert witness list to add Moss as an expert, and (3) has determined that Plaintiffs would not, in the exercise of reasonable diligence, have determined to call Moss in place of Mr. Hittelman.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2034.620, subds. (a), (b), (c)(1).)  The court further notes that “Defendants do not oppose allowing Plaintiffs to augment their expert witness list to add [Moss] as their standard of care expert in place of the deceased Paul Hittelman.”  (Opp., p. 2:6-8.)  The court therefore grants Plaintiffs’ motion and shall permit Plaintiffs to augment their expert witness list to add Moss, who shall be permitted to testify regarding the matters that were previously set forth in Plaintiffs’ November 16, 2021 designation, as requested by Defendants in their opposition and agreed to by Plaintiffs in their reply.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2034.260; Opp., p. 4:6-10 [“Mr. Moss’s testimony must be limited to the scope outlined in Plaintiff’s November 16, 2021 designation”]; Reply, p. 1:25-26 [Plaintiffs “do not oppose the requested limitation in the scope of testimony”].)

Further, the court will set a date for Moss’s deposition pursuant to (1) Code of Civil Procedure section 2034.620, subdivision (d), and (2) the parties’ agreement, as expressed in their opposition and reply papers.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2034.620, subd. (d) [“Leave to augment or amend is conditioned on the moving party making the expert available immediately for a deposition”]; Reply, p. 1:25-26 [Plaintiffs “do not oppose . . . the [request for the] Court to set a deposition date”].)

The court denies Defendants’ request that the court require Plaintiffs to pay the costs and expenses incurred in taking the deposition of Moss.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 2034.620, subd. (d).) 

ORDER

            The court grants plaintiffs Michael LaPorta and Beverlee LaPorta’s motion to allow additional expert witness testimony.

            The court orders that plaintiffs Michael LaPorta and Beverlee LaPorta may augment their expert witness list to add Robert Moss as an expert.  The court orders that Robert Moss may testify on the issues set forth in “Plaintiffs’ Expert Witness Designation” served on November 16, 2021 by plaintiffs Michael LaPorta and Beverlee LaPorta.

Motion for Order Appointing Discovery Referee

The court considered the moving, opposition, and reply papers filed in connection with this motion.

EVIDENTIARY OBJECTIONS

The court rules on plaintiff DB Maple Avenue, LLC’s evidentiary objections, filed on March 4, 2024, as follows:

Objections Nos. 1, 3, and 5-10 are overruled.

Objections Nos. 2 and 4 are sustained.

DISCUSSION

Plaintiff DB Maple Avenue, LLC (“Plaintiff”) moves the court for an order appointing Hon. Howard L. Halm (Ret.) as a discovery referee in this action (1) to monitor future depositions and (2) to adjudicate any issues that may arise therein.  Plaintiff contends that the appointment of a discovery referee is necessary because counsel for defendants 450 Maple Condominium Owners Association, LB Property Management, Inc., Pamela Maxwell, Susan Yeung, and Victoria Lerman (“Defendants”) passed a note to and improperly coached defendant Pamela Maxwell during her deposition.

“When the parties do not consent, the court may, upon the written motion of any party, or of its own motion, appoint a referee in the following cases . . . [¶¶] (5) When the court in any pending action determines that it is necessary for the court to appoint a referee to hear and determine any and all discovery motions and disputes relevant to discovery in the action and to report findings and make a recommendation thereon.”  (Code Civ. Proc., § 639, subd. (a)(5).) 

The court finds that Plaintiff has not shown that it is “necessary” to appoint a discovery referee to monitor and adjudicate issues arising at future depositions taken in this action.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 639, subd. (a)(5).)  Although the court recognizes that Plaintiff has submitted evidence showing that, on one occasion, counsel for Defendants passed a note to Maxwell during her deposition, the court finds that this conduct does not necessitate the appointment of a discovery referee to monitor future depositions.  (Kashfian Decl., ¶ 19.)  The court therefore exercises its discretion to deny Plaintiff’s motion.  (Code Civ. Proc., § 639, subd. (a)(5).)

The Los Angeles County Bar Association has adopted “Guidelines for Civility in Litigation,” which the Los Angeles Superior Court has adopted as civility in litigation recommendations to members of the bar.  (Los Angeles Superior Court Local Rules, Rule 3.26, Appendix 3.A.)  The court expects all counsel to comply with those guidelines for civility, including those governing conduct in depositions in Appendix 3.A, section (e)(7)-(11).

 

 

 

 

 

 

ORDER

            The court denies plaintiff DB Maple Avenue, LLC’s motion for discovery referee.

            The court orders defendants 450 Maple Condominium Owners Association, LB Property Management, Inc., Pamela Maxwell, Susan Yeung, and Victoria Lerman to give notice of this ruling.
